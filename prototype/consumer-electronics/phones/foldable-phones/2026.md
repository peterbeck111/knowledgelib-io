# Best Foldable Phones (2026)

## Summary

The foldable phone market in 2026 has matured significantly, with lighter builds, more durable hinges, larger cover screens, and software finally optimized for folding form factors. The category splits into two main designs: book-style foldables that unfold into tablet-sized screens (7.9-10 inches), and clamshell flip phones that fold compact for pocketability. Samsung continues to dominate market share with three distinct foldable tiers, while Google, Honor, and Motorola offer compelling alternatives. The Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 7 (~$1,999) is the consensus best overall book-style foldable with a 200MP camera, 8-inch inner display, and Snapdragon 8 Elite processor, while the Honor Magic V5 (~$1,799) challenges it as the thinnest book-style foldable at just 8.8mm folded with a massive 5,820mAh battery. [src1, src2, src5]

For flip-phone lovers, the Samsung Galaxy Z Flip 7 (~$1,099) leads with a new edge-to-edge 4.1-inch cover screen and the thinnest profile yet at 6.5mm unfolded, while the Motorola Razr Ultra 2025 (~$1,299) offers the largest outer display on any flip phone at 4.0 inches with a 165Hz inner display. Budget seekers now have real options: the Motorola Razr 2025 at $700 delivers the core flip experience, and the Samsung Galaxy Z Flip 7 FE at ~$960 brings Samsung's foldable tech to a lower price point. The most ambitious device is the Samsung Galaxy Z TriFold (~$2,899), a tri-fold phone that unfurls from 6.5 inches to a 10-inch tablet, representing the next frontier of foldable design. [src1, src4, src6, src7]

## Best for Each Use Case

### Best Overall: Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 7 (~$1,999) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/samsung-galaxy-z-fold-7)
The Galaxy Z Fold 7 earns the top spot from Tom's Guide, Android Authority, and multiple other reviewers as the best all-around foldable phone in 2026. It is the thinnest Galaxy Fold ever at 4.2mm unfolded and 8.9mm folded, with a stunning 8-inch Dynamic LTPO AMOLED 2X inner display and a usable 6.5-inch cover screen. The 200MP main camera is a massive upgrade over previous Fold generations, and the Snapdragon 8 Elite processor with 12GB RAM handles multitasking with up to three split-screen windows seamlessly. It ships with Android 16 and One UI 8, with seven years of OS and security updates guaranteed. [src1, src2, src3]

### Best Battery Life: Honor Magic V5 (~$1,799) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/honor-magic-v5)
The Honor Magic V5 uses silicon-carbon battery technology to pack a massive 5,820mAh cell into a frame that measures just 8.8mm folded and 4.1mm unfolded, making it the thinnest book-style foldable on the market. Tom's Guide called it "just as good as the Galaxy Z Fold 7 and Pixel 10 Pro Fold," with a 7.95-inch BOE OLED inner display hitting 5,000 nits peak brightness. The 50MP primary camera with a 50MP 3.5x periscope telephoto delivers versatile photography, and 66W wired plus 50W wireless charging ensures fast top-ups. Available globally, though US buyers may need to import. [src2, src4, src5]

### Best Camera Processing: Google Pixel 10 Pro Fold (~$1,799) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/google-pixel-10-pro-fold)
Google's second-generation foldable brings several firsts to the category: IP68 water and dust resistance (the first book-style foldable to achieve this), Qi2 magnetic wireless charging, and a 5x optical telephoto. Powered by the Tensor G5 chip with 16GB RAM, it excels at computational photography with Google's AI-driven photo processing, Night Sight, Magic Eraser, and Best Take features. The 8-inch Super Actua Flex inner display and 6.4-inch cover display both hit 3,000 nits peak brightness. At 258g it is heavier than competitors, but the IP68 durability and Google's software polish make it the most worry-free foldable to use daily. [src1, src2, src8]

### Best Tablet Experience: Samsung Galaxy Z TriFold (~$2,899) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/samsung-galaxy-z-trifold)
The most innovative foldable of 2026, the Galaxy Z TriFold unfolds from a standard 6.5-inch phone into a massive 10-inch tablet with a near-4:3 aspect ratio. Powered by Snapdragon 8 Elite with 16GB RAM and a 5,600mAh battery with 45W charging (a first for Samsung foldables), it targets power users who want a true tablet replacement. The dual-hinge design folds to just 3.9mm at its thinnest point when fully open. At $2,899 for the 512GB model, it is firmly ultra-premium, and it sold out within minutes of its US launch in January 2026. Currently only available through Samsung.com. [src1, src6]

### Best Flip Phone: Samsung Galaxy Z Flip 7 (~$1,099) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/samsung-galaxy-z-flip-7)
The Z Flip 7 represents the biggest generational leap for Samsung's clamshell line, with a new edge-to-edge 4.1-inch cover display that makes the phone genuinely usable without opening it. At just 6.5mm unfolded and 13.7mm folded, it is the slimmest Galaxy Flip ever. The 6.9-inch inner AMOLED display runs at 120Hz with 2,600 nits peak brightness. Samsung DeX support is new for the Flip line, enabling a desktop-like experience when connected to a monitor. The 50MP main camera with Dual Pixel autofocus and OIS produces excellent results. [src1, src2, src4]

### Best Flip Display: Motorola Razr Ultra 2025 (~$1,299) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/motorola-razr-ultra-2025)
The Razr Ultra 2025 pushes flip phone displays to new limits with a 7.0-inch Super HD pOLED inner display running at 165Hz — the highest refresh rate on any foldable — and a 4.0-inch OLED cover screen that spans the entire front half. The Snapdragon 8 Elite processor with 16GB RAM and up to 1TB storage makes it the most powerful flip phone available. The 4,700mAh battery with 68W TurboPower charging (50% in 8 minutes) solves the perennial flip phone battery anxiety. Dual 50MP cameras (wide + ultrawide) round out a premium package, though the $1,299 price is steep for a flip. [src1, src4, src7]

### Best Budget Foldable: Motorola Razr (2025) (~$700) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/motorola-razr-2025)
The base Motorola Razr 2025 brings foldable technology to a sub-$1,000 price point without feeling like a compromise. It features a 6.9-inch AMOLED inner display, a 3.6-inch external OLED screen, and a 50MP main camera with a 13MP ultrawide. The MediaTek Dimensity 7300X processor handles everyday tasks smoothly, and the 4,500mAh battery provides all-day endurance. While it lacks the flagship processor and premium materials of the Ultra, it delivers the core flip phone experience — compact when closed, full-screen when open — at a price that makes foldables accessible to mainstream buyers. [src1, src2, src4]

--> The Samsung Galaxy Z Flip 7 (~$1,099) is the strongest pick with its 4.1-inch cover screen, DeX support, and 6.5mm unfolded thickness. For a budget option, the Motorola Razr 2025 at $700 delivers the core flip experience with a 6.9-inch inner display and all-day battery. [src1, src4]

--> The Google Pixel 10 Pro Fold (~$1,799) has the best computational photography (Night Sight, Magic Eraser, Best Take) and the only 5x optical telephoto on a foldable. ### If user needs maximum durability and water resistance
--> The Google Pixel 10 Pro Fold is the only book-style foldable with IP68 water and dust resistance. Most other foldables carry IP48 (water resistant, limited dust protection). No flip phone currently matches IP68. [src1, src2, src8]

### If user wants the largest possible screen for productivity
--> The Samsung Galaxy Z TriFold (~$2,899) unfolds to a 10-inch tablet with near-4:3 aspect ratio, but availability is extremely limited and it is only sold through Samsung.com. The next-best option is the Z Fold 7 with its 8-inch inner display and three-app split-screen multitasking. [src1, src6]

### If user is in the Apple ecosystem and willing to wait
--> No foldable iPhone exists as of late April 2026 — the rumored iPhone Fold (possibly branded "iPhone Ultra") is widely reported for September 2026 launch alongside the iPhone 18 Pro lineup, with a possible December slip. Users who cannot wait should choose the Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 7 or Google Pixel 10 Pro Fold and rely on iCloud/cross-platform apps for ecosystem continuity. [src10]

### Default recommendation
--> For most users new to foldables, the Samsung Galaxy Z Flip 7 (~$1,099) offers the best entry point: it is compact, relatively affordable for the category, and the 4.1-inch cover screen makes it genuinely practical without unfolding. For users who want tablet-like productivity, the Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 7 (~$1,999) is the safest all-around choice. [src1, src2, src4, src9]

## Key Market Trends (2026)

- **Tri-fold devices arrive**: The Samsung Galaxy Z TriFold is the first commercially available tri-fold phone, proving that two-hinge designs are viable for consumers. Huawei's Mate XT preceded it in China, and more competitors are expected to follow in 2026-2027. [src1, src6]
- **Thinner-is-better race intensifies**: The Honor Magic V5 at 8.8mm folded, the Z Fold 7 at 8.9mm, and the Z Flip 7 at 6.5mm unfolded show that manufacturers have largely solved the thickness problem that plagued early foldables. Silicon-carbon batteries enable larger capacities in thinner frames. [src2, src5]
- **Cover screens become fully functional**: The Z Flip 7's 4.1-inch and Razr Ultra's 4.0-inch cover screens can run full apps, reply to messages, take selfies, and handle payments without opening the phone. This transforms flip phones from novelties into genuinely practical devices. [src1, src4, src7]
- **Durability improving but not solved**: The Pixel 10 Pro Fold's IP68 rating is a breakthrough, while most foldables still carry IP48 ratings (water resistant but limited dust protection). Screen crease visibility continues to decrease with each generation, though it remains detectable under direct light. [src1, src2, src8]
- **Price stratification widens**: The market now spans from $700 (Razr 2025) to $2,899 (Z TriFold), with options at every price tier. Budget foldables under $1,000 are the fastest-growing segment, making the form factor mainstream. [src1, src4]
- **Apple's foldable arrives late 2026 (rumored)**: The iPhone Fold (rumored Apple branding: "iPhone Ultra") is widely reported for September 2026 launch alongside the iPhone 18 Pro lineup, with a possible December slip. As of late April 2026, pricing has firmed at $1,999 starting (matching Galaxy Z Fold 7), $2,199 for 512GB, $2,399 for 1TB. Leaks suggest a 7.8-inch inner display with 4:3 aspect ratio, 5.5-inch cover display, Touch ID instead of Face ID, dual rear cameras (no telephoto), a claimed "crease-free" hinge, and ~9.5mm closed / ~4.5mm open dimensions. iOS 19 at WWDC 2026 (June) is expected to debut dedicated foldable support features. [src10]
- **The summer 2026 foldable wave**: The Z Fold 8 (July 22, 2026), Z Flip 8, Razr 2026 family (April 29, 2026), and rumored iPhone Fold (September 2026) compress most major foldable launches into a five-month window. Samsung's S Pen return on the Z Fold 8, the Z Fold 8 "Wide" 4:3 variant, and the Razr Ultra 2026's $1,499 price point all point to feature differentiation rather than thinness as the next competitive frontier.
