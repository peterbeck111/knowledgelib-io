Summary

Apple's current iPhone lineup spans seven models from $599 to $1,999, and the right choice depends on your priorities: camera quality, battery life, screen size, design, or budget. The iPhone 17 ($799) remains the best choice for most people, delivering a 120Hz ProMotion display, the A19 chip with Apple Intelligence, dual 48MP cameras, and 256GB base storage at a price that balances performance with value. It finally brings ProMotion to the non-Pro lineup, closing the most significant gap between tiers. [src1, src2]

For users who want the absolute best, the iPhone 17 Pro Max ($1,199) earns the top overall spot with its 6.9-inch display, A19 Pro chip with vapor chamber cooling, triple 48MP camera system with 4x optical telephoto (8x sensor-crop “optical-quality” reach), and up to 39 hours of video playback — the longest battery life of any iPhone ever. Tom's Guide's battery test measured 17 hours and 54 minutes of real-world use, about 20 minutes longer than the iPhone 16 Pro Max and 3.5 hours longer than the Galaxy S25 Ultra. The iPhone 17 Pro ($1,099) packs the same camera system and chip into a more compact 6.3-inch frame. [src1, src2, src8]

The iPhone 17e ($599), which launched March 11, 2026 to replace the discontinued iPhone 16e, is now ~7 weeks in market and consensus picks call it “actually a good buy.” It runs the A19 chip, 256GB base storage, MagSafe wireless charging at 15W, and Apple's new C1X modem — all at the same $599 price. Tom's Guide measured 12 hours 35 minutes of active battery life, nearly matching the standard iPhone 17's 12 hours 47 minutes. The iPhone Air ($999) occupies a unique niche as Apple's thinnest-ever iPhone at 5.6mm, powered by the A19 Pro chip but with a single rear camera and mixed reviews on battery life. As of February 2026, Apple also began selling refurbished iPhone 16 Pro and Pro Max directly, offering another value path for buyers who want Pro features at lower prices. [src1, src5, src6, src7]

Best for Each Use Case

Best Overall: iPhone 17 Pro Max (~$1,199) — Check price

The iPhone 17 Pro Max is the most capable iPhone Apple has ever made. Its A19 Pro chip with vapor chamber cooling delivers sustained peak performance — nearly 20% faster single-core and 30% faster multi-core than the iPhone 16 Pro Max. The triple 48MP camera system with 4x true optical telephoto (extending to 8x optical-quality and 40x max digital via sensor cropping) is unmatched in the lineup, and 39 hours of video playback is category-leading. The 6.9-inch display at 3,000 nits peak brightness with anti-glare coating makes it outstanding for media consumption. The aluminum unibody design improves heat dissipation over prior titanium models, with new anodized color finishes including Silver, Cosmic Orange, and Deep Blue. TechRadar's testing confirmed over 30 hours before requiring a charge. [src1, src2, src8, src9]

Best for Most People: iPhone 17 (~$799) — Check price

The iPhone 17 is the sweet spot for the vast majority of buyers. It matches the Pro models' 120Hz ProMotion display at 3,000 nits with always-on functionality, starts at 256GB (double the iPhone 16), and includes dual 48MP cameras with an upgraded 18MP Center Stage front camera. The A19 chip handles Apple Intelligence identically to the rest of the iPhone 17 lineup. Tom's Guide measured 12 hours 47 minutes of active battery life. At $799, it saves $300-$400 over the Pro models while delivering 90% of the experience. Tom's Guide calls it the best value pick of the lineup. [src1, src2]

Best for Photography: iPhone 17 Pro / Pro Max (~$1,099-$1,199) — Check price

Both Pro models share the same triple 48MP Fusion camera system. The new tetraprism telephoto delivers 4x true optical zoom (100mm equiv) and a 56% larger sensor than its predecessor; the 8x “optical-quality” mode is a high-quality sensor crop that DPReview rates as visibly superior to digital zoom on rivals. ProRes RAW recording, Apple Log 2, and external SSD recording make these the first iPhones that function as a true camera system for professionals. The camera app offers 0.5x, 1x, 2x, 4x, and 8x zoom shortcuts. [src1, src3, src8]

Best for Battery Life: iPhone 17 Pro Max (~$1,199) — Check price

With up to 39 hours of video playback and a 5,088 mAh battery (eSIM model, up from 4,685 mAh prior gen), the iPhone 17 Pro Max delivers the longest battery life Apple has ever shipped. Tom's Guide measured 17h 54m of real-world active use — 20 minutes longer than the 16 Pro Max and 3.5 hours longer than the Galaxy S25 Ultra. Fast charging reaches 50% in 20 minutes with a 40W adapter. MagSafe wireless charging supports 25W. [src1, src2, src8]

Best Budget iPhone: iPhone 17e (~$599) — Check price

The iPhone 17e launched March 11, 2026, replacing the discontinued iPhone 16e at the same $599 price with major upgrades. It packs the A19 chip (binned with 4-core GPU), 256GB base storage (double the 16e), MagSafe wireless charging at 15W (up from 7.5W Qi on 16e), Apple's faster C1X modem, and Ceramic Shield 2 glass that is 3x more scratch-resistant. The 48MP camera gains improved portrait algorithms borrowed from the iPhone Air. The trade-offs: 60Hz display, single rear camera, and no Camera Control button. Tom's Guide rated it 4/5 stars, calling it "what the iPhone 16e should've been from the start." Tom's Guide measured 12h 35m of active battery life — nearly matching the standard iPhone 17. After 7 weeks in market it has displaced the iPhone 16 as the entry-level value pick (more storage, faster chip, MagSafe, and $100 cheaper). [src1, src4, src5, src6]

Best Large-Screen Value: iPhone 16 Plus (~$799) — Check price

If a big 6.7-inch display and long battery life are top priorities but you want to avoid the $1,199 Pro Max price, the iPhone 16 Plus delivers strong all-day battery life at a lower price point. The A18 chip supports Apple Intelligence, and the Camera Control button adds quick camera access. The main compromise versus the iPhone 17 is the 60Hz display and 12MP ultra-wide camera. Note: as of April 2026, US availability has tightened — check Apple, AT&T, or Verizon for current stock. [src1, src2]

Most Stylish / Lightest: iPhone Air (~$999) — Check price

At just 5.6mm thick and 165g, the iPhone Air is Apple's thinnest and lightest full-size iPhone. It runs the same A19 Pro chip found in the Pro models (minus one GPU core), has a beautiful 6.5-inch 120Hz display at 3,000 nits, and features a titanium unibody design. However, reviews remain mixed: praised for design and A19 Pro chip, criticized for weak mono speaker output, limited battery life (3,149 mAh), and the single 48MP camera lacking ultra-wide and telephoto lenses. Battery life of 27 hours video playback trails the iPhone 17 and Pro models. [src1, src2, src3]

Decision Logic

If budget < $600

→ iPhone 17e ($599). The new budget champion with A19 chip, 256GB storage, MagSafe, and Ceramic Shield 2. Replaces the discontinued iPhone 16e with strictly better specs at the same price. [src4, src5, src6]

If budget is $600-$800

→ iPhone 17 ($799) if 120Hz and dual 48MP cameras matter. iPhone 17e ($599) if saving $200 matters more than the second camera and 120Hz. iPhone 16 ($699) is no longer the value pick — iPhone 17e is faster, has more storage, and costs $100 less. [src1, src2, src4]

If primary use is photography or video

→ iPhone 17 Pro or Pro Max ($1,099-$1,199). Triple 48MP system with 4x true optical telephoto (8x optical-quality via sensor crop), ProRes RAW, Apple Log 2. The Pro Max adds longer battery for extended shoots. Camera quality differences are most pronounced in telephoto zoom and low-light scenarios. [src1, src3, src8]

If user wants the largest screen

→ iPhone 17 Pro Max ($1,199) for 6.9-inch at 120Hz, or iPhone 16 Plus ($799) for 6.7-inch at 60Hz to save $400 (subject to availability). [src1, src2]

If user prioritizes thin/light design

→ iPhone Air ($999). At 5.6mm and 165g, it is the thinnest and lightest iPhone. Same A19 Pro chip as the Pro models. Trade-offs: single rear camera, mono speaker, and shorter battery life than iPhone 17. [src1, src3]

If user wants maximum battery life

→ iPhone 17 Pro Max ($1,199). 39h video playback, 5,088 mAh, 17h 54m in Tom's Guide real-world testing. No other iPhone comes close. The iPhone 17e (26h video, 4,005 mAh) is the budget battery leader. [src1, src8]

If user wants Pro features at a discount

→ Apple's refurbished iPhone 16 Pro / Pro Max (available direct from Apple since February 2026). One-year warranty, full Apple Intelligence support via A18 Pro, and titanium frame — typically 15% off MSRP. Better value than buying new iPhone 16 Plus if Pro camera matters. [src7]

Default recommendation

→ iPhone 17 ($799). Consensus pick across Tom's Guide and MacRumors buyer's guides. ProMotion 120Hz display at 3,000 nits, dual 48MP cameras, 256GB base storage, A19 chip with Apple Intelligence. Best balance of performance, features, and price. [src1, src2]

Key Market Trends (2026)

  • iPhone 17e replaces iPhone 16e (March 2026): Apple launched the iPhone 17e on March 11, 2026 at $599, discontinuing the 16e. Key upgrades: A19 chip, 256GB base storage (2x), MagSafe at 15W, C1X modem (2x faster than C1), and Ceramic Shield 2 glass. After 7 weeks in market it is the consensus budget pick. [src4, src5, src6]
  • ProMotion reaches the standard iPhone: The iPhone 17 is the first non-Pro iPhone with a 120Hz display and always-on functionality at 3,000 nits. This eliminates the most visible difference between Pro and non-Pro tiers. [src1, src2]
  • Vapor chamber cooling on Pro models: The A19 Pro uses vapor chamber cooling for sustained performance under load, a first for iPhone. Pro models switched from titanium to aluminum frames to further improve heat dissipation. [src1, src8]
  • iPhone Air draws mixed reviews: Apple's thinnest phone at 5.6mm polarizes reviewers — praised for design and A19 Pro chip, criticized for single camera, mono speaker, and battery life concerns. Sales have reportedly disappointed Apple, with plans for iPhone Air 2 uncertain. [src1, src2, src3]
  • Storage floor rising: iPhone 17, 17e, Air, and Pro models all start at 256GB (up from 128GB). Only Pro Max offers 2TB; iPhone 17 Pro caps at 1TB; iPhone 17, Air, and 17e cap at 512GB. Only the prior-gen iPhone 16 and 16 Plus still start at 128GB. [src5, src8]
  • Apple-direct refurbished Pro models (Feb 2026): Apple began selling refurbished iPhone 16 Pro and Pro Max directly through its certified-refurbished store, typically discounted ~15% from MSRP with one-year Apple warranty. Opens a new value path for Pro features below $999. [src7]
  • Apple Intelligence is the baseline: Every current iPhone model supports Apple Intelligence with 8GB RAM. This is no longer a differentiator. [src2, src4]

Important Caveats

  • Prices listed are US MSRP from Apple. Carrier deals, trade-in credits ($40-$700), and regional pricing can significantly alter the effective cost. Refurbished iPhone 16 Pro / Pro Max are now available directly from Apple at lower price points.
  • Unlocked iPhone 17 costs $829 ($30 more than the carrier-locked $799 price). iPhone 17 Pro, Pro Max, Air, and 17e have no unlocked surcharge.
  • Apple Intelligence features require iPhone 17/16/Air/17e models with 8GB RAM. Older iPhones (iPhone 15 and earlier without 8GB RAM) do not support Apple Intelligence.
  • The iPhone 16e has been discontinued as of March 2026 following the iPhone 17e launch. Remaining stock may be available through third-party retailers.
  • iPhone 16 Plus stock has tightened in some US channels as of early 2026 — confirm availability with Apple, AT&T, or Verizon before assuming it is in stock.
  • Battery life figures are Apple's internal test results for local video playback under ideal conditions. Real-world usage with screen brightness, 5G, and background apps will yield lower numbers.
  • Camera quality differences between iPhone 17 and iPhone 17 Pro are most pronounced in telephoto zoom and low-light scenarios. For standard daylight photography, the iPhone 17's dual camera system is excellent.
  • The iPhone 17e uses a binned A19 chip with a 4-core GPU (vs 5-core on the standard iPhone 17). Gaming performance is measurably lower, though everyday tasks feel identical.
  • iPhone 17 Pro telephoto: 4x is the true optical zoom (100mm equiv); 8x “optical-quality” is a sensor-crop digital zoom of the 48MP tetraprism sensor that retains very high quality but is not strictly optical.
