# Best 85-Inch TVs (2026)

## Summary

The 85-inch TV market in early 2026 spans a wide performance and price range, from ~$1,000 budget Mini-LEDs to $4,000+ premium OLEDs. Mini-LED technology dominates this size class, with the Samsung QN85QN90F (~$2,800-3,200) earning the top overall pick for its combination of Neo QLED Mini-LED brightness, Samsung Vision AI processing, and outstanding gaming features including four HDMI 2.1 ports with Game Hub. The Hisense 85U8QG (~$2,000-2,400) offers the best price-to-performance ratio with 5,000 nits peak brightness, 165Hz native refresh, and LD5600 local dimming zones — rivaling TVs costing $1,000 more. [src1, src2, src4]

For buyers willing to spend more, the LG OLED83C5PUA (~$2,600-3,200) delivers the best picture quality in this size class with perfect blacks, infinite contrast, and wide viewing angles — the only OLED option near 85 inches. The TCL 85QM8K (~$1,800-2,200) is the brightness champion among value picks with 5,000+ nits QD-Mini LED and Bang & Olufsen audio. Budget shoppers can get excellent 85-inch Mini-LED performance from the TCL 85QM6K (~$1,000-1,300) or Hisense 85U7N (~$1,100-1,400), both offering 144Hz panels, HDMI 2.1, and Dolby Vision at prices that were unimaginable two years ago. [src2, src3, src5]

### Best Overall: Samsung QN85QN90F (~$2,800-3,200) -- [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/samsung-85qn90f)
The 2025 Neo QLED Mini-LED flagship delivers the best all-around experience at 85 inches. Samsung Vision AI automatically optimizes picture and sound for every scene. Neo Quantum HDR+ with 2,500+ nits peak brightness handles any content in any lighting. Four full HDMI 2.1 ports, Game Hub for cloud gaming, FreeSync Premium Pro, and Object Tracking Sound+ with Dolby Atmos make it equally strong for gaming and movies. Glare-free screen coating handles bright rooms well. [src1, src2, src6]

### Best Value: Hisense 85U8QG (~$2,000-2,400) -- [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/hisense-85u8qg)
The standout value proposition in the 85-inch market. 5,000 nits peak brightness and LD5600 local dimming zones deliver picture quality that competes with TVs costing $1,000 more. Native 165Hz with VRR 288 makes it excellent for gaming. 4.1.2 channel surround with built-in subwoofer and up-firing speakers. Dolby Vision IQ, HDR10+, IMAX Enhanced. Google TV with Chromecast built-in. At $2,000-2,400, it undercuts the Samsung QN90F by $800+ while matching or exceeding it in raw brightness. [src1, src4, src7]

### Best OLED / Dark Room: LG OLED83C5PUA (~$2,600-3,200) -- [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/lg-oled83c5)
The only OLED option near 85 inches (83-inch panel). Perfect per-pixel black levels, infinite contrast ratio, and near-180-degree viewing angles make it the undisputed choice for dark-room movie watching. OLED evo panel with AI Super Upscaling 4K. webOS 25 with AI voice search. Four HDMI 2.1 ports with 120Hz and VRR for gaming. Dolby Vision, Dolby Atmos, and Filmmaker Mode. Wow Orchestra syncs with LG soundbars. The trade-off: lower peak brightness (~1,300 nits) means Mini-LEDs outperform it in bright rooms. [src2, src3, src6]

### Best for Gaming: Samsung QN85QN90F (~$2,800-3,200) -- [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/samsung-85qn90f)

Object Tracking Sound+ places audio spatially based on on-screen action. For budget gaming, the Hisense 85U75QG (~$1,300-1,600) with 165Hz and VRR 288 is a strong alternative at half the price. ## Decision Logic

### If budget < $1,500
--> TCL 85QM6K (~$1,000-1,300) or Hisense 85U7N (~$1,100-1,400). Both offer 144Hz Mini-LED with HDMI 2.1. The TCL has higher brightness; the Hisense has better local dimming and Dolby Vision IQ. [src4, src5]

### If budget is $1,500-$2,500
--> Hisense 85U8QG (~$2,000-2,400) for the best value at any 85-inch price point. 5,000 nits brightness, 165Hz, and premium audio for the price of a mid-range competitor. TCL 85QM8K (~$1,800-2,200) is slightly cheaper with similar brightness. [src1, src4, src7]

### If primary use is dark-room movie watching
--> LG OLED83C5PUA for perfect blacks and infinite contrast. If OLED is over budget, the Hisense 85U8QG with LD5600 local dimming offers the best LED black levels in this size. [src2, src3]

### If primary use is gaming
--> Samsung QN85QN90F for four HDMI 2.1 ports and Game Hub. On a tighter budget, the Hisense 85U75QG (~$1,300-1,600) with 165Hz and VRR 288 delivers premium gaming at nearly half the price. [src1, src2]

### If room is very bright with direct sunlight
--> TCL 85QM8K (5,000+ nits) or Hisense 85U8QG (5,000 nits) for the highest brightness. Avoid the LG OLED C5 in very bright rooms — OLED brightness cannot compete with Mini-LED in high-ambient-light conditions. [src1, src4]

### Default recommendation
--> Hisense 85U8QG (~$2,000-2,400). The best balance of price, brightness, gaming performance, and audio quality at 85 inches. It handles all content types well and undercuts premium competitors by $800-1,000 with comparable or superior brightness. [src1, src4, src7]

## Key Market Trends (2026)

- **85-inch prices have collapsed**: Two years ago, a quality 85-inch Mini-LED TV cost $3,000+. In 2026, the TCL QM6K delivers capable Mini-LED at $1,000. Price-per-inch has dropped 40-50% across the segment. [src4, src5]
- **Mini-LED brightness exceeds 5,000 nits**: The Hisense U8QG and TCL QM8K both hit 5,000+ nits, making the brightness gap between LED and OLED wider than ever. For bright-room viewers, Mini-LED is the clear choice. [src1, src4]
- **OLED remains limited at 85 inches**: LG's 83-inch C5 is the only OLED option near this size under $4,000. Samsung's QD-OLED line tops out at 77 inches. True 85-inch OLED panels remain a premium niche. [src2, src3]
- **165Hz panels entering mid-range**: Hisense's U8QG and U75QG both offer 165Hz native refresh with VRR 288, features that were flagship-only in 2024. Combined with HDMI 2.1, 85-inch TVs are increasingly viable as primary gaming displays. [src1, src7]
- **Samsung Vision AI and AI processing**: The 2025 model year emphasizes AI-driven scene optimization across brands. Samsung's Vision AI, LG's AI Super Upscaling, and Sony's cognitive XR processor all use machine learning to automatically adjust picture and sound settings. [src2, src6]

## Important Caveats

- Prices are approximate US street prices as of March 2026. Sales events (Prime Day, Black Friday) can drop prices 20-40%. Prices outside the US vary significantly.
- The LG OLED83C5PUA is 83 inches, not 85. It is included because it is the closest OLED alternative to 85-inch Mini-LED TVs.
- Brightness figures (nits) are peak measurements in small HDR highlights. Full-screen sustained brightness is typically 30-50% of peak values.
- Weight figures are approximate without stand. Add 15-25 lbs for the included stand. Professional wall mounting is recommended for TVs over 75 lbs.
- Smart TV platforms (Google TV, Tizen, webOS) collect viewing data by default. Review privacy settings during initial setup.
- 85-inch TVs require 200-450W depending on panel type and brightness. OLED consumes less power on average than full-brightness Mini-LED.
