# Best Mini LED TVs (2026)

## Summary

Mini LED backlighting has become the dominant technology for premium LCD TVs in 2025-2026, delivering dramatically improved contrast, HDR performance, and peak brightness compared to standard LED or QLED sets. The technology uses thousands of individually controlled tiny LEDs behind the panel, enabling precise local dimming that approaches -- but does not match -- OLED black levels. The best Mini LED TVs now exceed 3,000 nits peak brightness, making them the top choice for bright rooms and HDR content. [src1, src2]

The Sony BRAVIA 9 is the best Mini LED TV RTINGS has tested, with 1,512 dimming zones (65-inch) and near-OLED contrast thanks to Sony's 22-bit LED driver technology. For bright rooms, the Samsung QN90F leads with its Glare-Free 2.0 anti-reflective coating and 720 zones (65-inch), while the Hisense U8QG (2025) pushes the value envelope with 5,000 nits peak brightness, native 165Hz, and up to 5,600 dimming zones at prices starting under $1,000. The TCL QM9K represents the performance flagship from TCL with up to 6,500 nits and 6,000 dimming zones. At the budget end, the TCL QM6K and QM7K bring Mini LED benefits to the $500-$900 range. [src1, src3, src5, src6]

Looking ahead to 2026, RGB Mini LED is the major technology shift. Both TCL and Hisense are releasing RGB Mini LED TVs that replace the traditional white LED + color filter approach with red, green, and blue LEDs, promising wider color gamuts and better energy efficiency. Samsung's 2026 lineup introduces the MH90, MH80, and MH70 Mini LED series. The transition is expected to narrow the gap with OLED further. [src7]

## Best for Each Use Case

### Best Overall: Sony BRAVIA 9 (~$2,700 for 65") -- [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/sony-bravia-9-65)
The Sony BRAVIA 9 is the best Mini LED TV RTINGS has ever tested. Its 1,512 dimming zones (65-inch) combined with Sony's proprietary 22-bit LED driver deliver local dimming precision that rivals OLED -- during HDR content, you'd be hard-pressed to spot blooming artifacts. Peak brightness reaches approximately 2,800 nits at a 10% window. Sony's XR Processor produces the most natural, cinema-grade colors of any Mini LED. The trade-off: only 120Hz refresh rate (no 144Hz or 165Hz), and the premium price places it above most competitors. Available in 65", 75", and 85". [src1, src5]

### Best for Bright Rooms: Samsung QN90F (~$1,500 for 65") -- [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/samsung-qn90f-65)
Samsung's flagship 4K Mini LED features Glare-Free 2.0 technology that virtually eliminates reflections while maintaining deep blacks -- the best anti-glare solution on any TV. The 65-inch model has 720 dimming zones, with 900 on the 75-inch. The NQ4 AI Gen3 processor handles upscaling and local dimming, while four HDMI 2.1 ports support 4K at 165Hz with FreeSync Premium Pro. No Dolby Vision support (HDR10+ only). At its current street price of approximately $1,500 (down from $2,500 MSRP), it offers strong value. [src1, src2, src4]

### Best Value Premium: Hisense U8QG (~$1,100 for 65") -- [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/hisense-u8qg-65)
The 2025 Hisense U8QG pushes extraordinary specs at a mid-range price: up to 5,000 nits peak brightness, approximately 5,600 dimming zones (size-dependent), native 165Hz refresh rate, and a 4.1.2-channel speaker system. It supports both Dolby Vision IQ and HDR10+. The upgrade from the U8N includes a jump from 144Hz to 165Hz and expanded dimming zones. At roughly $1,100 for 65 inches, it competes with TVs costing two to three times more on raw specs. The Hi-View Engine X processor provides real-time AI picture optimization. [src2, src3]

### Best for Gaming: Samsung QN90F (~$1,500 for 65") -- [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/samsung-qn90f-65)
The QN90F excels for gaming with four HDMI 2.1 ports all supporting 4K at 165Hz, FreeSync Premium Pro, and Samsung's Gaming Hub for cloud gaming. Input lag is low at approximately 9.5ms. The matte Glare-Free screen prevents reflections during daytime gaming sessions. Samsung's Game Motion Plus provides additional motion smoothing options for fast-paced titles. The Hisense U8QG is a strong alternative at lower cost with 165Hz and VRR 288 support. [src2, src4]

### Best for Movies: Sony BRAVIA 7 (~$1,200 for 65") -- [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/sony-bravia-7-65)
Sony's mid-range Mini LED inherits the BRAVIA 9's XR Backlight Master Drive technology and XR Processor, delivering cinematic picture quality with accurate colors, excellent shadow detail, and refined motion processing. The 65-inch model offers approximately 512 dimming zones. It supports Dolby Vision, HDR10, and HLG. The trade-offs are limited HDMI 2.1 ports (only 2) and higher input lag, making it better suited for movie watching than competitive gaming. At its current sale price of approximately $1,200, it represents excellent value for cinema enthusiasts. [src2, src3]

### Best Budget Mini LED: Hisense U8N (~$800 for 65") -- [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/hisense-u8n-65)
The outgoing Hisense U8N remains one of the best-value Mini LED TVs ever made. It offers approximately 3,000 nits peak brightness with double the dimming zones of its predecessor, a 144Hz native refresh rate, Dolby Vision IQ, HDR10+, and an anti-glare panel. At its current discounted price of around $800 for 65 inches, it delivers Mini LED performance that was flagship-tier just two years ago. The main downside versus the U8QG successor is the lower 144Hz refresh rate and fewer dimming zones. [src1, src3]

### Best Large Screen: Hisense U9N (~$3,000 for 75") -- [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/hisense-u9n-75)
For buyers wanting extreme brightness and screen presence, the Hisense U9N delivers 5,000 nits peak brightness with over 5,300 dimming zones in a 75-inch or 85-inch package. The 82W 4.1.2-channel speaker system with Dolby Atmos is room-filling. Native 144Hz with AMD FreeSync Premium Pro handles gaming well. Available in 75" ($3,000) and 85" ($4,000), with a 65" size added for 2025. [src3, src6]

## Key Market Trends (2026)

- **RGB Mini LED is the next major leap**: 2026 is being called "the year of RGB Mini LED" by RTINGS. TCL and Hisense are shipping RGB Mini LED TVs that use red, green, and blue LEDs instead of white LEDs with color filters, promising 20-30% wider color gamut and improved efficiency. Samsung's MH-series will also adopt RGB Mini LED. [src7]
- **Dimming zone counts are exploding**: Premium models now exceed 5,000-6,000 zones (TCL QM9K, Hisense U9N), dramatically reducing blooming artifacts. Even mid-range TVs like the TCL QM7K offer 2,500 zones. This was flagship territory just one year ago. [src6, src7]
- **Peak brightness race continues**: The TCL QM9K reaches 6,500 nits, Hisense U8QG/U9N hit 5,000 nits. These figures were unthinkable two years ago. The practical ceiling for most content is 1,000-4,000 nits, but higher headroom improves HDR tone mapping. [src3, src6]
- **Mini LED prices continue falling**: Entry-level Mini LED TVs now start under $400 for 55 inches (TCL QM5K). The Hisense U8N at ~$800 for 65" delivers what would have been $2,000+ performance in 2023. [src2, src3]
- **Anti-glare coatings go mainstream**: Samsung's Glare-Free 2.0 on the QN90F set a new standard, and competitors are following. Hisense and TCL both offer anti-reflective panels on their 2025 models. [src4]
- **OLED gap is narrowing**: With RGB Mini LED, the contrast and color accuracy gap between Mini LED and OLED continues to shrink. Mini LED maintains advantages in brightness, burn-in immunity, and price per inch. [src7]

## Important Caveats

- Prices are approximate US street prices for 65-inch models as of March 2026. Launch MSRPs are significantly higher; discounts of 30-50% are common within 3-6 months.
- Dimming zone counts and peak brightness figures vary by screen size within the same model line. Smaller sizes (43-55") typically have fewer zones and lower brightness than 65"+ models.
- Peak brightness is measured at small windows (2-10% of screen area). Full-screen sustained brightness is substantially lower.
- Samsung QN90F does not support Dolby Vision -- it uses HDR10+ exclusively. This affects Apple TV+, Netflix, and Disney+ content that primarily uses Dolby Vision.
- The 2026 RGB Mini LED models (TCL, Hisense, Samsung MH-series) have been announced but most are not yet independently reviewed. Current recommendations are based on tested, available models.
- Mini LED TVs produce more heat than OLED and may require more clearance in enclosed media cabinets.
