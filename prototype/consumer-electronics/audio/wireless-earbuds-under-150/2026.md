## Key Market Trends (2026)

- **Soundcore Liberty 5 launch reshaped the ANC value tier**: Liberty 5 (Q1 2026) brought Adaptive ANC 3.0 and Dolby Audio at $89-129, displacing the Liberty 4 NC as the price/performance champion. Liberty 4 NC still excellent but now positioned as the budget legacy pick. [src3, src8]
- **Touch-display cases trickling down**: Soundcore Liberty 4 Pro brought the case-display feature (previously $250+ flagship-only) to $129. Expect imitators by H2 2026. [src2]
- **Bose ANC at sub-$80**: The Skullcandy x Bose Method 360 has dropped to ~$77 from a $129 launch — the cheapest ever access to flagship Bose ANC algorithms. [src3]
- **Hi-res codecs at budget prices**: LDAC and aptX Lossless now available under $70 (CMF Buds Pro 2, EarFun Air Pro 4+, Soundcore Space A40, Liberty 4 NC). The codec gap between budget and flagship has fully closed. [src1, src2]
- **Bluetooth 5.3/5.4/6.0 + LE Audio (LC3)**: EarFun Air Pro 4+ ships with BT 6.0; LC3 codec offers lower latency and better power efficiency. Expect BT 6.0 to become baseline by late 2026. [src2]
- **AI-enhanced call clarity**: Soundcore Liberty 5 introduced AI-driven 6-mic call processing at $129 — tech previously reserved for $250+ Sony / Bose flagships. [src8]
- **Multipoint is baseline**: Bluetooth multipoint (connect to 2 devices simultaneously) is now standard even in budget earbuds. Nearly every model listed supports it (exceptions: JLab Go Pods ANC, Sony WF-C510). [src1, src2, src3]
- **CMF Buds 2 Plus raises the budget sound bar**: The CMF Buds 2 Plus (Q2 2026) scored 4.8 overall and 4.9 on timbre in SoundGuys testing — performance that matches earbuds at 2-3x the price. The 61.5h total battery also sets a new category record. [src9]
- **Spatial audio still flagship-only**: Dolby Atmos head-tracking is not yet standard under $150 — Liberty 5 includes Dolby Audio but without head-tracking. [src4, src8]

## Important Caveats

- Prices are approximate street prices as of April 2026. Sales and regional pricing vary; Soundcore and EarFun models in particular fluctuate 20-40% week-to-week on Amazon.
- Codec support requires a compatible source device. LDAC and LHDC require Android or a compatible DAP. iPhones are limited to SBC and AAC.
- Driver size alone does not determine sound quality. The Sony WF-C710N uses a 5mm driver yet outperforms larger-driver competitors in subjective tests. Tuning and DSP matter more. [src2]
- ANC performance varies by ear tip fit. Lab measurements assume optimal seal — a poor fit can cut effective isolation by 10-20 dB.
- Manufacturer ANC reduction percentages (e.g., Soundcore's "98.5%") are marketing figures from controlled tests and not directly comparable across brands. SoundGuys' independent lab figures (~80-85%) are more reliable for cross-brand comparison.
