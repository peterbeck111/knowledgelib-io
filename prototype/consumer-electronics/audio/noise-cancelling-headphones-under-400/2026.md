# Best Noise-Cancelling Headphones Under $400 (2026)

## Summary

The $200–400 noise-cancelling headphone segment in late April 2026 has consolidated around two flagships whose street prices keep falling: the Sony WH-1000XM6 (~$309–398 street, MSRP $449) is the consensus best overall pick across RTINGS, Tom's Guide, and What Hi-Fi?, with its QN3 processor and 12-microphone array delivering 87% measured ambient-noise reduction. The Bose QuietComfort Ultra (2nd Gen) (~$349–399) remains the comfort and ease-of-use champion at 85% measured ANC reduction with 30h battery and aptX Adaptive. SoundGuys' head-to-head measured Sony at 37h 14min real-world battery and Bose at 27h 37min. [src1, src3, src5, src8]

The Sennheiser Momentum 4 (~$289) still dominates marathon battery life at 56+ hours with ANC on. The Bowers & Wilkins PX7 S3 (~$399, MSRP $449) is the new best-sound pick, with What Hi-Fi? and Tom's Guide both calling it the most musical option in the bracket — 30h battery, LDAC, and ANC measured at 83–84% reduction. The Sony WH-1000XM5 has dropped to ~$250–329 as the XM6 displaced it, making it the strongest value flagship. The Marshall Monitor III ANC now offers a category-leading 70h with ANC on / 100h off after a 2026 firmware update. [src2, src3, src4, src7]

## Best for Each Use Case

### Best Overall: Sony WH-1000XM6 (~$309–398) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/sony-wh-1000xm6)
The XM6's HD NC Processor QN3 is 7x faster than the QN1 in the XM5 and processes 12 microphones in real time — SoundGuys measured 87% ambient noise reduction, the best in the category. What Hi-Fi? calls it "the most detailed, dynamic, precise and open sound heard from a wireless Sony flagship." Real-world battery measured at 37h 14min with ANC. The asymmetrical headband distributes weight evenly, and the new design folds compactly. MSRP is $449, but street prices have dropped to ~$309–398 by late April 2026, with Adorama offering $309 and Amazon regularly at $368–398 — making it a strong value in the under-$400 bracket. [src1, src3, src5, src8]

### Best Comfort + ANC: Bose QuietComfort Ultra 2nd Gen (~$349–399) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/bose-qc-ultra-2)
SoundGuys measured 85% ambient noise reduction — within 2 percentage points of the Sony XM6, but with notably better passive isolation thanks to plusher ear cushions. Tom's Guide names it "Best Overall" specifically for delivering "the best ANC ever, bar none" combined with all-day comfort. The 2nd Gen adds 30h battery (up from 24h on Gen 1), aptX Adaptive for Android lossless streaming, Bluetooth 5.4, and a Cinema Mode for immersive spatial audio. CustomTune ear profiling personalizes the sound on first wear. The pick if you wear headphones >4 hours/day. [src2, src3, src5, src8]

### Best Sound: Bowers & Wilkins PX7 S3 (~$399–449) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/bowers-wilkins-px7-s3)
New 2026 entry. Tom's Guide calls it the best for "premium audio quality with incredible, well-rounded sound" and What Hi-Fi? gave it a full 5-star review for "stunning sound." Bluetooth 5.3 with aptX Adaptive and LDAC. ANC measured at 83–84% reduction by SoundGuys — slightly behind Sony/Bose but well within the top tier and "less intrusive" in tone. 30h battery, lightest of the flagships at 236g, and B&W's signature warm, refined tuning. The pick for users who prioritize music listening over absolute ANC performance. MSRP $449 but routinely $399 on Amazon. [src3, src4, src7]

### Best Value Flagship: Sony WH-1000XM5 (~$250–329) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/sony-wh-1000xm5)
With the XM6 displacing it, the XM5 routinely sells for $250–329 — a ~30% discount from its $399 MSRP. Dual-processor ANC with 8 microphones, LDAC hi-res audio, 30h battery, and ultralight 250g. Its 30mm drivers deliver the balanced, refined sound Sony has tuned across five generations. DSEE Extreme upscales compressed audio. The XM5 set the benchmark every competitor measured against for three years and remains a top-3 pick across all major reviewers in 2026. [src1, src2, src4]

### Best Battery Life: Sennheiser Momentum 4 (~$289–350) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/sennheiser-momentum-4)
SoundGuys measured 56h 21min with ANC on — more than double Sony XM6 (37h) and Bose QC Ultra (28h). Supports aptX Adaptive for high-bitrate streaming on Android. Sound quality is consistently praised as ahead of the pack at this price. Smart Pause auto-detects when you remove the headphones. At ~$289, it undercuts both Sony and Bose flagships while delivering comparable sound. The pick for travelers, weekly commuters, and anyone who hates charging. [src2, src5]

### Best Features: JBL Tour One M3 (~$300–400) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/jbl-tour-one-m3)
The standout feature is the SMART Tx — a remote/transmitter that lets you control the headphones AND act as a Bluetooth transmitter for sources without Bluetooth (airplane entertainment, gym equipment). Eight microphones power 3-stage adaptive ANC. JBL's specs page now claims 70h with ANC on (revised up from 35h via 2026 firmware optimization) and 70h ANC off. LDAC, LC3 codec support, JBL Spatial 360 with head tracking. SoundGuys calls its ANC "a credible competitor to the AirPods Max, Bose QC Ultra, and Sony XM6." [src2, src6]

### Best for Travel: Marshall Monitor III ANC (~$250–350) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/marshall-monitor-iii-anc)
Tom's Guide names it "Best Battery Life" for the entire ANC category at 70h ANC on / 100h ANC off — the longest-lasting in the bracket. Foldable hard case, premium build, Marshall's signature aesthetic. Now widely on sale at ~$250 (down from $380 MSRP). LC3 codec support and Soundstage spatial audio. The pick for long-haul flights and multi-week trips where you can't reliably charge. [src2, src3]

### Best Value Comfort: Bose QuietComfort (~$249–349) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/bose-quietcomfort)
Reviewers consistently find its noise cancellation "basically as good as headphones costing $100–200 more." At 240g and with Bose's signature plush earcups, engineered for all-day wear. The simplified feature set (no spatial audio, no aptX) keeps the price below $300 on sale. If comfort and ANC are your only priorities and you don't need the Ultra's spatial audio, this delivers ~90% of the experience at ~60% of the price. [src3]

### Best Design: Nothing Headphone 1 (~$300) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/nothing-headphone-1)
KEF-tuned acoustics — Nothing partnered with the British speaker company for the audio profile. 80h battery with ANC off (35h with ANC on). Physical roller and paddle controls replace touch surfaces for reliable tactile feedback. LDAC support, 8-band EQ via the Nothing X app, and the transparent industrial design that defines Nothing's product line. IP52 rating is rare for over-ear ANC headphones. [src2]

### Best Lightweight: Sennheiser ACCENTUM Plus (~$200–249) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/sennheiser-accentum-plus)
At 227g, it's the lightest ANC headphone in this roundup. 53 hours of battery with ANC on. aptX Adaptive codec support. Available in both USB-C and 3.5mm wired modes. Sennheiser's adaptive ANC adjusts to your environment. A strong pick for all-day office or commute use where weight matters more than absolute ANC depth. [src2]

## Decision Logic

### If budget is $200–300
--> Sony WH-1000XM5 (~$250–329) is the strongest value pick — flagship-tier ANC and LDAC at mid-range pricing. Sennheiser Momentum 4 (~$289) if battery life matters most. [src1, src2]

### If budget is $300–400 and primary use is travel/commute
--> Sony WH-1000XM6 (~$309–398) for best ANC + comfort combo, or Marshall Monitor III ANC (~$250–350) if 70h+ battery for long-haul flights matters more than peak ANC depth. [src1, src3]

### If primary use is music listening at home
--> Bowers & Wilkins PX7 S3 (~$399) for best-in-class sound quality. What Hi-Fi? 5-star and Tom's Guide "Best Sound" pick. ANC is 83–84% — within 3 points of category leaders. [src3, src4, src7]

### If user has Apple devices only (iPhone/iPad)
--> LDAC and aptX Adaptive offer no benefit. Choose on ANC and comfort: Bose QC Ultra 2nd Gen (best comfort), Sony WH-1000XM6 (best ANC), or Sony WH-1000XM5 (best value) — all use AAC on iOS. [src5, src8]

### If user has Android with Snapdragon Sound (Galaxy S23+, Pixel 8+)
--> Bose QC Ultra 2nd Gen unlocks aptX Lossless. Otherwise Sony XM6 with LDAC delivers higher bit-rate streaming. [src5, src8]

### If wears headphones >4 hours/day
--> Bose QC Ultra 2nd Gen for plush cushions and proven all-day comfort, or Sennheiser ACCENTUM Plus (227g) if weight matters most. [src2, src3]

### If user hates charging
--> Marshall Monitor III ANC (70h ANC on / 100h off) or JBL Tour One M3 (70h ANC on). Both more than double Sony/Bose flagships. [src2, src3]

### Default recommendation
--> Sony WH-1000XM6 (~$309–398). Best overall pick across RTINGS, Tom's Guide, and What Hi-Fi? in 2026. Best ANC measurement (87%), strong battery (37h), broad codec support, and proven build. [src1, src3, src4]

## Important Caveats

- Prices are approximate US street prices as of late April 2026. Major sales (Memorial Day, Prime Day) routinely cut another 10–20% — check current prices before recommending.
- "Under $400" includes models with MSRP up to $449 that regularly sell below $400 (Sony XM6, B&W PX7 S3, Bose QC Ultra 2nd Gen). Confirm current price before assuming under-budget.
- Codec availability depends on your source device. LDAC requires Android; aptX Adaptive requires Android with Snapdragon 8 Gen 1+; Apple devices are limited to SBC and AAC regardless of headphone capability.
- ANC performance varies with ear-pad seal and head shape. Over-ear designs degrade with glasses that break the seal. RTINGS' and SoundGuys' measurements assume optimal fit.
- The Bose QuietComfort Ultra exists in both 1st Gen and 2nd Gen versions. The 2nd Gen (late 2025) adds 30h battery, aptX Adaptive, and BT 5.4. The 1st Gen (24h, BT 5.3) is a better deal if found under $300 — confirm version before purchase.
- JBL Tour One M3 battery rating was revised from 35h to 70h with ANC on after 2026 firmware optimization. Older reviews cite the lower number.
