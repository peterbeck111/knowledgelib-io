What are the best Qi2 wireless chargers in 2026?

Summary

The Qi2 wireless charging standard has matured significantly in Q1 2026, with the Qi2.2 update (introduced July 2025) enabling 25W charging speeds, magnetic alignment for precise positioning, and improved thermal controls [src4, src5]. Apple's iPhone 17 series fully embraces Qi2 at 25W, Samsung's new Galaxy S26 Ultra now supports 25W Qi2 (though the base S26 is limited to 15W and still requires a magnetic case), and Google's Pixel 10 Pro XL remains the first Android phone to natively support 25W Qi2 with built-in magnets [src4, src7].

The market has responded with a wave of 25W multi-device stations. ESR's CryoBoost line leads on raw speed with active cooling that reduces device temperatures by up to 6.5 degrees F [src5, src6]. Belkin's new UltraCharge 3-in-1 Foldable with ChillBoost passive cooling has emerged as a strong value pick, dropping from $100 to $70 on frequent sales [src2, src8]. Budget options from ESR and Belkin deliver full 25W Qi2.2 charging in single-pad form for under $30 [src2, src3].

One important caveat: while Qi2.2 promises 25W, real-world sustained speeds often drop to 15W or lower once thermal limits are reached, typically after the first 5-10 minutes [src3, src4]. Active cooling (ESR CryoBoost, Anker AirCool, iOttie CryoFlow) extends the high-speed window but does not eliminate throttling. For most users, the difference between 15W and 25W chargers translates to about 5-10 minutes saved over a full charge cycle [src3].

Best Overall: ESR CryoBoost Foldable 3-in-1 (25W) (~$90) — Check price

It folds down to just 15.8mm thick for travel, charges iPhone, Apple Watch, and AirPods simultaneously, and ships with a 50W USB-C wall charger and cable. Price has dropped from $120 to ~$90 on frequent sales. [src2, src6]

Best Value 25W: Belkin UltraCharge 3-in-1 Foldable (25W) (~$70-100) — Check price

Belkin's new UltraCharge 3-in-1 Foldable is the biggest value story in Q1 2026. Key Market Trends (Q1 2026)

  • 25W price war in full swing: The Belkin UltraCharge 3-in-1 Foldable (25W) hit a $70 all-time low in March 2026, the Anker Prime dropped to $116, and the ESR CryoBoost regularly sells for ~$90. A year ago, 25W 3-in-1 chargers cost $130+. Single-pad 25W chargers like the Belkin UltraCharge are now just ~$30. [src2, src8]
  • Active vs passive cooling debate: Active cooling (ESR CryoBoost at 25dB, Anker AirCool) sustains 25W longer but adds fan noise. Belkin's ChillBoost passive cooling delivers competitive 0-50% times (29 min) with zero noise. The real-world difference narrows as ambient temperatures drop. [src3, src6, src8]
  • Galaxy S26 keeps Qi2 but still no built-in magnets: Samsung's Galaxy S26 Ultra supports 25W Qi2 but the entire S26 line still requires a magnetic case for alignment — Samsung cites that 83% of users already use cases. The base S26 is limited to 15W wireless. This remains a friction point for cross-platform Qi2 adoption. [src7]
  • Qi2.2 device expansion: Beyond iPhone 17 and Pixel 10 Pro XL, the Galaxy S26 Ultra is now the third device supporting 25W wireless. The Wireless Power Consortium's theoretical 50W ceiling suggests further speed increases in future Qi2 revisions. [src4, src5]
  • Price compression at 15W: Basic Qi2 15W charging pads have dropped below $20, with the ESR Mini at ~$17. The value proposition at 15W is now so strong that 25W becomes the new differentiator for premium chargers. [src2, src6]
  • IKEA enters Qi2 with $9.99 donut charger (April 2026): IKEA launched three Qi2-certified Västmärke chargers on April 10, 2026: a donut-shaped pad ($9.99) with a 47-inch concealed cable, a charging stand ($24.99), and a bowl with integrated lighting ($24.99). All 15W only (no 25W Qi2.2), IKEA-exclusive, compatible with Pixel 10, iPhone 12+ and other Qi2 devices. Cheapest Qi2 charger from a mainstream brand. [src9]

Important Caveats

  • Prices are approximate US retail as of March 2026 and fluctuate frequently on Amazon. The Belkin UltraCharge 3-in-1 was $70 on sale in March but may revert to $100. Check current prices before purchasing.
  • 25W charging requires both a Qi2.2-certified charger AND a compatible device (iPhone 17 series, Galaxy S26 Ultra, Pixel 10 Pro XL). Older devices max out at 15W or 7.5W regardless of charger capability. The base Galaxy S26 only supports 15W wireless.
  • Real-world sustained charging speeds are lower than peak 25W due to thermal throttling. Active cooling extends the fast-charging window but does not eliminate it entirely. Passively cooled chargers may deliver only 5-10 minutes of sustained 25W before dropping to 15W.
  • Samsung Galaxy S25/S26 series require a compatible magnetic case for Qi2 magnetic alignment; without one, charging works but alignment is manual and speeds may be inconsistent.
  • Apple Watch charging requires an MFW-certified module; not all third-party chargers include this. Verify before purchase if Watch charging is important.
