# Best Qi2 Wireless Chargers (2026)

## Summary

The Qi2 wireless charging standard has matured significantly in Q1 2026, with the Qi2.2 update (introduced July 2025) enabling 25W charging speeds, magnetic alignment for precise positioning, and improved thermal controls [src4, src5]. Apple's iPhone 17 series fully embraces Qi2 at 25W, Samsung's new Galaxy S26 Ultra now supports 25W Qi2 (though the base S26 is limited to 15W and still requires a magnetic case), and Google's Pixel 10 Pro XL remains the first Android phone to natively support 25W Qi2 with built-in magnets [src4, src7]. An iPhone 17 can go from 0 to 50% in about 30 minutes wirelessly — fast enough to rival wired charging in many scenarios [src5, src6].

The market has responded with a wave of 25W multi-device stations. ESR's CryoBoost line leads on raw speed with active cooling that reduces device temperatures by up to 6.5 degrees F [src5, src6]. Anker's Prime station delivers 25W with AirCool fan technology and a smart display, now regularly discounted to ~$116 [src1]. Belkin's new UltraCharge 3-in-1 Foldable with ChillBoost passive cooling has emerged as a strong value pick, dropping from $100 to $70 on frequent sales [src2, src8]. Budget options from ESR and Belkin deliver full 25W Qi2.2 charging in single-pad form for under $30 [src2, src3].

One important caveat: while Qi2.2 promises 25W, real-world sustained speeds often drop to 15W or lower once thermal limits are reached, typically after the first 5-10 minutes [src3, src4]. Active cooling (ESR CryoBoost, Anker AirCool, iOttie CryoFlow) extends the high-speed window but does not eliminate throttling. For most users, the difference between 15W and 25W chargers translates to about 5-10 minutes saved over a full charge cycle [src3].

## Key Market Trends (Q1 2026)

- **25W price war in full swing**: The Belkin UltraCharge 3-in-1 Foldable (25W) hit a $70 all-time low in March 2026, the Anker Prime dropped to $116, and the ESR CryoBoost regularly sells for ~$90. A year ago, 25W 3-in-1 chargers cost $130+. Single-pad 25W chargers like the Belkin UltraCharge are now just ~$30 [src2, src8].
- **Active vs passive cooling debate**: Active cooling (ESR CryoBoost at 25dB, Anker AirCool) sustains 25W longer but adds fan noise.
