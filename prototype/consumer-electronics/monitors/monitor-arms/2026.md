# Best Monitor Arms and Mounts (2026)

## Summary

The monitor arm market in 2026 is dominated by Ergotron, which holds three of the top five positions across major review sites. The Ergotron LX Pro (~$209) is the consensus best overall pick, offering tool-free installation, smooth Constant Force Technology, and a 10-year warranty. For heavy ultrawide monitors up to 49 inches (42 lbs), the Ergotron HX (~$280) remains unmatched. Budget shoppers can get 80% of premium performance from the VIVO Premium Aluminum STAND-V001Q (~$40) or the MSI MAG MT201 (~$70) which supports an impressive 44 lbs. [src1, src2, src4]

The Herman Miller Jarvis (~$195) matches the Ergotron LX Pro in smoothness and range of motion while offering an industry-leading 15-year warranty, though the parent company Fully has faced uncertainty. The Ergotron Trace (~$350) sets the premium benchmark with its refined design and straight-up-and-down pole movement, while the Humanscale M10 (~$590) targets high-end office environments with patented weight-compensating spring technology. All models listed support standard VESA 75x75mm and 100x100mm mounting patterns. [src1, src2, src7]

## Best for Each Use Case

### Best Overall: Ergotron LX Pro (~$209) -- [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/ergotron-lx-pro)
The Ergotron LX Pro earns its top ranking with an exceptionally easy tool-free installation using a clever handhold clamp mechanism. It offers 360 degrees of swivel, -15 to 75 degrees of tilt, and 13 inches of height adjustment via patented Constant Force Technology. The lift mechanism has passed Ergotron's 10,000-cycle motion test, ensuring years of smooth operation. Supports monitors up to 34 inches and 22 lbs with VESA 75x75 or 100x100mm. [src1, src2, src5]

### Best for Heavy/Ultrawide Monitors: Ergotron HX (~$280) -- [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/ergotron-hx)
The Ergotron HX is the only arm rated to hold monitors up to 42 lbs and screen sizes up to 49 inches. For massive ultrawide monitors like the Samsung Odyssey G9 or LG 49WQ95C, the HX is unmatched. Its overbuilt construction uses Velcro straps for cable management instead of cheap zip ties. Available with standard or HD pivot for deeply curved 1000R panels. [src2, src4, src6]

### Best Warranty: Herman Miller Jarvis (~$195) -- [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/herman-miller-jarvis)
Wirecutter's top pick after assessing 25 monitor arms. Moves smoothly into a wide range of positions, holds monitors up to 32 inches securely, and is easy to set up. The standout feature is a monumental 15-year warranty that exceeds Ergotron's 10-year coverage. Offers 13 inches of vertical range, 24 inches of side-to-side extension, 90-degree tilt, and 360-degree pan. [src1, src4]

### Best Premium: Ergotron Trace (~$350) -- [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/ergotron-trace)
The most refined monitor arm available under $400. Achieves straight up-and-down movement with smooth spring action inside the center pole. The single arm pans 240 degrees, rotates 360 degrees, and tilts 80 degrees. PCWorld calls it the benchmark for what a high-end ergonomic monitor mount should feel like. Supports monitors up to 38 inches and 21.5 lbs. [src2, src7]

### Best Budget Heavy-Duty: MSI MAG MT201 (~$70) -- [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/msi-mag-mt201)
A remarkable value with 44 lbs of weight capacity at just $70, far exceeding many arms costing three times more. Supports screens from 17 to 49 inches with VESA 75x75 and 100x100mm patterns. Spring-assisted adjustment with +50/-20 degree tilt, 180-degree swivel, and 180-degree rotation. Includes both clamp and grommet mounting hardware. [src2, src5, src6]

### Best Value Under $50: VIVO Premium Aluminum STAND-V001Q (~$40) -- [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/vivo-premium-aluminum)
Delivers 80% of the Ergotron experience at roughly 20% of the price. Pneumatic gas spring arm allows tool-free height adjustment for monitors up to 32 inches and 20 lbs. Aluminum construction with integrated cable management. Includes both clamp and grommet mounting hardware. Over 17,000 positive reviews with a 4.5/5 average on Amazon. [src4, src6]

### Best Ultra-Budget: NB North Bayou F80 (~$30) -- [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/north-bayou-f80)
The cheapest legitimate gas-spring monitor arm available. Supports 17-30 inch monitors up to 19.8 lbs with aluminum alloy construction. Offers 85 degrees of backward tilt, 30 degrees forward, 360-degree rotation, 10 inches of lift, and 90-degree swivel. Over 17,000 positive reviews. Cable management may feel flimsy on the upper arm segment. [src4, src6]

## Decision Logic

### If monitor weighs over 25 lbs or is 35+ inches ultrawide
--> Ergotron HX (~$280) is the only mainstream arm rated for 20-42 lbs. The MSI MAG MT201 (~$70) handles up to 44 lbs on paper but lacks the build quality for premium heavy panels. For deeply curved 1000R ultrawides, get the HX with HD pivot (B0959D7HHH). [src2, src4]

### If budget < $50
--> VIVO Premium Aluminum STAND-V001Q (~$40) for monitors up to 20 lbs/32 inches. NB North Bayou F80 (~$30) if monitor is under 19.8 lbs and 30 inches. Both have gas springs and VESA 75/100 support. Skip the Amazon Basics arm; the VIVO offers better build quality at the same price. [src4, src6]

### If primary concern is long-term durability and warranty
--> Herman Miller Jarvis (~$195) with its 15-year warranty, or any Ergotron model with 10-year warranty. Avoid budget arms from unknown brands with 1-2 year warranties if the arm will hold an expensive monitor. [src1, src4]

### If mounting dual monitors side-by-side
--> Ergotron LX Dual (~$300) for two monitors up to 27 inches / 20 lbs each. For larger dual monitors, use two individual Ergotron LX or LX Pro arms on the same desk edge for independent positioning. [src2, src3]

### If you want the smoothest, most premium experience
--> Ergotron Trace (~$350) for refined vertical movement and modern aesthetics. Humanscale M10 (~$590) if budget is no concern and you need maximum weight capacity with patented weight-compensating springs. [src2, src7]

### Default recommendation
--> Ergotron LX Pro (~$209) is the safest pick for unknown requirements. Tool-free setup, 22 lb capacity covers most standard and ultrawide monitors up to 34 inches, 10-year warranty, and smooth Constant Force Technology. [src1, src2, src5]

## Key Market Trends (2026)

- **Ergotron LX Pro replaces the classic LX as top pick**: The upgraded LX Pro with tool-free installation and refined design has become the new consensus best overall, while the original LX remains excellent at a slightly lower price. [src1, src2]
- **Heavy-duty budget arms from gaming brands**: MSI's entry into the monitor arm market with the MAG MT201 (44 lbs at $70) is disrupting the category. Gaming peripheral brands are leveraging their ultrawide monitor expertise to build arms that match. [src2, src5]
- **15-year warranties becoming competitive**: Herman Miller's 15-year Jarvis warranty has pushed Ergotron and others to compete on longevity, signaling industry confidence in modern gas-spring and constant-force mechanisms. [src1, src4]
- **Ultrawide adoption driving heavy-duty demand**: As 34-inch and 49-inch ultrawide monitors become mainstream for productivity and gaming, demand for arms rated above 25 lbs has surged. The Ergotron HX remains the gold standard here. [src2, src6]

## Important Caveats

- Prices are approximate US street prices as of April 2026. Sales, regional pricing, and color variants can shift prices significantly.
- All listed arms require VESA-compatible monitors (75x75mm or 100x100mm mounting holes). Some monitors, particularly Apple displays and certain Samsung models, do not include VESA mounts without an adapter.
- Gas spring arms have minimum weight thresholds. Mounting a monitor below the minimum weight causes the arm to drift upward. Check both the minimum and maximum weight ratings.
- Desk thickness and edge profile matter. Clamp mounts need a flat edge and sufficient thickness (typically 0.4-3.9 inches). Rounded desk edges or desks thinner than 0.8 inches may require grommet mounting.
- Cable management quality varies widely. Ergotron uses external routing clips on most models; the Trace and Humanscale M10 offer superior integrated cable channels.
