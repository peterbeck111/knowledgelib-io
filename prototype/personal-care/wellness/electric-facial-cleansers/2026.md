## Key Market Trends (2026)

- **Silicone has replaced bristles**: The majority of new devices launching in 2025-2026 use silicone touchpoints rather than nylon bristles. Silicone is antibacterial, requires no replacement heads (eliminating recurring costs), and is gentler across all skin types. The shift accelerated after Clarisonic's discontinuation in 2020. [src1, src3, src7]
- **Multi-function devices emerging**: The FOREO Luna 4 Plus combines cleansing, LED light therapy, and microcurrent in one device at ~$349. PMD's ActiveWarmth integrates heated massage into cleansing. The trend is toward all-in-one skincare tools that replace multiple devices. [src7, src8]
- **Heat therapy integration**: Devices like the PMD Clean Pro and NagraCoola CLIE now include heated massage modes that open pores and boost serum absorption. This bridges facial cleansing with anti-aging treatment in a single device. [src2, src3]
- **App connectivity declining**: While FOREO Luna 3 required app registration even to unlock charging, the Luna 4 series has reduced app dependency (except the Plus model). The trend is toward standalone devices with hardware controls, as consumers resisted mandatory app pairing. [src1, src7]
- **Price compression at the low end**: Effective sonic silicone devices now start under $35 (NagraCoola, Spa Sciences), and the PMD Smart at ~$60 delivers near-premium performance. Premium devices justify their price through durability, battery longevity, and material quality. [src1, src2, src4]
