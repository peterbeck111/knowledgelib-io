# Best Electric Shavers (2026)

## Summary

The electric shaver market in 2026 is dominated by three brands: Braun, Panasonic, and Philips Norelco. Braun and Panasonic produce foil shavers (oscillating blades behind a thin metal screen, moved in straight strokes), while Philips uses a rotary design with three circular spinning heads that pivot independently. The biggest news this year is Braun's 96xx generation Series 9 Pro+ (models 9660CCPS and 9697CC), which introduces the new 96M cassette with titanium-nitride-coated blades and a Pro SensoAdapt sensor that reads beard density 300 times per second — a massive upgrade from the previous 13x/sec AutoSense. Across major review sites, the Braun Series 9 Pro+ remains the consensus best overall shaver for most men, combining premium closeness with exceptional skin comfort. [src1, src2, src7]

For the absolute closest shave, the Panasonic Arc6 ES-LS9A-K delivers 84,000 cross-cutting actions per minute via six blades and a 22-direction flexible head — the closest an electric razor has come to matching a manual blade. MenReviewHub rates it 10/10 for closeness, though comfort drops to 7/10 due to its aggressive cutting and louder 74 dB operation. For rotary users, the Philips Norelco i9000 Prestige Ultra remains the flagship (TIME Best Invention 2025), while the new Philips Norelco 9500 S9985/84 (~$240) has emerged as a strong mid-premium rotary option, featuring 72 self-sharpening Dual Steel Precision Blades, 150,000 cutting actions per minute, and a Smart Hair Sensor that reads density 500 times per second. Reviewed.com named it their best overall electric razor after testing by a professional barber on three different face types. [src3, src5, src6]

Prices range from under $30 for entry-level models to $400+ for flagships with smart cleaning stations and PowerCases. The gap between mid-range and premium has continued to narrow: the Braun Series 8 (~$180) inherits much of the Series 9 Pro+'s technology at a lower price, and the Panasonic Arc5 ES-LV67-K (~$130) remains the best value pick for raw closeness with its 14,000 CPM motor delivering 70,000 cross-cuts per minute. Replacement heads should be changed every 12-18 months, with costs between $25-60 depending on the model. [src1, src2, src4]

## Best for Each Use Case

### Best Overall: Braun Series 9 Pro+ 9660CCPS (~$350) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/braun-series-9-pro-plus-9660)
The 2026 revision of Braun's flagship introduces the 96M cassette — the most significant shaving head upgrade in a decade — with two new titanium-nitride-coated blades inside the outermost foil elements. The Pro SensoAdapt sensor now reads beard density 300 times per second (up from 13x/sec), delivering real-time motor adjustment per stroke. ShaverCheck calls comfort "stellar" and rates it as "one of the best out there for users with sensitive skin." MenReviewHub scores it 9/10 closeness and 10/10 comfort. The 6-in-1 SmartCare Center cleans, charges, lubricates, dries, and stores the shaver. The 9697CC variant (~$380) adds a ProComfort massage head. For those wanting the previous generation at a lower price, the 9567cc (~$270) remains excellent. [src2, src6, src7]

### Best for Closest Shave: Panasonic Arc6 ES-LS9A-K (~$300) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/panasonic-arc6)
The six-blade head — two finish blades, two lift blades, and two titanium-coated thick stubble blades — delivers the closest electric shave available, approaching manual razor territory. The ultra-fast linear motor drives 84,000 cross-cutting actions per minute, and the 22-direction flexible pivoting head follows every facial contour. MenReviewHub rates it a perfect 10/10 for closeness, noting it "literally obliterates hair." Drawbacks include louder operation at 74 dB, slightly shorter battery life at 50 minutes, and more aggressive cutting that may irritate sensitive skin (comfort rated 7/10). The shaver-only ES-LS8A-K variant runs ~$250, saving $50 by dropping the cleaning station. [src2, src3, src6]

## Key Market Trends (2026)

- **AI-adaptive sensors hit 300x/sec**: Braun's new Pro SensoAdapt in the 96xx generation reads beard density 300 times per second, up from 13x/sec in the previous generation. Philips' SenseIQ reads 500 times per second on the 9500 and S9000 Prestige. Real-time motor adjustment per stroke is now the biggest differentiator between mid-range and premium tiers. [src2, src7]
- **96M cassette backward compatibility**: Braun's most significant shaving head upgrade in a decade is backward compatible with older Series 9 Pro+ bodies, allowing existing owners to upgrade for ~$50-60 instead of buying a new $350 shaver. This is unprecedented for premium electric shavers. [src7]
- **Six-blade plateau**: The Panasonic Arc6 remains the only six-blade system on the market. No competitor has followed, suggesting diminishing returns beyond five blades. The closeness improvement over five-blade systems is measurable but the tradeoffs in comfort and noise (74 dB) limit mass appeal. [src3, src6]
- **USB-C charging now standard**: Most 2025-2026 models have moved to USB-C, replacing proprietary charging cables. The Philips Norelco 9500, OneBlade 360, and Panasonic Arc6 all charge via USB-C. Braun still uses proprietary charging on some models. [src2, src5]
- **App-connected shaving**: The Philips Norelco 9500 integrates Bluetooth with the GroomTribe app for real-time motion and pressure feedback. While niche, it signals the industry treating shavers as connected health devices rather than simple grooming tools. [src5]

## Important Caveats

- Prices are approximate US street prices as of April 2026. Prices vary by retailer, bundle, and color variant. Holiday sales can drop flagship models 30-40%.
- Foil vs. rotary is significantly a personal preference. Foil shavers generally shave closer and work best in straight strokes; rotary shavers are more comfortable for many users and better at contouring around jawlines, Adam's apples, and neck curves.
- Battery life figures are manufacturer claims. Real-world usage varies — Wirecutter found the Braun Series 8 delivered 132 minutes in testing despite a 60-minute official rating, while the Panasonic Arc6 delivered closer to 45-50 minutes versus its 50-minute claim. [src1, src2]
- Electric shavers require a 2-4 week adjustment period. Skin and beard hair need time to adapt from manual razors. Initial shaves may feel less close.
- Replacement heads cost $25-60 depending on model and should be changed every 12-18 months for optimal performance.
- The Braun Series 9 Pro+ 96xx generation (9660CCPS, 9697CC, 9677CC, 9667CC) became available in the US in early 2026. All four variants share the same 96M cassette and Pro SensoAdapt sensor — differences are bundled accessories and color. Pricing may still be settling in Q2 2026.
