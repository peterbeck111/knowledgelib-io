# Best Carry-On Luggage (2026)

## Summary

The carry-on luggage market in 2026 is defined by a clear divide between softside bags favored by frequent business travelers and hardshell options preferred for durability and protection — plus an emerging third category: smart-trackable bags. After synthesizing hands-on testing from Wirecutter (200+ hours of research), GearJunkie (20 bags tested across 10+ flights), Outdoor Gear Lab (retested Nov 2025), and CNN Underscored (750,000+ miles of testing), the Travelpro Platinum Elite 21" Expandable Spinner remains the best overall softside pick for most travelers, combining premium organization, self-aligning MagnaTrac wheels, and a lifetime warranty at ~$391 [src1, src2, src3]. For hardshell enthusiasts, the Away Bigger Carry-On (~$295) delivers exceptional capacity at 47.9 liters with a polycarbonate shell and built-in compression system [src4, src5].

The defining product launch of early 2026 is the **July Carry On With CaseSafe** — the world's first suitcase with an integrated, trackable TSA lock that works with both Apple Find My and Google Find Hub natively (no AirTag required). Named Best Overall Carry-On of 2026 by Men's Journal, it combines July's aerospace-grade German polycarbonate shell, silent spinner wheels, and an ejectable USB-C battery with built-in tracking that survives battery separation [src9, src10]. The CaseSafe lock is being positioned as a new industry standard, with Outdoor Gear Lab's November 2025 retest confirming July's thesis that connected luggage is the next durable platform shift [src2].

Budget-conscious travelers have strong options in 2026, with the Rockland Melbourne 20" available for as little as ~$55 on sale and the Travelpro Maxlite Air V2 at ~$157, both delivering core features like spinner wheels and TSA locks without the premium price tag [src3, src7]. At the luxury end, the TUMI Alpha International Dual Access Carry-On (~$950) and Briggs & Riley Baseline Essential (~$649-$729) justify their prices with FXT ballistic nylon construction, lifetime warranties, and business-focused organization features [src1, src2, src5, src6]. The ultralight trend also continues to mature — the July Carry-On Light Expandable at just 4.9 lbs remains the lightest hardside spinner tested, solving the perennial weight-vs-capacity trade-off [src7, src8]. Front-access laptop compartments (Monos Carry-On Pro, Delsey Chatelet Air 2.0), expansion zippers adding 2-8 liters of bonus capacity, and USB-C charging have become table stakes across nearly every model [src1, src2, src3, src5].

--> Travelpro Maxlite Air V2 (~$157) offers the best value-to-feature ratio in this tier — 46L capacity, 6.7 lbs, polycarbonate construction, and TSA lock. No other bag in this price range matches its combination of capacity, weight, and build quality. [src2, src5]

### If the user has ever had a bag gate-checked, lost, or delayed
--> July Carry On With CaseSafe (~$495). The integrated Apple Find My + Google Find Hub tracker (powered independently of the removable USB-C battery) keeps the bag visible even when it's taken from your control at the gate. Men's Journal named it Best Overall Carry-On of 2026 and no competitor currently offers native cross-platform tracking without relying on a loose AirTag. [src2, src9, src10]

### If primary use is business travel with suits/garments
--> Prioritize softside with garment support over hardshell. The Briggs & Riley Baseline Essential (~$729) has a flat interior designed for garment bags and an unconditional lifetime warranty. The TUMI Alpha (~$950) adds dual-access entry and USB 4.0 charging for road warriors who expense luggage. [src1, src2, src5, src6]

### If flying budget carriers (Spirit, Frontier, Ryanair)
--> Choose the smallest-dimension bag available. The Rockland Melbourne (20" x 13" x 9") and Delsey Chatelet Air 2.0 (21" x 14.2" x 9.6") are the most compact options. The July Carry-On Light (21.25" x 14.17" x 8.66" unexpanded) also fits strict limits while weighing just 4.9 lbs. Avoid the Away Bigger Carry-On and Travelpro Platinum Elite, which exceed strict budget carrier limits. Always check your airline's current policy. [src4, src5, src7]

### If durability and warranty matter most
--> Briggs & Riley offers the only unconditional lifetime warranty in the industry — any damage, any cause, repaired free forever. Travelpro and July offer limited lifetime warranties. Away and Monos offer limited lifetime warranties but with more exclusions. Budget bags (Rockland) typically have 3-5 year limited warranties. [src1, src6, src8]

### If weight is the top priority (international weight limits, frequent lifting)
--> July Carry-On Light Expandable (~$275) at 4.9 lbs is the lightest option by a wide margin — nearly 3 lbs lighter than the Travelpro Platinum Elite. The Samsonite Freeform (~$200) at 6.6 lbs and Travelpro Maxlite Air V2 (~$157) at 6.7 lbs are the next lightest. For strict 7 kg international weight limits, every pound of empty weight directly reduces packing capacity. [src2, src5, src7]

### Default recommendation
--> For 2026, split the default: the July Carry On With CaseSafe (~$495) if budget allows and the user cares about tracking, otherwise the Travelpro Platinum Elite 21" (~$391) remains the safest, no-drama pick — Wirecutter's top softside for 5+ consecutive years and endorsed again by Outdoor Gear Lab in November 2025. [src1, src2, src3, src9]

## Key Market Trends (2026)

- **Trackable luggage becomes the new default conversation**: July's CaseSafe (launched March 2026) is the first carry-on with a built-in TSA lock that speaks natively to both Apple Find My and Google Find Hub, with the tracker powered independently of the ejectable USB-C battery. The Points Guy and Men's Journal treat it as a platform shift rather than a feature — expect Away, Monos, and Samsonite to respond within 12-18 months. [src2, src9, src10]
- **Ultralight carry-ons gaining momentum**: The July Carry-On Light Expandable at 4.9 lbs represents a wave of sub-5-lb hard-shell spinners that eliminate the weight penalty traditionally associated with polycarbonate luggage. This matters increasingly as international airlines enforce 7-10 kg carry-on weight limits. [src7, src8]
- **Front-access laptop pockets becoming standard**: Multiple brands including Monos, Delsey, and Calpak now offer built-in front-access compartments for laptops and tablets, reducing the need to open the full suitcase at security checkpoints. [src3, src5]
- **Expansion capacity as a core feature**: Nearly every carry-on tested in 2026 includes an expansion zipper adding 2-8 liters of bonus capacity, reflecting rising airline fees for checked bags that push travelers to maximize their carry-on. [src1, src2]
- **Sustainability materials on the rise**: Brands like Calpak, Samsonite, and July are increasingly using recycled polycarbonate, recycled polyester from plastic bottles, and vegan leather trims, though premium sustainability options still command a price premium. [src5, src7]
- **Ejectable USB-C battery as a safety requirement**: FAA and most international carriers now require lithium-ion batteries to be removable before gate-check. The new generation (July CaseSafe, TUMI Alpha, Travelpro Platinum Elite) uses tool-free eject systems; older integrated-battery designs are being quietly phased out. [src1, src3, src9]
- **Self-aligning and noise-reducing wheels**: Travelpro's MagnaTrac magnet-aligned wheels, July's SilentMove Series 2, and Monos's "whisper-quiet" wheel technology represent a broader push toward smoother, quieter rolling performance. [src2, src6, src7]

- Carry-on size limits vary by airline. Most bags listed here comply with major US domestic airlines (22" x 14" x 9"), but some may exceed limits on budget carriers (Spirit, Frontier) or strict international airlines. Always verify your airline's current policy.
- Weight specs are manufacturer-stated and may not account for all accessories. Actual packed weight will be higher.
- Hardshell vs. softside is a personal preference trade-off: hardshells protect contents better but cannot flex into tight overhead bins; softside bags offer more organizational pockets and flexibility.
- This comparison focuses on spinner-wheel roller carry-ons. Travel backpacks and duffel bags with wheels serve different use cases and are covered separately.
