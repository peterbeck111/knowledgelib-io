What are the best VPNs for streaming in 2026?

Confidence: 0.90 Sources: 8 Verified: 2026-04-15 Freshness: quarterly

Summary

The streaming-VPN landscape in 2026 is a cat-and-mouse game. Netflix, BBC iPlayer, and Disney+ invest heavily in proxy detection, rotating IP blocklists weekly — a provider that unblocks five regions today may be burned next week. Five providers have demonstrated consistent success across multi-month testing cycles: NordVPN, Surfshark, ExpressVPN, Proton VPN, and Private Internet Access. [src1, src2, src4] Atlas VPN shut down in April 2024 and migrated its users to NordVPN, reducing meaningful streaming-VPN competition. [src8]

Peak WireGuard speeds now routinely exceed 1,200 Mbps on flagship providers, making 4K streaming trivial on any modern connection. Surfshark led speed tests at 1,615 Mbps, with ExpressVPN (1,479 Mbps, Lightway) and Proton VPN (1,521 Mbps) close behind. [src1, src2] For pure Netflix catalog coverage, Windscribe unblocks 32 libraries — more than any other tested provider — while NordVPN reliably unblocks 15+ regions. [src4] For devices without native VPN support (Samsung, LG smart TVs, older Apple TVs), Smart DNS services like ExpressVPN's MediaStreamer bypass geo-restrictions without encryption and do not count against the device limit. [src7]

The recommended default is NordVPN (~$3.39/mo on 2-year) for consistent unblocking plus 10 devices and a native Apple TV app; Surfshark (~$1.99/mo) is the best value with unlimited simultaneous connections. Avoid Mullvad and CyberGhost for Netflix/iPlayer in 2026 — both are frequently blocked. [src4, src6]

Best for Each Use Case

Best Overall: NordVPN (~$3.39/mo on 2-year)

Consensus #1 across Tom's Guide, PCWorld, and CyberInsider. Unblocks 15+ Netflix regions (US, UK, Canada, Australia, France, Germany, Japan, India, Korea, Brazil, and more), plus BBC iPlayer, Hulu, Disney+, and Prime Video. 1,256 Mbps average on WireGuard (NordLynx), 10 simultaneous connections, native Apple TV and Fire TV apps, included Smart DNS (SmartPlay). Independently audited no-logs policy. [src1, src2, src4]

Best Netflix Catalog Coverage: Windscribe (~$5.75/mo) or Surfshark (~$1.99/mo)

Windscribe unblocks 32 Netflix libraries — more than any other tested provider — but has slower peak speeds (~500 Mbps) and no Apple TV app. [src4] Surfshark is a better all-rounder at lower cost, unblocking 16+ Netflix regions including Turkey, Singapore, and Taiwan. [src2]

Best for BBC iPlayer Abroad: NordVPN or ExpressVPN

Both consistently unblock iPlayer via UK servers. ExpressVPN's Lightway protocol delivers 1,374–1,479 Mbps on UK–EU routes, eliminating buffering on 1080p streams. [src1] Mullvad does not work with iPlayer as of March 2026 tests and should be avoided if iPlayer is the priority. [src6]

Best for 4K Streaming Speed: Surfshark (~$1.99/mo)

1,615 Mbps peak in WireGuard tests — the fastest in the category — plus unlimited simultaneous connections let you stream on every device in the household at once. QR-code login simplifies setup on streaming sticks. [src1, src2]

Best for Fire TV / Apple TV Native App: NordVPN or ExpressVPN

Both offer native tvOS and Fire OS apps (ExpressVPN first to ship a tvOS 17+ app). For older Apple TVs or Samsung/LG smart TVs that cannot run a VPN app, ExpressVPN's MediaStreamer Smart DNS works without eating into the device limit. [src1, src7]

Best Budget: Surfshark (~$1.99/mo on 2-year)

Lowest effective price among providers with reliable Netflix unblocking. Unlimited device connections replace the per-seat math entirely. Private Internet Access is cheaper at renewal (~$2.03/mo) but unblocks fewer Netflix regions and struggles with Hulu. [src2, src4]

Best for Privacy-Maximum Streamers: Proton VPN (~$2.99/mo Plus tier)

Open-source apps, Swiss jurisdiction, no-logs policy audited twice. Streaming unblocking is only available on the Plus tier (not Free). Encountered minor issues with 9Now in testing but solid across Netflix, BBC iPlayer, Hulu, and Disney+. [src1]

Best for Hotstar / India: CyberGhost or ExpressVPN

CyberGhost operates 37 virtual Indian servers (Mumbai) explicitly optimized for Disney+ Hotstar — the only major provider that publicly lists Hotstar-specific servers. ExpressVPN also reliably unblocks Hotstar from the US, UK, and Australia. [src5]

Best for China / Restricted Regions: ExpressVPN or NordVPN

Both offer obfuscated ("stealth") protocols — ExpressVPN's Lightway and NordVPN's NordWhisper/obfuscated servers — that disguise VPN traffic as regular HTTPS. Mullvad, Proton, and most other providers are blocked at the Great Firewall. Verify within the 30-day refund window. [src1, src4]

Key Market Trends (2026)

  • Proprietary streaming protocols dominate: WireGuard forks (NordLynx) and custom protocols (ExpressVPN's Lightway, Proton's Stealth) deliver 1,200+ Mbps while handling Netflix's deep-packet inspection better than vanilla WireGuard/OpenVPN. [src1]
  • Streaming-optimized server clusters: CyberGhost, Surfshark, and NordVPN now group servers by streaming service (Netflix US, iPlayer UK, Hotstar IN) rather than just by geography. This shifts the load when a subset of IPs is banned. [src2, src5]
  • Smart DNS resurgence: MediaStreamer (ExpressVPN), SmartPlay (NordVPN), and Surfshark's Smart DNS enable streaming on devices that cannot run VPN apps. Faster than VPN because there is no encryption, but no privacy benefit. [src7]
  • Market consolidation: Atlas VPN shut down April 2024; Nord Security now owns NordVPN, Surfshark, and Atlas. Kape owns ExpressVPN, CyberGhost, PIA, and ZenMate. Two companies control most of the top-tier streaming-VPN market. [src8]
  • Obfuscation is table stakes for restricted regions: China, UAE, Iran, and Russia have escalated VPN blocking. Only providers with active obfuscation (ExpressVPN Lightway, NordVPN NordWhisper, Proton Stealth, Astrill) reliably connect. [src1]
  • Netflix blocks rotate weekly: The 2024–2026 trend is automated residential-IP detection that burns proxy IPs within days of discovery. Even top providers now see 1–2 day "dark windows" after a block wave. [src4]

Important Caveats

  • Streaming service unblocking changes week-to-week; any data in this card may be stale within 30 days. Always verify within a provider's money-back window (typically 30 days).
  • Netflix's proxy detection targets datacenter IP ranges; residential-IP providers are more durable but more expensive and not the focus of this card.
  • Using a VPN to access content outside your region may violate the streaming service's Terms of Use. It is rarely illegal, but accounts can be restricted or banned. This is a legal gray zone that users accept at their own risk.
  • Price figures are 2-year introductory rates. Renewal prices often jump 2–3x.
  • Free VPNs (including free tiers of Proton, Windscribe, and TunnelBear) rarely unblock streaming services reliably; the paid tiers are a different product.
  • In China, UAE, Iran, Russia, and a growing list of jurisdictions, unauthorized VPN use can carry legal penalties. Verify local law before subscribing.
  • Native Apple TV apps require tvOS 17 or newer. Older Apple TV 4K models may need MediaStreamer Smart DNS instead.
  • Peak-speed figures are lab benchmarks on gigabit connections; real-world throughput depends on your ISP, server load, and distance. Any of the top five providers comfortably exceed the ~25 Mbps needed for 4K streaming.
