# Outbound Sales Playbook

## Purpose

This recipe builds and executes a multi-channel outbound sales system — cold email sequences, LinkedIn outreach, and cold calling — that generates qualified meetings from a targeted prospect list. The output is a configured, running outbound engine with templates, sequences, timing cadences, and performance tracking that produces 5-15 qualified meetings per 100 prospects contacted. [src1]

## Prerequisites

- [ ] **ICP definition and prospect list** — 200+ contacts matching ICP with verified email addresses and LinkedIn URLs from [Lead Scraping Pipeline](/business/lead-generation/lead-scraping-enrichment-pipeline/2026)
- [ ] **Value proposition** — 1-2 sentence positioning statement from [Positioning Framework](/business/brand-strategy/positioning-statement-framework/2026)
- [ ] **CRM configured** — pipeline stages ready to receive outbound leads from [CRM Setup Guide](/business/startup-sales/crm-setup-guide/2026)
- [ ] **Email domain** — separate outbound domain (not your main domain) with SPF, DKIM, and DMARC configured
- [ ] **LinkedIn profile** — optimized personal profile (not company page) with professional photo, headline mentioning value prop

## Constraints

- Email authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC) is mandatory since February 2024 — unauthenticated emails land in spam. [src3]
- LinkedIn connection request limit: approximately 100 per week. Exceeding triggers account restrictions lasting 1-4 weeks. [src2]
- Cold email response rate benchmark: 1-8.5% depending on targeting and personalization. Plan 4-7 touches, not single emails. [src1]
- New email domains must be warmed up for 2-4 weeks before sending at volume. Start at 10 emails/day and increase by 5-10 per day. [src7]
- CAN-SPAM (US) and GDPR (EU): all cold emails must include company address, opt-out link, and truthful subject lines. B2B work emails are generally permissible. [src3]
- Cold calling connect rates: 2-5%. Expect 40-60 dials per 1-3 live conversations. Best times: Tuesday-Thursday, 10am-12pm and 2pm-4pm local time. [src6]

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Set Up Email Infrastructure

**Duration**: 1-2 hours (plus 2-4 weeks warmup)
**Tool**: Domain registrar + email provider + warmup service

Configure a separate outbound domain to protect your main domain reputation. [src3] [src7]

```
Email Infrastructure Setup:
────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. Register outbound domain:
   Buy a domain similar to your main one (e.g., if main is acme.com,
   buy acme-mail.com or getacme.com) — $10-15/year at Cloudflare or Namecheap

2. Set up email hosting:
   Google Workspace ($6/user/mo) or Microsoft 365 ($6/user/mo)
   Create outbound mailbox: yourname@outbound-domain.com

3. Configure DNS authentication:
   SPF:   TXT record — v=spf1 include:_spf.google.com ~all
   DKIM:  TXT record — generated in Google Admin console
   DMARC: TXT record — v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:dmarc@yourdomain.com

4. Start warmup:
   Connect mailbox to warmup service (Instantly includes free warmup)
   Day 1-7:   Send 10 emails/day (warmup tool handles this)
   Day 8-14:  Increase to 25 emails/day
   Day 15-21: Increase to 40 emails/day
   Day 22+:   Production volume (50-75 emails/day max per mailbox)

5. Monitor deliverability:
   Check mail-tester.com — score should be 9/10 or higher
   Monitor bounce rate — keep below 3%
```

**Verify**: Send test email to mail-tester.com and score 9/10+. SPF, DKIM, DMARC all pass.
**If failed**: If DKIM fails, regenerate the key in your email admin console. If SPF fails, check that you have only one SPF record per domain.

### Step 2: Write Cold Email Sequences

**Duration**: 2-3 hours
**Tool**: Document editor + email outreach tool

Create a 5-touch email sequence with increasing personalization and urgency. [src1] [src3]

```
5-Touch Cold Email Sequence:
────────────────────────────────────────────────

EMAIL 1 (Day 1) — Value-First Opener
  Subject: [specific observation about their company]
  Body structure:
  - Line 1: Personalized observation (research their company/role)
  - Line 2: Bridge to pain point ("Companies like yours often struggle with...")
  - Line 3: Value statement (what you do in 1 sentence)
  - Line 4: Social proof (1 metric or customer name)
  - Line 5: Soft CTA ("Worth a 15-min chat to explore?")
  Length: 60-90 words max

EMAIL 2 (Day 3) — Follow-Up with Proof
  Subject: Re: [original subject]
  Body: "Wanted to share how [similar company] achieved [metric]
  in [timeframe] using [your approach]. [1-sentence case study].
  Would this be relevant for [their company]?"
  Length: 40-60 words

EMAIL 3 (Day 7) — Different Angle
  Subject: New subject line — different pain point or trigger
  Body: Reference a different problem you solve or a recent
  trigger event at their company (new funding, hiring, product launch)
  Length: 50-70 words

EMAIL 4 (Day 12) — Social Proof Stack
  Subject: Re: [Email 3 subject]
  Body: Share 2-3 bullet points of results from similar customers.
  End with specific availability: "I have 15 min open Tuesday
  at 2pm or Thursday at 10am — either work?"
  Length: 40-60 words

EMAIL 5 (Day 18) — Breakup Email
  Subject: "Should I close your file?"
  Body: "I've reached out a few times about [pain point].
  If the timing isn't right, totally understand. If things change,
  here's my calendar link: [link]. Wishing you the best with [their goal]."
  Length: 30-50 words

Key rules:
  - Max 90 words per email (shorter = higher reply rates)
  - Personalize line 1 of every email (not just {first_name})
  - No attachments in cold emails (triggers spam filters)
  - Always include unsubscribe option
  - A/B test subject lines (run 2 variants, 50/50 split)
```

**Verify**: Each email is under 90 words. Line 1 is personalized beyond first name. Unsubscribe link present. Sequence configured in tool with correct delays.
**If failed**: If open rates are below 30%, subject lines need work. If reply rates are below 1%, personalization and value proposition need improvement.

### Step 3: Build LinkedIn Outreach Sequence

**Duration**: 1-2 hours
**Tool**: LinkedIn (manual or Expandi/Dripify)

Create a LinkedIn sequence that runs in parallel with email. [src2] [src4]

```
LinkedIn Outreach Sequence:
────────────────────────────────────────────────

TOUCH 1 (Day 0) — Profile View
  Action: Visit prospect's LinkedIn profile
  Purpose: They see your name in notifications — creates familiarity

TOUCH 2 (Day 1) — Connection Request
  Message (max 300 characters):
  "Hi [name], I noticed [specific observation — their post,
  company news, shared connection]. Would love to connect and
  exchange insights on [relevant topic]."
  Do NOT pitch in the connection request.

TOUCH 3 (Day 3, if accepted) — Thank You + Value
  Message: "Thanks for connecting, [name]. I saw [their company]
  is [doing X]. We've been helping similar companies [achieve Y].
  Would you be open to a quick 15-min chat next week?"

TOUCH 4 (Day 7, if no reply) — Content Share
  Message: Share a relevant article, report, or insight.
  "Thought you might find this interesting — [brief description].
  Relevant to what [their company] is doing in [area]."

TOUCH 5 (Day 14, if no reply) — Direct Ask
  Message: "Hi [name], wanted to follow up one more time.
  We helped [similar company] [achieve specific result].
  Happy to share how — 15 min this week? If not the right time,
  no worries at all."

Benchmark response rates (2025):
  Connection request acceptance: 25-40%
  Reply to first message: 9-12%
  Personalized requests vs generic: 9.36% vs 5.44% reply rate
  Best days: Tuesday (6.90%), Monday (6.85%)
```

**Verify**: Connection requests are personalized (not generic). Messages are under 300 characters for requests, under 500 for messages. Weekly limit stays under 100 requests.
**If failed**: If acceptance rate is below 20%, profile needs optimization (professional photo, clear headline, recent activity).

### Step 4: Set Up Multi-Channel Cadence

**Duration**: 1 hour
**Tool**: Spreadsheet or outreach orchestration tool

Combine email and LinkedIn into a synchronized sequence. [src5] [src6]

```
14-Day Multi-Channel Cadence:
────────────────────────────────────────────────

Day 1:  LinkedIn profile view
Day 1:  Cold email #1 (value-first opener)
Day 2:  LinkedIn connection request (personalized)
Day 3:  Cold email #2 (follow-up with proof)
Day 5:  LinkedIn message (if connected) — thank you + value
Day 7:  Cold email #3 (different angle)
Day 8:  Cold call attempt #1 (leave voicemail if no answer)
Day 10: LinkedIn message (content share)
Day 12: Cold email #4 (social proof stack)
Day 14: Cold call attempt #2
Day 18: Cold email #5 (breakup email)
Day 21: LinkedIn message (final follow-up)

Volume planning (per rep per week):
  Emails:     75-100 (across active sequences)
  LinkedIn:   50-80 connection requests + messages
  Calls:      40-60 dials (if calling is included)
  Total:      165-240 touchpoints per week

Expected results per 100 prospects:
  Email opens:        40-60
  Email replies:      3-8
  LinkedIn accepts:   25-40
  LinkedIn replies:   5-10
  Call connects:      2-5
  Meetings booked:    5-15
```

**Verify**: Cadence configured with correct timing. No channel has more than 1 touch per day to avoid looking spammy. Total weekly volume is within tool limits.
**If failed**: If meetings per 100 prospects is below 3, review prospect list quality first (are they ICP?), then email copy, then LinkedIn profile.

### Step 5: Launch, Monitor, and Optimize

**Duration**: Ongoing — 2-4 hours/week
**Tool**: Outreach tool + CRM + spreadsheet

```
Weekly Outbound Review Checklist:
────────────────────────────────────────────────

Metrics to track:
  Email open rate:           Target > 40% (if < 30%, fix subject lines)
  Email reply rate:          Target > 3% (if < 1%, fix value prop + personalization)
  Email bounce rate:         Target < 3% (if > 5%, clean list + check domain health)
  LinkedIn accept rate:      Target > 25% (if < 15%, fix connection request message)
  LinkedIn reply rate:       Target > 8% (if < 5%, fix follow-up messages)
  Meeting book rate:         Target > 5% of contacted (if < 3%, review full sequence)
  Meeting show rate:         Target > 80% (if < 70%, send calendar reminders + agenda)

Optimization actions by week:
  Week 1-2: Run sequences, collect baseline data (do not change anything)
  Week 3:   A/B test subject lines (biggest lever for opens)
  Week 4:   A/B test email body copy (biggest lever for replies)
  Week 5+:  Test different value props, CTAs, and prospect segments

Common fixes:
  Low opens     → Test shorter subject lines (3-5 words), remove spam trigger words
  Low replies   → More personalization in line 1, shorter emails, clearer CTA
  Low meetings  → CTA too vague ("let me know" vs "Tuesday at 2pm?")
  High bounces  → Email list quality issue, verify emails before sending
```

**Verify**: Dashboard shows all key metrics updating weekly. At least 2 A/B tests running after week 2.
**If failed**: If metrics are not tracking, check that tracking pixels and click tracking are enabled in your outreach tool.

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Emails going to spam | Missing SPF/DKIM/DMARC or cold domain not warmed | Run mail-tester.com; fix DNS records; restart warmup for 2 weeks |
| LinkedIn account restricted | Exceeded 100 connection requests/week | Stop all automation; wait for restriction to lift (1-4 weeks); reduce volume |
| Bounce rate > 5% | Bad email data or expired addresses | Pause sequence; re-verify emails; remove hard bounces immediately |
| Zero replies after 200 emails | Value proposition not resonating with audience | Test completely different angle; survey 5 prospects for feedback; check ICP |
| Prospects reply angry/negative | Over-contacting or irrelevant messaging | Review touchpoint frequency; add longer delays; improve targeting |
| Tool account suspended | Violated platform terms (too many sends, spam reports) | Contact support; reduce volume; switch to manual outreach temporarily |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Sending 500 cold emails on day one from a new domain
New domains with no sending history get immediately flagged as spam. Your domain reputation will be destroyed before you send your first real campaign, and recovery takes 4-8 weeks. [src7]

### Correct: Warm up new domains for 2-4 weeks before production sends
Start with 10 emails/day from a warmup tool, increase gradually. Only begin real outbound after mail-tester.com shows 9/10+.

### Wrong: Sending the same generic template to 1,000 prospects
Generic cold emails get 0.5-1% reply rates. Prospects receive 50+ cold emails per week and can instantly identify mass blasts. Low engagement signals further damage deliverability. [src3]

### Correct: Personalize the first line of every email
Research each prospect for 60-90 seconds. Reference their company, recent news, a post they wrote, or a shared connection. Personalized first lines increase reply rates by 2-3x over generic templates.

### Wrong: Pitching in the LinkedIn connection request
Connection requests with a sales pitch have 40% lower acceptance rates than requests that lead with genuine interest or shared context. [src2]

### Correct: Connect first, pitch second
Use the connection request to establish relevance. Save the pitch for the first follow-up message after they accept.
