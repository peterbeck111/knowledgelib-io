# Sales Strategy for Early-Stage Startups

## Purpose

This recipe builds a startup's sales function from zero: founder-led sales process design, CRM setup, outreach sequences, first 10-20 customer acquisition, and transition triggers for hiring the first sales rep. The output is a documented sales playbook, a configured CRM with deal stages, outreach templates, and clear criteria for when to hire — not a theoretical sales strategy document. [src1]

## Prerequisites

- [ ] **ICP definition** — company size, industry, buyer role, and pain points documented
- [ ] **Working product or demo** — prospects need to see the product in action
- [ ] **Pricing model defined** — at least a preliminary pricing structure (can iterate)
- [ ] **Landing page live** — with clear value proposition and signup/demo request option
- [ ] **Founder availability** — 10-20 hours/week dedicated to sales in the first 3-6 months

## Constraints

- Founders must personally close the first 10-20 customers. Delegating before understanding the buyer kills product-market fit learning. [src1]
- Do not hire a sales rep until you have 50+ demos completed and a repeatable win rate above 20%. Below that, you do not have a process to hand off. [src5]
- 80% of B2B sales require 5+ follow-up touches. Most founders give up after 2-3 contacts. Persistence is the single biggest differentiator. [src4]
- First sales hire should be a full-cycle Account Executive (AE), not a VP of Sales. You need someone who closes deals, not someone who builds org charts. [src7]
- Average B2B SaaS sales cycle is 30-90 days for SMB ($1K-10K ACV) and 3-9 months for mid-market ($10K-50K ACV). Plan pipeline accordingly.

## Tool Selection Decision

```
Which path?
├── Pre-revenue, testing demand
│   └── PATH A: Manual sales — personal network + warm outreach, free CRM
├── First 1-10 customers, founder-led
│   └── PATH B: Structured founder sales — CRM + cold outreach tools
├── 10-20 customers, preparing for first hire
│   └── PATH C: Documented process — full CRM + outreach automation + playbook
└── 20+ customers, scaling
    └── PATH D: Sales team foundation — paid CRM + SDR/AE tools + hiring
```

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Set Up CRM and Define Pipeline Stages

**Duration**: 1-2 hours
**Tool**: HubSpot CRM (free)

Configure your CRM before sending a single outreach email. Every conversation, email, and deal must be tracked from day one.

```
Recommended Pipeline Stages (Early B2B SaaS):
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
Stage 1: Lead (prospect identified, not yet contacted)
Stage 2: Contacted (outreach sent, awaiting response)
Stage 3: Discovery Call Scheduled (meeting booked)
Stage 4: Discovery Completed (needs qualified)
Stage 5: Demo/Proposal Sent (product shown, pricing shared)
Stage 6: Negotiation (terms being discussed)
Stage 7: Closed Won / Closed Lost

HubSpot Setup:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Sign up at hubspot.com/products/crm (free)
2. Go to Settings → Objects → Deals → Pipelines
3. Create pipeline with stages above
4. Set deal properties: company, contact, ACV, close date, source
5. Install HubSpot email tracking extension (Gmail/Outlook)
6. Create views: "My Active Deals," "Stalled (no activity 7+ days)"
```

**Verify**: You can create a test deal, move it through all stages, and the pipeline dashboard shows accurate reporting.
**If failed**: If HubSpot's pipeline editor is confusing, start with just 4 stages (Lead → Meeting → Proposal → Won/Lost) and add granularity later as you understand your actual sales motion.

### Step 2: Build Your Prospect List and Outreach Sequences

**Duration**: 3-5 hours
**Tool**: Apollo.io + LinkedIn

Build your first 100-prospect target list and create outreach sequences.

```
Prospect List Building:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. In Apollo.io (free tier: 10,000 email credits):
   - Filter by: company size, industry, job title, location
   - Match your ICP exactly — do not broaden to "any company"
   - Export 100 prospects to start (name, title, company, email)

2. Import to HubSpot CRM as Contacts
   - Tag with source: "outbound-cold-q1-2026"
   - Create deals for each prospect in Stage 1 (Lead)

Cold Outreach Sequence (5 emails over 14 days):
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
Email 1 (Day 0): Problem-led opener
  Subject: "[Pain point] at [Company]?"
  Body: 1 sentence about their specific challenge +
        1 sentence about how you solve it +
        CTA: "Would a 15-min call make sense?"
  Length: under 100 words

Email 2 (Day 3): Value-add follow-up
  Subject: RE: [original subject]
  Body: Share a relevant insight, stat, or case study +
        Soft CTA: "Thought you'd find this useful"

Email 3 (Day 7): Social proof
  Subject: "How [similar company] solved [problem]"
  Body: Brief customer story or metric +
        CTA: "Happy to show you how — 15 min this week?"

Email 4 (Day 10): Different angle
  Subject: "[New angle — industry trend or news hook]"
  Body: Tie your product to a current industry event +
        CTA: Open-ended question

Email 5 (Day 14): Breakup email
  Subject: "Should I close your file?"
  Body: "I've reached out a few times — sounds like timing
        may not be right. I'll step back. If [problem]
        becomes a priority, my calendar is here: [link]"
```

**Verify**: 100 prospects loaded in CRM, 5-email sequence drafted and reviewed, first batch of 20 emails sent. Track open rates and replies in HubSpot.
**If failed**: If open rates are below 20%, rewrite subject lines — they are too generic. If reply rates are below 2%, the email body is not personalized enough or the pain point does not resonate.

### Step 3: Run Discovery Calls and Demos

**Duration**: Ongoing (2-5 calls per week)
**Tool**: Calendly + Zoom/Google Meet

Convert responses into discovery calls and demos. The discovery call is where you learn whether the prospect is a fit — the demo is where you show them the solution.

```
Discovery Call Framework (20-30 minutes):
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. SITUATION (2 min): "Tell me about your current process for [X]"
2. PROBLEM (5 min): "What's the biggest challenge with [X]?"
3. IMPACT (3 min): "What does that cost you in time/money?"
4. SOLUTION FIT (5 min): "Let me show you how we handle that..."
5. NEXT STEPS (3 min): "Here's what I suggest as a next step..."

Qualification Criteria (BANT):
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
Budget:    Can they afford your price? Is there a budget allocated?
Authority: Is this the decision maker or influencer?
Need:      Is the pain point real and urgent?
Timeline:  When do they need a solution? This quarter? This year?

Score: 3-4 of 4 = qualified → proceed to demo
       2 of 4 = nurture → add to email sequence
       0-1 of 4 = disqualify → move to Closed Lost

Post-Call Checklist:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
□ Update CRM deal stage and notes within 1 hour
□ Send follow-up email within 24 hours summarizing key points
□ Schedule next step (demo, proposal, intro to other stakeholders)
□ Log objections and questions for playbook documentation
```

**Verify**: You are completing 2-5 discovery calls per week, updating CRM after every call, and moving at least 30% of calls to next stage.
**If failed**: If you cannot get prospects on calls, your outreach emails need stronger CTAs or your value proposition is unclear. If calls do not convert to demos, your discovery questions may not be uncovering real pain.

### Step 4: Close Deals and Document the Sales Process

**Duration**: Ongoing (first 10-20 deals)
**Tool**: CRM + document editor

Close your first deals while meticulously documenting what works. This documentation becomes your sales playbook.

```
Sales Playbook Documentation (build as you sell):
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. ICP REFINEMENT
   - Which company types convert best? (size, industry, stage)
   - Which buyer personas close fastest? (title, department)
   - What trigger events indicate buying readiness?

2. OBJECTION HANDLING LOG
   Objection: "It's too expensive"
   Response that works: "[specific response]"
   Win rate with this response: X%

   Objection: "We already use [competitor]"
   Response that works: "[specific response]"
   Win rate with this response: X%

3. KEY METRICS (track from day one)
   - Outreach → Response rate: ___%
   - Response → Discovery call rate: ___%
   - Discovery → Demo rate: ___%
   - Demo → Proposal rate: ___%
   - Proposal → Close rate: ___%
   - Overall: Outreach → Close rate: ___%
   - Average sales cycle length: ___ days
   - Average deal size (ACV): $___

4. WINNING PATTERNS
   - Best email subject lines (by open rate)
   - Best discovery questions (by conversion)
   - Best demo flow (by close rate)
   - Most effective objection responses
```

**Verify**: First 10 deals closed with full CRM history. Sales playbook document exists with ICP notes, objection handling, conversion metrics at each stage, and at least 5 winning patterns documented.
**If failed**: If you cannot close deals, the issue is usually one of: wrong ICP (prospects do not have the pain), wrong pricing (too high for value or too low to signal quality), or weak demo (not connecting product to stated pain). Diagnose by analyzing where deals stall in the pipeline.

### Step 5: Evaluate Transition Triggers and Hire First Rep

**Duration**: Evaluation at 20+ customers
**Tool**: CRM data + hiring platform

Use data from your CRM to determine if you are ready to hire your first sales rep. Do not hire based on feeling — hire based on metrics. [src5]

```
Hire-Ready Checklist (all must be true):
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
□ 50+ demos completed by founder
□ Win rate above 20% (demos → closed deals)
□ Documented sales process in playbook
□ Average sales cycle length is predictable (±2 weeks)
□ Pipeline generates enough leads that founder cannot handle volume
□ Revenue or funding supports $60-100K base + commission for AE

First Sales Hire Profile:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
Title: Account Executive (full-cycle)
NOT: VP of Sales, Head of Sales, SDR
WHY: You need someone who prospects, demos, and closes — not manages

Ideal candidate:
  - 2-5 years of B2B SaaS sales experience
  - Has sold at similar ACV range
  - Comfortable with ambiguity and building process
  - Strong at documentation (will evolve the playbook)
  - Has worked at a startup before (ideally Series A or earlier)

Compensation benchmark (2025-2026):
  - Base: $60-80K (junior) or $80-100K (experienced)
  - OTE: 50/50 split (base = 50% of OTE, commission = 50%)
  - Example: $80K base + $80K commission at quota = $160K OTE
  - Quota: 3-5x their OTE as annual target
    ($160K OTE → $480K-800K annual quota)

Ramp timeline:
  - Month 1: Shadow founder on 10+ calls, study playbook
  - Month 2: Lead calls with founder observing
  - Month 3: Independent calls, first solo closes expected
  - Month 4-6: Ramping to 50-75% of full quota
  - Month 7+: Full quota
```

**Verify**: CRM data supports all checklist items. Job description written with clear expectations, comp structure, and ramp plan. First 3-5 candidates sourced.
**If failed**: If metrics do not support hiring, you are not ready. Continue founder-led sales until the playbook is solid. Hiring a rep to "figure it out" without a playbook wastes $100K+ and 6 months.

## Quality Benchmarks

**If below minimum**: If outreach response rate is under 3%, audit email personalization and ICP fit. If demo → close rate is under 15%, the product demo is not connecting to stated pain or pricing is misaligned.

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Zero responses to outreach | Wrong ICP, generic emails, or bad data | Verify email addresses, rewrite with specific pain points, narrow ICP |
| Discovery calls but no demos scheduled | Prospects are not qualified or pain is not urgent | Tighten qualification criteria (BANT), focus on prospects with active buying signals |
| Demos but no closes | Demo does not address stated pain, or pricing objections | Restructure demo to lead with pain → solution, address pricing earlier in process |
| Won deals but high churn | Selling to wrong customers or overpromising | Audit churned accounts for ICP fit, align sales messaging with actual product capabilities |
| Pipeline stalls at negotiation stage | Decision maker not involved, or procurement process unclear | Ask for stakeholder map earlier, include decision makers in demos |
| First sales hire underperforming | Playbook not documented enough, or wrong hire profile | More founder shadowing, simplify the process, assess if hire has right experience level |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Hiring a VP of Sales as the first sales hire
A VP of Sales builds and manages a team. With zero sales process, zero playbook, and zero pipeline, a VP has nothing to manage. They will spend months "building strategy" while closing zero deals. This is a $200K+ mistake. [src7]

### Correct: First hire is a full-cycle Account Executive
Hire someone who prospects, runs discovery calls, demos, and closes. They should be comfortable doing everything. Only hire a sales leader after you have 2-3 reps successfully following the playbook.

### Wrong: Sending 1,000 cold emails before doing 10 warm calls
Mass cold outreach without conversation data leads to generic messaging that nobody responds to. The first 10-20 sales conversations teach you what resonates — this information is priceless. [src1]

### Correct: Start with warm outreach to your network, then expand to cold
First 5 deals should come from personal network, introductions, and communities where you are active. Use those conversations to build the messaging that scales to cold outreach.

### Wrong: Building a "sales machine" before product-market fit
Investing in sales tools, processes, and hires before knowing who buys and why is premature optimization. You will build the wrong process and hire the wrong people. [src3]

### Correct: Validate demand with founder-led sales first
The founder's job is to learn, not to scale. Use the first 10-20 customers to understand buying patterns, objections, and value perception. Only then build the machine.

Use this recipe when a startup founder needs to build a sales function from scratch — from first outbound email to first hire decision. Requires an ICP definition and a working product.
