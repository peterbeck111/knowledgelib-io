# Marketing Efficiency Execution Recipe: Channel Audit to Budget Reallocation

## Purpose

This recipe produces a fully audited marketing efficiency baseline (MER + channel-level CAC/ROAS), an attribution-validated channel scorecard, and an executed quarterly budget reallocation plan — resulting in 15-25% MER improvement and 10-20% CAC reduction within two quarters. Companies that adopt MER as their primary efficiency metric achieve 15-20% higher marketing ROI compared to peers relying solely on channel-level ROAS. It outputs live dashboards with automated alerts and a documented reallocation playbook for repeatable quarterly optimization. [src3]

## Prerequisites
<!-- Agents: verify ALL prerequisites before executing. Missing prerequisites = failed execution. -->

- [ ] **Channel spend data (6+ months)** available from finance/marketing ops — consolidated across all ad platforms, agencies, content production, and events
- [ ] **Revenue or conversion data by channel** available from CRM or analytics — even basic last-click attribution is sufficient to start
- [ ] **Google Analytics 4** property configured with conversion events — [GA4 Setup](https://analytics.google.com)
- [ ] **Finance alignment** on budget flexibility — CFO/VP Finance approves quarterly reallocation of up to 25% per channel
- [ ] **Minimum 3 active marketing channels** — fewer channels should use simple incrementality testing instead of this full framework

## Constraints
<!-- Hard rules. Agents: enforce throughout execution. Violating these = broken output or legal risk. -->

- Do not reallocate more than 25% of any single channel's budget in one quarter — sudden cuts destroy compounding channels like SEO and content. [src5]
- Marketing mix modeling requires at least $50K/month across 3+ channels for statistically valid results. Below this threshold, use simpler ROAS comparison. [src8]
- Attribution models systematically undercount branded search and organic — apply 20-30% correction factor before cutting these channels. [src4]
- Reserve 10-15% of total budget for testing new channels — over-optimization of existing channels creates fragility. [src5]
- Measurement investment should be 5-10% of total marketing spend. Below 5%, you are flying blind. Above 10%, you are over-measuring and under-executing. [src8]

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Audit All Marketing Spend

**Duration**: 1-2 weeks
**Tool**: Google Sheets or Excel + ad platform exports

Consolidate every marketing dollar into a single ledger. Export spend data from all ad platforms (Google Ads, Meta, LinkedIn, TikTok), pull invoices for content production, agency fees, event costs, and tool subscriptions. Categorize by channel: paid search, paid social, SEO/content, email, events, partnerships, direct mail, and other. Marketing budgets average 7-12% of total company revenue — use this as a sanity check. [src6]

```
Channel Spend Audit Template:
| Channel          | Monthly Spend | % of Total | Revenue Attributed | ROAS  | CAC    |
|------------------|--------------|------------|-------------------|-------|--------|
| Paid Search      | $X           | X%         | $X                | X.Xx  | $X     |
| Paid Social      | $X           | X%         | $X                | X.Xx  | $X     |
| SEO/Content      | $X           | X%         | $X                | X.Xx  | $X     |
| Email            | $X           | X%         | $X                | X.Xx  | $X     |
| Events           | $X           | X%         | $X                | X.Xx  | $X     |
| Partnerships     | $X           | X%         | $X                | X.Xx  | $X     |
| TOTAL            | $X           | 100%       | $X                | X.Xx  | $X     |
```

Include hidden costs: internal labor allocation to marketing, tool licenses buried in IT budgets, and creative production split across teams. [src6]

**Verify**: All channels accounted for; total spend matches finance records within 5%; no channel missing
**If failed**: If spend data is incomplete, work with finance to reconcile. If a channel has no revenue data, mark as "attribution gap" and proceed

### Step 2: Calculate MER Baseline and Channel Benchmarks

**Duration**: 1 week
**Tool**: Google Sheets or attribution platform dashboard

Calculate Marketing Efficiency Ratio: MER = Total Revenue / Total Marketing Spend (expressed as ratio — higher is better). A healthy MER falls between 3:1 and 5:1, meaning $3-$5 revenue per marketing dollar. Alternatively expressed as spend-to-revenue percentage (lower is better). [src3] [src4]

Industry MER benchmarks by vertical (spend as % of revenue, 2025-2026 median): Automotive 27%, Sports & Outdoors 29%, Home & Garden 32%, Apparel 36%, Electronics 41%, Food & Beverage 47%, Health & Wellness 51%. High-margin businesses may thrive at 3:1; lower-margin brands need 5:1+ to break even. [src3]

Calculate channel-level CAC and compare to 2026 industry standards:

| Channel | B2B CAC | B2C CAC | Notes |
|---------|---------|---------|-------|
| Thought Leadership SEO | $647 | $298 | Lowest organic CAC for B2B |
| Email Marketing | $510 | $287 | Highest ROI at 20:1 to 40:1 but capacity-constrained |
| Webinars/Public Speaking | $518-$603 | $251-$472 | Strong for B2B — low CAC, high trust |
| Social Media Marketing | $658 | $212 | Organic social — B2C strength |
| Paid Search (PPC) | $802 | $290 | Google Ads CPL up 5.13% in 2025 |
| Video Marketing | $815 | $301 | Growing channel — 3:1 to 6:1 ROI |
| LinkedIn Ads | $982 | N/A | Most expensive social; B2B decision-maker targeting |
| Content Marketing | $1,254 | $890 | High upfront CAC but compounds over 12 months |
| Referral Programs | $150 | $120 | Lowest CAC across all channels |
| Outbound Sales (SDR) | $1,980 | N/A | Highest CAC; effective only for large deal sizes |

[src1]

Average B2B SaaS CAC hit $1,200 in 2026 (up from $702 in 2025), with sales cycles averaging 134 days (up from 107 in 2022). Target a 3:1 LTV-to-CAC ratio as baseline health check. [src2]

**Verify**: MER calculated with 6-month trend; all channel-level CAC and ROAS computed; benchmarks compared
**If failed**: If revenue attribution is unreliable, use blended MER only and flag channel-level data as directional

### Step 3: Upgrade Attribution Model

**Duration**: 2-4 weeks
**Tool**: Per path — GA4 (Path A), Triple Whale (Path B/C), Northbeam (Path D)

**Path A (GA4 only)**: Configure GA4 data-driven attribution. Set up conversion events for all revenue actions. Enable cross-device tracking. Compare GA4 attributed conversions to ad platform self-reported conversions — typical discrepancy is 20-40%. [src4]

**Path B/C (Triple Whale)**: Connect Shopify store. Triple Whale provides 7 attribution models and ties closely to in-platform numbers. MER tracking is built in. Shopify-only — if on WooCommerce, BigCommerce, or custom, use Northbeam instead. Cost: $199-$999/month scaling with ad spend. [src7]

**Path C/D (Northbeam)**: Connect all ad platforms and ecommerce. Northbeam assigns fractional credit (e.g., 0.6 TikTok + 0.3 Facebook + 0.1 Snapchat = 1.0 order) — solves over-attribution that inflates platform-reported ROAS. Supports Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce, Magento, and custom builds. Includes MMM for brands spending $250K+/month. Cost: $1,000-$5,000+/month. Northbeam pricing is roughly 2-3x Triple Whale's, with daily or near-real-time data refresh vs T+1. [src7]

MTA (multi-touch attribution) can be set up in weeks with granular click-level data. MMM requires years of aggregate historical data but captures offline channels and external factors (seasonality, macro). Best practice in 2026 is a hybrid approach — unified marketing measurement (UMM) combining MTA for tactical decisions and MMM for strategic allocation. [src4]

```
Attribution Upgrade Checklist:
[ ] Current model documented (last-click, first-touch, multi-touch)
[ ] Attribution platform selected and connected to ad accounts
[ ] Conversion events mapped (purchase, signup, lead, MQL)
[ ] UTM parameters standardized across all channels
[ ] First 2 weeks of data collected for comparison
[ ] Discrepancy report: platform-reported vs. attribution-tool vs. CRM actuals
```

**Verify**: Attribution platform collecting data; first comparison report generated; discrepancy quantified
**If failed**: If platform integration fails, fall back to GA4 data-driven attribution and manual reconciliation

### Step 4: Build Channel ROI Scorecard and Identify Waste

**Duration**: 2-3 weeks
**Tool**: Attribution platform + Google Sheets

Rank every channel by efficiency using attributed data. For each channel calculate: ROAS (revenue / spend), CAC (spend / new customers), pipeline contribution (%), and time-to-impact (days from spend to revenue). [src1]

Segment channels into tiers:
- **Growth drivers**: High ROAS + scalable (increase budget)
- **Optimizable**: Decent ROAS but inefficient execution (optimize before scaling)
- **Experimental**: Unproven channels in test phase (maintain test budget)
- **Cut candidates**: Low ROAS after correction factors applied (reduce budget)

Identify waste: flag channels where CPA increased >20% in 90 days; identify audience overlap between paid channels (typical waste: 15-25%); audit bottom 20% of campaigns within each channel — they typically consume 30-40% of channel budget while producing <5% of results. [src8]

Apply correction factors before cutting: organic/SEO channels underperform in months 1-6 but dominate long-term. Content ROI in month 3 is typically 20% of month 12 ROI. Evaluate organic on 12-month ROAS, not 90-day. Apply 20-30% correction to branded search and organic for attribution undercounting. [src4] [src1]

**Verify**: All channels scored and tiered; top 3 and bottom 3 identified; waste estimated in dollars
**If failed**: If results are ambiguous for key channels, extend analysis 2 weeks or run incrementality tests (pause channel for 2 weeks, measure revenue impact)

### Step 5: Execute Budget Reallocation

**Duration**: 2-4 weeks
**Tool**: Ad platforms + finance approval + monitoring dashboards

Build the reallocation plan following the 70-20-10 framework: 70% to proven high-ROI channels, 20% to promising growth opportunities, 10% to experimental channels. The 70-20-10 is a dynamic system — test channels that hit success thresholds graduate from 10% to 20%, and scaling channels that prove themselves at higher spend graduate to 70%. [src6]

Recommended 2026 channel allocation benchmarks:
- Content Marketing & SEO/AEO: 25-30% (ROI 5:1 to 10:1)
- Email Marketing: 15-20% (ROI 20:1 to 40:1)
- Paid Search: 10-15% (ROI 2:1 to 4:1)
- Paid Social: 10-15% (ROI 1.5:1 to 3:1)
- Video Marketing: 10-12% (ROI 3:1 to 6:1)
- Marketing Technology & AI Tools: 8-10% (15-25% efficiency gains)
- Influencer & Partnerships: 5-8% (ROI 5.78:1)
- Testing & Innovation: 5%

[src5]

During seasonal peaks, shift to 80-15-5 to maximize high-performing periods. During slower periods, consider 60-25-15 to accelerate learning and testing. [src6]

```
Reallocation Execution Checklist:
[ ] Reallocation plan approved by CMO/VP Marketing and Finance
[ ] Ad platform budgets updated (do NOT cut any channel to zero in one quarter)
[ ] Content calendar adjusted for increased/decreased investment
[ ] Agency/vendor contracts modified with 30-day notice
[ ] Monitoring dashboard configured with daily spend, weekly ROAS, monthly MER
[ ] Early warning thresholds set: pause scaling if winning channel ROAS drops >15% within 30 days
[ ] Team briefed on changes with rationale and expected outcomes
```

**Verify**: Budget changes live in all platforms; monitoring dashboards operational; no channel cut by more than 25%
**If failed**: If winning channels show diminishing returns at higher spend (marginal CPA increase 10-20% is normal during scaling), find next-best alternatives. If MER drops >10% from baseline after 30 days, revert changes and re-analyze.

### Step 6: Build Continuous Optimization Dashboards

**Duration**: 2-3 weeks
**Tool**: Looker/Tableau (enterprise), Google Sheets + Looker Studio (growth-stage), or attribution platform native dashboards

Create real-time MER dashboard with channel-level drill-down. Set automated alerts for CPA increases >20% on 7-day rolling average. Build weekly efficiency report: spend, revenue, MER, channel-level ROAS, CAC trend. [src8]

Establish the ongoing optimization cadence:
- **Weekly**: Check automated alerts, review CPA trends
- **Monthly**: Reallocation checkpoint — adjust 5-10% based on 30-day rolling data
- **Quarterly**: Full scorecard refresh, rebalance 10-15% based on quarter data
- **Semi-annual**: Incrementality tests on top 3 channels (pause-and-measure)
- **Annual**: MMM refresh for organizations spending $500K+/year [src6]

**Output files**:
- `marketing-efficiency-audit.xlsx` — Channel-level MER, ROAS, CAC with 6-month trends and waste identification
- `budget-reallocation-plan.xlsx` — Quarterly shift plan with expected impact, monitoring thresholds, rollback triggers
- `channel-scorecard.xlsx` — Tiered channel ranking with correction factors applied
- `dashboard-config.md` — Dashboard setup documentation for ongoing monitoring

**Verify**: Dashboards operational; first weekly report generated; quarterly review scheduled
**If failed**: If dashboard tool budget is rejected, use Google Sheets with manual weekly updates as minimum viable alternative

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| MER shows no improvement after reallocation | Attribution model inaccurate or reallocation too conservative | Upgrade attribution (Step 3); increase reallocation magnitude by 5-10% |
| Channel CAC data inconsistent across sources | Ad platform self-reporting inflated; CRM data incomplete | Use attribution platform as source of truth; reconcile monthly with finance |
| Attribution platform integration fails | API permissions, pixel misconfiguration, or platform incompatibility | Check API keys; verify pixel fires on all conversion pages; contact platform support |
| Winning channel ROAS drops after budget increase | Diminishing returns at higher spend (audience saturation) | Reduce scaling pace; expand audience targeting; test creative refresh |
| Finance rejects reallocation plan | Insufficient data or risk tolerance | Present incrementality test data; propose smaller initial reallocation (10% vs 25%) |
| MER drops >10% after reallocation | Second-order effects — cutting awareness channels reduced demand generation | Revert cuts immediately; re-analyze with 20-30% organic correction factor applied |
| Triple Whale shows different numbers than Northbeam | Different attribution methodologies — Triple Whale tracks closer to platform-reported; Northbeam uses fractional credit | Pick one as source of truth; use the other as cross-validation; document discrepancy [src7] |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Optimizing only for last-click attribution
Last-click over-credits bottom-funnel channels (branded search, retargeting) and under-credits awareness channels (content, social) that created the demand. Between 2023 and 2025, CAC increased ~40% across channels partly because brands over-invested in capture channels and under-invested in demand generation based on flawed last-click data. [src2]

### Correct: Use MER as portfolio metric alongside multi-touch or ML-based attribution
MER captures halo effects and cross-channel influence invisible to platform-reported ROAS. A brand showing 2.0 ROAS in Meta Ads Manager may actually achieve 3.5 MER across all channels, revealing unmeasured organic and email benefits. Track both — MER for strategic clarity, ROAS for tactical precision. [src4]

### Wrong: Cutting SEO and content budget when short-term ROAS looks low
Content ROI in month 3 is typically 20% of month 12 ROI. SEO delivers B2B CAC of $647 vs. $982 for LinkedIn Ads, but only after 6-12 months of compounding. Cutting content to fund paid channels trades long-term compounding for short-term marginal gains. [src1]

### Correct: Evaluate organic channels on 12-month ROAS with correction factors
Compare mature content ROI (month 12+) to paid channel ROI. Apply 20-30% attribution correction factor for organic channels. Content & SEO should receive 25-30% of total budget for 5:1 to 10:1 long-term ROI. [src5]

### Wrong: Reallocating budget based on a single month of data
Monthly channel performance varies 20-40% due to seasonality, creative cycles, and market noise. One bad month is noise; two is a signal; three is a trend. [src8]

### Correct: Make reallocation decisions on quarterly data with 90-day rolling averages
Use 90-day rolling averages to smooth volatility. Only reallocate when trends persist across a full quarter. Follow the 70-20-10 framework: 70% proven, 20% promising, 10% experimental. Adjust cadence for seasonal peaks (80-15-5) and slow periods (60-25-15). [src5] [src6]
