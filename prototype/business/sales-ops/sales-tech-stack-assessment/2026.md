# Sales Tech Stack Assessment

## Purpose

This assessment evaluates whether a company has the right sales technology for its current revenue stage and selling motion. It diagnoses gaps, redundancies, and adoption failures across five technology layers: CRM foundation, sales engagement, pipeline intelligence, CPQ/deal management, and enablement. The output identifies which tools to add, consolidate, or retire — and critically, where low adoption is wasting existing investments. Use this after a sales process maturity assessment reveals technology-related gaps or when onboarding a new RevOps leader. [src1]

## Constraints
<!-- Agents: read before running this assessment with a user. -->

- Assess process maturity first — adding technology to a broken process amplifies dysfunction, not productivity
- Tool-specific recommendations age quickly; focus on capability gaps rather than specific vendor names
- Average sales tech spend is $1,200/rep/year, but 67% of features go unused — utilization matters more than purchase
- High-growth SDR teams average 5 core applications; exceeding 8-10 tools typically creates integration overhead that offsets productivity gains
- This assessment does not replace vendor evaluation — it identifies what capabilities are needed, not which vendor to buy

## Assessment Dimensions

### Dimension 1: CRM Foundation

**What this measures**: Whether the CRM serves as a reliable system of record with clean data, proper customization, and integration as the hub of all sales technology.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No CRM or CRM used as a basic contact list; reps track deals in spreadsheets | Less than 30% of deal data in CRM; no pipeline reporting from CRM; no integrations |
| 2 | Emerging | CRM deployed (Salesforce/HubSpot) but poorly configured; basic opportunity tracking with many data gaps | 40-60% field completion; no custom objects for the business model; 1-2 basic integrations |
| 3 | Defined | CRM properly configured for the sales motion; required fields enforced; core integrations active (email, calendar, marketing) | 80%+ data completion; automated activity capture; standard dashboards; 3-5 integrations |
| 4 | Managed | CRM is data hub; bi-directional integrations with marketing, support, billing; custom objects match the business model; data governance active | Single source of truth; automated data enrichment; CRM hygiene scoring; API-driven architecture |
| 5 | Optimized | CRM is revenue intelligence platform; AI-driven insights; natural language queries; predictive analytics native to CRM | Einstein/AI assistant active; automated next-best-actions; self-cleaning data; real-time revenue intelligence |

**Red flags**: Multiple CRMs in use; reps bypass CRM entirely; leadership uses spreadsheets for forecasting; no CRM admin or dedicated operations person. [src2]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "Does your VP of Sales trust a CRM pipeline report without cross-referencing a spreadsheet?"

### Dimension 2: Sales Engagement & Outreach

**What this measures**: Tools for managing multi-channel outreach sequences, tracking buyer engagement, and automating repetitive prospecting tasks.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | Manual email outreach; no sequencing; reps manage follow-ups via personal memory or sticky notes | No outbound cadence tool; no email tracking; no call recording or logging |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic email sequencing tool (Mailchimp or basic CRM sequences); some call tracking; no multi-channel orchestration | Single-channel sequences; no A/B testing; manual call logging; no engagement scoring |
| 3 | Defined | Dedicated sales engagement platform (Outreach/Salesloft); multi-channel sequences (email, phone, LinkedIn); engagement analytics | Multi-step sequences with branching; reply detection; basic engagement scoring; call recording |
| 4 | Managed | AI-powered engagement (personalized messaging, optimal send times); integrated across channels; buyer intent signals incorporated | AI-generated email drafts; intent-based prioritization; conversation intelligence integrated; A/B testing at scale |
| 5 | Optimized | Autonomous prospecting agents; real-time buyer signal ingestion; fully orchestrated multi-channel campaigns with AI optimization | AI SDR agents handling initial outreach; dynamic sequence adjustment; website visitor identification feeding directly into sequences |

**Red flags**: SDRs spending more than 50% of time on manual data entry; no visibility into email open/reply rates; reps use personal email for outreach. [src3]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "How many touches are in your average outbound sequence, and how do you know which messages work?"

### Dimension 3: Pipeline Intelligence & Analytics

**What this measures**: Tools for understanding deal health, predicting outcomes, and surfacing revenue insights beyond basic CRM reporting.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No analytics beyond basic CRM reports; pipeline reviewed in spreadsheets or slide decks | No dashboard tool; manual data pulls; no deal scoring; retrospective-only analysis |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic BI tool connected to CRM (Tableau/Looker/native CRM dashboards); some pipeline reporting | Standard pipeline dashboards; no predictive capabilities; manual forecast roll-ups |
| 3 | Defined | Revenue intelligence platform (Clari/Gong/Chorus) providing deal health signals; conversation intelligence capturing call insights | Deal health scoring; call recording and analysis; basic forecast intelligence; win/loss patterns visible |
| 4 | Managed | AI-powered forecasting with 85%+ accuracy; deal risk alerts; automated coaching insights from conversation data | Predictive deal scoring; forecast accuracy tracked and improving; AI-surfaced coaching moments; pipeline leak detection |
| 5 | Optimized | Real-time revenue intelligence with prescriptive recommendations; autonomous pipeline management; integrated across all revenue data sources | AI forecast accuracy 90-95%; prescriptive next actions; pipeline anomaly detection; multi-source signal integration |

**Red flags**: No conversation intelligence despite having inside sales team; forecast discussions based on gut feel despite having CRM data; no win/loss analysis capability. [src4]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "Can you tell me right now which deals in your pipeline are at risk, and how do you know?"

### Dimension 4: CPQ & Deal Management

**What this measures**: Tools for pricing, quoting, contract management, and streamlining the quote-to-close process.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | Pricing in spreadsheets; manual quote generation in Word/Google Docs; no approval workflows | Each quote is custom-built; pricing errors common; no version control; approval via email |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic quoting tool or CRM-native quotes; simple price books; manual discount approval process | Standardized templates exist; basic price book in CRM; email-based approval chains |
| 3 | Defined | CPQ platform (Salesforce CPQ/DealHub/PandaDoc); automated pricing rules; structured approval workflows; e-signature integrated | Automated discount guardrails; approval routing by deal size; e-signature (DocuSign/PandaDoc); quote-to-cash under 48 hours |
| 4 | Managed | Advanced CPQ with guided selling; subscription billing integration; multi-currency/multi-entity support; renewal automation | AI-suggested upsells during quoting; billing system integration; automated renewal quotes; self-serve pricing for SMB |
| 5 | Optimized | AI-powered dynamic pricing; real-time competitive pricing intelligence; fully automated quote-to-revenue lifecycle | AI optimizes pricing per deal; competitive intelligence feeds into pricing; zero-touch renewal processing; revenue recognition automated |

**Red flags**: Average time from verbal agreement to signed contract exceeds 2 weeks; reps regularly apply incorrect pricing; no e-signature integration; discounting has no guardrails. [src5]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "How long does it take from verbal 'yes' to a signed contract, and how many manual steps are involved?"

### Dimension 5: Sales Enablement & Training

**What this measures**: Tools for onboarding, continuous training, content management, and ensuring reps have the right resources at the right time.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No enablement platform; onboarding is informal shadowing; content scattered across drives and Slack | No content repository; reps create their own decks; no onboarding curriculum; no coaching tools |
| 2 | Emerging | Shared drive or wiki for content; basic onboarding checklist; occasional training sessions | Google Drive/SharePoint with sales content; onboarding takes 3+ months; training is event-based |
| 3 | Defined | Dedicated enablement platform (Highspot/Seismic/Showpad); structured onboarding program; content organized by deal stage | Searchable content library; 30-60 day onboarding program; stage-specific content recommendations; basic content analytics |
| 4 | Managed | AI-powered content recommendations; just-in-time learning; coaching cadence with recorded practice; content engagement tracked | AI recommends content per deal stage; micro-learning modules; call coaching with AI feedback; content usage correlated with win rates |
| 5 | Optimized | Adaptive learning paths; AI-generated coaching from real calls; content automatically updated based on win/loss patterns | Personalized rep development plans; AI role-play for practice; auto-generated battle cards; content effectiveness measured per deal outcome |

**Red flags**: Reps spend more than 30 minutes per day searching for content; onboarding takes more than 90 days to ramp; no coaching cadence exists; content is more than 6 months old. [src2]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "If a rep needs a competitive battle card right now, where do they go and how long does it take to find it?"

## Scoring & Interpretation

### Overall Score Calculation

All dimensions are weighted equally. For companies with complex product lines, weight CPQ 1.5x. For companies with large SDR teams, weight engagement 1.5x.

```
Overall Score = (CRM Foundation + Sales Engagement + Pipeline Intelligence + CPQ/Deal Mgmt + Enablement) / 5
```

### Score Interpretation

| Overall Score | Maturity Level | Interpretation | Recommended Next Step |
|---------------|---------------|----------------|----------------------|
| 1.0 - 1.9 | Critical | Missing foundational technology; manual processes limit scale; data blind spots everywhere | Invest in CRM first, then engagement — process before tools |
| 2.0 - 2.9 | Developing | Basic tools in place but underutilized; significant capability gaps remain | Focus on adoption of existing tools before buying new ones; close the biggest capability gap |
| 3.0 - 3.9 | Competent | Solid tech foundation; ready for intelligence and optimization layers | Add revenue intelligence; optimize integrations; measure tool ROI |
| 4.0 - 4.5 | Advanced | Well-integrated stack; AI-augmented workflows; data-driven selling | Fine-tune AI capabilities; consolidate vendor sprawl; advanced analytics |
| 4.6 - 5.0 | Best-in-class | Fully integrated, AI-powered revenue technology platform | Maintain; evaluate emerging agentic AI capabilities; share best practices |

## Common Pitfalls in Assessment

- **Shiny object syndrome**: Teams buy new tools instead of driving adoption of existing ones. Before any new purchase, audit utilization of current stack — 67% of features go unused. [src4]
- **Integration neglect**: Tools bought in isolation create data silos. Every new tool should have a clear integration architecture before purchase — if it cannot connect to CRM bidirectionally, reconsider.
- **Over-tooling at early stage**: Seed-stage companies with 8+ sales tools are spending money on sophistication they cannot utilize. Start with CRM + email tracking + e-signature; add tools only when process demands it.
- **Under-investing at growth stage**: Conversely, $15M+ ARR companies still running on spreadsheets and basic CRM are leaving efficiency gains on the table. Revenue intelligence and conversation intelligence have clear ROI at this stage.
