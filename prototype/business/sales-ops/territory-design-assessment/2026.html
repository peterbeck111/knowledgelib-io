Territory Design Assessment

How effective is territory design — coverage model, account distribution, whitespace analysis?

Type: Assessment Confidence: 0.82 Sources: 6 Verified: 2026-03-09

Purpose

This assessment evaluates the effectiveness of sales territory design across five dimensions — coverage model, account distribution balance, whitespace identification, data-driven methodology, and dynamic adjustment capability. Poor territory design is a leading cause of missed quotas: only about 43% of reps meet quota, and unbalanced territories are a primary contributor. [src1]

Constraints

  • Requires CRM account data, quota attainment history, and rep capacity metrics for meaningful scoring
  • Most relevant for B2B sales organizations with 10+ quota-carrying reps
  • Should be run by Sales Ops or RevOps with VP Sales input
  • Evaluates territory design, not individual rep performance
  • Re-run annually before territory planning; also after M&A or significant headcount changes

Common Pitfalls in Assessment

  • Rep performance conflation: Poor territory design masks good reps and inflates weak ones. Normalize for territory potential before drawing conclusions.
  • Geography bias: Many organizations default to geographic territories when buyers don't buy geographically.
  • Fairness vs. optimization tension: Perfectly balanced territories may not maximize revenue. Assess whether imbalances are strategic or accidental.
  • Annual planning trap: Territory design done once a year becomes stale by Q2. Evaluate the ability to adapt mid-year.
