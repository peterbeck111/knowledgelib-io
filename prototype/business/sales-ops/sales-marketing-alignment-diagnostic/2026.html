Sales-Marketing Alignment Diagnostic

How aligned are sales and marketing — lead handoff, SLA compliance, attribution maturity?

Type: Assessment Confidence: 0.84 Sources: 6 Verified: 2026-03-09

Purpose

This assessment evaluates the alignment maturity between sales and marketing teams across five dimensions — shared definitions, lead handoff process, SLA compliance, attribution and measurement, and strategic planning. Only 8% of companies report strong alignment, yet aligned organizations achieve 32% higher year-over-year revenue growth and 38% higher win rates. This diagnostic identifies specific misalignment gaps and routes to targeted improvement actions. [src1]

Constraints

  • Requires input from both sales and marketing leaders — single-side assessments produce biased results skewed by 1.0-1.5 points
  • Most relevant for B2B companies with dedicated sales and marketing teams (10+ people combined)
  • Should be facilitated by RevOps, an executive sponsor, or an external party to ensure neutrality
  • Diagnoses alignment between functions, not individual team performance
  • Re-run quarterly; alignment degrades with personnel changes and strategy shifts

Common Pitfalls in Assessment

  • Single-side assessment bias: When only one team participates, scores inflate by 1.0-1.5 points. Both teams must provide input independently.
  • Process-existence fallacy: Having an SLA or attribution model does not mean it is used. Ask "when was this last updated?" and "who looks at this weekly?"
  • Attribution credit wars: Teams fighting over credit often have deeper misalignment in definitions and strategy. Fix definitions first.
  • Confusing meetings with alignment: Regular meetings without structured agendas and action items are activity, not alignment.
