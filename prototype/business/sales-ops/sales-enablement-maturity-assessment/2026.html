Sales Enablement Maturity Assessment

How mature is sales enablement — content utilization, onboarding, training, competitive intelligence?

Type: Assessment Confidence: 0.84 Sources: 6 Verified: 2026-03-09

Purpose

This assessment evaluates the maturity of a sales enablement program across five dimensions — strategic alignment, content management, onboarding, ongoing training, and competitive intelligence. 90% of organizations now have enablement teams, but average maturity is only 3.68 out of 5. This diagnostic identifies specific enablement gaps and routes to improvement actions. [src1]

Constraints

  • Requires input from both enablement leaders and frontline sales managers
  • Most relevant for organizations with a dedicated enablement function or person
  • Should be run by RevOps, CRO, or VP Sales with enablement team input
  • Evaluates enablement holistically, not any single dimension in isolation
  • Re-run bi-annually; align with enablement planning cycles

Common Pitfalls in Assessment

  • Content volume vs. effectiveness: 500 assets at 10% utilization is worse than 50 at 60%. 50% of engagement comes from 10% of content.
  • Training completion vs. behavior change: High completion rates don't equal skill improvement. 84% of training forgotten in 3 months.
  • Enablement as event planning: If primary activities are SKO and webinars, it's level 2 regardless of quality.
  • Manager coaching gap: Frontline coaching is the most powerful channel. Without it, direct-to-rep programs lose 50%+ impact.
