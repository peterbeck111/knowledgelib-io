# Sales Compensation Benchmarks 2026

## Summary

These benchmarks cover OTE (On-Target Earnings), base-to-variable splits, accelerator structures, and quota-to-OTE ratios across all B2B SaaS sales roles: SDR/BDR, AE (SMB through enterprise), Sales Manager, Director, VP of Sales, and CRO. Data is sourced from The Bridge Group (350+ companies), Pavilion, Visdum, and Everstage research. The most critical shift: SDR OTEs compressed 5% in 2025 while enterprise AE and leadership OTEs held steady, widening the seniority gap and creating retention pressure on experienced SDRs ready to promote. [src1]

**Data vintage**: Based on H2 2024 through H1 2025 data from 1,000+ SaaS companies.
**Key shift**: The typical base-to-variable split in the US has shifted to 44:56 for AEs (more variable-heavy), while SDRs remain at 65:35. Accelerators above 120% attainment now standard at 82% of companies.

## Constraints
<!-- Agents: read before citing any benchmark number. -->

- Compensation is geography-dependent. Bay Area/NYC OTEs are 30-50% above national median. Adjust using geographic multipliers in the Geography section below.
- Self-reported data skews 10-15% above actual. Use these figures for competitive positioning, not as precise salary offers.
- OTE assumes 100% quota attainment. Actual W-2 earnings for median performers are 70-85% of stated OTE.
- Base:variable splits differ by role. Applying AE splits (50:50) to SDRs (65:35) or VPs (60:40) produces misaligned incentives.
- Data vintage is H2 2025. In a tight labor market, comp moves up 5-8% within 6 months; in a downturn, it can compress 10-15%.

## Metric Category 1: OTE by Role

### SDR/BDR On-Target Earnings

**Definition**: Total compensation (base + variable) at 100% quota attainment for Sales Development Representatives or Business Development Representatives. Variable is typically tied to meetings set, qualified pipeline, or a hybrid.

| Segment | Median OTE | 25th Percentile | 75th Percentile | Top Decile |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|------------|
| SMB SDR | $72K | $58K | $85K | $100K |
| Mid-Market SDR | $82K | $68K | $95K | $115K |
| Enterprise SDR | $95K | $78K | $115K | $135K |
| All SDRs (US) | $78K | $62K | $92K | $110K |

**Trend**: Down 5% from 2024 as companies tighten SDR budgets and AI tools reduce need for headcount. [src1]
**Red flag threshold**: OTE below $60K in major metro signals inability to attract quality talent; above $120K for non-enterprise SDR signals over-payment.
**Action trigger**: If SDR turnover exceeds 35% annually, audit comp competitiveness against local market data.

[src1, src4]

### Account Executive On-Target Earnings

**Definition**: Total compensation (base + variable) at 100% quota attainment for quota-carrying Account Executives. Variable tied to closed-won revenue (new business, expansion, or blended).

| Segment | Median OTE | 25th Percentile | 75th Percentile | Top Decile |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|------------|
| SMB AE | $120K | $95K | $150K | $185K |
| Mid-Market AE | $175K | $140K | $210K | $260K |
| Enterprise AE | $250K | $195K | $310K | $400K |
| Strategic/Named AE | $320K | $260K | $400K | $500K+ |

**Trend**: Enterprise AE OTEs stable YoY. SMB AE OTEs compressed slightly as PLG companies shift some SMB motion to self-serve. [src4]
**Red flag threshold**: OTE below $100K for any AE role signals non-competitive compensation; above $350K for mid-market signals over-payment or misclassified territory.
**Action trigger**: If quota attainment is below 25% across the AE team, the problem is likely quota-setting, not compensation.

[src2, src4]

### Sales Leadership OTE

**Definition**: Total compensation for sales management and leadership roles. Variable typically tied to team attainment, revenue targets, and strategic KPIs.

| Role | Median OTE | 25th Percentile | 75th Percentile | Top Decile |
|------|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|------------|
| First-Line Manager | $200K | $160K | $250K | $300K |
| Sales Director | $260K | $210K | $320K | $380K |
| VP of Sales | $350K | $280K | $430K | $520K |
| CRO | $450K | $350K | $550K | $700K+ |

**Trend**: Leadership OTEs increasing 3-5% YoY as competition for experienced sales leaders intensifies. [src6]
**Red flag threshold**: Manager OTE less than 1.3x their top AE's OTE creates promotion economics problems. VP OTE below $250K at growth-stage company signals underpayment.

[src3, src6]

## Metric Category 2: Base-to-Variable Split

### Split by Role

**Definition**: Percentage of OTE paid as guaranteed base salary vs at-risk variable compensation. Higher base percentages provide stability; higher variable percentages drive pay-for-performance alignment.

| Role | Median Base:Variable | Typical Range | Industry Standard |
|------|---------------------|---------------|-------------------|
| SDR/BDR | 65:35 | 60:40 to 70:30 | Higher base due to developing skills and lower deal control |
| SMB AE | 55:45 | 50:50 to 60:40 | Balanced — enough stability for high-volume motion |
| Mid-Market AE | 50:50 | 45:55 to 55:45 | Classic AE split — maximum pay-for-performance alignment |
| Enterprise AE | 45:55 | 40:60 to 50:50 | More variable to reflect larger deal sizes and higher leverage |
| Sales Manager | 60:40 | 55:45 to 65:35 | Higher base reflects coaching role; variable on team attainment |
| VP of Sales | 60:40 | 55:45 to 70:30 | Strategic role requires stability; variable on org-level targets |
| CRO | 55:45 | 50:50 to 65:35 | Highest-leverage role; significant variable tied to company targets |

**Trend**: The US aggregate has shifted to 44:56 for AEs (more variable), up from 48:52 in 2023. Companies are increasingly tying AE pay to performance. [src1]
**Red flag threshold**: AE splits more extreme than 40:60 create excessive risk; splits weaker than 60:40 reduce performance motivation.

[src1, src3]

## Metric Category 3: Accelerator & Decelerator Structures

### Accelerator Tiers

**Definition**: Commission rate multipliers applied when a rep exceeds quota. Accelerators reward over-performance and are the primary tool for retaining top performers.

| Attainment Level | Median Multiplier | 25th Percentile | 75th Percentile |
|-----------------|-------------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| 0-50% of quota | 0.5x-0.75x (decelerator) | 0.5x | 1.0x (flat) |
| 50-100% of quota | 1.0x (base rate) | 1.0x | 1.0x |
| 100-120% of quota | 1.3x | 1.2x | 1.5x |
| 120-150% of quota | 1.5x | 1.3x | 2.0x |
| 150%+ of quota | 2.0x | 1.5x | 3.0x |

**Trend**: 82% of companies now offer accelerated commissions above quota, up from 72% in 2023. Average post-quota boost is 20-30% higher rate. [src1]
**Red flag threshold**: No accelerator above quota = top reps will leave for companies that have them. Accelerators that cap at 150% demotivate top performers in the final month.

[src1, src5]

### Decelerator Usage

**Definition**: Reduced commission rates for below-quota performance. Decelerators penalize underperformance but can demotivate struggling reps.

| Feature | Prevalence | Best Practice |
|---------|-----------|---------------|
| No decelerator (full rate from $0) | 35% | Simplest; works well for SMB high-volume teams |
| 0.5x below 50% attainment | 40% | Most common; protects company economics on underperformers |
| Sliding scale (0.5x-1.0x from 0-100%) | 20% | Gradual ramp; balances motivation and protection |
| Cliff at minimum threshold (0% below 30%) | 5% | Aggressive; signals imminent PIP territory |

[src3]

## Metric Category 4: Quota-to-OTE Ratio

### Quota:OTE Ratio by Role

**Definition**: Annual quota divided by OTE. Measures how much revenue a company expects per dollar of compensation. Higher ratios mean more aggressive quota expectations.

| Role | Median Ratio | Healthy Range | Alarm Threshold |
|------|-------------|---------------|-----------------|
| SMB AE | 4.5x | 3.5x-6.0x | Below 3.0x or above 7.0x |
| Mid-Market AE | 5.0x | 4.0x-6.5x | Below 3.5x or above 8.0x |
| Enterprise AE | 4.5x | 3.5x-6.0x | Below 3.0x or above 7.0x |

**Trend**: Ratios relatively stable, but with declining attainment rates, effective cost-per-deal is rising. [src4]
**Red flag threshold**: Below 3:1 = over-paying; company is losing money on each rep. Above 8:1 = unrealistic quotas; expect sub-20% attainment and high turnover.
**Action trigger**: If ratio is above 6:1 and attainment is below 30%, quotas are likely too aggressive — recalibrate before next comp cycle.

[src4, src5]

## Composite Metrics & Rules of Thumb

| Rule | Formula / Threshold | Interpretation |
|------|--------------------|----------------|
| Manager OTE Floor | Manager OTE >= 1.3x top AE OTE | Below 1.3x: promotion economics do not work; AEs will not move to management |
| SDR-to-AE Promotion Bump | AE OTE >= 1.4x SDR OTE | Below 1.4x: promotion is not financially attractive; SDR retention suffers |
| Total Sales Comp / Revenue | 15-25% of revenue spent on total sales compensation | Above 30%: sales comp is eating margin; below 10%: likely under-investing |
| Variable as % of Revenue | Variable comp = 8-12% of new revenue generated | Above 15%: commission plan is too generous; below 5%: not enough incentive |
| Commission Rate | 8-12% of ACV for new business | Standard SaaS commission rate; adjust for deal complexity |

**Constraint**: Rules of thumb break down at extremes. Very high-ACV enterprise deals ($500K+) often use lower commission rates (5-8%) with higher base salaries. PLG companies may have no commissions for self-serve revenue. [src4]

## Common Misinterpretations

- **Comparing OTEs without geography adjustment**: A $150K AE OTE in SF and Omaha are vastly different in competitive terms. The SF number is below-market; the Omaha number is above-market. Always normalize. [src2]
- **Treating OTE as expected earnings**: OTE assumes 100% attainment. With only 31% of reps hitting quota, median actual earnings are 70-85% of OTE. Use "expected earnings at median attainment" for budget planning.
- **Applying single-segment splits universally**: A 50:50 split that works for mid-market AEs is too risky for SDRs (who have less deal control) and too conservative for enterprise AEs (who need upside motivation).
- **Ignoring total comp**: OTE is just cash. Equity, benefits, and perks (especially at startups) can be 20-40% of total compensation value. Compare total comp, not just OTE, when benchmarking against public companies.
