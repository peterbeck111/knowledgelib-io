Competitor Analysis Framework

How do I analyze individual competitors — positioning, pricing, features, funding, traffic, strengths, weaknesses?

Type: Execution Recipe Confidence: 0.88 Sources: 6 Verified: 2026-03-11

Purpose

This recipe produces a detailed profile of an individual competitor covering 8 analysis dimensions: company overview, positioning, pricing, product/features, growth signals, customer sentiment, SWOT assessment, and strategic implications. The output serves as both strategic intelligence and a sales-ready battle card.

Prerequisites

  • Competitor name and URL from Competitive Landscape Map
  • Your product positioning — target customer, value proposition, differentiators
  • Analysis purpose defined
  • Web access

Constraints

  • Use publicly available data only. [src2]
  • Traffic estimates have 20-40% error margins. Use for comparison, not absolutes. [src6]
  • Reviews are selection-biased. Weight by volume and recency. [src3]
  • Job postings reveal strategy with 3-6 month lag. [src4]
  • Profiles older than 6 months should be refreshed. [src1]

Execution Flow

Step 1: Company Overview and Positioning

Duration: 30 minutes. Gather core data: founding year, HQ, employees, funding history, key executives, tagline, value proposition, target customer.

Step 2: Pricing Analysis

Duration: 20 minutes. Document pricing model, tiers, free offering, enterprise tier, pricing trends. Check G2 and web archive for hidden pricing.

Step 3: Product and Feature Analysis

Duration: 30 minutes. Map core features with maturity ratings, unique features, gaps, integrations, platform coverage, product velocity.

Step 4: Growth and Traction Signals

Duration: 30 minutes. Website traffic trends, search interest, hiring signals, social following, customer count estimates.

Step 5: Customer Sentiment Analysis

Duration: 20 minutes. Mine G2, Capterra, Reddit reviews for recurring strength and weakness themes. Identify churn reasons and switching patterns.

Step 6: SWOT and Battle Card

Duration: 20 minutes. Synthesize SWOT with evidence and strategic implications. Generate battle card with win/lose themes, objection handling, and trap questions.

Anti-Patterns

Wrong: Analyzing through your own lens

Listing weaknesses your target customer does not care about is useless intelligence. [src1]

Correct: Analyze through the customer's buying criteria

Evaluate each competitor against what matters to your target customer.

When This Matters

Use after landscape mapping identifies top 3-5 threats. Each profile informs positioning, pricing, features, and sales strategy.
