# Brand Identity Creation Recipe: Name to Brand Kit

## Purpose

This recipe produces a complete, deployable brand identity for a startup — brand name secured with domain registration and trademark clearance, logo and visual identity system designed, messaging framework documented, and brand guidelines packaged for team use — within 3-16 weeks at $300-$50,000+ depending on execution model. Startups with coherent brand identity see 22% higher click-through rates and 15% lower customer acquisition costs compared to unbranded competitors. The output is a ready-to-use brand kit that enables consistent marketing, investor materials, and product design from day one. [src1]

## Prerequisites
<!-- Agents: verify ALL prerequisites before executing. Missing prerequisites = failed execution. -->

- [ ] **Target customer profile** defined (demographics, psychographics, pain points) — if not: [Customer Discovery Playbook](/business/startup/customer-discovery-playbook/2026)
- [ ] **Product or service** defined at least at MVP level — you need to know what the brand represents
- [ ] **Competitive landscape** mapped — 5-10 competitors with their positioning, visual style, and messaging
- [ ] **Budget approved** — minimum $300 for DIY, $1K for freelancers, $5K for studio, $20K for agency
- [ ] **2-3 brand decision-makers** identified — more stakeholders produce worse outcomes through design-by-committee
- [ ] **Design platform account** — Canva (free tier) or Figma (free Starter) at minimum

## Constraints
<!-- Hard rules. Agents: enforce throughout execution. Violating these = broken output or legal risk. -->

- Do not design before defining strategy — every hour of design without strategy produces ~40 minutes of rework. [src4]
- Trademark search is mandatory before finalizing any name — ~30% of "perfect" names fail trademark or domain checks. Density of registered trademarks for 4-5 letter words is near 100% in major economies. [src7]
- USPTO base filing fee is $350/class (effective Feb 2025). Custom goods descriptions add $200/class surcharge. Attorney fees add $1,000-$3,000. [src5]
- Limit revision rounds to 3 per deliverable — beyond that, the brief is wrong, not the design. [src2]
- Maximum 2 typefaces in the identity system — additional fonts add cognitive load and page weight. [src8]
- Brand guidelines must be documented in a shareable format — undocumented brands drift within 6 months. [src1]
- Color palette must pass WCAG AA contrast: 4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text (18pt+ or 14pt+ bold). [src8]

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Define Brand Positioning

**Duration**: 3-5 days
**Tool**: Google Docs or Notion

Map 5-10 competitor brands: what they claim, how they look, what tone they use. Identify positioning white space — territories competitors have not claimed. Complete the brand positioning statement using the template below. Define 3-5 brand personality traits (adjectives that describe how the brand behaves). Create a "brand is / brand is not" matrix to prevent drift. [src1]

```
Brand Positioning Brief Template:
─────────────────────────────────
Target Customer: [who — demographics + psychographics]
Category: [what market you compete in]
Key Benefit: [primary value delivered — one sentence]
Reason to Believe: [proof/differentiator — why you, not competitors]
Personality Traits: [3-5 adjectives — e.g., bold, approachable, precise]
Brand Values: [3-5 principles — e.g., transparency, speed, craftsmanship]
Brand Is: [e.g., expert but accessible, premium but not exclusive]
Brand Is Not: [e.g., corporate, jargon-heavy, cheap, generic]
Competitive Positioning Map:
  [Competitor 1]: claims [X], looks [Y], tone [Z]
  [Competitor 2]: claims [X], looks [Y], tone [Z]
  White space identified: [what no competitor owns]
```

**Verify**: Positioning statement completed; 3+ personality traits defined; "brand is / brand is not" matrix has 5+ entries per column; brief fits on 1-2 pages
**If failed**: If stakeholders cannot agree on positioning within 1 week, the problem is strategic alignment — resolve before proceeding. Run a forced-rank exercise: each stakeholder ranks the top 3 differentiators independently, then compare. [src4]

### Step 2: Generate and Clear Brand Name

**Duration**: 5-10 days
**Tool**: Brainstorming + USPTO TESS (free) + Namecheap (domain check) + Namechk.com (social handles)

Generate 50-100 name candidates across five categories: descriptive (Paystack), suggestive (Airbnb), invented (Spotify), portmanteau (Netflix = Internet + Flicks), and metaphorical (Amazon). Screen for pronunciation (can it be said over the phone?), spelling (can it be typed from hearing?), and memorability. Shortlist to 10-15 candidates. [src4]

Run clearance checks on the top 5 candidates:

```
Name Clearance Checklist (per candidate):
──────────────────────────────────────────
Candidate: _______________
[ ] 1. Google exact-match search — no direct competitor in same industry
[ ] 2. USPTO TESS (tess2.uspto.gov) — no "Live" marks in relevant Nice class
[ ] 3. Domain availability — .com strongly preferred; .io, .co acceptable for tech
     Check at Namecheap or Cloudflare Registrar ($9-$15/year for .com)
[ ] 4. Social handle check — Instagram, X/Twitter, LinkedIn via Namechk.com
[ ] 5. App store search — no competing apps with same name
[ ] 6. Urban Dictionary check — no unintended slang meanings
[ ] 7. Cross-language check — no offensive meanings in target markets
Result: CLEAR / BLOCKED (reason: ___)
```

Register domain immediately upon clearance — domains get snatched after trademark searches. File trademark application at USPTO ($350/class base fee). If using free-text goods description instead of ID Manual selection, add $200/class surcharge. Average timeline from filing to registration: 11-12 months. Consider the Madrid Protocol for international protection across 120+ countries through a single application. [src5] [src7]

**Verify**: Name selected; domain registered; trademark search clear in relevant classes; primary social handles secured (minimum: Instagram, X/Twitter, LinkedIn)
**If failed**: If all 5 candidates fail clearance, generate a new batch focused on invented/coined names — these have the highest clearance rate because they do not exist as dictionary words. Avoid 4-5 letter dictionary words entirely (near 100% trademark saturation). [src7]

### Step 3: Design Logo and Visual Identity

**Duration**: 1-4 weeks (varies by path)
**Tool**: Path A: Looka ($20-$96) | Path B: Fiverr ($50-$2,200) or 99designs ($299-$1,314) | Path C: Figma (free) | Path D: Agency

**Path A (DIY / AI-Generated)**: Use Looka — enter brand name and preferences, select from AI-generated options. Download logo files (SVG, PNG). Use Canva Brand Kit to set up colors and fonts. Total: $20-$96. Quality is functional but may lack distinctiveness. [src3]

**Path B (Freelancer)**: Post on Fiverr (budget: $150-$500 for quality logo) or run a 99designs contest ($299-$1,314). Brief the designer with your positioning statement from Step 1 plus 5-10 visual references (logos you admire, style mood board). Request 3-5 concepts in initial round, select 1-2 for refinement through 2-3 revision rounds. Platform fees add 5-20% to posted prices. [src3]

**Path C (Designer DIY)**: Open Figma (free Starter plan). Design logo using vector tools. Select typefaces from Google Fonts (free, web-optimized). Build component library for consistent usage across touchpoints. [src8]

**Path D (Agency)**: Brief agency with positioning document from Step 1. Expect 2-3 concept presentations over 3-4 weeks. Agency handles all production files and guidelines. Budget $5,000-$25,000 for logo alone; $20,000-$100,000+ for full identity system. [src2]

For all paths, define the color system:

```
Color System Template:
──────────────────────
Primary:    [1-2 brand colors — hex, RGB, CMYK values]
Secondary:  [2-3 supporting colors]
Neutral:    [2-3 grays/neutrals for text and backgrounds]
Functional: Success (#22C55E), Warning (#F59E0B), Error (#EF4444)

Contrast Validation (WCAG AA minimum):
  Primary on white: [ratio] — must be ≥ 4.5:1
  Primary on dark:  [ratio] — must be ≥ 4.5:1
  Check at: webaim.org/resources/contrastchecker/
```

Select typography: one heading font (distinctive) + one body font (readable). Maximum 2 typefaces total. Define a type scale: body (16px), small (14px), h1 (36-48px), h2 (24-30px), h3 (20-24px).

Logo deliverables checklist:
- SVG (vector, scalable)
- PNG at 1200px, 512px, 256px, 64px, 32px (favicon)
- Dark background version
- Single-color version (for embossing/engraving)
- Social profile square crop (400x400px minimum)

**Verify**: Logo works at 16px (favicon) and 1200px (banner); all color combinations pass WCAG AA (4.5:1 for text, 3:1 for large text); type scale defined with heading + body fonts
**If failed**: If logo does not work at small sizes, simplify — remove detail, increase stroke weight, reduce to single mark. If colors fail accessibility, adjust lightness values while keeping hue. [src8]

### Step 4: Build Messaging Framework

**Duration**: 3-5 days
**Tool**: Google Docs or Notion

Write three versions of the brand story at increasing detail: elevator pitch (30 seconds / 50 words), boilerplate (100 words), and long-form narrative (300-500 words). Define 3-5 key messages, each with a headline, supporting copy, and proof point. Create a messaging matrix — messages adapted by audience: customers, investors, partners, media. [src6]

Define brand voice guidelines — distinguish between voice (consistent personality) and tone (contextual adaptation):

```
Messaging Framework:
────────────────────
Elevator Pitch (50 words max): ___
Boilerplate (100 words): ___
Long-form Narrative (300-500 words): ___

Key Messages:
  1. [Headline] — [Supporting copy] — [Proof point]
  2. [Headline] — [Supporting copy] — [Proof point]
  3. [Headline] — [Supporting copy] — [Proof point]

Voice (3 descriptors): [e.g., confident, clear, warm]
Tone Rules:
  Marketing: [enthusiastic, benefit-focused]
  Support: [empathetic, solution-oriented]
  Investor: [data-driven, visionary]
  Press: [authoritative, quotable]

Vocabulary:
  We say: [build, empower, simplify]
  We never say: [leverage, synergy, disrupt]
  Contractions: [yes/no]
  Emoji policy: [when appropriate/never]
```

62% of brands plan to invest in AI-assisted communication by 2026 — include AI voice instructions in guidelines so AI tools produce on-brand content. [src6]

**Verify**: Elevator pitch passes 5-second comprehension test (show to 10 strangers — 7+ should be able to explain what the company does); all claims have proof points; voice guidelines include 3+ "we say / we never say" examples
**If failed**: If strangers cannot explain the business after hearing the pitch, the positioning is unclear — return to Step 1. [src6]

### Step 5: Assemble Brand Guidelines Document

**Duration**: 2-5 days
**Tool**: Path A/B: Canva (brand kit template) | Path C: Figma | Path D: Agency deliverable

Compile all identity elements into a single shareable document (PDF, 15-30 pages). This is the single most important deliverable — it prevents brand drift and enables consistent execution by anyone on the team, including non-designers. [src8]

```
Brand Guidelines Structure:
───────────────────────────
1. Brand Story & Positioning (2-3 pages)
   — Mission, vision, values, positioning statement
2. Logo Usage (3-4 pages)
   — Clear space rules, minimum size, do's and don'ts
   — Approved variations (full, icon, monochrome)
   — Forbidden treatments (stretch, recolor, add effects)
3. Color Palette (2-3 pages)
   — Primary, secondary, functional with hex/RGB/CMYK
   — Approved color combinations with contrast ratios
4. Typography (2-3 pages)
   — Type scale (h1-h6, body, caption)
   — Font files/links and fallback stack
   — Line height and spacing rules
5. Imagery & Photography Style (2-3 pages)
   — Mood, composition, filter/treatment guidelines
   — Stock photo do's and don'ts
6. Voice & Tone (2-3 pages)
   — Voice descriptors, tone spectrum, vocabulary rules
   — AI voice instructions for content generation tools
7. Applications (3-5 pages)
   — Business card layout, email signature, social templates
   — Pitch deck title slide, letterhead, invoice
```

Store guidelines on a cloud platform (Google Drive, Notion, or dedicated tools like Frontify) so designers, marketers, and external partners can access them. Canva Pro Brand Kit allows up to 1,000 brand kits with uploaded logos, brand colors, and brand fonts for automated template consistency. [src8]

**Verify**: Non-designer team member can use guidelines to create a consistent social media post without asking for help; guidelines cover all 7 sections above
**If failed**: Guidelines are too complex — simplify to essential rules with clear visual examples. If nobody reads the guidelines, create a 1-page "brand cheat sheet" with the 5 most critical rules. [src2]

### Step 6: Launch Brand Assets and Secure All Touchpoints

**Duration**: 1-3 days
**Tool**: Social platforms, email provider, design tool (Canva/Figma)

Set up social media profiles with consistent branding: profile photo (logo mark, 400x400px), banner image (branded, platform-specific dimensions), bio text (elevator pitch). Create email signature template using brand colors and logo. Design pitch deck title slide using brand identity. Prepare press kit: logo package (ZIP), boilerplate, founder photos. [src1]

```
Launch Checklist:
─────────────────
[ ] Website — logo, colors, fonts, messaging applied
[ ] LinkedIn company page — profile, banner, about section
[ ] Instagram — profile photo, bio, link
[ ] X/Twitter — profile, banner, bio
[ ] Email signature — all team members updated
[ ] Pitch deck — title slide uses brand identity
[ ] Press kit — ZIP with logos, boilerplate, photos
[ ] Brand guidelines — distributed to all team members
[ ] Canva Brand Kit / Figma library — shared with team
[ ] Google Drive / Notion — guidelines + assets folder shared
```

Distribute brand guidelines to all team members with explicit instruction: "Use this for every customer-facing material." Assign one person as "brand police" for the first 90 days to catch inconsistencies early. [src1]

**Verify**: All touchpoints (website, social, email, pitch deck) use consistent logo, colors, and tone; all team members have access to guidelines and asset files
**If failed**: Inconsistency detected — identify rogue materials and update; create a Slack channel or shared doc for brand questions during the first 90 days

## Error Handling

<!-- Common failures and recovery actions. Agents need this to handle
     execution errors without human intervention. -->

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| All name candidates fail trademark search | Common words in saturated categories | Generate invented/coined names (Spotify-style) — highest clearance rate. Avoid 4-5 letter dictionary words entirely. [src7] |
| Desired .com domain taken | Popular word or phrase | Try .io/.co for tech startups; add prefix "get", "try", "use" (e.g., getlooka.com); do not pay >$500 for premium domains at early stage [src4] |
| Stakeholders cannot agree on logo direction | Too many decision-makers or unclear brief | Reduce to 2 voters; re-run Step 1 positioning alignment; use forced-rank voting [src2] |
| Designer delivers off-brand concepts | Positioning brief not shared or unclear | Re-brief with "brand is / brand is not" matrix and 5+ visual reference examples |
| Brand colors fail WCAG accessibility | Colors too similar in lightness | Use WebAIM contrast checker; adjust lightness values while preserving hue; test all color pairs |
| Freelancer misses deadline | Scope creep or underestimated complexity | Set milestone payments tied to deliverables; have backup freelancer identified; cap revision rounds at 3 [src2] |
| Logo looks blurry at small sizes | Too much detail in the mark | Create simplified "icon" version for favicon/app icon; reduce to single shape |
| USPTO surcharge ($200/class) applied | Used free-text goods description instead of ID Manual | Re-file using terms from the USPTO ID Manual to avoid surcharge [src5] |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Designing a logo before defining brand strategy
Without positioning, designers create something "pretty" that does not communicate anything meaningful. The logo then gets redesigned when strategy is finally articulated, wasting 30-50% of the initial design investment. This is the single most common and expensive mistake in startup branding. [src4]

### Correct: Complete brand positioning brief before any visual work
The brief constrains creative exploration in productive ways — designers produce better work with clear strategic direction than with a blank canvas. Spend 3-5 days on Step 1 before opening any design tool. [src1]

### Wrong: Involving 8+ people in brand approval decisions
Large committees produce mediocre compromise brands. Every additional stakeholder reduces distinctiveness and extends timeline by ~30%. The result is a "Frankenstein" brand that satisfies no one and stands for nothing. [src2]

### Correct: Limit brand approvals to 2-3 stakeholders maximum
One person owns the final decision (usually CEO), with 1-2 trusted advisors providing input. Everyone else is informed after decisions are made, not consulted during. [src1]

### Wrong: Skipping trademark search to save time or money
Launching with an infringing name means cease-and-desist letters, forced rebranding (10-50x original investment), and destroyed brand equity built over months of marketing. This is the single highest-risk branding shortcut. In 2025-2026, 4-5 letter dictionary word trademarks are nearly 100% saturated. [src7]

### Correct: Run trademark and domain checks on every name candidate before falling in love
Budget $350 for USPTO filing per class. Register domain within 48 hours of name selection. Use the Madrid Protocol for international protection if targeting multiple markets. [src5] [src7]

## When This Matters

Use when a startup founder or agent needs to actually build a brand identity — not plan one, but produce the tangible deliverables (name, logo, colors, messaging, guidelines) ready for deployment. Requires a target customer profile and product definition as inputs; produces a complete brand kit as output. Especially critical before GTM launch (brand must exist before marketing), fundraising rounds (investors judge brand quality as a proxy for execution quality), or hiring (candidates evaluate brand professionalism when considering offers).
