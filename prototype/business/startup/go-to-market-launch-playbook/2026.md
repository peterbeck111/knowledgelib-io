# 90-Day Go-to-Market Launch Recipe

## Purpose

This recipe produces an activated, measurable pipeline engine within 90 days — with validated primary and secondary channels, documented CAC by channel, 3x+ pipeline coverage against quarterly revenue target, and a repeatable GTM playbook ready to hand off to the next hire. It outputs a channel performance scorecard with real conversion rates, pipeline velocity data, and cost benchmarks that prove (or disprove) your go-to-market motion before you scale spend. [src1]

## Prerequisites
<!-- Agents: verify ALL prerequisites before executing. Missing prerequisites = failed execution. -->

- [ ] **ICP definition** with firmographics, pain points, and buying triggers — [Customer Discovery Playbook](/business/startup/customer-discovery-playbook/2026)
- [ ] **Pricing model** validated with at least 10 prospects — [Pricing Experimentation Playbook](/business/startup/pricing-experimentation-playbook/2026)
- [ ] **Working product** with at least 5 design partner users actively using it
- [ ] **Website or landing page** live with clear value proposition and CTA
- [ ] **CRM account** (HubSpot free or equivalent) with pipeline stages configured
- [ ] **Analytics** instrumented — attribution tracking requires data from day one

## Constraints
<!-- Hard rules. Agents: enforce throughout execution. Violating these = burned budget or broken pipeline. -->

- Phase 1 (channel selection) cannot be skipped — launching all channels simultaneously is the #1 GTM budget killer for startups. The average company runs 5 core channels, but only after validation. [src3]
- Do not activate more than 2 channels in the first 60 days. Adding channels increases complexity without improving fundamentals. [src1]
- No channel spend until tracking is confirmed working — unattributed pipeline is wasted learning. [src3]
- Pipeline coverage target is 3:1 pipeline-to-quota ratio. Teams with accurate pipelines achieve 28% higher revenue growth YoY. [src4]
- Kill any channel that cannot demonstrate pipeline influence after 90 days of investment. [src3]
- CAC has surged 40-60% between 2023 and 2025 — historical benchmarks underestimate current costs. [src2]

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Align Sales Motion to ACV and Set Pipeline Math

**Duration**: 3-5 days
**Tool**: Spreadsheet + CRM

Map ACV to recommended motion: under $5K = PLG; $5K-$25K = hybrid; over $25K = outbound. 71% of early-stage companies use sales-led because most B2B products have ACV above $10K. [src5]

Calculate pipeline targets using this formula:

```
Monthly pipeline needed = (quarterly revenue target / win rate) × coverage ratio

Example: $300K quarterly target / 20% win rate × 3.5 coverage = $5.25M pipeline needed

Win rate benchmarks:
  PLG trial-to-paid:        32% average, 56% top performers (AI-native)
  Outbound win rate:         20% average, 40%+ elite teams
  Inbound win rate:          25-35% average

Sales cycle benchmarks:
  SMB (ACV <$5K):           30-90 days (median 40 days)
  Mid-market ($5K-$100K):   60-120 days
  Enterprise ($100K+):       170+ days
```

**Verify**: Pipeline math documented with specific monthly pipeline creation target and win rate assumption
**If failed**: If win rate is unknown, use 20% (outbound) or 32% (PLG) as starting assumption and adjust after 60 days of data

### Step 2: Select and Score Channels

**Duration**: 3-5 days
**Tool**: Spreadsheet

Select one primary and one secondary channel based on motion path. Score each candidate channel by expected efficiency. Use these 2025-2026 CAC benchmarks for initial modeling: [src2]

```
CAC by Channel (B2B SaaS, 2025):
  Referral programs:         $150  (best ROI but low volume)
  Organic SEO / content:     $480-$942  (compounds over time, $290 once established)
  Outbound (email/LinkedIn): $267-$400  (SDR-driven: $1,980)
  Paid search (Google Ads):  $802  (B2B SaaS range: $1,200-$2,000)
  Paid social (LinkedIn):    $982  ($200-$250 cost per company influenced)
  Paid social (Facebook):    $230  (limited B2B targeting)

Channel efficiency score = (estimated pipeline per month × win rate) / monthly cost
```

For each selected channel, define: target audience, weekly activity targets, expected lead volume, and measurement criteria.

**Verify**: Channel selection matrix with expected pipeline contribution and cost estimate per channel
**If failed**: If no channel scores positively, revisit ACV and pricing — the channel is rarely the real problem [src3]

### Step 3: Build Tracking Infrastructure

**Duration**: 5-7 days
**Tool**: CRM (HubSpot/Salesforce), analytics (PostHog/GA4)

```
Pipeline stages (configure in CRM):
  Lead → MQL → SQL → Opportunity → Proposal → Closed Won / Lost

Attribution setup:
  1. UTM parameters on all campaign links
  2. Lead source tracking (first-touch and multi-touch)
  3. Meeting source attribution
  4. Pipeline creation source tagging

Weekly dashboard metrics:
  - Leads by channel (volume + cost per lead)
  - MQL-to-SQL conversion (benchmark: 18-22%, top: 25-35%)  [src7]
  - Pipeline created ($) by source
  - Pipeline velocity (days per stage)
```

Build ICP target list: 200-500 accounts for outbound; persona definitions for PLG. For outbound, use Apollo.io or LinkedIn Sales Navigator to build named account lists.

**Verify**: Test pipeline stages by creating and advancing a test lead. Dashboard shows data within 24 hours.
**If failed**: If CRM integration does not pass data, switch to manual tracking in spreadsheet for first 2 weeks while debugging

### Step 4: Activate Primary Channel

**Duration**: 2-4 weeks to launch, then ongoing
**Tool**: Per motion path (see Tool Selection)

Execute the primary channel with these motion-specific targets: [src1] [src4]

**PATH A (PLG)**:
- Launch free trial or freemium tier
- Target: 5-15% signup-to-activation rate; top performers reach 65%+
- Optimize onboarding for value delivery in under 5 minutes
- Weekly metrics: signups, activation rate, trial-to-paid conversion

**PATH B (Hybrid)**:
- Launch product trial AND outbound sequences simultaneously
- Outbound: 50-100 personalized emails/week; target 5.8% reply rate (2025 average, down from 6.8% in 2024)
- Timeline-based email hooks achieve 10% reply rate vs 4.4% for problem-statement hooks
- Trial: monitor activation; outbound drives qualified trial signups

**PATH C (Outbound)**:
- Send 50-100 personalized emails/week via Apollo/Instantly
- Add LinkedIn touchpoints: multichannel (email + phone + LinkedIn) increases engagement 287% vs single-channel
- Target: 5-10 qualified meetings/week
- Qualify leads within 24 hours — 5-minute response = 8x higher conversion

**PATH D (Community-Led)**:
- Launch or join relevant developer community (Discord, Slack, GitHub)
- Publish 2-4 technical content pieces/week
- Present at 2-3 meetups or virtual events/month
- Target: community members to trial conversion

Spend minimum 4 weeks on primary channel before evaluating. Most channels need 60+ days to show meaningful results. [src3]

**Verify**: Primary channel producing measurable lead flow by week 4. Leading indicators (emails sent, content published, signups) trending upward weekly.
**If failed**: If zero pipeline after 4 weeks of consistent effort, diagnose messaging first (A/B test subject lines, CTAs). If messaging validated and still zero, channel may be wrong for your buyer — switch to alternative from tool selection table.

### Step 5: Activate Secondary Channel and Build Pipeline

**Duration**: Weeks 5-8 (start 2-3 weeks after primary)
**Tool**: Per motion path secondary channel

Launch secondary channel only after primary is running and producing some signal. Apply messaging that performed best in primary channel. Set initial targets at 50% of primary channel targets.

Cross-pollinate channels: use outbound to drive trial signups (hybrid), use content to warm outbound targets, use community discussions to inform content topics.

Run weekly pipeline reviews from week 5: [src4]
- New pipeline created (count and dollar value)
- Pipeline advanced (stage progression)
- Pipeline lost (deals stalled or closed-lost)
- Pipeline velocity: days in each stage

Qualify using BANT for SMB or MEDDPICC for enterprise. Only count opportunities with identified budget, timeline, and decision-maker as pipeline. Average B2B deal now involves 6.3 stakeholders — map the buying committee early. [src1]

**Verify**: At least one channel producing qualified pipeline by week 6; pipeline coverage above 2x
**If failed**: If leads come but don't convert to pipeline — messaging or qualification issue, not channel issue. If zero qualified leads after 6 weeks of active execution across 2 channels — revisit ICP and product-market fit before spending more.

### Step 6: Calculate CAC, Optimize, and Validate

**Duration**: Weeks 9-11
**Tool**: CRM + spreadsheet

Calculate CAC per channel after 6-8 weeks of data: [src2] [src6]

```
CAC per channel = total channel spend / customers acquired from channel
Blended CAC    = total GTM spend / total new customers
LTV:CAC ratio  = customer LTV / blended CAC (target: 3:1 or higher)
CAC payback    = CAC / monthly gross margin per customer (target: <12 months)

Healthy benchmarks (2025-2026):
  LTV:CAC ratio:         3:1 minimum, 4:1-6:1 top quartile
  CAC payback:           <12 months for SaaS
  Pipeline coverage:     3:1 minimum, 3.5:1 target
  Top quartile:          $1.00 to acquire $1 new ARR
  Bottom quartile:       $2.82 to acquire $1 new ARR
```

Identify the biggest conversion drop-off between pipeline stages. A/B test messaging at that bottleneck. Companies reducing sales cycles to 30-45 days achieve 38% higher velocity than those in the 76-90 day range. [src7]

**Verify**: At least one channel has CAC under LTV/3; pipeline velocity is measurable and improving
**If failed**: If all channels have LTV:CAC below 1.5:1, the problem is likely pricing, not channels. If CAC is borderline (2:1 to 3:1), continue optimizing for 2-4 more weeks before declaring.

### Step 7: Scale Winner and Document Playbook

**Duration**: Weeks 12-13
**Tool**: CRM, documentation tool

Increase investment in highest-efficiency channel by 50-100%. Hire or contract for specific skills needed: SDR for outbound ($60K-$120K/year), content writer for SEO ($80K-$150K/year). Automate repeatable tasks: email sequences, lead routing, weekly reporting.

Document the complete GTM playbook:
- Exact sequences and templates that work
- Targeting criteria and ICP refinements
- Benchmark metrics: response rates, conversion rates, cycle times, CAC
- Onboarding guide for next GTM hire
- Quarterly pipeline model: target pipeline = (revenue goal / win rate) x 3.5

**Output files**:
- `channel-scorecard.csv` — CAC, LTV:CAC, payback, pipeline contribution per channel
- `gtm-playbook.md` — Documented sequences, templates, targeting, benchmarks
- `quarterly-plan.md` — Next quarter pipeline model with hiring and budget requirements

**Verify**: 3x+ pipeline coverage achieved; at least one channel producing pipeline predictably; playbook documented
**If failed**: If pipeline coverage is 2-3x, continue optimizing for 2-4 more weeks. If below 2x after 13 weeks, reassess product-market fit.

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Zero replies after 200+ outbound emails | Email deliverability issue or targeting mismatch | Check domain warmup, SPF/DKIM records; test different ICP segment; try timeline-based hooks (10% reply rate vs 4.4%) [src1] |
| High lead volume but zero pipeline conversion | MQL definition too loose or product demo fails to convert | Tighten MQL criteria; audit demo flow; survey lost deals for objection patterns |

| Secondary channel cannibalized primary | Attribution overlap without proper tracking | Implement multi-touch attribution; separate campaigns with distinct UTMs |
| CRM data is unreliable after week 4 | Reps not updating pipeline stages | Enforce weekly pipeline hygiene review; automate stage updates where possible |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Launching 5+ channels simultaneously on day one
Spreading budget and attention across many channels guarantees none get enough investment to generate signal. B2B deals now require 62 touchpoints across minimum 3 channels and 192 days from first touch to close — but you build those touchpoints sequentially, not all at once. [src1] [src3]

### Correct: Activate one primary channel, add one secondary after 2-3 weeks
Focus creates signal. You need 4-6 weeks of concentrated effort on a single channel before you can measure whether it works. The average mature company runs 5+ channels, but they validated them one at a time.

### Wrong: Choosing PLG because it seems cheaper when ACV is over $25K
PLG works for high-volume, low-ACV products. Enterprise buyers expect sales engagement and involve 6.3 stakeholders on average — forcing them through self-serve creates friction, not savings. [src5] [src1]

### Correct: Match motion to ACV — PLG under $5K, hybrid $5K-$25K, outbound over $25K
Motion-market fit matters as much as product-market fit. There is no correlation between GTM motion and growth rate — every motion works if matched to the right ACV and buyer profile. [src3]

### Wrong: Declaring a channel "doesn't work" after 2-3 weeks
Content marketing ROI is 748% but takes approximately 9 months to break even. Outbound needs 100+ touches to generate statistically meaningful data. [src1]

### Correct: Commit to 60-90 day evaluation windows with leading and lagging indicators
Set leading indicator targets (emails sent, content published, signups) for weeks 1-4, and lagging indicator targets (pipeline created, deals closed) for weeks 5-12. [src3]

## When This Matters

Use when a startup has a working product and defined ICP but has not yet built a repeatable pipeline generation process. This recipe executes the GTM launch — it does not plan it. Requires ICP definition, validated pricing, and a working product as inputs. Produces a validated pipeline engine with documented CAC as output. Critical for post-seed companies demonstrating traction for Series A, or bootstrapped companies transitioning from founder-led sales to scalable GTM.
