# When to Choose Epicor Kinetic

## Definition

Epicor Kinetic is a cloud ERP purpose-built for discrete and mixed-mode manufacturers in the mid-market ($50M-$750M revenue), supporting make-to-order, make-to-stock, engineer-to-order, project-centric, job shop, repetitive, and batch processing manufacturing modes. [src1] Named a Leader in the 2025 Gartner Magic Quadrant for Cloud ERP for Product-Centric Enterprises for the third consecutive year, Kinetic offers native manufacturing execution, supply chain management, and shop floor control that generic ERPs like NetSuite or SAP Business One cannot match without significant customization. [src2]

## Key Properties

- **Manufacturing modes**: Make-to-order, make-to-stock, engineer-to-order, project-centric, job shop, repetitive, and batch processing — all supported natively [src5]
- **Target market**: Discrete manufacturers with $50M-$750M annual revenue; strong in two-tier ERP scenarios [src1]
- **Pricing**: Starting at ~$80/user/month; total implementation cost typically $100K-$500K [src1]
- **AI capabilities**: Epicor Prism module provides agentic AI for supplier communications, RFQ automation, and onboarding acceleration [src2]
- **Gartner recognition**: Leader in Cloud ERP for Product-Centric Enterprises for three consecutive years (2023-2025) [src2]
- **Native capabilities**: Built-in mobile access, analytics, and shop floor dashboards without third-party dependencies [src4]

## Constraints
<!-- Agents: read this section before recommending this concept/framework.
     These are hard boundaries on when and how it applies. -->

- Purpose-built for discrete and mixed-mode manufacturing — not suitable for process manufacturing (food, chemicals, pharma) where recipe/batch management, allergen tracking, and shelf-life control are required [src5]
- Steep learning curve reported by users due to extensive customization and configuration capabilities [src2]
- Financial management module is functional but less comprehensive than Sage Intacct or NetSuite for organizations where accounting is the primary driver [src3]
- CRM and e-commerce capabilities are limited compared to full-suite ERPs like NetSuite [src3]
- Training resources are reported as needing improvement — organizations should budget for extended training programs [src2]

## Application Checklist

### Step 1: Confirm discrete/mixed-mode manufacturing fit
- **Inputs needed**: Manufacturing mode(s), product complexity, production volume, shop floor requirements
- **Output**: Manufacturing mode compatibility assessment — Epicor should support all production modes natively
- **Constraint**: If the organization performs process manufacturing (recipes, formulations, batch tracking), Epicor is the wrong platform — evaluate Infor M3-based editions instead [src5]

### Step 2: Assess total cost within budget range
- **Inputs needed**: User count, module requirements, implementation complexity, current systems to migrate
- **Output**: Total cost estimate ($100K-$500K for implementation + ongoing subscription)
- **Constraint**: If total first-year budget is under $80K, Epicor may be too expensive — consider SAP Business One or Acumatica Manufacturing Edition [src1]

### Step 3: Evaluate manufacturing depth vs breadth trade-off
- **Inputs needed**: Requirements for CRM, e-commerce, HR, financial reporting alongside manufacturing
- **Output**: Gap analysis showing where Epicor needs supplemental systems vs where NetSuite provides broader coverage
- **Constraint**: If more than 40% of critical requirements are non-manufacturing (CRM, e-commerce, advanced financials), a full-suite ERP like NetSuite provides better overall value [src3]

### Step 4: Plan for learning curve and training
- **Inputs needed**: Current user technical proficiency, change management readiness, training budget
- **Output**: Training plan with timeline and budget allocation
- **Constraint**: Users consistently report steep learning curves — budget for 2-4 weeks of structured training per user group and plan a phased rollout [src2]

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Choosing Epicor for a process manufacturer
A food or chemical manufacturer selects Epicor Kinetic because of its manufacturing reputation, then discovers it lacks recipe management, allergen tracking, catch-weight processing, and shelf-life management — core requirements for process manufacturing. [src5]

### Correct: Matching manufacturing mode to ERP platform
Discrete and mixed-mode manufacturers choose Epicor Kinetic. Process manufacturers (food, chemicals, pharma) choose Infor CloudSuite with M3 engine. Validate manufacturing mode fit before engaging implementation partners. [src5]

### Wrong: Selecting SAP Business One for complex manufacturing
A mid-market manufacturer ($100M revenue) with complex BOMs, multi-level routing, and shop floor scheduling selects SAP Business One for its SAP brand. SAP Business One's manufacturing module is designed for simpler operations and cannot handle this complexity without extensive customization. [src3]

### Correct: Using SAP Business One for simpler manufacturing, Epicor for complex
SAP Business One fits smaller manufacturers with simpler production requirements. Epicor Kinetic is the right choice when production complexity demands native MRP, scheduling, quality management, and shop floor control. [src3]

### Wrong: Underestimating the learning curve
An organization deploys Epicor Kinetic with minimal training, expecting users to learn on the job. Adoption stalls, workarounds emerge, and the system delivers only 30-50% of its potential value. [src2]

### Correct: Investing in structured training and phased rollout
Budget for comprehensive training (2-4 weeks per user group), designate super-users in each department, and deploy in phases starting with the module where the team has the strongest buy-in. [src4]

## Common Misconceptions

- **Misconception**: Epicor Kinetic is only for small job shops.
  **Reality**: While Epicor has strong roots in job shops, Kinetic serves the full $50M-$750M mid-market range and supports make-to-order, make-to-stock, engineer-to-order, repetitive, and batch processing. It is also used in two-tier ERP scenarios where a corporate SAP/Oracle installation needs a manufacturing-focused subsidiary system. [src1]

- **Misconception**: Epicor Kinetic and SAP Business One are interchangeable for manufacturing.
  **Reality**: SAP Business One has a basic manufacturing module suitable for simpler operations. Epicor Kinetic offers significantly superior manufacturing capabilities including advanced MRP, multi-level scheduling, shop floor control, and quality management. They serve different complexity levels. [src3]

- **Misconception**: Epicor Kinetic is an on-premises legacy system.
  **Reality**: Kinetic is Epicor's cloud-native platform with a modern browser-based interface, built-in analytics, and native mobile access. While it evolved from Epicor ERP 10, Kinetic represents a full cloud transformation. [src4]
