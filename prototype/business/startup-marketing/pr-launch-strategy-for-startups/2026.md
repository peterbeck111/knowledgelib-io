# PR Launch Strategy for Startups

## Purpose

This recipe executes a startup launch campaign across three channels: press outreach (journalist pitching + press release distribution), Product Hunt launch, and community seeding — producing a press kit, journalist outreach tracker, optimized Product Hunt listing, and launch day timeline. The output is earned media coverage, Product Hunt ranking, and community awareness that drives traffic and signups in the first 30 days post-launch. [src4]

## Prerequisites

- [ ] **Working product or demo** — journalists and Product Hunt users need something tangible to evaluate
- [ ] **Company narrative/story** — unique angle, origin story, key metrics, market problem being solved
- [ ] **Press kit assets** — logo (PNG + SVG), founder headshots, product screenshots (5+), one-paragraph company description
- [ ] **Active website/landing page** with email capture — [Landing Page Deployment](/software/landing-pages/landing-page-platform-deployment/2026)
- [ ] **Social media profiles** set up and active — [Social Media Strategy](/business/startup-marketing/social-media-strategy-by-platform/2026)
- [ ] **Email marketing** configured — [Email Marketing Setup](/business/startup-marketing/email-marketing-setup-playbook/2026)

## Constraints

- Average journalist receives 50+ pitches daily. Generic mass pitches get under 3% response rate. Personalized pitches referencing recent articles achieve 15-25% response rates. [src3]
- Product Hunt homepage is manually curated and has become significantly more selective since 2025. Pre-launch preparation of 4-8 weeks is minimum for a competitive launch. [src2]
- Press releases must be 300-500 words. Longer releases are not read and reduce pick-up rates. [src1]
- PR agencies charge $3,000-5,000/month minimum retainer. DIY launches cost $0-500 total. Most seed-stage startups should start DIY. [src6]
- Embargoed exclusives work: 57% of journalists prefer receiving exclusives. Offer your top-tier journalist a 24-48 hour exclusive before wider distribution. [src3]

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Build the Press Kit (Weeks 1-2)

**Duration**: 4-6 hours
**Tool**: Google Docs + Canva + hosting platform

Create a comprehensive press kit that makes it effortless for journalists to write about you.

```
Press Kit Checklist:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Press release (300-500 words)
   - Headline: newsworthy angle, not product description
   - Opening paragraph: who, what, when, why, significance
   - Body: key features, market context, founder quote
   - Boilerplate: one-paragraph company description
   - Contact: PR email, founder availability

2. Company fact sheet (one page)
   - Founded date, location, team size
   - Key metrics (users, revenue, growth — only share what's public)
   - Funding status (if applicable)
   - Mission statement

3. Visual assets folder
   - Logo: PNG (transparent) + SVG + dark/light variants
   - Founder headshots: high-res (300 DPI), professional
   - Product screenshots: 5-8 showing key features
   - Product demo GIF or video (30-60 seconds)

4. Host the press kit
   - Create a /press page on your website
   - Or use Notion/Google Drive with public sharing link
   - Include direct download links for all assets
```

**Verify**: The press kit page loads correctly, all assets download, and the press release reads clearly to someone unfamiliar with your company.
**If failed**: If the press release sounds promotional rather than newsworthy, rewrite the headline as a trend or problem statement rather than a product announcement. Journalists cover trends, not product features.

### Step 2: Research and Build Journalist Target List (Weeks 2-3)

**Duration**: 6-10 hours
**Tool**: Manual research or media database (press.farm, Muck Rack)

Build a targeted list of 30-50 journalists who cover your specific space. Quality of targeting matters far more than list size.

```
Journalist Research Process:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Identify 10-15 outlets that cover your space:
   - Tier 1 (dream coverage): TechCrunch, The Verge, Wired, Bloomberg
   - Tier 2 (realistic targets): industry-specific pubs, regional tech blogs
   - Tier 3 (high probability): niche newsletters, podcast hosts, bloggers

2. For each outlet, find 2-3 journalists who:
   - Cover your specific category (not just "tech" — your subcategory)
   - Have published relevant articles in the last 3 months
   - Are actively posting on X/Twitter (signal they're engaged)

3. Build the tracker spreadsheet:
   Name | Outlet | Email | X Handle | Recent Article | Pitch Angle | Status

4. Find email addresses:
   - Check bylines and author pages on the outlet
   - Common patterns: first@outlet.com, first.last@outlet.com
   - Use Hunter.io (free: 25 lookups/mo) for verification
   - Follow on X/Twitter and engage with their content 2 weeks pre-pitch

Pitch personalization requirements:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
MINIMUM: Reference a specific article they wrote in the last 60 days
BETTER: Connect your story to a trend they've been tracking
BEST: Offer data or an angle they haven't covered yet
```

**Verify**: You have 30-50 journalists in your tracker with verified emails, recent article references, and personalized pitch angles. At least 10 are Tier 2 or Tier 3 (realistic targets).
**If failed**: If you cannot find enough journalists, broaden to adjacent categories (e.g., if you're a fintech, include journalists who cover "future of banking" or "digital payments," not just "fintech startups").

### Step 3: Prepare Product Hunt Launch (Weeks 3-5)

**Duration**: 8-15 hours over 2-3 weeks
**Tool**: Product Hunt + design tool

Product Hunt's homepage is manually curated and highly selective in 2025-2026. Preparation is critical. [src2]

```
Product Hunt Pre-Launch (4-8 weeks before):
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Create maker profile: complete bio, headshot, link social accounts
2. Engage daily: upvote 5-10 products, leave thoughtful comments
   (Build reputation before asking for support)
3. Recruit a hunter (optional but helpful):
   - Top hunters have established follower bases
   - Reach out via X/Twitter with a warm introduction
   - Or self-hunt if your maker profile has engagement history

Product Hunt Page Optimization:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Tagline: 60 chars max, customer-focused benefit (not feature list)
   Example: "Build landing pages 10x faster with AI" not "AI page builder"
2. Images: 5-8 screenshots/GIFs showing the product in action
   - First image = most important (determines click-through)
   - 1270x760px recommended resolution
3. Description: 260-character limit, lead with the value proposition
4. Topics: Select 3-5 relevant topics (choose specific over broad)
5. First comment (maker comment): Authentic founder story
   - Why you built this
   - Who it's for
   - One ask for the community

Launch Day Selection:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
- Weekday launches: More traffic, more competition
- Weekend launches: Less competition, lower traffic
- Tuesday-Thursday: Best balance for most products
- Launch at 12:01 AM PST (Product Hunt resets at midnight PST)
```

**Verify**: Product Hunt page is fully configured with tagline, images, description, topics, and maker comment drafted. At least 20 people have been personally notified about launch day.
**If failed**: If your maker profile has zero engagement history, spend 2 additional weeks actively commenting and upvoting before launch. Cold launches from new profiles rarely get featured.

### Step 4: Execute Journalist Outreach (Week 5-6)

**Duration**: 8-12 hours over 1-2 weeks
**Tool**: Email client (Gmail or dedicated email)

Send personalized pitches to your journalist list in a strategic sequence.

```
Outreach Sequence:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
Week 1: Exclusive offer to #1 target journalist
  - Offer 24-48 hour exclusive first look
  - If accepted: provide press kit + schedule founder interview
  - If no response after 48h: move to next Tier 1 journalist

Week 1-2: Personalized pitches to Tier 2 (15-20 journalists)
  - Send Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10am recipient's local time
  - Subject line: specific, newsworthy (not "Check out our startup")
  - Body: 3-4 paragraphs max
    P1: Personal hook (reference their article)
    P2: Your news in 2-3 sentences (lead with why it matters)
    P3: Key metric or proof point
    P4: Offer: demo, interview, exclusive data

Week 2: Follow-up (one follow-up only)
  - Wait 3-5 business days after initial pitch
  - Reply to original thread (not new email)
  - Keep to 2-3 sentences: "Checking if this caught your eye"
  - Add one new data point or angle

Week 2-3: Tier 3 outreach (20-30 journalists/bloggers/podcasters)
  - Can be slightly less personalized but still reference their work
  - Higher response rate expected (25-40% for niche publications)
```

**Verify**: All pitches sent, tracked in spreadsheet with dates and status. At least 5-10 positive responses or interview requests across all tiers.
**If failed**: If response rate is under 5%, audit your pitch: is the subject line specific and newsworthy? Does the first line reference their work? Is the news itself genuinely novel? Consider adjusting the angle to tie into a broader trend.

### Step 5: Execute Launch Day (Launch Week)

**Duration**: Full day (launch day) + follow-up week
**Tool**: All channels

Coordinate the launch across all channels for maximum impact.

```
Launch Day Timeline (if combining PH + press):
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
12:01 AM PST: Product Hunt goes live
  - Post maker comment immediately
  - Share PH link on X/Twitter, LinkedIn

6:00 AM: Activate supporter network
  - Send pre-prepared message to supporters: "We're live on PH!"
  - Ask for genuine reviews (not just upvotes)

8:00 AM: Press embargo lifts (if applicable)
  - Exclusive article publishes
  - Share on all social media channels
  - Send email blast to subscriber list

10:00 AM-2:00 PM: Peak engagement window
  - Respond to every PH comment within 30 minutes
  - Share on X/Twitter 2-3 times with different angles
  - Post in relevant communities (Reddit, Slack, Discord)

3:00 PM: Mid-day boost
  - Share updated metrics (upvotes, signups, press mentions)
  - Thank supporters publicly

6:00 PM-12:00 AM: Evening push
  - Share in timezone-appropriate international communities
  - Final push on social media

Post-Launch Week:
  - Day 2-3: Follow up with journalists who didn't respond
  - Day 3-5: Publish founder reflection post on LinkedIn/blog
  - Day 7: Compile results: press mentions, PH ranking, traffic, signups
  - Day 14: Thank-you notes to all journalists who covered you
  - Day 30: Post-launch analysis and next steps
```

**Verify**: Product Hunt listing is live and receiving engagement. Press coverage is published or confirmed. Social media posts are live and generating traffic. Email blast has been sent.
**If failed**: If Product Hunt engagement is low by midday, ask your network for genuine reviews (not vote manipulation). If press coverage is delayed, check with the journalist — embargoes sometimes cause scheduling issues.

## Quality Benchmarks

| Quality Metric | Minimum Acceptable | Good | Excellent |
|---------------|-------------------|------|-----------|
| Press mentions (DIY) | 2-3 articles | 5-10 articles | 15+ articles |
| Journalist response rate | > 5% | > 15% | > 25% |
| Product Hunt daily ranking | Top 10 | Top 5 | #1-3 Product of the Day |
| Product Hunt upvotes | 100+ | 300+ | 700+ |
| Launch day website traffic | 500 visitors | 2,000 visitors | 10,000+ visitors |
| Email signups from launch week | 50+ | 200+ | 1,000+ |

**If below minimum**: If press mentions are zero, audit pitch quality — the angle may not be newsworthy. If PH ranking is outside top 20, pre-launch community building was insufficient. Invest 2 more weeks in community engagement and try a re-launch with a major update.

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Zero journalist responses | Generic pitches or wrong journalist targets | Rewrite pitch with specific article reference, narrow target list to 10 perfect-fit journalists |
| Product Hunt not featured on homepage | Product didn't pass editorial review | Engage with PH community for 2+ weeks, improve listing quality, consider re-launching with a significant update |
| Press coverage but no traffic | Articles didn't include links or CTA | Follow up with journalists to add link, publish own content referencing the coverage |
| Journalist asks for exclusivity you already offered elsewhere | Multiple exclusive offers outstanding | Immediately clarify with first journalist, offer second journalist a different angle or timing |
| Product Hunt vote manipulation flagged | Too many votes from same network in short time | Stop asking for votes, focus on genuine reviews and comments, contact PH support |
| Launch day technical issues (site down) | Traffic spike exceeded hosting capacity | Pre-scale hosting before launch, use CDN, have a status page ready |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Sending identical mass pitches to 200+ journalists
Mass pitches have under 3% response rate. Journalists immediately recognize and delete template emails. Some will publicly shame bad pitches on X/Twitter, damaging your brand. [src3]

### Correct: Send 30-50 highly personalized pitches
Each pitch should reference a specific article the journalist wrote within the last 60 days and explain why your story connects to their beat. A 30-pitch campaign with 15% response rate produces more coverage than a 200-pitch blast at 2%.

### Wrong: Launching on Product Hunt without community engagement history
Cold launches from new profiles with zero engagement rarely get featured on the homepage. Product Hunt's editorial team prioritizes products from active community members. [src5]

### Correct: Invest 4-8 weeks in genuine community participation
Upvote, comment, and engage authentically on Product Hunt daily for at least 4 weeks before your launch. Build relationships with other makers and top hunters.

### Wrong: Hiring a PR agency at seed stage with $0 revenue
PR agencies require $3,000-5,000/month minimum and need 3-6 month commitments. At seed stage, this is $18,000-30,000 that could fund months of product development. [src6]

### Correct: DIY PR until product-market fit is validated
Founder-led PR produces more authentic stories and costs $0. Hire an agency only after you have revenue, a validated product, and a specific PR goal (e.g., Series A announcement) that justifies the investment.

This recipe handles the launch execution, not the product development or pre-launch validation.
