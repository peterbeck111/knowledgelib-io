PR Launch Strategy for Startups

How do I launch a startup — press outreach, Product Hunt playbook, community seeding, DIY vs agency cost benchmarks?

Type: Execution Recipe Confidence: 0.89 Sources: 7 Verified: 2026-03-12

Purpose

This recipe executes a startup launch campaign across three channels: press outreach, Product Hunt, and community seeding — producing a press kit, journalist outreach tracker, optimized Product Hunt listing, and launch day timeline. The output is earned media coverage, Product Hunt ranking, and community awareness that drives traffic and signups. [src4]

Prerequisites

  • Working product or demo — journalists and PH users need something tangible
  • Company narrative/story — unique angle, origin story, key metrics
  • Press kit assets — logo files, founder headshots, product screenshots (5+)
  • Active website/landing page with email capture
  • Social media profiles set up and active
  • Email marketing configured for subscriber capture

Constraints

  • Journalists receive 50+ pitches daily. Generic mass pitches get under 3% response. Personalized pitches achieve 15-25%. [src3]
  • Product Hunt homepage is manually curated and selective since 2025. Pre-launch prep of 4-8 weeks minimum. [src2]
  • Press releases must be 300-500 words maximum. [src1]
  • PR agencies charge $3,000-5,000/month minimum. Most seed-stage startups should DIY. [src6]
  • 57% of journalists prefer receiving exclusives. Offer top-tier journalist 24-48h exclusive. [src3]

Execution Flow

Step 1: Build the Press Kit (Weeks 1-2)

Duration: 4-6 hours · Tool: Google Docs + Canva + hosting platform

Create press release (300-500 words with newsworthy headline), company fact sheet, visual assets folder (logo, headshots, screenshots), and host on a /press page or shared drive.

Verify: Press kit page loads, assets download, press release reads clearly to outsiders. · If failed: Rewrite headline as a trend statement rather than product announcement.

Step 2: Research and Build Journalist Target List (Weeks 2-3)

Duration: 6-10 hours · Tool: Manual research or media database

Build a targeted list of 30-50 journalists across 3 tiers: dream outlets (Tier 1), realistic targets (Tier 2), high-probability niche (Tier 3). Each entry needs verified email, recent article reference, and personalized pitch angle. [src3]

Verify: 30-50 journalists with verified emails and personalized angles. · If failed: Broaden to adjacent categories.

Step 3: Prepare Product Hunt Launch (Weeks 3-5)

Duration: 8-15 hours over 2-3 weeks · Tool: Product Hunt + design tool

Pre-launch (4-8 weeks): build maker profile, engage daily, recruit hunter. Optimize page: tagline (60 chars), 5-8 images (1270x760px), description (260 chars), 3-5 topics, maker comment. Launch Tuesday-Thursday at 12:01 AM PST. [src2] [src5]

Verify: PH page fully configured, 20+ people notified of launch day. · If failed: Spend 2 more weeks in community engagement before launching.

Step 4: Execute Journalist Outreach (Weeks 5-6)

Duration: 8-12 hours · Tool: Email

Send exclusive to #1 target first (24-48h window). Then personalized pitches to Tier 2 (Tue-Thu 8-10am). One follow-up after 3-5 business days. Tier 3 outreach in parallel.

Verify: All pitches sent and tracked. 5-10 positive responses. · If failed: If response rate < 5%, audit pitch personalization and newsworthiness of angle.

Step 5: Execute Launch Day

Duration: Full day + follow-up week · Tool: All channels

12:01 AM PST: PH goes live + maker comment. 6 AM: activate supporter network. 8 AM: embargo lifts, press publishes, email blast. 10 AM-2 PM: peak engagement (respond to every PH comment within 30 min). Post-launch: follow up with journalists, write reflection post, compile results at day 7 and day 30.

Verify: PH listing live and engaging, press coverage published, social and email blasts sent. · If failed: Focus on genuine PH reviews if engagement is low; check with journalists on delayed coverage.

Anti-Patterns

Wrong: Mass-pitching 200+ journalists with identical emails

Mass pitches have under 3% response rate. Journalists recognize and delete template emails immediately. [src3]

Correct: Send 30-50 highly personalized pitches

Reference a specific article from the last 60 days. A 30-pitch campaign at 15% response rate beats 200 pitches at 2%.

Wrong: Hiring a PR agency at seed stage with $0 revenue

$3,000-5,000/month for 3-6 months = $18,000-30,000 that could fund product development. [src6]

Correct: DIY PR until product-market fit is validated

Founder-led PR produces more authentic stories. Hire an agency only after revenue and a specific PR goal justify the investment.

When This Matters

Use this recipe when a startup is ready to launch publicly and needs a structured plan for press outreach, Product Hunt, and community seeding. Requires a working product, compelling narrative, and basic marketing infrastructure already in place.
