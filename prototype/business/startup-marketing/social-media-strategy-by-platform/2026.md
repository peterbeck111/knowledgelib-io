# Social Media Strategy by Platform for Early-Stage Startups

## Purpose

This recipe produces a complete social media launch strategy for an early-stage startup: platform selection (1-2 platforms based on B2B vs B2C model), content pillar definition, posting cadence by platform, tool setup, and a 30-day content calendar ready for execution. The output is a configured social media management tool with scheduled posts and reusable templates — not a document about social media theory. [src1]

## Prerequisites

- [ ] **ICP definition** available from persona research — who is the target audience, where do they spend time online
- [ ] **Brand voice guidelines** — tone, vocabulary, and examples of on-brand communication
- [ ] **Buffer account** — sign up at [Buffer](https://buffer.com/signup) (free tier: 3 channels, 10 scheduled posts each)
- [ ] **Canva account** — sign up at [Canva](https://www.canva.com/signup) (free tier: 250,000+ templates)
- [ ] **Business social media profiles** created on target platforms (LinkedIn company page, X/Twitter account, Instagram business account, etc.)

## Constraints

- Early-stage startups must focus on 1-2 platforms maximum. Spreading across 5+ platforms dilutes quality and burns founder time. [src1]
- Minimum viable posting cadence is 3 posts per week. Below that threshold, platform algorithms deprioritize content and audience retention drops. [src2]
- Organic social media takes 90+ days of consistent posting to show measurable traction. Do not evaluate ROI before the 90-day mark. [src3]
- B2B LinkedIn posts have a 24-48 hour engagement window; X/Twitter posts decay in 2-4 hours. Cadence must match platform velocity. [src6]
- Budget $0-50/month for tools in the first 6 months. Do not invest in paid social until organic content-market fit is validated.

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Select Your 1-2 Primary Platforms

**Duration**: 30 minutes
**Tool**: Decision matrix

Apply the platform selection matrix based on your ICP and business model. The goal is maximum audience overlap with minimum effort.

```
B2B Platform Selection Matrix:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
Primary (pick 1):
  LinkedIn — 97% of B2B marketers use it, 3.1x ROI for lead gen [src7]

Secondary (pick 0-1 based on capacity):
  X/Twitter — fast engagement loop, good for dev/tech audiences
  YouTube — long shelf life, search-driven discovery, high effort

B2C Platform Selection Matrix:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
Primary (pick 1):
  Instagram — 420% average ROI by 2026, visual-first [src4]
  TikTok — highest organic reach for new accounts, video-only

Secondary (pick 0-1 based on capacity):
  Facebook — largest user base (3.07B), best retargeting, older demo
  Pinterest — high purchase intent, long content shelf life
```

**Verify**: You have selected exactly 1-2 platforms with clear rationale tied to your ICP's demographics and content consumption habits.
**If failed**: If you cannot decide, default to LinkedIn (B2B) or Instagram (B2C). These have the broadest reach and most forgiving algorithms for beginners.

### Step 2: Define 3-4 Content Pillars

**Duration**: 45 minutes
**Tool**: Document editor

Content pillars are the 3-4 recurring themes that anchor all your posts. Every post should map to exactly one pillar.

```
Example Content Pillars (B2B SaaS):
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. EDUCATE    — Industry insights, how-to threads, data points
              Ratio: 40% of posts
2. PROVE      — Customer stories, case studies, metrics
              Ratio: 25% of posts
3. ENGAGE     — Polls, questions, hot takes, trend commentary
              Ratio: 20% of posts
4. PROMOTE    — Product updates, launches, offers
              Ratio: 15% of posts

Example Content Pillars (B2C DTC):
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. SHOWCASE   — Product photos, lifestyle imagery, UGC reposts
              Ratio: 35% of posts
2. EDUCATE    — How-to use, tips, ingredient/material deep dives
              Ratio: 25% of posts
3. ENTERTAIN  — Trending audio, memes, behind-the-scenes
              Ratio: 25% of posts
4. CONVERT    — Limited offers, social proof, testimonials
              Ratio: 15% of posts
```

**Verify**: You have 3-4 pillars with defined ratios that sum to 100%. Each pillar has 3+ example post ideas.
**If failed**: If pillars feel too similar, merge them. If you cannot think of 3+ post ideas per pillar, the pillar is too narrow — broaden it.

### Step 3: Set Platform-Specific Posting Cadence

**Duration**: 20 minutes
**Tool**: Spreadsheet or Buffer calendar

Set your posting schedule based on researched optimal frequencies. Start at the minimum viable cadence and increase only when you can maintain quality. [src2]

```
Recommended Startup Posting Cadence (2026):
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
Platform       Minimum  Recommended  Maximum    Best Times
LinkedIn       3/week   4-5/week     1/day      Tue-Thu 8-10am
X/Twitter      1/day    2-3/day      5/day      Mon-Fri 9am-12pm
Instagram      3/week   4-5/week     1/day      Mon/Wed/Fri 11am-1pm
TikTok         3/week   4-5/week     1-2/day    Tue-Thu 7-9pm
Facebook       3/week   4-5/week     1/day      Wed-Fri 1-4pm
YouTube        1/week   2/week       3/week     Thu-Sat 2-4pm
```

**Verify**: Your weekly posting schedule has specific days and times for each platform, with posts mapped to content pillars.
**If failed**: If the cadence feels unsustainable, cut to the minimum (3/week) on your primary platform and drop the secondary platform entirely until you build a content backlog.

### Step 4: Set Up Buffer and Connect Accounts

**Duration**: 20 minutes
**Tool**: Buffer

Configure your social media management tool for multi-platform scheduling.

```
Buffer Setup Checklist:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Sign up at buffer.com/signup (free: 3 channels, 10 posts each)
2. Connect your social profiles:
   - LinkedIn: Company Page (requires admin access)
   - X/Twitter: Business account
   - Instagram: Business or Creator account (requires Facebook Page)
3. Set posting schedule per channel:
   - Go to Publishing → Posting Schedule
   - Add time slots matching your cadence from Step 3
4. Enable analytics tracking (Buffer free includes basic analytics)
5. Install the Buffer browser extension for easy content sharing
```

For teams needing approval workflows or more than 3 channels, upgrade to Buffer Essentials ($5/channel/month) or consider Hootsuite ($99/month for 10 channels). [src5]

**Verify**: All target social profiles appear in the Buffer dashboard and you can create a test draft post for each channel.
**If failed**: Instagram connection requires a Facebook Business Page linked to the Instagram account. If connection fails, verify the Facebook-Instagram link in Instagram Settings > Account > Linked Accounts.

### Step 5: Create Content Templates and First-Week Calendar

**Duration**: 60-90 minutes
**Tool**: Canva + Buffer

Build reusable templates to reduce per-post creation time from 30 minutes to 10 minutes.

```
Template Checklist by Platform:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
LinkedIn (B2B):
  □ Text-only thought leadership template (hook → insight → CTA)
  □ Carousel template (branded slides, 5-10 pages)
  □ Poll template (question + 4 options + context)

Instagram (B2C):
  □ Product showcase template (branded frame, product photo)
  □ Carousel/educational template (swipeable tips)
  □ Stories template (poll, quiz, behind-the-scenes)
  □ Reel cover template (branded thumbnail)

X/Twitter:
  □ Thread opener template (hook + promise)
  □ Single insight tweet template
  □ Quote retweet commentary template
```

In Canva, create branded templates with your logo, fonts, and colors. Save them in a "Social Media Templates" folder. Then populate Buffer with 5-7 days of posts using these templates.

**Verify**: You have at least 3 templates per platform saved in Canva, and Buffer shows 5-7 days of scheduled posts.
**If failed**: If visual design is a bottleneck, start with text-only posts on LinkedIn/X and skip visual templates. Text posts outperform image posts on LinkedIn in engagement rate.

### Step 6: Launch and Establish 30-Day Tracking

**Duration**: 30 minutes
**Tool**: Buffer analytics + spreadsheet

Set up a simple tracking system to measure what works in the first 30 days.

```
30-Day Social Media Tracking Sheet:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
Week | Posts | Impressions | Engagement Rate | Followers | Top Post
  1  |       |             |                 |           |
  2  |       |             |                 |           |
  3  |       |             |                 |           |
  4  |       |             |                 |           |

Key Metrics by Platform:
LinkedIn: Impressions, engagement rate, profile views, connection requests
X/Twitter: Impressions, engagement rate, profile visits, link clicks
Instagram: Reach, engagement rate, saves, profile visits
TikTok: Views, engagement rate, shares, follower growth rate
```

Review metrics weekly. After 30 days, identify your top 3 performing posts, analyze what made them work (format, topic, time, hook), and double down on that pattern.

**Verify**: Tracking spreadsheet is created with baseline metrics recorded. Buffer analytics dashboard shows data flowing from connected accounts.
**If failed**: If Buffer analytics are insufficient, supplement with native platform analytics (LinkedIn Analytics, Instagram Insights, X Analytics) which provide more granular data at no cost.

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Zero engagement after 2 weeks of posting | Content not reaching target audience or poor hooks | Audit post hooks, add relevant hashtags (Instagram) or engage in comments on trending posts in your niche before posting |
| Instagram connection fails in Buffer | Facebook Business Page not linked to Instagram account | Go to Instagram Settings > Account > Linked Accounts > Facebook, connect the Business Page first |
| LinkedIn engagement drops after initial spike | Network exhausted — only existing connections see posts | Engage with 10 posts from others daily to expand algorithmic reach beyond 1st-degree connections |
| Posting feels unsustainable at set cadence | Too many platforms or too-high frequency for available time | Drop to 1 platform at minimum cadence (3/week), batch-create content in 2-hour weekly sessions |
| Content looks generic compared to competitors | Over-reliance on templates without personalization | Add founder voice, behind-the-scenes content, and specific data points from your business |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Posting on 5+ platforms simultaneously from day one
Startups spread across LinkedIn, X, Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, YouTube, and Pinterest simultaneously produce mediocre content everywhere. With 3-5 hours/week, that is less than 30 minutes per platform — not enough for meaningful engagement. [src1]

### Correct: Master one platform, then expand
Commit to one primary platform for 90 days. Only add a second platform when you can maintain minimum viable cadence (3 posts/week) on the first without quality dropping.

### Wrong: Only posting promotional content
Companies that post more than 20% promotional content see engagement rates drop by 50% or more. Audiences unfollow brands that feel like ad feeds. [src3]

### Correct: Follow the 80/20 rule
80% value content (educate, entertain, engage) and 20% promotional content (product updates, offers, CTAs). Even the 20% promotional posts should lead with value.

### Wrong: Ignoring engagement and treating social media as a broadcast channel
Posting content without responding to comments, engaging with other accounts, or participating in conversations signals to algorithms that the account is low-quality. [src2]

### Correct: Spend 50% of social media time on engagement
For every hour spent creating and scheduling posts, spend an equal hour responding to comments, commenting on others' posts, and joining relevant conversations.
