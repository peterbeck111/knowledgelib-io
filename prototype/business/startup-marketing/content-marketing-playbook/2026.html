Content Marketing Playbook

How do I execute content marketing — content pillars, editorial calendar, content types by funnel stage, cost benchmarks?

Type: Execution Recipe Confidence: 0.89 Sources: 7 Verified: 2026-03-12

Purpose

This recipe produces a complete content marketing operation — content pillars, a 12-week editorial calendar, content types mapped to funnel stages, a production workflow, and distribution checklist — ready for execution. The output is a repeatable content engine that generates organic traffic, builds thought leadership, and feeds the sales funnel with qualified leads. [src1]

Prerequisites

  • ICP definition with content consumption habits — ICP Definition Framework
  • Marketing strategy confirming content as selected channel — Marketing Strategy Framework
  • Brand voice guidelines — tone, perspective, and key messaging pillars
  • Google Search Console verified at search.google.com (free)
  • CMS or blog platform — Ghost, WordPress, Webflow CMS, or Hashnode

Constraints

  • Minimum 8 published articles before evaluating content channel effectiveness. [src3]
  • Blog articles cost $200-500 each for quality startup content. [src4]
  • Content distribution requires equal effort to production. [src1]
  • SEO-driven content takes 3-6 months to rank. Do not evaluate organic ROI before month 4. [src5]
  • AI-generated content without human editing gets penalized by Google in 2025+. [src6]
  • Ideal content mix: 50% TOFU, 30% MOFU, 20% BOFU. [src5]

Execution Flow

Step 1: Define 3-5 Content Pillars

Duration: 1-2 hours · Tool: Whiteboard or Notion

Define 3-5 core themes: (1) Problem your product solves (TOFU), (2) Your product category (MOFU), (3) Success with your product (BOFU), (4) Industry thought leadership (TOFU/MOFU), (5) Adjacent problems (TOFU). Each pillar needs 5+ target keywords and 3+ example titles. [src1]

Verify: Each pillar has keywords and example titles. Pillars cover full funnel. · If failed: ICP is too broad — narrow and identify specific search queries.

Step 2: Map Content Types to Funnel Stages

Duration: 30 minutes · Tool: Reference table

TOFU (50%): blog posts, short video, infographics, social threads. MOFU (30%): comparison guides, product demos, white papers, webinars, email courses. BOFU (20%): case studies, ROI calculators, tutorials, testimonials, pricing pages. [src2] [src5]

Verify: 50/30/20 ratio maintained. Each stage has 2+ content types selected. · If failed: Add 1 comparison guide (MOFU) and 1 case study (BOFU) per month minimum.

Step 3: Build 12-Week Editorial Calendar

Duration: 1-2 hours · Tool: Notion, Google Sheets, or Asana

Plan 12 weeks with titles, pillars, funnel stages, formats, target keywords, owners, and dates. Alternate TOFU with MOFU/BOFU (every 4th article). Schedule 1 cornerstone piece (3000+ words) per month. Leave 20% of slots flexible for trending topics.

Verify: 12 weeks planned with content mix following 50/30/20. · If failed: Start with 4-week plan. Perfect is the enemy of published.

Step 4: Create Content Production Workflow

Duration: 30 minutes · Tool: Notion template

5-day workflow per article: Day 1 brief & research (1-2 hrs), Day 2-3 draft (2-4 hrs), Day 4 edit & SEO optimize (1-2 hrs), Day 5 design & publish (1 hr), Day 5-7 distribute (1-2 hrs).

Verify: Workflow documented with time estimates. Every article follows brief → draft → edit → publish → distribute. · If failed: Minimum viable: brief → draft+edit → publish → distribute.

Step 5: Set Up Distribution Checklist

Duration: 30 minutes · Tool: Notion checklist

Same day: LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Google Search Console submission, communities. Within 48 hrs: email newsletter, carousel repurpose, Reddit. Within 1 week: internal links, newsletter pitches, short-form video repurpose. Monthly: update top articles, create PDF lead magnets.

Verify: 8+ distribution channels documented. · If failed: Start with LinkedIn + email + GSC submission only.

Step 6: Measure and Optimize (Monthly)

Duration: 1-2 hours monthly · Tool: GA4 + Google Search Console

Track organic sessions, top pages, engagement metrics, blog-to-signup conversion rate. Action items: double down on winning topics, update decaying articles, kill underperforming formats, test 1 new format/month. [src3]

Verify: Monthly review completed with 2+ action items. · If failed: Use GSC data only (free, minimal setup).

Anti-Patterns

Wrong: Publishing AI-generated articles without human editing

Mass-producing thin AI content that restates search results. Google penalizes unhelpful AI content. [src6] [src7]

Correct: Use AI to assist, humans to differentiate

AI generates 80% of draft; humans add original data, expert quotes, screenshots, and unique analysis.

Wrong: Only producing TOFU awareness content

Writing only "What is [topic]?" articles generates traffic but no pipeline. [src2] [src5]

Correct: Follow the 50/30/20 content mix

30% MOFU (comparisons, guides), 20% BOFU (case studies, tutorials). Link every TOFU article to MOFU content.

Wrong: Publishing without distributing

Writing great content and hoping Google finds it. New sites have zero authority. [src1]

Correct: Equal time on distribution and production

For every hour writing, spend one hour distributing: LinkedIn, communities, email, internal linking, newsletter pitches.

When This Matters

Use this recipe when content marketing has been selected as a channel and the startup needs to build the actual content operation. Requires ICP definition and brand positioning as inputs. Handles content planning and execution, not overall marketing strategy.
