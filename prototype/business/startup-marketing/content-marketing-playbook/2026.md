# Content Marketing Playbook

## Purpose

This recipe produces a complete content marketing operation — content pillars, a 12-week editorial calendar, content types mapped to funnel stages, a production workflow, and distribution checklist — ready for execution. The output is a repeatable content engine that generates organic traffic, builds thought leadership, and feeds the sales funnel with qualified leads. [src1]

## Prerequisites

- [ ] **ICP definition** with content consumption habits — which blogs they read, which podcasts they listen to, which communities they join — [ICP Definition Framework](/business/customer-research/icp-definition-framework/2026)
- [ ] **Marketing strategy** confirming content as a selected channel — [Marketing Strategy Framework](/business/startup-marketing/marketing-strategy-framework-for-startups/2026)
- [ ] **Brand voice guidelines** — tone (professional/casual), perspective (first person/editorial), and key messaging pillars
- [ ] **Google Search Console** verified for your domain at [search.google.com](https://search.google.com/search-console) (free)
- [ ] **CMS or blog platform** set up — Ghost, WordPress, Webflow CMS, or Hashnode
- [ ] **Basic keyword research tool** — free: Google Search Console, Ubersuggest; paid: Ahrefs ($129/mo), Semrush ($139/mo)

## Constraints

- Minimum 8 published articles before evaluating if content marketing is working. Fewer produces no signal. [src3]
- Blog articles cost $200-500 each for quality startup content (research + writing + editing). Budget accordingly. [src4]
- Content distribution requires equal effort to production. Publishing without promotion wastes 50% of investment. [src1]
- SEO-driven content takes 3-6 months to rank in Google. Do not evaluate organic ROI before month 4. [src5]
- AI-generated content without human editing, original data, or expert input gets penalized by Google and ignored by discerning readers. Use AI to assist, not replace. [src6]
- Ideal content mix for B2B SaaS: 50% TOFU, 30% MOFU, 20% BOFU. [src5]

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Define 3-5 Content Pillars

**Duration**: 1-2 hours
**Tool**: Whiteboard or Notion

Content pillars are the 3-5 core themes your content library revolves around. Every piece of content maps to one pillar. [src1]

```
Content Pillar Template:
─────────────────────────────────────────
Pillar 1: [Problem your product solves]
  Keywords: [5-10 target keywords]
  Funnel: Primarily TOFU (awareness)
  Content types: How-to guides, industry trends, data reports
  Example titles: "How to [solve problem] in 2026", "[Industry] trends..."

Pillar 2: [Your product category]
  Keywords: [5-10 comparison/alternative keywords]
  Funnel: Primarily MOFU (consideration)
  Content types: Comparison guides, alternatives, use cases
  Example titles: "[Product] vs [competitor]", "Best [category] tools..."

Pillar 3: [Success with your product]
  Keywords: [5-10 tutorial/integration keywords]
  Funnel: Primarily BOFU (decision)
  Content types: Tutorials, case studies, integration guides
  Example titles: "How to [use case] with [product]", "Case study: ..."

Pillar 4: [Industry expertise/thought leadership]
  Keywords: [5-10 thought leadership keywords]
  Funnel: TOFU/MOFU
  Content types: Opinion pieces, research, predictions
  Example titles: "Why [industry trend] matters", "The state of..."

Pillar 5: [Adjacent problem] (optional)
  Keywords: [5-10 adjacent topic keywords]
  Funnel: TOFU
  Content types: Guides for adjacent workflows
  Example titles: "Complete guide to [adjacent topic]"
```

**Verify**: Each pillar has 5+ target keywords, a primary funnel stage, and 3+ example titles. Pillars cover the full funnel (at least one TOFU, one MOFU, one BOFU).
**If failed**: If unable to identify 3 pillars, the ICP is too broad. Narrow the ICP and identify which specific problems your audience searches for online.

### Step 2: Map Content Types to Funnel Stages

**Duration**: 30 minutes
**Tool**: Reference table

Select content types that match each funnel stage. [src2] [src5]

```
TOFU (Top of Funnel) — Awareness — 50% of content:
─────────────────────────────────────────
Goal: Get found, educate, build trust
Content types:
  • Blog posts (how-to, trends, listicles) — 1000-2000 words
  • Short-form video (TikTok, Reels, Shorts) — 30-90 seconds
  • Infographics and data visualizations
  • Podcasts or podcast guest appearances
  • Social media threads (LinkedIn, Twitter/X)
  • Industry reports and surveys
Metrics: Organic traffic, impressions, social shares, email signups

MOFU (Middle of Funnel) — Consideration — 30% of content:
─────────────────────────────────────────
Goal: Nurture interest, demonstrate expertise, capture leads
Content types:
  • Comparison guides ([product] vs [competitor])
  • Product demo videos (3-10 minutes)
  • White papers and research reports (gated)
  • Webinars and live workshops
  • Email courses (5-7 emails over 2 weeks)
  • Templates and downloadable tools
Metrics: Email signups, content downloads, demo requests, time on page

BOFU (Bottom of Funnel) — Decision — 20% of content:
─────────────────────────────────────────
Goal: Convert, prove ROI, reduce objections
Content types:
  • Case studies with specific results
  • ROI calculators and interactive tools
  • Product tutorials and integration guides
  • Customer testimonial videos
  • Free trial guides and onboarding walkthroughs
  • Pricing comparison pages
Metrics: Trial starts, demo bookings, closed deals, conversion rate
```

**Verify**: Content type mix follows approximately 50/30/20 ratio. Each funnel stage has at least 2 content types selected.
**If failed**: If only producing TOFU content, add 1 comparison guide (MOFU) and 1 case study (BOFU) per month minimum.

### Step 3: Build 12-Week Editorial Calendar

**Duration**: 1-2 hours
**Tool**: Notion, Google Sheets, or Asana

```
Editorial Calendar Template (Week N):
─────────────────────────────────────────
| Date | Title | Pillar | Funnel | Format | Target KW | Owner | Status |
|------|-------|--------|--------|--------|-----------|-------|--------|
| Mon  | [title] | [1-5] | TOFU | Blog | [keyword] | [name] | Draft |
| Wed  | [title] | [1-5] | MOFU | Guide | [keyword] | [name] | Idea  |
| Fri  | [title] | [1-5] | TOFU | Video | [keyword] | [name] | Idea  |

Publishing cadence by budget:
─────────────────────────────────────────
$0/mo (founder):        1 article/week = 12 articles in 12 weeks
$500-2K/mo:             2-3 articles/week = 24-36 articles
$2K-5K/mo:              4-6 articles/week = 48-72 articles
$5K-15K/mo:             8+ articles/week = 96+ articles

Calendar rules:
• No more than 2 TOFU articles in a row — alternate with MOFU/BOFU
• Every 4th article should be a MOFU or BOFU piece
• Schedule 1 "cornerstone" long-form guide (3000+ words) per month
• Leave 20% of calendar slots flexible for trending topics
```

**Verify**: 12 weeks planned with titles, pillars, funnel stages, and owners assigned. Mix follows 50/30/20 ratio across the full calendar. At least 1 cornerstone piece per month.
**If failed**: Start with 4-week plan instead. Perfect is the enemy of published.

### Step 4: Create Content Production Workflow

**Duration**: 30 minutes
**Tool**: Notion template or documented process

```
Production Workflow (per article):
─────────────────────────────────────────
Day 1: Brief & Research (1-2 hours)
  • Create content brief: target keyword, search intent, audience,
    key points, competing articles (top 3 results), unique angle
  • Gather data, quotes, and sources

Day 2-3: Draft (2-4 hours)
  • Write first draft following brief
  • Include: introduction hook, subheadings (H2/H3), key takeaways,
    internal links, CTA, meta description
  • Minimum word count: 1200 words for TOFU, 2000+ for cornerstone

Day 4: Edit & Optimize (1-2 hours)
  • Self-edit or peer review for clarity, accuracy, and flow
  • SEO optimization: keyword placement (title, H1, first 100 words,
    subheadings), meta description, alt text for images
  • Add internal links to 2-3 related articles
  • Add external links to 2-3 authoritative sources

Day 5: Design & Publish (1 hour)
  • Create featured image and in-article graphics (Canva)
  • Format in CMS, set categories and tags
  • Preview on mobile and desktop
  • Publish and submit URL to Google Search Console

Day 5-7: Distribute (1-2 hours)
  • See distribution checklist in Step 5
```

**Verify**: Workflow documented with time estimates per step. Every article goes through brief → draft → edit → optimize → publish → distribute.
**If failed**: If skipping editing step, quality will drop and rankings will suffer. Minimum viable workflow: brief → draft + edit (same person) → publish → distribute.

### Step 5: Set Up Distribution Checklist

**Duration**: 30 minutes
**Tool**: Notion checklist or spreadsheet

```
Content Distribution Checklist (per article):
─────────────────────────────────────────
Same day as publish:
□ Share on LinkedIn (personal + company page) with native text
□ Share on Twitter/X with key takeaway thread
□ Submit URL to Google Search Console (URL inspection → Request indexing)
□ Share in relevant Slack/Discord communities (add value, not spam)

Within 48 hours:
□ Send to email newsletter list (if applicable)
□ Repurpose into 2-3 LinkedIn carousel slides or infographic
□ Cross-post to relevant subreddits (as helpful answer, not promotion)
□ Post in relevant Facebook or LinkedIn groups

Within 1 week:
□ Add internal links FROM 2-3 older articles TO the new article
□ Pitch to newsletter curators in your niche
□ Repurpose into short-form video (30-60 seconds) for TikTok/Reels

Monthly (batch):
□ Update top 5 performing articles with fresh data and new sections
□ Repurpose top 3 articles into downloadable PDF guides (MOFU lead magnets)
□ Compile top articles into email digest for subscribers
```

**Verify**: Distribution checklist has 8+ channels documented. Each new article follows the checklist within 7 days of publication.
**If failed**: If distribution seems overwhelming, start with just 3 channels: LinkedIn, email newsletter, and Google Search Console submission.

### Step 6: Measure and Optimize (Monthly)

**Duration**: 1-2 hours monthly
**Tool**: Google Analytics 4 + Google Search Console

```
Monthly Content Review:
─────────────────────────────────────────
Traffic metrics:
□ Total organic sessions (GA4 → Acquisition → Organic Search)
□ Top 10 pages by organic traffic
□ Pages with growing vs declining traffic
□ New keywords ranking (GSC → Performance → New queries)

Engagement metrics:
□ Average time on page for blog content
□ Bounce rate by content type
□ Most-shared articles (social shares)

Conversion metrics:
□ Blog → signup conversion rate (GA4 → Conversions by source page)
□ Content-assisted conversions (multi-touch attribution)
□ Email signups from content

Action items:
□ Double down: Write more on topics of top 3 performing articles
□ Update: Refresh articles that peaked 3+ months ago
□ Kill: Stop producing content types with zero engagement after 8+ articles
□ Experiment: Test 1 new content format or distribution channel per month
```

**Verify**: Monthly review completed with documented findings. At least 2 action items from the data (articles to update, topics to double down on, formats to test).
**If failed**: If analytics are not set up, start with Google Search Console data only (impressions, clicks, average position). This is free and requires minimal setup.

## Quality Benchmarks

| Quality Metric | Minimum Acceptable | Good | Excellent |
|---------------|-------------------|------|-----------|
| Content pillars defined | 2 pillars | 3-4 pillars with keywords | 5 pillars with topic clusters |
| Publishing frequency | 1 article/week | 2-3 articles/week | 5+ articles/week |
| Content mix (TOFU/MOFU/BOFU) | All TOFU | 60/30/10 | 50/30/20 |
| Distribution channels used | 1 (blog only) | 3 (blog + social + email) | 5+ with repurposing |
| Organic traffic growth (month 4+) | Flat | 10-20% MoM growth | 30%+ MoM growth |
| Blog → signup conversion rate | < 0.5% | 1-2% | 3-5% |

**If below minimum**: Focus on consistency (1 article/week for 8 weeks) before optimizing. Consistent mediocre content outperforms sporadic excellent content for building organic traffic.

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Zero organic traffic after 4 months | Targeting keywords too competitive, or thin content | Target long-tail keywords (4+ words) with search volume 100-1000. Add 500+ words to existing articles with original data. |
| High traffic but no conversions | Missing CTAs, no MOFU/BOFU content, wrong audience | Add email signup CTAs to every article. Create 2 MOFU comparison guides. Verify traffic is actually ICP. |
| Content production bottleneck | Writer burnout or unclear briefs | Batch content briefs weekly. Reduce frequency temporarily. Hire freelancer for $150-300/article. [src4] |
| Declining traffic on older articles | Content decay (outdated information) | Update top 10 articles quarterly with fresh data, new sections, and current year in title. |
| Inconsistent publishing schedule | No editorial calendar or accountability | Use Notion/Asana with due dates and assigned owners. Schedule weekly 15-min editorial standup. |
| Google ranking dropped after AI content surge | Thin AI-generated content penalized | Audit content for originality. Add expert quotes, original data, screenshots, and first-hand experience. [src6] |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Publishing AI-generated articles without human editing or original insight
Mass-producing thin AI content that restates search results. Google's helpful content system penalizes sites dominated by unhelpful, AI-generated content. Readers can tell the difference. [src6] [src7]

### Correct: Use AI to assist, humans to differentiate
Use AI for research compilation, first draft outlines, and repurposing. Add original data, expert quotes, screenshots, first-hand experience, and unique analysis. The 80/20 rule: AI generates 80% of the draft, humans add the 20% that makes it valuable.

### Wrong: Only producing top-of-funnel awareness content
Writing only "What is [topic]?" articles generates traffic but no pipeline. Without MOFU and BOFU content, visitors leave without converting. [src2] [src5]

### Correct: Follow the 50/30/20 content mix
Ensure 30% of content is MOFU (comparisons, guides, templates) and 20% is BOFU (case studies, tutorials, pricing pages). Every TOFU article should link to at least one MOFU article.

### Wrong: Publishing without distributing
Writing a great article and hoping Google finds it. New sites and new articles have zero authority. Without active distribution, content sits unread for months. [src1]

### Correct: Spend equal time on distribution as production
For every hour spent writing, spend one hour distributing: LinkedIn posts, community sharing, email newsletter, internal linking from older articles, and pitching to newsletter curators.
