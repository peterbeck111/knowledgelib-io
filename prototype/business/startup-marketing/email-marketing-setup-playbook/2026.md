# Email Marketing Setup Playbook

## Purpose

This recipe sets up a complete email marketing system for an early-stage startup: ESP selection and configuration, domain authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC), signup form deployment, a 5-email welcome sequence, and list building foundations. The output is a fully operational email marketing system with authenticated sending, an active welcome automation, and embedded signup forms ready to capture leads. [src3]

## Prerequisites

- [ ] **ICP definition** available — who you are targeting and what content they value
- [ ] **Registered domain name** with DNS management access at your registrar
- [ ] **Website or landing page** where signup forms will be embedded
- [ ] **Lead magnet** (optional but recommended) — PDF guide, template, checklist, or free tool to incentivize signups
- [ ] **5-10 content ideas** for the welcome sequence emails

## Constraints

- SPF, DKIM, and DMARC are mandatory for bulk senders (5,000+ emails/day) since Google/Yahoo (Feb 2024) and Microsoft Outlook (May 2025). Even at small scale, authentication improves inbox placement. [src2]
- Never purchase email lists. Purchased lists have 50%+ bounce rates, trigger spam complaints, and get your ESP account suspended. [src3]
- Welcome email must fire within 5 minutes of signup. Delayed welcome emails see 50% lower open rates than immediate ones. [src4]
- Start DMARC at p=none for 2-4 weeks before escalating to p=quarantine, then p=reject. Jumping directly to reject risks blocking legitimate emails. [src6]
- Keep your sending volume consistent. Sudden spikes (sending 10,000 emails after weeks of silence) trigger spam filters.

## Tool Selection Decision

```
Which path?
├── Creator/newsletter-first (B2B or B2C)
│   └── PATH A: Kit (ConvertKit) — free up to 10,000 subscribers
├── E-commerce or visual-heavy B2C
│   └── PATH B: Mailchimp — drag-and-drop templates, e-commerce integrations
├── Media/newsletter as the product
│   └── PATH C: Beehiiv — built for newsletter businesses, referral system
```

|------|-----|-----------|-----------|----------|

| D: Developer | Resend | 3,000 emails/mo, 100/day | $20/mo (50,000 emails/mo) | Dev-first teams, custom email flows |

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Select and Configure ESP

**Duration**: 15-20 minutes
**Tool**: ESP platform

Sign up for your selected ESP and complete initial setup.

```
Kit (ConvertKit) Setup:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Sign up at app.kit.com/users/sign_up
2. Complete onboarding wizard:
   - Creator type (blogger, coach, SaaS, etc.)
   - Current list size
   - Import existing subscribers if any (CSV upload)
3. Go to Settings → Email → Sending Domain
4. Add your custom domain (e.g., mail.yourdomain.com)
5. Kit will display required DNS records — proceed to Step 2

Mailchimp Setup:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Sign up at login.mailchimp.com/signup
2. Complete profile: business name, website, address (CAN-SPAM requirement)
3. Go to Settings → Domains → Add & Verify Domain
4. Mailchimp displays DNS records — proceed to Step 2
```

**Verify**: You can log into the ESP dashboard, see the settings page, and the ESP is asking for domain verification DNS records.
**If failed**: If email verification is stuck, check spam folder for the ESP's confirmation email. Some ESPs require business email (not Gmail) for domain verification.

### Step 2: Configure Email Authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)

**Duration**: 30-45 minutes (plus up to 48 hours for DNS propagation)
**Tool**: Domain registrar DNS panel

Add DNS records for email authentication. This is the most critical step for deliverability. [src2]

```
SPF Record:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
Type: TXT
Host: @
Value: v=spf1 include:_spf.google.com include:{esp-spf-domain} ~all

# ESP-specific SPF includes:
# Kit:      include:transmail.net
# Mailchimp: include:servers.mcsv.net
# Beehiiv:  include:amazonses.com
# Resend:   include:amazonses.com

DKIM Record:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
Type: CNAME (or TXT depending on ESP)
Host: {provided by ESP — typically a long selector string}
Value: {provided by ESP}
# Use 2048-bit keys (default on most modern ESPs)

DMARC Record:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
Type: TXT
Host: _dmarc
Value: v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:dmarc-reports@yourdomain.com; ruf=mailto:dmarc-reports@yourdomain.com; pct=100

# Start with p=none (monitor only)
# After 2-4 weeks with clean reports: p=quarantine
# After 4-8 weeks: p=reject
```

**Verify**: Use MXToolbox (mxtoolbox.com/SuperTool.aspx) to check SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records. All three should show as "Pass." Send a test email to mail-tester.com to get a deliverability score (target: 9/10 or higher). [src5]
**If failed**: DNS propagation can take up to 48 hours. If records do not appear after 4 hours, verify there are no conflicting TXT records on the same hostname. Only one SPF record is allowed per domain — merge all includes into a single record.

### Step 3: Create Signup Forms and Lead Capture

**Duration**: 30-45 minutes
**Tool**: ESP form builder + website

Build email capture forms and deploy them across your digital properties.

```
Signup Form Checklist:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Embedded website form (header, footer, or sidebar)
   - Fields: Email only (or Email + First Name maximum)
   - Fewer fields = higher conversion (email-only converts 30% better)

2. Popup/modal form (triggered by exit intent or time delay)
   - Delay trigger: 30-60 seconds or 50% scroll depth
   - Show once per session, not on every page load

3. Dedicated landing page for lead magnet
   - Headline: Clear value proposition
   - Social proof: subscriber count or testimonial
   - Single CTA: "Get the [Lead Magnet]" or "Subscribe"

4. Link-in-bio for social media profiles
   - Point to landing page or direct ESP signup form

Form Creation in Kit:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
Landing Pages & Forms → Create New → Select Form type
→ Design with visual editor → Copy embed code
→ Paste into website HTML where form should appear
```

**Verify**: Submit a test email through each form. Verify the subscriber appears in your ESP list within 60 seconds and receives the welcome email within 5 minutes.
**If failed**: If forms do not appear on the website, check that the embed code is placed correctly in the HTML. If the form submits but no subscriber appears, check ESP form settings for double opt-in — the subscriber may need to confirm via email first.

### Step 4: Build the 5-Email Welcome Sequence

**Duration**: 60-90 minutes
**Tool**: ESP automation builder

Create an automated welcome sequence that introduces new subscribers to your brand and drives them toward a first action. Welcome emails generate 4x more opens and 5x more clicks than standard campaigns. [src7]

```
Welcome Sequence Structure:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
Email 1: Immediate (within 5 minutes of signup)
  Subject: "Welcome to [Brand] — here's your [lead magnet]"
  Content: Deliver the promised lead magnet, introduce yourself,
           set expectations for email frequency and content
  CTA: Download/access the lead magnet

Email 2: Day 2
  Subject: "[First Name], the #1 mistake [audience] makes with [topic]"
  Content: Share your most valuable insight or contrarian take
           Position yourself as an authority
  CTA: Reply with their biggest challenge (drives engagement)

Email 3: Day 4
  Subject: "How [Customer/User] achieved [specific result]"
  Content: Case study or social proof story
           Show what's possible with your product/approach
  CTA: Check out the product/service or read the full case study

Email 4: Day 7
  Subject: "The [tool/framework] I wish I had when starting [activity]"
  Content: Provide a genuinely useful resource, tool, or template
           Build goodwill and demonstrate expertise
  CTA: Download the resource or try the tool

Email 5: Day 10
  Subject: "Quick question, [First Name]"
  Content: Ask what they're struggling with most
           Offer to help or point to relevant resources
  CTA: Reply to the email (boosts sender reputation)

Automation Setup in Kit:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
Automate → Visual Automations → New Automation
→ Trigger: "Joins a form" (select your signup form)
→ Add Email → Set delay between emails
→ Write each email → Activate the automation
```

**Verify**: Subscribe with a test email address and verify all 5 emails arrive on the correct schedule with proper formatting, working links, and correct personalization.
**If failed**: If emails do not fire, check that the automation is set to "Active" (not Draft). If personalization tags show raw code (e.g., {{ subscriber.first_name }}), check that the merge tag syntax matches your ESP's format.

### Step 5: Set Up Analytics and Deliverability Monitoring

**Duration**: 20-30 minutes
**Tool**: ESP analytics + external tools

Configure tracking to measure performance and catch deliverability issues early.

```
Essential Metrics to Track:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
Welcome Sequence Performance:
  - Open rate target: > 50% (industry average for welcome emails)
  - Click rate target: > 10%
  - Unsubscribe rate: < 0.5% per email
  - Bounce rate: < 2% (hard bounces)

Deliverability Monitoring:
  - Set up Google Postmaster Tools (postmaster.google.com)
    → Add and verify your domain
    → Monitor spam rate (must stay below 0.1%)
    → Monitor domain reputation (target: High)

  - Check DMARC reports weekly
    → Reports arrive at the email set in your DMARC rua= tag
    → Use free DMARC report parsers: dmarcian.com, mxtoolbox.com

List Hygiene Schedule:
  - Monthly: Remove hard bounces (automatic on most ESPs)
  - Quarterly: Re-engage or remove subscribers inactive for 90+ days
  - Never let bounce rate exceed 2% — ESPs may suspend your account
```

**Verify**: Google Postmaster Tools shows your domain as verified. ESP dashboard shows deliverability metrics (open rates, bounce rates). DMARC reports are arriving at your monitoring email.
**If failed**: If Google Postmaster Tools shows no data, you need at least 100 sends to Gmail addresses before data appears. If DMARC reports are not arriving, verify the rua= email address in your DMARC TXT record is correct.

## Quality Benchmarks

| Quality Metric | Minimum Acceptable | Good | Excellent |
|---------------|-------------------|------|-----------|
| Mail-tester.com score | 7/10 | 9/10 | 10/10 |
| Welcome email open rate | > 40% | > 55% | > 70% |
| Welcome sequence click rate | > 5% | > 10% | > 15% |
| Hard bounce rate | < 5% | < 2% | < 0.5% |
| Signup form conversion rate | > 1% | > 3% | > 5% |
| DMARC alignment rate | > 80% | > 95% | 100% |

**If below minimum**: Check authentication records first (SPF/DKIM/DMARC). If authentication passes but open rates are low, audit subject lines and send times. If bounce rates are high, your list source may be contaminated — remove unverified addresses.

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Emails landing in spam folder | Missing or misconfigured SPF/DKIM/DMARC | Verify all three records at mxtoolbox.com, fix any failures, wait 24-48h for propagation |
| SPF PermError: too many lookups | SPF record exceeds 10 DNS lookup limit | Consolidate includes using SPF flattening tools (e.g., autospf.com) |
| DKIM signature invalid | Wrong CNAME/TXT record or selector mismatch | Re-copy the exact DKIM record from ESP settings, ensure no trailing spaces |
| ESP account suspended | High bounce rate or spam complaints | Appeal with ESP support, clean list of invalid addresses, slow down send rate |
| Welcome email not triggering | Automation set to Draft or wrong trigger form | Activate the automation, verify trigger is connected to the correct signup form |
| Low open rates (< 20%) | Poor subject lines or bad send times | A/B test subject lines, send at 9-10am recipient local time, check inbox vs. spam placement |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Collecting email addresses without email authentication configured
Sending emails from an unauthenticated domain causes 10-30% of emails to land in spam from day one, poisoning your sender reputation before you even start building it. [src2]

### Correct: Configure SPF, DKIM, and DMARC before sending any marketing emails
Complete Step 2 (authentication) before Step 3 (signup forms). Every email sent from an unauthenticated domain damages your reputation with inbox providers.

### Wrong: Asking for 5+ fields on the signup form
Every additional field beyond email address reduces conversion rates by 15-25%. Name + email + company + phone + job title forms convert at 1/5th the rate of email-only forms. [src3]

### Correct: Email-only signup forms with progressive profiling
Capture email only at signup. Collect additional data through welcome sequence replies, link click behavior, and in-app preferences over time.

### Wrong: Sending the same generic newsletter to all subscribers
Blasting identical content to your entire list ignores subscriber interests, increases unsubscribes, and trains spam filters to deprioritize your emails. [src4]

### Correct: Segment from day one using tags and behaviors
Tag subscribers by signup source, content interests, and engagement level. Even simple segments (active vs. inactive, by topic interest) improve open rates by 20-40%.
