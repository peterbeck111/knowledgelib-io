# SEO Strategy Playbook

## Purpose

This recipe builds a complete SEO operation for a startup — keyword strategy, content cluster architecture, technical SEO foundation, link building plan, and performance measurement — with realistic timelines by website maturity. The output is a prioritized keyword portfolio, content cluster map, technical audit with fixes, and link building target list that compounds organic traffic over 6-12 months. [src1]

## Prerequisites

- [ ] **ICP definition** with search behavior documented — what queries they use, which results they trust — [ICP Definition Framework](/business/customer-research/icp-definition-framework/2026)
- [ ] **Content marketing foundation** — content pillars and editorial calendar established — [Content Marketing Playbook](/business/startup-marketing/content-marketing-playbook/2026)
- [ ] **Google Search Console** verified at [search.google.com](https://search.google.com/search-console) (free)
- [ ] **Google Analytics 4** installed with conversion events configured
- [ ] **SEO tool account** — [Ahrefs](https://ahrefs.com/pricing) ($129/mo Lite) or [Semrush](https://www.semrush.com/pricing/) ($139/mo Pro) — or Ubersuggest ($29/mo) for tighter budgets
- [ ] **Screaming Frog** installed — [screaming frog.co.uk](https://www.screamingfrog.co.uk/seo-spider/) (free up to 500 URLs)

## Constraints

- SEO takes 4-12 months for meaningful results. New sites face 1-3 month "sandbox" with limited visibility. Do not kill SEO efforts before month 6. [src5] [src6]
- Core Web Vitals: LCP < 2.5s, INP < 200ms, CLS < 0.1. Failing these hurts rankings and user experience. [src3]
- Paid link schemes violate Google guidelines and risk manual penalties. Focus on earning links through content quality. [src4]
- AI-generated content without human expertise, original data, or unique analysis is penalized by Google's helpful content system. [src7]
- Google AI Overviews are reducing CTR for informational queries. Focus on high-intent, transactional, and comparison keywords where organic results still get clicks. [src7]
- Long-tail keywords (4+ words) are easier to rank for and often have higher conversion intent than head terms. [src1]

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Technical SEO Audit and Foundation

**Duration**: 2-4 hours
**Tool**: Screaming Frog + Google PageSpeed Insights + Google Search Console

Run a comprehensive technical audit before investing in content. Fixing technical issues first ensures Google can crawl and index everything you publish. [src3]

```
Technical SEO Audit Checklist:
─────────────────────────────────────────
Crawlability:
□ Robots.txt allows Googlebot on all important pages
□ XML sitemap exists and is submitted to GSC
□ No orphan pages (pages with zero internal links)
□ Redirect chains limited (max 2 hops)
□ No broken internal links (404s)
□ Clean URL structure (lowercase, hyphens, descriptive)

Indexability:
□ Important pages return 200 status
□ No accidental noindex tags on key pages
□ Canonical tags point to correct URLs
□ Duplicate content resolved (www vs non-www, trailing slashes)
□ Pagination handled correctly (rel="next"/"prev" or load-more)

Performance (Core Web Vitals):
□ LCP < 2.5 seconds (optimize largest image/text block)
□ INP < 200ms (minimize JavaScript blocking)
□ CLS < 0.1 (set width/height on images, avoid layout shifts)
□ Enable Brotli/GZIP compression
□ Implement browser caching (Cache-Control headers)
□ Optimize images (WebP format, lazy loading, correct dimensions)

Mobile:
□ Responsive design passes Google Mobile-Friendly Test
□ Font size > 16px on mobile
□ Tap targets > 48x48px with adequate spacing

Security & HTTPS:
□ HTTPS enforced (HTTP redirects to HTTPS)
□ SSL certificate valid (no mixed content warnings)

Schema Markup:
□ Organization schema on homepage
□ Article/BlogPosting schema on content pages
□ FAQ schema where applicable
□ BreadcrumbList schema for navigation
```

**Verify**: Screaming Frog crawl shows < 5% error rate. PageSpeed Insights scores > 80 on mobile for all key pages. GSC shows no critical indexing issues.
**If failed**: Prioritize fixes by impact: (1) crawlability blockers, (2) Core Web Vitals failures, (3) indexing issues. Fix critical items before publishing new content.

### Step 2: Keyword Research and Strategy

**Duration**: 2-3 hours
**Tool**: Ahrefs or Semrush + Google Search Console

Build a keyword portfolio of 100+ targets organized by cluster, intent, and priority. [src1] [src2]

```
Keyword Research Process:
─────────────────────────────────────────
1. Seed keyword brainstorm (30 min):
   • List 10-20 seed terms from ICP pain points
   • Include: product category, problems solved, alternatives
   • Example seeds for a project management SaaS:
     "project management software", "team collaboration tool",
     "agile project tracking", "Asana alternative"

2. Expand with tools (1 hour):
   • Ahrefs → Keywords Explorer → enter seeds → Related Terms
   • Filter: KD (Keyword Difficulty) < 30 for new sites
   • Filter: Volume > 100 (or > 50 for very niche B2B)
   • Export top 200 keywords

3. Competitor keyword gap (30 min):
   • Ahrefs → Competitive Analysis → enter 3 competitors
   • Find keywords competitors rank for that you don't
   • Filter: KD < 40, position 4-20 (winnable)

4. Classify by intent (30 min):
   • Informational: "what is", "how to", "guide"
   • Commercial investigation: "best", "vs", "review", "comparison"
   • Transactional: "pricing", "buy", "sign up", "free trial"
   • Navigational: brand names, product names

5. Prioritize (30 min):
   Priority score = (Volume × Intent value) / Difficulty
   • Intent value: Transactional = 3, Commercial = 2, Informational = 1
   • High priority: Score > 5, KD < 30
   • Medium: Score 2-5 or KD 30-50
   • Low: Score < 2 or KD > 50
```

**Verify**: 100+ keywords exported with columns: keyword, volume, difficulty, intent, priority, assigned cluster. At least 20 keywords with KD < 30 and volume > 100.
**If failed**: If < 20 low-difficulty keywords found, niche is very competitive. Target long-tail keywords (4+ words) and comparison keywords ("[competitor] alternative").

### Step 3: Build Content Cluster Architecture

**Duration**: 1-2 hours
**Tool**: Spreadsheet or Ahrefs Content Explorer

Organize keywords into topic clusters with pillar pages and supporting articles. [src1] [src2]

```
Content Cluster Template:
─────────────────────────────────────────
CLUSTER: [Topic Area — e.g., "Project Management for Remote Teams"]
Pillar page: "The Complete Guide to [Topic]" (3000+ words)
  Target: [high-volume keyword, KD 20-40]
  URL: /blog/[topic]-guide

Supporting articles (8-15 per cluster):
  1. "[Topic] for [use case]" → links to pillar
     Target: [long-tail keyword]
  2. "[Topic] vs [alternative]" → links to pillar
     Target: [comparison keyword]
  3. "How to [specific task]" → links to pillar
     Target: [informational keyword]
  4. "Best [tools] for [topic]" → links to pillar
     Target: [commercial keyword]
  ...

Internal linking rules:
  • Every supporting article links to its pillar page
  • Pillar page links to every supporting article
  • Supporting articles cross-link to 2-3 siblings
  • Use descriptive anchor text (not "click here")

Aim for 3-5 clusters covering your core product areas.
Each cluster = 1 pillar + 8-15 supporting articles.
Total initial target: 30-75 articles.
```

**Verify**: 3-5 clusters mapped with pillar pages and supporting articles. Internal link structure documented. Each cluster targets a coherent topic area.
**If failed**: Start with 1 cluster. Better to have 1 complete, well-linked cluster than 5 fragmented ones.

### Step 4: On-Page SEO for Every Article

**Duration**: 15-30 minutes per article (added to writing workflow)
**Tool**: SEO tool + CMS

```
On-Page SEO Checklist (per article):
─────────────────────────────────────────
Title tag:
□ Contains primary keyword (near the beginning)
□ < 60 characters
□ Compelling and click-worthy (not just keyword)

Meta description:
□ Contains primary keyword
□ < 160 characters
□ Includes value proposition and CTA

URL:
□ Contains primary keyword
□ Short, descriptive, lowercase, hyphens
□ No dates or IDs in URL (e.g., /blog/seo-guide not /blog/2026/03/12/seo-guide)

Headings:
□ H1 = title (contains primary keyword), one per page
□ H2s contain secondary keywords or question variants
□ Logical heading hierarchy (H1 → H2 → H3)

Content:
□ Primary keyword in first 100 words
□ Secondary keywords naturally distributed throughout
□ Minimum 1200 words (2000+ for pillar pages)
□ Original data, screenshots, examples, or expert quotes
□ Answers the search intent completely (check top 3 results)

Internal links:
□ Link to pillar page from cluster article
□ Link to 2-3 related supporting articles
□ Use descriptive anchor text

Images:
□ Descriptive alt text with keywords where natural
□ Compressed (< 200 KB per image, WebP format)
□ Width and height attributes set (prevents CLS)

Schema:
□ Article/BlogPosting structured data
□ FAQ schema if article contains Q&A sections
```

**Verify**: Top 10 published articles all pass on-page checklist. Ahrefs/Semrush on-page audit shows no critical issues.
**If failed**: Start with title tag, meta description, and internal links only. These three have the highest impact per minute invested.

### Step 5: Link Building (Month 2+)

**Duration**: 2-4 hours/week ongoing
**Tool**: Ahrefs + email outreach

Start link building after publishing 10+ quality articles. Links from other websites signal authority to Google. [src4]

```
Link Building Strategies (ranked by effectiveness for startups):
─────────────────────────────────────────
1. Create linkable assets (highest ROI):
   • Original research and surveys
   • Free tools and calculators
   • Comprehensive industry guides (3000+ words)
   • Data visualizations and infographics
   → These attract links naturally without outreach

2. Guest posting on niche blogs:
   • Find blogs where your ICP reads
   • Pitch unique article ideas (not self-promotional)
   • Include contextual link to your relevant content
   • Target: 2-4 guest posts/month
   → Use Ahrefs Content Explorer → filter by DR 30-70

3. HARO / journalist queries:
   • Sign up at connectively.us (formerly HARO)
   • Respond to journalist queries in your expertise area
   • Provide expert quotes with bio link
   • Target: respond to 5-10 queries/week
   → Typical success rate: 5-10% of pitches get published

4. Broken link building:
   • Find 404 pages on competitor and industry sites
   • Create equivalent content on your site
   • Email webmaster: "Hey, your link to [page] is broken.
     I have a similar resource at [your URL]"
   • Target: 10-20 outreach emails/week

5. Resource page link building:
   • Search: "[your topic] + resources" or "useful links"
   • Find pages that curate helpful links
   • Email suggesting your content as an addition
```

**Verify**: After 3 months of link building, 5+ referring domains gained from DR 30+ sites. Ahrefs shows growing referring domain count month-over-month.
**If failed**: If guest post pitches are rejected, improve pitch quality. If no responses, target smaller/newer blogs (DR 20-40) that are more responsive.

### Step 6: Measure, Report, and Iterate (Monthly)

**Duration**: 1-2 hours monthly
**Tool**: Google Search Console + Ahrefs + GA4

```
Monthly SEO Review:
─────────────────────────────────────────
Google Search Console:
□ Total clicks and impressions (month-over-month trend)
□ Average position for target keywords
□ New queries appearing (content discovery)
□ Pages with declining impressions (content decay)
□ Indexing issues (Coverage report)

Ahrefs/Semrush:
□ Referring domains gained/lost
□ Domain Rating/Authority trend
□ Keyword position changes (track top 50 keywords)
□ Competitor movement on shared keywords

GA4:
□ Organic traffic sessions and users
□ Organic traffic → conversion rate
□ Revenue attributed to organic search
□ Top landing pages by organic conversions

Action items:
□ Update 2-3 articles showing position decline
□ Create new content for keywords gaining impressions but low CTR
□ Build links to pages stuck on page 2 (positions 11-20)
□ Remove or merge thin content (< 300 words, zero traffic)
□ Submit updated sitemap if new content published
```

**Verify**: Monthly report shows trending improvements in at least 2 of: total clicks, average position, referring domains, organic conversions.
**If failed**: If no improvement after 6 months: audit content quality (are you matching search intent?), check technical issues (are pages indexed?), verify link building is producing links (not just outreach).

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Pages not indexed after 2 weeks | Crawlability issue, noindex tag, or thin content | Check GSC Coverage report. Submit URL for indexing. Verify no noindex tags. Add internal links. |
| Rankings dropped after Google update | Content quality or link profile issue | Audit affected pages against Google's helpful content guidelines. Add original data, remove AI-generated filler. |
| Zero traffic on published articles | Targeting too-competitive keywords or no indexing | Check if indexed (site:yourdomain.com/url). If indexed, target lower-KD keywords. If not, fix indexing. |
| Core Web Vitals failing | Large images, render-blocking JS, layout shifts | Compress images to WebP, defer non-critical JS, add width/height to all images. [src3] |
| Organic traffic growing but conversions flat | Wrong intent keywords or weak conversion pages | Shift content mix toward commercial and transactional keywords. Optimize conversion pages (CTAs, social proof). |
| Link building outreach getting no replies | Poor pitch quality or wrong targets | Personalize pitches (reference specific content). Target smaller blogs (DR 20-40). Follow up after 5 days. [src4] |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Targeting only high-volume, high-difficulty keywords
New startup websites targeting keywords with KD > 50 and volume > 10K. These are dominated by established sites with thousands of backlinks. Result: zero rankings after 6 months of work. [src1] [src2]

### Correct: Start with long-tail, low-difficulty keywords
Target keywords with KD < 30 and 4+ words. These convert better (more specific intent) and are achievable for new sites. Build authority through many small wins, then compete for harder keywords.

### Wrong: Publishing 50 thin articles instead of 15 comprehensive ones
Mass-producing short, surface-level articles that add no unique value. Google rewards depth and expertise, not volume. [src7]

### Correct: Fewer, better articles with original insight
Write 1500-2500 word articles with original data, expert quotes, screenshots, and actionable steps. One comprehensive article that ranks #3 drives more traffic than 10 thin articles on page 5.

### Wrong: Ignoring technical SEO while focusing on content
Publishing great content on a site with crawlability issues, broken links, and failing Core Web Vitals. Google cannot rank what it cannot crawl and index. [src3]

### Correct: Technical SEO first, then content
Run the technical audit (Step 1) before publishing content at scale. Fix critical crawlability and performance issues. Then every article published benefits from a healthy technical foundation.
