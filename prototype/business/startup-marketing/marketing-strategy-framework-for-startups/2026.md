# Marketing Strategy Framework for Startups

## Purpose

This recipe produces a stage-appropriate marketing strategy document with prioritized channel selections, budget allocations, a 90-day experiment plan, and measurable success criteria — calibrated to whether the startup is pre-PMF (learning mode) or post-PMF (scaling mode). The output is an actionable marketing plan that prevents the two most common mistakes: spreading budget across too many channels before PMF, and under-investing in proven channels after PMF. [src1]

## Prerequisites

- [ ] **ICP definition** — documented ideal customer profile with demographics, pain points, and where they spend time online — [ICP Definition Framework](/business/customer-research/icp-definition-framework/2026)
- [ ] **Product positioning** — clear value proposition and competitive differentiation — [Startup Positioning Framework](/business/brand-strategy/startup-positioning-framework/2026)
- [ ] **Current metrics baseline** — MRR (or revenue), churn rate, NPS/CSAT, traffic sources, conversion rates (even if approximate)
- [ ] **Google Analytics 4** — installed on product/website at [analytics.google.com](https://analytics.google.com) (free)
- [ ] **Budget clarity** — monthly marketing budget approved or estimated based on runway

## Constraints

- Pre-PMF: 80%+ of marketing effort must focus on learning, not scaling. Premature scaling burns cash without building durable acquisition. [src4] [src5]
- Seed stage: budget $5K-25K/mo. Do not exceed this before channel-market fit is validated. [src2]
- Maximum 2 primary channels for first 90 days. Spreading across 5+ channels guarantees none reach statistical significance for learning. [src1]
- Series A: budget $40K-170K/mo. Only scale channels with proven CAC:LTV ratio > 3:1. [src2]
- Do not hire a VP of Marketing before PMF. Founders must own the GTM learning loop personally — outsourcing this pre-PMF leads to generic strategies that miss market signals. [src5]
- Budget allocation benchmarks: seed-stage 10-20% of funding, Series A 25-40% of funding toward marketing. [src2] [src6]

## Tool Selection Decision

```

├── Pre-PMF (no retention metrics, still searching for fit)
│   ├── B2B SaaS → PATH A: Founder-led outbound + content experiments
│   ├── B2C SaaS → PATH B: Community + product-led growth experiments
│   ├── Marketplace → PATH C: Supply-side acquisition + demand-side experiments
│   └── E-commerce → PATH D: Paid social testing + influencer experiments

│   ├── B2B → PATH E: Content + outbound scaling with attribution
│   └── B2C → PATH F: Paid + organic scaling with attribution
└── Strong PMF (retention proven, ready to scale)
    ├── B2B → PATH G: Multi-channel scaling (content + paid + partnerships)
    └── B2C → PATH H: Performance marketing + brand building
```

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Audit Current State and Define PMF Stage

**Duration**: 1-2 hours
**Tool**: Analytics dashboard + metrics spreadsheet

Assess where the startup is on the PMF spectrum. This determines everything else.

```
PMF Assessment Checklist:
─────────────────────────────────────────
□ Sean Ellis test: >40% of users "very disappointed" if product disappeared?
  → Yes: PMF achieved   → No: Pre-PMF

□ Retention curve: Does usage flatten (not decline to zero)?
  → Yes: Retention signal   → No: Product issue, not marketing issue

□ NPS > 40?
  → Yes: Strong advocacy   → No: Focus on product before marketing

□ Organic referrals happening without incentive?
  → Yes: PMF signal   → No: Pre-PMF

□ Revenue growing without paid acquisition?
  → Yes: PMF signal   → No: Pre-PMF

Score: 0-1 "yes" = Pre-PMF | 2-3 = Early PMF | 4-5 = Strong PMF
```

**Verify**: PMF stage is clearly classified. Metrics baseline is documented with current values for: MRR, monthly churn, NPS, top 3 traffic sources, trial-to-paid conversion rate.
**If failed**: If metrics are unavailable, classify as Pre-PMF and focus Step 2 on instrumenting analytics before channel selection.

### Step 2: Select Primary Channels (Maximum 2)

**Duration**: 1 hour
**Tool**: Channel scoring matrix (spreadsheet or Notion)

Score each potential channel against four criteria. Select the top 2 scoring channels. [src1]

```
Channel Scoring Matrix (1-5 scale each):
─────────────────────────────────────────
                    Reachability  Trust    Cost     Speed   Total
                    (can target   Transfer (CAC     (time to
                     ICP?)        (proof?) viable?) results)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SEO/Content         ___          ___      ___      ___     ___
LinkedIn outbound   ___          ___      ___      ___     ___
Cold email          ___          ___      ___      ___     ___
Google Ads          ___          ___      ___      ___     ___
Meta Ads (FB/IG)    ___          ___      ___      ___     ___
LinkedIn Ads        ___          ___      ___      ___     ___
Community (Reddit)  ___          ___      ___      ___     ___
Product-led growth  ___          ___      ___      ___     ___
Partnerships        ___          ___      ___      ___     ___
Events/webinars     ___          ___      ___      ___     ___
Influencer          ___          ___      ___      ___     ___
Referral program    ___          ___      ___      ___     ___
```

**Channel selection rules by model:** [src1]

```
B2B SaaS (ACV < $5K):  Content/SEO + product-led growth
B2B SaaS (ACV $5-50K): Content/SEO + outbound (LinkedIn/email)
B2B SaaS (ACV > $50K): Outbound + events/partnerships
B2C SaaS:              Paid social + organic/community
Marketplace:           Paid + SEO (demand) + outreach (supply)
E-commerce:            Paid social + influencer/UGC
Dev tools:             Content/SEO + community (GitHub, Discord)
```

**Verify**: Exactly 2 channels selected with clear rationale tied to ICP behavior and business model. [src1]
**If failed**: If unable to choose between 3+ channels, run a 2-week micro-test ($500 budget per channel) to generate data for the decision.

### Step 3: Allocate Budget by Stage

**Duration**: 30 minutes
**Tool**: Spreadsheet

Apply stage-appropriate budget allocation. [src2] [src6]

```
Pre-PMF Budget Template ($5K-15K/mo):
─────────────────────────────────────────
Channel experiments:     40%  ($2K-6K)    ← Primary + secondary channel spend
Content creation:        25%  ($1.3K-3.8K) ← Blog, social, landing pages
Tools & analytics:       15%  ($750-2.3K)  ← GA4, CRM, email, design
Customer research:       20%  ($1K-3K)     ← Interviews, surveys, user tests
─────────────────────────────────────────
Growth/ad spend:          0%              ← Do NOT scale spend pre-PMF

Post-PMF Budget Template ($25K-100K/mo):
─────────────────────────────────────────
Proven channel scaling:  50%  ($12.5-50K)  ← Double down on what works
New channel experiments: 15%  ($3.8-15K)   ← Test adjacent channels
Content & brand:         20%  ($5-20K)     ← Build moat, reduce CAC over time
Tools & infrastructure:  10%  ($2.5-10K)   ← Marketing automation, analytics
Team/freelancers:         5%  ($1.3-5K)    ← Fill capability gaps
```

**Verify**: Total budget matches approved monthly spend. No single channel exceeds 50% of budget pre-PMF (prevents over-concentration before learning).
**If failed**: Reduce scope to 1 channel + content. A $3K/mo budget focused on one channel produces more learning than $3K spread across three.

### Step 4: Design 90-Day Experiment Plan

**Duration**: 1 hour
**Tool**: Notion, Google Sheets, or Airtable

Create a structured experiment backlog with hypothesis, success criteria, and timeline for each test. [src7]

```
Experiment Template:
─────────────────────────────────────────
Experiment #: [sequential number]
Channel: [selected channel from Step 2]
Hypothesis: "If we [action], then [ICP segment]
            will [desired behavior] because [reason]"
Budget: $[amount] over [duration]
Success metric: [specific measurable outcome]
Minimum viable signal: [threshold to continue/kill]
Timeline: [start date] → [end date] (min 2 weeks)
Owner: [name]

Example (B2B content):
Experiment #1
Channel: LinkedIn organic content
Hypothesis: "If we publish 3x/week founder thought
            leadership posts about [pain point], then
            CTOs at 50-200 person SaaS companies will
            engage (like/comment) and visit our site."
Budget: $0 (founder time: 5 hrs/week)
Success metric: 50+ profile views/week, 5+ website
               visits from LinkedIn/week
Minimum viable signal: 20+ profile views/week by week 3
Timeline: Mar 15 → Jun 15 (90 days)
Owner: CEO/Founder
```

Plan 4-6 experiments per 90-day cycle. Stagger starts by 2 weeks so you can give each experiment focused attention.

**Verify**: Each experiment has a clear hypothesis, measurable success metric, budget, and kill criteria. Experiments are staggered, not all starting simultaneously.
**If failed**: If you cannot define a measurable outcome for an experiment, the channel is too vague — narrow the tactic (e.g., "SEO" → "publish 4 comparison pages targeting [competitor] alternative keywords").

### Step 5: Build Measurement and Attribution

**Duration**: 30-60 minutes
**Tool**: GA4 + CRM + UTM conventions

```
UTM Convention for Consistent Attribution:
─────────────────────────────────────────
utm_source:   platform name (google, linkedin, twitter, email)
utm_medium:   channel type (cpc, organic, social, email, referral)
utm_campaign: experiment ID (exp-001-linkedin-content)
utm_content:  variant or creative (post-a, post-b)

Example URLs:
yourapp.com?utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=exp-001-founder-content&utm_content=pain-point-post-a
yourapp.com?utm_source=google&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign=exp-003-search-ads&utm_content=competitor-keyword-v1
```

**Key metrics to track per channel:** [src3]

```
Awareness:   Impressions, reach, website visits (by source)
Interest:    Time on site, pages/session, content engagement
Activation:  Signups, trial starts, demo requests (by source)
Revenue:     Conversion rate, CAC by channel, LTV:CAC ratio
Retention:   Churn rate (cohorted by acquisition channel)
```

**Verify**: UTM conventions documented and shared with team. GA4 goals/conversions configured for key actions (signup, trial start, purchase). First-touch and last-touch attribution reports accessible.
**If failed**: Start with UTM tracking on all links, even without full attribution. Imperfect attribution is better than no attribution.

### Step 6: Execute, Review, and Iterate (90-Day Cycles)

**Duration**: Ongoing (weekly 30-min reviews, monthly deep reviews)
**Tool**: Experiment tracker + analytics

```
Weekly Review Checklist (30 minutes):
─────────────────────────────────────────
□ Check each active experiment's key metric vs target
□ Flag any experiment below minimum viable signal threshold
□ Identify top-performing content/ads for doubling down
□ Review budget pacing — on track vs actual spend
□ Document one insight or learning per experiment

Monthly Deep Review (2 hours):
─────────────────────────────────────────
□ Calculate CAC per channel (total spend / new customers)
□ Calculate LTV:CAC ratio per channel
□ Kill experiments below threshold after minimum run time
□ Promote winning experiments to "scaling" budget
□ Add new experiments to backlog for next 30 days
□ Adjust budget allocation based on performance data

90-Day Strategy Review:
─────────────────────────────────────────
□ Identify top 2 channels by LTV:CAC ratio
□ Reallocate 70% of budget to top 2 channels
□ Design next 90-day experiment plan
□ Assess PMF progress — stage changed?
□ Update ICP if customer learnings warrant it
```

**Verify**: At least 4 experiments completed per 90-day cycle. Clear winners and losers identified with data. Budget reallocated toward best-performing channels.
**If failed**: If no experiments reached statistical significance, experiments were too short (minimum 2 weeks) or too small (minimum $500 spend per paid experiment). Increase duration and budget per experiment, reduce number of parallel experiments.

## Quality Benchmarks

| Quality Metric | Minimum Acceptable | Good | Excellent |
|---------------|-------------------|------|-----------|
| Channels selected | 1 channel defined | 2 channels with rationale | 2 channels with scoring matrix |
| Budget allocation | Lump sum defined | Broken down by category | Category + per-experiment allocation |
| Experiment plan | 2 experiments defined | 4-6 with hypotheses | 4-6 with hypotheses, success criteria, kill thresholds |
| Measurement setup | UTM tracking on links | GA4 + UTM + CRM tracking | Full attribution model with cohort analysis |
| Review cadence | Monthly reviews | Weekly + monthly | Weekly + monthly + 90-day strategic review |
| CAC tracking | Not tracked | Tracked per channel | Tracked per channel with LTV:CAC ratio |

**If below minimum**: Start with Step 2 (channel selection) and Step 4 (experiment plan). Even without formal measurement, running structured experiments with documented hypotheses produces learning.

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| No traffic from selected channel after 30 days | Wrong channel for ICP, or insufficient effort | Verify ICP actually uses this channel (interview 5 customers). If confirmed, increase activity frequency 2x. If not confirmed, switch channel. |
| High traffic but zero conversions | Messaging mismatch or wrong audience segment | A/B test landing page messaging. Interview visitors who did not convert. Check if traffic is actually ICP. |
| CAC too high (> 1/3 of LTV) | Targeting too broad or channel saturated | Narrow targeting to highest-intent segment. Test different creative/messaging. Consider adjacent channel. [src3] |
| Budget exhausted before experiment completes | Budget too small for channel minimum viable test | Reduce number of parallel experiments. Minimum $500/experiment for paid channels, 4 weeks for organic. |
| Team disagreement on channel priority | No shared decision framework | Use the scoring matrix from Step 2 with data. Remove opinion by requiring evidence for each score. |
| PMF stage misclassified | Optimistic assessment without data | Re-run PMF assessment with actual metrics. Use Sean Ellis test with real users (minimum 40 responses). [src5] |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Scaling paid ads before PMF
Spending $10K+/mo on Google Ads or Meta Ads before validating that users retain and convert at sustainable rates. Result: high CAC, low LTV, rapid cash burn with no learning about product-market fit. [src4] [src5]

### Correct: Use paid ads as learning tools pre-PMF
Pre-PMF, paid ads serve one purpose: fast feedback. Spend $500-2K to test messaging, measure click-through rates on value propositions, and validate demand for specific features. Do not optimize for conversions until retention is proven.

### Wrong: Copying a competitor's channel mix
Adopting the same channels as a well-funded competitor (e.g., their podcast + conference sponsorships + large content team) without their budget, brand awareness, or domain authority. [src1]

### Correct: Choose channels where you have asymmetric advantage
Select channels where the startup has a unique edge: founder's personal network, deep domain expertise, access to a niche community, or willingness to do things that don't scale (manual outreach, handwritten notes, 1:1 demos).

### Wrong: Measuring marketing by vanity metrics only
Reporting impressions, followers, or page views without connecting them to pipeline or revenue. This creates an illusion of progress while actual customer acquisition stalls. [src3]

### Correct: Measure by acquisition funnel metrics from day one
Track visitors → signups → activated users → paying customers by channel. Even if numbers are small pre-PMF, the funnel structure reveals where drop-off occurs and which channel produces highest-quality leads.

## When This Matters

Use this recipe when the agent needs to build a complete marketing strategy for a startup, not execute a specific channel. This produces the strategic plan that downstream execution recipes (content marketing, SEO, paid acquisition) implement. Requires ICP definition and positioning as inputs.
