# Visual Identity Brief Template

## Purpose

This recipe produces a complete visual identity brief document covering five core pillars: color palette (with exact values), typography system (font pairing and hierarchy), logo requirements (formats, sizes, variations), imagery style direction (photography and illustration guidelines), and an asset delivery checklist. The output is a production-ready brief that can be executed DIY, handed to a freelance designer, or used as the foundation for an agency engagement — with budget-appropriate guidance for each path. [src1]

## Prerequisites

- [ ] **Brand strategy or positioning statement** — mission, values, target audience, and 3-5 personality attributes (e.g., "innovative, approachable, trustworthy")
- [ ] **Competitor visual audit** — screenshots or URLs of 3-5 competitors showing their color palettes, typography, and overall visual approach
- [ ] **Canva or Milanote account** — for mood board creation (free tier sufficient)
- [ ] **Coolors.co** — for palette generation and exploration (free, no account needed)
- [ ] **WebAIM Contrast Checker** — for WCAG accessibility validation (free, web-based)

## Constraints

- Color palette must include exact HEX, RGB, and CMYK values — approximate color names are insufficient for production use. [src7]
- All text-on-background color combinations must meet WCAG 2.1 AA minimum contrast ratios: 4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text (18px+ bold or 24px+ regular). [src3]
- Logo files must be delivered in vector format (SVG for web, EPS/PDF for print) — PNG/JPG alone are not scalable and will fail at large sizes or print resolution. [src5]
- Typography system must not exceed 2-3 typefaces. More creates visual chaos, increases page load times, and makes guidelines harder to maintain. [src4]
- Font licensing must match usage context — Google Fonts are free for all uses; Adobe Fonts require a Creative Cloud subscription; purchased fonts have specific license terms.

## Tool Selection Decision

```
Which path?
├── User is non-designer AND budget = DIY ($0-500)
│   └── PATH A: DIY Free — Canva + Coolors + Google Fonts
├── User is non-designer AND budget = freelancer ($500-5K)
│   └── PATH B: Brief for Freelancer — structured brief + mood board to hand off
├── User is intermediate+ AND budget = DIY ($0-500)
│   └── PATH C: Designer Self-Build — Figma + Google Fonts + Coolors
└── User has budget = agency ($5K-50K+)
    └── PATH D: Agency Brief — comprehensive RFP-ready brief document
```

| B: Brief for Freelancer | Brief doc + Canva mood board | $500-5,000 | 2-3 hrs brief + designer time | Professional, coherent |

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Brand Foundation Capture

**Duration**: 30-45 minutes
**Tool**: Document editor (Google Docs, Notion, or plain text)

Translate your brand strategy into visual direction by answering these foundational questions. These answers drive every visual decision that follows.

```
VISUAL IDENTITY BRIEF — FOUNDATION
═══════════════════════════════════════
Brand Name:           ___________________
Tagline (if any):     ___________________

1. BRAND PERSONALITY (pick 3-5 from each row, or write your own):
   Modern ○ Classic ○ Playful ○ Serious ○ Luxurious ○ Accessible
   Bold ○ Subtle ○ Warm ○ Cool ○ Edgy ○ Trustworthy
   Minimal ○ Rich ○ Organic ○ Geometric ○ Technical ○ Human

   Selected attributes: _____________, _____________, _____________

2. TARGET AUDIENCE:
   Primary:    ___________________
   Age range:  ___________________
   Context:    ___________________  (where they encounter the brand)

3. COMPETITOR POSITIONING:
   Brand A: ___________ — visual style: ___________
   Brand B: ___________ — visual style: ___________
   Brand C: ___________ — visual style: ___________

   We want to DIFFER from competitors by: ___________
   We want to SHARE with the category: ___________

4. PRIMARY CHANNELS (rank 1-5):
   ___ Website/web app
   ___ Social media (Instagram, LinkedIn, TikTok)
   ___ Print materials (business cards, brochures)
   ___ Product packaging
   ___ Presentations/pitch decks
```

**Verify**: All fields populated with specific answers, not vague aspirations. "Professional" is too generic — "approachable expertise for first-time SaaS buyers" is specific enough to guide design.
**If failed**: If brand personality attributes are unclear, complete a brand positioning exercise first.

### Step 2: Color Palette Definition

**Duration**: 45-60 minutes
**Tool**: Coolors.co + WebAIM Contrast Checker

Build a structured color palette using the 60-30-10 rule: primary color (60% usage), secondary color (30%), accent color (10%), plus neutrals. [src3]

```
COLOR PALETTE SPECIFICATION
═══════════════════════════════════════

Step 2a: Select Primary Color
   Start from brand personality → color psychology mapping:
   Trust/stability → Blue family (#1E40AF to #3B82F6)
   Energy/urgency  → Red family (#DC2626 to #EF4444)
   Growth/health   → Green family (#16A34A to #22C55E)
   Creativity      → Purple family (#7C3AED to #A855F7)
   Warmth/optimism → Orange/Yellow (#EA580C to #F59E0B)
   Luxury/power    → Black + Gold (#1C1917 + #D97706)

Step 2b: Build Full Palette at Coolors.co
   1. Lock your primary color
   2. Generate complementary/analogous combinations
   3. Test 10-15 combinations, save top 3

Step 2c: Document Exact Values
─────────────────────────────────────────
Role        | Name     | HEX     | RGB           | CMYK
Primary     | ________ | #______ | ___,___,___   | __,__,__,__
Secondary   | ________ | #______ | ___,___,___   | __,__,__,__
Accent      | ________ | #______ | ___,___,___   | __,__,__,__
Dark neutral| ________ | #______ | ___,___,___   | __,__,__,__
Light neutral| _______ | #______ | ___,___,___   | __,__,__,__
White       | ________ | #______ | ___,___,___   | __,__,__,__
─────────────────────────────────────────

Step 2d: Accessibility Check (WebAIM)
   Primary on White:    contrast ratio = __:1  Pass/Fail (need ≥ 4.5:1)
   White on Primary:    contrast ratio = __:1  Pass/Fail
   Dark neutral on Light: contrast ratio = __:1  Pass/Fail
   Accent on White:     contrast ratio = __:1  Pass/Fail

Step 2e: Usage Rules
   - Primary: headers, buttons, CTAs, brand marks
   - Secondary: supporting UI elements, section backgrounds
   - Accent: highlights, links, notifications, badges
   - Dark neutral: body text, headings on light backgrounds
   - Light neutral: page backgrounds, card surfaces
```

**Verify**: All 6 color roles defined with exact HEX values. All text-on-background combinations pass WCAG AA at WebAIM.
**If failed**: If contrast fails, adjust lightness/darkness of the specific color. Do not compromise on accessibility — adjust the palette, not the standard.

### Step 3: Typography System

**Duration**: 30-45 minutes
**Tool**: Google Fonts (free) or Adobe Fonts (paid)

Define a two-font system: one for headings (personality), one for body text (readability). [src4]

```
TYPOGRAPHY SPECIFICATION
═══════════════════════════════════════

Step 3a: Select Heading Font (personality-driven)
   Brand personality → font style mapping:
   Modern/clean    → Geometric sans: Inter, Outfit, Plus Jakarta Sans
   Traditional     → Serif: Playfair Display, Lora, Merriweather
   Technical       → Monospace-influenced: JetBrains Mono, Space Mono
   Friendly        → Rounded: Nunito, Quicksand, Comfortaa
   Bold/confident  → High-contrast: Sora, Clash Display, Cabinet Grotesk

Step 3b: Select Body Font (readability-driven)
   Rules: Must be highly legible at 16px, good x-height, open counters
   Safe picks: Inter, Source Sans 3, Noto Sans, IBM Plex Sans, DM Sans
   Serif options: Source Serif 4, Noto Serif, Literata, Charter

Step 3c: Test Pairing
   Classic pairings that work:
   Playfair Display + Source Sans 3 (elegant + clean)
   Sora + Inter (modern + neutral)
   Lora + Noto Sans (warm serif + clean sans)
   Space Grotesk + DM Sans (technical + approachable)

Step 3d: Document Type Scale
─────────────────────────────────────────
Element     | Font         | Weight | Size    | Line Height | Letter Spacing
H1          | ___________  | ____   | ___px   | _____       | _____
H2          | ___________  | ____   | ___px   | _____       | _____
H3          | ___________  | ____   | ___px   | _____       | _____
Body        | ___________  | ____   | 16px    | 1.5         | normal
Body small  | ___________  | ____   | 14px    | 1.5         | normal
Caption     | ___________  | ____   | 12px    | 1.4         | 0.02em
Button      | ___________  | ____   | 14-16px | 1.0         | 0.04em
─────────────────────────────────────────

Step 3e: Font Loading Strategy (web)
   - Use Google Fonts CDN (free) or self-host WOFF2 files
   - Set font-display: swap for performance
   - Define fallback stack: {selected font}, system-ui, sans-serif
```

**Verify**: Heading and body fonts are visually distinct (serif + sans, or contrasting sans-serif weights). Body text is readable at 16px on mobile. Font files are available for all required platforms.
**If failed**: If fonts look too similar, increase contrast — pair a serif heading font with a sans-serif body font.

### Step 4: Logo Requirements Specification

**Duration**: 30-45 minutes
**Tool**: Document editor

Define what the logo must do, how many variations are needed, and exact file deliverables. [src5]

```
LOGO REQUIREMENTS SPECIFICATION
═══════════════════════════════════════

Step 4a: Logo Type Decision
   [ ] Wordmark (text only — e.g., Google, Coca-Cola)
   [ ] Lettermark (initials — e.g., IBM, HBO)
   [ ] Icon + wordmark (symbol + name — e.g., Slack, Spotify)
   [ ] Abstract mark (geometric symbol — e.g., Nike, Pepsi)
   [ ] Emblem (enclosed badge — e.g., Starbucks, Harvard)

Step 4b: Required Variations
   1. Primary logo (full color, horizontal or stacked)
   2. Secondary logo (simplified — icon only or abbreviated)
   3. Favicon (16x16, 32x32, 48x48 px — must be recognizable)
   4. Dark background version (reversed/white version)
   5. Monochrome version (single color for limited contexts)
   6. Social media profile (square crop, 400x400 px minimum)

Step 4c: Clear Space and Minimum Size
   Clear space: minimum padding = height of a key letter (e.g., "x")
                around all sides of logo
   Minimum size: ___px wide for digital, ___mm for print
   (Logo must remain legible at minimum size)

Step 4d: File Deliverables Checklist
─────────────────────────────────────────
Format | Usage              | Variations Needed
SVG    | Web, app, digital  | All 6 variations
EPS    | Print production   | Primary, monochrome
PDF    | Print, documents   | Primary, secondary
PNG    | Social, email, web | All 6 at 1x, 2x, 3x
ICO    | Favicon            | 16, 32, 48px
─────────────────────────────────────────

Step 4e: Usage Rules (what NOT to do)
   - Do not stretch or distort proportions
   - Do not change logo colors outside approved palette
   - Do not place on busy/low-contrast backgrounds
   - Do not add effects (drop shadows, gradients, outlines)
   - Do not rearrange logo elements
   - Do not use below minimum size
```

**Verify**: At least 4 logo variations specified. File format list includes both vector (SVG/EPS) and raster (PNG). Clear space and minimum size are defined.
**If failed**: If unsure about logo type, create a decision matrix scoring each type against brand personality attributes from Step 1.

### Step 5: Imagery Style Direction

**Duration**: 45-60 minutes
**Tool**: Canva, Milanote, or Pinterest (mood board creation)

Define the visual style for photography, illustrations, icons, and graphic elements. [src1]

```
IMAGERY STYLE GUIDE
═══════════════════════════════════════

Step 5a: Photography Direction
   Style:        [ ] Candid/natural  [ ] Styled/polished  [ ] Documentary
   Lighting:     [ ] Bright/airy     [ ] Moody/dramatic    [ ] Natural/soft
   Color grading: [ ] Warm tones     [ ] Cool tones        [ ] Neutral/true
   Subjects:     [ ] People-focused  [ ] Product-focused   [ ] Environment
   Composition:  [ ] Centered/simple [ ] Dynamic/asymmetric [ ] Lifestyle context

   DO use: _________________________________________
   DON'T use: ______________________________________

Step 5b: Illustration / Icon Style
   Style:        [ ] Line art    [ ] Flat/geometric  [ ] 3D/isometric
   Stroke weight: [ ] Thin (1-2px) [ ] Medium (2-3px) [ ] Bold (3px+)
   Color:        [ ] Brand palette only  [ ] Extended palette  [ ] Monochrome
   Complexity:   [ ] Minimal/simple      [ ] Moderate detail   [ ] Rich/detailed

Step 5c: Graphic Elements
   Shapes:       [ ] Rounded/soft  [ ] Sharp/angular  [ ] Organic/irregular
   Patterns:     [ ] None          [ ] Geometric      [ ] Organic/texture
   Borders:      [ ] Rounded (___px radius)  [ ] Sharp  [ ] None
   Shadows:      [ ] None  [ ] Subtle (elevation)  [ ] Dramatic

Step 5d: Create Mood Board
   1. Open Canva or Milanote (free account)
   2. Create board titled "[Brand Name] Visual Identity Mood Board"
   3. Add 10-15 images across these categories:
      - 3-4 images: color palette references
      - 3-4 images: typography in context
      - 3-4 images: photography style reference
      - 2-3 images: overall brand feel / aspirational
   4. Arrange with key reference image at center
   5. Export as PDF and PNG for brief attachment
```

**Verify**: Mood board contains 10-15 images with clear visual consistency. Photography direction is specific enough that two different photographers would produce similar results.
**If failed**: If mood board images feel scattered, remove outliers and identify the common thread. Constrain to one dominant visual theme.

### Step 6: Compile Brief and Delivery Checklist

**Duration**: 30-45 minutes

Assemble all components into a single deliverable document with an asset checklist.

```
VISUAL IDENTITY BRIEF — FINAL ASSEMBLY
═══════════════════════════════════════

Brief Document Structure:
1. Brand Foundation (from Step 1)
   - Brand personality attributes
   - Target audience
   - Competitive positioning
2. Color Palette (from Step 2)
   - Full palette with HEX/RGB/CMYK values
   - Usage rules and ratios (60-30-10)
   - Accessibility verification results
3. Typography System (from Step 3)
   - Font selections with type scale
   - Pairing rationale
   - Web loading strategy
4. Logo Requirements (from Step 4)
   - Logo type and variations needed
   - Clear space and minimum size
   - File format checklist
5. Imagery Direction (from Step 5)
   - Photography/illustration guidelines
   - Mood board (attached)
   - Do/don't examples
6. Asset Delivery Checklist (below)

─────────────────────────────────────────
ASSET DELIVERY CHECKLIST
─────────────────────────────────────────
[ ] Color palette swatch file (ASE or CLR)
[ ] Logo — all variations in SVG, EPS, PNG
[ ] Favicon files (ICO + PNG at 16, 32, 48px)
[ ] Social media profile/cover templates
[ ] Font files or CDN links
[ ] Type scale reference sheet
[ ] Mood board (PDF + PNG)
[ ] Brand guidelines document (PDF)
[ ] Business card template
[ ] Email signature template
[ ] Presentation template (Google Slides / Keynote)
[ ] Social media post templates (3-5 formats)
─────────────────────────────────────────

BUDGET TIER GUIDANCE:
DIY ($0-500):
   Execute Steps 1-5 yourself using free tools.
   Optional: $50-200 for a Fiverr logo.
   Timeline: 1-2 weeks part-time.

Freelancer ($500-5,000):
   Complete Steps 1-5 as the brief, hand to freelancer.
   Freelancer delivers logo + guidelines + templates.
   Timeline: 2-4 weeks.

Boutique Agency ($5,000-20,000):
   Provide Steps 1 and 5 (foundation + mood board).
   Agency handles palette, typography, logo, full guidelines.
   Timeline: 4-8 weeks.

Full-Service Agency ($20,000-50,000+):
   Provide Step 1 (brand foundation) only.
   Agency conducts research, builds complete identity system.
   Timeline: 8-16 weeks.
```

**Output files**:
- `visual-identity-brief.pdf` — Complete brief document with all 5 sections
- `mood-board.pdf` — Reference mood board with 10-15 images
- `asset-checklist.md` — Tracking checklist for all deliverable assets
- `color-palette.json` — Machine-readable palette specification

## Quality Benchmarks

| Quality Metric | Minimum Acceptable | Good | Excellent |
|---------------|-------------------|------|-----------|
| Color palette completeness | Primary + secondary defined | 5 colors with HEX values | Full palette with HEX, RGB, CMYK + accessibility verified |
| Typography specification | Font names chosen | Fonts + size scale documented | Full type scale + weights + line heights + fallbacks |
| Logo requirements | Logo type decided | Variations + formats listed | Full spec with clear space, min size, usage rules |
| Imagery direction | General mood described | Mood board with 5+ images | 10-15 image mood board + specific do/don't guidelines |
| Brief usability | Notes for self-reference | Organized enough for freelancer | Agency-ready RFP with all specs, mood board, and checklist |

**If below minimum**: Complete the specific step worksheet before proceeding. A brief with vague color descriptions ("blue-ish") or missing font specifications will produce inconsistent results regardless of who executes it.

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Color palette feels generic or similar to competitor | Started with common industry colors without differentiation | Return to Step 2a, choose a non-obvious color family. Use competitor audit to identify avoided colors |
| Fonts look too similar (weak hierarchy) | Selected two fonts from same classification | Replace one font with different classification: pair serif heading + sans body, or vice versa [src4] |
| WCAG contrast check fails for primary color | Primary color too light or too saturated | Darken the color for text use; keep bright version for backgrounds/accents only |
| Mood board images feel scattered/incoherent | Mixed too many visual directions | Remove bottom 5 images, identify the dominant thread, add 3-5 more in that direction only |
| Logo brief too vague for designer | Missing variation specs or format requirements | Complete Step 4 checklist in full — designers need explicit deliverable lists, not creative direction alone |
| Budget misaligned with expectations | Scope exceeds budget tier | Either reduce scope (fewer deliverables) or increase budget. DIY path cannot produce agency-quality results |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Choosing colors based on personal preference alone
Selecting brand colors because the founder "likes blue" without considering color psychology, competitor differentiation, or accessibility. Result: a palette that blends into the competitive landscape and fails contrast requirements on half the website. [src3]

### Correct: Strategic color selection with accessibility verification
Start from brand personality attributes, map to color psychology, check against competitor palettes for differentiation, then verify every text-on-background combination against WCAG AA standards before finalizing.

### Wrong: Using 4+ fonts across the brand
Mixing multiple typefaces for variety leads to visual inconsistency, slower page loads, and guidelines that are impossible to follow. Each new font adds 50-150KB of web weight and another variable for designers to misapply. [src4]

### Correct: Strict two-font system with weight variation
Select one heading font and one body font. Use weight (bold, semibold, regular, light) and size variation within those two families to create hierarchy. This produces cleaner results and is dramatically easier to maintain.

### Wrong: Delivering only PNG logo files
Raster-only logos pixelate when scaled up for print, banners, or presentations. Many founders pay for a logo and receive only JPG/PNG files that are unusable at larger sizes. [src5]

### Correct: Require vector deliverables as primary format
SVG and EPS are non-negotiable. Every designer or design tool can produce them. Raster PNG files are generated FROM vectors, not the other way around.

### Wrong: Skipping the brief and going straight to design
Hiring a designer or opening Canva without a structured brief produces endless revision cycles. Without defined brand attributes, color direction, and logo specs, the designer is guessing — and will guess wrong on the first 2-3 attempts.

### Correct: Complete the brief first, then execute or delegate
Even a 2-hour brief dramatically reduces revision cycles. Designers who receive a structured brief with mood board typically deliver acceptable first drafts 3x more often than those working from a vague conversation. [src8]

## When This Matters

Use this recipe when a brand needs to go from zero (or scattered) visual assets to a structured, production-ready visual identity. The output is a complete brief that can be self-executed (DIY), handed to a freelancer, or used as an agency RFP. Requires brand positioning/strategy as input — do not start visual identity work before the brand's audience, values, and personality are defined.
