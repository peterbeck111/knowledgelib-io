# Brand Messaging Framework

## Purpose

This recipe produces a complete brand messaging framework document containing a validated positioning statement, 3-4 value propositions with supporting proof points, competitive differentiators, and a messaging hierarchy that cascades from strategic narrative down to feature-level messages. The output is a single-source-of-truth document that marketing, sales, and product teams use to maintain consistent messaging across all channels. [src1]

## Prerequisites

- [ ] **Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)** available from market research or persona-builder agent -- must include target segment, jobs-to-be-done, pain points, and desired outcomes
- [ ] **Competitive landscape analysis** -- top 3-5 competitors identified with their positioning and key claims
- [ ] **Product/service feature list** -- complete inventory of capabilities, features, and unique attributes
- [ ] **Customer interview data** -- minimum 10 customer/prospect conversations with documented quotes and pain points
- [ ] **Google Docs, Notion, or Markdown editor** -- for framework document creation
- [ ] **Spreadsheet tool** -- Google Sheets or Excel for competitive messaging audit

## Constraints

- Positioning must be based on validated customer research -- statements built on assumptions fail when tested. Minimum 10 customer interviews required before finalizing. [src1]
- Each value proposition requires at least 2 verifiable proof points. Claims without evidence are marketing fluff that erodes trust. [src6]
- Messaging must differentiate from actual competitors. If a claim applies equally to your top 3 competitors, it is not a differentiator -- it is table stakes. [src1]
- Test messaging with target audience before full adoption. Qualitative testing with 9-17 respondents is sufficient for saturation. [src3]
- Review and update quarterly. Messaging frameworks decay when products evolve, competitors reposition, or market conditions shift.

## Tool Selection Decision

```
Which path?
├── User has extensive customer research AND clear competitors
│   └── PATH A: Research-First — extract positioning from existing data
├── User has some research AND needs competitive differentiation
│   └── PATH B: Competitive-Led — audit competitors first, find white space
├── User is pre-launch AND has minimal customer data
│   └── PATH C: Hypothesis-Driven — build draft, validate with prospects
└── User is repositioning/rebranding AND has existing messaging
    └── PATH D: Audit-and-Rebuild — assess current messaging, rebuild gaps
```

| Path | Tools | Cost | Time | Output Quality |
|------|-------|------|-------|---------------|
| A: Research-First | Docs + interview transcripts | $0 | 8-12 hours | Highest — grounded in real data |
| B: Competitive-Led | Docs + spreadsheet + competitor sites | $0 | 10-15 hours | High — strong differentiation |
| C: Hypothesis-Driven | Docs + Wynter/surveys | $0-599 | 12-20 hours | Moderate — requires validation |
| D: Audit-and-Rebuild | Docs + existing materials | $0 | 6-10 hours | High — builds on proven elements |

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Competitive Messaging Audit

**Duration**: 2-3 hours
**Tool**: Spreadsheet + competitor websites

Map what competitors actually say to identify claimed vs. unclaimed positioning space.

```
COMPETITIVE MESSAGING AUDIT MATRIX
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Competitor | Positioning | Value Props | Proof Points | Tone
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
[Comp 1]   | "..."       | 1. ...      | - Case study | Professional
           |             | 2. ...      | - Stat: ...  |
           |             | 3. ...      | - Award: ... |
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
[Comp 2]   | "..."       | 1. ...      | - ...        | ...
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
[Comp 3]   | "..."       | 1. ...      | - ...        | ...
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ANALYSIS:
Claims ALL competitors make (table stakes):
  1. ___________________________________
  2. ___________________________________

Claims NO competitor makes (white space):
  1. ___________________________________
  2. ___________________________________

Claims only ONE competitor owns:
  1. ___________________________________
```

**Verify**: Audit covers at least 3 direct competitors with data from their website, sales materials, and product pages.
**If failed**: If fewer than 3 competitors exist (new category), shift to category-creation positioning per April Dunford's framework. [src1]

### Step 2: Customer Insight Extraction

**Duration**: 2-4 hours
**Tool**: Interview notes, survey data, review mining

Extract the language customers actually use to describe their problems and your solution. This is the raw material for messaging. [src3]

```
CUSTOMER INSIGHT EXTRACTION WORKSHEET
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Source: [Customer interviews / surveys / reviews / support tickets]

JOBS-TO-BE-DONE (what they are trying to accomplish):
  1. "___________________________________" (frequency: ___/10 interviews)
  2. "___________________________________" (frequency: ___/10)
  3. "___________________________________" (frequency: ___/10)

PAINS (what frustrates them about current solutions):
  1. "___________________________________" (frequency: ___/10)
  2. "___________________________________" (frequency: ___/10)
  3. "___________________________________" (frequency: ___/10)

GAINS (outcomes they desire):
  1. "___________________________________" (frequency: ___/10)
  2. "___________________________________" (frequency: ___/10)
  3. "___________________________________" (frequency: ___/10)

EXACT PHRASES customers use (mine these for copy):
  - "___________________________________"
  - "___________________________________"
  - "___________________________________"

WHY THEY CHOSE YOU (for existing customers):
  1. "___________________________________" (frequency: ___/10)
  2. "___________________________________" (frequency: ___/10)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

**Verify**: Insights come from actual customer conversations, not internal assumptions. Each insight should appear in at least 3 separate interviews to indicate a pattern.
**If failed**: If fewer than 10 data points exist, run 5-10 prospect interviews before proceeding. Use the jobs, pains, and gains framework from the Value Proposition Canvas. [src4]

### Step 3: Build the Positioning Statement

**Duration**: 1-2 hours
**Tool**: Document editor

Craft a positioning statement using the proven formula. This is an internal alignment tool, not a tagline. [src1]

```
POSITIONING STATEMENT FORMULA
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
For [TARGET CUSTOMER — specific segment from ICP]
who [NEED/PROBLEM — primary job-to-be-done or pain from Step 2]
[PRODUCT/BRAND NAME] is a [MARKET CATEGORY]
that [KEY BENEFIT — primary value delivered]
unlike [PRIMARY ALTERNATIVE — what they use today]
our product [KEY DIFFERENTIATOR — what you do that alternatives cannot].

EXAMPLE:
For mid-market SaaS companies
who struggle to convert free trial users to paid customers
Acme Analytics is a product-led growth platform
that identifies high-intent users and triggers personalized conversion flows
unlike generic analytics tools
our platform combines behavioral scoring with automated intervention.
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

POSITIONING VALIDATION CHECKLIST:
  [ ] Target customer is specific (not "everyone")
  [ ] Need comes from customer research, not assumption
  [ ] Category is recognizable (buyers can compare)
  [ ] Benefit is outcome-focused, not feature-focused
  [ ] Differentiator is verifiable and not claimed by competitors
  [ ] Statement is 25-35 words (excluding example labels)
```

**Verify**: Read the positioning statement to 3 people unfamiliar with your product. They should be able to explain back: (1) who it is for, (2) what it does, (3) why it is different. If they cannot, rewrite. [src1]
**If failed**: If clarity fails, the category choice is likely wrong. Reconsider whether you are defining the category correctly -- sometimes shifting the category frame changes everything.

### Step 4: Define Value Propositions and Messaging Pillars

**Duration**: 2-3 hours
**Tool**: Document editor

Create 3-4 messaging pillars, each with a value proposition, supporting messages, and proof points. The hierarchy flows top-down. [src5]

```
MESSAGING HIERARCHY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

LEVEL 1 — STRATEGIC NARRATIVE (why this category matters now)
  "____________________________________________"
  (1-2 sentences — the market shift or trend that creates urgency)

LEVEL 2 — POSITIONING STATEMENT (from Step 3)
  "____________________________________________"

LEVEL 3 — VALUE PROPOSITIONS (3-4 pillars)

  PILLAR 1: [Theme Name — e.g., "Speed"]
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  Value Statement: "____________________________________________"
    (One sentence: what benefit + for whom + measured how)
  Supporting Message 1: "____________________________________________"
  Supporting Message 2: "____________________________________________"
  Proof Points:
    - [Data point]: "____________________________________________"
    - [Customer quote]: "____________________________________________"
    - [Third-party validation]: "____________________________________________"

  PILLAR 2: [Theme Name — e.g., "Simplicity"]
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  Value Statement: "____________________________________________"
  Supporting Message 1: "____________________________________________"
  Supporting Message 2: "____________________________________________"
  Proof Points:
    - [Data point]: "____________________________________________"
    - [Customer quote]: "____________________________________________"
    - [Case study]: "____________________________________________"

  PILLAR 3: [Theme Name — e.g., "ROI"]
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  Value Statement: "____________________________________________"
  Supporting Message 1: "____________________________________________"
  Supporting Message 2: "____________________________________________"
  Proof Points:
    - [Data point]: "____________________________________________"
    - [Customer quote]: "____________________________________________"
    - [Benchmark]: "____________________________________________"

LEVEL 4 — FEATURE MESSAGES (specific capabilities per pillar)
  Pillar 1 features: [Feature A → benefit], [Feature B → benefit]
  Pillar 2 features: [Feature C → benefit], [Feature D → benefit]
  Pillar 3 features: [Feature E → benefit], [Feature F → benefit]

LEVEL 5 — PROOF POINTS (evidence for every claim)
  Types: customer quotes, case studies, performance data,
         third-party reviews, awards, certifications
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

**Verify**: Each value proposition answers three questions: (1) What benefit? (2) For whom specifically? (3) How is it measured or proven? If any answer is missing, the value proposition is incomplete. [src2]
**If failed**: If you cannot find 2 proof points per pillar, that pillar is aspirational, not real. Either find proof or demote it to a supporting message under another pillar.

### Step 5: Competitive Differentiation Mapping

**Duration**: 1-2 hours
**Tool**: Spreadsheet

Map each messaging pillar against competitor claims to confirm genuine differentiation. [src1]

```
DIFFERENTIATION VALIDATION MATRIX
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
                  | You | Comp 1 | Comp 2 | Comp 3 |
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Pillar 1 claim    | [Y] | [Y/N]  | [Y/N]  | [Y/N]  |
Pillar 2 claim    | [Y] | [Y/N]  | [Y/N]  | [Y/N]  |
Pillar 3 claim    | [Y] | [Y/N]  | [Y/N]  | [Y/N]  |
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

DIFFERENTIATION TIERS:
  UNIQUE — only you can claim this (strongest)
  SUPERIOR — others claim it but you prove it better
  PARITY — everyone claims it (drop as differentiator)

For each "PARITY" result:
  → Reframe the claim with specificity that competitors lack
  → OR replace with a claim from the white space analysis (Step 1)
  → OR accept as table stakes and do not position as differentiator
```

**Verify**: At least 2 of 3 pillars score "UNIQUE" or "SUPERIOR." If all pillars are "PARITY," the positioning needs rework.
**If failed**: Return to Step 1 competitive audit. Look for attributes competitors have that you lack, then invert -- sometimes what you chose NOT to build is your differentiator.

### Step 6: Message Testing

**Duration**: 3-5 days (calendar time for recruiting and collecting responses)
**Tool**: Wynter, Typeform, or manual outreach

Test your messaging with 9-17 members of your target audience before adoption. Measure four dimensions. [src3]

```
MESSAGE TESTING PROTOCOL
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

SAMPLE: 9-17 members of target audience (not employees, not friends)

MATERIALS TO TEST:
  1. Positioning statement (from Step 3)
  2. Top 3 value propositions (from Step 4)
  3. Homepage/landing page draft copy (if available)

QUESTIONS PER MESSAGE (open-ended):
  CLARITY:
    - "What do you think this product/service does?"
    - "What, if anything, is confusing?"

  RELEVANCE:
    - "How relevant is this to your work/life?"
    - "Does this describe a problem you actually have?"

  DIFFERENTIATION:
    - "How is this different from [competitor] or your current solution?"
    - "What stands out as unique?"

  CREDIBILITY:
    - "Do you believe these claims? Why or why not?"
    - "What evidence would make this more convincing?"

ANALYSIS:
  For each message, tally:
    Clarity score:         ___/5 (avg across respondents)
    Relevance score:       ___/5
    Differentiation score: ___/5
    Credibility score:     ___/5

  PASS = all dimensions ≥ 3.5/5
  REVISE = any dimension < 3.5 (rewrite and retest)
  FAIL = clarity < 3.0 (fundamental positioning problem)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

**Verify**: Responses come from target audience members, not internal stakeholders. Internal feedback biases toward confirmation.
**If failed**: If clarity scores below 3.0, the problem is usually the category frame (Step 3) or jargon in the value propositions (Step 4). Rewrite using exact customer language from Step 2.

### Step 7: Finalize and Distribute Framework Document

**Duration**: 1-2 hours
**Tool**: Document editor

Compile the tested, validated messaging into a single-source-of-truth document.

```
BRAND MESSAGING FRAMEWORK — [Company Name]
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Version: 1.0
Date: [YYYY-MM-DD]
Owner: [Name/Role]
Review cycle: Quarterly

1. STRATEGIC NARRATIVE
   [From Step 4, Level 1]

2. POSITIONING STATEMENT
   [Validated statement from Step 3 + Step 6]

3. TARGET AUDIENCE
   [From ICP — segment, role, pain points]

4. MESSAGING PILLARS
   [3-4 pillars from Step 4, each with:]
   - Value statement
   - Supporting messages (2 per pillar)
   - Proof points (2-3 per pillar)
   - Feature messages (2-3 per pillar)

5. COMPETITIVE DIFFERENTIATORS
   [From Step 5 — unique/superior claims only]

6. BRAND VOICE AND TONE NOTES
   [Brief guidance: formal/casual, technical/accessible, etc.]

7. MESSAGING DO'S AND DON'TS
   [Common misuses to avoid]

DISTRIBUTION:
  → Marketing team: full document
  → Sales team: positioning + pillars + proof points
  → Product team: positioning + feature messages
  → Executives: strategic narrative + positioning
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

**Output files**:
- `messaging-framework-v1.md` -- Complete messaging framework document
- `positioning-statement.md` -- Standalone positioning statement for quick reference
- `message-testing-results.md` -- Testing feedback and iteration log

## Quality Benchmarks

| Quality Metric | Minimum Acceptable | Good | Excellent |
|---------------|-------------------|------|-----------|
| Customer research inputs | 10 interviews | 20 interviews + survey | 50+ multi-source data points |
| Proof points per pillar | 2 per pillar | 3 per pillar | 4+ per pillar with mix of types |
| Message test clarity score | 3.5/5 | 4.0/5 | 4.5+/5 |
| Differentiator strength | 1 unique claim | 2 unique claims | 3 unique + all pillars superior |
| Internal adoption | Marketing uses it | Marketing + sales use it | All customer-facing teams aligned |

**If below minimum**: Re-run customer research (Step 2) and message testing (Step 6). Messaging frameworks built without customer input fail 80% of the time.

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Cannot identify differentiators from competitors | Product lacks genuine differentiation or wrong category frame | Reframe market category — shifting category changes what "different" means. Consider a sub-category or new category creation. [src1] |
| Customer interviews yield contradictory insights | Talking to multiple personas as if they were one | Segment interview data by persona/role, build persona-specific messaging layers |
| Positioning statement fails clarity test | Too much jargon or trying to say too many things | Reduce to single primary benefit, use customer language verbatim from Step 2 |
| Proof points are weak or aspirational | Product is early-stage with limited traction | Use design partner quotes, beta metrics, or industry benchmark comparisons as interim proof |
| Internal teams reject the framework | Built without stakeholder input | Run a positioning workshop with stakeholders before finalizing — get buy-in early, not at delivery |
| Value propositions all sound the same | Pillars are too similar or derived from features not outcomes | Restructure pillars around customer outcomes (speed, cost, risk) rather than product features |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Building messaging from features instead of customer outcomes
Starting with your product feature list and describing what each feature does produces messaging that sounds like a spec sheet. Customers do not buy features -- they buy outcomes and solutions to their problems. [src2]

### Correct: Start from customer pain points and work backward
Extract jobs-to-be-done and pain points from customer interviews (Step 2), then map features to outcomes. The messaging hierarchy flows from customer need to your solution, not from your product to its specs.

### Wrong: Using internal language instead of customer language
Teams that build messaging in conference rooms produce jargon-heavy copy that scores below 3.0 on clarity tests. Internal terms like "unified platform" or "end-to-end solution" mean nothing to buyers. [src3]

### Correct: Mine customer interviews for exact phrases
Pull direct quotes from customer conversations and use their vocabulary. If customers say "I waste 2 hours a day on manual data entry," your messaging should mirror that language, not translate it into corporate-speak.

### Wrong: Treating the messaging framework as a one-time deliverable
Frameworks created and never updated become fiction within 6-12 months as products evolve, competitors reposition, and market conditions change.

### Correct: Schedule quarterly reviews with framework owner
Assign ownership to a specific person. Review quarterly against: new customer feedback, competitive moves, product changes, and message testing data. Version the document and communicate updates.

## When This Matters

Use this recipe when an agent or founder needs to produce a validated brand messaging framework from scratch or rebuild one that is not working. It requires customer research data as input and produces a structured document that all customer-facing teams can reference for consistent messaging.
