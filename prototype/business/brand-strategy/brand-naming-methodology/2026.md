# Brand Naming Methodology

## Purpose

This recipe produces a scored shortlist of 3-5 startup name candidates, each validated for domain availability, trademark conflicts, linguistic safety, and social handle availability — ready for final selection, domain registration, and trademark filing. The output replaces gut-feel naming with a structured, risk-aware process that prevents the two most expensive naming mistakes: trademark infringement and unusable domains.

## Prerequisites

- [ ] **Business concept or value proposition** available from founder or `agents/startup/idea-structuring` — [Startup Idea Structuring Template](/business/startup-planning/startup-idea-structuring-template/2026)
- [ ] **Target customer profile** available from founder or `agents/startup/persona-builder` — defines the audience whose perception matters most
- [ ] **Geographic scope decision** — which countries/regions need trademark protection (affects which databases to search)
- [ ] **Web browser** — all screening tools are web-based, no software installation required
- [ ] **Spreadsheet tool** — Google Sheets, Excel, or Notion for the scoring matrix

## Constraints

- Trademark search at USPTO covers US federal marks only. Common-law rights (unregistered marks) require additional web searches and state-level database checks. [src3]
- The USPTO replaced TEAS Plus and TEAS Standard with a single base filing fee of $350 per class of goods/services, effective January 18, 2025. Additional surcharges of $100-$200 per class apply for insufficient information or free-text descriptions not in the Trademark ID Manual. [src4]
- WIPO Global Brand Database contains 50+ million records across 70+ databases but does not include all national trademark registrations — supplement with direct searches of key national IP offices. [src5]
- Domain aftermarket prices are unpredictable. Set a hard ceiling before searching to prevent emotional overspend on "perfect" .com names.
- Social media handles can be squatted within days of a name becoming publicly associated with a funded startup. Reserve handles immediately upon shortlisting, before any public announcement.

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Define Naming Criteria

**Duration**: 30-45 minutes
**Tool**: Spreadsheet

Before brainstorming, define what a good name looks like for this specific startup. This prevents subjective arguments later.

```
NAMING CRITERIA WORKSHEET
═══════════════════════════════════════
Define the brand positioning inputs:

1. What does the company DO? (one sentence)
   ___________________________________________

2. Who is the primary customer?
   ___________________________________________

3. What feeling should the name evoke?
   [ ] Trust/reliability  [ ] Innovation/speed
   [ ] Warmth/care        [ ] Power/authority
   [ ] Fun/playfulness    [ ] Simplicity/clarity

4. What naming style fits the brand?
   [ ] Descriptive — e.g., Whole Foods, General Electric
   [ ] Evocative — e.g., Nike, Patagonia, Airbnb
   [ ] Invented — e.g., Kodak, Spotify, Xerox
   [ ] Compound — e.g., Facebook, YouTube, PayPal
   [ ] Founder — e.g., Ford, Disney, Dell
   [ ] Acronym — e.g., IBM, BMW, NASA
   [ ] Metaphorical — e.g., Apple, Amazon, Target
   [ ] Playful/Lexical — e.g., Krispy Kreme, Dribbble

5. Hard requirements:
   [ ] Must have .com available (or budget to acquire)
   [ ] Must work in languages: __________________
   [ ] Must be ≤ N syllables: __________________
   [ ] Must avoid similarity to: ________________
```

**Verify**: All 5 sections completed with specific answers, not "TBD."
**If failed**: If the founder cannot articulate positioning (questions 1-3), pause naming and complete brand positioning first.

### Step 2: Brainstorm Name Candidates

**Duration**: 1-3 hours (then 48-hour cooling period)
**Tool**: Whiteboard/doc + AI generators

Generate a long list of 30-50 candidates using multiple approaches. Do not evaluate during brainstorming — volume first, filtering later. [src2]

```
BRAINSTORMING FRAMEWORK
═══════════════════════════════════════
Round 1: Descriptive seeds (15 min)
  List words that describe what you do:
  _____, _____, _____, _____, _____

Round 2: Evocative associations (15 min)
  List words that describe how it FEELS:
  _____, _____, _____, _____, _____

Round 3: Metaphor exploration (15 min)
  What is your product like? (animals, nature, mythology)
  _____, _____, _____, _____, _____

Round 4: Compound combinations (15 min)
  Combine pairs from Rounds 1-3:
  _____, _____, _____, _____, _____

Round 5: Invented words (15 min)
  Blend syllables, add suffixes (-ly, -ify, -io, -ize):
  _____, _____, _____, _____, _____

Round 6: AI generation (15 min)
  Input keywords into Namelix, Squadhelp, or ChatGPT.
  Prompt: "Generate 20 brand names for a [description]
  that feel [adjective]. Mix styles: invented words,
  metaphors, and compound names."
  _____, _____, _____, _____, _____

FULL CANDIDATE LIST (aim for 30-50):
1. ____________  11. ____________  21. ____________
2. ____________  12. ____________  22. ____________
...
```

Wait 48 hours before evaluating. Names that seemed brilliant in the brainstorm session often lose appeal — and overlooked names gain it. [src2]

**Verify**: Candidate list has 30+ names spanning at least 3 different naming styles.
**If failed**: If under 20 candidates, run another brainstorm session with different participants or a different AI tool.

### Step 3: Screen Linguistically

**Duration**: 1-2 hours
**Tool**: Google Translate + native speaker checks

Check every shortlisted name for unintended meanings, pronunciation problems, and cultural issues in target markets. [src6]

```
LINGUISTIC SCREENING CHECKLIST
═══════════════════════════════════════
For each candidate name, check:

□ Pronounceability test
  - Can a native English speaker say it correctly on first try?
  - Can speakers of target languages say it?
  - Is there only ONE obvious pronunciation?

□ Spelling test
  - Can someone spell it after hearing it once?
  - No ambiguous letters (is it -er or -ar? -ie or -y?)

□ Meaning check in target languages
  - Google Translate the name into: Spanish, French,
    German, Portuguese, Mandarin, Japanese, Arabic
  - Search "[name] meaning in [language]" for slang/vulgar hits
  - Check: does it sound like a negative word phonetically?

□ Competitor similarity check
  - Google "[name] + [industry]" — any conflicts?
  - Say the name aloud — does it sound like a competitor?

□ Visual check
  - Write it in lowercase: any unfortunate letter combinations?
    (e.g., "therapist finder" → "therapistfinder.com")
  - Write it in ALL CAPS: still readable?

SCREENING RESULTS:
Name            | Pronounce | Spell | Meaning | Visual | PASS?
________________|___________|_______|_________|________|______
________________|___________|_______|_________|________|______
________________|___________|_______|_________|________|______
```

**Verify**: All candidates passing linguistic screening have been checked in at least the top 5 languages for the target market.
**If failed**: If a name fails pronunciation or has negative foreign-language meaning, eliminate it immediately — these problems do not diminish with brand investment.

### Step 4: Check Domain Availability

**Duration**: 30-60 minutes
**Tool**: Namecheap, GoDaddy, or Dynadot

Check domain availability for all linguistically-cleared candidates. [src7]

```
DOMAIN AVAILABILITY CHECK
═══════════════════════════════════════
For each candidate, check in this priority order:

1. ExactMatch.com — the gold standard
   Search at: namecheap.com, godaddy.com, or dynadot.com

2. If .com is taken, check:
   - Is it actively used or parked?
   - Is it for sale? At what price?
   - Can you buy it via broker? (Sedo, Afternic, Dan.com)

3. Alternative TLD options (in preference order):
   - .co — closest to .com, widely recognized
   - .io — strong for tech/developer companies
   - .ai — strong for AI companies
   - .app — good for software products
   - .dev — good for developer tools
   - Industry-specific: .health, .finance, .legal, etc.

4. Domain hack options:
   - e.g., del.icio.us, bit.ly, instagr.am
   - Creative but harder to communicate verbally

DOMAIN RESULTS:
Name        | .com    | Price  | .co/.io | Alt TLD | Notes
____________|_________|________|_________|_________|______
____________|_________|________|_________|_________|______
____________|_________|________|_________|_________|______

Budget ceiling for domain acquisition: $________
```

**Verify**: Each finalist name has at least one viable domain option within budget. Any aftermarket domain under negotiation has been price-checked on comparable sales.
**If failed**: If the .com is taken by an active business in a related industry, the name is likely a trademark risk too — eliminate it.

### Step 5: Search Trademarks

**Duration**: 1-2 hours
**Tool**: USPTO Trademark Search + WIPO Global Brand Database

Check for existing trademark registrations and pending applications that would block your use. [src3]

```
TRADEMARK SEARCH PROCESS
═══════════════════════════════════════

Step 5a: USPTO Search (US marks)
  URL: https://tmsearch.uspto.gov/
  - Search exact name
  - Search phonetic variants (sounds-like)
  - Search with wildcard: name* and *name
  - Check BOTH Live AND Dead marks
  - Note the Nice Classification classes relevant to your business
    (Class 9: software, Class 35: advertising/business, Class 42: SaaS)

Step 5b: WIPO Global Brand Database (international)
  URL: https://branddb.wipo.int/
  - Search exact name across all member countries
  - Filter by Nice Classification relevant to your business
  - Check Madrid System registrations

Step 5c: EUIPO (if EU market)
  URL: https://euipo.europa.eu/eSearch/
  - Search EU trademark register
  - Check both word marks and figurative marks

Step 5d: Common-law search
  - Google: "[name]" + your industry keywords
  - Check state business registries (SOS websites)
  - Search LinkedIn, Crunchbase, AngelList for companies using the name

TRADEMARK RESULTS:
Name     | USPTO  | WIPO   | EUIPO  | Common Law | Risk Level
_________|________|________|________|____________|___________
_________|________|________|________|____________|___________

Risk levels:
  CLEAR   = No conflicts found in any database
  CAUTION = Dead mark or distant industry — consult attorney
  BLOCKED = Live mark in same or related class — eliminate
```

**Verify**: Every finalist has been searched in USPTO + WIPO at minimum. Any "CAUTION" results have a clear rationale for why they are not blocking.
**If failed**: If you find a live mark in your Nice Classification class, the name is blocked — do not proceed. For "CAUTION" cases, budget $500-1,500 for a professional trademark opinion from an IP attorney before committing.

### Step 6: Check Social Handle Availability

**Duration**: 15-30 minutes
**Tool**: Namechk or Namecheckr

Check username availability across social platforms for all trademark-cleared names.

```
SOCIAL HANDLE CHECK
═══════════════════════════════════════
URL: https://namechk.com/ (checks 100+ platforms)
Alternative: https://www.namecheckr.com/

Priority platforms to check:
  □ X / Twitter
  □ Instagram
  □ LinkedIn (company page)
  □ Facebook (page)
  □ YouTube
  □ TikTok
  □ GitHub (if tech company)
  □ Reddit (subreddit)

HANDLE RESULTS:
Name     | X   | IG  | LI  | FB  | YT  | TikTok | Available %
_________|_____|_____|_____|_____|_____|________|____________
_________|_____|_____|_____|_____|_____|________|____________

NOTE: If exact handle is taken, check variants:
  - get[name], [name]hq, [name]app, try[name], use[name]

IMPORTANT: Reserve available handles IMMEDIATELY.
Create accounts on all priority platforms within 24 hours
of shortlisting. You can always delete them later.
```

**Verify**: Each finalist has 70%+ handle availability across priority platforms, or acceptable variants exist.
**If failed**: If a name has poor social availability and no good variants, deprioritize it unless domain and trademark signals are exceptionally strong.

### Step 7: Score and Select Final Name

**Duration**: 30-45 minutes
**Tool**: Scoring spreadsheet

Apply the scoring matrix to all candidates that passed Steps 3-6. [src1] [src8]

```
NAME SCORING MATRIX
═══════════════════════════════════════
Score each criterion 1-5 (1=poor, 5=excellent)

Criterion          | Weight | Name A | Name B | Name C
___________________|________|________|________|_______
Memorability       | 0.15   |  ___   |  ___   |  ___
Pronounceability   | 0.10   |  ___   |  ___   |  ___
Distinctiveness    | 0.15   |  ___   |  ___   |  ___
Brand fit          | 0.15   |  ___   |  ___   |  ___
Domain quality     | 0.15   |  ___   |  ___   |  ___
Trademark safety   | 0.15   |  ___   |  ___   |  ___
Social handles     | 0.05   |  ___   |  ___   |  ___
Scalability        | 0.10   |  ___   |  ___   |  ___
___________________|________|________|________|_______
WEIGHTED TOTAL     | 1.00   |  ___   |  ___   |  ___

Scoring guidance:
  Memorability:    Can someone recall it after hearing once?
  Pronounceability: One obvious pronunciation, easy to say
  Distinctiveness: Stands out from competitors in the space
  Brand fit:       Aligns with positioning and audience
  Domain quality:  .com available or affordable, short URL
  Trademark safety: CLEAR in USPTO/WIPO, no common-law conflicts
  Social handles:  Available on priority platforms
  Scalability:     Works if company expands to new products/markets

RECOMMENDATION:
  Winner: _________________ (Score: ___/5.00)
  Runner-up: ______________ (Score: ___/5.00)
  Reserve: ________________ (Score: ___/5.00)

NEXT STEPS:
  □ Register domain for winner + runner-up
  □ Reserve social handles for winner
  □ File USPTO trademark application (Class ___) — $350/class
  □ If international: file Madrid Protocol application — $600/class
```

**Verify**: At least 3 people (including non-founders) have independently scored the finalists. Average scores to reduce individual bias.
**If failed**: If top two names score within 0.2 points, they are statistically tied — choose the one with stronger trademark and domain signals, as those are the hardest to fix later.

## Quality Benchmarks

| Quality Metric | Minimum Acceptable | Good | Excellent |
|---------------|-------------------|------|-----------|
| Candidate pool size | 20+ brainstormed | 30+ brainstormed | 50+ from multiple methods |
| Linguistic screening depth | English + 2 languages | English + 5 languages | Native speaker review in all target markets |
| Trademark search coverage | USPTO only | USPTO + WIPO | USPTO + WIPO + EUIPO + common law |
| Scoring participants | Founder only | 2-3 people | 5+ including target customers |
| Social handle availability | 50%+ platforms | 70%+ platforms | 90%+ platforms |

**If below minimum**: Re-run brainstorm with broader inputs (more participants, different AI tools, different naming styles) and expand trademark search to WIPO at minimum.

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| All .com domains taken for shortlisted names | Common words or popular naming patterns used | Shift to invented or compound names — higher trademark and domain availability |
| USPTO shows live mark in same class | Name already protected in your industry | Eliminate immediately — do not attempt to "design around" a live mark without IP counsel |
| Name has negative meaning in key target language | Skipped linguistic screening or missed a language | Eliminate and run more thorough screening on remaining candidates |
| Social handles squatted after name leaked | Name discussed publicly before handles reserved | Reserve handles for all finalists before any public discussion, investor pitch, or domain purchase |
| Scoring produces a tie between finalists | Criteria weights too evenly distributed | Break ties using trademark safety first, then domain quality — these are hardest to change later |
| Domain owner asking unreasonable price | Premium .com, no alternatives considered | Set walk-away price before negotiation. Consider .co or .io plus plan to acquire .com post-funding |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Falling in love with one name before checking availability
Founders frequently brainstorm a "perfect" name, build emotional attachment, then discover the .com costs $25,000, there is a live trademark in Class 42, and the Instagram handle is taken by an inactive account. Sunk-cost bias then leads to overspending or legal risk. [src2]

### Correct: Generate 30+ candidates before checking any availability
Treat naming as a funnel. Start wide, filter systematically. Emotional attachment should only develop after a name has passed all screening steps.

### Wrong: Choosing a descriptive name for long-term trademark protection
Descriptive names (e.g., "General Computing Solutions") are nearly impossible to trademark because they describe a category, not a brand. The USPTO will likely reject them on descriptiveness grounds. [src3]

### Correct: Prefer invented, evocative, or metaphorical names for trademarkability
Invented words (Xerox, Spotify) and arbitrary/fanciful names (Apple for computers) receive the strongest trademark protection. If a descriptive name is desired for clarity, plan to build secondary meaning over years. [src1]

### Wrong: Skipping trademark search because "we're just a small startup"
Trademark infringement liability applies regardless of company size. A cease-and-desist letter from a trademark holder can force an expensive rebrand — new domain, new packaging, new marketing materials, lost brand equity. [src4]

### Correct: Always search USPTO + WIPO before registering a domain or ordering business cards
A 2-hour trademark search costs $0 and prevents $10,000-$100,000 in rebrand costs. Treat it as a non-negotiable gate in the naming process.

## When This Matters

Use this recipe when a founder or team needs to select a brand name for a new company or major product and wants to avoid the most common and expensive naming mistakes. Requires at minimum a defined business concept and target customer as inputs. Produces a scored shortlist with full availability validation, ready for domain registration and trademark filing.
