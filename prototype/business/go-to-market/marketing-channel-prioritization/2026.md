# Marketing Channel Prioritization Decision Framework

## Summary

This framework helps determine which marketing channels to invest in first based on business model, budget, and time-to-results requirements. Email marketing delivers the highest dollar-for-dollar ROI at $36-$44 per $1 spent, followed by SEO at $22 per $1 and Google Ads at $8 per $1. The critical insight: most companies spread budget too thin across 6-8 channels when 2-3 well-funded channels outperform. Start with the minimum viable budget per channel — anything below that threshold generates negative ROI due to insufficient data and reach. [src1]

## Constraints

- Requires defined ICP and business model before channel selection is meaningful
- Paid channel commitments are semi-irreversible mid-quarter — switching costs include lost learnings and wasted creative
- Minimum viable budgets represent floor spend for statistical significance, not optimal spend
- Channel ROI degrades as competition increases — first-mover advantage is real and temporary
- Attribution models affect perceived ROI — last-touch inflates paid, undervalues content/SEO

## Decision Inputs

| Input | Why It Matters | How to Assess |
|-------|---------------|---------------|
| Business model (B2B/B2C/DTC) | B2B and B2C have fundamentally different channel economics and sales cycles | Check ACV, sales cycle length, and buyer persona |
| Monthly marketing budget | Determines how many channels can be funded above minimum viable threshold | Total budget minus personnel and tools costs |
| Growth stage | Pre-PMF companies need learning velocity; post-PMF need scalable acquisition | Current ARR and growth rate trajectory |
| Time-to-results urgency | Paid delivers in days, SEO in 6-12 months, brand in 12-24 months | Ask: when does the board/investors expect pipeline? |
| Internal capabilities | Some channels require specialized skills (SEO, paid media buying, content) | Audit current team skills and hiring timeline |

## Decision Logic

### If budget < $5K/month AND time horizon > 6 months
→ **Email + SEO + organic social**. These three channels have the highest long-term ROI and lowest minimum viable budgets. Email at $36-$44 per $1 spent dominates all other channels on pure ROI. [src1]

### If budget $5K-$25K/month AND need results within 90 days
→ **Google Ads + Email + retargeting**. Paid search captures existing demand immediately with $8 return per $1 spent. Pair with email nurture for leads not ready to buy. [src2]

### If budget > $25K/month AND B2B with ACV > $25K
→ **Content/SEO + LinkedIn paid + events + email**. Higher ACV justifies higher CAC channels. Events generate 4:1 pipeline-to-cost ratio for enterprise. Allocate 40-50% to content/SEO, 25-30% to paid, 20-30% to events. [src3]

### If product has self-serve motion or viral mechanics
→ **Product-led growth + content/SEO + community**. Invest in reducing time-to-value before scaling paid acquisition. PLG companies achieve 2x faster revenue growth. [src4]

### Default recommendation
→ **Email + SEO + one paid channel matching your buyer's intent**. When inputs are ambiguous, this combination covers immediate results (paid), medium-term nurture (email), and long-term compounding (SEO). [src1]

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Spreading budget across 6+ channels at $2K each
Companies allocate $2K/month across six channels, none reaching minimum viable spend. Every channel underperforms because there is insufficient data for optimization and insufficient reach for impact. [src2]

### Correct: Fund 2-3 channels above minimum viable threshold
Concentrate 80% of budget on 2-3 primary channels funded well above minimums. Reserve 10-20% for testing one new channel per quarter. Kill or scale based on 90-day performance. [src6]

### Wrong: Choosing channels based on competitor activity
Copying competitor channel mix ignores differences in audience, budget, brand equity, and internal capabilities. Competitors may be losing money on channels they are too committed to exit.

### Correct: Choose based on your buyer's information journey
Map your ICP's decision process — where they research, what they trust, how they evaluate. Match channels to stages: awareness (content, social), consideration (search, email), decision (events, direct). [src4]

### Wrong: Abandoning SEO because results take 6 months
Companies kill SEO programs at month 4 because pipeline hasn't materialized, then restart 6 months later — permanently stuck in the ramp period. [src1]

### Correct: Run SEO as a parallel long-term investment
Fund SEO separately from performance budget. Measure leading indicators (rankings, traffic, backlinks) monthly. Expect pipeline contribution starting month 6-9, with ROI crossing paid channels by month 12-18.

## Cost Benchmarks

| Scenario | Email | SEO / Content | Google Ads | Paid Social | Events |
|----------|-------|---------------|------------|-------------|--------|
| **Startup (<$1M ARR)** | $500-$2K/mo | $3K-$8K/mo | $3K-$10K/mo | $2K-$8K/mo | $5K-$15K/event |
| **Growth ($1M-$10M ARR)** | $2K-$8K/mo | $8K-$25K/mo | $10K-$50K/mo | $8K-$30K/mo | $15K-$50K/event |
| **Scale ($10M+ ARR)** | $8K-$30K/mo | $25K-$100K/mo | $50K-$250K/mo | $30K-$150K/mo | $50K-$250K/event |
| **Average CAC** | $5-$15 | $25-$60 (B2B) | $50-$200 (B2B) | $30-$150 | $200-$800/lead |

**Hidden cost multipliers**: Add 15-20% for marketing tools (CRM, analytics, automation), 10-15% for creative production, and 20-30% for agency/contractor management if outsourcing. [src3]
