# Marketplace Industry Benchmarks 2026

## Summary

Comprehensive benchmarks for two-sided marketplaces covering take rates by vertical, GMV growth targets, liquidity metrics, unit economics, and valuation multiples. Take rates range from 2-3% for lead generation to 25-30% for managed service marketplaces. The post-ZIRP reset brought marketplace valuations to 2.3x EV/Revenue median, well below the 5.6x long-term average, with profitability now outweighing GMV growth in investor evaluation. [src1]

**Data vintage**: Based on H2 2025 data from 200+ marketplace companies across all verticals and stages.
**Key shift**: Profitability is the new growth — investors now prioritize contribution margin and path to EBITDA over GMV growth rate. AI-powered matching is improving liquidity metrics across categories.

## Constraints
<!-- Agents: read before citing any benchmark number. -->

- These benchmarks represent venture-backed, technology-enabled marketplaces. Do not apply to classified-ads platforms, traditional wholesalers, or Amazon 1P.
- Take rates vary 5-10x by vertical. A 5% take rate is excellent for physical goods but poor for managed services. Always compare within vertical.
- GMV metrics overstate business size. A $1B GMV marketplace at 10% take rate generates the same revenue as a $100M GMV marketplace at 100% take rate (direct sale). Always clarify basis.
- Data collected H2 2025. Marketplace dynamics shift rapidly with competitive entries, regulatory changes, and unit economics evolution.
- Maturity stage matters — early-stage marketplaces subsidize take rates and growth. Compare within same maturity cohort.

## Take Rates

### Take Rate by Marketplace Vertical

**Definition**: Net revenue as percentage of GMV. Includes commission, payment processing fees retained, and ancillary revenue (advertising, insurance, logistics). Excludes pass-through payment processing costs.

| Vertical | Median Take Rate | Range | Typical Structure |
|----------|-----------------|-------|-------------------|
| Managed Services | 25-30% | 20-40% | Commission + service fee + insurance |
| Food Delivery | 20-28% | 15-35% | Commission + delivery fee + ad revenue |
| Ride-Sharing / Mobility | 22-28% | 18-35% | Commission + surge pricing + subscription |
| Staffing / Freelance | 15-20% | 10-30% | Commission + premium placement |
| Physical Goods (curated) | 12-18% | 8-25% | Commission + fulfillment + advertising |
| Physical Goods (open) | 8-15% | 5-20% | Commission + promoted listings |
| B2B / Wholesale | 5-10% | 2-15% | Commission + SaaS fee + logistics |
| Real Estate / Lead Gen | 2-5% | 1-8% | Referral fee or subscription |

**Trend**: Take rates expanding 1-2pp YoY as marketplaces layer on ancillary services (payments, logistics, insurance, advertising). [src1, src3]
**Red flag threshold**: Take rate declining YoY in a mature marketplace signals competitive pressure or supplier power shift.
**Action trigger**: If net take rate < 10% and gross margin < 50%, evaluate whether platform is capturing sufficient value for sustainability.

[src1, src3]

### Realized vs Nominal Take Rate

**Definition**: Realized take rate accounts for refunds, chargebacks, promotions, and seller incentives. Gap between nominal and realized is the "take rate leakage."

| Marketplace Type | Nominal Take Rate | Realized Take Rate | Leakage |
|------------------|-------------------|--------------------|---------|
| High-frequency (food, rides) | 25% | 18-22% | 3-7pp |
| Medium-frequency (goods) | 15% | 12-14% | 1-3pp |
| Low-frequency (real estate) | 3% | 2.5-2.8% | 0.2-0.5pp |

**Red flag threshold**: Leakage exceeding 5pp of nominal take rate signals over-reliance on promotions.

[src1]

## GMV Growth

### GMV Growth by Maturity Stage

**Definition**: Quarter-over-quarter GMV growth rate. GMV = total transaction value processed through the platform.

| Stage | Median QoQ Growth | 25th Pct | 75th Pct | Top Decile |
|-------|--------------------|----------|----------|------------|
| Pre-PMF (<$1M GMV/mo) | 25% QoQ | 10% | 40% | 60%+ |
| Scaling ($1-50M GMV/mo) | 15% QoQ | 8% | 25% | 35% |
| Mature ($50M+ GMV/mo) | 5% QoQ | 2% | 10% | 18% |

**Trend**: Overall ecommerce GMV growth stabilized at 8-12% annually. High-growth marketplaces still exceed 20% QoQ in early stages. [src4]
**Red flag threshold**: Scaling marketplace below 8% QoQ growth suggests liquidity or product-market fit issues.
**Action trigger**: If GMV growth decelerating for 3+ consecutive quarters, audit supply/demand balance and geographic expansion opportunities.

[src2, src4]

## Liquidity Metrics

### Seller Liquidity (Sell-Through Rate)

**Definition**: Proportion of listings that result in a transaction within a defined time window (typically 30 days). Measures whether supply gets matched with demand.

| Marketplace Type | Target | Healthy | Alarm |
|------------------|--------|---------|-------|
| Goods (commoditized) | >70% | 50-70% | <40% |
| Goods (unique / handmade) | >40% | 25-40% | <15% |
| Services (on-demand) | >80% | 60-80% | <50% |
| Services (project-based) | >50% | 30-50% | <20% |

**Red flag threshold**: Seller liquidity below 30% in a mature marketplace signals demand-side deficit.

### Buyer Liquidity (Search-to-Fill Rate)

**Definition**: Percentage of buyer searches or requests that result in a transaction. Measures whether demand finds adequate supply.

| Marketplace Type | Target | Healthy | Alarm |
|------------------|--------|---------|-------|
| Goods (broad catalog) | >15% | 8-15% | <5% |
| Services (on-demand) | >60% | 40-60% | <30% |
| Services (project-based) | >25% | 15-25% | <10% |

**Action trigger**: If buyer liquidity declining while supply growing, the matching algorithm or search UX needs improvement.

[src2, src6]

### Supply-Side Retention

**Definition**: Percentage of active sellers/providers still active after 12 months. Active = at least one transaction per quarter.

| Marketplace Type | Median | 25th Pct | Top Decile |
|------------------|--------|----------|------------|
| Physical goods | 62% | 45% | 78% |
| Services | 48% | 32% | 65% |
| Food delivery (restaurants) | 72% | 58% | 85% |

**Trend**: Top marketplaces achieve supply-side GMV retention above 100% — retained sellers grow their volume 2-3x over time. [src4]
**Red flag threshold**: Supply retention below 40% indicates marketplace is not delivering sufficient value to sellers.

## Unit Economics

### Contribution Margin by Stage

**Definition**: (Net revenue - variable costs) / net revenue. Variable costs include payment processing, customer support, fraud/chargebacks, and seller acquisition.

| Stage | Median | 25th Pct | 75th Pct |
|-------|--------|----------|----------|
| Pre-PMF | -15% | -40% | 5% |
| Scaling | 25% | 10% | 40% |
| Mature | 45% | 30% | 60% |

**Red flag threshold**: Scaling marketplace with negative contribution margin after 18+ months of operation needs unit economics rework.

### Break-Even Timeline

**Definition**: Months from launch to positive unit economics (contribution margin > 0) on a cohort basis.

| Marketplace Type | Median | Healthy Range |
|------------------|--------|---------------|
| Physical goods | 14 months | 8-20 months |
| Services | 10 months | 6-16 months |
| Managed marketplace | 20 months | 12-30 months |

[src4, src6]

## Valuation

### Valuation Multiples

| Multiple | 2024 Median | 2025 Median | Long-Term Average |
|----------|-------------|-------------|-------------------|
| EV / Revenue | 3.1x | 2.3x | 5.6x |
| EV / Gross Profit | 14x | 12x | 18x |
| EV / GMV | 0.35x | 0.25x | 0.65x |

**Trend**: Post-ZIRP reset stabilizing. EV/Gross Profit is the most stable comparator across marketplace types since it normalizes for take rate differences. [src5]
**Red flag threshold**: EV/Revenue below 1x for a growing marketplace suggests severe investor skepticism about path to profitability.

[src5]

## Composite Metrics & Rules of Thumb

| Rule | Formula / Threshold | Interpretation |
|------|---------------------|----------------|
| Net take rate > 10% | Net revenue / GMV > 10% | Sufficient value capture for sustainability |
| Seller liquidity > 60% | Transactions / listings > 60% | Healthy demand-supply balance |
| Supply retention > 50% | 12-month active seller retention | Marketplace delivering value to supply side |
| Contribution margin > 30% | (Net rev - variable costs) / net rev | Path to profitability exists |
| GMV retention > 100% | Cohort GMV year 2 / year 1 | Retained sellers growing — compounding growth |
| Break-even < 18 months | Months to positive unit economics | Strong underlying economics |

**Constraint**: Early-stage marketplaces intentionally suppress take rates for growth. Apply these rules only to marketplaces past product-market fit (>$1M GMV/month sustained). Take rate benchmarks from public companies include ancillary revenue that pre-scale marketplaces cannot replicate. [src2]

## Segment Definitions

| Segment | Definition | Typical Characteristics |
|---------|-----------|------------------------|
| Physical Goods Marketplace | Facilitates sale of tangible products | Inventory risk varies (held vs dropship), logistics complexity, returns |
| Services Marketplace | Matches service providers with buyers | Higher take rates, trust/quality signals critical, local network effects |
| Managed Marketplace | Platform controls service delivery | Highest take rates, highest ops cost, strongest moats |
| Lead Generation Platform | Generates referrals, not transactions | Lowest take rates, revenue detached from GMV, subscription models common |
| B2B Marketplace | Facilitates business-to-business trade | Larger order sizes, longer sales cycles, credit/financing often included |

## Common Misinterpretations

- **Comparing GMV across marketplace types**: A $1B GMV goods marketplace at 12% take rate ($120M revenue) is fundamentally different from a $1B GMV services marketplace at 25% take rate ($250M revenue). Always compare on net revenue or gross profit basis. [src2]
- **Treating take rate as pure profit**: Take rate includes payment processing (2.5-3%), fraud costs (0.5-1%), and seller acquisition amortization. Actual gross margin on take rate is typically 60-70% of the headline number.
- **Applying mature marketplace benchmarks to early-stage**: Early-stage marketplaces intentionally subsidize both sides. Expecting 25% take rates and 45% contribution margins at $500K GMV/month is unrealistic.
- **Ignoring supply-side economics**: Seller churn is the silent marketplace killer. A marketplace can grow GMV while losing money if it constantly replaces churning supply with expensive new supply acquisition.
