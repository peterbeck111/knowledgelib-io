# Insurance Premium Benchmarks 2026

## Summary

The commercial insurance market in 2026 favors buyers across most lines, with D&O premiums declining 5.2% year-over-year and cyber insurance experiencing its first-ever premium decrease of 2.3% in 2024 as competitive conditions prevailed. [src1, src3] General liability and E&O premiums remain relatively stable, with small businesses paying $500-$1,500 annually for standard GL coverage and $735-$936 annually for E&O. The exception is cyber insurance for large enterprises, where S&P projects total premiums to reach $23 billion by 2026, a 15-20% increase.

**Data vintage**: Based on Q4 2025 data from broker reports (WTW, Founder Shield, Embroker) and carrier aggregators (MoneyGeek, Insureon).
**Key shift**: D&O and cyber markets softening after the 2021-2022 hard market; competitive pressure driving rate reductions for companies with strong risk profiles.

## Constraints
<!-- Agents: read before citing any benchmark number. -->

- Premium data is US-centric. UK, EU, and APAC markets have different regulatory frameworks — adjust 15-30% for non-US markets.
- Rates shown are for standard risk profiles. Companies with claims history, high-risk industries, or complex structures pay 2-5x more.
- Self-reported and broker-aggregated data; actual premiums depend on specific underwriting factors.
- Data collected Q4 2025; insurance markets can shift rapidly based on catastrophic events or regulatory changes.
- Coverage limits shown are typical minimums; actual needs depend on company-specific risk assessment and contractual requirements.

## Directors & Officers (D&O) Liability

### D&O Premium by Company Size

**Definition**: Annual premium for Directors & Officers liability insurance providing coverage for personal liability of directors and officers for wrongful acts in managing the company. Covers defense costs, settlements, and judgments.

| Company Size / Stage | Annual Premium Range | Typical Coverage Limit | Cost per $1M Coverage |
|---------------------|---------------------|----------------------|----------------------|
| Startup (pre-revenue) | $3,500-$6,000 | $1M-$2M | $3,500-$4,000 |
| Startup ($10M-$50M funding) | $6,000-$12,000 | $5M | $2,000-$3,000 |
| Startup ($50M-$100M funding) | $10,000-$15,000 | $5M-$10M | $1,500-$2,500 |
| Private company (<$50M revenue) | $5,000-$10,000 | $1M-$5M | $2,000-$5,000 |
| Private company ($50M-$250M) | $10,000-$30,000 | $5M-$10M | $2,000-$3,000 |
| Public company (small-cap) | $50,000-$200,000 | $10M-$25M | $5,000-$8,000 |
| Public company (large-cap) | $200,000-$1M+ | $25M-$100M+ | $3,000-$10,000 |
| Nonprofit | $400-$750 | $1M | $400-$750 |

**Trend**: D&O costs declined 5.2% YoY; nearly 70% of primary policies saw reductions in 2025. Market expected to remain soft through H1 2026. [src1]
**Red flag threshold**: D&O premium exceeding 0.5% of company revenue for private companies, or exceeding $10,000 per $1M of coverage for public companies, suggests adverse risk factors needing attention.

## Composite Metrics & Rules of Thumb

| Rule | Formula / Threshold | Interpretation |
|------|--------------------|----------------|
| Total insurance budget | 0.25-0.75% of revenue | Typical all-lines insurance spend for SMBs |
| D&O sizing rule | D&O limit >= 10% of company valuation | Minimum coverage for startups and growth-stage companies |
| Cyber coverage floor | Cyber limit >= 2x average breach cost in industry | Based on IBM Cost of a Data Breach report (~$4.9M average) |
| GL per employee | $50-$200/employee/year (office-based) | Quick sizing for low-risk professional services |
| Bundle discount | Package policy saves 15-25% | BOP (Business Owner's Policy) bundles GL + property + cyber basics |

**Constraint**: Rules of thumb fail for companies with unusual risk profiles — heavy litigation history, international operations, or high-value professional services. Always get multiple broker quotes for accurate pricing. [src1]

## Common Misinterpretations

- **Confusing rate changes with premium changes**: Rates may decline 5% but your premium increases because your revenue or headcount grew. Always track both rate-per-unit and total premium. [src1]
- **Comparing premiums across industries without adjustment**: A $5,000 GL premium is expensive for a consulting firm but cheap for a construction company. Industry classification is the single largest premium driver.
- **Assuming cyber insurance replaces cybersecurity investment**: Cyber insurers increasingly require security controls (MFA, EDR, backups) as underwriting conditions. Poor security posture leads to coverage denial or 2-5x premium loading.
