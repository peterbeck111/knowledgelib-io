Insurance Premium Benchmarks 2026

What are insurance premiums 2026 — D&O, E&O, cyber, GL by company size and industry?

Type: Benchmark Data Vintage: Q4 2025 Confidence: 0.80 Sources: 6 Verified: 2026-03-11

Summary

The commercial insurance market in 2026 favors buyers across most lines, with D&O premiums declining 5.2% year-over-year and cyber insurance experiencing its first-ever premium decrease of 2.3% in 2024 as competitive conditions prevailed. General liability and E&O premiums remain relatively stable, with small businesses paying $500-$1,500 annually for standard GL coverage and $735-$936 for E&O. [src1, src3]

Data vintage: Based on Q4 2025 data from broker reports (WTW, Founder Shield, Embroker) and carrier aggregators.

Key shift: D&O and cyber markets softening after the 2021-2022 hard market; competitive pressure driving rate reductions for companies with strong risk profiles.

Constraints

  • Premium data is US-centric. UK, EU, and APAC markets have different regulatory frameworks — adjust 15-30% for non-US.
  • Rates shown are for standard risk profiles. Companies with claims history or high-risk industries pay 2-5x more.
  • Self-reported and broker-aggregated data; actual premiums depend on specific underwriting factors.
  • Data collected Q4 2025; insurance markets can shift rapidly based on catastrophic events.
  • Coverage limits shown are typical minimums; actual needs depend on company-specific risk assessment.

Common Misinterpretations

  • Confusing rate changes with premium changes: Rates may decline 5% but your premium increases because revenue or headcount grew. Always track both rate-per-unit and total premium.
  • Comparing premiums across industries without adjustment: A $5,000 GL premium is expensive for consulting but cheap for construction. Industry classification is the single largest driver.
  • Assuming cyber insurance replaces cybersecurity investment: Insurers increasingly require security controls (MFA, EDR, backups) as underwriting conditions. Poor security leads to denial or 2-5x loading.
