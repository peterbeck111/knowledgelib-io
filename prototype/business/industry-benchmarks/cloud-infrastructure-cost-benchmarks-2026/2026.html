Cloud Infrastructure Cost Benchmarks 2026

What are cloud costs 2026 — AWS vs Azure vs GCP compute, storage, network comparison?

Type: Benchmark Data Vintage: Q4 2025 Confidence: 0.84 Sources: 6 Verified: 2026-03-11

Summary

Cloud infrastructure costs in 2026 show AWS, Azure, and GCP converging on similar pricing for most workloads, with differentiation driven by CPU architecture (Arm vs x86), discount programs, and data transfer policies. Organizations spend a median of $12M+ annually, with 27% wasted on average. [src1]

Data vintage: Based on Q4 2025 published pricing, Flexera State of Cloud (750+ orgs), and cross-provider benchmarks.

Key shift: AWS doubled inter-AZ transfer fees and raised cross-region costs 25-40%. Arm instances now the default cost-optimization choice.

Constraints

  • Cloud pricing changes constantly. AWS averages 197 monthly price changes. Verify before committing.
  • On-demand prices are rack rates. Most enterprises use reserved/savings plans at 30-72% discount.
  • Same instance type costs 10-30% more in EU or APAC vs US East regions.
  • Data transfer (egress) costs are the hidden budget-breaker, not compute or storage.
  • 27% of cloud spend is wasted on average. Benchmarks assume reasonable optimization.

Common Misinterpretations

  • Comparing on-demand prices as actual costs: Enterprise customers on 3-year reservations pay 50-72% less. On-demand comparisons are starting points only.
  • Ignoring data transfer: A compute-cheaper provider becomes expensive with heavy egress or cross-region traffic. Model the full stack.
  • Treating all regions as equivalent: US East is consistently cheapest. EU and APAC add 10-30%.
  • Assuming multi-cloud saves money: Multi-cloud adds 10-20% management overhead. It's a risk strategy, not cost optimization.
