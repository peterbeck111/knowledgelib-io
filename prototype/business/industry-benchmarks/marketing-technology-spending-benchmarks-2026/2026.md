# Marketing Technology Spending Benchmarks 2026

## Summary

Marketing technology accounts for 22.4% of total marketing budgets in 2025, down from 25.4% in 2024, as CMOs shift spend toward paid media (30.6% of budget). The global martech market reached $589 billion in 2025. The Gartner CMO Spend Survey of 402 marketing leaders found that marketing budgets remained flat at 7.7% of company revenue, while martech utilization continues to decline — marketers use only 33% of their stack's capabilities, down from 58% in 2020. License costs represent only 30-40% of true total cost of ownership. [src1, src4]

**Data vintage**: Based on Gartner CMO Spend Survey (February-March 2025) and Q4 2025 vendor pricing data.
**Key shift**: Martech budget share declined to 22.4% as paid media grew to 30.6%. AI investments are the primary growth area, with 49% of marketers reporting improved time efficiency from GenAI.

## Constraints
<!-- Agents: read before citing any benchmark number. -->

- Gartner survey covers 402 CMOs at predominantly $1B+ revenue companies. Mid-market and SMB budgets differ significantly in absolute terms and allocation ratios.
- Martech utilization rates (33% of capabilities used) mean significant waste. Raw spending figures overstate effective investment by roughly 2-3x.
- Global market size ($589B) includes all marketing software worldwide. Individual company spend depends on stack maturity, industry, and tech debt level.
- License costs are only 30-40% of true TCO. Integration, staffing, training, and maintenance multiply actual costs 2-3x beyond the subscription price.
- Data from Q1-Q2 2025. Rapid AI tool proliferation and vendor consolidation may shift 2026 allocations significantly.

## Metric Category 1: Marketing Budget Allocation

### Marketing Budget as Percentage of Revenue

**Definition**: Total marketing spend (inclusive of martech, paid media, labor, and agencies) divided by company revenue.

| Company Size | Marketing % of Revenue | Martech % of Marketing | Martech % of Revenue |
|-------------|----------------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| Enterprise ($1B+) | 7.7% | 22.4% | 1.7% |
| Mid-Market ($100M-$1B) | 8-10% | 20-25% | 1.8-2.5% |
| SMB ($10M-$100M) | 10-14% | 15-20% | 1.5-2.8% |
| Startup (<$10M) | 15-25% | 10-15% | 1.5-3.8% |

**Trend**: Enterprise marketing budgets flat at 7.7% of revenue for two consecutive years. 59% of CMOs report insufficient budget to execute strategy. [src1]
**Red flag threshold**: Marketing spend below 5% of revenue for growth-stage companies indicates chronic underinvestment.

### Marketing Budget Breakdown by Category

**Definition**: Allocation of total marketing budget across major spending categories.

| Category | % of Marketing Budget | YoY Change | Role |
|----------|---------------------|------------|------|
| Paid Media | 30.6% | +11% | Performance and brand media |
| Martech | 22.4% | -12% | Software and platforms |
| Labor | 21.9% | -5% | Internal marketing staff |
| Agencies | 20.7% | -8% | External services and creative |

**Trend**: Paid media gained share at the expense of martech, labor, and agencies. CMOs are consolidating agency relationships and reducing platform redundancy. [src1]
**Red flag threshold**: Martech exceeding 30% of marketing budget without corresponding utilization gains suggests platform sprawl.

[src1]

## Metric Category 2: Martech Stack Costs by Company Size

### Total Cost of Ownership by Tier

**Definition**: True all-in cost of martech stack including license fees, integration, staffing, training, and maintenance. License fees are typically 30-40% of total cost.

| Company Tier | License Fees/Year | True TCO/Year | FTEs Dedicated | Typical Stack Size |
|-------------|------------------|--------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Startup/SMB (<$10M revenue) | $12,000 – $50,000 | $30,000 – $120,000 | 0.5-1 FTE | 3-8 tools |
| Growth ($10M-$100M) | $50,000 – $150,000 | $120,000 – $350,000 | 2-4 FTEs | 8-15 tools |
| Mid-Market ($100M-$500M) | $150,000 – $500,000 | $400,000 – $1.2M | 4-8 FTEs | 12-25 tools |
| Enterprise ($500M-$1B) | $500,000 – $1.5M | $1.2M – $4M | 8-15 FTEs | 20-40 tools |
| Large Enterprise ($1B+) | $800,000 – $3M+ | $2M – $8M+ | 15-30 FTEs | 30-90+ tools |

**Trend**: Enterprise stacks continue to grow in complexity. The average enterprise uses 91 different martech tools. TCO typically runs 2.5-3x license costs when accounting for staffing and integration. [src3]
**Red flag threshold**: License costs exceeding $5,000/tool/year for tools used by fewer than 10 users signals likely redundancy.

### Cost Per Category in Typical Stack

**Definition**: Estimated annual license cost by martech category for a mid-market B2B company.

| Category | Typical Annual Cost | % of Stack Spend | Key Players |
|----------|-------------------|-----------------|-------------|
| CRM | $30,000 – $120,000 | 20-25% | Salesforce, HubSpot, Microsoft Dynamics |
| Marketing Automation | $15,000 – $80,000 | 15-20% | HubSpot, Marketo, Pardot, ActiveCampaign |
| Analytics & BI | $10,000 – $60,000 | 10-15% | Google Analytics, Tableau, Looker, Amplitude |
| Content Management | $5,000 – $40,000 | 8-12% | WordPress, Contentful, Webflow |
| Email & Messaging | $5,000 – $30,000 | 5-10% | Mailchimp, SendGrid, Braze, Iterable |
| CDP / Data Platform | $20,000 – $100,000 | 12-18% | Segment, mParticle, Treasure Data |
| Ad Tech / Paid Media | $10,000 – $50,000 | 8-12% | Google Ads, Meta, The Trade Desk |
| AI & Personalization | $10,000 – $60,000 | 8-15% | Emerging category — fastest growing |

**Trend**: CRM remains the largest single category. CDP and AI/personalization are the fastest-growing categories, with AI tools projected to reach 15%+ of stack spend by 2027. [src2, src3]
**Red flag threshold**: Spending more on AI tools than on core CRM before achieving 70%+ CRM utilization indicates misallocated investment.

[src2, src3]

## Metric Category 3: Utilization & ROI

### Martech Utilization Rates

**Definition**: Percentage of purchased martech capabilities actively used by the marketing organization. Measured as features used / features available.

| Year | Utilization Rate | Direction |
|------|-----------------|-----------|
| 2020 | 58% | Baseline |
| 2022 | 42% | ↓ 16 pts |
| 2024 | 36% | ↓ 6 pts |
| 2025 | 33% | ↓ 3 pts |

**Trend**: Utilization has declined every year since 2020, from 58% to 33%. Organizations are buying more capabilities than they can operationalize. 32% of organizations acknowledge not using full capabilities of their stack. [src4, src5]
**Red flag threshold**: Utilization below 25% indicates severe platform sprawl. Audit and consolidate before adding new tools.

### Martech ROI & Satisfaction

**Definition**: Perceived return on martech investment and satisfaction levels among marketing leaders.

| Metric | 2025 Value | Trend |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| CMOs reporting insufficient budget | 59% | ↓ 5pts (improving) |
| Marketers disappointed in martech payoff | 54.9% | ↑ 6pts (worsening) |
| High-performing organizations (positive ROI) | 15% | Stable |
| Average annual waste on unused SaaS licenses | $21M (enterprise) | ↑ increasing |
| Stack complexity as top blocker | 47% | Stable |

**Trend**: Despite growing budgets and AI investments, over half of marketers report disappointment in martech returns. Only 15% of organizations qualify as high performers demonstrating positive ROI. Average enterprise wastes $21M/year on unused SaaS licenses. [src4, src5]
**Red flag threshold**: If more than 60% of marketing team reports dissatisfaction with current stack, a comprehensive audit is overdue.

[src4, src5]

## Composite Metrics & Rules of Thumb

| Rule | Formula / Threshold | Interpretation |
|------|---------------------|----------------|
| TCO Multiplier | True cost = License fees x 2.5-3.0 | Never budget license cost alone; staffing and integration multiply actual spend |
| Utilization-Adjusted Spend | Effective spend = Total martech / utilization rate | At 33% utilization, every $1 of martech delivers $0.33 of value |
| Stack Consolidation Signal | >50 tools AND <40% utilization = consolidate | Audit for overlapping capabilities and reduce tool count by 30-40% |
| AI Investment Ceiling | AI tools < 15% of martech budget until core stack >70% utilized | Prioritize core platform adoption before AI add-ons |
| Revenue-Based Martech Budget | Martech = 1.5-2.5% of revenue (mid-market) | Quick sanity check for martech budget appropriateness |

**Constraint**: These rules apply primarily to B2B and B2B2C companies. Pure B2C companies with high ad spend may have different ratios, with martech as low as 15% of marketing budget. [src1, src3]

## Common Misinterpretations

- **Treating license cost as total cost**: License fees are only 30-40% of true TCO. A $100K/year martech stack actually costs $250K-$300K when accounting for integration, staffing, and maintenance. Always use TCO for budgeting.
- **Assuming more tools equals more capability**: The average enterprise uses 91 tools but only utilizes 33% of capabilities. Adding tools without improving utilization creates waste and complexity, not competitive advantage.
- **Applying enterprise benchmarks to SMBs**: Gartner's 22.4% martech allocation is based on $1B+ companies. SMBs often allocate 15-20% of marketing to technology, with fundamentally different stack compositions.
- **Conflating market size with company spend**: The $589B global market includes all marketing software. Individual company spend ranges from $30K (startup) to $8M+ (enterprise). Always use company-size-appropriate benchmarks.
