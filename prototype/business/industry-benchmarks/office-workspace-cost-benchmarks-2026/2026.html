Office & Workspace Cost Benchmarks 2026

What are workspace costs 2026 — per employee by city, coworking vs dedicated vs remote?

Type: Benchmark Data Vintage: Q4 2025 Confidence: 0.82 Sources: 6 Verified: 2026-03-11

Summary

The national average full-service office listing rate stands at $32.55 per square foot annually (Q1 2026), but actual workspace costs vary 4-5x across markets — from $22/sq ft in Detroit to $95+/sq ft in Midtown Manhattan. Companies save $11,000+ per employee annually by adopting hybrid or remote models versus dedicated office space. Coworking dedicated desks average $300/month nationally, offering 40-70% savings over traditional leases for small teams. [src2, src5]

Data vintage: Based on Q4 2025 and Q1 2026 data from NAIOP, CommercialCafe, CoworkingCafe, and Global Workplace Analytics.

Key shift: Office vacancy rates near 20% in major metros are driving landlord concessions and accelerating flex-space adoption as companies right-size post-pandemic.

Constraints

  • Pricing data is US-centric. London is 20-40% higher; most Asian cities 30-50% lower than equivalent US tier.
  • Traditional office costs assume Class A space with standard build-out; Class B/C is 30-50% cheaper.
  • Coworking prices are list prices; enterprise agreements (10+ desks) get 15-25% discounts.
  • Data collected Q4 2025; commercial real estate markets vary by quarter and respond to rate changes.
  • Per-employee costs assume 75-150 sq ft/person depending on model; actual space needs vary.

Common Misinterpretations

  • Comparing lease rates to coworking directly: Lease rates are per sq ft; coworking is per person. Convert: $35/sq ft at 125 sq ft = $365/person/month, close to a $300 dedicated desk.
  • Ignoring total occupancy cost: Base rent is only 60-70% of total. Add utilities (10-15%), maintenance (5-10%), insurance (2-3%), and build-out amortization (10-20%).
  • Assuming remote work is free: Fully remote requires $50-$200/month stipends, collaboration tools ($15-$30/employee/month), and team gatherings ($1,000-$3,000/employee/year). Still 40-60% cheaper, but not zero.
