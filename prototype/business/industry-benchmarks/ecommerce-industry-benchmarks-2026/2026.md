# Ecommerce Industry Benchmarks 2026

## Summary

Comprehensive ecommerce performance benchmarks covering conversion rates, average order values, cart abandonment, return rates, and customer acquisition costs across 7 major verticals. The global average conversion rate reached 2.5-3.3% in 2025, with mobile commerce exceeding 60% of all transactions but converting at roughly half the desktop rate (1.8% vs 3.9%). Cart abandonment remains stubbornly high at 78.8% globally. [src1]

**Data vintage**: Based on H2 2025 data from 25,000+ ecommerce stores and multiple industry reports.
**Key shift**: Mobile-first is now the baseline — desktop-only benchmarks are obsolete. DTC customer acquisition costs rose 40% in two years, forcing brands toward retention-first strategies.

## Constraints
<!-- Agents: read before citing any benchmark number. -->

- These benchmarks represent DTC ecommerce stores on Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce. Do not apply to Amazon 1P sellers, B2B ecommerce, or marketplace-only businesses.
- Conversion rates are session-based (orders / sessions). Visitor-based rates are 10-20% lower due to multi-session buyers. Always specify which basis.
- Primarily US and Western Europe data. APAC markets (higher mobile adoption, different payment infrastructure) may vary 20-30%.
- Data collected H2 2025. Q4 holiday data inflates conversion and AOV by 15-25% vs annual average. Use non-Q4 figures for baseline planning.
- Only compare stores within the same vertical. Cross-vertical comparison is misleading — food converts 4x higher than luxury.

## Conversion Metrics

### Conversion Rate by Vertical

**Definition**: Orders / sessions over a 30-day period. Excludes marketplace sales. Includes all traffic sources.

| Vertical | Median | 25th Pct | 75th Pct | Top Decile |
|----------|--------|----------|----------|------------|
| Food & Beverage | 4.8% | 3.2% | 6.0% | 8.5% |
| Beauty & Cosmetics | 3.8% | 2.5% | 4.9% | 7.2% |
| Health & Wellness | 3.2% | 2.0% | 4.5% | 6.8% |
| Home & Garden | 2.4% | 1.5% | 3.5% | 5.2% |
| Fashion & Apparel | 2.2% | 1.4% | 3.2% | 4.8% |
| Consumer Electronics | 1.6% | 0.9% | 2.5% | 3.8% |
| Luxury | 1.2% | 0.6% | 1.8% | 2.8% |

**Trend**: Overall ecommerce conversion up 0.3pp YoY as mobile UX improves, but growth concentrated in food/beauty/health. [src1, src2]
**Red flag threshold**: Below 1% on a DTC site with paid traffic indicates fundamental UX or targeting problems.
**Action trigger**: If below 25th percentile for your vertical, audit page load speed, checkout flow, and traffic quality before increasing ad spend.

| Luxury | $142 | $95-$230 | 5.2:1 |

**Action trigger**: If CAC rising > 15% QoQ, diversify beyond paid social into email, SEO, and referral channels.

## Composite Metrics & Rules of Thumb

| Rule | Formula / Threshold | Interpretation |
|------|---------------------|----------------|
| Revenue per session > $2.50 | Revenue / sessions | Healthy unit economics per visit |
| Repeat purchase rate > 25% | Return customers / total customers | Healthy brand loyalty — reduces CAC dependency |
| Email revenue share > 25% | Email revenue / total revenue | Reduced paid channel dependency |
| Add-to-cart rate > 7.5% | Add-to-cart events / sessions | Healthy product-market fit signal |
| LTV:CAC > 3:1 | Customer LTV / CAC | Sustainable acquisition economics |

**Constraint**: Q4 metrics inflate all composite rules by 15-25%. Use Q1-Q3 averages for conservative planning. Revenue per session varies 5x by vertical — always compare within vertical. [src1]

## Common Misinterpretations

- **Comparing session-based vs visitor-based conversion rates**: Session-based rates are 10-20% higher than visitor-based because returning visitors create multiple sessions. Always confirm the denominator before benchmarking.
- **Applying Q4 benchmarks to annual planning**: Holiday season inflates conversion rates by 15-25% and AOV by 20-30%. Use Q1-Q3 averages for realistic baseline targets.
- **Ignoring device mix when comparing conversion rates**: A store with 80% mobile traffic will always show a lower blended rate than one with 50% mobile. Compare desktop and mobile rates separately.
- **Treating return rate as pure loss**: Returns drive repeat purchases — customers who return items have 40% higher 12-month LTV than those who never return. The net impact matters more than the gross rate.
