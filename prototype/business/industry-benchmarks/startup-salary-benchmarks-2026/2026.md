# Startup Salary Benchmarks 2026

## Summary

Comprehensive startup compensation benchmarks covering base salary, equity grants, and total compensation across engineering, product, design, marketing, sales, and executive roles by funding stage (seed through Series C+). Overall startup salaries rose 5.8% over the past three years, with AI/ML roles surging 5-9% in H1 2025 alone. Equity packages remained static despite salary increases, creating a growing gap between cash and total compensation. [src1]

**Data vintage**: Based on H1 2025 data from 40,000+ employees at 5,000+ startups (Carta) plus aggregate data from Wellfound, Ravio, and TopStartups.io.
**Key shift**: Two-tier market emerging — AI/ML and security roles command 15-25% premiums while non-AI engineering salaries plateau. Late-stage startups now pay 31-34% more than early-stage for senior talent.

## Constraints
<!-- Agents: read before citing any benchmark number. -->

- These benchmarks represent US-based, VC-backed startups. Bootstrapped companies and non-US markets require significant adjustment.
- Stage matters — seed to Series C+ compensation differs 30-40% for senior roles. Always specify funding stage.
- Geography creates 20-40% variance. SF/NYC at the top; remote-first companies often use 3-4 geographic tiers with 10-15% decrements per tier.
- Title inflation is severe at startups. A "Senior Engineer" at a seed company may map to L3-L4 at a big tech company. Use level, not title, for benchmarking.
- Equity data uses 409A / FMV at grant date. Early-stage equity (seed/A) has 80-90% probability of being worth $0. Do not add equity value to salary for "total comp" without risk-adjusting.
- Data from H1 2025. AI-related roles shift 2-3x faster than other functions.

## Composite Metrics & Rules of Thumb

| Rule | Formula / Threshold | Interpretation |
|------|---------------------|----------------|
| Equity = 0.5x salary at seed | Equity FMV / base salary | Fair risk-reward balance for early hires |
| 50th percentile = table stakes | Market median cash comp | Below 50th pct = retention risk within 6 months |
| Eng = 60-70% of headcount cost | Engineering comp / total comp | Typical for product-led startups |
| Founder salary < market until B | Founder comp vs. market | Board governance expectation |
| AI premium = 15-25% | AI role comp / equivalent non-AI | Current market premium for AI/ML talent |
| Equity refresh = 25-50% of initial | Annual refresh grants | Retention mechanism post-cliff |

**Constraint**: Equity value calculations are speculative at early stages. An employee with 0.5% at seed has an 80-90% chance of that equity being worth nothing. Risk-adjust equity value: multiply by probability-weighted expected outcome, not latest valuation. [src1]

## Common Misinterpretations

- **Adding equity face value to base for total comp**: Early-stage equity has an 80-90% chance of being worthless. A $150K salary + "0.5% worth $500K" does not equal $650K total comp. Risk-adjust or treat equity as a separate, high-variance component. [src1]
- **Comparing titles across stages**: A "Senior Engineer" at a 10-person seed startup is a fundamentally different role than a "Senior Engineer" at a 500-person Series C. Use level frameworks (L3, L5, etc.), not titles.
- **Ignoring geographic adjustments**: A $200K salary in San Francisco has roughly the same purchasing power as $140K in Austin or $120K in Raleigh. Compare on cost-of-living-adjusted basis for retention planning.
- **Treating AI premiums as permanent**: The 15-25% AI/ML premium reflects current scarcity. As AI education expands and tools commoditize some AI tasks, premiums may narrow to 5-10% within 2-3 years.
