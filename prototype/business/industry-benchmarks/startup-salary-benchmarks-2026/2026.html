Summary

Comprehensive startup compensation benchmarks covering base salary, equity grants, and total compensation across engineering, product, design, marketing, sales, and executive roles by funding stage. AI/ML roles surged 5-9% in H1 2025 while equity packages remained static. [src1]

Data vintage: H1 2025 data from 40,000+ employees at 5,000+ startups.

Key shift: Two-tier market — AI/ML roles command 15-25% premiums while non-AI salaries plateau.

Constraints

  • US-based, VC-backed startups. Bootstrapped and non-US require adjustment.
  • Stage differences of 30-40% for senior roles. Always specify funding stage.
  • SF/NYC 20-40% higher than national average. Remote companies use geographic tiers.
  • Title inflation is severe — map to levels (L3, L5), not titles.
  • Early-stage equity has 80-90% probability of being worth $0. Risk-adjust before combining with salary.

Common Misinterpretations

  • Adding equity face value to salary: Early-stage equity has 80-90% chance of being worthless. Do not combine for "total comp" without risk-adjusting.
  • Comparing titles across stages: "Senior Engineer" at a seed startup differs from Series C. Use levels (L3-L7), not titles.
  • Ignoring geographic adjustments: $200K in SF equals roughly $140K in Austin in purchasing power.
  • Treating AI premiums as permanent: The 15-25% premium reflects current scarcity. May narrow to 5-10% as supply catches up.
