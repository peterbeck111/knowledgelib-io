What do companies actually pay for SaaS 2026 — CRM, MAP, HRIS, ERP, analytics real pricing?

Type: Benchmark Data Vintage: Q4 2025 Confidence: 0.80 Sources: 6 Verified: 2026-03-11

Summary

SaaS vendor pricing in 2026 reflects the steepest price increases in a decade, with major vendors implementing 6-25% annual hikes. Per-seat pricing remains dominant but is increasingly supplemented by credit and consumption models (up 126% YoY). [src1]

Data vintage: Based on Q4 2025 published pricing and negotiated deal data across major SaaS categories.

Key shift: 2025 marked aggressive B2B pricing increases while credit-based AI pricing models grew from 35 to 79 companies.

Constraints

  • List prices and negotiated prices differ 15-40% for enterprise deals. These are published list prices unless noted.
  • Per-seat pricing supplemented by consumption models, making direct comparisons difficult without usage patterns.
  • Implementation costs (1-3x annual license) are excluded from all figures.
  • Vendor pricing pages change frequently. Verify current prices before negotiations.
  • IT budgets growing at 2.8% annually while vendors hike 9-25%. Expect negotiation leverage.

Common Misinterpretations

  • Comparing list prices across vendors: Enterprise-negotiated Salesforce at $100/seat may be cheaper than HubSpot list at $100 if HubSpot doesn't discount. Compare effective pricing at your volume.
  • Ignoring implementation costs: A $50K/year ERP with $150K implementation = $200K first year. Always ask about total first-year cost.
  • Per-seat vs per-contact confusion: A $100/seat CRM and $100/1000-contacts MAP are different units. Normalize to cost-per-user or cost-per-revenue.
  • Treating SaaS as fixed cost: Usage-based models mean costs scale with consumption. Budget 20-40% variance above baseline.
