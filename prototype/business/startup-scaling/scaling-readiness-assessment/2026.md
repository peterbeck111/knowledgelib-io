# Startup Scaling Readiness Assessment

## Purpose

This recipe produces a quantitative Scaling Readiness Scorecard that evaluates your startup across four dimensions — product-market fit evidence, unit economics health, process maturity, and team capacity — and generates a prioritized Pre-Scale Action Plan identifying exactly which gaps must close before scaling spend. 74% of startups fail due to premature scaling according to the Startup Genome Report, making this assessment the single most important gate before increasing burn rate. [src1]

## Prerequisites
<!-- Agents: verify ALL prerequisites before executing. Missing prerequisites = failed execution. -->

- [ ] **3+ months of revenue/user data** available from internal analytics — monthly revenue, user counts, churn rates
- [ ] **Customer acquisition data** available — cost per channel, conversion rates, payback period
- [ ] **Retention data** available — cohort retention curves (weekly or monthly)
- [ ] **Analytics platform access** — Mixpanel, Amplitude, PostHog, or equivalent
- [ ] **Survey tool** — for running Sean Ellis PMF test if not already done
- [ ] **Spreadsheet tool** — Google Sheets or Excel for the scoring framework

## Constraints
<!-- Hard rules. Agents: enforce throughout execution. Violating these = broken output or legal risk. -->

- Minimum 50 paying customers or 500 active users required for statistical significance. Below this, the assessment produces unreliable scores.
- Unit economics must use fully-loaded CAC (include salaries, tools, overhead), not just ad spend. Marginal CAC understates true acquisition cost by 40-60%.
- Retention must be measured on cohort basis, not aggregate. Aggregate retention masks declining cohorts behind new user growth.
- Assessment must be refreshed quarterly. Markets, teams, and products change — a passing score at Q1 does not guarantee Q2 readiness.
- Revenue growth alone does not prove PMF. Paid acquisition can manufacture growth that collapses when spend stops. [src2]

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Gather Raw Data

**Duration**: 1-2 hours
**Tool**: Analytics platform + internal records

Collect the following data points into a single spreadsheet:

**Revenue & Growth:**
- Monthly revenue for last 3-12 months
- Month-over-month growth rate
- Revenue by channel/segment

**Customers & Retention:**
- Total active customers/users
- Monthly cohort retention (Month 1 through Month 6+)
- Churn rate (logo churn and revenue churn)
- NPS or CSAT scores if available

**Unit Economics:**
- Fully-loaded CAC by channel (ad spend + salaries + tools + overhead)
- Average revenue per user (ARPU) monthly
- Gross margin percentage
- LTV estimate (ARPU x gross margin x average lifespan)

**Team:**
- Current headcount by function
- Open roles and time-to-fill history
- Key person dependencies (single points of failure)
- Management span of control

**Verify**: All 4 data sections populated. Missing sections should be flagged, not skipped.
**If failed**: If retention data is unavailable, use payment/login frequency as proxy. If CAC data is missing, estimate from total marketing spend / new customers.

### Step 2: Score Dimension 1 — Product-Market Fit

**Duration**: 45-60 minutes
**Tool**: Survey tool + spreadsheet

Run the Sean Ellis PMF survey if not already done: ask existing users "How would you feel if you could no longer use [product]?" with options: Very disappointed, Somewhat disappointed, Not disappointed. [src3]

**PMF Scoring Rubric:**

| Signal | Score 0 | Score 1 | Score 2 | Score 3 |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| Sean Ellis "Very Disappointed" | < 20% | 20-30% | 30-40% | > 40% |
| Month-3 Cohort Retention | < 15% | 15-30% | 30-50% | > 50% |
| Organic/WOM Growth Share | < 10% | 10-25% | 25-40% | > 40% |
| NPS Score | < 0 | 0-30 | 30-50 | > 50 |
| Engagement Depth (DAU/MAU) | < 10% | 10-20% | 20-40% | > 40% |

Calculate PMF Score = sum of all signal scores / 15 (maximum). Convert to 0-100 scale.

- **Score < 40**: PMF not achieved. Do not scale. Return to product iteration.
- **Score 40-60**: Weak PMF. Scaling is high-risk. Address specific weak signals first.
- **Score 60-80**: Moderate PMF. Can begin cautious scaling in strongest segments.
- **Score > 80**: Strong PMF. Green light for aggressive scaling. [src2]

**Verify**: PMF score calculated with at least 3 of 5 signals measured.
**If failed**: If survey response rate is below 30%, incentivize completion or use behavioral proxies (retention + engagement) as substitute.

### Step 3: Score Dimension 2 — Unit Economics

**Duration**: 45-60 minutes
**Tool**: Spreadsheet

Calculate unit economics health using these benchmarks: [src6]

| Metric | Score 0 | Score 1 | Score 2 | Score 3 |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| LTV:CAC Ratio | < 1:1 | 1-2:1 | 2-3:1 | > 3:1 |
| CAC Payback Period | > 24 months | 18-24 months | 12-18 months | < 12 months |
| Gross Margin | < 40% | 40-60% | 60-75% | > 75% |
| Revenue Churn (monthly) | > 5% | 3-5% | 1-3% | < 1% |
| Net Revenue Retention | < 80% | 80-100% | 100-120% | > 120% |

Calculate Unit Economics Score = sum / 15 x 100.

- **Score < 40**: Economics are broken. Scaling will accelerate losses. Fix pricing, reduce CAC, or improve retention first.
- **Score 40-60**: Marginal economics. Scaling requires careful monitoring and likely price/packaging changes.
- **Score 60-80**: Healthy economics. Can scale with confidence but watch for CAC inflation at higher volumes.
- **Score > 80**: Excellent economics. Aggressive scaling justified.

**Verify**: All 5 metrics calculated with real data (not projections).
**If failed**: If LTV cannot be calculated (too early), use 6-month cumulative revenue per customer as proxy and note the limitation.

### Step 4: Score Dimension 3 — Process Maturity

**Duration**: 30-45 minutes
**Tool**: Spreadsheet

Assess operational readiness — can the business handle 3-5x volume without breaking? [src4]

| Process Area | Score 0 | Score 1 | Score 2 | Score 3 |
|-------------|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| Customer Onboarding | Ad hoc, founder-led | Documented but manual | Partially automated | Fully automated + self-serve |
| Support/Success | Founder inbox | Shared inbox + FAQ | Ticketing system + KB | Tiered support + SLA |
| Sales Process | Founder sells everything | Basic playbook exists | Repeatable by non-founders | Predictable pipeline |
| Billing & Collections | Manual invoicing | Payment processor | Automated billing + dunning | Full revenue operations |
| Product Development | Founder builds alone | Small team, ad hoc | Sprint-based, roadmap exists | Prioritization framework + velocity tracking |

Calculate Process Maturity Score = sum / 15 x 100.

- **Score < 40**: Founder-dependent. Scaling will create chaos. Systematize core processes first.
- **Score 40-60**: Partially systematized. Can scale 2x but not 5x. Invest in tooling.
- **Score 60-80**: Well-systematized. Ready for significant scale.
- **Score > 80**: Operational excellence. Process is not the bottleneck.

**Verify**: All 5 process areas assessed honestly (founder bias toward overrating is common).
**If failed**: If assessment feels unreliable, interview 2-3 team members independently and average scores.

### Step 5: Score Dimension 4 — Team Capacity

**Duration**: 30-45 minutes
**Tool**: Spreadsheet

Assess whether the team can execute at scale: [src5]

| Factor | Score 0 | Score 1 | Score 2 | Score 3 |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| Key Person Risk | 3+ single points of failure | 2 SPOFs | 1 SPOF | No SPOFs, cross-trained |
| Management Capacity | No managers, founders manage all | 1-2 leads, high span of control | Department leads, reasonable spans | Management layer in place |
| Hiring Pipeline | No pipeline, reactive | Some pipeline, slow fills | Active pipeline, < 60 day fills | Strong pipeline, < 30 day fills |
| Culture Documentation | Nothing written | Values stated informally | Values + handbook documented | Values lived + reinforced |
| Founder Bandwidth | Founders overloaded, in weeds | Founders mostly operational | Founders 50% strategic | Founders primarily strategic |

Calculate Team Capacity Score = sum / 15 x 100.

- **Score < 40**: Team is the bottleneck. Scaling will burn out key people. Hire or restructure first.
- **Score 40-60**: Team is stretched. Can handle moderate growth but not aggressive scaling.
- **Score 60-80**: Team is capable. Ready for significant scaling.
- **Score > 80**: Team is strong. Not the constraint.

**Verify**: Assessment includes input from at least 2 team members, not just founder self-assessment.
**If failed**: If team is < 5 people, some factors (management capacity, culture documentation) may not apply — adjust scoring denominator accordingly.

### Step 6: Generate Composite Scorecard and Action Plan

**Duration**: 30-45 minutes
**Tool**: Spreadsheet

Combine all four dimension scores into the Scaling Readiness Scorecard:

**Composite Score Calculation:**
- PMF Score (weight: 35%) — most important, scaling without PMF is fatal
- Unit Economics Score (weight: 30%) — determines if scaling is financially viable
- Process Maturity Score (weight: 20%) — determines if scaling is operationally possible
- Team Capacity Score (weight: 15%) — determines if people can execute

**Overall Readiness Grade:**
- **A (80-100)**: Ready to scale aggressively. All dimensions strong.
- **B (60-79)**: Ready to scale cautiously. Address specific weaknesses.
- **C (40-59)**: Not ready. Significant gaps must close. Timeline: 3-6 months of focused work.
- **D (20-39)**: Premature scaling would be destructive. Return to fundamentals.
- **F (0-19)**: Business model may need rethinking. Do not scale.

**Action Plan Generation:**
For each dimension scoring below 60, generate a specific remediation plan:
1. Identify the lowest-scoring signals within that dimension
2. Assign owner and timeline for improvement
3. Define the target score and how to measure progress
4. Set a reassessment date

**Output files**:
- `scaling-readiness-scorecard.xlsx` — Full scorecard with all 4 dimensions, 20 signals, and composite grade
- `pre-scale-action-plan.md` — Prioritized gap list with owners, timelines, and success criteria
- `assessment-summary.md` — Executive summary suitable for board or investor communication

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Scaling on vanity metrics
Running a scaling readiness assessment using only top-line revenue growth and user signups. Revenue can grow from paid acquisition while retention is collapsing — the Startup Genome Report found that 74% of high-growth startup failures were due to premature scaling. [src1]

### Correct: Use leading indicators
Score based on retention curves, unit economics, and process maturity. Revenue growth is an output, not an input to readiness. If Month-3 retention is below 30% and CAC payback exceeds 18 months, the business is not ready to scale regardless of revenue trajectory.

### Wrong: One-time assessment
Treating the scorecard as a one-time gate check and never revisiting. Markets, competitors, and team dynamics change.

### Correct: Quarterly reassessment
Run the full assessment quarterly. Track scores over time to identify trends. A declining PMF score (even if still above threshold) is an early warning signal that requires investigation.

### Wrong: Founder-only self-assessment
Founders rating their own process maturity and team capacity without input from the team. Founders systematically overrate operational readiness. [src5]

### Correct: Multi-rater assessment
Include at least 2-3 team members in the assessment. Average scores across raters. Use the gap between founder and team scores as a diagnostic signal — large gaps indicate awareness problems.

## When This Matters

Use this recipe when a startup is considering increasing spend (hiring, marketing, infrastructure) by more than 50%. The assessment should be the gate check before any significant scaling investment. Running this assessment saves an average of $50K-$500K in wasted scaling spend by identifying gaps before they become expensive failures.
