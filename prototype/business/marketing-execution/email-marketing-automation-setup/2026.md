# Email Marketing Automation Setup

## Purpose

This recipe configures a complete email marketing automation system — from ESP account setup and domain authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC) through a 5-7 email welcome sequence, behavioral and demographic segments, automation triggers, and a 4-week deliverability warmup plan. The output is a fully operational email automation platform sending authenticated emails with a warm sender reputation, automated welcome flow converting new subscribers, and segmented lists ready for targeted campaigns. [src1]

## Prerequisites

- [ ] **Brand messaging** with value proposition, key benefits, and brand voice defined
- [ ] **Lead capture mechanism** — signup form, landing page, or lead magnet live and collecting emails
- [ ] **Domain with DNS access** — ability to add TXT/CNAME records for SPF, DKIM, DMARC
- [ ] **ESP account** created — [Mailchimp](https://mailchimp.com), [Klaviyo](https://klaviyo.com), [ConvertKit](https://convertkit.com), [MailerLite](https://mailerlite.com), or [HubSpot](https://hubspot.com)
- [ ] **Google Postmaster Tools** — registered at [postmaster.google.com](https://postmaster.google.com) for deliverability monitoring
- [ ] **Privacy compliance** — CAN-SPAM physical address (US), GDPR consent mechanism (EU), CASL opt-in (Canada) as applicable

## Constraints

- SPF, DKIM, and DMARC authentication is mandatory — Google, Yahoo, and Microsoft reject unauthenticated bulk email. [src1]
- Domain warmup requires 2-4 weeks minimum. Sending high volume from a new domain triggers spam filters immediately. [src4]
- Spam complaint rate must stay below 0.3%. Exceeding this triggers deliverability penalties that can take months to recover from. [src1]
- Hard bounce rate must stay below 2%. Run list verification before every campaign to avoid reputation damage. [src3]
- One-click unsubscribe (RFC 8058 List-Unsubscribe-Post header) is required for all marketing email — enforced by Gmail and Yahoo since February 2024. [src1]

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: ESP Account Setup and Domain Configuration

**Duration**: 30-60 minutes
**Tool**: ESP dashboard + DNS registrar

Create the ESP account and configure the sending domain. Every ESP requires domain verification and authentication before sending.

```
ESP Account Setup:
─────────────────────────────────────────
1. Create account at chosen ESP
2. Navigate to: Settings → Domains → Add Sending Domain
3. Enter your business domain (e.g., yourcompany.com)
4. ESP generates DNS records to add:
   • CNAME records for DKIM (typically 2 records)
   • TXT record for domain verification

Domain Verification:
─────────────────────────────────────────
1. Log into your DNS registrar (Cloudflare, Namecheap, etc.)
2. Add the CNAME records provided by your ESP
3. Add the TXT verification record
4. Wait 15-60 minutes for DNS propagation
5. Return to ESP → click "Verify Domain"
```

**Verify**: ESP shows domain as "Verified" or "Authenticated." Send a test email — check it arrives in inbox (not spam) and passes authentication checks.
**If failed**: DNS propagation can take up to 48 hours. Double-check record values for typos. Use MXToolbox (mxtoolbox.com) to validate DNS records.

### Step 2: Email Authentication — SPF, DKIM, DMARC

**Duration**: 30-45 minutes
**Tool**: DNS registrar

Configure all three authentication protocols. This is non-negotiable for deliverability in 2025-2026. [src1]

```
SPF Record:
─────────────────────────────────────────
Type: TXT
Host: @ (or blank)
Value: v=spf1 include:{esp-spf-domain} ~all

Examples by ESP:
  Mailchimp:  v=spf1 include:servers.mcsv.net ~all
  Klaviyo:    v=spf1 include:_spf.klaviyo.com ~all
  ConvertKit: v=spf1 include:_spf.convertkit.com ~all
  HubSpot:    v=spf1 include:hubspot.com ~all

DKIM Records:
─────────────────────────────────────────
(Provided by ESP — typically 2 CNAME records)
Copy exact values from ESP dashboard.

DMARC Record:
─────────────────────────────────────────
Type: TXT
Host: _dmarc
Value: v=DMARC1; p=quarantine; rua=mailto:dmarc@yourdomain.com; pct=100

DMARC policy progression:
  Week 1-2:  p=none     (monitor only — collect reports)
  Week 3-4:  p=quarantine (flag suspicious emails)
  Week 5+:   p=reject   (block unauthenticated emails)
```

**Verify**: Use Google Admin Toolbox (toolbox.googleapps.com/apps/checkmx) or MXToolbox to verify all three records. Send test email and check headers for "PASS" on SPF, DKIM, and DMARC.
**If failed**: If SPF fails, check for conflicting SPF records (only one TXT SPF record allowed per domain). If DKIM fails, regenerate keys in ESP and re-add CNAME records.

### Step 3: Welcome Sequence Creation (5-7 Emails)

**Duration**: 2-3 hours
**Tool**: ESP automation/flow builder

Build the welcome sequence. Welcome emails achieve 83.63% average open rate vs 43% for regular campaigns, making this the highest-impact automation to build first. [src2] [src3]

```
Welcome Sequence Structure:
─────────────────────────────────────────
Email 1 — Immediate (0 delay after signup)
  Subject: Welcome + deliver promised value
  Content: Thank subscriber, deliver lead magnet/discount,
           set expectations for future emails
  CTA: Use the lead magnet / claim the offer
  Goal: Immediate engagement (drives open rates for future emails)

Email 2 — Day 1 (24 hours after signup)
  Subject: Brand story + why you exist
  Content: Founder story, mission, what makes you different
  CTA: Follow on social / visit website
  Goal: Build brand connection

Email 3 — Day 3
  Subject: Top content / best products / key feature
  Content: Showcase 3-5 best items (products, articles, features)
  CTA: Browse / read / try the feature
  Goal: Drive first meaningful interaction

Email 4 — Day 5
  Subject: Social proof
  Content: Customer testimonials, case studies, review highlights,
           user numbers, trust signals
  CTA: See customer results / read case study
  Goal: Build trust and reduce purchase anxiety

Email 5 — Day 7
  Subject: Overcome objections + FAQ
  Content: Address top 3 objections, compare to alternatives,
           answer common questions
  CTA: Start free trial / shop now
  Goal: Remove barriers to conversion

Email 6 — Day 10 (optional)
  Subject: Exclusive offer or deeper value
  Content: Limited-time discount, extended trial, bonus content,
           insider access
  CTA: Claim the offer (urgency element)
  Goal: Convert fence-sitters

Email 7 — Day 14 (optional)
  Subject: Community + what's next
  Content: Invite to community, preview upcoming content,
           ask preferences (drives segmentation)
  CTA: Join community / set preferences
  Goal: Transition from welcome to ongoing engagement

Flow Logic:
─────────────────────────────────────────
[Signup] → Email 1 (immediate)
  → Wait 24h → Email 2
    → Wait 48h → Email 3
      → Wait 48h → Email 4
        → Wait 48h → Email 5
          → [IF not converted] Wait 72h → Email 6
            → Wait 96h → Email 7
          → [IF converted] → Exit flow → enter post-purchase flow
```

**Verify**: Send yourself through the full flow. Check timing, links, images, personalization tokens, mobile rendering. Verify exit conditions work (converters leave the flow).
**If failed**: If emails look broken on mobile, use single-column layouts (600px max width). If personalization tokens show raw code, check merge tag syntax matches your ESP format.

### Step 4: Segment Setup

**Duration**: 45-60 minutes
**Tool**: ESP audience/segment builder

Create dynamic segments that automatically update as subscriber behavior changes. [src6]

```
Essential Segments (create all):
─────────────────────────────────────────
1. Engagement-Based Segments:
   • Active (opened or clicked in last 30 days)
   • Engaged (opened or clicked in last 90 days)
   • At-Risk (no opens in 30-90 days)
   • Inactive (no opens in 90+ days)
   • Super-Engaged (clicked 3+ emails in 30 days)

2. Lifecycle Segments:
   • New Subscribers (joined in last 14 days)
   • Customers (made a purchase / converted)
   • Repeat Customers (2+ purchases)
   • VIP (top 10% by revenue or engagement)
   • Churned (customer, no activity 90+ days)

3. Source-Based Segments:
   • Organic Signup (website form)
   • Paid Acquisition (from ad campaigns)
   • Lead Magnet (specific content download)
   • Referral (referred by another customer)

4. Behavioral Segments (e-commerce):
   • Browsed but didn't buy (last 7 days)
   • Abandoned cart (last 24 hours)
   • Category interest (browsed 3+ items in category)

5. Demographic Segments (if data available):
   • By location/region
   • By company size (B2B)
   • By industry/role (B2B)
```

**Verify**: Check each segment populates correctly. Active segment should show contacts who opened recent emails. New Subscribers segment should only contain last 14 days of signups. Run a count check — segments should not overlap excessively.
**If failed**: If segments show 0 contacts, check filter logic (AND vs OR conditions). If segments show all contacts, criteria are too broad — narrow the time windows.

### Step 5: Automation Trigger Configuration

**Duration**: 30-45 minutes
**Tool**: ESP automation builder

Set up event-based triggers beyond the welcome sequence. These run automatically based on subscriber actions.

```
Core Automation Triggers:
─────────────────────────────────────────
Trigger 1: Welcome Flow
  Event: "Added to List" (new subscriber)
  Action: Enter welcome sequence (Step 3)

Trigger 2: Re-engagement Flow
  Event: "No email opens in 60 days"
  Action: Send 3-email re-engagement sequence:
    Email 1: "We miss you" + best content
    Email 2: "Last chance" + special offer
    Email 3: "Goodbye" (confirm unsubscribe or re-engage)
  If no open after Email 3: move to suppression list

Trigger 3: Post-Purchase (e-commerce)
  Event: "Order placed"
  Action: Thank you → Usage tips (Day 3) → Review request (Day 14)

Trigger 4: Abandoned Cart (e-commerce)
  Event: "Cart abandoned" (no purchase within 1 hour)
  Action: Reminder (1h) → Urgency (24h) → Discount (48h)

Trigger 5: Date-Based
  Event: "Subscriber anniversary" or "Birthday"
  Action: Send celebration email with special offer

Trigger 6: Segment Entry
  Event: "Contact enters At-Risk segment"
  Action: Send re-engagement offer within 48 hours

ESP-Specific Setup:
─────────────────────────────────────────
Klaviyo: Flows → Create Flow → choose trigger → build sequence
Mailchimp: Automations → Create → Customer Journey → set trigger
ConvertKit: Automations → New Automation → choose trigger event
HubSpot: Marketing → Workflows → Create → Enrollment triggers
```

**Verify**: Test each trigger by performing the triggering action (signup, cart abandonment simulation, etc.). Confirm the correct automation fires and contacts enter the right flow.
**If failed**: If triggers don't fire, check that the ESP integration (Shopify, website, etc.) is properly connected and sending events. Verify trigger conditions match the event data format.

### Step 6: Deliverability Warmup Plan

**Duration**: 15 minutes setup, 4 weeks execution
**Tool**: ESP + Google Postmaster Tools

Gradually increase sending volume to build sender reputation. Sending too many emails too fast from a new domain is the most common reason for landing in spam. [src4]

```
4-Week Warmup Schedule:
─────────────────────────────────────────
Week 1 — Foundation (5-20 emails/day)
  Day 1-2: Send to 5-10 most engaged contacts only
  Day 3-5: Increase to 15-20 contacts
  Day 6-7: Monitor — check Google Postmaster for reputation
  Target: 100% inbox placement, 0 spam complaints

Week 2 — Gradual Expansion (20-50 emails/day)
  Day 8-10: Send to 25-35 contacts per day
  Day 11-14: Increase to 40-50 contacts per day
  Content: High-value content only (not promotions)
  Target: >95% inbox placement, <0.1% complaint rate

Week 3 — Scaling (50-200 emails/day)
  Day 15-17: Send to 75-100 contacts per day
  Day 18-21: Increase to 150-200 contacts per day
  Mix: 80% value content, 20% soft promotional
  Target: >90% inbox placement, <0.2% complaint rate

Week 4 — Normal Operations (200+ emails/day)
  Day 22-25: Send to 200-500 contacts per day
  Day 26-28: Full list sends (if list is <5K)
  Mix: Normal content/promotional ratio
  Target: >85% inbox placement, <0.3% complaint rate

Warmup Best Practices:
─────────────────────────────────────────
• Send to most engaged contacts first (recent openers/clickers)
• Use consistent From name and From email throughout
• Monitor Google Postmaster daily during warmup
• If spam complaints spike, pause for 48 hours, reduce volume 50%
• Maintain consistent daily volume — don't skip days then blast
• Include text-only version of every email (improves deliverability)
```

**Verify**: Google Postmaster shows "High" domain reputation after 4 weeks. Inbox placement rate above 85%. No blacklist appearances (check at mxtoolbox.com/blacklists).
**If failed**: If reputation drops to "Medium" or "Low," immediately cut volume by 50%. Send only to highest-engaged subscribers for 1 week. Check for spam traps in your list — run through a list cleaning service (ZeroBounce, NeverBounce).

## Quality Benchmarks

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Emails landing in spam | Missing SPF/DKIM/DMARC or poor sender reputation | Verify DNS records with MXToolbox. Pause sending, fix authentication, restart warmup at Week 1 volume. [src1] |
| High bounce rate (> 5%) | Stale or purchased email list | Run list through verification service (ZeroBounce, NeverBounce). Remove all invalid addresses before next send. |
| Welcome emails not sending | Automation trigger misconfigured | Check trigger event (list subscription vs form submission). Verify flow is "Active" not "Draft." Test with manual list addition. |
| Low open rates (< 20%) | Subject lines weak or sending at wrong times | A/B test subject lines (question vs benefit vs number). Test send times (Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11 AM local). Check From name recognition. |
| Unsubscribe rate > 1% per email | Content not matching expectations set at signup | Review signup promise vs email content alignment. Reduce frequency. Add preference center for frequency/topic control. |
| DMARC alignment failures | SPF/DKIM domain mismatch with From domain | Ensure ESP sends from your domain (not theirs). Check DKIM signing domain matches your From domain. |
| ESP account suspended | Spam complaints exceeded threshold | Contact ESP support. Clean list aggressively. Implement double opt-in. Restart with confirmed subscribers only. |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Sending to full list on day one with a new domain
Blasting 5,000 emails from a brand-new sending domain. Result: immediate spam classification, domain reputation destroyed before it was ever established, and months of recovery effort. [src4]

### Correct: Follow the 4-week warmup plan
Start at 5-10 emails/day to most engaged subscribers. Increase gradually. Monitor Google Postmaster daily. Only reach full volume after establishing a "High" sender reputation.

### Wrong: Skipping domain authentication
Sending email without SPF, DKIM, and DMARC configured. Result: Gmail and Yahoo reject or spam-folder emails by default. Open rates drop to 5-10% because emails never reach the inbox. [src1]

### Correct: Configure all three protocols before first send
Set up SPF, DKIM, and DMARC (Step 2) before sending any email. Verify all three pass using header inspection on a test email. Start DMARC at p=none, escalate to p=reject over 4 weeks.

### Wrong: Welcome sequence that only sells
Every email in the welcome sequence pushes a purchase. Subscribers feel like they joined a sales pitch, not a valuable relationship. Unsubscribe rates spike above 2% per email.

### Correct: Follow the 70/30 rule — 70% value, 30% promotion
Lead with value (tips, education, stories, social proof) for the first 4-5 emails. Introduce promotional content gradually. The conversion email should feel earned, not forced. [src2]
