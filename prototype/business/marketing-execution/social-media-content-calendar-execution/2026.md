# Social Media Content Calendar Execution

## Purpose

This recipe produces a fully operational social media content calendar with batch-created assets, scheduled posts across all target platforms, engagement response templates, and a weekly analytics tracking workflow. The output is a repeatable 4-week rolling system that a solo founder or small team can maintain in 3-5 hours per week, covering platform-specific content creation, scheduling via Buffer/Hootsuite/Later, content repurposing from long-form to short-form, and standardized engagement responses. [src1]

## Prerequisites
<!-- Agents: verify ALL prerequisites before executing. Missing prerequisites = failed execution. -->

- [ ] **Social media strategy** available from strategy phase — [Social Media Strategy by Platform](/business/startup-marketing/social-media-strategy-by-platform/2026)
- [ ] **Brand assets** — logo, brand colors, fonts, and at least 3 image templates (create in Canva if unavailable)
- [ ] **Business/Creator accounts** on target platforms — personal accounts cannot auto-publish via scheduling tools
- [ ] **Scheduling tool account** — [Buffer](https://buffer.com) (free tier: 3 channels, 10 posts each) or [Later](https://later.com) (free trial: 14 days)
- [ ] **Design tool account** — [Canva](https://www.canva.com) (free tier sufficient for basic use)
- [ ] **Content pillars defined** — 3-5 recurring themes that align with brand positioning and audience needs

## Constraints
<!-- Hard rules. Agents: enforce throughout execution. Violating these = broken output or legal risk. -->

- Platform API auto-publish requirements: Instagram needs Business/Creator account connected through Facebook Business Suite; TikTok auto-publish requires Creator account. Without these, posts will be sent as push notifications for manual publishing. [src1]
- Image specs (2025-2026): Instagram feed 1080x1080px or 1080x1350px; Instagram/TikTok Reels 1080x1920px; LinkedIn 1200x627px; X/Twitter 1600x900px; Facebook 1200x630px. Verify current specs before batch creation. [src2]
- Scheduling tools on free tiers cap channels and queue depth — Buffer Free allows 3 channels with 10 scheduled posts each; Later Starter starts at $25/month for 1 social set. [src1]
- Never schedule trend-dependent content more than 1 week ahead — trends expire; only schedule evergreen content 2-4 weeks out. [src6]
- Engagement response time target: reply within 1 hour during business hours for DMs and tagged mentions; within 4 hours for comments. Delayed responses lose 50%+ of engagement lift. [src4]

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Platform Audit and Account Setup

**Duration**: 1-2 hours
**Tool**: Target social platforms + scheduling tool

Audit all existing social media accounts and configure them for automated publishing.

1. List all target platforms and verify account type (Business/Creator vs Personal)
2. Convert personal accounts to Business/Creator accounts where needed:
   - Instagram: Settings > Account > Switch to Professional Account > Business
   - LinkedIn: Create Company Page if not existing (requires personal profile)
   - TikTok: Settings > Manage Account > Switch to Business Account
   - Facebook: Create/claim Business Page through Meta Business Suite
3. Connect all accounts to your chosen scheduling tool via OAuth
4. Set timezone in scheduling tool to match primary audience timezone
5. Enable auto-publish (not push notification) for each connected account

**Verify**: Each connected account shows "Connected" with auto-publish enabled in scheduling tool dashboard. Test by scheduling a post 5 minutes ahead and confirming it publishes without manual intervention.
**If failed**: If auto-publish fails, check account type (must be Business/Creator), re-authenticate via OAuth, or check platform-specific requirements in scheduling tool documentation.

### Step 2: Content Pillar and Calendar Framework Setup

**Duration**: 1-2 hours
**Tool**: Spreadsheet or scheduling tool calendar

Define content pillars and map them to a weekly posting schedule.

1. Define 3-5 content pillars based on strategy (e.g., Educational, Behind-the-Scenes, User-Generated, Promotional, Thought Leadership)
2. Assign each pillar a color code for visual calendar management
3. Create a weekly posting grid per platform:

```
WEEKLY CONTENT GRID TEMPLATE
===========================
           Mon    Tue    Wed    Thu    Fri    Sat    Sun
LinkedIn:  Edu    --     TL     --     Edu    --     --
Instagram: BTS    Promo  Edu    UGC    BTS    Promo  Edu
X/Twitter: Edu    TL     Edu    TL     Edu    --     --
TikTok:    BTS    Edu    --     BTS    Edu    --     BTS
Facebook:  Edu    --     Promo  --     UGC    --     Edu

Pillar Key: Edu=Educational, BTS=Behind-the-Scenes,
TL=Thought Leadership, UGC=User-Generated, Promo=Promotional
```

4. Set optimal posting times per platform based on audience analytics or industry defaults:
   - LinkedIn: Tue-Thu 8-10am and 12pm
   - Instagram: Mon-Fri 11am-1pm and 7-9pm
   - X/Twitter: Mon-Fri 8-10am and 12-3pm
   - TikTok: Tue-Thu 7-9pm, Sat 11am-1pm
   - Facebook: Wed-Fri 1-4pm

**Verify**: Calendar framework covers all target platforms with at least the minimum posting frequency per platform. No single pillar exceeds 40% of total posts.
**If failed**: If frequency is too high for available resources, reduce to top 2-3 platforms and increase per-platform quality.

### Step 3: Batch Content Creation

**Duration**: 2-3 hours per week of content
**Tool**: Canva + CapCut (or alternatives from tool selection)

Batch-create one full week of content assets in a single session.

1. Open Canva and create a Brand Kit (Pro) or folder with brand colors, fonts, and logo
2. Create platform-specific templates for each content pillar:
   - Instagram carousel: 1080x1080px, 5-10 slides
   - Instagram/TikTok Reels cover: 1080x1920px
   - LinkedIn post image: 1200x627px
   - X/Twitter image: 1600x900px
3. Batch-produce one week of visual assets:
   - Start with the highest-effort format (video or carousel)
   - Adapt each piece for other platforms (resize, reformat, adjust text length)
   - Export all assets with naming convention: `YYYY-MM-DD_platform_pillar_title.ext`
4. Write captions per platform in a document or spreadsheet:
   - LinkedIn: 150-300 words, professional tone, 3-5 hashtags
   - Instagram: 100-200 words, conversational, 10-15 hashtags (in first comment or end of caption)
   - X/Twitter: Under 280 characters, punchy, 1-2 hashtags
   - TikTok: 30-100 words, casual tone, 3-5 trending hashtags
   - Facebook: 50-150 words, conversational, 1-3 hashtags
5. Include a CTA in every post (question, link, save prompt, share prompt)

**Verify**: At least 1 week of content created with platform-appropriate dimensions and captions. All assets follow brand guidelines. Each post has a CTA.
**If failed**: If batch creation takes too long, reduce to 3 posts per platform per week and increase gradually. Use Canva's Magic Resize to speed up cross-platform adaptation.

### Step 4: Content Scheduling

**Duration**: 30-60 minutes per week
**Tool**: Buffer / Hootsuite / Later

Load all batch-created content into the scheduling tool.

1. Upload all visual assets to the scheduling tool media library
2. Create posts for each platform using the weekly content grid:
   - Attach the platform-specific visual asset
   - Paste the platform-specific caption
   - Set publish time according to the optimal times from Step 2
   - Add first-comment text for Instagram (hashtags or follow-up question)
3. For tools with bulk upload (Hootsuite, Sprout Social):
   - Prepare a CSV with columns: date, time, platform, caption, image_url
   - Use bulk upload to schedule an entire week at once
4. Review the visual calendar — check for gaps, overlapping themes, and consecutive promotional posts
5. If using team workflows, submit posts for approval before finalizing

**Verify**: All posts appear in the scheduling calendar at correct times. Visual preview shows correct images cropped properly. No scheduling conflicts (two posts to same platform within 1 hour).
**If failed**: If images appear incorrectly cropped, re-upload with exact platform dimensions. If scheduling conflicts exist, space posts at least 2 hours apart on the same platform.

### Step 5: Engagement Templates and Response Workflow

**Duration**: 1 hour (one-time setup)
**Tool**: Scheduling tool saved replies or document

Create standardized response templates for common engagement scenarios. [src4]

1. Create saved reply templates for each category:

```
ENGAGEMENT RESPONSE TEMPLATES
=============================

[QUESTION - Product/Service]
"Great question! [Brief answer]. For more details, check out
[link]. Let us know if you have any other questions!"

[POSITIVE FEEDBACK]
"Thank you so much for the kind words! We really appreciate
your support. [Personal touch based on their comment]."

[COMPLAINT/NEGATIVE]
"We're sorry to hear about your experience. We'd like to
help resolve this. Could you DM us with your [order
number/details] so we can look into it right away?"

[FEATURE REQUEST]
"Thanks for the suggestion! We love hearing ideas from
our community. We've noted this and shared it with our
team."

[SPAM/IRRELEVANT]
[Do not reply. Hide/delete comment. Block repeat offenders.]

[USER-GENERATED CONTENT]
"Love this! Thanks for sharing. Mind if we repost this
to our page? We'll tag you of course!"
```

2. Set up engagement check schedule:
   - Morning (9am): Check overnight comments and DMs, respond to urgent items
   - Midday (12pm): Respond to morning engagement, engage with industry posts
   - Evening (5pm): Final engagement sweep, respond to afternoon comments
3. Configure notifications: enable push notifications for DMs and mentions, disable for likes

**Verify**: At least 6 response templates saved in scheduling tool or accessible document. Engagement check schedule added to daily routine.
**If failed**: If scheduling tool lacks saved replies, create a Google Doc or Notion page with templates for quick copy-paste access.

### Step 6: Content Repurposing Workflow

**Duration**: 1-2 hours per week
**Tool**: Canva + CapCut + scheduling tool

Systematically repurpose top-performing content across platforms and formats. [src3]

1. Weekly repurposing audit — identify top 3 performing posts from the past week by engagement rate
2. Apply the pillar-to-micro framework:

```
REPURPOSING CHAIN
=================
Long-form content (blog, podcast, video)
├── Extract 3-5 key points → LinkedIn text posts
├── Create quote graphics → Instagram carousel
├── Cut 15-60 second clips → TikTok/Reels/Shorts
├── Pull stats or data → X/Twitter thread
└── Compile highlights → Facebook summary post

Top Instagram carousel
├── Convert to LinkedIn document/PDF post
├── Animate slides → short video for TikTok
└── Extract single best slide → X/Twitter image

Top-performing tweet/post
├── Expand into LinkedIn long-form post
├── Create visual version → Instagram graphic
└── Record video commentary → TikTok/Reels
```

3. For video repurposing:
   - Use CapCut or Descript to extract clips from long-form video
   - Add captions (85% of social video is watched without sound)
   - Adjust aspect ratio: 16:9 for YouTube, 9:16 for Reels/TikTok/Shorts, 1:1 for feed posts
4. Maintain a repurposing log to avoid recycling the same content too frequently (minimum 4-week gap for recycled content)

**Verify**: At least 3 pieces of repurposed content generated per week from existing assets. Each repurposed piece is adapted for the target platform (not cross-posted identically).
**If failed**: If repurposing takes too long, focus on the single highest-performing post and create just 2 platform-adapted versions.

### Step 7: Analytics Setup and Weekly Review

**Duration**: 30 minutes setup, 30 minutes per weekly review
**Tool**: Scheduling tool analytics + spreadsheet

Configure analytics tracking and establish a weekly review cadence. [src6]

1. Set up a weekly metrics tracking spreadsheet:

```
WEEKLY SOCIAL MEDIA METRICS TRACKER
====================================
                  Week 1  Week 2  Week 3  Week 4  Trend
LinkedIn:
  Posts published   __      __      __      __     __
  Impressions       __      __      __      __     __
  Engagement rate   __      __      __      __     __
  Follower growth   __      __      __      __     __

Instagram:
  Posts published   __      __      __      __     __
  Reach             __      __      __      __     __
  Engagement rate   __      __      __      __     __
  Follower growth   __      __      __      __     __

[Repeat for each platform]

Top 3 posts this week:
1. Platform: __ | Pillar: __ | Engagement: __ | Why it worked: __
2. Platform: __ | Pillar: __ | Engagement: __ | Why it worked: __
3. Platform: __ | Pillar: __ | Engagement: __ | Why it worked: __

Bottom 3 posts this week:
1. Platform: __ | Pillar: __ | Engagement: __ | Why it failed: __
```

2. Schedule a weekly 30-minute analytics review:
   - Pull metrics from scheduling tool native analytics
   - Record in tracking spreadsheet
   - Identify top 3 and bottom 3 posts
   - Adjust next week's content mix based on what performed
3. Monthly: review pillar performance — if a pillar consistently underperforms (below average engagement 3 weeks running), replace or modify it

**Verify**: Analytics dashboard configured and first week's baseline metrics recorded. Weekly review calendar event set.
**If failed**: If scheduling tool analytics are insufficient, use native platform analytics (Instagram Insights, LinkedIn Analytics, etc.) and manually compile in spreadsheet.

## Quality Benchmarks

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Auto-publish fails — post sent as notification | Account type is Personal, not Business/Creator | Convert to Business/Creator account, re-authenticate in scheduling tool |
| Image appears cropped incorrectly | Wrong dimensions for platform | Re-export from Canva at exact platform specs (e.g., 1080x1350 for Instagram portrait) |
| Engagement rate drops below 1% | Content not resonating, posting at wrong times, algorithm change | Audit top-performing competitor content, test new posting times, increase video/carousel ratio |
| Scheduling tool API disconnects | OAuth token expired or platform API changes | Re-authenticate account in scheduling tool settings; check tool status page |
| Hashtags shadowbanned on Instagram | Using banned or flagged hashtags | Audit all hashtags against Instagram's banned list; replace with niche-specific tags |
| Content calendar gaps — missed posting days | Batch creation not completed on schedule | Set a recurring calendar block for weekly batch creation; reduce posting frequency to sustainable level |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Cross-posting identical content to all platforms
Posting the same caption, image, and hashtags to LinkedIn, Instagram, X, and TikTok. Each platform has different audience expectations, format requirements, and algorithm preferences. Cross-posted content consistently underperforms native content by 30-50% in engagement. [src3]

### Correct: Platform-native adaptation
Create one core message, then adapt format, tone, length, and visuals for each platform. A LinkedIn post should read differently from an Instagram caption, even if the core insight is the same. Use the repurposing workflow in Step 6.

### Wrong: Scheduling 4+ weeks of content and walking away
Queuing a full month of content removes the ability to respond to trends, news, and audience feedback. Content feels stale and disconnected from current conversations. [src2]

### Correct: Rolling 1-2 week scheduling with weekly batch creation
Schedule evergreen content up to 2 weeks out. Schedule trend-sensitive content no more than 1 week ahead. Reserve 20% of weekly slots for reactive/timely content.

### Wrong: Ignoring engagement after posting
Publishing content without responding to comments and DMs signals to algorithms that the post is not generating meaningful interaction. Algorithms deprioritize content from accounts with low reply rates. [src4]

### Correct: Active engagement within the first hour
Respond to every comment within the first 60 minutes of posting. This signals high engagement to algorithms and extends organic reach. Use engagement templates from Step 5 for speed.
