# Meeting Culture Redesign Decision Framework

## Summary

This framework helps organizations diagnose meeting overload and select the right intervention level — from incremental meeting hygiene improvements to a full async-first transformation. Employees now average 11.3 hours per week in meetings, with 51% reporting overtime specifically because meetings prevent task completion during work hours [src1]. The default recommendation is to start with a meeting audit and no-meeting days (lowest risk, measurable within 2 weeks) before escalating to async-first protocols, which yield 19-31% productivity gains but require 3-6 months of cultural change [src3].

## Constraints
<!-- Agents: read before walking through this decision with a user. -->

- Meeting load must be measured by role before prescribing solutions — executives (17-23 hrs/week), managers (11 hrs), and ICs (6-8 hrs) have fundamentally different meeting profiles [src2]
- Async-first transitions fail without documentation culture — teams must have a shared wiki or knowledge base before reducing synchronous touchpoints [src5]
- Calendar fragmentation (many short gaps between meetings) causes more productivity loss than total meeting hours — measure both [src6]
- No-meeting days show measurable results within 2 weeks; full async-first transitions require 3-6 months of sustained effort [src4]
- Cultural inertia is the primary blocker — 67% of executives deem their own meetings failures yet continue scheduling them [src2]

| Input | Why It Matters | How to Assess |

| Leadership buy-in level | Executive modeling is the #1 predictor of success; without it, limit to team-level experiments | Ask: will executives cancel their own recurring meetings? |

## Decision Logic

<!-- If/then rules for agents to evaluate programmatically. -->

### If meeting load < 8 hrs/week AND deep work blocks exist
→ **Meeting Hygiene only**. The meeting volume is healthy. Focus on quality improvements: agendas required, 25/50-minute defaults, clear decision documentation. [src6]

### If meeting load 8-15 hrs/week AND team is hybrid/distributed
→ **Async-Lite Protocol**. Replace all status-update meetings with async standups (Loom, Slack, written updates). Keep decision meetings synchronous but shorter. Expected savings: 3-5 hrs/week per person. [src7]

### If meeting load 8-15 hrs/week AND team is co-located
→ **No-Meeting Days + shorter defaults**. Implement 1-2 meeting-free days per week. Change default meeting length from 60 to 25 minutes. Asana's implementation of this halved average meeting lengths. [src4]

### If meeting load > 15 hrs/week AND leadership supports change
→ **Full Async-First Transformation**. GitLab's model: document everything, default to async, meetings require justification. Employees resolve 85% of questions without scheduling meetings, saving 15 hours/month per person. [src5]

### Default recommendation
→ **No-Meeting Days (1-2 per week)**. When inputs are ambiguous, this is the lowest-risk intervention with the fastest measurable impact. Can be implemented in one week, measured within two, and reversed instantly. [src2]

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Declaring "async-first" without building documentation infrastructure
Organizations announce async-first policies but lack a searchable knowledge base. Within weeks, employees revert to meetings because there's no reliable way to find written decisions. GitLab invested 18 months building its handbook before going fully async. [src5]

### Correct: Build the documentation habit first
Spend 4-8 weeks requiring written meeting notes and decision logs in a shared wiki. Once teams consistently document decisions, async communication has a foundation to stand on. The infrastructure must precede the policy.

### Wrong: Applying blanket meeting reduction targets across all roles
Mandating "reduce meetings by 50%" hurts managers (who need coordination meetings) while being insufficient for executives (who may need 70% reduction). One-size-fits-all targets create resentment and gaming. [src2]

### Correct: Set role-specific meeting budgets
ICs: max 6-8 hrs/week. Managers: max 12-15 hrs/week. Executives: max 15-18 hrs/week. These budgets account for the different coordination needs of each level while still protecting deep work time.

### Wrong: Canceling meetings without replacing the communication channel
Eliminating standup meetings without providing an async alternative (written updates, Slack check-ins) creates information vacuums that lead to more ad-hoc interruptions — often worse than the original meetings. [src7]

### Correct: Replace before you remove
For every meeting canceled, designate the async replacement: daily standup → written async update, weekly status → shared dashboard, brainstorming → async idea board with 48-hour collection period. Test the replacement for 2 weeks before confirming the cancellation.
