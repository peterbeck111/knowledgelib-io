# Commerce Platform Selection by Revenue Tier

## Definition

Commerce platform selection by revenue tier is a structured framework for matching ecommerce platforms — Shopify Plus, BigCommerce Enterprise, commercetools, and Salesforce Commerce Cloud — to an organization's annual revenue, technical capability, and commerce complexity. The framework segments platforms into three architectural categories: monolithic SaaS (Shopify Plus, BigCommerce), composable/MACH (commercetools), and enterprise suite (Salesforce Commerce Cloud), each optimizing for different trade-offs between time-to-market, total cost of ownership, and customization depth. Revenue tier is the primary segmentation axis because it correlates with transaction volume, integration complexity, and the engineering resources available to operate the platform. [src2]

## Key Properties

- **Architecture spectrum**: Monolithic SaaS (Shopify Plus, BigCommerce) offers faster deployment with constrained customization; composable MACH (commercetools) offers maximum flexibility with higher implementation cost; enterprise suite (SFCC) offers deep CRM integration with the highest TCO [src1]
- **Revenue tier mapping**: Under $10M suits Shopify/BigCommerce standard; $10M-$50M suits Shopify Plus or BigCommerce Enterprise; $50M-$500M suits commercetools or SFCC; $500M+ typically requires composable or enterprise suite with multi-region, multi-brand support [src6]
- **TCO variance by tier**: Shopify Plus starts at ~$2,300/month with predictable scaling; BigCommerce Enterprise uses custom pricing with zero transaction fees; commercetools starts at ~$40K/year on usage-based pricing; SFCC charges 1-3% of GMV with custom enterprise licensing [src4] [src5]
- **Implementation timeline**: Shopify Plus deploys in 4-12 weeks; BigCommerce Enterprise in 6-16 weeks; commercetools in 3-9 months; SFCC in 6-18 months [src6]
- **B2B capability maturity**: BigCommerce leads in native B2B features (price lists, quote management); Shopify Plus added B2B suite in 2024; commercetools supports B2B via API composition; SFCC offers B2B through Commerce Cloud B2B edition [src2]

## Constraints

     These are hard boundaries on when and how it applies. -->

- Composable platforms (commercetools) require minimum 2-3 full-time developers for ongoing maintenance — organizations without this capacity face runaway agency costs [src1]
- Transaction fee structures differ fundamentally: Shopify charges 0.15-0.5% on non-Shopify Payments transactions; BigCommerce charges zero additional transaction fees; commercetools charges per API call; SFCC charges percentage of GMV [src4]
- Platform comparisons published by vendors (Shopify, commercetools, Salesforce) consistently favor the publishing vendor — weight independent analyst data more heavily [src3]
- Migration cost between platforms ranges from $50K-$500K+ depending on catalog size, integration count, and data complexity — this makes platform selection a 3-5 year commitment [src6]
- Headless/composable architecture benefits only materialize when the organization has complex multi-channel requirements — for single-storefront B2C, monolithic SaaS delivers equivalent results at lower cost [src2]

## Application Checklist

### Step 1: Classify revenue tier and growth trajectory
- **Inputs needed**: Current annual online revenue, 3-year growth projection, number of SKUs, geographic markets, sales channels (web, mobile, POS, marketplace)
- **Output**: Revenue tier classification and complexity profile
- **Constraint**: Use projected revenue at 3 years, not current — replatforming costs $50K-$500K, so select for where the business will be, not where it is today [src6]

### Step 2: Assess technical capability and integration requirements
- **Inputs needed**: In-house developer count, current tech stack, required third-party integrations (ERP, OMS, PIM, CRM), headless/multi-storefront requirements
- **Output**: Technical fit matrix — which platforms the team can realistically operate
- **Constraint**: If the team cannot maintain API integrations independently, eliminate composable platforms (commercetools) regardless of revenue tier — agency dependency at composable scale costs $200K-$500K/year [src1]

### Step 3: Calculate 3-year TCO for shortlisted platforms
- **Inputs needed**: Expected GMV, transaction volumes, integration costs, theme/frontend development costs, hosting (if applicable), support tier pricing
- **Output**: Side-by-side 3-year TCO comparison including license, implementation, maintenance, and opportunity cost
- **Constraint**: Include transaction fees in TCO — Shopify's 0.15-0.5% on non-Shopify Payments transactions can exceed $100K/year at $50M+ GMV [src4]

### Step 4: Validate against commerce model requirements
- **Inputs needed**: B2B vs B2C vs hybrid needs, multi-currency and multi-language requirements, marketplace or multi-vendor needs, AI/personalization requirements
- **Output**: Platform shortlist ranked by commerce model fit
- **Constraint**: Do not select based on current features alone — evaluate platform roadmap and ecosystem maturity. A platform strong today but with declining market share creates future migration risk [src3]

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Selecting commercetools because it is "the most modern" architecture
Organizations adopt composable commerce for architectural purity without the engineering team to support it. They end up spending 3-5x more on agency fees than they would on a monolithic platform, with slower time-to-market. [src1]

### Correct: Match architecture to organizational capability
Select composable only when you have 3+ dedicated commerce engineers, complex multi-channel requirements, and a 3-year roadmap that justifies the higher upfront investment. For most brands under $50M revenue, Shopify Plus or BigCommerce Enterprise delivers equivalent business outcomes. [src2]

### Wrong: Choosing Shopify Plus because of low entry price without modeling scale costs
A brand at $2M revenue selects Shopify Plus for its $2,300/month price point. At $50M revenue, the combined platform fees, app subscriptions, and Shopify Payments lock-in create a TCO that exceeds alternatives. [src4]

### Correct: Model TCO at projected 3-year revenue
Calculate total costs at current revenue and at projected 3-year revenue. Include transaction fees, app subscriptions, custom development, and payment processing. Compare against BigCommerce (zero transaction fees) and commercetools (usage-based pricing) at the same revenue level. [src4]

### Wrong: Selecting SFCC solely because the company already uses Salesforce CRM
Organizations assume that CRM integration justifies the highest-TCO platform. In practice, Salesforce CRM integrates with Shopify Plus and commercetools via standard APIs — the integration advantage of SFCC is incremental, not transformational. [src4]

### Correct: Quantify the actual integration value
Evaluate whether native Salesforce integration saves more than the TCO premium (typically 2-4x higher than Shopify Plus). For most organizations, the integration savings do not offset the cost difference. SFCC is justified when the business requires deep Marketing Cloud, Service Cloud, and Commerce Cloud orchestration across 50+ markets. [src6]

## Common Misconceptions

- **Misconception**: Headless commerce is always better than monolithic SaaS.
  **Reality**: Headless (commercetools) adds $100K-$300K in frontend development costs and requires ongoing engineering. For single-storefront B2C businesses, monolithic SaaS (Shopify Plus, BigCommerce) delivers equivalent conversion rates at 30-50% lower TCO. Headless only justifies its cost when serving 3+ distinct frontends or channels. [src1]

- **Misconception**: BigCommerce and Shopify Plus are interchangeable for mid-market brands.
  **Reality**: BigCommerce charges zero additional transaction fees and offers stronger native B2B features. Shopify Plus has a larger app ecosystem and faster deployment. The choice depends on B2B needs (BigCommerce wins) vs ecosystem breadth and simplicity (Shopify Plus wins). [src2]

- **Misconception**: Salesforce Commerce Cloud is the default for enterprise brands over $100M.
  **Reality**: commercetools has surpassed SFCC in Gartner Peer Insights satisfaction scores for complex enterprise deployments. SFCC's primary advantage is native Salesforce ecosystem integration — without that requirement, composable alternatives often deliver lower TCO and faster feature velocity. [src3]
