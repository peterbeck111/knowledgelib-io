# Expansion Revenue Playbook

## Purpose

This recipe builds a segment-specific expansion revenue engine — defining activation thresholds, configuring upsell triggers, implementing cross-sell methodology, and establishing CSQL pipelines — that drives measurable NRR improvement. The output is a working expansion system with per-account readiness scores, automated trigger alerts, segment playbooks (SMB/Mid-Market/Enterprise), and an NRR tracking dashboard. [src1]

## Prerequisites
<!-- Agents: verify ALL prerequisites before executing. Missing prerequisites = failed execution. -->

- [ ] **Customer health scores** operational in CS platform or spreadsheet — minimum 3 months of scoring history
- [ ] **Product usage data** accessible via analytics platform API or export — feature adoption, login frequency, usage vs. plan limits
- [ ] **Current NRR calculated** by segment — baseline metric required before optimization
- [ ] **Customer segmentation** defined in CRM — SMB/Mid-Market/Enterprise tiers with ACV boundaries
- [ ] **Billing system access** — ability to query contract dates, current plan, add-on history
- [ ] **CS platform or spreadsheet** — tool for tracking CSQLs and expansion pipeline

## Constraints
<!-- Hard rules. Agents: enforce throughout execution. Violating these = broken output or legal risk. -->

- Activation-first rule: never initiate expansion motions on accounts that have not reached defined activation milestones. Premature upselling degrades trust and accelerates churn. [src6]
- Expansion CAC must remain below $0.28 per $1 of expansion ARR to preserve the 4x cost advantage over new logo acquisition. [src1]
- Health score must be green (75+/100) before any expansion conversation. Expanding unhealthy accounts creates negative NRR drag. [src3]
- Segment NRR targets differ materially: Enterprise 118%+, Mid-Market 108%+, SMB 97%+. One-size-fits-all expansion plays underperform. [src4]
- QBR cadence must precede expansion motions. Accounts without regular business reviews show 36% fewer expansion opportunities.

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Define Expansion Readiness Criteria

**Duration**: 1-2 days
**Tool**: CS platform or spreadsheet

Define the activation milestones that must be met before any account is eligible for expansion. These form the "expansion readiness gate" — no account passes through without meeting all criteria.

**Activation milestones (define per segment):**

| Criterion | SMB | Mid-Market | Enterprise |
|-----------|-----|-----------|-----------|
| Time since onboarding | > 30 days | > 45 days | > 60 days |
| Core feature adoption | 3+ key features used | 5+ key features used | 7+ key features, 2+ departments |
| Health score | >= 75 | >= 80 | >= 85 |
| Active users vs. seats | > 60% | > 70% | > 75% |
| Support ticket trend | Declining or stable | Declining | Declining |
| NPS/CSAT | >= 7 NPS | >= 8 NPS | >= 8 NPS, executive sponsor identified |

**Verify**: Review 20 recently-expanded accounts against these criteria. At least 80% should have met them pre-expansion. If not, adjust thresholds.
**If failed**: Criteria are too strict (< 15% of base qualifies) or too loose (> 60% qualifies). Recalibrate using historical expansion success data.

### Step 2: Identify and Configure Upsell Triggers

**Duration**: 2-3 days
**Tool**: Product analytics + CS platform

Build a trigger library — specific, measurable events that signal expansion readiness. Each trigger should have an automated alert and a defined response action. [src2]

**Trigger library:**

| Trigger | Signal | Detection Method | Response |
|---------|--------|-----------------|----------|
| Usage ceiling | > 80% of plan limit consumed | Usage API threshold alert | CSM outreach: usage review + next tier preview |
| Seat saturation | > 90% of seats in active use | Weekly seat utilization report | CSM outreach: team growth discussion |
| Feature exploration | User accesses 3+ gated/premium features in 14 days | Product analytics event tracking | In-app prompt + CSM notification |
| Headcount growth | Company adds 20%+ employees (external signal) | LinkedIn/funding news monitoring | CSM outreach: growth alignment call |
| Power user emergence | New user exceeds 2x average engagement in first 30 days | Cohort analysis in analytics | CSM notified: department expansion opportunity |
| Contract milestone | 60 days before renewal, high health score | Calendar trigger in CS platform | CSM initiates strategic review with expansion options |
| QBR outcome | Customer articulates unmet needs matching your product roadmap | QBR notes tagged in CS platform | Solution architect engaged, custom proposal within 5 business days |

Configure these as automated workflows in your CS platform. Each trigger should create a CSQL (Customer Success Qualified Lead) with context attached.

**Verify**: Run triggers against 90 days of historical data. Each trigger should fire on 5-15% of eligible accounts per quarter. Triggers firing on < 2% are too narrow; > 25% are too broad.
**If failed**: Adjust threshold values. If product analytics lacks the required events, instrument tracking first (add this as a prerequisite).

### Step 3: Build Cross-Sell Methodology

**Duration**: 2-3 days
**Tool**: CRM + CS platform

Cross-sell differs from upsell: it introduces a different product or module, not more of the same. Build a whitespace analysis framework. [src5]

**Whitespace analysis process:**

1. **Product adjacency map**: Document which products/modules complement each other and which customer use cases they address.
2. **Account coverage matrix**: For each account, map which products they own versus which they could use based on their industry, size, and use case.
3. **Trigger mapping**: Define which behaviors in Product A signal readiness for Product B.

**Cross-sell scoring model:**

| Factor | Weight | Scoring |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Product fit (use case match) | 30% | 1-5 scale based on industry/role alignment |
| Adoption depth in current product | 25% | Usage percentile within segment |
| Expressed interest (feature requests, QBR mentions) | 20% | Count of relevant requests in last 6 months |
| Budget timing (fiscal year, expansion budget) | 15% | Known budget cycle alignment |
| Champion availability | 10% | Active executive sponsor in place |

**Cross-sell playbook per product pair:**
- Define the "bridge narrative" — how Product A success leads naturally to Product B value
- Create a 3-touch sequence: educational content, peer case study, tailored demo
- Set a conversion timeline: 30-60 days from first touch to close for Mid-Market, 60-120 days for Enterprise

**Verify**: Whitespace analysis identifies cross-sell opportunities in at least 30% of accounts above health score threshold.
**If failed**: Product adjacency map may be too narrow. Review customer support requests and QBR notes for unmet needs that map to existing products.

### Step 4: Create Segment-Specific Expansion Playbooks

**Duration**: 2-3 days
**Tool**: Document + CS platform configuration

Build three distinct playbooks based on segment economics and buyer behavior. [src4]

**SMB Playbook (target NRR: 97-105%):**
- Motion: product-led, low-touch, self-serve upgrade paths
- Triggers: usage ceiling (primary), seat saturation (secondary)
- Channel: in-app prompts, automated email sequences, pricing page nudges
- Cycle: 7-14 days from trigger to close
- Ownership: pooled CS team or automated
- Key metric: self-serve upgrade rate > 5% of triggered accounts

**Mid-Market Playbook (target NRR: 108-115%):**
- Motion: CS-led with sales support for larger deals
- Triggers: QBR outcomes (primary), feature exploration (secondary), headcount growth
- Channel: CSM-led conversations, tailored demos, business case documents
- Cycle: 30-60 days from CSQL to close
- Ownership: named CSM, sales overlay for deals > $10K expansion ACV
- Key metric: CSQL-to-close rate > 25%

**Enterprise Playbook (target NRR: 118-130%):**
- Motion: strategic, multi-threaded, executive-sponsored
- Triggers: contract milestone (primary), power user emergence across departments, strategic initiative alignment
- Channel: executive business reviews, solution architecture workshops, pilot programs
- Cycle: 60-120 days from CSQL to close
- Ownership: strategic CSM + account executive + solution architect
- Key metric: average expansion deal size > 20% of current ACV

**Verify**: Each playbook has defined triggers, channels, timelines, owners, and success metrics. Review with CS leadership for feasibility.
**If failed**: Playbook may be too complex for current team size. Start with the segment that represents the largest expansion opportunity and build one playbook first.

### Step 5: Implement Tracking and CSQL Pipeline

**Duration**: 3-5 days
**Tool**: CS platform + CRM

Build the operational infrastructure to track expansion from trigger through close. [src3]

**CSQL pipeline stages:**

| Stage | Definition | Owner | SLA |
|-------|-----------|-------|-----|
| Trigger Fired | Automated trigger detected signal | System | Immediate |
| CSQL Created | CSM validates opportunity, adds context | CSM | 48 hours from trigger |
| Qualified | CSM confirms timing, budget, and champion | CSM | 5 business days from creation |
| Proposal Sent | Expansion offer presented to customer | CSM + Sales | 10 business days from qualification |
| Negotiation | Terms under discussion | Sales + CSM | 15 business days max |
| Closed-Won/Lost | Outcome recorded with reason codes | Sales | — |

**Required tracking fields:**
- Trigger source (which trigger fired)
- Expansion type (upsell/cross-sell/seat expansion)
- Segment
- Current ACV and expansion ACV
- Days in each stage
- Win/loss reason

Configure weekly pipeline reviews and monthly NRR reporting by segment.

**Verify**: Run a 2-week pilot with 20 accounts. CSQLs should move from Trigger Fired to Qualified within 7 business days. If pipeline stalls, check CSM capacity and trigger quality.
**If failed**: CSMs may be overwhelmed. Reduce trigger sensitivity or increase the health score threshold to focus on highest-probability accounts first.

### Step 6: Measure NRR Impact and Iterate

**Duration**: Ongoing (first measurement at 90 days)
**Tool**: BI dashboard + CS platform

Build an NRR impact dashboard and establish a continuous improvement loop.

**Dashboard components:**
- NRR by segment (monthly trend, 3-month rolling average)
- Expansion ARR (new upsell + cross-sell + seat expansion, by segment)
- CSQL conversion funnel (trigger > created > qualified > proposal > closed)
- Trigger effectiveness (conversion rate by trigger type)
- Expansion CAC (total CS + sales cost / expansion ARR)
- Time-to-expansion (median days from activation to first expansion)

**Iteration cadence:**
- Weekly: pipeline review, stuck-deal triage
- Monthly: trigger performance review, threshold adjustment
- Quarterly: full playbook review, segment target recalibration, trigger library expansion

**Verify**: After 90 days, NRR should show improvement trend. If NRR is flat, analyze which pipeline stage has the highest drop-off and address that stage specifically.
**If failed**: If NRR declines, immediately audit whether expansion motions are cannibalizing retention (check if expanded accounts churn at higher rates than non-expanded accounts).

## Quality Benchmarks

| Quality Metric | Minimum Acceptable | Good | Excellent |
|---------------|-------------------|------|-----------|
| NRR improvement (90-day) | +2 percentage points | +5 pp | +10 pp |
| CSQL-to-close rate | > 15% | > 25% | > 35% |
| Trigger accuracy (leads to real opportunity) | > 30% | > 50% | > 70% |
| Expansion CAC ratio | < $0.35 per $1 ARR | < $0.27 per $1 ARR | < $0.20 per $1 ARR |
| Time from trigger to proposal | < 15 business days | < 10 business days | < 7 business days |
| Self-serve upgrade rate (SMB) | > 3% of triggered | > 5% | > 8% |

**If below minimum**: Review trigger thresholds (likely too broad), check CSM capacity (overwhelmed teams drop CSQLs), or verify activation criteria (expanding unready accounts tanks conversion rates).

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| NRR declining despite expansion activity | Expanding unhealthy accounts — expansion churn exceeds expansion revenue | Immediately tighten health score threshold to 85+; audit last 20 expanded accounts for churn signals |
| Triggers firing on > 40% of base | Thresholds too permissive | Raise usage ceiling trigger from 80% to 90%; add health score gate to all triggers |
| Triggers firing on < 3% of base | Thresholds too restrictive or data gaps | Lower thresholds by 20%; verify product analytics event tracking is complete |
| CSQLs stalling at Qualified stage | CSMs lack expansion selling skills or time | Deploy expansion talk-track training; consider sales overlay for deals > $5K |
| Cross-sell conversion below 10% | Poor product-fit assessment or weak bridge narrative | Revisit whitespace analysis; interview 5 recent cross-sell losses for root cause |
| Self-serve upgrades near zero (SMB) | Upgrade UX is buried or pricing page unclear | A/B test in-app upgrade prompts; simplify pricing page; add usage-based nudges |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Expanding Before Activation
Pushing upsells on accounts that haven't reached core feature adoption or time-since-onboarding thresholds. Result: customer feels pressured, trust erodes, churn spikes within 2 quarters. Upsell churn is invisible in month 1 but devastating by month 6. [src1]

### Correct: Gate All Expansion Behind Activation
Enforce the readiness scorecard. No CSQL is created until the account passes all activation milestones for its segment. This may reduce expansion pipeline volume by 30-40% initially but improves close rates by 2-3x and eliminates expansion-driven churn.

### Wrong: One Playbook for All Segments
Running the same CSM-led expansion motion for SMB and Enterprise. SMB accounts cannot support high-touch QBRs, and Enterprise accounts will not respond to automated upgrade prompts. [src4]

### Correct: Segment-Specific Motions
SMB gets product-led self-serve paths. Mid-Market gets CSM-led with sales overlay. Enterprise gets strategic multi-threaded engagement. Measure NRR per segment independently.

### Wrong: Treating Cross-Sell Like Upsell
Approaching cross-sell as "buy more of what you have." Cross-sell requires a distinct bridge narrative showing how success in Product A creates a need that Product B addresses. Without this, cross-sell conversion stays below 10%. [src5]

### Correct: Build the Bridge Narrative
Map each cross-sell pair with a story: "Because you've achieved X with Product A, you're now positioned to unlock Y with Product B." Support with peer case studies from the same segment and industry.
