# Founder-Market Fit Assessment

## Purpose

This recipe produces a Founder-Market Fit Scorecard that evaluates the alignment between a specific founder (or founding team) and their target market across five dimensions: domain expertise, network strength, proprietary insight, unfair advantages, and obsession/commitment. The output includes a scored assessment, an inventory of durable advantages, a gap mitigation plan, and a 2-3 paragraph investor-ready narrative explaining why this founder is uniquely positioned to win in this market.

## Prerequisites

- [ ] **Target market identified** — specific industry, customer segment, and problem area defined (not just "SaaS" or "healthcare")
- [ ] **Founder readiness confirmed** — completed the Founder Readiness Self-Assessment — [Readiness Assessment](/business/startup-readiness/founder-readiness-self-assessment/2026)
- [ ] **Career history documented** — detailed resume or experience inventory including roles, projects, and outcomes
- [ ] **Network inventory started** — list of professional contacts in the target industry
- [ ] **5-10 potential customers identified** — people in the target market you can have conversations with

## Constraints

- Founder-market fit is an innate, unfair advantage that sets a founder apart from competitors. It cannot be manufactured, but it can be recognized and articulated. [src1]
- In 2026, investors dig deeper into lived experience and proprietary workflows. AI has commoditized code-writing, so domain expertise is the differentiated asset. [src5]
- Proprietary data, domain-specific workflows, and deep understanding of user behavior form the strongest competitive advantages. [src3]
- Founder-market fit attracts better hires, opens doors to early customers and distribution partners, and signals credibility to strategic investors. [src1]
- Assessment must be honest — claiming founder-market fit you do not have will be exposed during due diligence and customer conversations. [src4]

## Tool Selection Decision

```
Which path?
├── Founder has 5+ years in target domain
│   └── PATH A: Strong Fit — document and articulate existing advantages
├── Founder has 1-5 years or adjacent domain experience
│   └── PATH B: Developing Fit — identify transferable advantages + gap plan
├── Founder has <1 year but has proprietary insight
│   └── PATH C: Insight-Led — validate proprietary insight as primary advantage
└── Founder has no domain experience
    └── PATH D: No Fit — either pivot market or find domain expert co-founder
```

| Path | Focus | Likely Outcome | Investor Perception |
|------|-------|----------------|-------------------|
| A: Strong Fit | Articulation | Strong narrative, easy to fund | "This is the right person" |
| B: Developing Fit | Gap mitigation | Viable if gaps are addressed | "Promising, needs proof" |
| C: Insight-Led | Validation | High variance — validate fast | "Interesting if insight holds" |
| D: No Fit | Pivot or partner | Must add domain co-founder | "Why this market?" |

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Domain Expertise Assessment

**Duration**: 30-45 minutes
**Tool**: Self-assessment + evidence inventory

Evaluate your depth of knowledge and experience in the target market. [src2]

```
Domain Expertise Assessment:
═══════════════════════════════════════════
EXPERIENCE DEPTH
Years working in/with this industry:        ___
Number of roles directly in this space:     ___
Projects completed in this domain:          ___

KNOWLEDGE TEST (answer honestly):
Can you name the top 5 competitors?                     [ ] yes  [ ] no
Can you explain the typical customer buying process?     [ ] yes  [ ] no
Do you know the industry's regulatory landscape?         [ ] yes  [ ] no
Can you identify the top 3 unresolved pain points?       [ ] yes  [ ] no
Do you understand the unit economics of the industry?    [ ] yes  [ ] no

EVIDENCE INVENTORY:
- Published content (articles, talks, patents):  ___
- Industry certifications or credentials:        ___
- Previous products built for this market:       ___
- Advisory/consulting work in this domain:       ___

Score:
  10+ years + deep evidence               → 5/5
  5-10 years + moderate evidence           → 4/5
  2-5 years + some evidence                → 3/5
  1-2 years or adjacent domain             → 2/5
  <1 year, no direct experience            → 1/5
```

**Verify**: Can you pass a "30-minute investor grilling" on market dynamics without preparation? If not, your domain expertise may be thinner than you think.
**If failed**: If score is 1-2, you need a domain expert co-founder or 6-12 months of immersive market research before founding in this space.

### Step 2: Network Strength Mapping

**Duration**: 45-60 minutes
**Tool**: Spreadsheet or personal CRM

Your network in the target market determines distribution speed and first-customer acquisition. [src1]

```
Network Mapping Exercise:
═══════════════════════════════════════════
POTENTIAL CUSTOMERS (in target segment):
Can reach via warm intro:        ___ people
Can reach via 1-hop intro:       ___ people
Have existing relationship:      ___ people

INDUSTRY INSIDERS:
Domain experts you can call:     ___ people
Potential advisors:              ___ people
Potential early employees:       ___ people

DISTRIBUTION PARTNERS:
Channel partners reachable:      ___ organizations
Media/influencer contacts:       ___ people
Community leaders/organizers:    ___ people

INVESTORS:
Relevant VCs you know:           ___ people
Angels in this space:            ___ people

NETWORK QUALITY TEST:
Could you get 10 customer intros this week?     [ ] yes [ ] no
Could you recruit a domain CTO within 60 days?  [ ] yes [ ] no
Could you get a warm VC intro within 30 days?   [ ] yes [ ] no

Score:
  Strong across all categories (10+ each) → 5/5
  Strong customers + insiders (10+ each)  → 4/5
  Moderate (5-10 across categories)       → 3/5
  Weak (1-5 total warm connections)       → 2/5
  No connections in target market         → 1/5
```

**Verify**: Test your network by actually requesting 3 introductions this week. If your contacts do not respond or follow through, your network is weaker than listed.
**If failed**: A weak network can be built over 3-6 months through industry events, online communities, and deliberate relationship building. But it delays your ability to launch.

### Step 3: Proprietary Insight Identification

**Duration**: 30-45 minutes
**Tool**: Structured reflection + customer validation

A proprietary insight is something you know about the market that most people — including competitors — do not know or do not believe. [src3]

```
Proprietary Insight Framework:
═══════════════════════════════════════════
SOURCES OF INSIGHT:
1. Personal experience as a customer/user:
   Insight: ________________________________
   Evidence: _______________________________

2. Professional experience in the industry:
   Insight: ________________________________
   Evidence: _______________________________

3. Data you have access to:
   Insight: ________________________________
   Evidence: _______________________________

4. Cross-industry pattern recognition:
   Insight: ________________________________
   Evidence: _______________________________

5. Contrarian belief about this market:
   Insight: ________________________________
   Evidence: _______________________________

VALIDATION CHECKLIST:
For each insight, answer:
- Can I explain this in one sentence?           [ ] yes [ ] no
- Would a typical industry executive disagree?  [ ] yes [ ] no
- Can I point to data or experiences as proof?   [ ] yes [ ] no
- Is this insight actionable (builds a product)? [ ] yes [ ] no
- Have 3+ potential customers confirmed this?    [ ] yes [ ] no

Score:
  2+ validated proprietary insights      → 5/5
  1 validated + 1-2 unvalidated          → 4/5
  1 unvalidated but strongly evidenced   → 3/5
  Vague intuitions only                  → 2/5
  No differentiated insight              → 1/5
```

**Verify**: Present your top insight to 5 people in the industry. If all 5 agree immediately, it is not proprietary — it is obvious. If 3/5 push back but 2/5 say "that is exactly right," you may have a genuine insight.
**If failed**: Without a proprietary insight, you are entering a market with no informational advantage. This is survivable but makes fundraising and differentiation significantly harder.

### Step 4: Unfair Advantage Inventory

**Duration**: 20-30 minutes
**Tool**: Structured inventory

Unfair advantages are durable assets that cannot be easily copied. Catalog every advantage and assess durability. [src6]

```
Unfair Advantage Inventory:
═══════════════════════════════════════════
Category              Advantage Description    Durability
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Proprietary data:     ____________________     ___ years
Technical expertise:  ____________________     ___ years
Regulatory knowledge: ____________________     ___ years
Customer access:      ____________________     ___ years
Brand/reputation:     ____________________     ___ years
IP/patents:           ____________________     ___ years
First-mover position: ____________________     ___ years
Team composition:     ____________________     ___ years
Capital efficiency:   ____________________     ___ years
Partnership/access:   ____________________     ___ years

DURABILITY TEST:
- Could a well-funded competitor replicate this in <12 months?
  If yes → not a durable advantage
- Does this advantage compound over time?
  If yes → strong moat potential
- Is this advantage tied to you personally or to the company?
  If personal → risk if you leave, but strong for fundraising

Score:
  3+ durable (2yr+) advantages         → 5/5
  2 durable advantages                 → 4/5
  1 durable + 1-2 temporary            → 3/5
  Only temporary advantages            → 2/5
  No identifiable advantages           → 1/5
```

**Verify**: For each claimed advantage, ask: "Would a competitor with $10M agree this is hard to replicate?" If not, it is not truly unfair.
**If failed**: If no durable advantages exist, you are competing on execution speed alone. This is possible but significantly harder and less fundable.

### Step 5: Compile Scorecard and Investor Narrative

**Duration**: 30-45 minutes

```
FOUNDER-MARKET FIT SCORECARD
═══════════════════════════════════════════
Dimension                   Score   Weight   Weighted
Domain Expertise:           ___/5   × 0.30 = ______
Network Strength:           ___/5   × 0.20 = ______
Proprietary Insight:        ___/5   × 0.25 = ______
Unfair Advantages:          ___/5   × 0.15 = ______
Obsession/Commitment:       ___/5   × 0.10 = ______
                                    ─────────────────
TOTAL:                                        __/5.00

RECOMMENDATION:
  4.0+ → STRONG FIT — proceed with confidence, lead with this story
  3.0-3.9 → MODERATE FIT — proceed but address specific gaps
  2.0-2.9 → WEAK FIT — add a domain co-founder or pivot market
  <2.0 → NO FIT — this is not your market

INVESTOR NARRATIVE TEMPLATE:
"I've spent [X years] working in [market], where I [specific experience].
Through that work, I discovered that [proprietary insight], which most
people in the industry don't see because [reason]. My unfair advantage
is [advantage], which gives us [specific benefit] that would take a
competitor [X years/$Y] to replicate. Combined with my network of
[N] direct relationships with potential customers, I can reach first
revenue in [timeline] without burning capital on customer acquisition."
```

**Verify**: Present the investor narrative to 2-3 experienced investors or advisors. Their feedback on credibility is the ultimate validation of your founder-market fit claim.
**If failed**: If investors do not find the narrative compelling, the gap is either in the substance (weak fit) or the articulation (weak storytelling). Distinguish between the two before iterating.

## Quality Benchmarks

| Quality Metric | Minimum Acceptable | Good | Excellent |
|---------------|-------------------|------|-----------|
| Domain experience years | 1+ year | 3-5 years | 10+ years |
| Warm customer intros available | 5+ | 15+ | 50+ |
| Proprietary insights validated | 1 unvalidated | 1 validated | 2+ validated |
| Durable advantages identified | 1 | 2 | 3+ |
| Investor narrative feedback | "Interesting" | "Compelling" | "Obvious this is your market" |

**If below minimum**: If domain experience is zero and you cannot identify a single proprietary insight, you should either find a domain-expert co-founder or pivot to a market where you have genuine advantages.

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Cannot articulate proprietary insight | Have not spent enough time with customers | Conduct 10+ customer discovery interviews before re-assessing |
| Network appears strong but intros do not convert | Surface-level relationships | Invest 3 months deepening relationships before relying on network as advantage |
| All advantages are temporary (<1 year durability) | Building in a commoditized space | Identify how to create compounding advantages (data, brand, switching costs) |
| Domain expertise is adjacent, not direct | Working in related but different industry | Map exactly which knowledge transfers and which must be learned |
| Investor narrative feels generic | Not enough specificity | Replace every general claim with a specific number, example, or story |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Claiming founder-market fit based on being a user of a product category
Being a user of email does not give you founder-market fit to build an email startup. Consumption is not expertise. Investors see through this immediately during due diligence. [src4]

### Correct: Demonstrate professional depth, not consumer familiarity
Founder-market fit comes from years of professional work in the industry, proprietary data or access, deep relationships with the buyer persona, or a genuine contrarian insight with evidence. [src1]

### Wrong: Treating founder-market fit as a single checkbox
Some founders claim fit based on one dimension (e.g., "I know the technology") while being completely disconnected from the customer and market dynamics.

### Correct: Evaluate across all five dimensions
True founder-market fit requires strength across domain knowledge, network, insight, advantages, and commitment. Weakness in any single dimension must be mitigated by a co-founder, advisor, or deliberate gap-closing plan. [src3]

## When This Matters

Use this recipe after personal readiness is confirmed and a specific target market has been identified. It produces the core narrative that will drive fundraising, hiring, and customer acquisition. A strong founder-market fit score significantly increases the probability of receiving funding and reduces the time to first customer.
