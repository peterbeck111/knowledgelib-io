# Founder Readiness Self-Assessment

## Purpose

This recipe produces a structured Founder Readiness Scorecard that evaluates your preparedness across five critical dimensions: financial runway, time commitment capacity, skills inventory, risk tolerance, and intrinsic motivation. The output is a scored assessment with a clear go/no-go/conditional recommendation and a prioritized action plan to close any gaps before committing to founding a startup.

## Prerequisites

- [ ] **Current financial records** — bank statements, monthly expenses, debt obligations, and income sources for the past 6 months
- [ ] **Career history** — resume or skills inventory listing technical, business, and domain competencies
- [ ] **Time audit** — realistic accounting of current weekly time commitments (job, family, health, obligations)
- [ ] **Spreadsheet tool** — Google Sheets, Excel, or Notion for the runway calculator
- [ ] **3 trusted advisors** — mentors, former colleagues, or experienced founders willing to give honest feedback on your blind spots

## Constraints

- Financial runway calculations must use actual expense data, not optimistic estimates. Most founders underestimate expenses by 20-30%. [src3]
- Startups are counterintuitive — domain expertise matters more than general intelligence, and your instincts about what will work are often wrong at the start. [src1]
- You need three things to create a successful startup: good people, something customers actually want, and minimal spend. Deficiency in any one is often fatal. [src2]
- The 2025-2026 funding environment requires 18-24 months of runway to reach Series A metrics, up from 12-18 months in prior years. Plan for longer timelines. [src3]
- Self-assessment inherently suffers from optimism bias. External validation is mandatory, not optional.

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Financial Runway Assessment

**Duration**: 30-60 minutes
**Tool**: Spreadsheet

Calculate your personal financial runway — how long you can survive without income.

```
Monthly Expenses Worksheet:
─────────────────────────────────
Housing (rent/mortgage):        $______
Utilities + internet:           $______
Food + groceries:               $______
Transportation:                 $______
Insurance (health, life, etc.): $______
Debt payments (loans, cards):   $______
Dependents (childcare, etc.):   $______
Subscriptions + misc:           $______
─────────────────────────────────
TOTAL MONTHLY BURN:             $______

Liquid Savings:                 $______
Partner/Spouse income (if any): $______/mo

RUNWAY = (Liquid Savings) / (Monthly Burn - Partner Income)
       = ______ months

Score:
  24+ months  → 5/5 (Ready)
  18-24 months → 4/5 (Adequate)
  12-18 months → 3/5 (Tight — plan carefully)
  6-12 months  → 2/5 (Risky — build runway first)
  <6 months    → 1/5 (Not ready — keep earning)
```

**Verify**: Runway calculation uses actual bank statement data, not estimates. Cross-check with 3 months of statements.
**If failed**: If runway is under 12 months, proceed to the personal financial planning card before continuing this assessment.

### Step 2: Time Commitment Capacity

**Duration**: 20-30 minutes
**Tool**: Time audit worksheet

Startups demand 50-70 hours per week in the first year. Map your current commitments.

```
Weekly Time Audit:
─────────────────────────────────
Current job:                    ______ hrs
Commute:                        ______ hrs
Family obligations:             ______ hrs
Health/exercise:                ______ hrs
Sleep (7-8hrs × 7):            ______ hrs
Non-negotiable commitments:     ______ hrs
─────────────────────────────────
TOTAL COMMITTED:                ______ hrs
AVAILABLE (168 - committed):    ______ hrs

Can you reallocate job hours?
  Yes, can quit → +40-50 hrs available
  Yes, can go part-time → +20 hrs available
  No, need full income → limited to evenings/weekends

Startup time needed:
  Full-time founder: 50-70 hrs/week
  Side-project phase: 15-25 hrs/week

Score:
  Can dedicate 50+ hrs/week    → 5/5
  Can dedicate 30-50 hrs/week  → 4/5
  Can dedicate 15-30 hrs/week  → 3/5 (side-project mode)
  Can dedicate 5-15 hrs/week   → 2/5 (validation only)
  <5 hrs/week                  → 1/5 (not feasible now)
```

**Verify**: Time audit accounts for seasonal variations (holidays, school schedules, busy periods at work).
**If failed**: If available time is under 15 hours/week, defer full founding — use available time for idea validation only.

### Step 3: Skills Gap Analysis

**Duration**: 30-45 minutes
**Tool**: Skills matrix spreadsheet

Rate yourself honestly across the core competencies required to build a startup. [src7]

```
Skills Assessment Matrix (rate 1-5):
─────────────────────────────────────────────
BUILDING (Technical)
  Product development/coding:       ___/5
  Design/UX:                        ___/5
  Data analysis:                    ___/5

SELLING (Commercial)
  Sales/business development:       ___/5
  Marketing/growth:                 ___/5
  Fundraising/pitching:             ___/5

LEADING (Operational)
  Hiring/people management:         ___/5
  Financial management:             ___/5
  Strategic planning:               ___/5

DOMAIN (Market-specific)
  Industry knowledge:               ___/5
  Customer empathy:                 ___/5
  Regulatory understanding:         ___/5
─────────────────────────────────────────────

Scoring:
  Average ≥ 3.5 across categories  → 5/5
  Average ≥ 3.0, no category < 2   → 4/5
  Average ≥ 2.5, 1-2 gaps          → 3/5 (co-founder needed)
  Average < 2.5 or major gaps      → 2/5 (upskill first)
  Multiple categories at 1          → 1/5 (significant prep needed)

Critical gaps (score 1-2) that need a co-founder:
  ___________________________________
  ___________________________________
```

**Verify**: Have 2-3 trusted colleagues validate your self-ratings. Most founders overrate themselves by 0.5-1.0 points. [src1]
**If failed**: If you have critical gaps in both building AND selling, you need a co-founder before proceeding.

### Step 4: Risk Tolerance Calibration

**Duration**: 20 minutes
**Tool**: Questionnaire

Risk tolerance is not abstract — it is about specific scenarios you will face. [src4]

```
Risk Scenario Assessment (answer honestly):
─────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Your savings drop to 3 months of expenses.
   Can you continue working on the startup?
   [ ] Yes, without anxiety  [ ] Yes, with stress  [ ] No

2. After 12 months, no revenue and no funding.
   Do you keep going?
   [ ] Yes, pivot and try again  [ ] Maybe, depends  [ ] No, return to employment

3. A peer gets promoted to a $200K+ role.
   Does this affect your commitment?
   [ ] No effect  [ ] Some doubt  [ ] Significant doubt

4. Your startup idea is invalidated by customer feedback.
   Response?
   [ ] Pivot to new idea  [ ] Deeply discouraged  [ ] Want to quit

5. You need to invest $10K-$20K of personal savings.
   Comfortable?
   [ ] Yes  [ ] Uncomfortable but willing  [ ] Cannot risk it

6. Partner/family expresses concern about financial stability.
   Impact?
   [ ] Manageable  [ ] Significant stress  [ ] Potential relationship strain

Scoring:
  5-6 "resilient" answers       → 5/5
  3-4 "resilient" answers       → 3/5 (manageable)
  1-2 "resilient" answers       → 2/5 (high risk of abandonment)
  0 "resilient" answers         → 1/5 (founding may not be right path)
```

**Verify**: Discuss scenarios with partner/spouse if applicable. Misaligned expectations on risk are the top cause of founder personal crises.
**If failed**: Low risk tolerance is not a character flaw — it may mean bootstrapping or side-project mode is the better path.

### Step 5: Motivation & Commitment Assessment

**Duration**: 15 minutes
**Tool**: Reflection questionnaire

```
Motivation Assessment:
─────────────────────────────────────────────
1. WHY do you want to start a company?
   [ ] Solve a specific problem I've experienced
   [ ] Build something I'm passionate about
   [ ] Financial upside / get rich
   [ ] Be my own boss / autonomy
   [ ] Social proof / status
   [ ] No better options / pushed out

2. How long have you been thinking about this?
   [ ] <1 month    [ ] 1-6 months
   [ ] 6-12 months [ ] 1+ year

3. Have you talked to potential customers?
   [ ] Yes, 10+  [ ] Yes, 1-9  [ ] No

4. Have you built anything yet (prototype, landing page)?
   [ ] Yes, functional  [ ] Yes, mockup  [ ] No

5. Would you work on this even if you could never raise funding?
   [ ] Yes  [ ] Probably  [ ] No

Scoring:
  Problem-driven + 6mo+ thinking + customer contact → 5/5
  Passion-driven + some validation                  → 4/5
  Financial motivation + no validation              → 2/5
  Escape motivation + no preparation                → 1/5
```

**Verify**: If primary motivation is financial or escape-driven, this is a strong negative signal. Successful founders are almost always problem-driven or passion-driven.
**If failed**: If you have not spoken with any potential customers, do that before completing this assessment.

### Step 6: Compile Scorecard and Generate Recommendation

**Duration**: 15 minutes

```
FOUNDER READINESS SCORECARD
═══════════════════════════════════════
Dimension              Score   Weight   Weighted
Financial Runway:      __/5    × 0.30 = ______
Time Commitment:       __/5    × 0.20 = ______
Skills Coverage:       __/5    × 0.20 = ______
Risk Tolerance:        __/5    × 0.15 = ______
Motivation Strength:   __/5    × 0.15 = ______
                               ─────────────────
TOTAL WEIGHTED SCORE:                   __/5.00

RECOMMENDATION:
  4.0 - 5.0 → GO — Ready to found. Proceed to idea validation.
  3.0 - 3.9 → CONDITIONAL — Address specific gaps first (see action plan).
  2.0 - 2.9 → NOT YET — Significant preparation needed (6-12 months).
  < 2.0     → DEFER — Major gaps in multiple dimensions.

TOP 3 GAPS TO ADDRESS:
1. ___________________________________
2. ___________________________________
3. ___________________________________

ACTION PLAN:
For each gap, define: what, by when, how to measure completion.
```

**Verify**: Share scorecard with at least one experienced founder or advisor. Ask: "Does this match what you see?"
**If failed**: If advisor feedback significantly contradicts your self-assessment, re-do Steps 3 and 4 with their calibration.

## Quality Benchmarks

| Quality Metric | Minimum Acceptable | Good | Excellent |
|---------------|-------------------|------|-----------|
| Financial data accuracy | Uses estimates | Uses 3mo bank data | Uses 6mo+ actuals |
| External validation | 1 advisor review | 2 advisor reviews | 3+ advisor reviews |
| Customer conversations | 0 (pre-idea) | 1-5 informal | 10+ structured |
| Skills gap specificity | General categories | Specific skills listed | Skills + learning plan |
| Action plan completeness | Gaps identified | Actions + timelines | Actions + metrics + owners |

**If below minimum**: Re-run assessment with actual financial data and at least one external validator before making any decisions.

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Runway calculation wildly different from expected | Missing expense categories or irregular income | Add all credit card statements, include annual expenses amortized monthly |
| Skills scores all 4-5 (suspiciously high) | Self-assessment optimism bias | Have 3 colleagues independently rate you, average with self-score |
| Risk tolerance answers inconsistent | Answering aspirationally vs. honestly | Re-take after a bad day — your worst-case self is more honest |
| Motivation assessment unclear | Multiple competing motivations | Rank-order motivations, identify which you would act on if only one were possible |
| Advisor feedback contradicts self-assessment | Blind spots or stale self-image | Weight advisor input at 50% — they see what you cannot |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Quitting your job before completing financial assessment
Many first-time founders quit their job on enthusiasm alone, then discover their runway is 4-6 months — creating panic that degrades decision-making. 61% of startups saw runway shrink in 2025. [src3]

### Correct: Complete full financial assessment first
Calculate actual runway, build to minimum 18 months, then give notice. The extra 2-3 months of employment while preparing typically adds 4-6 months of runway.

### Wrong: Skipping external validation of self-assessment
Founders who only self-assess consistently overrate skills and underrate risk factors. Startups are counterintuitive — your instincts about your own readiness are often as wrong as your instincts about what customers want. [src1]

### Correct: Mandatory advisor calibration
Share your raw scorecard with 3 people who will be honest. Weight their average assessment at 50% alongside your self-assessment.

### Wrong: Treating readiness as binary (ready/not ready)
Many founders wait for perfect readiness, which never comes. Others leap before any assessment. Both fail at high rates.

### Correct: Use conditional readiness with specific gap-closing actions
A score of 3.0-3.9 with an action plan is often the ideal starting point — you are motivated enough to start but clear-eyed about what needs fixing.

## When This Matters

Use this recipe when someone is considering founding a startup but has not yet committed. It produces a structured, externally-validated readiness assessment that replaces gut feelings with scored data. The output feeds directly into co-founder evaluation (if gaps need a partner), financial planning (if runway is insufficient), or idea structuring (if readiness is confirmed).
