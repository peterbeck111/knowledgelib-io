# Co-Founder Evaluation Framework

## Purpose

This recipe produces a structured Co-Founder Compatibility Report that scores candidate co-founders across six critical dimensions, generates an evidence-based equity split recommendation, and outputs a co-founder agreement checklist. The deliverable replaces gut-feeling co-founder selection with a scored, documented evaluation that reduces the single largest cause of startup failure — co-founder conflict.

## Prerequisites

- [ ] **Individual readiness scorecards** — each co-founder candidate has completed the Founder Readiness Self-Assessment — [Founder Readiness Self-Assessment](/business/startup-readiness/founder-readiness-self-assessment/2026)
- [ ] **Skills inventories** — detailed skills matrix for each candidate (technical, commercial, operational, domain)
- [ ] **2-6 week trial period** — worked on a real project together (not just talked about working together)
- [ ] **Reference checks** — spoken with 2+ people who have worked with each candidate professionally
- [ ] **Legal counsel identified** — startup attorney or legal service for eventual co-founder agreement review

## Constraints

- Cofounders are for a startup what location is for real estate — you can change your idea but changing cofounders is hard. [src7]
- Co-founder conflict is the second leading cause of startup failure, with 65% of startup failures involving co-founder disputes. [src3]
- Equity should be viewed as the output of real work over the trial period, not a day-one guess. Work together 2-6 weeks before finalizing splits. [src1]
- Four-year vesting with a one-year cliff is the industry standard and is non-negotiable for investor readiness. [src1]
- Team composition substantially impacts business outcomes, with skills complementarity as a critical success factor for investors. [src3]

## Tool Selection Decision

```
Which path?
├── Evaluating someone you already know well (friend, colleague)
│   └── PATH A: Known Candidate — emphasis on work-style compatibility + role clarity
├── Evaluating someone from a matching platform (YC, events)
│   └── PATH B: New Candidate — emphasis on reference checks + extended trial
├── Multiple candidates to compare
│   └── PATH C: Comparative — score all candidates on same matrix
└── Already working together, need to formalize
    └── PATH D: Formalization — emphasis on equity + agreement terms
```

| Path | Focus | Timeline | Risk Level |
|------|-------|----------|------------|
| A: Known Candidate | Compatibility deep-dive | 2-3 weeks trial | Medium (friendship bias) |
| B: New Candidate | Verification + trial | 4-6 weeks trial | Higher (unknown quantity) |
| C: Comparative | Scoring matrix | 2-4 weeks per candidate | Lowest (multiple options) |
| D: Formalization | Equity + legal | 1-2 weeks | Low (already validated) |

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Skills Complementarity Assessment

**Duration**: 30-45 minutes per candidate
**Tool**: Spreadsheet scoring matrix

Map each founder's skills and identify coverage and overlap. The goal is maximum coverage with minimum critical gaps. [src3]

```
Skills Complementarity Matrix:
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════
Skill Area           Founder A    Candidate B    Gap?
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BUILDING
  Product/engineering    ___/5        ___/5       [ ]
  Design/UX              ___/5        ___/5       [ ]
  Data/analytics         ___/5        ___/5       [ ]

SELLING
  Sales/BD               ___/5        ___/5       [ ]
  Marketing/growth       ___/5        ___/5       [ ]
  Fundraising            ___/5        ___/5       [ ]

LEADING
  Hiring/management      ___/5        ___/5       [ ]
  Finance/ops            ___/5        ___/5       [ ]
  Strategy               ___/5        ___/5       [ ]

DOMAIN
  Industry knowledge     ___/5        ___/5       [ ]
  Customer network       ___/5        ___/5       [ ]
  Regulatory knowledge   ___/5        ___/5       [ ]

Complementarity Score:
  Max(A, B) across all skills → team score
  Areas where both score <3   → critical gaps
  Areas where both score 5    → overlap (potential conflict)

Score:
  0 critical gaps, <3 overlaps      → 5/5
  0 critical gaps, 3+ overlaps      → 4/5 (role clarity needed)
  1 critical gap, clear complement  → 3/5 (hire for gap early)
  2+ critical gaps                  → 2/5 (wrong team composition)
  Mostly overlapping skills         → 1/5 (not complementary)
```

**Verify**: Have a neutral third party review the matrix for optimistic bias. Founders consistently overrate their own and their friends' skills.
**If failed**: If 2+ critical gaps exist, this candidate does not sufficiently complement your skills. Look for a different profile.

### Step 2: Commitment and Alignment Assessment

**Duration**: 45-60 minutes (structured conversation)
**Tool**: Discussion framework

Have an explicit conversation covering every item below. Ambiguity here is the seed of future conflict. [src6]

```
Commitment Alignment Discussion:
═══════════════════════════════════════════
Topic                    Founder A    Candidate B    Aligned?
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Full-time start date:    _________    _________       [ ]
Minimum commitment:      ___ years    ___ years       [ ]
Financial runway:        ___ months   ___ months      [ ]
Outside work/consulting: yes/no       yes/no          [ ]
Geographic location:     _________    _________       [ ]
Remote vs. in-person:    _________    _________       [ ]
Funding approach:        bootstrap/VC bootstrap/VC    [ ]
Exit expectations:       _________    _________       [ ]
Risk if startup fails:   _________    _________       [ ]
Decision-making style:   _________    _________       [ ]

RED FLAG QUESTIONS:
1. "What happens if we disagree on a major decision?"
   A: _______________  B: _______________
2. "What would make you leave the startup?"
   A: _______________  B: _______________
3. "How do you handle stress and conflict?"
   A: _______________  B: _______________
4. "What are your personal financial obligations?"
   A: _______________  B: _______________

Score:
  9-10 items aligned + no red flags      → 5/5
  7-8 items aligned + minor differences  → 4/5
  5-6 items aligned + 1 red flag         → 3/5 (address before proceeding)
  <5 items aligned or 2+ red flags       → 1/5 (do not proceed)
```

**Verify**: Both founders document their answers independently BEFORE discussing. Compare for genuine alignment vs. one founder deferring to the other.
**If failed**: Misalignment on funding approach (bootstrap vs. VC) or commitment timeline (1 year vs. 5 years) is usually irreconcilable. Do not proceed.

### Step 3: Working Trial Period Evaluation

**Duration**: 2-6 weeks
**Tool**: Joint project with structured checkpoints

Do not formalize the partnership before working together on a real deliverable. [src1]

```
Trial Period Structure:
═══════════════════════════════════════════
Week 1-2: Define and start a joint project
  - Build a prototype, run customer interviews, or complete a hackathon project
  - Each founder takes ownership of distinct tasks
  - Hold daily standups (15 min)

Week 3-4: Stress-test collaboration
  - Introduce a deadline pressure scenario
  - Make a decision where you initially disagree
  - Handle a setback or failure together

Week 5-6 (if new candidate): Extended validation
  - Handle money (even small amounts — buying tools, splitting costs)
  - Present jointly to outsiders (mentors, potential customers)
  - Reflect independently on the partnership

EVALUATION AFTER TRIAL:
                                          Score
Work quality matches expectations?        ___/5
Communication style compatible?           ___/5
Handles disagreement constructively?      ___/5
Follows through on commitments?           ___/5
Brings energy and ideas vs. waiting?      ___/5
Would you enthusiastically hire them?     ___/5

Average score = Trial Score
  4.5+ → Strong fit
  3.5-4.4 → Good fit, minor concerns
  2.5-3.4 → Concerning — address issues
  <2.5 → Do not proceed
```

**Verify**: Ask yourself the "airport test" — would you be happy to be stuck in an airport with this person for 8 hours? And the "foxhole test" — do you trust them to have your back in a crisis?
**If failed**: If trial score is below 3.5, have an honest conversation about the concerns. If they cannot be resolved in a second 2-week sprint, this is not the right match.

### Step 4: Equity Split Negotiation

**Duration**: 1-2 hours (structured discussion)
**Tool**: Equity framework

YC recommends equal or near-equal splits. Research confirms that equal splits correlate with higher startup survival rates. [src1] [src5]

```
Equity Split Framework:
═══════════════════════════════════════════
BASELINE: Start with equal split (50/50 for 2 founders)

ADJUSTMENTS (only if strongly justified):
Factor                         Adjustment Range
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
Originated the idea:           0-5% (ideas are worth little)
Full-time earlier:             1-3% per quarter head start
Capital investment:            Treat as convertible note, not equity
Domain expertise advantage:    0-5% if clearly asymmetric
CEO designation:               0-2% (51/49 tie-breaking)

RECOMMENDED RANGES:
2 founders: 50/50 or 55/45 maximum
3 founders: 34/33/33 or 40/30/30 maximum

NEVER DO:
- 80/20 or worse (demotivates minority founder)
- Equity for the idea alone (no execution credit)
- Handshake deal without vesting
- Unequal splits without explicit documented reasoning

VESTING TERMS (non-negotiable):
- 4-year vesting schedule
- 1-year cliff (0% before 12 months, 25% at month 12)
- Monthly vesting after cliff
- Single-trigger or double-trigger acceleration (discuss)
```

**Verify**: Both founders independently write down what they think is fair before discussing. If proposals differ by more than 10%, there is a fundamental alignment problem.
**If failed**: If you cannot agree on equity within a 5% range after the trial period, this is a signal of deeper value misalignment. Consider whether the partnership should proceed. [src2]

### Step 5: Compile Report and Agreement Checklist

**Duration**: 1 hour

```
CO-FOUNDER COMPATIBILITY REPORT
═══════════════════════════════════════════
Dimension                    Score    Weight   Weighted
Skills Complementarity:      ___/5    × 0.25 = ______
Commitment Alignment:        ___/5    × 0.25 = ______
Trial Period Performance:    ___/5    × 0.25 = ______
Equity Agreement:            ___/5    × 0.10 = ______
Reference Checks:            ___/5    × 0.10 = ______
Personal Compatibility:      ___/5    × 0.05 = ______
                                      ─────────────────
TOTAL:                                          __/5.00

RECOMMENDATION:
  4.0+ → PROCEED — formalize agreement
  3.0-3.9 → CONDITIONAL — address flagged concerns first
  <3.0 → DO NOT PROCEED — fundamental compatibility issues

CO-FOUNDER AGREEMENT CHECKLIST:
[ ] Equity percentages and rationale
[ ] Vesting schedule (4yr / 1yr cliff)
[ ] Role definitions (CEO, CTO, etc.)
[ ] Decision-making authority by domain
[ ] Salary expectations (or $0 until funding)
[ ] IP assignment to company
[ ] Non-compete and non-solicitation terms
[ ] Departure terms (voluntary + involuntary)
[ ] Dispute resolution mechanism
[ ] Buyout provisions
```

**Verify**: Have a startup attorney review the agreement checklist before signing. Template agreements miss jurisdiction-specific issues.
**If failed**: If legal review surfaces issues, iterate on terms — do not skip legal review to save money.

## Quality Benchmarks

| Quality Metric | Minimum Acceptable | Good | Excellent |
|---------------|-------------------|------|-----------|
| Trial period length | 2 weeks | 4 weeks | 6+ weeks |
| Reference checks per candidate | 1 | 2 | 3+ professional references |
| Alignment topics discussed | 5/10 | 8/10 | 10/10 explicitly documented |
| Independent equity proposals | Within 15% | Within 10% | Within 5% |
| Legal review of agreement | Template only | Attorney-reviewed | Custom-drafted |

**If below minimum**: Do not formalize the co-founder agreement. Extend the trial period and have the difficult conversations you have been avoiding.

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Cannot agree on equity split | Fundamentally different value perceptions | Use the 6-factor scoring framework (experience, commitment, idea, capital, risk, expertise) to create an objective baseline [src4] |
| Trial period feels awkward or artificial | No real project to work on | Pick a concrete deliverable: build a landing page, run 20 customer interviews, or compete in a hackathon |
| Reference checks return vague/neutral feedback | Candidate gave safe references only | Ask each reference for "one more person who worked closely with them" and contact that person instead |
| One founder consistently defers to the other | Power imbalance or conflict avoidance | This is a red flag — the deferring founder will eventually resent decisions they did not truly agree with |
| Candidate wants to skip vesting | Does not understand startup norms | Explain that all serious investors require vesting. Refusal to vest is a disqualifying signal [src1] |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Splitting equity based on who had the idea
Ideas are worth almost nothing — execution is everything. Founders who take 70%+ because "it was my idea" create a demotivated partner who leaves within 18 months. [src1] [src5]

### Correct: Split equity based on forward-looking contribution
YC recommends equal or near-equal splits. The work is ahead of you, not behind. Adjust by 5-10% maximum for genuine asymmetries in commitment timing or capital invested. [src1]

### Wrong: Choosing a co-founder because you are friends
Friendship and working compatibility are different skills. Many friendships are destroyed by startup pressure because the friends never tested their ability to disagree productively, handle money together, or navigate power dynamics. [src6]

### Correct: Choose based on complementary skills and trial performance
A co-founder you met at an industry event and trialed for 4 weeks is often a better match than a college roommate. Evaluate based on the scoring framework, not personal affection.

### Wrong: Handshake deal with no vesting
Without vesting, a co-founder who leaves after 3 months retains their full equity stake, potentially blocking future fundraising and demotivating the remaining team. [src2]

### Correct: Standard vesting from day one
4-year vesting, 1-year cliff, no exceptions. This protects both founders equally and is expected by every serious investor.

## When This Matters

Use this recipe when a potential founder has confirmed personal readiness and is evaluating whether to partner with a specific co-founder candidate. It produces a documented compatibility assessment that replaces emotional decision-making with structured evaluation. The output determines whether to proceed to idea structuring as a team or continue searching.
