# Due Diligence Preparation Checklist

## Purpose

This recipe produces a complete, organized virtual data room with all documents required for investor due diligence — categorized by priority, staged for the correct funding round, and structured so investors can self-serve without constant founder involvement. The output is a ready-to-share data room that can cut 1-2 weeks off the closing timeline after a term sheet is signed.

## Prerequisites

- [ ] **Term sheet received or imminent** — due diligence is a post-term-sheet activity (but smart founders prepare in advance)
- [ ] **Corporate formation documents** — articles of incorporation, bylaws, certificates of good standing
- [ ] **Financial records** — at minimum: bank statements, P&L, balance sheet from accounting software ([QuickBooks](https://quickbooks.intuit.com), [Xero](https://www.xero.com), or [Wave](https://www.waveapps.com))
- [ ] **Cap table** — accurate to the share level on [Carta](https://carta.com), [Pulley](https://pulley.com), or a maintained spreadsheet
- [ ] **Virtual data room** — [Google Drive](https://drive.google.com), [Papermark](https://www.papermark.com), [DocSend](https://www.docsend.com), or [Digify](https://digify.com)

## Constraints

- Have Priority 1 documents ready before signing a term sheet. Being prepared can cut 1-2 weeks off closing. [src3]
- Due diligence takes 2-6 weeks from term sheet to close. Budget 4 weeks minimum, 6 for Series A with complex structures. [src7]
- Use a secure data room with access controls. Never share sensitive documents via email attachments — they get forwarded. [src4]
- Cap table accuracy is the #1 deal-killer. A single discrepancy in authorized shares, option pool, or SAFE conversion can delay closing by weeks. [src1]
- AI startups face additional technical diligence in 2025-2026: model governance, data provenance, and IP ownership of training data. [src2]

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Set Up the Data Room Structure

**Duration**: 30 minutes
**Tool**: Google Drive, Papermark, or chosen VDR

Create the folder structure before gathering documents. This structure matches how investors navigate diligence. [src4] [src6]

```
Data Room Folder Structure:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
01_Corporate/
  ├── Articles of Incorporation
  ├── Bylaws or Operating Agreement
  ├── Certificate(s) of Good Standing
  ├── Board meeting minutes
  ├── Board consents and resolutions
  └── Organizational chart

02_Cap_Table_and_Equity/
  ├── Cap table (current, fully diluted)
  ├── Stock purchase agreements
  ├── SAFE/Convertible note agreements
  ├── Option plan and grant schedule
  ├── 409A valuation (most recent)
  └── Vesting schedules

03_Financial/
  ├── Income statement (P&L) — inception to date, monthly
  ├── Balance sheet — current
  ├── Cash flow statement
  ├── Bank statements — last 12 months
  ├── Financial projections model
  ├── Monthly burn rate analysis
  └── Accounts receivable and payable aging

04_Legal/
  ├── All prior funding documents (SAFEs, notes, priced round docs)
  ├── Material contracts (>$25K or strategic importance)
  ├── Pending or threatened litigation
  ├── Insurance policies (D&O, E&O, general liability)
  └── Regulatory filings and licenses

05_IP_and_Technology/
  ├── Patent filings and granted patents
  ├── Trademark registrations
  ├── IP assignment agreements (all employees and contractors)
  ├── Open source software usage log
  └── Technical architecture overview

06_Team_and_HR/
  ├── Key employee contracts
  ├── Contractor agreements (with IP assignment clauses)
  ├── Employee handbook (if exists)
  ├── Benefits summary
  └── Org chart with reporting lines

07_Product_and_Metrics/
  ├── Product roadmap
  ├── Key metrics dashboard (MRR, users, churn, CAC, LTV)
  ├── Customer list (top 10-20 by revenue)
  ├── Cohort retention data
  └── NPS or customer satisfaction data

08_Pitch_Materials/
  ├── Current pitch deck
  ├── Executive summary / one-pager
  └── Press coverage and testimonials
```

**Verify**: All 8 folders created. Naming convention is consistent (XX_CategoryName).
**If failed**: Use the exact folder names above. Investors expect this structure and will navigate it without guidance.

### Step 2: Gather Priority 1 Documents (Must-Have)

**Duration**: 3-5 days
**Tool**: Legal counsel, accounting, cap table platform

Priority 1 documents are requested in 100% of diligence processes. Missing any of these will delay closing. [src1] [src3]

```
Priority 1 — Always Required:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
CORPORATE:
[  ] Articles of Incorporation (or Certificate of Formation)
[  ] Certificate of Good Standing (from state of incorporation)
[  ] Bylaws or Operating Agreement

CAP TABLE & EQUITY:
[  ] Fully diluted cap table (shares, not just percentages)
[  ] All SAFE and convertible note agreements
[  ] All prior equity round documents
[  ] Stock option plan + grant schedule
[  ] 409A valuation (must be < 12 months old)

FINANCIAL:
[  ] Income statement (P&L) — monthly, inception to date
[  ] Balance sheet — most recent month
[  ] Bank statements — last 6-12 months
[  ] Financial projections model (from pitch process)

LEGAL:
[  ] Employee/founder IP assignment agreements
[  ] Any pending or threatened litigation disclosure
[  ] Material contracts list (>$25K annual value)
```

**Verify**: Every checkbox above is checked. If any document does not exist (e.g., no IP assignments), note it explicitly — do not leave a gap.
**If failed**: The three most common missing documents are: (1) IP assignment agreements, (2) 409A valuation, (3) accurate cap table. These take 1-4 weeks to obtain — start immediately.

### Step 3: Gather Priority 2 Documents (Usually Required)

**Duration**: 3-5 days (parallel with Priority 1)
**Tool**: Legal, HR, product team

Priority 2 documents are requested in 70-90% of diligence processes. Having them ready prevents back-and-forth delays. [src2] [src5]

```
Priority 2 — Usually Required:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
CORPORATE:
[  ] Board meeting minutes (all historical)
[  ] Board consents and written resolutions
[  ] State and local business registrations

FINANCIAL:
[  ] Tax returns (federal and state, all years)
[  ] Accounts receivable and payable aging
[  ] Monthly burn rate analysis

LEGAL:
[  ] Insurance policies (D&O is highest priority)
[  ] Key customer contracts
[  ] Key vendor/partner agreements
[  ] Regulatory licenses or permits

TEAM:
[  ] Key employee offer letters/contracts
[  ] Contractor agreements
[  ] Non-compete and non-solicitation agreements
[  ] Employee stock vesting schedules

PRODUCT:
[  ] Product roadmap (next 12 months)
[  ] Key metrics dashboard
[  ] Customer list (top 10-20 by revenue)
```

**Verify**: At least 80% of Priority 2 items are gathered or explicitly documented as N/A.
**If failed**: For missing documents, create a "To Be Provided" placeholder in the data room noting when it will be available. Never leave empty folders with no explanation.

### Step 4: Gather Priority 3 Documents (Sometimes Required)

**Duration**: Ongoing (gather as requested)
**Tool**: Various

Priority 3 documents are requested in 30-60% of diligence processes, usually for Series A and later. [src2]

```
Priority 3 — Sometimes Required:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
CORPORATE:
[  ] Subsidiary or foreign entity documents
[  ] Corporate structure diagram (if multi-entity)

FINANCIAL:
[  ] Detailed revenue by customer/segment
[  ] Cash flow projections (13-week)
[  ] Debt schedule (if any loans/credit lines)

IP & TECHNOLOGY:
[  ] Patent filings and status
[  ] Open source license compliance audit
[  ] Data privacy compliance documentation (GDPR, CCPA)
[  ] Security audit reports or SOC 2 status
[  ] AI model governance documentation (for AI startups)

PRODUCT:
[  ] Technical architecture diagram
[  ] Cohort retention analysis
[  ] NPS scores and customer satisfaction data
[  ] Competitive analysis

TEAM:
[  ] Employee handbook
[  ] Benefits and compensation summary
[  ] Organizational chart with reporting lines
```

**Verify**: Priority 3 items are identified and tracked. Documents that exist are in the data room; items that don't exist have a clear note.
**If failed**: Priority 3 gaps are acceptable as long as they are documented. Investors understand early-stage companies lack enterprise-grade documentation.

### Step 5: Verify Cap Table Accuracy

**Duration**: 1-2 hours
**Tool**: Carta, Pulley, or spreadsheet

Cap table errors are the single most common reason for diligence delays. Verify every element. [src1]

```
Cap Table Verification Checklist:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
[  ] Authorized shares matches Articles of Incorporation exactly
[  ] Total issued shares + unissued option pool + unissued authorized = authorized total
[  ] Every SAFE has a conversion scenario modeled (pre-money, post-money, cap, discount)
[  ] Every convertible note shows principal + accrued interest + conversion terms
[  ] Option pool percentage matches board approval
[  ] All founder shares show correct vesting schedule and cliff
[  ] Pro forma cap table shows post-money ownership after this round
[  ] Fully diluted share count includes all SAFEs, notes, and option grants
[  ] No discrepancies between cap table platform and legal documents
```

**Verify**: Cap table totals reconcile to legal documents. No unaccounted shares.
**If failed**: If cap table and legal docs do not match, halt and resolve with legal counsel before proceeding. This is a blocking issue.

### Step 6: Set Permissions and Share the Data Room

**Duration**: 30 minutes
**Tool**: Data room platform

Configure access controls before sharing. Investors should see documents, not have edit access. [src4]

```
Access Control Setup:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Set all folders to VIEW ONLY (no download if using Papermark/DocSend)
2. Add investor email(s) with individual access links
3. Enable analytics (view tracking, time spent, page-level engagement)
4. Add NDA requirement before access (if using DocSend or Digify)
5. Set expiration date on access links (30-60 days post-close)
6. Watermark sensitive documents with investor name (prevents leaks)

Do NOT:
- Grant edit access to any folder
- Share a public link (use individual email-gated access)
- Share financial data or cap table via email attachment
```

**Verify**: Test access by opening the data room in an incognito browser using a test email. All documents viewable, none editable.
**If failed**: Check sharing settings. Google Drive defaults can be too permissive — verify each folder's sharing level individually.

## Quality Benchmarks

| Quality Metric | Minimum Acceptable | Good | Excellent |
|---------------|-------------------|------|-----------|
| Priority 1 completeness | 80% of items | 100% of items | 100% + explanatory notes |
| Priority 2 completeness | 50% of items | 80% of items | 100% of items |

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Cap table does not reconcile | Shares issued without updating cap table | Audit every issuance against board minutes; engage counsel to reconcile |
| No 409A valuation | Never obtained one or expired (>12 months) | Engage 409A provider immediately (Carta, Eqvista, Aranca) — takes 2-4 weeks |
| Missing IP assignments | Contractors or early employees never signed | Have legal counsel prepare and send assignment agreements immediately |
| Financial records incomplete | Informal bookkeeping in early days | Hire bookkeeper (Bench, Pilot, Kruze) to reconstruct financials |
| Investor requests document not in data room | Uncommon request or missing from checklist | Upload within 24 hours; acknowledge and provide timeline if not immediately available |
| Data room access issues | Permissions misconfigured | Test with incognito browser; re-share with individual links |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Scrambling to gather documents after receiving a term sheet
Post-term-sheet diligence has a clock. Every day spent searching for documents is a day the deal can fall apart. Investors lose confidence when founders cannot produce basic corporate documents promptly. [src3]

### Correct: Maintain a living data room updated quarterly
Start the data room at incorporation and update it quarterly. When a term sheet arrives, you share a link — not start a document hunt. This signals operational maturity.

### Wrong: Sharing documents via email attachments
Email attachments get forwarded, lack version control, and provide no analytics. Sensitive financial and cap table data shared via email can end up with parties who should not see it. [src4]

### Correct: Use a dedicated data room with access controls
Individual access links, view-only permissions, watermarking, and expiration dates. Track who views what and for how long — this data also tells you which investors are most engaged.

### Wrong: Providing a cap table that "roughly shows" ownership percentages
A cap table that uses percentages instead of share counts, or that does not model SAFE/note conversions, signals the founder does not understand their own ownership structure. This is a red flag for investors. [src1]

### Correct: Cap table accurate to the share level with conversion scenarios
Every SAFE, note, and option grant modeled. Fully diluted share count matches legal documents exactly. Pro forma post-money table available for the current round.

## When This Matters

Use this recipe when a founder has received a term sheet (or expects one within 4 weeks) and needs to prepare for investor due diligence. Also useful proactively — the best time to start a data room is at incorporation, not at fundraising. Requires corporate formation documents to exist — this recipe handles organization and preparation, not legal formation.
