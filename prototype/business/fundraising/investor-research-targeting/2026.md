# Investor Research & Targeting

## Purpose

This recipe produces a ranked, tiered investor list of 50-80 targets matched by stage, sector, check size, and geography — with warm introduction paths identified, contact details verified, and an outreach priority matrix ready for systematic fundraising execution. The output is a CRM-ready spreadsheet that turns investor research from guesswork into a structured pipeline.

## Prerequisites

- [ ] **Completed pitch deck** — from [Pitch Deck Structure by Stage](/business/fundraising/pitch-deck-structure-by-stage/2026) or equivalent
- [ ] **Company details** — stage, sector, geography, traction metrics, raise amount clearly defined
- [ ] **Crunchbase account** — free at [crunchbase.com](https://www.crunchbase.com/register) (Pro $49/mo for exports and advanced filters)
- [ ] **Signal by NFX account** — free at [signal.nfx.com](https://signal.nfx.com) for AI-matched investor recommendations
- [ ] **LinkedIn account** — for verifying investor activity and finding mutual connections
- [ ] **Google Sheets or Airtable** — for tracking the investor pipeline

## Constraints

- Target 50-80 investors for seed, 30-50 for Series A. Quality over quantity — spray-and-pray approaches damage your reputation in tight VC networks. [src4]
- Warm introductions convert at 10-20x the rate of cold outreach. Exhaust all warm paths before cold emails. [src5]
- VC databases are 3-6 months stale. Always verify: Is the fund still actively deploying? Has the partner moved firms? Is the fund's check size still aligned? [src2]
- Most VCs invest in 1-2% of inbound deals. Plan for 90%+ rejection rates — this is normal, not a signal about your company. [src1]
- Never send a pitch deck without researching the specific partner's thesis and recent portfolio. Generic outreach is immediately filtered. [src4]

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Define Your Investor Profile

**Duration**: 30 minutes
**Tool**: Spreadsheet

Before searching any database, define exactly what you are looking for. This prevents wasting hours on mismatched investors.

```
Investor Profile Template:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Stage:       [pre-seed / seed / Series A]
Check size:  [$X - $Y]  (your round size ÷ 3-5 = ideal lead check)
Sector:      [primary sector + adjacent sectors]
Geography:   [investor geography: local / national / global]
Fund status: [actively deploying — avoid funds that just closed or are in fundraising mode]
Recent deals: [invested in companies similar to yours within last 12 months]
Thesis fit:  [partner-level thesis alignment, not just firm-level]

Example:
  Stage: Seed ($2M round)
  Check size: $400K-$1M (lead), $100K-$300K (follow)
  Sector: B2B SaaS, fintech, vertical software
  Geography: US-based, open to remote-first
  Fund status: Fund raised in 2024-2025, actively deploying
  Recent deals: Invested in compliance, payments, or developer tools
```

**Verify**: You can describe your ideal investor in one sentence without saying "someone who invests in startups."
**If failed**: Your criteria are too broad. Narrow by adding sector specificity or geographic constraints.

### Step 2: Build Initial List from Databases

**Duration**: 2-3 hours
**Tool**: Crunchbase, Signal by NFX, OpenVC

Use multiple databases in sequence. Each catches investors the others miss. [src2] [src6]

```
Database Research Sequence:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Signal by NFX (signal.nfx.com) — FREE
   - Enter your startup profile (stage, sector, location)
   - AI matches you to investors by fit score
   - Shows mutual connections for warm intros
   - Export top 30-50 matched investors

2. Crunchbase (crunchbase.com) — FREE or $49/mo
   - Search: Investors → Filter by: stage, sector, location, recent activity
   - Sort by recent investment date (avoid dormant funds)
   - For each investor: check portfolio for competitors or adjacent companies
   - Export to CSV (Pro required for export)

3. OpenVC (openvc.app) — FREE
   - Browse curated investor lists by stage and sector
   - Transparent check size and sector focus data
   - Add investors Crunchbase missed

4. AngelList / Wellfound — FREE
   - Search angel investors and syndicates
   - Particularly strong for pre-seed and seed angels
   - Check recent activity (some profiles are stale)

5. Twitter/X and LinkedIn — FREE
   - Search "[sector] investor" or "[stage] VC"
   - Follow active investors who share thesis posts
   - Note investors who engage with companies like yours
```

**Expected output**: Raw list of 80-120 investors in a spreadsheet with columns: Name, Firm, Stage, Sector, Check Size, Location, Recent Deal, Source.
**Verify**: List has 80+ investors. If under 80, broaden sector criteria or add adjacent geographies.
**If failed**: If your sector is very niche, expand to adjacent sectors. A healthcare AI VC will also look at AI infrastructure plays.

### Step 3: Research and Score Each Investor

**Duration**: 2-3 hours
**Tool**: Crunchbase, LinkedIn, fund websites

For each investor on the raw list, research and score on 4 dimensions. This eliminates wasted outreach. [src4]

```
Scoring Framework (1-5 per dimension, max 20):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Stage fit (1-5):
   5 = Primarily invests at your exact stage
   3 = Invests at your stage among other stages
   1 = Rarely invests at your stage

2. Sector fit (1-5):
   5 = Has thesis in your exact sector + recent relevant deals
   3 = General tech investor, no sector conflict
   1 = Sector focus doesn't match at all

3. Activity recency (1-5):
   5 = Made 3+ investments in last 6 months
   3 = Made 1-2 investments in last 12 months
   1 = No visible investment activity in 12+ months

4. Warm intro availability (1-5):
   5 = Direct mutual connection can make intro
   3 = Second-degree connection or portfolio founder can intro
   1 = No path to warm introduction identified

Tier Assignment:
  Score 16-20: TIER 1 — Perfect fit, prioritize first
  Score 11-15: TIER 2 — Good fit, approach after Tier 1
  Score 6-10:  TIER 3 — Acceptable, use as backup
  Score 1-5:   DROP — Remove from list
```

**Verify**: Final scored list has 50-80 investors with 10-15 in Tier 1, 20-30 in Tier 2, 15-30 in Tier 3.
**If failed**: If fewer than 10 in Tier 1, revisit scoring criteria — you may be too strict on one dimension.

### Step 4: Map Warm Introduction Paths

**Duration**: 1-2 hours
**Tool**: LinkedIn, Signal by NFX, personal network

For every Tier 1 and Tier 2 investor, identify the strongest warm introduction path. [src5]

```
Warm Intro Path Priority (strongest to weakest):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Portfolio founder → investor (strongest: they know the investor's decision style)
2. Co-investor → investor (strong: professional peer relationship)
3. Mutual friend/colleague → investor (good: personal trust)
4. Accelerator/incubator mentor → investor (good: institutional relationship)
5. LinkedIn 2nd-degree connection → investor (acceptable: weaker but warm-ish)
6. No warm path → cold outreach (last resort: 1-3% response rate)

For each Tier 1 investor, add to spreadsheet:
- Intro path: [type from list above]
- Connector name: [who will make the intro]
- Connector strength: [how well they know the investor]
- Ask strategy: [how to frame the intro request]
```

**Verify**: At least 60% of Tier 1 investors have a warm intro path identified.
**If failed**: Use Signal by NFX mutual connection finder. Ask existing advisors and investors for their VC connections. Attend industry events where target investors speak.

### Step 5: Prepare the Outreach Tracker

**Duration**: 30 minutes
**Tool**: Google Sheets or Airtable

Create the final outreach tracking spreadsheet that becomes your fundraising CRM.

```json
{
  "columns": [
    "investor_name", "firm", "partner", "tier",
    "stage_fit", "sector_fit", "activity_score", "warm_intro_score",
    "total_score", "check_size", "portfolio_companies",
    "intro_path", "connector", "outreach_date",
    "response_date", "meeting_date", "meeting_notes",
    "status", "next_action", "follow_up_date"
  ]
}
```

**Verify**: Spreadsheet is populated with 50-80 investors, sorted by tier then score. Status column ready for tracking.
**If failed**: If the spreadsheet feels overwhelming, start with Tier 1 only (10-15 investors) and expand to Tier 2 after initial meetings.

## Quality Benchmarks

| Quality Metric | Minimum Acceptable | Good | Excellent |
|---------------|-------------------|------|-----------|
| Total investors on list | 30 | 50-60 | 80+ |
| Tier 1 investors | 5 | 10-15 | 20+ |
| Warm intro paths identified | 30% of Tier 1 | 60% of Tier 1 | 80%+ of Tier 1 |
| Sector fit (avg score) | 3.0/5 | 3.5/5 | 4.0+/5 |
| Data freshness | Verified within 6 months | Within 3 months | Within 1 month |

**If below minimum**: Expand search to adjacent sectors, broader geography, or angel investors and syndicates. Use Signal by NFX AI matching to find non-obvious fits.

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Too few investors match criteria | Sector too niche or geography too restrictive | Broaden to adjacent sectors; add angel investors and syndicates |
| Fund is no longer actively investing | Fund in fundraising mode or fully deployed | Check Crunchbase for fund vintage; remove and replace |
| Partner has left the firm | Database not updated | Verify on LinkedIn; find new partner at same firm or follow partner to new firm |
| No warm intro paths found | Limited network in VC ecosystem | Join accelerator, attend demo days, use Signal by NFX; ask existing angels for VC intros |
| Investor already backed a competitor | Portfolio conflict | Remove from list — portfolio conflicts are almost always disqualifying |
| Response rate below 5% on cold outreach | Email quality or targeting issue | Switch to warm intros; A/B test subject lines; personalize opening line per investor |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Spray-and-pray cold emailing 500 investors
Mass emailing with generic messages destroys your reputation in the VC community, which is surprisingly small and connected. Investors talk to each other — a poorly targeted blast will follow you. [src4]

### Correct: Target 50-80 investors with personalized, researched outreach
Reference their portfolio companies, recent blog posts, or investment thesis. Explain specifically why your company fits their thesis, not why they should invest generally.

### Wrong: Relying on a single database for investor research
Crunchbase misses many angels and emerging managers. PitchBook misses early-stage micro-VCs. No single database covers the full investor landscape. [src2] [src6]

### Correct: Cross-reference 3-4 databases
Use Signal by NFX for AI-matching, Crunchbase for institutional VCs, OpenVC for curated lists, and LinkedIn for verification. Each catches investors the others miss.

### Wrong: Skipping warm intro research and going straight to cold email
Cold outreach converts at 1-3% at best. Warm introductions convert at 15-40%. The time spent finding a warm path pays for itself 10x in meeting rates. [src5]

### Correct: Map warm paths before any outreach
For every Tier 1 target, identify at least one warm introduction path. Use portfolio founders, accelerator networks, and mutual connections.
