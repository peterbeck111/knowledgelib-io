# Pitch Deck Structure by Stage

## Purpose

This recipe produces a complete, stage-appropriate pitch deck structure for pre-seed, seed, or Series A fundraising — with the exact slide order, required content per section, and a pre-send checklist that catches the most common mistakes investors use to reject decks. The output is a slide-by-slide outline document ready for content population using presentation software.

## Prerequisites

- [ ] **Business concept and value proposition** clearly defined — at minimum a 2-sentence description of what the company does and for whom
- [ ] **Traction data** (if available) — user counts, revenue, growth rates, retention metrics from analytics or CRM
- [ ] **Market research** — TAM/SAM/SOM estimates with cited sources (government data, industry reports, or bottoms-up calculations)
- [ ] **Team bios** — 2-3 sentence summaries of each co-founder's relevant experience and achievements
- [ ] **Presentation tool** — access to [Google Slides](https://slides.google.com), [Figma](https://www.figma.com), [Canva](https://www.canva.com), PowerPoint, or Keynote

## Constraints

- Investors spend an average of 3 minutes 44 seconds reviewing a pitch deck. Every slide must earn its place. [src1]
- Seed decks: 10-12 slides maximum. Series A decks: 12-15 slides, with appendix slides for deep-dive topics. [src4]
- The 60-second rule applies: if an investor cannot grasp a slide's key point in under 60 seconds, the slide needs simplification. [src1]
- Slides should use 28pt+ font for body text, 36pt+ for headers — if text is too small to read from 6 feet away, there is too much text. [src7]
- Financial projections must be bottom-up for Series A (revenue = customers x ARPU x retention), not top-down (X% of TAM). [src2]

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Determine Stage and Narrative Arc

**Duration**: 15 minutes
**Tool**: Decision framework below

Identify the funding stage and corresponding narrative goal. The narrative arc changes fundamentally between stages. [src5]

```
Stage Identification:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Pre-Seed:  No product yet, or very early prototype
           Narrative: "Here's a massive problem, here's why we're the team to solve it"
           Deck: 8-10 slides, vision-heavy

Seed:      Working product, some early users/customers
           Narrative: "The problem is real, our solution works, early signals show traction"
           Deck: 10-12 slides, balanced vision + evidence

Series A:  Product-market fit signals, growing revenue/usage
           Narrative: "The model works, here's proof it scales, here's how we accelerate"
           Deck: 12-15 slides + appendix, evidence-heavy
```

**Verify**: You can state the funding stage and the one-sentence narrative arc for your deck.
**If failed**: If unsure about stage, default to seed structure — it works for pre-seed meetings too with minor adjustments.

### Step 2: Build the Stage-Appropriate Slide Structure

**Duration**: 30 minutes
**Tool**: Presentation software

Create slides using the exact structure below for your stage. Order matters — investors expect this flow. [src1] [src3]

**Seed Deck Structure (10-12 slides):**

```
Slide 1:  Title — Company name, one-line description, your name, contact
Slide 2:  Problem — What pain exists, who feels it, how they cope today
Slide 3:  Solution — Your product/service, how it solves the problem
Slide 4:  Why Now — Market timing, technology shift, regulatory change
Slide 5:  Market Size — TAM, SAM, SOM with bottoms-up calculation
Slide 6:  Product — Demo screenshots, user flow, key features
Slide 7:  Business Model — How you make money, pricing, unit economics
Slide 8:  Traction — Users, revenue, growth rate, retention (order from most impressive)
Slide 9:  Competition — Positioning matrix (NOT a feature checklist)
Slide 10: Team — Founders + key hires, relevant achievements (not titles)
Slide 11: Ask — Amount raising, use of funds (3-4 categories), milestones
Slide 12: Appendix (optional) — Detailed financials, technical architecture, references
```

**Series A Deck Structure (12-15 slides):**

```
Slide 1:  Title — Company name, stage, round size
Slide 2:  Traction Summary — Key metrics dashboard (lead with strongest number)
Slide 3:  Problem — Refined problem statement with customer evidence
Slide 4:  Solution — Product with demonstrated PMF evidence
Slide 5:  Market Size — Bottoms-up TAM/SAM/SOM with expansion paths
Slide 6:  Product Deep Dive — Feature set, roadmap, technical moat
Slide 7:  Business Model — Unit economics (CAC, LTV, payback period, gross margin)
Slide 8:  Go-to-Market — Acquisition channels, funnel metrics, scalability proof
Slide 9:  Traction Detail — Cohort charts, retention curves, growth rate
Slide 10: Competition — Competitive landscape with defensibility thesis
Slide 11: Financial Projections — 3-year forecast, key assumptions, path to profitability
Slide 12: Team — Leadership team, key hires, advisory board
Slide 13: Ask — Amount, use of funds, 18-month milestones
Slide 14: Appendix A — Detailed financial model
Slide 15: Appendix B — Customer testimonials, press, partnerships
```

**Verify**: Slide count matches stage target (seed: 10-12, Series A: 12-15). Every slide has a clear single purpose.
**If failed**: If over the slide limit, merge related topics (e.g., Problem + Why Now can combine for seed). Never exceed 15 slides for seed or 20 for Series A.

### Step 3: Apply the Sequoia Framework Content Requirements

**Duration**: 45 minutes
**Tool**: Presentation software + notes

For each slide, populate the required content following the Sequoia framework. Each slide answers one specific question. [src3]

```
Content Requirements per Slide:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Title:        Company name + ≤10 word tagline + contact info
Problem:      3 bullet points max. Include who feels this pain and current alternatives
Solution:     Product screenshot/mockup + 3 key benefits. Show, don't tell
Why Now:      1-2 market trends making this possible TODAY (not 5 years ago)
Market Size:  TAM (top-down reference only), SAM (addressable), SOM (achievable Y1-Y2)
Product:      Screenshots or demo. Highlight UX, not feature lists
Business Model: Revenue formula. Price x Volume = Revenue. Show unit economics
Traction:     Ordered most→least impressive. Include timeframe for context
Competition:  2x2 matrix positioning OR landscape map. Never claim "no competition"
Team:         Photo + name + ONE achievement per person (not job titles)
Ask:          Dollar amount + use of funds pie chart + 3 milestones the raise unlocks
```

**Verify**: Each slide has no more than 30 words of body text (seed) or 50 words (Series A). Every claim has supporting data.
**If failed**: If a slide has too much text, split it into two slides or move details to appendix.

### Step 4: Order Slides for Maximum Impact

**Duration**: 15 minutes
**Tool**: Presentation software (drag-and-drop reorder)

After initial structure, reorder the middle sections (slides 2-10) from strongest to weakest content. YC advises: "What you do should be at the beginning and the ask at the end, but elements in the middle should be ordered from most impressive to least." [src1]

```
Reordering Decision:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
IF strongest asset is traction → Lead with Traction after Title
IF strongest asset is team    → Lead with Team after Problem
IF strongest asset is product → Lead with Product demo after Solution
IF strongest asset is market  → Lead with Market Size after Why Now

Always keep: Title first, Ask last
Always keep: Problem before Solution (establishes context)
```

**Verify**: Read just the slide titles in order — they should tell a coherent story even without content.
**If failed**: If the story flow breaks, insert a transition slide or restructure to maintain narrative arc.

### Step 5: Run the Pre-Send Checklist

**Duration**: 20 minutes
**Tool**: Checklist below

Before sending to any investor, verify every item. These are the top reasons decks get rejected. [src7] [src4]

```
Pre-Send Checklist:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
FORMAT:
[ ] Total slides within stage limit (seed: ≤12, Series A: ≤15 + appendix)
[ ] Font size ≥ 28pt body, ≥ 36pt headers
[ ] No slide has more than 6 lines of text
[ ] Company logo on every slide (header or footer)
[ ] Consistent color scheme (max 3 colors + neutrals)
[ ] All charts have labeled axes and units

CONTENT:
[ ] Problem slide includes WHO has the problem (specific persona)
[ ] Solution slide shows the product (screenshot/mockup), not just words
[ ] Market size uses bottoms-up methodology (not just "it's a $X billion market")
[ ] Competition slide exists and acknowledges real competitors
[ ] Traction metrics include timeframe ("$50K MRR" → "$50K MRR, 40% MoM growth")
[ ] Team slide highlights achievements, not just titles
[ ] Ask slide specifies exact amount AND use of funds breakdown
[ ] Financial projections (Series A) are bottoms-up with stated assumptions

COMMON MISTAKES:
[ ] No "we have no competition" claim [src7]
[ ] No unexplained acronyms or jargon
[ ] No vanity metrics without engagement/retention context [src7]
[ ] No vague asks ("we're raising $1-3M" — pick a number)
[ ] No missing contact info on title or closing slide
[ ] Deck works as standalone document (no verbal explanation needed)
```

**Verify**: Every checkbox is checked. If any fail, fix before sending.
**If failed**: The three most common fatal errors are: (1) no clear ask, (2) no traction evidence, (3) claiming no competition. Fix these first.

## Quality Benchmarks

| Quality Metric | Minimum Acceptable | Good | Excellent |
|---------------|-------------------|------|-----------|
| Slide count vs target | Within 2 of stage target | Exactly at target | At target with strong appendix |
| Word count per slide | < 50 words | < 30 words | < 20 words + visual |
| Traction data included | At least 1 metric | 3+ metrics with timeframes | Cohort data + growth curves |
| Source citations in market slide | 1 source | 2-3 sources | Primary research + 2 third-party |
| Time to present | < 20 minutes | < 15 minutes | 10 minutes with Q&A buffer |

**If below minimum**: Simplify the weakest slides first. Move detailed content to appendix. Focus on the three slides that matter most for your stage: Problem/Solution/Traction (seed) or Traction/Unit Economics/GTM (Series A).

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Deck exceeds slide limit | Trying to cover too many topics in main body | Move detailed sections (financials, technical architecture, customer list) to appendix |
| Investor requests more detail on specific slide | Slide was too high-level for their due diligence needs | Prepare 2-3 backup slides per main section; add to appendix for follow-up |
| Deck reads as "all vision, no proof" | Insufficient traction or market data | Add concrete numbers: even waitlist signups, LOIs, or pilot results count as traction |
| Narrative flow feels disconnected | Slides were built independently without story arc | Re-read slide titles as a sequence — they should form a logical argument |
| Design looks unprofessional | Inconsistent formatting, low-res images | Use a single template; ensure all images are 2x resolution; limit to 2 fonts |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Building a 25+ slide deck for seed stage
Seed investors evaluate hundreds of decks. A 25-slide deck signals the founder cannot prioritize or communicate concisely. Most investors will stop reading after slide 12. [src1]

### Correct: Keep seed decks to 10-12 slides with appendix
Move all detailed content (financial projections, technical architecture, customer testimonials) to clearly labeled appendix slides. The main deck tells the story; the appendix provides evidence on demand.

### Wrong: Claiming "we have no competition"
This is the single fastest way to lose investor credibility. Every problem has existing solutions, even if they are manual workarounds, spreadsheets, or incumbent products. [src7]

### Correct: Show a positioning matrix with honest competitive landscape
Use a 2x2 matrix or landscape map showing where competitors sit and where you differentiate. Acknowledge competitors and explain your defensible advantage (technology, data, network effects, speed).

### Wrong: Leading with market size for Series A
Series A investors start with traction and work backward. Leading with a $50B TAM slide before showing any customer data signals the founder is selling dreams, not a business. [src2]

### Correct: Lead Series A deck with traction dashboard
Open with your strongest metric (MRR, user growth, retention rate), then build the story of how you got there and where it goes.
