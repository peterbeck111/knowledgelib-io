# Marketing Maturity Assessment

## Purpose

This assessment evaluates marketing capability across six dimensions: brand strategy, demand generation, content operations, analytics and measurement, marketing operations, and customer engagement. It is designed for CMOs, VPs of Marketing, and marketing directors who need a structured diagnostic of their marketing function's current state. The output identifies specific capability gaps and routes to targeted improvement playbooks. [src1]

## Constraints
<!-- Agents: read before running this assessment with a user. -->

- Requires access to marketing analytics, CRM/MAP data, and budget information for accurate scoring
- Company must have at least 6 months of active marketing programs to score reliably
- Self-assessment bias is common — teams over-score by 0.5-1.0 points on average; request evidence for each score
- This assessment covers breadth of marketing function; use specific sub-assessments for deep dives into individual dimensions
- Re-run quarterly or after major organizational changes (new CMO, restructuring, budget shifts)

## Assessment Dimensions

<!-- Each dimension is scored independently. The structured format lets agents
     walk through this conversationally with a user, one dimension at a time. -->

### Dimension 1: Brand Strategy & Positioning

**What this measures**: How well the organization has defined, differentiated, and operationalized its brand across all customer touchpoints.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No documented brand guidelines; messaging inconsistent across channels | No brand book; each team creates its own messaging; visual identity varies |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic brand guidelines exist but not enforced; positioning is founder-driven | Brand deck exists but dated; no brand audit in 12+ months; sales creates own materials |
| 3 | Defined | Clear positioning, documented guidelines, and brand governance process | Updated brand book; brand council or review process; consistent visual identity |
| 4 | Managed | Brand tracked with awareness metrics; competitive positioning regularly updated | Brand tracking surveys; win/loss analysis includes brand factors; executive brand dashboard |
| 5 | Optimized | Brand drives measurable pipeline impact; brand equity quantified and reported to board | Brand-to-demand attribution; earned media monitored; brand is a defensible competitive moat |

**Red flags**: Inconsistent messaging across website, sales deck, and ads; no documented value propositions; brand guidelines older than 2 years. [src1]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "Can three different team members describe your positioning the same way?"

### Dimension 2: Demand Generation

**What this measures**: The maturity of inbound and outbound demand creation, from lead acquisition through pipeline contribution.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | Demand gen is reactive; no systematic lead generation; reliant on founder network | No MQL definitions; no lead scoring; marketing does not own pipeline targets |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic inbound running (blog, SEO, some paid); MQL definition exists but not enforced | 1-2 active channels; MQL volume tracked but not quality; no conversion rate analysis |
| 3 | Defined | Multi-channel demand gen with MQL/SQL handoff process; marketing owns pipeline targets | 3-5 active channels; SLA with sales; MQL-to-SQL conversion tracked; funnel dashboards |
| 4 | Managed | Predictable pipeline from diversified channels; attribution model in place; CAC by channel tracked | Multi-touch attribution; channel-level ROI; marketing-sourced pipeline exceeds 40% of total |
| 5 | Optimized | AI-augmented demand gen; real-time optimization; integrated ABM+inbound motion | Predictive lead scoring; intent data integration; marketing-sourced pipeline exceeds 50%; CAC declining YoY |

**Red flags**: Marketing cannot report pipeline contribution; no lead scoring model; zero-to-hero dependency on a single channel (typically paid search). [src5]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "What percentage of sales pipeline is marketing-sourced, and can you break down CAC by channel?"

### Dimension 3: Content Operations

**What this measures**: The ability to systematically plan, produce, distribute, and measure content across the buyer journey.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | Content created reactively on request; no editorial calendar; no content strategy | No content calendar; content requests via email/Slack; no content audit ever performed |
| 2 | Emerging | Editorial calendar exists; some content mapped to funnel stages; basic blog program | Monthly blog cadence; content calendar in spreadsheet; 1-2 content types (blog, ebook) |
| 3 | Defined | Content strategy aligned to buyer journey; content governance process; multi-format production | Documented content strategy; content mapped to personas and stages; 4+ content formats active |
| 4 | Managed | Content performance measured by pipeline impact; content repurposing at scale; SEO-driven planning | Content attribution to pipeline; content scoring model; keyword-driven editorial planning |
| 5 | Optimized | AI-assisted content production; personalization at scale; content drives measurable revenue | AI tools for creation/optimization; dynamic content personalization; content ROI reported to C-suite |

**Red flags**: Content team is "order takers" for sales requests; no measurement beyond pageviews; content older than 18 months still live without review. [src4]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "Do you have a documented content strategy tied to buyer personas, and can you measure content's impact on pipeline?"

### Dimension 4: Analytics & Measurement

**What this measures**: The sophistication of marketing measurement, from basic reporting through predictive analytics and attribution.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No marketing dashboard; metrics pulled manually from individual platforms | Reporting takes days; metrics inconsistent between reports; no single source of truth |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic dashboards exist (Google Analytics, CRM reports); vanity metrics dominate | GA4 configured but underutilized; reports focus on impressions, clicks, MQLs without pipeline context |
| 3 | Defined | Marketing dashboard with funnel metrics; first-touch or last-touch attribution in place | Centralized dashboard; MQL-to-opportunity conversion tracked; monthly marketing review cadence |
| 4 | Managed | Multi-touch attribution; marketing mix modeling; experimentation framework | Multi-touch attribution platform; A/B testing program; incrementality testing; marketing-finance alignment |
| 5 | Optimized | Predictive analytics; real-time optimization; ML-driven budget allocation | Predictive models for pipeline; automated bid management; causal inference for channel effectiveness |

**Red flags**: Marketing cannot answer "What is our CAC?" within 24 hours; no attribution model; reporting cadence is quarterly or less. [src2]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "What attribution model do you use, and can you report marketing ROI by channel within a week?"

### Dimension 5: Marketing Operations & Technology

**What this measures**: The operational infrastructure — processes, technology stack, data management, and team enablement — that powers marketing execution.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No MAP; manual processes for email, lead routing; no marketing ops role | No marketing automation; leads manually entered; campaigns managed in spreadsheets |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic MAP implemented but underutilized; email automation only; no lead lifecycle management | MAP handles email only; no lead scoring; no lifecycle stages; <20% of MAP features used |
| 3 | Defined | MAP fully utilized; lead lifecycle defined; campaign operations standardized; data hygiene process | Lead scoring active; lifecycle stages in CRM; campaign templates standardized; quarterly data cleansing |
| 4 | Managed | Integrated martech stack; revenue operations alignment; automated workflows across systems | MAP-CRM-analytics integrated; RevOps function exists; SLA compliance monitored; martech utilization >60% |
| 5 | Optimized | AI-augmented operations; self-healing data pipelines; composable martech architecture | AI in lead routing/scoring; CDPs for unified profiles; composable architecture; martech ROI measured |

**Red flags**: MAP utilized at <30% of capability; no dedicated marketing ops person; CRM data quality below 70% accuracy; manual lead routing. [src3]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "What percentage of your MAP's capabilities do you actively use, and do you have a dedicated marketing ops role?"

### Dimension 6: Customer Engagement & Lifecycle

**What this measures**: How well marketing engages customers beyond acquisition — retention marketing, advocacy programs, expansion revenue support, and customer experience.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | Marketing stops at acquisition; no post-sale marketing programs; customer comms are transactional | No customer newsletter; no advocacy program; customer marketing = product updates only |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic customer communications exist; NPS or CSAT surveys running; some case studies produced | Quarterly customer newsletter; annual NPS survey; 3-5 case studies; no systematic review program |
| 3 | Defined | Customer marketing programs active; review generation process; customer segmentation for comms | Customer marketing role/budget; G2/Trustpilot review program; segmented customer nurtures |
| 4 | Managed | Customer lifecycle marketing integrated with CS; expansion revenue supported by marketing; advocacy program | Customer health scoring informs marketing; ABM for expansion; reference program; customer community |
| 5 | Optimized | Predictive churn marketing; personalized lifecycle journeys; customers are primary revenue growth lever | ML-driven churn prediction triggers campaigns; NRR >120% with marketing contribution tracked; advocacy drives >20% of new pipeline |

**Red flags**: Marketing team cannot name their top 10 customers; zero budget allocated to customer marketing; no customer advisory board or community. [src6]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "What percentage of your marketing budget and team is dedicated to post-acquisition customer engagement?"

## Scoring & Interpretation

### Overall Score Calculation

Use a weighted average. Brand and Demand Gen are weighted higher because they directly impact pipeline and revenue.

```
Overall Score = (Brand × 1.0 + Demand Gen × 1.5 + Content × 1.0 + Analytics × 1.25 + Ops × 1.25 + Customer × 1.0) / 7.0
```

### Score Interpretation

| Overall Score | Maturity Level | Interpretation | Recommended Next Step |
|---------------|---------------|----------------|----------------------|
| 1.0 - 1.9 | Critical | Marketing is reactive with no systematic processes; significant risk of wasted spend and missed pipeline | Start with marketing ops foundations — MAP implementation, basic analytics, MQL definitions |
| 2.0 - 2.9 | Developing | Basic infrastructure in place but underutilized; marketing operates in silos without pipeline accountability | Focus on demand gen process, analytics dashboards, and sales-marketing alignment |
| 3.0 - 3.9 | Competent | Solid foundation across dimensions; marketing contributes to pipeline but optimization opportunities exist | Optimize with multi-touch attribution, content performance measurement, and advanced automation |
| 4.0 - 4.5 | Advanced | Marketing is a predictable revenue engine; data-driven decision making across channels | Fine-tune with predictive analytics, AI augmentation, and composable martech architecture |
| 4.6 - 5.0 | Best-in-class | Marketing drives competitive advantage; fully integrated with revenue operations | Maintain through continuous innovation, AI-first operations, and industry thought leadership |

## Common Pitfalls in Assessment

- **Self-assessment bias**: Teams consistently over-score by 0.5-1.0 points. Calibrate by asking for evidence and artifacts, not opinions. For each dimension, ask "Show me the dashboard/document/process" rather than "How would you rate this?" [src2]
- **Snapshot fallacy**: A single assessment is a point-in-time snapshot. Track quarterly for trend analysis. A score of 3.0 trending upward from 2.5 is healthier than a 3.5 trending downward from 4.0.
- **Dimension independence assumption**: Low scores in one dimension are often caused by another dimension's weakness. Poor analytics (Dim 4) often masks weak demand gen (Dim 2) because you cannot see what is not working.
- **Tool-maturity conflation**: Having advanced tools does not equal operational maturity. A company with Marketo, 6sense, and Snowflake can still score 2.0 if utilization is below 30%. [src3]
- **Comparing across industries**: B2B SaaS benchmarks do not apply to e-commerce, healthcare, or financial services. Always use segment-appropriate thresholds.
