Type: Benchmark Confidence: 0.85 Sources: 6 Verified: 2026-03-09

Summary

These benchmarks cover marketing spend as a percentage of revenue, budget allocation across channels and cost categories, and stage-specific investment guidelines. Marketing budgets flatlined at 7.7% of revenue for the second consecutive year per the Gartner 2025 CMO survey, with paid media growing its share while martech, labor, and agencies all declined. [src1]

Data vintage: Q1 2025 Gartner CMO Spend Survey (402 CMOs).

Key shift: Paid media now commands 30.6% of budgets while martech (22.4%), labor (21.9%), and agencies (20.7%) all lost share.

Constraints

  • Gartner survey = large enterprises ($1B+ revenue). Do not apply directly to startups/SMBs.
  • Half of CMOs report budgets of 6% or less — 7.7% median masks significant dispersion.
  • B2B SaaS growth-stage typically spends 15-25% on S&M combined, much higher than enterprise median.
  • Channel allocation is annual average — quarters vary with seasonality.
  • Budget level does not determine effectiveness — allocation and execution matter more.

Common Misinterpretations

  • Enterprise benchmarks applied to startups: 7.7% is for $1B+ companies. Series A SaaS routinely spends 30-50% on S&M. [src1]
  • Budget = effectiveness: Spending more does not guarantee better results. Allocation and execution matter more than total spend.
  • Marketing vs S&M confusion: Marketing excludes sales team comp. Combined S&M is 2-3x marketing-only. Always clarify.
  • Ignoring composition: Two companies at 10% have different outcomes if one puts 50% into paid and the other 30% into content.
