# Marketing Budget Benchmarks 2026

## Summary

These benchmarks cover marketing spend as a percentage of revenue, budget allocation across channels and cost categories, and stage-specific investment guidelines. The primary data source is the Gartner 2025 CMO Spend Survey of 402 CMOs. The headline finding: marketing budgets flatlined at 7.7% of overall company revenue for the second consecutive year, with paid media growing its share while martech, labor, and agency allocations all declined. [src1]

**Data vintage**: Based on Q1 2025 Gartner CMO Spend Survey data and supplementary 2025 industry reports.
**Key shift**: Marketing budgets held at 7.7% of revenue while internal cost structures shifted — paid media now commands 30.6% of budgets while martech (22.4%), labor (21.9%), and agencies (20.7%) all lost share.

## Constraints
<!-- Agents: read before citing any benchmark number. -->

- Gartner survey represents large enterprises ($1B+ revenue, 402 CMOs, primarily North America/UK/Europe). Do not apply directly to startups or SMBs.
- Half of CMOs report budgets of 6% or less — the 7.7% median masks significant dispersion between budget-conscious (4% or less) and big spenders (10.5%+).
- B2B SaaS companies at growth stage typically spend 15-25% of revenue on sales and marketing combined, much higher than the Gartner enterprise median.
- Channel allocation percentages are annual averages — individual quarters may vary significantly due to seasonality.
- These figures are inputs to marketing budgets, not guaranteed outputs. Budget level does not determine marketing effectiveness — allocation quality and execution matter more.

## Metric Category 1: Total Marketing Spend as % of Revenue

### Overall Marketing Budget

**Definition**: Total marketing department budget (including labor, technology, agencies, paid media, content production, events) as a percentage of total company revenue.

| Segment | Median | 25th Percentile | 75th Percentile | Top Decile |
|---------|--------|-----------------|-----------------|------------|
| Enterprise ($1B+ revenue) | 7.7% | 4.0% | 10.5% | 14%+ |
| Growth SaaS ($20M-$100M ARR) | 20-30% | 15% | 35% | 40%+ |
| Series A-B SaaS ($1M-$20M ARR) | 25-40% | 20% | 50% | 60%+ |
| Pre-revenue/Seed | N/A (absolute $) | $5K/mo | $30K/mo | $50K+/mo |

**Trend**: Enterprise marketing budgets flatlined at 7.7% for two consecutive years (2024-2025), down from pre-pandemic levels of 10-11%. Growth-stage SaaS budgets compressed slightly as the efficiency mandate strengthened. [src1]
**Red flag threshold**: Enterprise below 4% signals marketing is not a growth driver. Growth SaaS above 45% without corresponding pipeline growth signals inefficient spend.
**Action trigger**: If budget is below 25th percentile for your segment and pipeline is underperforming, present business case for budget increase.

[src1, src2]

### Marketing Budget by Industry

**Definition**: Marketing spend as percentage of total company revenue, segmented by industry vertical.

| Industry | Median (% of Revenue) | Range |
|----------|-----------------------|-------|
| Technology/Software | 8-15% | 5-25% |
| Professional Services | 6-10% | 4-15% |
| Financial Services | 7-12% | 5-18% |
| Healthcare/Pharma | 5-8% | 3-12% |
| Manufacturing | 3-5% | 2-8% |
| Consumer Goods/Retail | 8-14% | 5-20% |
| E-commerce/DTC | 15-25% | 10-35% |
| Education | 5-10% | 3-15% |

**Trend**: Technology and e-commerce lead in marketing intensity; manufacturing consistently lowest. [src3]
**Red flag threshold**: Spending significantly above industry range without proportional revenue growth.

[src1, src3]

## Metric Category 2: Budget Allocation by Cost Category

### Internal Cost Structure

**Definition**: How the total marketing budget is allocated across major cost categories — paid media, technology, labor, and agencies.

| Cost Category | 2025 Share | 2024 Share | Trend |
|--------------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Paid media | 30.6% | 27.9% | Up — largest category and growing |
| Marketing technology (martech) | 22.4% | 23.8% | Down — utilization concerns driving consolidation |
| Labor (internal headcount) | 21.9% | 24.4% | Down — AI and automation reducing headcount needs |
| Agencies (external) | 20.7% | 23.9% | Down — in-housing trend + AI replacing agency work |
| Other (events, research, content prod.) | 4.4% | — | Stable |

**Trend**: Paid media is the only growing category. CMOs are consolidating martech, reducing headcount (39% plan labor cost cuts), and cutting agency allocations. [src1, src6]
**Red flag threshold**: Labor below 15% signals insufficient in-house capability; agencies above 30% signals over-reliance on external resources.

[src1, src6]

### Martech Spend Breakdown

**Definition**: Marketing technology budget allocation across martech subcategories.

| Martech Subcategory | Typical Allocation |
|--------------------|-------------------|
| Marketing automation platform (MAP) | 25-35% of martech |
| Analytics and attribution | 15-20% of martech |
| CMS and web experience | 10-15% of martech |
| SEO and content tools | 8-12% of martech |
| ABM and intent data | 10-15% of martech |
| Social media management | 5-8% of martech |
| Data infrastructure (CDP, ETL) | 10-15% of martech |

**Red flag threshold**: MAP exceeding 40% of martech budget suggests over-investment in one tool or under-investment in analytics and data. [src4]

## Metric Category 3: Channel Allocation

### Digital Marketing Channel Budget Allocation

**Definition**: How the paid media and channel-specific marketing budget is allocated across channels.

| Channel | Typical Allocation (B2B) | Typical Allocation (B2C/DTC) |
|---------|------------------------|---------------------------|
| Paid search (Google/Bing) | 20-25% | 25-30% |
| Paid social (LinkedIn, Meta, etc.) | 15-20% | 20-25% |
| Content marketing & SEO | 25-30% | 15-20% |
| Email marketing | 10-15% | 10-15% |
| Events and webinars | 10-15% | 5-10% |
| Display/programmatic | 5-10% | 10-15% |
| Influencer/partnerships | 3-5% | 10-15% |

**Trend**: Content and SEO allocation increasing as organic channel ROI outperforms paid; event budgets recovering to 80% of pre-pandemic levels. [src5]
**Red flag threshold**: Single channel exceeding 40% of total channel budget creates dangerous dependency.

[src3, src5]

### The 70/20/10 Framework

**Definition**: Budget allocation framework for balancing proven strategies, emerging tactics, and experimental approaches.

| Category | Allocation | Description |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Proven strategies | 70% | Core channels with established ROI — SEO, email, proven paid campaigns |
| Emerging tactics | 20% | Channels with some track record but still being optimized — ABM, content syndication, AI-generated content |
| Experimental | 10% | New, high-risk innovations — AI agents, community-led growth, new platform early adoption |

**Constraint**: This framework applies to channel allocation within the marketing budget, not to the total budget itself. Companies below $5M ARR may need a more aggressive experimental allocation (15-20%) to find product-market fit for their marketing. [src5]

[src5]

## Composite Metrics & Rules of Thumb

| Rule | Formula / Threshold | Interpretation |
|------|--------------------|----------------|
| S&M as % of revenue (SaaS) | Total sales + marketing / revenue | Seed: 80-120%; Series A: 60-80%; Series B: 40-60%; Growth: 30-50%; Public: 20-35% |
| Marketing as % of S&M | Marketing budget / total S&M budget | Typically 35-45% of combined S&M; below 30% = under-invested in marketing |
| Martech as % of total budget | Martech spend / marketing budget | Healthy: 20-25%; Above 30% = over-tooled; Below 15% = under-invested in infrastructure |
| Agency dependency ratio | Agency spend / (agency + labor) | Below 0.3 = healthy in-housing; Above 0.5 = over-reliant on agencies |
| Paid media efficiency | Pipeline from paid / paid media spend | Below 3:1 = inefficient; 5:1+ = healthy; 10:1+ = exceptional |

**Constraint**: S&M ratios for SaaS companies are much higher than traditional enterprises because of the land-and-expand model. A SaaS company spending 40% of revenue on S&M may be more efficient than an enterprise spending 8% — context matters. [src1]

## Segment Definitions

| Segment | Definition | Typical Characteristics |
|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| Pre-revenue/Seed | Pre-product-market fit, <$1M ARR | Marketing = founder-led; budget is absolute dollars not % of revenue; focus on validation |
| Series A-B | $1M-$20M ARR, product-market fit established | Marketing team 2-8 people; high % of revenue to S&M; testing channels to find scalable motions |
| Growth Stage | $20M-$100M ARR, scaling repeatable motions | Marketing team 10-30; dedicated marketing ops; budget optimization important; multi-channel |
| Scale/Enterprise | $100M+ ARR or $1B+ revenue | Full marketing org; VP/C-level marketing leader; budget efficiency critical; global operations |

## Common Misinterpretations

- **Applying enterprise benchmarks to startups**: Enterprise budgets at 7.7% of revenue are completely inapplicable to Series A SaaS companies that routinely spend 30-50% of revenue on S&M. Always match company stage to the right benchmark set. [src1]
- **Budget level equals marketing effectiveness**: Spending more does not guarantee better results. A company spending 5% of revenue with excellent channel mix and execution can outperform one spending 15% with poor allocation. Focus on efficiency metrics alongside budget levels.
- **Confusing marketing budget with total S&M**: Marketing budget excludes sales team compensation in most benchmarks. Sales and marketing combined typically runs 2-3x the marketing-only budget. Always clarify which metric is being discussed.
- **Ignoring budget composition**: Two companies spending 10% of revenue on marketing can have completely different compositions. One spending 50% on paid media and 10% on content will have very different results than one spending 30% on content and 15% on paid. Composition matters more than total.
