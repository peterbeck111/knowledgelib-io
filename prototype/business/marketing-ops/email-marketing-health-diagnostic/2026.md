# Email Marketing Health Diagnostic

## Purpose

This assessment evaluates the health of a company's email marketing program across five critical dimensions: deliverability and authentication, list hygiene and growth, engagement and performance, automation and lifecycle, and compliance and privacy. The output is a composite health score (1-5) that pinpoints where the email program is leaking value — whether through inbox placement failures, list decay, engagement erosion, or compliance risk. Use this when email ROI is declining, deliverability issues are suspected, or as part of a periodic marketing ops review. [src1]

## Constraints
<!-- Agents: read before running this assessment with a user. -->

- Requires ESP analytics access and at least 3 months of send history for reliable scoring
- Open rates are unreliable as a standalone metric due to Apple MPP inflating opens by 30-40% — use click rates and conversion rates as primary engagement signals
- Deliverability benchmarks vary by ESP, industry, and sending volume — use as directional guides
- Assessment is diagnostic only — it identifies health issues but does not prescribe specific campaign strategies
- Re-run quarterly; email health can degrade rapidly after list purchases, domain changes, or ESP migrations

## Assessment Dimensions

<!-- Each dimension is scored independently. The structured format lets agents
     walk through this conversationally with a user, one dimension at a time. -->

### Dimension 1: Deliverability & Authentication

**What this measures**: Whether emails actually reach the inbox and whether the sending infrastructure is properly authenticated and trusted by mailbox providers.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No authentication configured; deliverability not monitored; high bounce rates (>5%); emails frequently land in spam | No SPF, DKIM, or DMARC records; bounce rate unknown; spam complaint rate not tracked; no seed testing |
| 2 | Emerging | SPF and DKIM configured but DMARC missing or set to none; deliverability checked sporadically; bounce rate 2-5% | Basic authentication in place but not enforced; occasional spam folder reports; no dedicated sending IP |
| 3 | Defined | SPF, DKIM, and DMARC (quarantine or reject) all configured; deliverability monitoring active; bounce rate <2%; dedicated sending infrastructure | DMARC at p=quarantine minimum; inbox placement monitored monthly; bounce handling automated; sending reputation tracked |
| 4 | Managed | BIMI implemented for brand visibility; DMARC at p=reject; deliverability monitored in real-time across major ISPs; hard bounce rate <0.5% | BIMI logo displays in supported inboxes; real-time ISP monitoring; sender reputation dashboards; proactive IP warming for new sends |
| 5 | Optimized | Automated deliverability optimization; predictive send-time and ISP-level throttling; 98%+ inbox placement rate; domain reputation management automated | AI-driven send optimization per ISP; automated IP rotation for high volume; near-perfect inbox placement; postmaster tools integrated |

**Red flags**: No DMARC record or DMARC set to p=none; bounce rate above 3%; no idea what percentage of emails reach inbox vs spam; using a shared sending domain with no subdomain isolation. [src1]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "What is your current DMARC policy, and do you monitor inbox placement rates across Gmail, Outlook, and Yahoo?"

### Dimension 2: List Hygiene & Growth

**What this measures**: The health, growth rate, and maintenance practices of the email subscriber list.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | List never cleaned; purchased or scraped lists used; no opt-in verification; list age and quality unknown | Hard bounces accumulate; no unsubscribe processing; list includes addresses from 3+ years ago with no engagement |
| 2 | Emerging | Hard bounces removed after sends; basic unsubscribe processing works; but no proactive list cleaning; growth via single opt-in only | Bounces cleaned reactively; some inactive subscribers identified but not acted upon; no re-engagement campaigns |
| 3 | Defined | Quarterly list hygiene runs removing invalid and inactive addresses; double opt-in implemented; sunset policy for unengaged subscribers (90-180 days) | List verification service used periodically; inactive subscribers suppressed or targeted with re-engagement; organic growth rate positive |
| 4 | Managed | Real-time email validation at point of capture; automated sunset flows; list segmented by engagement recency; monthly hygiene scoring | Every new signup validated immediately; automated 90-day sunset workflow; engagement-based segmentation drives send frequency |
| 5 | Optimized | Predictive churn scoring identifies at-risk subscribers before disengagement; list growth exceeds churn rate by 2x+; zero purchased lists; organic growth engine automated | ML predicts disengagement 30 days ahead; list growth from content-led acquisition; preference centers optimize frequency and topic |

**Red flags**: Any list purchases in the past 12 months; no unsubscribe processing; hard bounce rate above 2%; email list has not been cleaned in 6+ months; no double opt-in or verification at signup. [src4]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "When was the last time you cleaned your email list, and what percentage of your list has not opened or clicked in the past 6 months?"

### Dimension 3: Engagement & Performance

**What this measures**: How well email content resonates with subscribers, measured through click-through rates, conversion rates, and revenue attribution.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No engagement tracking beyond opens; click rates below 1%; no revenue attribution; blast-and-pray sending | Same content to entire list; no segmentation; no A/B testing; unsubscribe rate above 1% per send |
| 2 | Emerging | Click rates tracked (1-3%); basic segmentation (1-3 segments); occasional A/B testing on subject lines | Some segmentation by demographics; sporadic testing; conversion tracking exists but is incomplete |
| 3 | Defined | Click rates 3-6%; systematic A/B testing on subject, content, and timing; 5-10 segments based on behavior; revenue per email tracked | Regular testing cadence; behavioral segmentation active; email revenue contribution measured monthly; unsubscribe rate below 0.3% |
| 4 | Managed | Click rates 5-8%; dynamic content personalization; AI-driven send-time optimization; revenue per subscriber tracked by segment | Personalized product recommendations; predictive send timing; email contributes measurable percentage of total revenue |
| 5 | Optimized | Click rates above 6% for campaigns, above 10% for flows; fully personalized 1:1 content; email drives 20-30% of total digital revenue | AI-generated content variants per segment; real-time personalization; email is a primary revenue channel with clear attribution |

**Red flags**: Click rate below 1% consistently; unsubscribe rate above 0.5% per send; no A/B testing in 6+ months; no revenue attribution for email channel; entire list receives identical content. [src2]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "What is your average click-through rate, and can you tell me how much revenue email generated last month?"

### Dimension 4: Automation & Lifecycle

**What this measures**: The sophistication of automated email workflows that respond to subscriber behavior and lifecycle stages.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No automated emails; all sends are manual campaigns; no welcome series or transactional triggers | Every email is manually created and sent; new subscribers receive nothing until next campaign; no cart abandonment emails |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic welcome email (1-2 messages); transactional emails exist; 1-2 automated flows total | Simple welcome email; order confirmation; but no behavior-triggered sequences; automation covers <10% of sends |
| 3 | Defined | Welcome series (3-5 emails); cart abandonment flow; win-back campaign for lapsed customers; post-purchase sequence; automated flows generate 20-30% of email revenue | 4-6 core lifecycle flows active; triggered emails outperform campaigns on engagement; basic lead nurture for B2B |
| 4 | Managed | 10+ automated flows covering full lifecycle; behavior-triggered sequences for browse abandonment, re-engagement, cross-sell; flows generate 30-50% of email revenue | Comprehensive flow library; flows A/B tested and optimized quarterly; multi-step conditional logic based on engagement |
| 5 | Optimized | AI-powered journey orchestration; predictive triggers for churn prevention, upsell timing; flows generate 40-60% of email revenue; dynamic flow branching based on real-time behavior | AI determines optimal next email per subscriber; predictive churn triggers; flows continuously self-optimizing; email contributes to full customer lifecycle |

**Red flags**: No welcome email or series; no cart/browse abandonment recovery; 100% of email revenue comes from manual campaigns; automated flows have not been reviewed or updated in 12+ months. [src3]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "How many automated email flows are active, and what percentage of your email revenue comes from automated vs manual campaigns?"

### Dimension 5: Compliance & Privacy

**What this measures**: How well the email program adheres to global email regulations and respects subscriber privacy preferences.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No compliance awareness; no unsubscribe mechanism or non-functional; no consent records; sending to purchased lists | Missing or broken unsubscribe links; no consent audit trail; CAN-SPAM physical address missing; GDPR consent not captured for EU subscribers |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic CAN-SPAM compliance (unsubscribe link, physical address); but GDPR/CASL requirements not fully addressed; consent records incomplete | Unsubscribe works but takes >10 days; no granular consent tracking; EU subscribers not differentiated; no preference center |
| 3 | Defined | Fully compliant with CAN-SPAM, GDPR, and CASL; consent records stored with timestamps; one-click unsubscribe implemented; preference center available | Consent tracked per subscriber with date and source; unsubscribe processed within 24 hours; preference center offers frequency/topic choices |
| 4 | Managed | Automated compliance monitoring; jurisdiction-aware sending rules; regular compliance audits; data retention policies enforced | Automated rules prevent sending to unsubscribed or non-consented; quarterly compliance audit; data deletion requests processed automatically |
| 5 | Optimized | Privacy-first email strategy; real-time consent management across all channels; proactive compliance with emerging regulations; privacy as competitive advantage | Zero-party data collection strategy; cross-channel consent sync; ahead of regulatory requirements; privacy commitment visible to subscribers |

**Red flags**: Unsubscribe link missing or hidden; no GDPR consent records for EU subscribers; unsubscribe requests take more than 10 days; no physical address in emails; purchased list used in past 12 months. [src5]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "Can you show me the consent record for a random subscriber, including when and how they opted in?"

## Scoring & Interpretation

### Overall Score Calculation

All dimensions weighted equally for a general assessment. Weight Deliverability and Compliance more heavily (1.5x) for companies with recent deliverability problems or operating in highly regulated industries.

```
Overall Score = (Deliverability + List Hygiene + Engagement + Automation + Compliance) / 5
```

### Score Interpretation

| Overall Score | Maturity Level | Interpretation | Recommended Next Step |
|---------------|---------------|----------------|----------------------|
| 1.0 - 1.9 | Critical | Email program is actively damaging sender reputation and may face regulatory penalties; immediate remediation required | Fix authentication (SPF/DKIM/DMARC), clean list, implement unsubscribe; stop sending until deliverability is stabilized |
| 2.0 - 2.9 | Developing | Basic email operations functional but significant value leakage through poor deliverability, engagement, or compliance gaps | Implement quarterly list hygiene, build welcome series, establish testing cadence, ensure GDPR compliance |
| 3.0 - 3.9 | Competent | Healthy email program with solid foundations; ready for optimization through automation, personalization, and advanced analytics | Scale automation to 6-10 flows, implement behavioral segmentation, add revenue attribution |
| 4.0 - 4.5 | Advanced | High-performing email program generating measurable revenue; focus on marginal gains through AI and personalization | Deploy AI-driven personalization, predictive send timing, advanced lifecycle automation |
| 4.6 - 5.0 | Best-in-class | Email is a primary revenue driver with best-in-class deliverability, engagement, and compliance; focus is innovation | Explore AI content generation, cross-channel orchestration, predictive churn prevention |

## Common Pitfalls in Assessment

- **Open rate reliance**: Apple Mail Privacy Protection inflates open rates by 30-40%. Teams that score engagement based on open rates overestimate health. Use click rates and conversion rates as primary engagement signals. [src3]
- **Vanity list size**: A large list is not a healthy list. A 500K list with 20% engaged subscribers is less valuable than a 100K list with 60% engaged. Assess list quality, not just quantity.
- **Compliance complacency**: CAN-SPAM compliance alone is insufficient for companies with EU customers. GDPR requires explicit opt-in consent with audit trail — implicit consent or pre-checked boxes create legal exposure.
- **Authentication set-and-forget**: DMARC, SPF, and DKIM require ongoing monitoring as sending infrastructure changes. A configuration that was correct 12 months ago may be broken after ESP changes or new sending services.
