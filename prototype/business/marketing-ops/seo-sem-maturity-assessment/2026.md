# SEO/SEM Maturity Assessment

## Purpose

This assessment evaluates the maturity of an organization's search marketing program across technical SEO health, keyword and content strategy, paid search efficiency, analytics infrastructure, and AI search readiness. It is designed for marketing leaders, SEO managers, and digital marketing directors who need to diagnose gaps in their search visibility strategy and prioritize investments. The output identifies which dimensions need immediate attention and routes to specific improvement playbooks. [src3]

## Constraints
<!-- Agents: read before running this assessment with a user. -->

- Requires access to Google Search Console (minimum 3 months data), GA4, and ideally an enterprise SEO crawl tool (Screaming Frog, Ahrefs, or SEMrush)
- Technical SEO dimension requires a site crawl completed within the last 30 days — stale crawl data produces inaccurate scoring
- Paid search dimension requires Google Ads or Microsoft Ads account access with 90+ days of campaign data; skip this dimension if no paid search program exists
- Does NOT cover social media marketing, email marketing, or content quality — those are separate assessments
- Re-run quarterly; mandatory reassessment after Google core updates, site migrations, or major algorithm changes

## Assessment Dimensions

<!-- Each dimension is scored independently. The structured format lets agents
     walk through this conversationally with a user, one dimension at a time. -->

### Dimension 1: Technical SEO Health

**What this measures**: The structural and technical foundation that enables search engines to crawl, index, and rank the site effectively — including Core Web Vitals, crawlability, indexation, and site architecture.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No technical SEO awareness; site has major crawlability issues, no sitemap, broken robots.txt | Hundreds of 404 errors; no XML sitemap; CWV scores failing on all three metrics; no HTTPS |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic technical SEO in place but significant issues remain — slow pages, missing meta tags, thin content duplication | XML sitemap exists but stale; CWV partially passing; 20%+ pages have duplicate titles or missing meta descriptions |
| 3 | Defined | Technical foundation solid — CWV passing, clean sitemap, proper canonicals, structured data on key pages | CWV passing on 75%+ pages; structured data (Schema.org) on product/article pages; mobile-friendly; crawl budget managed |
| 4 | Managed | Automated technical monitoring; real-time alerts for crawl errors, indexation drops, and CWV regressions | Automated SEO monitoring (ContentKing, Lumar, or similar); log file analysis; proactive technical debt management |
| 5 | Optimized | Technical SEO fully automated with CI/CD integration; edge SEO capabilities; international SEO (hreflang) fully implemented | SEO tests in CI/CD pipeline; automated pre-deployment crawl checks; sub-second LCP; JS rendering fully handled [src1] |

**Red flags**: Core Web Vitals failing on mobile; no XML sitemap in Search Console; robots.txt blocking important pages; site not mobile-responsive. [src3]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "When was the last time someone ran a full technical SEO crawl of your site, and what were the top 3 issues found?"

### Dimension 2: Keyword & Content Strategy

**What this measures**: The sophistication of keyword research, search intent mapping, content gap analysis, and topical authority building.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No formal keyword strategy; content created based on internal assumptions, not search demand | No keyword research tools in use; content topics chosen by HiPPO (Highest Paid Person's Opinion); no rank tracking |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic keyword research exists; primary pages target head terms but no long-tail or intent-based strategy | Spreadsheet with 20-50 keywords tracked; no content gap analysis; keyword-to-page mapping incomplete |
| 3 | Defined | Comprehensive keyword universe mapped by intent (informational, commercial, transactional); content calendar aligned to keyword strategy | 200+ keywords tracked; content mapped to buyer journey stages; topic clusters established; search intent drives content type selection |
| 4 | Managed | Competitive keyword gap analysis drives content priorities; topical authority measured and managed; SERP feature targeting (featured snippets, PAA) | Regular competitive gap analysis; topic cluster performance dashboard; SERP feature capture rate tracked; entity-based SEO strategy |
| 5 | Optimized | AI-augmented keyword discovery; real-time SERP monitoring; predictive ranking models; programmatic content at scale for long-tail | ML-powered keyword opportunity scoring; automated content briefs from SERP analysis; entity graph maps the full topical landscape [src6] |

**Red flags**: Cannot name the top 10 keywords driving organic traffic; no content gap analysis completed in 12+ months; keyword strategy document does not exist or is over 1 year old. [src2]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "Do you have a documented keyword strategy that maps keywords to search intent and buyer journey stage, and when was it last updated?"

### Dimension 3: Paid Search (SEM) Efficiency

**What this measures**: The effectiveness and sophistication of paid search campaigns — from basic campaign structure to automated bidding, audience targeting, and ROAS optimization.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No paid search program, or running with minimal budget and no optimization; wasted spend on broad match without negative keywords | Broad match keywords with no negatives; single ad group campaigns; no conversion tracking; CPC and CTR unknown |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic campaign structure exists; some conversion tracking; manual bidding with infrequent optimization | Account has proper campaign structure; basic conversion tracking; optimized monthly at best; Quality Score below 5 on most keywords |
| 3 | Defined | Well-structured campaigns by intent; automated bidding (Target CPA or Target ROAS); negative keyword lists maintained; A/B testing on ad copy | Automated bidding active; Quality Score averaging 6-7; regular negative keyword mining; responsive search ads tested; ROAS tracked |
| 4 | Managed | Advanced audience layering; RLSA campaigns; cross-channel attribution connecting paid to organic; branded vs non-branded efficiency tracked | Audience segments layered on campaigns; branded CPL vs non-branded CPL tracked separately; Google Ads scripts automate monitoring |
| 5 | Optimized | AI-driven bid management; predictive budget allocation; Performance Max campaigns optimized; paid-organic integration maximizes total search share | ML-powered bid optimization; daypart and geo-bid adjustments automated; total search share tracked (paid + organic); incremental lift testing validates paid spend [src1] |

**Red flags**: No conversion tracking configured; same ad copy running for 6+ months without testing; Search Terms Report has not been reviewed in 90+ days; no negative keyword list.
**Quick diagnostic question**: "What is your average Quality Score, and when was the last time you reviewed your Search Terms Report for wasted spend?"

### Dimension 4: Analytics & Measurement Infrastructure

**What this measures**: The depth and reliability of search analytics infrastructure — from basic traffic reporting to predictive forecasting and attribution.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | Only GA4 pageview data; no Search Console integration; organic and paid search data not connected | Cannot separate branded vs non-branded organic traffic; no click-through rate or position data analyzed |
| 2 | Emerging | GA4 + Search Console connected; basic organic traffic reporting; paid search reporting in Google Ads only | Monthly organic traffic report exists; keyword rankings tracked but not analyzed for trends; paid and organic data in separate silos |
| 3 | Defined | Integrated search dashboard combining organic + paid data; keyword portfolio segmented by brand/non-brand; conversion tracking reliable | Combined SEO + SEM dashboard; rank tracking with trend analysis; organic and paid cannibalization analyzed; reliable goal/conversion setup |
| 4 | Managed | Search share of voice tracked; forecasting models predict organic traffic growth; attribution connects search to pipeline/revenue | SOV dashboard shows competitive position; organic traffic forecasting with confidence intervals; search-to-revenue attribution model |
| 5 | Optimized | AI-powered search analytics; real-time anomaly detection; predictive ranking models; AI search visibility (AEO) tracking integrated | Automated anomaly alerts; ML ranking predictions; AI overview appearance rate tracked; conversational search visibility measured [src5] |

**Red flags**: GA4 not properly configured (missing events, broken filters); no rank tracking tool in use; organic and paid teams do not share data or dashboards. [src4]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "Do you have a single dashboard that shows both organic and paid search performance together, including revenue attribution?"

### Dimension 5: AI Search Readiness

**What this measures**: How prepared the organization is for AI-driven search — including AI Overviews, LLM citations, answer engine optimization (AEO), and structured data for AI consumption.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No awareness of AI search impact; strategy is entirely traditional blue-link SEO | No discussion of AI Overviews, Perplexity, or ChatGPT search in strategy documents; no structured data beyond basics |
| 2 | Emerging | Awareness of AI search exists but no tactical response; monitoring AI Overview appearances passively | Team reads about AI search trends but has not changed content strategy; basic Schema.org markup exists |
| 3 | Defined | Content structured for AI consumption (clear Q&A format, factual density); AI Overview tracking integrated; entity markup expanded | Content formatted with clear heading hierarchy and direct answers; FAQ schema on relevant pages; AI citation monitoring active |
| 4 | Managed | Active AEO strategy; content optimized for LLM citation; brand mentions in AI responses tracked; structured data comprehensive | Brand appearing in AI Overviews for target queries; E-E-A-T signals strong; AI citation rate tracked monthly; knowledge panels claimed |
| 5 | Optimized | AI search strategy fully integrated with traditional SEO; brand is a cited authority in LLM responses; AI-specific content formats deployed | Machine-readable knowledge base published; API endpoints for AI agents; brand cited across multiple AI platforms; AI search drives measurable traffic [src5] |

**Red flags**: Team has never checked if the brand appears in AI Overviews for core queries; no Schema.org markup beyond basic organization; content written as marketing copy rather than factual, citable information.
**Quick diagnostic question**: "Have you checked how your brand appears in AI Overviews, ChatGPT, and Perplexity for your top 10 keywords?"

## Scoring & Interpretation

### Overall Score Calculation

Weighted average that gives higher weight to technical foundation and AI readiness given the 2025-2026 search landscape.

```
Overall Score = (Technical SEO Health × 1.2 + Keyword Strategy × 1.0 + SEM Efficiency × 0.8 + Analytics × 1.0 + AI Search Readiness × 1.0) / 5.0
```

### Score Interpretation

| Overall Score | Maturity Level | Interpretation | Recommended Next Step |
|---------------|---------------|----------------|----------------------|
| 1.0 - 1.9 | Critical | Search marketing is not a functional capability; significant traffic and revenue left on table | Fix technical SEO foundations; implement basic keyword tracking; configure conversion tracking |
| 2.0 - 2.9 | Developing | Basic search presence exists but major gaps in either technical health, strategy, or measurement | Address weakest dimension first; typically technical SEO health or analytics infrastructure |
| 3.0 - 3.9 | Competent | Solid search program in place; organization is competitive but not yet optimizing or adapting to AI search | Focus on competitive gap analysis, predictive analytics, and AI search readiness |
| 4.0 - 4.5 | Advanced | Search is a well-managed acquisition channel; optimization focus should be on AI search and cross-channel integration | Implement AEO strategy; predictive models; total search share optimization |
| 4.6 - 5.0 | Best-in-class | Search is a dominant acquisition channel with AI readiness; maintain edge through innovation | Pioneer AI search monetization; establish brand as citable authority across AI platforms |

## Common Pitfalls in Assessment

- **Technical debt denial**: Teams score technical SEO at 3-4 but have never run a comprehensive crawl. Validate by asking for the last crawl report and its error count. [src3]
- **Vanity ranking focus**: Tracking rankings for 10 vanity keywords while ignoring the long-tail that drives 70%+ of organic traffic. Score keyword strategy based on coverage, not trophy keywords.
- **Paid-organic silos**: SEM and SEO teams operate independently with no shared dashboard or strategy. This creates keyword cannibalization and wasted budget on terms that rank organically.
- **AI search blindness**: Organizations that score 4+ on traditional SEO but 1-2 on AI search readiness face significant disruption risk as AI search grows from 10% to 30%+ of queries in 2025-2026. [src5]
