# Marketing Metrics Benchmarks 2026

## Summary

These benchmarks cover customer acquisition cost (CAC), conversion rates (lead-to-MQL, MQL-to-SQL, SQL-to-close), channel-level performance, and marketing efficiency metrics across B2B segments. Data is drawn from multiple 2025 industry reports covering 400-1,500+ companies. The most critical shift: CAC rose 14% YoY through 2025 while overall growth slowed, creating an efficiency squeeze that is separating sustainable businesses from those burning cash on acquisition. [src1]

**Data vintage**: Based on H1-Q4 2025 data from multiple industry reports covering B2B SaaS and technology companies.
**Key shift**: Median CAC increased 14% YoY driven by rising paid channel costs, while organic channel conversion rates remained stable — accelerating the shift toward content-led and PLG acquisition models.

## Constraints
<!-- Agents: read before citing any benchmark number. -->

- These benchmarks represent B2B SaaS and technology companies primarily in the US and Western Europe. Do not apply to B2C, healthcare, or financial services without significant adjustment.
- Figures are medians unless labeled otherwise. Mean is skewed by outliers; use median for planning, 75th percentile for ambitious targets.
- Primarily US data. Adjust CAC +15-25% for Western Europe, -20-30% for India/Southeast Asia.
- Data collected H1-Q4 2025. If more than 9 months old when citing, search for updated figures before using.
- Only compare companies within the same segment row. Cross-segment comparison (e.g., SMB CAC vs enterprise CAC) is misleading.

## Metric Category 1: Acquisition Metrics

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

**Definition**: Total sales + marketing spend divided by new customers acquired in the period. Include salaries, tools, ad spend, content production, and events. Exclude customer success and onboarding costs.

| Segment | Median | 25th Percentile | 75th Percentile | Top Decile |
|---------|--------|-----------------|-----------------|------------|
| SMB/PLG (ACV <$10K) | $200-$500 | $150 | $800 | $120 |
| Mid-Market ($10K-$100K ACV) | $500-$1,000 | $350 | $1,500 | $250 |
| Enterprise ($100K+ ACV) | $1,000-$5,000 | $800 | $8,000 | $600 |
| All B2B SaaS (blended) | $1,200 | $600 | $2,500 | $400 |

**Trend**: Up 14% YoY driven by rising paid channel costs and increased competition for keywords. [src1]
**Red flag threshold**: CAC exceeding 12-month contract value signals unsustainable acquisition economics.
**Action trigger**: If CAC > 18-month payback, review channel mix and shift toward organic/PLG motions.

[src1, src6]

### CAC by Channel

**Definition**: Fully loaded customer acquisition cost attributed to the primary acquisition channel, including proportional overhead.

| Channel | Median CAC (B2B SaaS) | Relative Efficiency |
|---------|----------------------|---------------------|
| Referral/word-of-mouth | $150 | Most efficient |
| Organic search (SEO) | $480-$942 | High efficiency (drops to $290 at scale) |
| Content marketing | $500-$700 | High efficiency, slow ramp |
| Email marketing | $400-$600 | High efficiency for existing database |
| Paid search (Google Ads) | $802 | Moderate efficiency |
| LinkedIn paid | $1,100-$1,500 | Lower efficiency, higher intent |
| Paid social (other) | $700-$1,000 | Moderate efficiency |
| Events/conferences | $1,500-$3,000 | Low efficiency, high ACV deals |

**Red flag threshold**: Any single channel exceeding 50% of total acquisition spend creates dangerous dependency. [src1]
**Action trigger**: If paid channel CAC exceeds 2x organic channel CAC, increase organic investment.

[src1, src3]

### Cost Per Lead (CPL)

**Definition**: Total channel spend divided by leads generated. Includes ad spend, content production, and channel-specific overhead. Does not include sales costs.

| Channel | Median CPL (B2B) |
|---------|------------------|
| SEO/Organic search | $50-$100 |
| Content syndication | $100-$200 |
| Google Ads (Search) | $70 |
| LinkedIn Ads | $110 |
| Facebook/Instagram Ads | $40-$80 |
| Webinars | $50-$100 |
| Events/conferences | $200-$500 |

**Trend**: LinkedIn CPL up 18% YoY; Google Ads CPL up 12% YoY; organic channels stable. [src3]
**Red flag threshold**: CPL exceeding $300 for SMB targets (ACV <$10K) signals channel mismatch.

[src3]

## Metric Category 2: Conversion Metrics

### Lead-to-MQL Conversion Rate

**Definition**: Percentage of raw leads (form fills, trial signups, content downloads) that meet marketing qualification criteria. Criteria vary by organization but typically include firmographic fit and engagement threshold.

| Industry/Segment | Median | Top Quartile |
|-----------------|--------|-------------|
| B2B SaaS | 31% | 45% |
| B2B Technology | 28% | 40% |
| Professional Services | 35% | 50% |
| Manufacturing | 22% | 35% |
| Financial Services | 18% | 30% |

**Trend**: Stable YoY; AI-powered lead scoring improving conversion for early adopters. [src5]
**Red flag threshold**: Below 15% indicates either poor targeting or overly strict MQL criteria.

[src5]

### MQL-to-SQL Conversion Rate

**Definition**: Percentage of marketing qualified leads accepted by sales as sales qualified leads. This is the handoff metric and the biggest bottleneck in most funnels.

| Industry/Segment | Median | Top Quartile |
|-----------------|--------|-------------|
| B2B SaaS | 13% | 25% |
| B2B Technology | 15% | 28% |
| Professional Services | 18% | 30% |
| Manufacturing | 10% | 20% |
| Financial Services | 12% | 22% |

**By acquisition channel**:

| Channel | MQL-to-SQL Rate |
|---------|----------------|
| SEO/Organic | 51% |
| Email marketing | 46% |
| Webinars | 30% |
| Paid search (PPC) | 26% |
| Social media | 18% |
| Content syndication | 6-8% |

**Trend**: SEO-sourced MQLs continue to convert at 2-3x the rate of paid channels. [src2]
**Red flag threshold**: Below 10% overall indicates misalignment between marketing and sales on lead quality definitions.
**Action trigger**: If MQL-to-SQL is below 10%, audit MQL scoring criteria and align with sales on acceptance criteria.

[src2, src4]

### SQL-to-Opportunity Win Rate

**Definition**: Percentage of sales-qualified leads that convert to closed-won deals. Measures sales effectiveness on marketing-sourced pipeline.

| Segment | Median | Top Quartile |
|---------|--------|-------------|
| SMB (ACV <$10K) | 22% | 35% |
| Mid-Market ($10K-$100K) | 18% | 28% |
| Enterprise ($100K+) | 15% | 25% |

**Trend**: Stable to slightly declining as buyer committees expand and deal cycles lengthen. [src4]
**Red flag threshold**: Below 10% for any segment indicates fundamental lead quality or sales process issues.

[src4]

## Metric Category 3: Efficiency Metrics

### CAC Payback Period

**Definition**: Months required to recover customer acquisition cost from gross margin. Formula: CAC / (monthly revenue per customer x gross margin %).

| Segment | Median | Healthy Range | Alarm Threshold |
|---------|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| SMB/PLG | 6 months | 3-9 months | >12 months |
| Mid-Market | 12 months | 8-15 months | >18 months |
| Enterprise | 18 months | 12-24 months | >30 months |

**Red flag threshold**: Payback period exceeding contract length signals negative unit economics.
**Action trigger**: If payback >18 months for non-enterprise, review pricing or reduce CAC.

[src1]

### Marketing-Sourced Pipeline Percentage

**Definition**: Percentage of total sales pipeline value generated by marketing activities (first-touch or multi-touch attributed).

| Segment | Median | Top Quartile |
|---------|--------|-------------|
| SMB/PLG | 60-80% | >80% |
| Mid-Market | 40-55% | >60% |
| Enterprise | 25-40% | >45% |

**Trend**: Increasing across all segments as marketing takes on more pipeline responsibility. [src4]
**Red flag threshold**: Below 25% for mid-market indicates marketing is not contributing meaningfully to revenue.

[src4]

### Email Marketing Performance

**Definition**: Key email metrics across B2B marketing communications.

| Metric | Median (B2B) | Top Quartile |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Open rate | 21% | 30%+ |
| Click-through rate (CTR) | 2.6% | 4.5%+ |
| Click-to-open rate (CTOR) | 12% | 18%+ |
| Unsubscribe rate | 0.3% | <0.15% |
| Bounce rate | 0.7% | <0.3% |

**Trend**: Open rates declining 2-3% YoY as inbox competition increases; CTR stable for segmented sends. [src3]
**Red flag threshold**: Open rate below 15% suggests list hygiene issues or poor subject lines.

[src3]

## Composite Metrics & Rules of Thumb

| Rule | Formula / Threshold | Interpretation |
|------|--------------------|----------------|
| LTV:CAC > 3:1 | Customer lifetime value / CAC > 3.0 | Sustainable unit economics; below 3:1 = margin squeeze |
| CAC Payback < 12 months | CAC / monthly gross margin < 12 | Healthy cash efficiency; above 18 months = danger zone |
| Marketing-sourced pipeline > 40% | Marketing pipeline / total pipeline > 40% | Marketing is a revenue driver, not a cost center |
| MQL:SQL > 15% | SQL / MQL > 15% | Healthy sales-marketing alignment on lead quality |
| Content ROI > 3x | Pipeline from content / content spend > 3.0 | Content is generating meaningful pipeline return |

**Constraint**: LTV:CAC ratio is unreliable when LTV is based on fewer than 2 years of cohort data. CAC payback benchmarks assume 70-80% gross margins typical of SaaS; adjust for lower-margin businesses. [src1]

## Segment Definitions

| Segment | Definition | Typical Characteristics |
|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| SMB/PLG | ACV <$10K, <100 employees, or product-led motion | Self-serve signup; short sales cycles (days-weeks); high volume, low touch |
| Mid-Market | ACV $10K-$100K, 100-1000 employees | Sales-assisted; 1-3 month cycles; 2-4 person buying committee |
| Enterprise | ACV >$100K, 1000+ employees | Sales-led; 3-12 month cycles; 5-10+ person buying committee; procurement involved |
| PLG (cross-segment) | Product-qualified leads as primary motion | Free/trial entry; usage-based conversion; low CAC but requires product investment |

## Common Misinterpretations

- **Comparing logo churn to revenue churn**: They measure different things. A company can have 10% logo churn but negative net revenue churn if expansion exceeds losses. Always clarify which churn metric is being discussed. [src1]
- **Applying enterprise benchmarks to SMB**: Enterprise NRR above 130% is common due to seat expansion; expecting this in SMB sets unrealistic targets. SMB NRR of 100-110% is healthy.
- **Treating benchmarks as targets**: Median means half of companies are below this number. Use 75th percentile for ambitious targets, median for sanity checks. A company at median is not "good" — it is average.
- **Ignoring fully loaded CAC**: Many companies report CAC using only ad spend. Fully loaded CAC (including salaries, tools, overhead) is typically 2-3x the ad-spend-only number. Always confirm the CAC definition before comparing.
