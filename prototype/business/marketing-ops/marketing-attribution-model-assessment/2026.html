Marketing Attribution Model Assessment

Which attribution model to use — first touch, last touch, multi-touch, incrementality?

Type: Assessment Confidence: 0.84 Sources: 6 Verified: 2026-03-10

Purpose

This assessment evaluates the maturity of a company's marketing attribution and measurement capabilities across five dimensions: data infrastructure, model sophistication, cross-channel integration, privacy-readiness, and organizational alignment. The output is a composite maturity score (1-5) that identifies gaps in measurement capability and routes to specific improvement paths. [src1]

Constraints

  • Requires access to marketing analytics platforms and 6+ months of multi-channel campaign data
  • Not meaningful for single-channel businesses or companies spending less than $50K/year on marketing
  • Attribution model selection depends on sales cycle length, channel diversity, and data infrastructure
  • Assessment is diagnostic only — pair with decision cards for model selection recommendations
  • Re-run every 6 months or after major privacy regulation changes or platform policy updates

Common Pitfalls in Assessment

  • Platform self-reporting bias: Ad platforms over-claim conversions by 20-60%. Never use platform ROAS as ground truth. [src4]
  • Last-click nostalgia: Teams resist MTA because direct-response numbers look worse. Frame MTA as more accurate, not worse.
  • Measurement theater: A sophisticated unused model is worse than a simple model that drives decisions.
  • Privacy panic: Some teams abandon measurement when cookies degrade rather than investing in alternatives like MMM.

When This Matters

Fetch when a user asks which attribution model to use, wants to evaluate marketing measurement capabilities, is preparing for cookie deprecation, cannot reconcile ROI numbers across channels, or needs to justify marketing spend to finance or the board.
