# Demand Generation Channel Assessment

## Purpose

This assessment evaluates the effectiveness and maturity of an organization's demand generation channels across six categories: organic/SEO, paid digital, content marketing, events and webinars, partnerships and referrals, and product-led/outbound motions. It is designed for demand gen leaders and CMOs who need to identify underperforming channels, over-dependencies, and untapped opportunities. The output provides channel-level scoring with clear routing to optimization actions. [src1]

## Constraints
<!-- Agents: read before running this assessment with a user. -->

- Channel effectiveness depends on ACV, sales motion, and ICP — there is no universally "best" channel
- Requires minimum 3 months of channel-level data (12 months preferred) for reliable scoring
- Multi-touch attribution is essential for accurate assessment — single-touch models systematically over-credit first or last touch
- ROI timelines vary dramatically: paid (weeks), email (weeks), SEO (6-12 months), community (12-18 months) — compare channels at appropriate time horizons
- Assess channel interaction effects — channels often amplify each other (content + SEO, ABM + events, email + content)

## Assessment Dimensions

### Dimension 1: Organic Search & SEO

**What this measures**: The maturity and effectiveness of organic search as a demand generation channel — content-driven SEO, technical SEO, and organic traffic-to-pipeline contribution.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No SEO strategy; website not optimized; zero organic pipeline contribution | No keyword research; no SEO tool; organic traffic is incidental; no tracking of organic leads |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic SEO in place; blog with irregular publishing; some keyword targeting | SEO tool (Ahrefs/SEMrush) in use; monthly blog posts; organic traffic growing but not attributed to pipeline |
| 3 | Defined | Systematic SEO program; keyword-driven content calendar; organic traffic generating MQLs | Weekly publishing cadence; topic clusters strategy; organic contributing 15-25% of MQLs; technical SEO solid |
| 4 | Managed | SEO is a top pipeline channel; programmatic content at scale; competitive keyword positions | Organic contributes 30-40% of MQLs; featured snippets and SERP features; MQL-to-SQL from organic exceeds 40% |
| 5 | Optimized | SEO drives dominant pipeline share; AI-augmented content; topical authority established | Organic contributes 40%+ of pipeline; 748%+ ROI; topical authority in core domains; organic CAC declining YoY |

**Red flags**: Zero organic traffic growth over 6 months; no keyword rankings in top 20 for target terms; content published without keyword strategy. [src1]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "What percentage of your MQLs come from organic search, and do you have a keyword-driven content strategy?"

### Dimension 2: Paid Digital (Search, Social, Display)

**What this measures**: The effectiveness and efficiency of paid acquisition channels — paid search, paid social, display/programmatic — including cost efficiency and conversion rates.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No paid campaigns or sporadic campaigns without tracking | No Google Ads; no LinkedIn Ads; or campaigns running without conversion tracking |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic paid search and/or social running; limited targeting; CPC-focused optimization | Google Ads with basic campaigns; LinkedIn with broad targeting; focus on clicks not conversions |
| 3 | Defined | Multi-platform paid strategy; conversion-optimized; CAC tracked by channel | Google + LinkedIn + retargeting; conversion tracking in place; A/B testing ads; CAC by platform known |
| 4 | Managed | Advanced paid strategy: audience segmentation, automated bidding, creative testing at scale | Lookalike/intent audiences; automated bid strategies; creative testing framework; ROAS targets met |
| 5 | Optimized | AI-driven paid media: predictive audiences, dynamic creative, cross-channel optimization | AI bid management; DCO (dynamic creative); predictive budget allocation; paid CAC declining while volume grows |

**Red flags**: No conversion tracking; optimizing for clicks instead of pipeline; single ad creative running unchanged for months; no negative keyword management. [src3]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "What is your CAC by paid channel, and are you tracking all the way through to closed-won revenue?"

### Dimension 3: Content Marketing

**What this measures**: Content as a demand generation engine — not just content production, but content's contribution to pipeline through lead magnets, nurture, and thought leadership.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | Content is occasional blog posts; no gated assets; no content-to-pipeline tracking | Sporadic blog; no ebooks/whitepapers; no lead magnets; content not linked to demand gen |
| 2 | Emerging | Regular content with some gated assets; content drives downloads but not tracked to pipeline | Monthly blog; 2-3 gated assets; content generates leads but no pipeline attribution |
| 3 | Defined | Content strategy tied to buyer journey; lead magnets at each stage; content contributing to MQLs | Content mapped to awareness/consideration/decision; 5+ gated assets; content-sourced MQLs tracked |
| 4 | Managed | Content is a pipeline engine; repurposing at scale; content ROI measured and reported | Content attributed to 20%+ of pipeline; multi-format repurposing; content ROI > 3x; pillar content strategy |
| 5 | Optimized | Thought leadership drives inbound demand; AI-augmented production; content flywheel operating | Content creates market category; AI-assisted at scale; content ROI > 5x; 59%+ cite content as primary acquisition driver |

**Red flags**: Content team measured on volume (posts/month) instead of pipeline impact; no gated content in bottom-of-funnel stages; content older than 12 months not updated. [src4]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "Can you attribute pipeline revenue to specific content assets, and what is your content ROI?"

### Dimension 4: Events & Webinars

**What this measures**: The maturity of events (conferences, field events, virtual) and webinars as demand generation channels, including pre-event, during-event, and post-event execution.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No event strategy; occasional conference attendance without plan | Attend 1-2 conferences; no hosted events; no webinars; no post-event follow-up process |
| 2 | Emerging | Monthly webinars or quarterly events; basic registration tracking | Monthly webinar program; attend 3-5 conferences; registrations tracked but not pipeline attributed |
| 3 | Defined | Integrated event strategy; pre/post-event nurture; pipeline attribution in place | Event calendar with clear objectives; pre-event outreach; post-event follow-up SLA; events attributed to pipeline |
| 4 | Managed | Events as a strategic channel; executive events program; field marketing driving enterprise deals | Executive roundtables; field events driving $100K+ deals; webinar program with 30%+ MQL-to-SQL conversion |
| 5 | Optimized | Events ecosystem: owned conference + community events + partner events; events drive category creation | Owned annual conference; events drive 20%+ of pipeline; community-led events; events create competitive moat |

**Red flags**: No follow-up process after events; event ROI not measured; webinar attendance declining without strategy adjustment; events chosen by executives without data. [src2]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "Can you tell me the pipeline generated from your last three events, and do you have a structured post-event follow-up process?"

### Dimension 5: Partnerships & Referrals

**What this measures**: The maturity of partnership channels — technology partnerships, agency/consulting partnerships, customer referral programs, and co-marketing relationships.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No partnership program; referrals are informal and untracked | No partner program; referrals happen occasionally via personal relationships; no tracking |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic referral incentive exists; 1-2 informal technology partnerships | Customer referral bonus offered; integration partnerships listed; no co-marketing; <5% of pipeline from partners |
| 3 | Defined | Formal partner program; co-marketing activities; referral pipeline tracked | Partner portal or program; co-webinars/content with 3+ partners; referrals contributing 10-15% of pipeline |
| 4 | Managed | Strategic partnership channel; partner-sourced pipeline measured; dedicated partner marketing | Partner marketing team/person; partner-sourced pipeline at 15-25%; tiered partner program; ecosystem strategy |
| 5 | Optimized | Partnership ecosystem drives significant pipeline; platform/marketplace strategy; partner-first GTM | Partner-sourced pipeline exceeds 25%; marketplace listing drives inbound; partner channel at lowest CAC ($150 median) |

**Red flags**: "We have a partner page but no one manages it"; no referral tracking in CRM; partner agreements without joint pipeline targets. [src1]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "What percentage of your pipeline comes from referrals and partnerships, and do you have a formal partner program?"

### Dimension 6: Product-Led Growth & Outbound

**What this measures**: The maturity of product-led growth (free/trial/freemium motions) and structured outbound programs (SDR/BDR) as demand generation channels.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No PLG motion; outbound is ad hoc cold calling without targeting or messaging framework | No free tier/trial; outbound is untargeted cold outreach; no ICP definition for outbound |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic free trial or outbound SDR team; limited targeting; low conversion rates | Free trial exists but poor activation; SDR team with broad lists; <2% outbound reply rate |
| 3 | Defined | Structured PLG with activation flows or outbound with ICP targeting and multi-touch sequences | PLG with onboarding + activation email sequence; or outbound with ICP lists, 5-touch sequences, 5%+ reply rate |
| 4 | Managed | PLG with PQL scoring and sales-assist triggers; or outbound with intent data and personalization at scale | PQL model driving sales intervention; or intent-based outbound with 10%+ meeting rate; channel-specific CAC tracked |
| 5 | Optimized | PLG+sales hybrid motion; or AI-augmented outbound with predictive targeting and autonomous sequencing | PLG driving 60%+ of acquisition with sales expansion; or AI-driven outbound with predictive ICP scoring |

**Red flags**: Free trial with no activation onboarding; SDRs without ICP definition; outbound measured on activity (calls/emails) instead of pipeline; PLG conversion rate below 1%. [src2]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "Do you have a PLG motion, and if so what is your free-to-paid conversion rate? For outbound, what is your meeting booking rate?"

## Scoring & Interpretation

### Overall Score Calculation

Weight channels based on their typical contribution to pipeline. Organic and Paid carry higher weight because they are the highest-volume channels for most B2B companies.

```
Overall Score = (Organic × 1.5 + Paid × 1.25 + Content × 1.0 + Events × 1.0 + Partnerships × 1.0 + PLG/Outbound × 1.25) / 7.0
```

### Score Interpretation

| Overall Score | Maturity Level | Interpretation | Recommended Next Step |
|---------------|---------------|----------------|----------------------|
| 1.0 - 1.9 | Critical | No systematic demand gen; pipeline is opportunistic or founder-dependent | Establish 2-3 core channels with clear tracking; start with content + SEO + one paid channel |
| 2.0 - 2.9 | Developing | Channels running but poorly optimized; limited attribution; single-channel dependency likely | Implement attribution, optimize top 2-3 channels, test one new channel per quarter |
| 3.0 - 3.9 | Competent | Multi-channel demand gen contributing to pipeline; optimization opportunities exist | Optimize channel mix based on CAC data; implement multi-touch attribution; test ABM |
| 4.0 - 4.5 | Advanced | Diversified, data-driven channel mix; predictable pipeline from multiple sources | Fine-tune with AI optimization; expand partner ecosystem; implement predictive models |
| 4.6 - 5.0 | Best-in-class | Demand gen is a predictable, efficient revenue engine with diversified channels | Maintain through continuous testing; category creation; ecosystem leadership |

## Common Pitfalls in Assessment

- **Single-channel dependency**: Over 50% of pipeline from one channel is a critical risk. If that channel degrades (Google algorithm change, LinkedIn price increase), pipeline collapses. Diversification target: no channel exceeds 35% of pipeline. [src1]
- **Attribution model bias**: First-touch attribution over-credits awareness channels (paid social, content); last-touch over-credits conversion channels (paid search, direct). Use multi-touch to see reality.
- **Comparing channels at wrong time horizons**: Paid search shows ROI in weeks; SEO takes 6-12 months. Comparing Q1 investment to Q1 return for SEO penalizes organic unfairly. Use 12-month rolling windows.
- **Ignoring channel interaction effects**: Email amplifies content, ABM amplifies events, SEO amplifies all inbound. Killing a "low-performing" channel can degrade other channels that depend on it. [src3]
- **Activity metrics instead of pipeline metrics**: Measuring channels by MQLs or leads instead of pipeline and closed-won. A channel that generates 1,000 MQLs with 2% conversion is worse than one generating 100 MQLs with 25% conversion.
