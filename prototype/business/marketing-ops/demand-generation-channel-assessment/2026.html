Demand Generation Channel Assessment

How effective are demand gen channels — organic, paid, events, partnerships, PLG, outbound?

Type: Assessment Confidence: 0.83 Sources: 6 Verified: 2026-03-09

Purpose

This assessment evaluates the effectiveness and maturity of demand generation channels across six categories: organic/SEO, paid digital, content marketing, events and webinars, partnerships and referrals, and product-led/outbound motions. Designed for demand gen leaders and CMOs who need to identify underperforming channels, over-dependencies, and untapped opportunities. [src1]

Constraints

  • Channel effectiveness depends on ACV, sales motion, ICP, and industry — no universally "best" channel
  • Requires min. 3 months channel data (12 months preferred) for reliable scoring
  • Multi-touch attribution required — single-touch systematically misattributes
  • ROI timelines differ: paid (weeks), SEO (6-12 months), community (12-18 months)
  • Assess channel interaction effects — channels often amplify each other

Common Pitfalls

  • Single-channel dependency: >50% from one channel is critical risk. Target: no channel exceeds 35%. [src1]
  • Attribution model bias: First-touch over-credits awareness; last-touch over-credits conversion. Use multi-touch.
  • Wrong time horizons: Paid shows ROI in weeks; SEO in 6-12 months. Use 12-month rolling windows for fair comparison.
  • Ignoring interaction effects: Killing "low-performing" channels can degrade others that depend on them. [src3]
  • Activity vs pipeline metrics: 1,000 MQLs at 2% conversion < 100 MQLs at 25% conversion.
