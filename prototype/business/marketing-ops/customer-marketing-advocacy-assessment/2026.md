# Customer Marketing & Advocacy Assessment

## Purpose

This assessment evaluates the maturity of a company's customer marketing and advocacy program across five dimensions: expansion and upsell marketing, customer reference and evidence, community and advocacy, voice of customer (NPS/CSAT), and customer lifecycle communications. The output is a composite maturity score (1-5) that reveals whether post-sale marketing is driving retention, expansion, and advocacy — or is an afterthought. Use this when net revenue retention (NRR) is below target, when the sales team cannot find customer references, or when evaluating whether customer marketing deserves its own dedicated function. [src1]

## Constraints
<!-- Agents: read before running this assessment with a user. -->

- Requires access to NPS/CSAT data, expansion revenue metrics, and customer success data for reliable scoring
- Not meaningful for pre-PMF startups with fewer than 50 customers
- Product-led and sales-led companies have fundamentally different customer marketing profiles — use appropriate benchmarks
- Assessment is diagnostic — identifies gaps but does not prescribe specific campaigns
- Re-run semi-annually; customer marketing maturity can shift rapidly after CS reorgs or NRR changes

## Assessment Dimensions

<!-- Each dimension is scored independently. The structured format lets agents
     walk through this conversationally with a user, one dimension at a time. -->

### Dimension 1: Expansion & Upsell Marketing

**What this measures**: How effectively marketing supports expansion revenue through upsell, cross-sell, and upgrade campaigns targeting existing customers.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No marketing involvement in expansion; upsell is purely CS/sales-driven; no customer-targeted campaigns | Marketing focuses exclusively on new logo acquisition; no expansion-focused content; no customer segmentation for upsell |
| 2 | Emerging | Occasional expansion campaigns (product launch emails to existing customers); but not systematic; expansion revenue not attributed to marketing | Ad hoc product update emails; no structured upsell program; marketing does not track expansion influence |
| 3 | Defined | Structured expansion campaigns based on usage data and customer segments; marketing influences 10-20% of expansion revenue; product adoption content exists | Usage-triggered upgrade campaigns; customer segmentation by plan/usage tier; product adoption email series; expansion pipeline tracked |
| 4 | Managed | Marketing systematically drives 20-40% of expansion pipeline; in-app messaging and personalized campaigns based on feature usage; cross-sell playbooks per segment | Behavioral triggers drive upgrade prompts; in-app and email coordinated; expansion revenue attributed to marketing; A/B testing on expansion campaigns |
| 5 | Optimized | AI-driven expansion recommendations; predictive models identify expansion-ready accounts; marketing drives 40%+ of expansion pipeline; personalized expansion journeys | ML predicts expansion timing per account; dynamic upgrade paths based on usage patterns; expansion marketing is a primary revenue contributor |

**Red flags**: Marketing has zero expansion revenue attribution; no customer-targeted campaigns exist; expansion is entirely manual via CS/sales outreach; product usage data not used for marketing. [src5]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "What percentage of your expansion revenue does marketing influence, and do you have automated campaigns triggered by product usage?"

### Dimension 2: Customer Reference & Evidence

**What this measures**: The maturity of the customer reference program, case study library, and social proof assets used in sales and marketing.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No case studies; no reference customers identified; sales finds references ad hoc by asking around | No case study library; reference requests go to random CS managers; no testimonials on website; no G2/peer review strategy |
| 2 | Emerging | A few case studies exist but are outdated; some reference customers identified but not tracked; reviews exist but are not managed | 3-5 case studies but older than 12 months; informal list of "friendlies" for references; some G2/Capterra reviews but not systematically collected |
| 3 | Defined | Active case study program producing 4-8 per year; reference database with 20+ volunteers segmented by use case/industry; peer review strategy on G2/Capterra | Reference database maintained; case studies refreshed annually; review generation campaigns quarterly; sales can request references through a formal process |
| 4 | Managed | Reference program with 50+ active references segmented by industry, company size, and use case; reference usage tracked to prevent fatigue; video testimonials produced | Reference matching system; usage tracking prevents over-asking; reference pipeline automated; 10+ video testimonials; ROI-focused case studies |
| 5 | Optimized | Self-service reference matching for sales; customer-generated content at scale; advocacy platform manages participation; reference program drives measurable pipeline influence | Advocacy platform tracks all customer evidence activities; references linked to deal influence; customer evidence contributes to 15-25% of closed-won deals; always-on review generation |

**Red flags**: Fewer than 3 current case studies; no identified reference customers; sales team cannot find references when prospects ask; no presence on peer review sites (G2, Capterra). [src2]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "How many active reference customers can you deploy for sales calls this month, and when was your most recent case study published?"

### Dimension 3: Community & Advocacy Programs

**What this measures**: Whether the company has built a community of engaged advocates who actively promote the brand without being asked.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No customer community; no advocacy program; customer engagement ends at onboarding | No community platform; no customer events; no advocacy program; no user group |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic customer community (Slack/forum) with low engagement; annual customer event or user conference; no structured advocacy program | Community exists but fewer than 10% of customers participate; user conference is product-demo focused; no incentive for advocacy |
| 3 | Defined | Active community with regular engagement (weekly discussions); customer advisory board (CAB) meets quarterly; advocacy program with defined tiers and rewards | Community engagement rate above 20%; CAB provides product input; advocacy rewards program with participation tracking; customer champions identified |
| 4 | Managed | Community is a primary support channel reducing ticket volume; advocacy program drives measurable referrals; customer events generate pipeline; ambassador program active | Community reduces support tickets by 15-25%; advocacy referrals tracked in CRM; ambassador program with structured benefits; customer speakers at industry events |
| 5 | Optimized | Self-sustaining community where customers help each other and create content; advocacy drives 10-20% of new pipeline through referrals; community insights influence product roadmap | Community NPS above 70; customer-generated content library; referral pipeline measurable; community insights directly feed product team; advocates speak at conferences independently |

**Red flags**: No customer community of any kind; no customer advisory board; no advocacy or referral program; customer events are pure product demos with no community element. [src4]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "Do you have a customer community, and what percentage of your new pipeline comes from customer referrals?"

### Dimension 4: Voice of Customer (NPS/CSAT)

**What this measures**: How systematically the company collects, analyzes, and acts on customer feedback through NPS, CSAT, and other feedback mechanisms.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No systematic customer feedback collection; NPS/CSAT not measured; feedback comes through ad hoc channels | No NPS program; customer sentiment unknown; feedback collected only when customers complain; no closed-loop process |
| 2 | Emerging | NPS or CSAT survey sent annually or semi-annually; results shared but not systematically acted upon; basic sentiment tracking | Annual NPS survey; results shared in leadership meeting; no closed-loop follow-up with detractors; response rate below 20% |
| 3 | Defined | Quarterly NPS/CSAT surveys with 30%+ response rate; closed-loop process for detractors; results segmented by cohort and shared cross-functionally | Quarterly cadence; detractor follow-up within 48 hours; NPS trends tracked over time; results shared with product, CS, and marketing |
| 4 | Managed | Continuous or transactional NPS (triggered by key moments); real-time feedback dashboards; NPS-driven customer segmentation; promoters activated for advocacy | In-journey NPS at key milestones; real-time sentiment dashboard; promoters automatically invited to reference/advocacy programs; detractor recovery playbook |
| 5 | Optimized | Predictive satisfaction scoring from product usage data; NPS integrated with product analytics and revenue data; customer health score combines NPS + usage + support data | AI predicts satisfaction from behavioral signals; NPS correlates to expansion/churn at account level; health score drives automated interventions; closed-loop process under 24 hours |

**Red flags**: NPS not measured; NPS measured but results not shared beyond the team that collects it; no follow-up with detractors; NPS response rate below 15%; survey sent less than annually. [src6]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "What is your current NPS, how often do you measure it, and what happens when a customer gives a detractor score?"

### Dimension 5: Customer Lifecycle Communications

**What this measures**: How effectively marketing communicates with customers throughout their lifecycle — from onboarding through renewal and expansion.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No structured customer communications post-sale; customers only hear from CS or support; marketing ignores existing customers | No customer newsletter; no onboarding emails from marketing; no product adoption content; customers are "out of sight, out of mind" for marketing |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic customer newsletter (monthly); some product update emails; but communications not segmented or personalized | Generic monthly newsletter to all customers; product release announcements; no behavioral targeting; same content regardless of plan or usage |
| 3 | Defined | Segmented customer communications by lifecycle stage (onboarding, adoption, renewal); product education content library; renewal nurture campaigns | Onboarding email series; product tips based on feature adoption; renewal reminder campaigns 90 days out; customer content distinct from prospect content |
| 4 | Managed | Personalized lifecycle communications triggered by usage events; in-app messaging coordinated with email; customer content hub with training, best practices, certifications | Behavioral triggers drive communications; in-app and email orchestrated; customer academy or knowledge base; health-score-based outreach |
| 5 | Optimized | AI-personalized customer journeys; predictive communications (churn risk triggers, expansion opportunity); omnichannel lifecycle orchestration across email, in-app, community, and events | AI determines next-best-action per customer; churn prevention triggered before risk materializes; customer journey fully orchestrated across channels |

**Red flags**: Marketing does not send any communications to existing customers; no onboarding email series; no renewal nurture campaign; customers only hear from the company when invoices arrive. [src3]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "What automated email sequences do existing customers receive, and are they segmented by lifecycle stage or product usage?"

## Scoring & Interpretation

### Overall Score Calculation

All dimensions weighted equally for a general assessment. Weight Expansion Marketing and VoC more heavily (1.5x) for companies focused on NRR improvement.

```
Overall Score = (Expansion Marketing + Reference & Evidence + Community & Advocacy + VoC + Lifecycle Communications) / 5
```

### Score Interpretation

| Overall Score | Maturity Level | Interpretation | Recommended Next Step |
|---------------|---------------|----------------|----------------------|
| 1.0 - 1.9 | Critical | Customer marketing is nonexistent; growth is 100% new-logo dependent; NRR likely below 100% | Start with NPS program and basic customer newsletter; identify 10 reference candidates; assign customer marketing ownership |
| 2.0 - 2.9 | Developing | Basic customer marketing exists but is reactive and fragmented; expansion revenue is CS-driven | Build reference program; launch advocacy program; implement quarterly NPS with closed-loop process |
| 3.0 - 3.9 | Competent | Structured customer marketing function driving measurable value; ready to scale | Expand automation; build customer community; integrate advocacy with sales process; scale reference program |
| 4.0 - 4.5 | Advanced | Customer marketing is a primary driver of NRR and pipeline contribution | Deploy AI-driven expansion; build self-sustaining community; predictive satisfaction scoring |
| 4.6 - 5.0 | Best-in-class | Customer marketing is fully integrated across the business; advocacy drives significant pipeline | Innovate on community-led growth; customer co-creation programs; market-facing advocacy at scale |

## Common Pitfalls in Assessment

- **Confusing customer success with customer marketing**: CS owns the relationship and outcomes; customer marketing owns the programs, content, and campaigns that support advocacy, expansion, and community. These are complementary but distinct functions. [src1]
- **NPS theater**: Measuring NPS without acting on it is worse than not measuring at all. The value is in the closed-loop process (detractor recovery, promoter activation), not the score itself.
- **Reference burnout**: Over-asking the same 5 reference customers erodes goodwill. Track reference usage and rotate across a pool of 20+ active references. Set participation limits per customer per quarter.
- **Advocacy without value exchange**: Expecting customers to advocate without providing value in return (exclusive access, networking, co-marketing opportunities) leads to program attrition. The best programs are genuinely valuable to participants.
