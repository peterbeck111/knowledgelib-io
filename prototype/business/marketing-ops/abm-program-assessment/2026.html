ABM Program Maturity Assessment

How mature is an ABM program — account selection, personalization, sales coordination, ROI?

Type: Assessment Confidence: 0.83 Sources: 6 Verified: 2026-03-09

Purpose

This assessment evaluates the maturity of an account-based marketing program across five dimensions: account selection and ICP definition, content personalization and engagement, sales-marketing alignment, measurement and attribution, and buying group orchestration. It is designed for VP Marketing, demand gen leaders, and ABM managers who need to diagnose whether their ABM program is generating real pipeline impact or just running account-targeted campaigns. The output identifies specific maturity gaps and routes to improvement playbooks. [src1]

Constraints

  • Requires ABM platform data (Demandbase, 6sense, Terminus) or CRM with account-level tracking
  • Most meaningful for B2B companies with ACV above $25K
  • Must involve both marketing ops and sales leadership
  • Evaluates ABM program maturity, NOT ABM technology stack
  • Re-run quarterly; mandatory after ICP changes or ABM platform migration

Common Pitfalls in Assessment

  • ABM theater: Running targeted ads and calling it ABM. True ABM requires account-level strategy, personalization, sales alignment, and measurement. Validate by asking about sales involvement.
  • Lead scoring as account scoring: Aggregating individual lead scores to account level rather than true account scoring with firmographic, technographic, and intent data.
  • Technology over strategy: Over-investing in ABM tech while under-investing in ICP, content, and alignment. A $100K platform with poor ICP produces expensive noise.
  • Pilot paralysis: Running a small ABM pilot for 2+ years without scaling. Scale what works, fix or stop what doesn't.
