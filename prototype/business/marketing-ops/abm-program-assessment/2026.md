# ABM Program Maturity Assessment

## Purpose

This assessment evaluates the maturity of an account-based marketing program across five dimensions: account selection and ICP definition, content personalization and engagement, sales-marketing alignment, measurement and attribution, and buying group orchestration. It is designed for VP Marketing, demand gen leaders, and ABM managers who need to diagnose whether their ABM program is generating real pipeline impact or just running account-targeted campaigns. The output identifies specific maturity gaps and routes to improvement playbooks for each weak dimension. [src1]

## Constraints
<!-- Agents: read before running this assessment with a user. -->

- Requires access to ABM platform data (Demandbase, 6sense, Terminus, or equivalent) or CRM with account-level tracking for reliable scoring
- Most meaningful for B2B companies with ACV above $25K — below that threshold, ABM economics rarely justify the investment over traditional demand gen
- Must involve both marketing ops and sales leadership — ABM is a joint function and cannot be assessed from one side alone
- Does NOT assess ABM technology stack maturity or specific tool capabilities — those are separate evaluations
- Re-run quarterly; mandatory reassessment after ICP changes, new market entry, or ABM platform migration

## Assessment Dimensions

<!-- Each dimension is scored independently. The structured format lets agents

### Dimension 1: Account Selection & ICP Definition

**What this measures**: The rigor and data-driven nature of how target accounts are identified, scored, tiered, and maintained — from ad hoc lists to predictive ICP models.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No formal ICP; target account list is a sales wish list or based on company size alone | Account list created from sales opinions; no firmographic, technographic, or intent data used; no tiering |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic ICP defined with firmographic criteria; account list exists but is static and not scored | ICP document exists with revenue/industry/geo criteria; 100-500 accounts on a spreadsheet; no regular refresh; no intent signals |
| 3 | Defined | Data-driven ICP using firmographic + technographic + intent data; accounts scored and tiered (Tier 1/2/3); list refreshed quarterly | Account scoring model combining 3+ data sources; tier-based account allocation; quarterly ICP review; 40%+ of respondents report well-defined ICP [src1] |
| 4 | Managed | Predictive ICP model using ML; real-time intent signals trigger account promotion/demotion; buying committee mapped per account | Predictive account scoring; real-time intent monitoring; buying committee contacts identified per account; ICP model validated against win/loss data [src4] |
| 5 | Optimized | Self-refining ICP model that learns from closed-won/lost patterns; dynamic account universe expands automatically; lookalike modeling identifies net-new accounts | ML-driven ICP that auto-adjusts based on conversion data; dynamic account expansion; buying group coverage tracked per account; ICP accuracy measured and improving [src3] |

**Red flags**: Target account list has not been refreshed in 6+ months; ICP is a one-paragraph description with no scoring criteria; same list used regardless of product or campaign; no intent data integrated. [src2]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "How do you decide which accounts to target, and when was the last time you removed accounts that no longer fit your ICP?"

### Dimension 2: Content Personalization & Engagement

**What this measures**: The depth and sophistication of how content and messaging are personalized for target accounts — from generic campaigns to 1:1 account-specific experiences.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No account-specific content; same campaigns sent to everyone; ABM = targeted ads with the same creative | Generic content used for all accounts; no personalization beyond {company_name} merge fields; landing pages are one-size-fits-all |
| 2 | Emerging | Industry-level personalization exists; some content tailored by vertical or company size | Content variants by industry (3-5 versions); basic display ad personalization; personalized email subject lines but generic body |
| 3 | Defined | Tier-based personalization: Tier 1 gets custom content, Tier 2 gets industry-specific, Tier 3 gets programmatic | Custom landing pages for Tier 1 accounts; industry-specific content journeys for Tier 2; programmatic ads for Tier 3; web personalization active |
| 4 | Managed | Multi-channel personalized journeys orchestrated by account stage; content mapped to buying committee roles; 42% higher conversion from tiered personalization | Persona-specific content for each buying committee role; orchestrated multi-channel sequences (email, ads, web, direct mail); engagement scoring by account [src5] |
| 5 | Optimized | AI-driven content personalization at scale; real-time content recommendations based on account behavior; 1:1 account experiences for strategic accounts | AI content engine recommends next-best content per account; dynamic website personalization; personalized microsites for top accounts; content consumption drives deal progression [src1] |

**Red flags**: All accounts receive the same email nurture; no web personalization for target accounts; content team creates without input from ABM team on account priorities; personalization is limited to name/company insertion. [src3]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "If a Tier 1 target account visits your website today, will they see anything different from a random visitor?"

### Dimension 3: Sales-Marketing Alignment

**What this measures**: The degree to which sales and marketing operate as a unified team on target accounts — from siloed handoffs to shared KPIs and coordinated engagement.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | Sales and marketing operate independently; no shared account view; marketing generates leads, sales ignores them | No shared account list; marketing measured on MQLs, sales on quota; no regular alignment meetings; mutual blame culture |
| 2 | Emerging | Shared target account list exists; some coordination on key accounts; marketing passes MQAs (marketing qualified accounts) | Shared spreadsheet of target accounts; monthly alignment meeting exists; MQA handoff process defined but inconsistently followed |
| 3 | Defined | SLA between sales and marketing; shared dashboards; account-level engagement visible to both teams; regular pipeline reviews | Written SLA with MQA criteria and response time; shared CRM dashboard; weekly or biweekly account review meetings; 67% better deal closing with alignment [src5] |
| 4 | Managed | Single KPI contract (ICP tiers, MQA thresholds, SLA timelines); coordinated outreach orchestration; sales uses marketing content in deals | Joint KPI dashboard; sales triggers marketing plays and vice versa; coordinated multi-thread engagement; sales reps consume account intelligence from marketing [src1] |
| 5 | Optimized | Unified revenue team model; AI-assisted account orchestration; buying group engagement coordinated across sales, marketing, and CS | Single revenue team with shared comp incentives; AI recommends next-best actions for each account; buying group coverage tracked; 2-3x higher win rates from alignment [src3] |

**Red flags**: Marketing and sales cannot agree on target account list; no SLA exists; sales does not know which accounts marketing is engaging; marketing has no visibility into deal progression. [src2]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "Do sales and marketing share a single target account dashboard, and when is the last time you jointly reviewed account engagement data?"

### Dimension 4: Measurement & Attribution

**What this measures**: The ability to measure ABM program impact on pipeline, revenue, and ROI — from vanity metrics to multi-touch account-level attribution.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No ABM-specific measurement; success measured by campaign-level metrics (impressions, clicks, form fills) | Reporting focused on vanity metrics; no account-level engagement tracking; cannot state ABM pipeline or revenue contribution |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic account-level engagement tracked; some pipeline metrics exist but attribution is unclear | Account engagement scores exist; pipeline from target accounts reported but not attributed to ABM activities specifically |
| 3 | Defined | ABM metrics framework in place: engagement score, pipeline velocity, win rate, deal size; account-level attribution model | Monthly ABM dashboard with engagement, pipeline, and revenue metrics; target vs non-target account performance comparison; account-level ROI calculated [src6] |
| 4 | Managed | Multi-touch account-level attribution; ABM ROI compared against other channels; pipeline velocity and deal size impact quantified | Multi-touch attribution at account level; ABM vs non-ABM pipeline velocity comparison; ABM deal size premium tracked; quarterly ROI review with executive stakeholders |
| 5 | Optimized | Predictive ABM analytics; AI forecasts account conversion probability; incrementality testing validates ABM's true lift | Predictive account scoring with conversion probability; A/B incrementality tests isolate ABM effect; ABM influences 73%+ of revenue at mature organizations [src5] |

**Red flags**: ABM team reports on impressions and clicks rather than pipeline; cannot state the ROI of ABM program; no comparison between ABM and non-ABM account performance; measurement is activity-based not outcome-based. [src6]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "Can you tell me the pipeline value, win rate, and average deal size for ABM-targeted accounts vs non-ABM accounts in the last two quarters?"

### Dimension 5: Buying Group Orchestration

**What this measures**: The ability to identify, engage, and orchestrate outreach to the full buying committee within target accounts — beyond single-contact lead-based engagement.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | Lead-based model; engaging individual contacts, not buying groups; no multi-stakeholder awareness | One contact per account in CRM; outreach to single person; no buying committee mapping; traditional MQL funnel applied to ABM |
| 2 | Emerging | Awareness that buying groups matter; some multi-contact tracking per account; beginning to identify decision-maker roles | Multiple contacts per target account identified; role-based tagging started; still engaging individual contacts independently rather than as a group |
| 3 | Defined | Buying committee mapped for Tier 1 accounts (avg 5-7 roles identified); engagement tracked at buying group level; multi-thread outreach strategy | Buying group template per account; engagement scores at group level; multi-thread outreach to champion, economic buyer, and influencer; avg 11 stakeholders mapped per deal [src1] |
| 4 | Managed | Buying group coverage gaps identified and closed proactively; role-specific content journeys; buying group engagement predicts deal outcome | Coverage analysis identifies missing stakeholders; persona-specific engagement sequences; buying group engagement score predicts pipeline progression |
| 5 | Optimized | AI-powered buying group discovery; automatic stakeholder identification; orchestrated engagement across buying group with sequenced multi-channel touches | AI identifies new stakeholders from intent and engagement data; automated buying group coverage alerts; 2-3x win rates from buying group focus vs lead-based [src1] |

**Red flags**: Only one contact per target account in CRM; no concept of buying committee in ABM strategy; outreach is to a single person regardless of deal size; no tracking of how many stakeholders are engaged per opportunity. [src3]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "For your top 10 target accounts, how many contacts from each buying committee have you identified and engaged in the last 90 days?"

## Scoring & Interpretation

### Overall Score Calculation

Weighted average that emphasizes account selection and sales alignment as foundational elements.

```
Overall Score = (Account Selection × 1.2 + Personalization × 1.0 + Sales Alignment × 1.2 + Measurement × 0.8 + Buying Group × 0.8) / 5.0
```

### Score Interpretation

| Overall Score | Maturity Level | Interpretation | Recommended Next Step |
|---------------|---------------|----------------|----------------------|
| 1.0 - 1.9 | Critical | ABM exists in name only — running targeted campaigns without the strategy, alignment, or measurement to justify the investment | Establish ICP, build shared account list, implement basic account engagement tracking |
| 2.0 - 2.9 | Developing | ABM program is emerging with some account-level focus but lacks the alignment, personalization, or measurement to prove ROI | Build sales-marketing SLA; implement tier-based personalization; establish ABM metrics dashboard |
| 3.0 - 3.9 | Competent | Solid ABM program with defined processes; proving pipeline impact; ready to scale and optimize | Focus on buying group orchestration, predictive scoring, and multi-touch attribution |
| 4.0 - 4.5 | Advanced | ABM is a proven revenue driver with strong sales alignment and measurement; optimize with AI and predictive models | Implement AI-driven account scoring, automated buying group discovery, and incrementality testing |
| 4.6 - 5.0 | Best-in-class | ABM is the primary revenue engine with predictive capabilities, full buying group orchestration, and proven ROI; only 13% of companies reach this level | Maintain innovation edge; pioneer AI-native ABM; share learnings to attract talent [src3] |

## Common Pitfalls in Assessment

- **ABM theater**: Running targeted display ads and calling it ABM. True ABM requires account-level strategy, personalization, sales alignment, and measurement — not just targeted advertising. Validate by asking about sales involvement and account-specific content. [src2]
- **Lead scoring masquerading as account scoring**: Using individual lead scores aggregated to account level rather than true account-level scoring with firmographic, technographic, and intent signals. Ask what data sources feed the scoring model. [src4]
- **Technology over strategy**: Over-investing in ABM technology while under-investing in ICP definition, content personalization, and sales alignment. A $100K ABM platform with a poorly defined ICP produces expensive noise.
- **Pilot paralysis**: Running a small ABM pilot for 2+ years without scaling. If the pilot works, scale it; if it does not, diagnose why and either fix or stop — eternal pilots waste resources. [src3]
