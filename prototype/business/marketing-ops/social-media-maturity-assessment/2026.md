# Social Media Maturity Assessment

## Purpose

This assessment evaluates the maturity of a company's social media marketing program across five dimensions: strategy and governance, content and creative, community and engagement, paid social and performance, and measurement and analytics. The output is a composite maturity score (1-5) that identifies where the social media program is underperforming relative to its potential and routes to specific improvement areas. Use this when social media feels like a time sink without clear ROI, when scaling social presence, or when aligning social strategy with broader marketing objectives. [src1]

## Constraints
<!-- Agents: read before running this assessment with a user. -->

- Requires access to social media analytics and at least 6 months of posting history for reliable scoring
- B2B and B2C social media maturity look fundamentally different — apply segment-appropriate benchmarks
- Platform dynamics shift rapidly — engagement rate benchmarks change quarterly; treat as directional guides
- Assessment is diagnostic only — it identifies maturity gaps, not which specific platforms to prioritize
- Re-run every 6 months or after major platform algorithm changes or strategy pivots

## Assessment Dimensions

<!-- Each dimension is scored independently. The structured format lets agents
     walk through this conversationally with a user, one dimension at a time. -->

### Dimension 1: Strategy & Governance

**What this measures**: Whether social media has a documented strategy tied to business objectives with clear governance, roles, and approval processes.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No documented social media strategy; posting is reactive and ad hoc; no clear ownership or approval process | Intern manages social; no content calendar; posts happen when someone remembers; no brand guidelines for social |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic social presence on 2-3 platforms; informal strategy based on "post regularly"; one person owns social but no governance | Content calendar exists but is inconsistently followed; posting frequency varies widely; no crisis communication plan |
| 3 | Defined | Documented strategy with platform-specific goals tied to marketing objectives; content calendar planned monthly; roles and approval workflow defined | Written strategy document reviewed quarterly; brand voice guidelines for social; crisis communication protocol exists; content calendar planned 30 days ahead |
| 4 | Managed | Social media strategy integrated into overall marketing strategy; executive sponsorship; cross-functional content contributors with governance framework | Strategy aligned with quarterly marketing plans; social listening informs product and PR; dedicated social team with defined roles; agency/tool governance in place |
| 5 | Optimized | Social strategy drives business outcomes with measurable OKRs; agile content production reacting to trends in hours; social as a core revenue and brand channel | Social has its own P&L or clear revenue attribution; real-time trend response capability; social insights inform company-wide decisions; employee advocacy program active |

**Red flags**: No documented strategy; social managed by an intern with no oversight; posting only when someone remembers; no brand voice guidelines for social media. [src2]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "Can you share your social media strategy document, and how are social goals tied to business objectives?"

### Dimension 2: Content & Creative

**What this measures**: The quality, consistency, and variety of content produced for social channels, including format diversity and brand alignment.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | Only text posts or repurposed blog links; no visual assets created for social; inconsistent brand presentation | Generic stock images; same content posted across all platforms without adaptation; no video content |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic branded graphics using templates; some variety in formats; platform-native content occasionally created | Canva templates for posts; occasional short video; content adapted for 1-2 platforms but not all; limited content library |
| 3 | Defined | Platform-specific content formats (carousels, short video, stories); consistent brand visual identity; content pillars defined; mix of educational, entertaining, and promotional | Content pillars documented; short-form video produced regularly; platform-specific adaptations; content repurposing workflow exists |
| 4 | Managed | High-production-value content including professional video; UGC and employee content programs; content performance tracked and optimized per format | A/B testing content formats; UGC collection and curation workflow; format performance analysis drives content mix; 60%+ content is video |
| 5 | Optimized | AI-assisted content creation and personalization; real-time trend-jacking capability; audience-specific content variants; content drives measurable engagement and conversion | AI generates content variants for testing; content production pipeline under 24 hours for trend content; audience segments receive tailored content |

**Red flags**: Only sharing blog links; no video content; same content copy-pasted across all platforms; no content calendar; content quality inconsistent. [src4]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "What percentage of your social content is video, and do you create platform-specific content or cross-post the same thing everywhere?"

### Dimension 3: Community & Engagement

**What this measures**: How actively the brand builds and nurtures a community on social media beyond broadcasting content.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | Social media used as a broadcast channel only; comments and DMs ignored or rarely answered; no community interaction | Post-and-ghost approach; response time to comments/DMs exceeds 48 hours or never; no engagement with follower content |
| 2 | Emerging | Responses to comments and DMs within 24 hours on most posts; some proactive engagement with relevant accounts; basic sentiment monitoring | Reactive community management; some engagement with mentions; no formalized response protocol |
| 3 | Defined | Response SLA (under 4 hours business hours); proactive engagement strategy (commenting on industry content, engaging with prospects); community guidelines published | Formalized response protocols; proactive engagement cadence; social listening for brand mentions; community moderation active |
| 4 | Managed | Community manager role dedicated; active participation in industry conversations; social selling integration; user-generated content encouraged and amplified | Dedicated community management; social listening drives PR and product insights; ambassador or creator program active; social selling playbook |
| 5 | Optimized | Thriving owned community (private group, forum, or ambassador program); community-generated insights drive product decisions; social as a customer acquisition and retention channel | Brand community with organic engagement; community members create content independently; social listening influences product roadmap; NPS promoters activated as advocates |

**Red flags**: DMs go unanswered for days; no response to customer complaints on social; zero proactive engagement with follower or industry content; social used purely for broadcasting. [src3]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "What is your average response time to comments and DMs, and do you proactively engage with accounts beyond your own followers?"

### Dimension 4: Paid Social & Performance

**What this measures**: The sophistication of paid social media campaigns, including targeting, optimization, and ROI measurement.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No paid social spend; or boosting posts randomly with no targeting strategy; no conversion tracking | Occasional boosted posts; no audience targeting beyond basic demographics; no pixel or conversion tracking installed |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic paid campaigns on 1-2 platforms; interest-based targeting; some conversion tracking; ROAS measured but unreliable | Campaign manager used on Meta or LinkedIn; basic audience targeting; conversion pixel installed but not fully configured |
| 3 | Defined | Multi-platform paid strategy; lookalike and retargeting audiences; A/B testing of creative and audiences; ROAS tracked reliably by campaign | Full-funnel paid strategy (awareness + consideration + conversion); retargeting active; creative testing cadence; CPA and ROAS reported monthly |
| 4 | Managed | Advanced audience segmentation using first-party data; dynamic creative optimization; cross-platform attribution; paid social integrated with CRM | First-party data uploaded for custom audiences; dynamic product ads; cross-platform frequency management; leads tracked through to revenue in CRM |
| 5 | Optimized | AI-driven bid optimization and budget allocation; predictive audience modeling; incrementality testing for paid social; paid social is a measurable revenue engine | ML optimizes bids in real-time; incrementality tests validate paid social lift; budget allocation automated across platforms; clear revenue attribution |

**Red flags**: Boosting posts as entire paid strategy; no retargeting; paid social spend with no conversion tracking; ROAS unknown or unmeasured; no creative testing. [src5]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "What is your paid social ROAS, and do you use retargeting and lookalike audiences?"

### Dimension 5: Measurement & Analytics

**What this measures**: How rigorously social media performance is measured, reported, and used to inform strategy decisions.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No regular reporting; metrics limited to follower count and likes; no business outcome tracking | Vanity metrics only (followers, likes); no reporting cadence; analytics tools not configured; no link tracking |
| 2 | Emerging | Monthly reporting on engagement metrics; UTM-tagged links for some campaigns; basic awareness of reach and impressions | Monthly analytics review; some UTM tracking; engagement rate calculated; but no revenue or conversion attribution |
| 3 | Defined | Monthly reporting tied to marketing KPIs; engagement, reach, click-through, and conversion all tracked; social analytics tool in use | UTM tracking standard; conversion tracking active; monthly report includes engagement, traffic, and leads from social; benchmarked against prior periods |
| 4 | Managed | Real-time dashboards; social attributed to pipeline and revenue; competitive benchmarking; sentiment analysis | Live dashboards showing social contribution to pipeline; social ROI calculated; competitive share of voice tracked; sentiment trends monitored |
| 5 | Optimized | Predictive analytics for content performance; social data integrated into marketing attribution model; social listening drives business intelligence | AI predicts content performance before posting; social data feeds into MMM or MTA; social intelligence shared across product, sales, and PR teams |

**Red flags**: No reporting beyond follower count; no UTM tracking on social links; unable to measure social media's contribution to traffic, leads, or revenue; no analytics tool in use. [src6]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "Can you show me last month's social media report, and does it include traffic and leads generated — not just likes and followers?"

## Scoring & Interpretation

### Overall Score Calculation

All dimensions weighted equally for a general assessment. For B2B companies, weight Strategy and Measurement more heavily (1.5x). For B2C/DTC, weight Content and Paid Social more heavily (1.5x).

```
Overall Score = (Strategy + Content + Community + Paid Social + Measurement) / 5
```

### Score Interpretation

| Overall Score | Maturity Level | Interpretation | Recommended Next Step |
|---------------|---------------|----------------|----------------------|
| 1.0 - 1.9 | Critical | Social media is a time sink with no measurable business impact; either invest properly or reduce to minimal presence | Document strategy and goals; assign clear ownership; implement basic analytics tracking |
| 2.0 - 2.9 | Developing | Social has some foundation but lacks consistency, measurement, and integration with broader marketing | Create content calendar with content pillars; implement UTM tracking; set up paid social with retargeting |
| 3.0 - 3.9 | Competent | Solid social media operation with measurement in place; ready for optimization and scaling | Scale paid social with advanced targeting; build community programs; integrate social data with CRM |
| 4.0 - 4.5 | Advanced | High-performing social media program driving measurable business outcomes | Implement AI-driven content optimization; build employee advocacy program; scale social commerce |
| 4.6 - 5.0 | Best-in-class | Social media is a core business channel with predictive analytics and proven revenue impact | Innovate with emerging platforms and formats; social intelligence drives company-wide decisions |

## Common Pitfalls in Assessment

- **Vanity metric obsession**: Teams celebrate follower growth while ignoring engagement rate decline. A 50K-follower account with 0.5% engagement is less valuable than a 5K account with 5% engagement. Focus on engagement quality and conversion metrics. [src1]
- **Platform proliferation**: Being on every platform poorly is worse than being excellent on 2-3. Score platform selection quality, not quantity. B2B companies do not need TikTok; B2C companies may not need LinkedIn.
- **Organic-only trap**: Organic reach has declined to 2-5% on most platforms. Teams that refuse to invest in paid social while expecting organic growth are fighting a losing battle. Assess paid and organic as complementary strategies.
- **B2B/B2C benchmark mixing**: B2B engagement rates (0.5-2%) and B2C engagement rates (1-5%) are fundamentally different. Applying B2C benchmarks to B2B social creates unrealistic expectations and false alarms.
