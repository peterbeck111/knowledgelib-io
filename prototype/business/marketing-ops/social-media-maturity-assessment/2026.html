Social Media Maturity Assessment

How mature is social media strategy — organic reach, paid ROI, platform selection, B2B vs B2C?

Type: Assessment Confidence: 0.83 Sources: 6 Verified: 2026-03-10

Purpose

This assessment evaluates the maturity of a company's social media marketing program across five dimensions: strategy and governance, content and creative, community and engagement, paid social and performance, and measurement and analytics. The output is a composite maturity score (1-5) that identifies where the social media program is underperforming. [src1]

Constraints

  • Requires social media analytics and at least 6 months of posting history
  • B2B and B2C social media maturity are fundamentally different — use appropriate benchmarks
  • Platform dynamics shift rapidly — engagement benchmarks change quarterly
  • Assessment is diagnostic only — does not prescribe which platforms to prioritize
  • Re-run every 6 months or after major platform algorithm changes or strategy pivots

Common Pitfalls in Assessment

  • Vanity metric obsession: Celebrating follower growth while engagement declines. Focus on engagement quality and conversions. [src1]
  • Platform proliferation: Being on every platform poorly is worse than excelling on 2-3.
  • Organic-only trap: Organic reach has declined to 2-5%. Refusing to invest in paid social while expecting growth is unsustainable.
  • B2B/B2C benchmark mixing: B2B engagement rates (0.5-2%) and B2C (1-5%) are different. Cross-applying creates false alarms.
