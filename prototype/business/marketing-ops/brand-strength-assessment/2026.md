# Brand Strength Assessment

## Purpose

This assessment evaluates the health and strength of a brand across five dimensions: brand awareness and recall, share of voice, sentiment and perception, customer loyalty (NPS correlation), and brand consistency. It is designed for CMOs, brand managers, and marketing leaders who need to diagnose whether their brand is growing, stagnating, or declining relative to competitors. The output quantifies brand health across measurable dimensions and routes to specific improvement actions for each weak area. [src1]

## Constraints
<!-- Agents: read before running this assessment with a user. -->

- Requires brand tracking survey data or social listening tool access — without either, only Dimensions 1 (awareness via search data) and 5 (consistency audit) can be reliably scored
- NPS data requires minimum 100 responses per measurement period for statistical significance; smaller samples produce unreliable scores
- Most meaningful for companies with $500K+ annual marketing spend — early-stage startups should focus on product-market fit before brand assessment
- This assessment evaluates brand health and perception, NOT financial brand valuation — use separate financial models for M&A or balance sheet purposes
- Re-run quarterly for trend tracking; full reassessment annually or after major brand events (rebrand, crisis, acquisition)

## Assessment Dimensions

<!-- Each dimension is scored independently. The structured format lets agents
     walk through this conversationally with a user, one dimension at a time. -->

### Dimension 1: Brand Awareness & Recall

**What this measures**: The extent to which the target audience recognizes and can recall the brand, measured through both aided and unaided awareness, branded search volume, and direct traffic trends.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No brand awareness measurement; brand is unknown outside existing customer base | No branded search volume tracked; unaided awareness below 5% in target market; direct traffic negligible |
| 2 | Emerging | Some brand awareness exists but is not systematically measured; brand known only in narrow niche | Branded search volume exists but flat or declining; aided awareness 10-25%; brand recall inconsistent |
| 3 | Defined | Brand awareness systematically tracked; aided awareness 25-50% in target market; branded search growing | Quarterly brand tracking survey in place; branded search volume growing 10%+ YoY; Google Trends shows positive trajectory [src4] |
| 4 | Managed | Strong awareness with high unaided recall; brand is a considered option for majority of target buyers | Unaided awareness 30%+; aided awareness 50-70%; branded search volume significantly above category average; direct traffic is substantial channel |
| 5 | Optimized | Brand is category-defining; top-of-mind for target audience; brand name used as verb or synonym for category | Unaided awareness 50%+; aided awareness 80%+; branded search dominates category; brand is referenced in AI responses for category queries [src2] |

**Red flags**: Cannot cite any brand awareness metrics; no branded search volume tracked; zero brand tracking surveys conducted; brand is never mentioned in earned media. [src1]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "If you surveyed 100 people in your target market, how many would name your brand unprompted when asked about your category?"

### Dimension 2: Share of Voice (SOV)

**What this measures**: The proportion of total market conversation, media coverage, social mentions, and search visibility that the brand captures relative to competitors.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No share of voice tracking; competitor mentions not monitored | No social listening tool; no competitive media monitoring; cannot compare brand visibility to competitors |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic social media monitoring exists; SOV tracked on one or two channels but not across the full landscape | Social listening tool captures mentions but no competitive benchmarking; SOV known for social but not search, media, or events |
| 3 | Defined | SOV tracked across social, search, and earned media; competitive benchmarking updated quarterly | SOV dashboard covers social mentions, branded search share, and media coverage; competitive set of 3-5 brands tracked quarterly [src1] |
| 4 | Managed | SOV tracked in real-time across all channels; SOV vs share of market (SOM) correlation analyzed; excess SOV strategy in place | Real-time SOV dashboard; SOV/SOM ratio tracked (Binet & Field's extra SOV principle applied); SOV investment strategy documented |
| 5 | Optimized | SOV dominance in category; AI search share of voice tracked; predictive models forecast SOV impact on market share | Category-leading SOV; AI overview brand mention rate tracked; SOV drives market share budget allocation models [src3] |

**Red flags**: Cannot state what percentage of category conversation the brand owns; no competitive monitoring in place; SOV has been declining for 2+ quarters without response. [src2]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "What percentage of total category mentions (social + search + media) does your brand capture, and how does that compare to your market share?"

### Dimension 3: Sentiment & Perception

**What this measures**: How positively or negatively the brand is perceived across customer reviews, social media, press coverage, and survey data — and whether perception aligns with intended positioning.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No sentiment tracking; brand perception unknown beyond anecdotal feedback | No review monitoring; no social sentiment analysis; perception based on assumptions from sales conversations |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic review monitoring on key platforms (G2, Trustpilot, Google Reviews); sentiment measured but not systematically analyzed | Reviews monitored on 1-2 platforms; overall sentiment tracked but not segmented by attribute, audience, or time period |
| 3 | Defined | Systematic sentiment analysis across reviews, social media, and earned media; brand perception survey conducted at least annually | Sentiment dashboard covering reviews, social, and media; brand attributes tracked (innovative, trustworthy, etc.); perception vs positioning gap identified [src6] |
| 4 | Managed | Real-time sentiment monitoring with alert thresholds; sentiment segmented by audience, product line, and geography; crisis detection automated | Real-time sentiment alerts; audience-specific sentiment profiles; brand perception trends analyzed monthly; crisis early warning system |
| 5 | Optimized | AI-powered sentiment analysis with nuance detection; competitive sentiment benchmarking; brand perception predicts business outcomes | ML sentiment analysis detects sarcasm, context, and emerging themes; sentiment scores correlate with lead quality and close rates; AI brand perception tracked [src1] |

**Red flags**: No one monitors online reviews; last brand perception survey was 2+ years ago; significant gap between intended brand positioning and actual perception with no action plan. [src2]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "What are the top 3 words customers use to describe your brand, and do they match what you intend?"

### Dimension 4: Customer Loyalty & NPS Correlation

**What this measures**: The depth of customer commitment to the brand, measured through NPS, retention rates, referral behavior, and the correlation between brand strength and customer loyalty metrics.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No NPS or loyalty tracking; customer retention data not connected to brand metrics | No NPS survey; churn tracked operationally but not analyzed for brand-related causes; no referral program |
| 2 | Emerging | NPS measured but infrequently (annual or less); NPS not segmented or correlated with other brand metrics | Annual NPS survey with overall score only; no segment-level analysis; NPS not discussed in brand strategy meetings |
| 3 | Defined | NPS measured quarterly with segment-level breakdowns; NPS trends tracked; basic correlation with retention analyzed | Quarterly NPS by segment; promoter/detractor driver analysis conducted; NPS trend dashboard exists; referral rate tracked [src5] |
| 4 | Managed | NPS fully integrated into brand health model; NPS correlated with revenue expansion, retention, and referral rates; closed-loop feedback system | NPS-to-revenue correlation established; detractor recovery program; promoter referral activation; NPS predicts expansion revenue |
| 5 | Optimized | Predictive loyalty modeling beyond NPS; customer lifetime value correlated with brand engagement; brand community drives organic growth | Multi-metric loyalty model (NPS + CSAT + CES); predictive churn model includes brand engagement signals; brand community generates 20%+ of new logos [src5] |

**Red flags**: NPS has not been measured in 12+ months; NPS score exists but nobody acts on detractor feedback; brand team and customer success team do not share NPS data. [src3]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "What is your current NPS, how has it trended over the last 4 quarters, and what do you do with detractor feedback?"

### Dimension 5: Brand Consistency & Governance

**What this measures**: How consistently the brand is presented across all touchpoints — visual identity, messaging, tone of voice, and experience — and whether governance processes ensure consistency at scale.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No brand guidelines; brand presentation varies wildly across teams, channels, and regions | No brand style guide; logo used inconsistently; messaging varies by channel; no brand approval process |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic brand guidelines exist but are not enforced; significant inconsistencies across channels | Brand guide exists but is outdated or not followed; sales creates materials without brand review; partner materials off-brand |
| 3 | Defined | Comprehensive brand guidelines covering visual identity, messaging, and tone; brand review process exists for key materials | Up-to-date brand guide; templates for common materials; brand review required for external materials; basic brand training for new hires |
| 4 | Managed | Brand governance system with digital asset management; brand compliance measured and tracked; localization guidelines for international markets | DAM system with approved assets; brand compliance audit conducted annually; localization playbook for key markets; brand council or committee meets monthly |
| 5 | Optimized | Automated brand compliance monitoring; AI-powered brand consistency tools; brand architecture fully documented for multi-product or multi-brand portfolios | Automated brand compliance checking on new materials; brand architecture clearly maps sub-brands and endorsed brands; brand training is part of onboarding; brand equity protected legally [src2] |

**Red flags**: Brand guide is 3+ years old; different teams use different logos or color schemes; customer experience of the brand varies dramatically by channel; no brand approval process exists.
**Quick diagnostic question**: "If I looked at your website, sales deck, email campaigns, and social media today, would they look and sound like they came from the same company?"

## Scoring & Interpretation

### Overall Score Calculation

Equal-weighted average across all five dimensions, as each represents a critical pillar of brand strength.

```
Overall Score = (Awareness & Recall + Share of Voice + Sentiment & Perception + Customer Loyalty & NPS + Brand Consistency) / 5
```

### Score Interpretation

| Overall Score | Maturity Level | Interpretation | Recommended Next Step |
|---------------|---------------|----------------|----------------------|
| 1.0 - 1.9 | Critical | Brand is invisible or damaged; no measurement infrastructure; vulnerable to competitors and crises | Establish brand tracking fundamentals: NPS survey, social listening, branded search monitoring |
| 2.0 - 2.9 | Developing | Brand exists but is inconsistently managed and measured; awareness is limited to existing customers | Build brand tracking infrastructure; define and enforce brand guidelines; implement quarterly measurement |
| 3.0 - 3.9 | Competent | Brand is systematically tracked and managed; awareness is solid in core market; ready to scale | Focus on SOV growth strategy; deepen sentiment analysis; strengthen loyalty-to-revenue correlation |
| 4.0 - 4.5 | Advanced | Brand is a competitive advantage; well-governed and measured; driving business outcomes | Optimize AI brand visibility; build brand community; pioneer predictive brand models |
| 4.6 - 5.0 | Best-in-class | Brand is category-defining with dominant awareness, sentiment, and loyalty; brand is a moat | Maintain innovation edge; explore brand extension; ensure AI platforms cite and recommend the brand |

## Common Pitfalls in Assessment

- **Awareness vs equity confusion**: High aided awareness does not equal strong brand. A brand can be well-known and poorly perceived. Always score awareness and sentiment independently. [src2]
- **NPS in isolation**: NPS is one signal, not the complete picture. A high NPS with declining SOV means loyal customers but shrinking reach. Assess all dimensions together.
- **Internal perception bias**: Brand teams consistently overestimate sentiment and consistency scores. Validate with external data: customer surveys, social listening, and third-party audits. [src1]
- **Measurement without action**: Many organizations track brand metrics quarterly but never change strategy based on the data. Assess whether brand data actually drives decisions, not just dashboards.
