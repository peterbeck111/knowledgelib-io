Brand Strength Assessment

How to assess brand strength — awareness, share of voice, sentiment, NPS correlation?

Type: Assessment Confidence: 0.82 Sources: 6 Verified: 2026-03-09

Purpose

This assessment evaluates the health and strength of a brand across five dimensions: brand awareness and recall, share of voice, sentiment and perception, customer loyalty (NPS correlation), and brand consistency. It is designed for CMOs, brand managers, and marketing leaders who need to diagnose whether their brand is growing, stagnating, or declining relative to competitors. The output quantifies brand health across measurable dimensions and routes to specific improvement actions for each weak area. [src1]

Constraints

  • Requires brand tracking survey data or social listening tool access — without either, only partial scoring is possible
  • NPS data requires minimum 100 responses per measurement period for statistical significance
  • Most meaningful for companies with $500K+ annual marketing spend
  • Evaluates brand health and perception, NOT financial brand valuation
  • Re-run quarterly for trend tracking; full reassessment annually or after major brand events

Common Pitfalls in Assessment

  • Awareness vs equity confusion: High aided awareness does not equal strong brand. A brand can be well-known and poorly perceived. Score awareness and sentiment independently.
  • NPS in isolation: High NPS with declining SOV means loyal customers but shrinking reach. Assess all dimensions together.
  • Internal perception bias: Brand teams overestimate sentiment and consistency. Validate with customer surveys, social listening, and third-party audits.
  • Measurement without action: Tracking brand metrics quarterly but never changing strategy. Assess whether data drives decisions, not just dashboards.
