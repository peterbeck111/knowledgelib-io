# Remote Team Setup Guide

## Purpose

This recipe produces a fully operational remote team infrastructure — configured communication tools, documented async-first norms, a time zone overlap policy, virtual culture rituals, security/access setup, and a remote onboarding flow — enabling a distributed team to collaborate effectively from day one. [src1]

## Prerequisites
<!-- Agents: verify ALL prerequisites before executing. Missing prerequisites = failed execution. -->

- [ ] **Team roster with locations** available from founder or HR — list of names, roles, cities, and UTC offsets
- [ ] **Company values document** available from founder — 3-5 core values and working principles
- [ ] **Budget approval** — approved monthly per-person budget for SaaS tools
- [ ] **Domain for email** — company email domain configured (required for SSO and tool provisioning)
- [ ] **Admin access** — founder or ops lead has admin credentials for creating workspace accounts

## Constraints
<!-- Hard rules. Agents: enforce throughout execution. Violating these = broken output or legal risk. -->

- Async-first is the default communication mode. Real-time meetings are reserved for relationship-building, brainstorming, and conflict resolution — never for one-directional status updates. [src2]
- Every tool decision not written in the handbook is invisible to the team. Documentation is the single source of truth. [src1]
- Minimum 2-hour daily overlap window across all time zones. Teams spanning 12+ hours require a "follow-the-sun" handoff protocol. [src5]
- Zero-trust security posture: SSO + MFA on all tools, no shared passwords, device management for company data access. [src1]
- Remote onboarding requires a structured 2-4 week program with assigned buddy — do not assume new hires will "figure it out." [src6]

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Select and Configure Communication Tools

**Duration**: 2-4 hours
**Tool**: Slack (or alternative)

Set up the primary real-time communication platform with a channel architecture that separates signal from noise.

```
Channel structure (create these channels):

#general          — Company-wide announcements (posting restricted to leadership)
#random           — Watercooler, off-topic, social
#team-{name}      — Per-team channels (e.g., #team-engineering, #team-product)
#project-{name}   — Per-project channels (time-bound, archived when done)
#standup          — Daily async standups (bot-assisted or manual)
#decisions        — All decisions logged with context and rationale
#kudos            — Public recognition and wins
#help-{topic}     — Support channels (e.g., #help-it, #help-hr, #help-tools)
```

Configure notification defaults: set "Do Not Disturb" from 6 PM to 9 AM local time, disable @channel for non-admins, require threads for all replies. [src2]

**Verify**: All team members can access workspace, channels are created, notification defaults are set.
**If failed**: Check email domain restrictions, re-send invitations, verify SSO configuration.

### Step 2: Establish Async-First Communication Norms

**Duration**: 4-6 hours (writing + team review)
**Tool**: Notion (or documentation platform)

Create the communication handbook section that defines when to use which channel and expected response times. This is the most critical step — without explicit norms, teams default to synchronous interruptions. [src3]

```
Communication norms document structure:

1. RESPONSE TIME EXPECTATIONS
   - @mention in Slack: respond within 4 business hours (not minutes)
   - Notion comment: respond within 24 hours
   - Email: respond within 24-48 hours
   - Emergency (outage/security): call or use #incidents channel — 15 min SLA

2. MEETING RULES
   - Default to async: "Could this be a Loom video or Notion doc instead?"
   - All meetings require a written agenda (shared 24h before)
   - All meetings produce written notes with action items (posted within 2h)
   - No meeting Wednesdays (company-wide deep work day)
   - Maximum 4 hours of meetings per person per week

3. ASYNC UPDATE FORMAT (daily standup)
   Post by end of your workday in #standup:
   - Done: What I completed today
   - Doing: What I'm working on tomorrow
   - Blocked: What I need help with (tag specific person)

4. DECISION-MAKING PROTOCOL
   - Write a proposal in Notion with context, options, and recommendation
   - Tag stakeholders with a review deadline (48-72 hours)
   - Silence = consent after the deadline passes
   - Log the decision in #decisions with link to proposal
```

Doist reports that 95% of their communication is async, with a 24-hour expected response window, and they have achieved 90% employee retention over 5 years with this model. [src3]

**Verify**: Communication handbook is published, all team members have read and acknowledged it.
**If failed**: Schedule a synchronous walkthrough session to answer questions and get buy-in.

### Step 3: Design Time Zone Overlap Policy

**Duration**: 2-3 hours
**Tool**: Notion + World Time Buddy or Similar

Map your team's time zones and define collaboration windows.

```
Time zone mapping exercise:

1. List all team members with their UTC offsets
2. Identify the maximum gap (e.g., UTC-8 to UTC+5 = 13 hours)
3. Find the natural overlap window where most people are in working hours
4. Define two tiers:
   - CORE HOURS: 2-4 hours when ALL team members are available
     (e.g., 9 AM-1 PM ET = 3 PM-7 PM CET = 10:30 PM-2:30 AM IST)
   - FLEX OVERLAP: additional hours when most people are available
     (e.g., 1 PM-5 PM ET = 7 PM-11 PM CET)

5. Rules for core hours:
   - All synchronous meetings happen during core hours only
   - Core hours are for collaboration, not heads-down work
   - Rotate meeting times quarterly so no region always gets the worst slot
```

For teams spanning 12+ hours (e.g., US West Coast to Southeast Asia), implement a follow-the-sun handoff protocol: each shift writes a handoff note at end-of-day summarizing work completed, decisions pending, and blockers — posted in the relevant project channel. [src5]

**Verify**: Time zone map published, core hours defined, all team members confirm the schedule works.
**If failed**: If no reasonable overlap exists, split into sub-teams by region with async-only cross-team communication and one weekly sync.

### Step 4: Build Virtual Culture Rituals

**Duration**: 3-4 hours (design) + ongoing
**Tool**: Slack + Loom + Notion

Remote culture does not happen organically — it must be designed and maintained. Build these five rituals: [src4] [src6]

```
Culture ritual calendar:

WEEKLY:
1. Virtual coffee roulette (Mon) — random 1:1 pairings via Donut bot in Slack
   Duration: 15-30 min, unstructured, no work topics required

2. Team wins roundup (Fri) — async post in #kudos
   Each person shares: one win, one learning, one appreciation for a teammate

BIWEEKLY:
3. Show & Tell (every other Wed) — 30 min synchronous during core hours
   2-3 team members demo something they built, learned, or are excited about
   Record on Loom for those who can't attend live

MONTHLY:
4. Virtual team activity (first Fri) — 60 min synchronous
   Rotate format: trivia, show your workspace, cooking together, online game
   Budget: $20-50/person for food/supplies delivered to their home

QUARTERLY:
5. All-hands + retrospective (synchronous, 90 min)
   Company updates, OKR review, open Q&A, anonymous feedback collection
   Follow up with written summary and action items within 24 hours
```

**Verify**: Rituals are scheduled in shared calendar, Donut bot or equivalent is configured, first month of activities is planned.
**If failed**: Start with just two rituals (coffee roulette + weekly wins) and add more as the team grows.

### Step 5: Set Up Security and Access Management

**Duration**: 4-8 hours
**Tool**: 1Password or Okta + device management

Distributed teams have a larger attack surface than co-located teams. Implement zero-trust principles from day one. [src1]

```
Security setup checklist:

1. IDENTITY & ACCESS
   - [ ] Set up SSO provider (1Password, Okta, or JumpCloud)
   - [ ] Enforce MFA on all accounts (hardware keys preferred, TOTP minimum)
   - [ ] Create role-based access groups (Engineering, Product, Marketing, Admin)
   - [ ] Principle of least privilege: default to read-only, grant write access per-project

2. DEVICE SECURITY
   - [ ] Require full-disk encryption on all devices (FileVault/BitLocker)
   - [ ] Install endpoint protection (company-managed or BYOD policy)
   - [ ] Enable remote wipe capability for company data
   - [ ] Mandate automatic OS updates

3. DATA HANDLING
   - [ ] Define data classification (public, internal, confidential, restricted)
   - [ ] No company data on personal cloud storage (Dropbox, personal Google Drive)
   - [ ] Encrypted communication for confidential topics (Signal or E2E-encrypted Slack)
   - [ ] VPN for accessing internal services (if applicable)

4. ACCESS LIFECYCLE
   - [ ] Automated provisioning: new hire gets all accounts within 24 hours
   - [ ] Automated deprovisioning: departing employee loses all access within 1 hour
   - [ ] Quarterly access review: audit who has access to what
```

**Verify**: All team members have SSO + MFA enabled, access groups are configured, security policy is documented.
**If failed**: If budget doesn't allow SSO, enforce strong unique passwords via 1Password Teams ($4/user/month) as minimum baseline.

### Step 6: Create Remote Onboarding Flow

**Duration**: 6-8 hours (one-time design)
**Tool**: Notion + Loom + Slack

Design a structured onboarding program that takes new hires from zero to productive in 2-4 weeks. Remote onboarding fails when companies treat it as "send a laptop and a Slack invite." [src6]

```
Remote onboarding timeline:

PRE-DAY 1 (1 week before):
- Ship laptop + peripherals (webcam, headset, monitor if budget allows)
- Send welcome email with: start date, first-day schedule, buddy assignment
- Pre-provision all tool accounts (SSO makes this instant)
- Add to #general and team channel in Slack

WEEK 1: ORIENTATION
- Day 1: Welcome call with manager (30 min) + buddy intro (30 min)
- Day 1: Self-paced handbook reading (communication norms, values, security)
- Day 2: Tool setup workshop (Loom recording available for async)
- Day 3: 1:1s with each team member (15 min each, spread across the week)
- Day 4: Shadow a team standup or project meeting
- Day 5: First small task assigned (designed to ship within 1 day)

WEEK 2: IMMERSION
- Complete first project or meaningful contribution
- Attend all recurring team rituals
- Buddy check-in: 15 min daily, shifting to every-other-day by end of week
- Manager check-in: 30 min mid-week

WEEK 3-4: AUTONOMY
- Own a workstream or project independently
- Contribute to a decision proposal in Notion
- Buddy check-in: twice per week, then as-needed
- Manager 1:1: weekly 30 min (continues permanently)
- 30-day feedback survey sent on Day 28

SUCCESS CRITERIA (Day 30):
- [ ] Has shipped at least one meaningful deliverable
- [ ] Can navigate all tools independently
- [ ] Has met every team member 1:1
- [ ] Knows who to ask for what (documented in team directory)
- [ ] Reports feeling "connected" on 30-day survey (score >= 7/10)
```

**Verify**: Onboarding template is published in Notion, buddy program is documented, first onboarding is scheduled.
**If failed**: Start with a simpler 1-week checklist and iterate based on feedback from the first 3 hires.

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Replicating the Office Online
Filling the calendar with daily standups, weekly all-hands, biweekly 1:1s, and ad-hoc "quick syncs" creates a remote team that is always in meetings and never doing deep work. GitLab's handbook explicitly states that meetings are for catalyzing async work, not replacing it. [src2]

### Correct: Async-Default with Intentional Sync
Default to written communication (Notion docs, Loom videos, Slack threads). Reserve synchronous time for relationship-building, creative brainstorming, and conflict resolution. Track meeting hours per person and enforce a weekly cap.

### Wrong: Hiring Across All Time Zones Without a Policy
Hiring the "best person regardless of location" sounds progressive but creates unmanageable overlap problems. A team spanning UTC-8 to UTC+8 has zero natural overlap hours, forcing someone to work nights permanently. [src5]

### Correct: Hire Within Overlap Constraints
Define acceptable time zone bands before opening a role. State the required overlap hours in the job posting. For global teams, organize into regional sub-teams with async-only cross-team protocols.

### Wrong: Treating Remote Onboarding as "Send Laptop + Slack Invite"
New hires who receive no structured onboarding in a remote setting report feeling lost and disconnected within the first week, leading to early attrition. [src6]

### Correct: Design a Structured 4-Week Program
Assign a buddy, create a day-by-day schedule for Week 1, build in social time with every team member, and set a clear 30-day milestone. Measure success with a satisfaction survey.
