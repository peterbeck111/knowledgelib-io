# Recruiting Strategy for Startups

## Purpose

This recipe builds a complete startup recruiting infrastructure — sourcing channels configured across Wellfound, LinkedIn, and referral networks; an ATS with structured interview scorecards; and a metrics dashboard tracking cost per hire, time to fill, and channel effectiveness. The output is a repeatable hiring machine that fills roles at $4,800-$12,000 cost per hire instead of the $25,000-$50,000 that agency recruiting costs for the same positions. [src2]

## Prerequisites
<!-- Agents: verify ALL prerequisites before executing. Missing prerequisites = failed execution. -->

- [ ] **Job descriptions** for all open roles — clear responsibilities, requirements, and compensation bands
- [ ] **Ideal candidate profiles** per role — must-have vs nice-to-have skills, experience level, culture signals
- [ ] **Compensation bands** with equity allocation — see [Startup Compensation Benchmarks](/business/startup-hiring/startup-compensation-benchmarks/2026)
- [ ] **Wellfound account** — sign up at [wellfound.com/recruit](https://wellfound.com/recruit) (free tier: unlimited job posts)
- [ ] **LinkedIn company page** claimed and populated with logo, description, and employee connections
- [ ] **Budget decision** — free-tier-only vs paid tools ($200-$500/month range)

## Constraints
<!-- Hard rules. Agents: enforce throughout execution. Violating these = broken output or legal risk. -->

- LinkedIn Recruiter Lite costs ~$170/month for 30 InMail credits. Each additional InMail is $10. Budget InMails for highest-value outreach only. [src2]
- Wellfound Basic (free) allows unlimited posts but limited sourcing. Essentials at $149/month unlocks advanced filters and screening questions. [src1]
- Employee referral bonuses in tech average $5,000 per successful hire. Budget this before announcing program to avoid reneging on promises. [src6]
- GDPR applies to all EU candidate data — store consent records, honor deletion requests within 30 days, and maintain data processing agreements with any third-party tools.
- Never skip structured scorecards. Companies using structured interviews are 81% more likely to make quality hires. Unstructured interviews have near-random predictive validity. [src5]

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Configure Your ATS

**Duration**: 2-4 hours
**Tool**: Ashby, Greenhouse, Lever, or Google Sheets (based on path)

Set up your applicant tracking system with a standardized pipeline. Every candidate moves through the same stages regardless of role.

**Standard pipeline stages**:
1. Applied / Sourced
2. Resume Screen (pass/reject within 48 hours)
3. Recruiter Phone Screen (20-30 minutes)
4. Hiring Manager Screen (30-45 minutes)
5. Technical / Work Sample Assessment (role-dependent)
6. Team Interview (2-3 interviewers, 45 minutes each)
7. Reference Check
8. Offer

For Path A (free), create a Google Sheet with columns: Candidate Name, Role, Source Channel, Stage, Days in Stage, Interviewer Notes, Scorecard Link, Disposition, Date.

**Verify**: Pipeline has 6-8 stages, all stages have clear pass/reject criteria documented, and at least one test candidate can move through the full flow.
**If failed**: Simplify to 5 stages — Screen, Manager Interview, Work Sample, Team Interview, Offer.

### Step 2: Launch Sourcing Channels

**Duration**: 3-5 hours
**Tool**: Wellfound, LinkedIn, job boards

**Wellfound setup**:
- Create company profile with funding stage, team size, tech stack, perks, and equity range
- Post each role with detailed descriptions — Wellfound candidates expect transparency on equity and salary
- Enable the "Interested" feature so candidates can signal interest before applying
- Wellfound's 8M+ profile database skews toward startup-ready candidates who expect equity and rapid growth [src1]

**LinkedIn setup**:
- Optimize company page: logo, banner, About section mentioning funding and mission
- For Path B+: activate LinkedIn Recruiter Lite (~$170/month) for advanced search filters and 30 InMails/month
- Use Boolean search strings: `("software engineer" OR "backend developer") AND ("startup" OR "Series A") NOT "agency"`
- Save searches for repeated sourcing — LinkedIn will notify you of new matching profiles

**Additional free channels** (all paths):
- Hacker News "Who's Hiring" thread (posted 1st of each month) — high-quality engineering candidates
- Work at a Startup (YC) — if YC-backed or can get listed through network
- Relevant Slack communities (e.g., Rands Leadership Slack, Elpha for women in tech, specific tech stack communities)
- Twitter/X — founders posting hiring threads get 3-5x engagement vs corporate job posts

**Verify**: At least 3 channels live with active job posts. Each post has salary/equity transparency, clear role description, and application instructions.
**If failed**: Start with Wellfound + LinkedIn only — these two channels cover 60-70% of startup candidate flow.

### Step 3: Build Your Referral Program

**Duration**: 2-3 hours
**Tool**: Internal documentation + communication

Employee referrals account for 30-50% of all hires at young companies and produce candidates who are hired 55% faster with 82% retention rates. [src6]

**Referral program components**:

1. **Bonus structure**: Set referral bonuses by role level:
   - Individual contributor: $2,500-$5,000
   - Senior / Staff: $5,000-$7,500
   - Director+: $7,500-$10,000
   - Payment schedule: 50% at start date, 50% after 90-day retention

2. **Submission process**: Create a simple form (Google Form or Typeform) with fields: Candidate name, LinkedIn URL, role referred for, relationship to referrer, why they'd be a good fit.

3. **Tracking**: Add "Referral" as a source channel in your ATS. Track referral-to-hire conversion rate (benchmark: 20-30% of referred candidates get hired vs 1-3% from job boards).

4. **Launch communication**: Send team-wide announcement with: open roles list, bonus amounts per role, how to submit, timeline for feedback on referrals (commit to 48-hour acknowledgment).

**Verify**: Referral program documented, form created, team notified, and at least one referral submitted within first week.
**If failed**: Simplify — send a Slack message listing open roles and asking teammates to DM you names. Formalize later.

### Step 4: Design Structured Interview Scorecards

**Duration**: 3-5 hours
**Tool**: ATS scorecard feature or Google Docs template

Structured interviews with scorecards are 81% more predictive of job performance than unstructured conversations. [src5]

**Scorecard design per role**:

1. **Define 3-4 evaluation categories** (mutually exclusive, collectively exhaustive): [src5]
   - Technical competence (role-specific skills)
   - Problem-solving ability (analytical thinking, debugging approach)
   - Communication and collaboration (clarity, empathy, conflict resolution)
   - Culture and motivation (alignment with mission, growth mindset, ownership)

2. **Set 4-6 attributes per category** with clear behavioral anchors:
   - Each attribute rated 1-4: Strong No (1), Lean No (2), Lean Yes (3), Strong Yes (4)
   - Include "Did Not Observe" option — do not force a rating without evidence [src4]
   - Write specific behavioral examples for each rating level

3. **Assign focus areas per interviewer**: Each interviewer covers 1-2 categories only. No duplication. This prevents every interviewer asking the same questions and ensures complete coverage. [src4]

4. **Create question banks**: 3-5 questions per attribute, mixing behavioral ("Tell me about a time...") and situational ("How would you handle...") formats.

5. **Build debrief template**: After all interviews, hiring team meets within 24 hours. Each interviewer presents their scorecard categories only. Overall recommendation: Strong Hire, Hire, No Hire, Strong No Hire.

**Verify**: Scorecards created for each open role, interviewers assigned to specific categories, question banks populated, debrief process documented.
**If failed**: Start with a single scorecard template covering 4 core attributes. Customize per role after first 5 interviews.

### Step 5: Set Up Hiring Funnel Metrics

**Duration**: 1-2 hours
**Tool**: Spreadsheet or ATS analytics

Track these metrics weekly from day one: [src2] [src3]

| Metric | Definition | Benchmark (Startup) |
|--------|-----------|---------------------|
| Time to fill | Days from job post to offer acceptance | 25-45 days |
| Cost per hire | Total recruiting spend / hires made | $4,800-$12,000 |
| Source effectiveness | Hires per channel / applications per channel | Referrals: 20-30% hire rate; Job boards: 1-3% |
| Passthrough rate | % candidates advancing to next stage | Screen to interview: 15-25%; Interview to offer: 20-30% |
| Offer acceptance rate | Offers accepted / offers extended | 75-90% |
| Quality of hire | 90-day performance rating + retention | >85% rated "meeting/exceeding expectations" |
| Candidate NPS | Post-process survey score | >50 (promoter territory) |

**Cost per hire formula**: (External costs + Internal costs) / Total hires
- External: job board fees, LinkedIn subscription, referral bonuses, agency fees, background checks
- Internal: recruiter salary (prorated), hiring manager interview hours, ATS subscription

**Verify**: Dashboard created with all 7 metrics, data sources connected (even if initially manual), and first week of data populated.
**If failed**: Start with 3 metrics only — time to fill, cost per hire, and source effectiveness. Add others after first hire.

### Step 6: Optimize and Iterate

**Duration**: Ongoing, 1-2 hours/week
**Tool**: ATS analytics + spreadsheet

After your first 5-10 hires, analyze channel performance and reallocate budget:

1. **Channel ROI analysis**: Calculate cost per hire by source. Referrals typically cost $1,500-$3,000 per hire vs $8,000-$15,000 for LinkedIn Recruiter vs $25,000-$50,000 for agencies. [src6]

2. **Funnel bottleneck identification**: Where are candidates dropping off? If screen-to-interview is below 15%, job descriptions may be attracting wrong candidates. If offer acceptance is below 70%, compensation or candidate experience needs improvement.

3. **Scorecard calibration**: After 10+ interviews, review score distributions. If every candidate scores 3+ on all attributes, criteria are too lenient. If most score below 2, role requirements may be unrealistic.

4. **Candidate experience audit**: Send brief survey after every rejection and every hire. Track time-to-response (target: 48 hours for every stage transition).

**Verify**: Month-over-month improvement in at least 2 of 7 core metrics. Channel mix adjusted based on data.
**If failed**: Focus on the single highest-impact bottleneck. Usually it is either sourcing volume (not enough candidates entering the funnel) or offer acceptance rate (losing candidates to competing offers).

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Posting and praying
Listing jobs on a single board and waiting for applications. At early stage, fewer than 1% of inbound applicants will be qualified. Passive candidates — people not actively looking — make up 70% of the talent pool but never see job posts. [src3]

### Correct: Active multi-channel sourcing
Allocate 60% of recruiting time to outbound sourcing (LinkedIn, referrals, community engagement) and 40% to inbound management. Personally message 20-30 candidates per week per open role.

### Wrong: Unstructured "culture fit" interviews
Asking each interviewer to "get a feel for the candidate" with no scorecard. This introduces severe bias and produces near-random hiring outcomes. [src4]

### Correct: Structured scorecards with assigned focus areas
Each interviewer evaluates specific competency categories using behavioral evidence and a 1-4 rating scale. Debrief is data-driven, not gut-driven.

### Wrong: Ignoring candidate experience
Taking 2+ weeks to respond, canceling interviews last-minute, or ghosting rejected candidates. In tight markets, 60% of candidates abandon processes that take too long. [src2]

### Correct: 48-hour SLA on every stage transition
Commit to responding within 2 business days at every stage. Send personalized rejection notes. Rejected candidates become future referral sources if treated well.
