# Job Description Template Library for Startups

## Purpose

This recipe produces a complete library of stage-appropriate job description templates across engineering, sales, marketing, operations, and customer success functions — with inclusive language verified, salary transparency compliant, and optimized for applicant conversion on LinkedIn, Indeed, and startup job boards. The output is a set of ready-to-customize JD documents, a bias-detection review checklist, and a multi-channel publishing workflow. [src1]

## Prerequisites

- [ ] **Role requirements defined** — what the hire needs to accomplish in the first 6-12 months
- [ ] **Compensation range approved** — salary band, equity percentage, and benefits package
- [ ] **Company context documented** — mission, stage, team size, culture values, tech stack (for engineering roles)
- [ ] **Hiring manager identified** — person who will own the role and conduct interviews
- [ ] **Legal compliance review** — awareness of salary transparency requirements in target hiring jurisdictions

## Constraints

- Job post length matters: 300-700 words is the optimal range. LinkedIn data shows posts under 300 words get 8.4% more applications, while Indeed data shows 700-2,000 word posts get 30% more applications. Optimize length per platform. [src1]
- Salary transparency is legally required in 13+ US states (CO, NY, CA, WA, CT, MD, NV, RI, HI, IL, MN, VT, MA) and EU-wide under the Pay Transparency Directive effective 2026. Omitting compensation reduces applicants by 30-40%. [src6]
- Gendered or exclusionary language reduces applicant diversity significantly. Research shows that JDs scoring 90+ on Textio's inclusion scale fill 25% faster than those scoring below 50. [src3]
- Use industry-standard job titles. Non-standard titles like "code ninja" or "growth hacker extraordinaire" reduce job search visibility on LinkedIn and Indeed by 50%+. [src2]
- Early-stage JDs (pre-seed through Series A) must lead with mission and equity upside — cash compensation alone cannot compete with BigTech offers at these stages. [src4]

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Audit Role Needs and Prioritize

**Duration**: 1-2 hours
**Tool**: Spreadsheet + hiring manager input

Define each open role with enough specificity to write a compelling JD. Do not write a JD until you can answer the core questions below for each role.

```
Role Audit Worksheet (complete per role):
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
Role title: [standard industry title]
Function: [Engineering | Sales | Marketing | Ops | CS | Product]
Seniority: [Junior | Mid | Senior | Lead | Director]
Stage context: [Pre-seed | Seed | Series A | Series B+]
Reporting to: [title of hiring manager]

Core questions:
1. What problem does this hire solve in the next 6 months?
2. What does success look like at 30/60/90 days?
3. What are the 3-5 must-have skills vs nice-to-haves?
4. What is the salary band and equity range?
5. Is this role remote, hybrid, or on-site? Which time zones?
6. What makes this role different from the same role at BigTech?

Priority order:
□ Revenue-generating roles first (sales, CS)
□ Product-building roles second (engineering, product)
□ Scaling roles third (marketing, ops)
```

**Verify**: Each role has all 6 questions answered with specific, measurable responses. "We need someone great" is not specific enough.
**If failed**: If hiring manager cannot articulate what success looks like, the role is not ready to hire for. Clarify business goals first.

### Step 2: Select and Customize Template by Function

**Duration**: 1-2 hours per role
**Tool**: Template library + text editor

Use the function-specific templates below. Each template follows a proven structure: hook, impact description, responsibilities as outcomes, requirements split into must-have vs nice-to-have, compensation, and company context. [src2]

```
UNIVERSAL JD STRUCTURE (all functions):
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. TITLE: Standard industry title (no jargon)
2. HOOK (2-3 sentences): Why this role matters NOW
   - Lead with the problem the hire will solve
   - Connect to company mission and stage
   - Create urgency without desperation

3. WHAT YOU'LL DO (5-7 bullet points):
   - Frame as outcomes, not tasks
   - Wrong: "Manage the engineering backlog"
   - Right: "Own the product roadmap for our core API,
     shipping features that serve 10K+ daily active users"
   - Include scope: team size, budget, tools

4. WHAT YOU BRING (split into two lists):
   Must-haves (3-5 items):
   - Years of experience with specific context
   - Technical skills (with specific tools/languages)
   - Domain knowledge directly relevant to the role
   Nice-to-haves (2-3 items):
   - Adjacent skills that accelerate onboarding
   - Industry-specific experience
   - Leadership or mentoring experience

5. COMPENSATION & BENEFITS:
   - Salary range (required in many jurisdictions)
   - Equity: share count or percentage range
   - Benefits: health, PTO, remote policy, learning budget
   - Stage-specific perks: flexible schedule, founding team access

6. ABOUT US (3-4 sentences):
   - What the company does and for whom
   - Stage, funding, team size
   - Culture values (2-3, be specific and honest)
   - Growth trajectory and what's next

ENGINEERING TEMPLATE ADDITIONS:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
- Specify exact tech stack: languages, frameworks, infra
- Include engineering culture signals: deploy frequency,
  code review practices, testing approach, on-call expectations
- Mention team structure: squad size, cross-functional?
- Example: "You'll work in Python and TypeScript, deploying
  to AWS via GitHub Actions, shipping to production 3-5x/week"

SALES TEMPLATE ADDITIONS:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
- Specify ACV range and target customer segment
- Include OTE breakdown (base + variable split)
- Mention GTM stage: founder-led → first rep → team build
- Quota expectations and ramp timeline
- CRM and sales tools used

MARKETING TEMPLATE ADDITIONS:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
- Specify channels owned: content, paid, product marketing
- Include budget responsibility and expected ROI metrics
- Mention stage: building from zero vs scaling existing
- Analytics tools and attribution model

OPERATIONS TEMPLATE ADDITIONS:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
- Specify operational scope: finance, HR, legal, office
- Include systems and tools managed
- Mention compliance requirements if applicable
- Cross-functional coordination responsibilities

CUSTOMER SUCCESS TEMPLATE ADDITIONS:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
- Specify portfolio size and revenue managed
- Include NRR/churn targets
- Mention CS tools: Gainsight, Vitally, Intercom
- Upsell/expansion responsibility (if applicable)
```

**Verify**: Each JD follows the universal structure, includes function-specific details, and answers all 6 audit questions from Step 1.
**If failed**: If the JD reads like a generic job board post, it lacks specificity. Add concrete numbers, tools, and outcomes.

### Step 3: Adapt for Startup Stage

**Duration**: 30-60 minutes per role
**Tool**: Text editor

Stage matters more than function for startup JDs. A Series A engineering JD looks different from a seed-stage engineering JD. [src4]

```
STAGE ADAPTATION MATRIX:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
PRE-SEED / SEED (1-10 employees):
  Tone: Founder-to-candidate, personal, direct
  Emphasis: Mission, equity upside, founding team impact
  Responsibilities: Broad ("you'll wear many hats")
  Requirements: Reduce to 2-3 must-haves maximum
  Compensation: Lead with equity, be transparent on cash
  Red flag avoidance: Don't list 15 requirements for a
  generalist role — you'll get zero applicants
  Ashby recommends: First-person voice, include a
  "Why You Shouldn't Apply" section for self-selection

SERIES A (10-50 employees):
  Tone: Professional but still personal
  Emphasis: Growth trajectory, career path, team building
  Responsibilities: More focused (clear swim lane)
  Requirements: 3-5 must-haves, 2-3 nice-to-haves
  Compensation: Full range with equity formula explained
  Key addition: Describe the team they'll join and who
  they'll work with — candidates evaluate the team

SERIES B+ (50-200 employees):
  Tone: Professional, structured, brand-consistent
  Emphasis: Scale challenges, leadership opportunity
  Responsibilities: Well-defined scope with metrics
  Requirements: Specific experience requirements
  Compensation: Competitive ranges with levels
  Key addition: Career progression framework if available

GROWTH (200+ employees):
  Tone: Brand voice, polished, consistent across roles
  Emphasis: Impact at scale, resources available
  Responsibilities: Defined by level and function
  Requirements: Structured competency framework
  Compensation: Banded by level with clear progression
  Key addition: DEI commitment, ERGs, learning programs
```

**Verify**: JD tone and content match the startup's actual stage. A seed-stage JD should not read like a Fortune 500 posting.
**If failed**: If the JD feels too corporate for the stage, strip formality. If too casual for a Series B+, add structure and specificity.

### Step 4: Optimize for Inclusion and SEO

**Duration**: 30-45 minutes per role
**Tool**: Textio, Gender Decoder, or Ongig Text Analyzer

Run every JD through bias detection and optimize for search visibility. This is not optional — biased language measurably reduces applicant quality and diversity. [src3]

```
INCLUSION CHECKLIST:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
□ Run through bias detection tool (Textio, Gender Decoder, or Ongig)
□ Remove gendered language:
  - "rockstar" → "high-performer"
  - "he/she" → "you" or "they"
  - "manpower" → "team capacity"
  - "aggressive" → "ambitious" or "driven"
□ Remove unnecessary requirements:
  - "CS degree required" → "CS degree or equivalent experience"
  - "10+ years experience" → question if truly needed
  - "Must be in office 5 days" → state if hybrid/remote possible
□ Add inclusive signals:
  - "We encourage applications from candidates of all backgrounds"
  - Specific accommodations statement
  - Visa sponsorship status (state clearly either way)
□ Verify readability: aim for 8th-grade reading level
□ Check for age bias: "digital native" or "young team" excludes

SEO OPTIMIZATION:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
□ Title matches how candidates search:
  "Senior Backend Engineer" not "Backend Wizard Level III"
□ Include location and remote status in title or subtitle
□ Use keywords candidates search: job title + seniority +
  key technology + "remote" or city name
□ First 150 characters are compelling (shown in search previews)
□ Avoid walls of text — use bullets, headers, white space
```

**Verify**: Bias tool score is 80+ (on Textio scale). JD title matches top search terms for the role on LinkedIn and Indeed.
**If failed**: If bias score is below 80, the tool will flag specific phrases. Replace each flagged phrase and re-run until score passes.

### Step 5: Build Approval Workflow

**Duration**: 30 minutes (one-time setup)
**Tool**: Document management + communication tool

Establish a review and approval process that prevents posting JDs with legal issues, compensation errors, or off-brand messaging.

```
APPROVAL WORKFLOW:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
Step 1: Hiring manager writes or customizes JD (using template)
Step 2: Recruiting/HR reviews for:
  □ Compensation within approved band
  □ Requirements are realistic for the role level
  □ No legal compliance issues (salary transparency, etc.)
Step 3: Bias detection tool scan (automated or manual)
Step 4: Final approval:
  - Seed/Series A: Founder or CEO signs off
  - Series B+: Hiring manager + HR lead sign off
Step 5: Post to ATS and distribute

Turnaround target:
  - New role: 2-3 business days from draft to live
  - Repost/refresh: Same day
  - Template role (repeat hire): 1 business day

Version control:
  - Save each JD version with date
  - Track which version generates best applicant quality
  - Update templates quarterly based on performance data
```

**Verify**: Workflow is documented, stakeholders are identified, and turnaround time expectation is set.
**If failed**: If approval takes more than 5 business days, the process is too complex. Simplify by reducing approvers to 2 maximum for early-stage startups.

### Step 6: Publish Across Channels

**Duration**: 1-2 hours per role
**Tool**: ATS or manual posting

Distribute the finalized JD across channels, adapting format and length per platform.

```
CHANNEL DISTRIBUTION PLAN:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
TIER 1 — Free, high-intent channels:
  □ Company careers page / website
  □ Wellfound (AngelList) — free for startups, startup-native audience
  □ LinkedIn organic post from founder (warm reach)
  □ Hacker News "Who's Hiring" (monthly, 1st of month, engineering)
  □ Internal referral blast to team + advisors

TIER 2 — Paid or effort-intensive channels:
  □ LinkedIn Jobs (promoted: $200-500/post)
  □ Indeed (free basic, $5-15/day sponsored)
  □ Industry-specific boards (e.g., BuiltIn, Key Values)
  □ Greenhouse/Ashby auto-distribution (if using ATS)

TIER 3 — Community and niche:
  □ Relevant Slack communities
  □ Twitter/X post from founder
  □ Reddit (r/forhire, industry subreddits)
  □ University career boards (for junior roles)

PLATFORM-SPECIFIC FORMATTING:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
LinkedIn: Keep under 300 words, lead with hook and impact,
  salary in first paragraph. LinkedIn posts with salary
  get 2x more applications.

Indeed: 700-2,000 words perform best. Include detailed
  responsibilities and full benefits section.

Wellfound: Emphasize equity, stage, and mission.
  Startup-savvy candidates expect transparency.

Hacker News: Plain text, no formatting. Lead with tech
  stack and what's interesting about the engineering
  challenges. Include salary range.
```

**Verify**: JD is live on at least 3 channels (careers page + 2 distribution channels). Each posting is formatted appropriately for the platform.
**If failed**: If a channel rejects the posting, check for formatting errors or policy violations (e.g., Indeed requires specific fields). Reformat and resubmit.

## Quality Benchmarks

| Quality Metric | Minimum Acceptable | Good | Excellent |
|---------------|-------------------|------|-----------|
| Bias/inclusion score | > 70 | > 85 | > 95 |
| Word count (LinkedIn) | 200-400 | 250-350 | ~300 |
| Word count (Indeed) | 500-2000 | 700-1500 | 900-1200 |
| Salary range included | Yes | Yes + equity | Yes + equity + benefits |
| Applications per week | > 10 | > 25 | > 50 |
| Qualified applicant rate | > 15% | > 30% | > 50% |
| Time to first qualified applicant | < 14 days | < 7 days | < 3 days |
| Diversity of applicant pool | Reflects market | Above market average | Exceeds market benchmarks |

**If below minimum**: If applications per week are under 10, audit the title (is it searchable?), the first paragraph (is the hook compelling?), and the salary range (is it competitive?). If qualified rate is under 15%, requirements are either too vague (attracting unqualified) or too restrictive (filtering out good candidates).

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Zero applications after 7 days | Title not searchable, salary missing, or JD buried on page 5 of job board | Promote the posting, rewrite title to match search terms, add salary range if missing |
| High volume but low quality | Requirements too vague, title too broad | Add 1-2 specific must-have requirements, narrow the title (e.g., "Engineer" → "Senior Backend Engineer, Python") |
| Candidates drop off after reading JD | JD is too long, requirements are excessive, or tone is off-putting | Shorten to 300-500 words, reduce requirements to 3-5 must-haves, adjust tone for stage |
| Bias tool flags multiple issues | Unconscious use of gendered or exclusionary language | Replace each flagged term, re-run scan, have a second reviewer check |
| Legal compliance risk flagged | Missing salary range in transparency-required jurisdiction | Add compensation range immediately — retroactive posting without salary may incur penalties |
| No diverse applicants | Posting only on homogeneous channels, or JD signals exclusion | Expand to diverse job boards (Jopwell, Techqueria, PowerToFly), audit JD for coded language |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Writing JDs as laundry lists of requirements
Listing 15-20 "required" qualifications produces a JD that reads like a compliance document. Research shows women apply when they meet 100% of requirements while men apply at 60%. Long requirement lists disproportionately filter out qualified diverse candidates. [src3]

### Correct: Split into 3-5 must-haves and 2-3 nice-to-haves
Must-haves are truly non-negotiable. Everything else is a nice-to-have. Be explicit about which is which. This increases applications from qualified candidates who might otherwise self-select out.

### Wrong: Using clever or cute job titles
"Growth Hacking Ninja" and "Chief Happiness Officer" are unsearchable on job boards and confuse candidates about the actual role. Non-standard titles reduce discoverability by 50%+ on LinkedIn. [src2]

### Correct: Use standard industry titles with clear seniority
"Senior Growth Marketing Manager" is searchable, clear, and attracts the right candidates. Add context in the description, not the title.

### Wrong: Copy-pasting the same JD across all platforms
LinkedIn rewards short, punchy posts (under 300 words). Indeed rewards detailed, keyword-rich posts (700-2,000 words). The same JD will underperform on at least one platform. [src1]

### Correct: Create a master JD, then platform-specific variants
Write the full 700-word version first, then create a 300-word LinkedIn summary and a longer Indeed version. Same content, different format.
