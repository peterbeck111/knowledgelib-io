# Investor Reporting Template — Monthly/Quarterly Update Framework

## Purpose

This recipe produces a complete investor update system: a reusable email template tailored to company stage, a metric presentation framework that tracks the same KPIs month-over-month, a narrative structure that balances wins and challenges, and a distribution workflow with recurring calendar automation. The output is a working update process that takes under 60 minutes per cycle after initial setup. [src1]

## Prerequisites
<!-- Agents: verify ALL prerequisites before executing. Missing prerequisites = failed execution. -->

- [ ] **Financial data** available from accounting software or bookkeeper — MRR (or revenue), burn rate, cash balance, runway in months
- [ ] **Product metrics** available from analytics platform — active users, signups, churn rate, NPS or CSAT
- [ ] **Investor contact list** available from cap table tool or founder spreadsheet — all investor emails
- [ ] **Prior period data** available — at least 2-3 months of historical metrics for trend display
- [ ] **Domain email** configured — send from founder@company.com, not personal Gmail (establishes professionalism)

## Constraints
<!-- Hard rules. Agents: enforce throughout execution. Violating these = broken output or legal risk. -->

- Send within 48 hours of period close — SaaStr recommends the "48-hour rule" as the top hack for investor happiness. [src4]
- Track identical metrics every update — switching metrics suggests cherry-picking. Investors notice and trust declines. [src2]
- Never skip an update during bad months — sporadic communication is worse than reporting losses. Companies that communicate regularly are 2x more likely to raise follow-on funding. [src1]
- Keep monthly updates to 250-750 words — busy investors skim; the Y Combinator template deliberately eliminates filler to maintain attention. [src3]
- Include 1-3 specific, actionable asks — vague "let me know if you can help" gets zero responses. Concrete asks like "intro to VP Eng at [Company]" get action. [src6]
- Use BCC or dedicated platform for distribution — never expose investor email addresses to each other via CC.

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Choose Reporting Cadence

**Duration**: 10 minutes
**Tool**: Decision only — no tool required

Select cadence based on company stage and investor expectations:

```
Pre-seed / Seed:      Monthly (recommended by YC, Visible, SaaStr)
Series A:             Monthly or bi-monthly
Series B+:            Quarterly (with monthly available on request)
Board members:        Always monthly, regardless of stage
```

Monthly updates are the default for early-stage companies. Quarterly reports work when the company has stable, predictable growth and a large investor base (50+). If in doubt, default to monthly — you can always reduce frequency later, but increasing it signals distress. [src1] [src4]

Set the send date: Choose a fixed day each month (e.g., 3rd business day of the month). Add a recurring calendar event with two reminders: (1) month-end +1 day: "Collect metrics," (2) month-end +2-3 days: "Send investor update." [src4]

**Verify**: Calendar events created for next 6 months with correct recurring pattern.
**If failed**: Set a manual phone reminder until calendar automation is working.

### Step 2: Select Metrics by Stage

**Duration**: 20 minutes
**Tool**: Spreadsheet or document

Select your core KPI set based on stage. Track the same metrics every month — never switch:

```
PRE-SEED (pre-revenue):
  ├── Cash on hand
  ├── Monthly burn rate
  ├── Runway (months)
  ├── Users / waitlist signups
  └── Key milestone progress (product, hires, LOIs)

SEED (early revenue):
  ├── MRR (monthly recurring revenue)
  ├── MRR growth rate (% month-over-month)
  ├── Cash on hand
  ├── Monthly burn rate
  ├── Runway (months)
  ├── Customers / active users
  └── Churn rate (if measurable)

SERIES A ($1M+ ARR):
  ├── ARR (annual run-rate)
  ├── MRR + MoM growth rate
  ├── Net dollar retention (NDR)
  ├── Gross margin
  ├── CAC and LTV (or LTV:CAC ratio)
  ├── Cash on hand + runway
  ├── Burn rate + burn multiple
  └── Headcount

SERIES B+ (scaling):
  ├── ARR + YoY growth rate
  ├── NDR / NRR
  ├── Gross margin + operating margin
  ├── Rule of 40 score
  ├── CAC payback period
  ├── Cash position + runway
  └── Key efficiency metrics (magic number, burn multiple)
```

Present metrics in a consistent table format. Always show current value, prior period, and change (absolute + percentage). [src2] [src3]

**Verify**: 4-7 metrics selected that match company stage; data sources identified for each.
**If failed**: If a metric cannot be reliably measured yet, replace with a qualitative milestone tracker — do not leave blanks.

### Step 3: Build Template Structure

**Duration**: 30-60 minutes
**Tool**: Email client, Notion, or Visible.vc

Build the reusable template following the proven structure used by Y Combinator, Visible.vc, and Carta. The template has six sections: [src1] [src2] [src3]

```
SUBJECT LINE FORMAT:
  "[Company Name] — [Month] [Year] Update"
  Example: "Acme Inc — February 2026 Update"

TEMPLATE STRUCTURE:

1. TL;DR (2-3 sentences)
   └── The single most important thing that happened this period.
       Busy investors often read only this. Make it count.

2. Key Metrics (table format)
   ┌────────────────┬──────────┬──────────┬─────────┐
   │ Metric         │ Current  │ Prior    │ Change  │
   ├────────────────┼──────────┼──────────┼─────────┤
   │ MRR            │ $42,000  │ $38,000  │ +10.5%  │
   │ Cash on Hand   │ $580K    │ $620K    │ -$40K   │
   │ Burn Rate      │ $40K/mo  │ $42K/mo  │ -4.8%   │
   │ Runway         │ 14.5 mo  │ 14.8 mo  │ -0.3 mo │
   │ Active Users   │ 1,240    │ 1,080    │ +14.8%  │
   │ Churn          │ 3.2%     │ 4.1%     │ -0.9pp  │
   └────────────────┴──────────┴──────────┴─────────┘

3. Highlights (3-5 bullets)
   └── Biggest wins: revenue milestones, key hires, product launches,
       partnerships, press, customer logos.

4. Lowlights / Challenges (1-3 bullets)
   └── Honest account of what went wrong or is at risk.
       Include what you are doing about it.

5. Asks (1-3 specific requests)
   └── Concrete, actionable. Example:
       "Looking for intro to [Name] at [Company] for partnership"
       "Need recommendation for VP Engineering candidates"
       "Seeking beta testers in fintech vertical"

6. Team Updates (optional, 1-2 sentences)
   └── New hires, departures, org changes.
       Include only if material.
```

Create the template in your chosen tool. For email, save as a draft or canned response. For Visible.vc, use the Standard template and customize. For Notion, create a page template with the sections above. [src1] [src5]

**Verify**: Template has all 6 sections, metric table has correct columns, subject line format defined.
**If failed**: Start with the Y Combinator template (simplest) and add sections incrementally.

### Step 4: Write the Narrative Sections

**Duration**: 20-40 minutes per update
**Tool**: Text editor

Write the narrative content for each section following these frameworks:

**TL;DR framework**: Lead with the headline metric or milestone. State the trajectory (accelerating, steady, decelerating). One sentence on the key driver. [src6]

```
GOOD TL;DR:
"MRR crossed $50K (+12% MoM), driven by 3 enterprise deals closed in
the last 2 weeks. We're on track for $75K MRR by Q2. Main risk:
churn ticked up to 4.2% — addressing with onboarding redesign."

BAD TL;DR:
"Great month! Lots of progress on many fronts. Excited about what's
ahead."
```

**Highlights framework**: Each bullet should follow the "what + so what" pattern — state the achievement, then explain why it matters. [src2]

```
GOOD: "Closed Acme Corp ($8K ACV) — our first enterprise deal,
       validates upmarket motion and increases average deal size 40%"
BAD:  "Signed new customer"
```

**Lowlights framework**: State the problem, quantify the impact, describe the fix in progress. Never present a problem without a plan. [src1] [src4]

```
GOOD: "Churn increased to 4.2% (from 3.1%) — root cause: onboarding
       drop-off at day 3. Deploying in-app checklist + day-2 email
       sequence by March 15."
BAD:  "Some customers churned this month"
```

**Asks framework**: Each ask should be specific enough that the investor can take action in under 5 minutes. Name the person, company, or type of help. [src3] [src6]

**Verify**: Each section follows its framework; lowlights include remediation plans; asks are specific and actionable.
**If failed**: Have a co-founder or advisor review the draft for vague language before sending.

### Step 5: Add Financial Runway Context

**Duration**: 10 minutes
**Tool**: Spreadsheet

Always include runway context alongside raw cash numbers. Investors care about time-to-zero more than absolute cash balance:

```
RUNWAY PRESENTATION:
  Cash on hand:       $580,000
  Monthly burn:       $40,000
  Runway:             14.5 months (at current burn)
  Runway if growth:   18+ months (if MRR growth continues at 10% MoM)
  Next raise target:  Q4 2026 (Series A at $2M+ ARR)
```

If runway drops below 6 months, flag it explicitly and state the plan (raise, cut burn, or bridge). Investors want to know you see the cliff coming. [src2] [src4]

**Verify**: Runway calculation matches cash / burn; scenario analysis included if burn rate is changing.
**If failed**: Use simple cash / burn formula — do not over-complicate with projections if data is uncertain.

### Step 6: Set Up Distribution and Tracking

**Duration**: 30-60 minutes (first time only)
**Tool**: Email client or Visible.vc

**Plain email path (A/B):**
1. Create a contact group in Gmail/Outlook named "Investors — Updates"
2. Always use BCC — never expose investor emails to each other
3. Save the template as a canned response (Gmail) or Quick Parts (Outlook)
4. Send mid-week (Wednesday or Thursday) between 9-11 AM in the majority timezone of your investors [src6]

**Visible.vc path (C):**
1. Sign up at visible.vc, import investor contacts
2. Select the "Standard Monthly Update" template
3. Customize sections to match the template built in Step 3
4. Connect data integrations (Stripe, QuickBooks, etc.) for automatic metric pulls
5. Schedule send for your chosen date

**Carta path (D):**
1. Navigate to Communications Center in Carta dashboard
2. Import financial snapshot from connected accounting software
3. Customize the template with your narrative sections
4. Set recipient list from existing stakeholders on cap table
5. Send with engagement tracking enabled

**Verify**: Test email sent to yourself; formatting correct; all links work; no investor emails visible in headers.
**If failed**: Send to a co-founder first for a formatting and content check before sending to investors.

### Step 7: Automate Data Collection

**Duration**: 30-60 minutes (first time only)
**Tool**: Varies by data source

Set up repeatable data pulls so assembling metrics takes minutes, not hours:

```
AUTOMATION CHECKLIST:
├── Stripe Dashboard → bookmark MRR chart + export to CSV
├── Analytics tool → save monthly report template (DAU, MAU, signups)
├── Accounting software → save P&L + cash flow monthly report
├── Spreadsheet → formulas that calculate MoM change, runway
└── Calendar → recurring task: "Export monthly data" on 1st of month
```

For Visible.vc users: connect Stripe, QuickBooks, Google Analytics, or Mixpanel integrations. Metrics auto-populate into the update draft. [src1]

For spreadsheet users: build a simple tracker with columns for each metric and rows for each month. Formulas auto-calculate MoM change and runway.

**Verify**: Metrics for prior month can be assembled in under 15 minutes using the automated workflow.
**If failed**: Start with manual data collection and automate one source at a time over the next 2-3 months.

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Metrics-Only Updates with No Narrative
Sending a spreadsheet of numbers without context. Investors cannot interpret whether 3.5% churn is a crisis or normal seasonality without the founder's perspective. This turns updates into homework that investors stop reading. [src2]

### Correct: Numbers + Story
Every metric gets one line of context. "Churn rose to 3.5% from 2.8% — two enterprise customers paused (not canceled) due to budget freeze. Reactivation expected Q2."

### Wrong: Annual Updates Disguised as "We're Heads Down"
Going silent for 6+ months then sending a long catch-up email. Investors interpret silence as trouble. Companies that regularly communicate with investors are twice as likely to raise follow-on funding. [src1]

### Correct: Consistent Monthly Cadence Even When Boring
Send every month, even if the update is "MRR flat at $35K, burn at $28K, 16 months runway. Focused on product rebuild — shipping v2 in April. Ask: need React Native contractor recommendations." Five sentences is fine.

### Wrong: Only Sharing Good News
Cherry-picking positive metrics and omitting challenges. Professional investors see through this immediately. When they eventually discover the problem (and they will), the trust damage is permanent. [src4]

### Correct: Balanced Reporting with Remediation Plans
Include a Lowlights section every month. Frame each challenge as: problem + impact + what you are doing about it. Investors respect transparency and often offer help for problems they can see.
