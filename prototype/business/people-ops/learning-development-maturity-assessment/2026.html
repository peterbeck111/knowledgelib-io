Learning & Development Maturity Assessment

How mature is L&D — training investment benchmarks, skills gap analysis, leadership development?

Type: Assessment Confidence: 0.84 Sources: 6 Verified: 2026-03-10

Purpose

This assessment evaluates the maturity of an organization's learning and development function across six critical dimensions: strategic alignment, skills architecture, learning delivery infrastructure, leadership development, measurement and ROI, and learning culture. The output is a composite maturity score (1-5) that identifies where L&D is adding value and where it is operating as a cost center without measurable impact. [src1]

Constraints

  • Requires access to L&D spend data, participation records, and skills inventory
  • Not meaningful for companies with fewer than 50 employees
  • Training investment benchmarks vary by industry — tech averages $1,400/employee/year vs manufacturing $800
  • Assessment is diagnostic only — identifies gaps, does not prescribe strategy
  • Re-run annually; L&D maturity can shift quickly with budget cuts or leadership changes

Common Pitfalls in Assessment

  • Confusing content volume with maturity: 10,000 courses with 5% utilization is less mature than 200 curated courses with 80% completion. [src1]
  • Leadership development afterthought: First-time managers get the least training despite highest impact on engagement.
  • Measurement stops at satisfaction: Happy sheets are the most common but least informative metric. [src6]
  • Skills taxonomy paralysis: Start with top 20 critical skills rather than building a comprehensive taxonomy first.
