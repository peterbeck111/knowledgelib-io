HR Tech Stack Assessment

What HR technology is needed at each company stage — HRIS, ATS, performance, learning, payroll?

Type: Assessment Confidence: 0.84 Sources: 6 Verified: 2026-03-10

Purpose

This assessment evaluates the maturity and fitness of a company's HR technology stack across six critical dimensions: core HRIS foundation, talent acquisition tools, performance and engagement platforms, learning and development systems, compensation and payroll infrastructure, and data integration and analytics capabilities. The output is a composite maturity score (1-5) that identifies technology gaps relative to company stage. [src1]

Constraints

  • Requires access to current HR tool inventory and contract details for accurate scoring
  • Not meaningful for companies under 10 employees where manual processes are acceptable
  • Assessment evaluates technology fitness, not HR process maturity
  • Vendor landscape evolves rapidly — focus on capability gaps, not product names
  • Re-run annually or when headcount crosses stage thresholds (25, 100, 500, 2000 employees)

Common Pitfalls in Assessment

  • Over-indexing on feature count: Teams score high because they purchased advanced tools but adoption is below 40%. Score based on utilization, not capabilities.
  • Stage mismatch: A 50-person startup does not need Level 5 tooling. Match tech to current stage and 12-month trajectory.
  • Ignoring integration: Best-of-breed tools with no integration create data silos worse than mediocre all-in-one platforms.
  • Vendor-driven assessment: Letting vendors assess your stack creates bias. Use internal HR leadership or independent advisors.
