# HR Tech Stack Assessment

## Purpose

This assessment evaluates the maturity and fitness of a company's HR technology stack across six critical dimensions: core HRIS foundation, talent acquisition tools, performance and engagement platforms, learning and development systems, compensation and payroll infrastructure, and data integration and analytics capabilities. The output is a composite maturity score (1-5) that identifies technology gaps relative to company stage and routes to specific investment priorities. Use this when diagnosing HR operational inefficiency, planning HR technology consolidation, preparing for rapid headcount scaling, or evaluating build-versus-buy decisions for HR tooling. [src1]

## Constraints
<!-- Agents: read before running this assessment with a user. -->

- Requires access to current HR tool inventory and contract details for accurate scoring
- Not meaningful for companies under 10 employees where manual processes are acceptable
- Assessment evaluates technology fitness, not HR process maturity — excellent tools with poor adoption still score low
- Vendor landscape evolves rapidly — specific tool recommendations become outdated within 12 months; focus on capability gaps, not product names
- Re-run annually or when headcount crosses stage thresholds (25, 100, 500, 2000 employees)

## Assessment Dimensions

<!-- Each dimension is scored independently. The structured format lets agents
     walk through this conversationally with a user, one dimension at a time. -->

### Dimension 1: Core HRIS Foundation

**What this measures**: Whether the organization has a reliable system of record for employee data that serves as the authoritative source for all downstream HR processes and integrations.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | Employee data scattered across spreadsheets, email, and paper files; no single source of truth | Multiple conflicting employee lists; manual data entry across systems; no org chart |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic HRIS deployed but underutilized; data incomplete or inconsistent; limited self-service | HRIS exists but <60% of employee data populated; manual workarounds common |
| 3 | Defined | HRIS is the system of record with complete employee profiles; self-service portal active; basic workflows automated | 90%+ data completeness; employees use self-service for PTO, profile updates; standard reports available |
| 4 | Managed | HRIS fully integrated with payroll, benefits, and time tracking; automated onboarding/offboarding workflows; compliance reporting automated | One-click onboarding provisioning; real-time headcount reporting; audit trail for all employee changes |
| 5 | Optimized | AI-powered HRIS with predictive analytics; automated compliance monitoring; real-time workforce planning; chatbot-driven employee self-service | Predictive attrition alerts; automated I-9/compliance workflows; natural language queries for HR data |

**Red flags**: HR team maintaining parallel spreadsheets alongside an HRIS; inability to produce a current headcount report in under 10 minutes; new hires not in the system within 24 hours of start date. [src4]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "Can you pull an accurate headcount report with demographic breakdown in under 5 minutes right now?"

### Dimension 2: Talent Acquisition Tools

**What this measures**: The sophistication and effectiveness of recruiting technology — from job posting and candidate sourcing through interview management and offer generation.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No ATS; tracking candidates via email, spreadsheets, or sticky notes; posting to job boards manually | No centralized candidate database; hiring managers lose track of applicants; no application tracking |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic ATS deployed but used primarily as a resume database; limited automation; no sourcing tools | ATS exists but <50% of positions tracked; manual interview scheduling; no pipeline analytics |
| 3 | Defined | Full ATS adoption with structured pipelines; automated job posting to multiple boards; interview scheduling integrated with calendars | All positions tracked in ATS; stage-based candidate tracking; basic sourcing metrics available |
| 4 | Managed | AI-powered candidate screening; CRM for talent nurturing; structured interview scorecards; integrated background checks; diversity analytics | Automated resume parsing and ranking; passive candidate pipeline; time-to-fill tracked by stage |
| 5 | Optimized | Predictive hiring models; AI-driven sourcing with outbound automation; video interview analysis; real-time recruiter productivity dashboards; skills-based matching | Quality-of-hire tracking automated; predictive time-to-fill; AI-generated job descriptions; automated candidate engagement |

**Red flags**: Recruiters cannot tell you how many active candidates are in the pipeline right now; interview feedback collected via email or chat; no standardized offer letter templates. [src1]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "How many active candidates do you have in your pipeline right now, and what's your conversion rate by stage?"

### Dimension 3: Performance & Engagement

**What this measures**: Whether the organization has technology enabling continuous performance feedback, goal tracking, engagement measurement, and employee development conversations.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No performance system; annual reviews done on paper or ad hoc; no engagement surveys | No documented goals; reviews are informal conversations; no engagement data collected |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic performance review tool (or spreadsheet-based process); annual review cycle only; occasional ad hoc surveys | Annual review forms exist but completion <70%; no goal-setting framework; engagement measured sporadically |
| 3 | Defined | Dedicated performance platform with goal-setting, review cycles, and basic engagement surveys; quarterly check-ins enabled | OKRs or similar goal framework; 80%+ review completion; semi-annual engagement surveys with basic analytics |
| 4 | Managed | Continuous feedback platform with real-time recognition; pulse surveys; 360-degree reviews; performance-compensation linkage | Weekly/bi-weekly feedback loops; real-time engagement dashboards; performance data informs compensation decisions |
| 5 | Optimized | AI-powered performance insights with bias detection; predictive engagement analytics; skills-based development recommendations; automated manager nudges | AI detects calibration bias; engagement driver analysis automated; flight risk predictions; personalized development paths |

**Red flags**: Managers cannot access their direct reports' goals; performance review completion takes more than 3 weeks; engagement survey results take more than 2 months to process and share. [src2]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "When was the last time every employee received written performance feedback, and how long did the cycle take?"

### Dimension 4: Learning & Development

**What this measures**: The maturity of technology supporting employee skill development, compliance training, career pathing, and knowledge management.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No LMS; training is informal, undocumented; compliance training tracked manually if at all | No training records; new hire onboarding is tribal knowledge; no compliance training completion tracking |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic LMS or training folder (Google Drive/SharePoint); compliance training exists but tracking is manual | LMS deployed but <40% adoption; limited course library; no skill gap analysis |
| 3 | Defined | Full LMS with course catalog, completion tracking, compliance automation, and manager-assigned learning paths | 70%+ LMS adoption; compliance training automated with reminders; basic reporting on training completion |
| 4 | Managed | Integrated learning platform with skill assessments, career pathing, content recommendations, and external course marketplace | Skills taxonomy defined; learning linked to performance goals; manager development dashboards; certification tracking |
| 5 | Optimized | AI-powered learning with personalized recommendations; real-time skill gap analysis; internal talent marketplace; learning-in-the-flow-of-work integrations | AI-generated learning paths based on role/goals; real-time skill inventory; internal gig marketplace; learning analytics tied to business outcomes |

**Red flags**: No record of who has completed mandatory compliance training; new hires report feeling unprepared after onboarding; no career development resources beyond ad hoc mentoring. [src2]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "Can you show me a report of compliance training completion rates across all employees right now?"

### Dimension 5: Compensation & Payroll

**What this measures**: The sophistication of compensation management, payroll processing, and benefits administration technology — from basic payroll to strategic compensation planning.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | Payroll processed manually or via accountant; no compensation bands; benefits enrollment on paper | Payroll errors common; no salary bands documented; benefits questions go to a single person |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic payroll provider (ADP Run, Gusto equivalent); informal salary ranges exist but not systematized; benefits enrollment partially online | Payroll runs on time but manual corrections needed monthly; compensation decisions ad hoc; limited benefits self-service |
| 3 | Defined | Full payroll platform with self-service; documented salary bands with market data; benefits platform with online enrollment and comparison tools | Error-free payroll; salary bands aligned to market data; employees can compare and select benefits online |
| 4 | Managed | Integrated compensation management with equity tracking; real-time market benchmarking; total rewards statements; multi-country payroll | Compensation decisions backed by current market data; equity vesting tracked automatically; total comp visible to employees |
| 5 | Optimized | AI-powered pay equity analysis; real-time compensation optimization; predictive modeling for retention-based pay; global payroll automation with tax compliance | Automated pay equity audits; scenario modeling for compensation changes; predictive turnover-cost analysis |

**Red flags**: Payroll corrections needed more than twice per quarter; no salary bands documented; inability to produce a total compensation statement for any employee. [src3]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "Do you have documented salary bands, and when were they last benchmarked against market data?"

### Dimension 6: Integration & Analytics

**What this measures**: How well HR tools are integrated with each other and with the broader business, and the sophistication of people analytics capabilities.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | HR tools are siloed with no integration; reporting requires manual data extraction and spreadsheet manipulation | Data exported from multiple systems and merged manually; no standard HR dashboards; decisions based on gut feel |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic data exports between systems; some manual reporting; HR data used reactively for compliance | CSV exports between HRIS and payroll; basic headcount reports; analytics limited to backward-looking compliance |
| 3 | Defined | Key integrations automated (HRIS-payroll, HRIS-ATS); standard HR dashboards; regular people analytics reporting | Automated data flow between core systems; monthly HR dashboards; basic workforce planning reports |
| 4 | Managed | Unified people analytics platform; real-time dashboards; HRIS as integration hub with bi-directional data flow; predictive analytics | Real-time workforce dashboards accessible to leaders; integration with finance/IT systems; predictive headcount modeling |
| 5 | Optimized | AI-driven people analytics with natural language queries; data mesh architecture; real-time impact measurement for all HR programs; embedded analytics in manager workflows | CEO-ready workforce dashboards auto-generated; AI answers ad hoc people questions; automated ROI measurement for all HR initiatives |

**Red flags**: HR team spends more than 20% of time on data extraction and reporting; leadership cannot get real-time headcount or cost data; HRIS and payroll show different employee counts. [src1]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "How long does it take to produce a board-ready workforce report, and how many tools do you have to touch to build it?"

## Scoring & Interpretation

### Overall Score Calculation

All six dimensions are weighted equally, as each represents a critical capability layer.

```
Overall Score = (HRIS + TA Tools + Performance + L&D + Comp/Payroll + Integration) / 6
```

### Score Interpretation

| Overall Score | Maturity Level | Interpretation | Recommended Next Step |
|---------------|---------------|----------------|----------------------|
| 1.0 - 1.9 | Critical | HR technology is severely underinvested — manual processes create compliance risk, data integrity issues, and inability to scale. Every added employee compounds the problem. | Prioritize HRIS foundation and payroll automation immediately. Budget 2-4% of revenue for HR tech. |
| 2.0 - 2.9 | Developing | Basic tools exist but are underutilized, poorly integrated, or misaligned with company stage. Significant manual work remains. | Consolidate to integrated platform; improve adoption of existing tools; add missing critical layers. |
| 3.0 - 3.9 | Competent | Solid foundation for current stage. Core processes are digitized and mostly automated. Ready for optimization rather than foundational investment. | Focus on integration, analytics, and advanced automation; consider AI-powered upgrades. |
| 4.0 - 4.5 | Advanced | Technology is a competitive advantage. Strong integration, good analytics, and proactive HR operations. Minor gaps in emerging capabilities. | Invest in AI-powered features, predictive analytics, and employee experience differentiation. |
| 4.6 - 5.0 | Best-in-class | HR technology is fully integrated, AI-powered, and delivering measurable business impact. Rare outside of HR tech companies and large enterprises. | Maintain and innovate. Pilot emerging technologies. Contribute to industry best practices. |

## Common Pitfalls in Assessment

- **Over-indexing on feature count**: Teams score themselves high because they purchased an advanced tool, ignoring that adoption is below 40%. Score based on actual utilization, not purchased capabilities. [src2]
- **Conflating company stage with maturity need**: A 50-person startup does not need Level 5 tooling. The right tech stack matches the company's current stage and 12-month growth trajectory, not aspirational state. [src1]
- **Ignoring integration as a dimension**: Best-of-breed tools with no integration create data silos that are worse than a mediocre all-in-one platform. Integration maturity is often the weakest and most overlooked dimension.
- **Vendor-driven assessment**: Letting technology vendors assess your stack creates inherent bias. This assessment should be run by internal HR leadership or an independent advisor.
