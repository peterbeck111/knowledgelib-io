# Design Partner Program Setup

## Purpose

This recipe produces a fully operational design partner program: outreach scripts, a lightweight MOU agreement, a feedback cadence structure, and a signed cohort of 3-5 design partners providing weekly structured feedback on your pre-PMF product. The output is a running program with scheduled sessions, documented expectations, and a clear graduation path to paid customers. [src1]

## Prerequisites

- [ ] **ICP definition** available from customer research or founder hypothesis — clear description of target buyer persona, company size, and pain point
- [ ] **Product demo or prototype** — functional prototype, Figma mockup, or working MVP that partners can interact with and provide feedback on
- [ ] **Problem hypothesis** — documented assumption about the problem you solve, who experiences it, and why existing solutions fail
- [ ] **Google Docs/Notion** — for agreement drafting and feedback tracking ($0)
- [ ] **Scheduling tool** — Calendly, Cal.com, or Google Calendar for recurring sessions ($0)
- [ ] **3+ warm introductions** — investors, advisors, or professional contacts who can introduce you to potential partners in your target market [src1]

## Constraints

- Design partners are not beta testers and not pilot customers. They collaborate on product direction before you have product-market fit. Do not conflate these programs. [src5]
- Recruit 3-7 partners maximum. Fewer than 3 risks building for edge cases; more than 10 creates unmanageable feedback overhead and slows iteration. [src1] [src7]
- All feedback IP must be assigned to the vendor in writing. Ambiguous IP ownership creates serious problems during fundraising diligence or acquisition. [src2] [src3]
- Do not optimize for early revenue from design partners. Prioritize raw, honest feedback over monetization — charging too early filters out candid criticism. [src1]
- Each engagement should have a hard end date of 6-12 weeks. Open-ended engagements lose momentum and delay product-market fit validation. [src4]

## Tool Selection Decision

```
Which path?
├── Founder has strong network (10+ warm intros available)
│   └── PATH A: Warm Outreach — leverage investors/advisors for introductions
├── Founder has moderate network (3-9 warm intros)
│   └── PATH B: Hybrid — warm intros + targeted cold outreach
├── Founder has weak network (mostly cold outreach needed)
│   └── PATH C: Cold Outreach — LinkedIn/Twitter DMs + community engagement
└── Founder is at an accelerator or has institutional investor backing
    └── PATH D: Investor-Facilitated — investors make introductions from portfolio
```

| Path | Tools | Cost | Time to First Partner | Success Rate |
|------|-------|------|----------------------|-------------|
| A: Warm Outreach | Email + Calendly | $0 | 1-2 weeks | High (40-60% conversion) |
| B: Hybrid | Email + LinkedIn + Calendly | $0 | 2-3 weeks | Moderate (25-40%) |
| C: Cold Outreach | LinkedIn Sales Nav + email | $0-80/mo | 3-4 weeks | Lower (10-20%) |
| D: Investor-Facilitated | Investor intros + Calendly | $0 | 1-2 weeks | Highest (50-70%) |

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Define Partner Selection Criteria

**Duration**: 1-2 hours
**Tool**: Google Docs or Notion

Establish your three-part selection framework before reaching out to anyone. Every prospective partner must pass all three filters. [src1]

```
DESIGN PARTNER SELECTION CRITERIA
══════════════════════════════════════════════

Filter 1: REPRESENTATIVENESS (ICP Match)
  - Company size:        _____ employees
  - Industry:            _____
  - Tech stack:          _____
  - Buying process:      [ ] Self-serve  [ ] Sales-led  [ ] Committee
  - Must represent your ACTUAL target buyer, not aspirational logos

Filter 2: URGENCY (Pain Level)
  - Problem frequency:   [ ] Daily  [ ] Weekly  [ ] Monthly
  - Current workaround:  _____
  - Cost of status quo:  $_____ /month (time, money, or opportunity)
  - Quick test: Will they commit to a 30-min call next week?
    [ ] Yes → proceed  [ ] No → deprioritize

Filter 3: CAPACITY (Ability to Engage)
  - Internal champion:   Name: _____ Title: _____
  - Champion is actual user (not just executive sponsor)? [ ] Yes [ ] No
  - Can commit to weekly 30-min sessions for 8-12 weeks? [ ] Yes [ ] No
  - Has authority to implement/test your product? [ ] Yes [ ] No

SCORING: Must pass ALL three filters. Failing any one = skip.
```

**Verify**: You have written criteria for all three filters with specific, measurable thresholds for your market.
**If failed**: If you cannot define your ICP clearly, stop and complete customer discovery interviews first before recruiting partners.

### Step 2: Build Outreach Pipeline

**Duration**: 2-4 hours
**Tool**: Google Sheets or CRM (HubSpot free tier)

Build a list of 15-25 prospects to yield 3-5 signed partners. Expect 30-50% conversion from warm intros and 10-20% from cold outreach. [src1] [src6]

```
PARTNER PROSPECT TRACKER (Google Sheets)
══════════════════════════════════════════════
Columns:
  A: Company Name
  B: Contact Name
  C: Title
  D: Source (warm intro / cold / community)
  E: Introducer Name (if warm)
  F: ICP Match Score (1-5)
  G: Urgency Signal (what pain indicator you observed)
  H: Status (prospect / contacted / call scheduled / interested / signed / declined)
  I: Next Action
  J: Notes

Sourcing channels (prioritize top-down):
1. Ask investors: "Who in your portfolio has [problem X]?"
2. Ask advisors: "Who do you know struggling with [problem X]?"
3. LinkedIn search: [ICP title] + [industry] + [pain keyword]
4. Community forums: relevant Slack groups, Discord servers, Reddit
5. Twitter/X: search for people complaining about the problem
6. Conference/meetup attendees from your target segment
```

**Verify**: Pipeline has at least 15 prospects with ICP match score of 3+ and at least 5 warm introductions.
**If failed**: If fewer than 5 warm intros, switch to Path B or C. Ask every person you contact for 2 additional referrals.

### Step 3: Craft Outreach and Conduct Discovery Calls

**Duration**: 3-5 hours over 1-2 weeks
**Tool**: Email + Calendly + Zoom/Google Meet

Send personalized outreach, then run 20-minute discovery calls to qualify prospects before proposing the partnership.

```
OUTREACH EMAIL TEMPLATE (Warm Intro)
══════════════════════════════════════════════
Subject: [Introducer] suggested we connect — [problem] in [their industry]

Hi [Name],

[Introducer] mentioned you're dealing with [specific pain point].
We're building [one-sentence product description] and looking for
3-5 design partners to shape the product before launch.

What this means for you:
- Free access to the product during development
- Direct influence on features and roadmap
- Weekly 30-min feedback sessions for 8 weeks
- Preferred pricing when we launch commercially

Time commitment: 30 minutes/week for 8-12 weeks.

Would you have 20 minutes this week to see if this is a fit?

[Calendly link]

OUTREACH EMAIL TEMPLATE (Cold)
══════════════════════════════════════════════
Subject: Solving [specific problem] — looking for design partners

Hi [Name],

I noticed [specific signal: their blog post, job posting, LinkedIn
comment, or public complaint about the problem].

We're building [one-sentence description] and recruiting 3-5 design
partners to co-develop the solution. Partners get free access,
roadmap influence, and preferred pricing at launch.

Commitment: 30 min/week for 8-12 weeks.

Interested in a quick 20-min call to explore fit?

[Calendly link]

DISCOVERY CALL AGENDA (20 minutes)
══════════════════════════════════════════════
Minutes 0-5:  Your background and what you're building (brief)
Minutes 5-12: Their pain — how they experience the problem today
  - "Walk me through how you handle [X] today."
  - "What's the most frustrating part?"
  - "What have you tried? What didn't work?"
Minutes 12-17: Partnership fit assessment
  - "If we built this, would you use it this quarter?"
  - "Who else on your team would need to be involved?"
  - "Can you commit to 30 min/week for 8 weeks?"
Minutes 17-20: Next steps
  - If qualified: "I'll send over our design partner MOU this week."
  - If not qualified: "Thanks — can I check back in 3 months?"
```

**Verify**: At least 5 discovery calls completed with 3+ prospects expressing strong interest (willing to commit time and sign an agreement).
**If failed**: If conversion from call to interest is below 30%, your problem framing may not resonate. Revisit your problem hypothesis before continuing outreach.

### Step 4: Draft and Send Design Partner Agreement

**Duration**: 2-3 hours (drafting) + 1-2 days (review cycle)
**Tool**: Google Docs

Draft a lightweight MOU covering scope, timeline, feedback expectations, IP assignment, and graduation terms. Keep it to 2-3 pages — heavy legal documents scare off early partners. [src4] [src3]

```
DESIGN PARTNER MOU — KEY SECTIONS
══════════════════════════════════════════════

1. PARTIES & PURPOSE
   "This MOU establishes a design partnership between [Company]
   ('Provider') and [Partner] ('Partner') to validate and shape
   [Product Name] through structured feedback and collaboration."

2. SCOPE
   - Job to be done: [specific workflow or problem]
   - Users involved: [role titles, number of seats]
   - 3-5 deliverables from Provider (e.g., working prototype,
     weekly builds, onboarding support)
   - Explicit NON-GOALS to prevent scope creep

3. TERM
   - Start date: [date]
   - End date: [8-12 weeks from start]
   - Decision meeting: scheduled for final week
   - Renewal: by written agreement only

4. FEEDBACK OBLIGATIONS
   - Weekly 30-45 min session (fixed day/time)
   - Named partner contact: [name, title]
   - Named provider contact: [name, title]
   - Partner agrees to provide candid product feedback

5. KPI (One Primary Metric)
   - Metric: [e.g., "reduction in manual processing time"]
   - Baseline: [current state]
   - Target: [goal — e.g., "30% reduction by week 8"]
   - Measurement: [how you'll track it]

6. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY
   - Provider retains ALL rights to the Product
   - Partner assigns all feedback, suggestions, and
     enhancement ideas to Provider
   - Partner retains ownership of their own data

7. CONFIDENTIALITY
   - Mutual NDA (attach separately or include inline)
   - 2-3 year confidentiality term
   - Standard carve-outs (public info, prior knowledge)

8. REFERENCE PERMISSIONS (Conditional on KPI Achievement)
   [ ] Logo use on Provider website
   [ ] Public testimonial quote
   [ ] Joint case study (Partner review before publication)
   Permissions unlock ONLY if KPI target is met.

9. FEES & GRADUATION
   - During partnership: $0 (free access)
   - Upon successful completion: preferred pricing offer
     at [X]% discount for [12] months
   - If KPI not met: partnership ends, no obligations

10. TERMINATION
    - Either party may terminate with 14 days written notice
    - Upon termination: access revoked, confidential info
      returned/destroyed

SIGNATURES
Provider: _____ Date: _____
Partner:  _____ Date: _____
```

**Verify**: Agreement covers all 10 sections above. IP assignment clause is explicit (not implied). Have a legal-savvy advisor review if possible, or use Common Paper's open-source template as a base. [src3]
**If failed**: If partners resist signing, simplify further — a mutual email confirming scope, timeline, and IP assignment is better than no agreement at all. Never proceed without IP assignment in writing. [src2]

### Step 5: Onboard Partners and Launch Feedback Cadence

**Duration**: 1-2 hours per partner
**Tool**: Calendly + Zoom/Meet + Google Sheets

Once agreements are signed, onboard each partner within 7 days. Set up the recurring feedback infrastructure. [src4] [src6]

```
PARTNER ONBOARDING CHECKLIST
══════════════════════════════════════════════
[ ] Signed MOU received and filed
[ ] Product access provisioned (account created, credentials sent)
[ ] Kickoff call scheduled within 7 days of signing
[ ] Recurring weekly session booked (same day/time for 8-12 weeks)
[ ] Partner added to feedback tracking spreadsheet
[ ] Slack/email channel created for async questions
[ ] Decision meeting pre-scheduled for final week

KICKOFF CALL AGENDA (45 minutes)
══════════════════════════════════════════════
Minutes 0-10:  Restate scope, KPI, and timeline
Minutes 10-25: Product walkthrough / initial setup together
Minutes 25-35: Collect baseline measurement for KPI
Minutes 35-40: Confirm weekly session format and expectations
Minutes 40-45: Q&A, confirm next session date

WEEKLY FEEDBACK SESSION TEMPLATE (30 minutes)
══════════════════════════════════════════════
Minutes 0-5:   KPI check — review metric trend
Minutes 5-10:  Review what was shipped since last session
Minutes 10-20: Structured feedback collection:
  - "What worked well this week?"
  - "What was frustrating or broken?"
  - "What's the one thing that would make the biggest
    difference to your workflow?"
Minutes 20-25: Prioritize next 1-2 things to build/fix
Minutes 25-30: Confirm action items for both sides

FEEDBACK LOG (Google Sheets)
══════════════════════════════════════════════
Columns:
  A: Date
  B: Partner Name
  C: Feedback Category (UX / Feature / Bug / Pricing / Workflow)
  D: Verbatim Feedback (exact words)
  E: Severity (blocker / important / nice-to-have)
  F: Frequency (how many partners mentioned this)
  G: Product Decision (build / defer / reject)
  H: Status (logged / in-progress / shipped / declined)
```

**Verify**: All partners have completed kickoff calls, have product access, and have the first 4 weekly sessions on the calendar. Feedback log is set up and accessible to the team.
**If failed**: If a partner misses the kickoff or first two sessions, they lack urgency. Replace them with the next prospect in your pipeline — do not chase disengaged partners.

### Step 6: Run the Program and Prepare Graduation

**Duration**: 8-12 weeks (ongoing)
**Tool**: All of the above

Execute weekly sessions, track feedback, and prepare for the graduation decision at the end of the term. [src6] [src8]

```
PROGRAM MANAGEMENT CADENCE
══════════════════════════════════════════════
Weekly:
  - Run all partner feedback sessions
  - Update feedback log after each session
  - Ship at least one partner-influenced improvement
  - Send async update to all partners: what you built, what's next

Bi-weekly:
  - Cross-partner analysis: which themes appear across 2+ partners?
  - Apply the "dependency test" for each feature request:
    "Would I build this if this partner disappeared tomorrow?"
  - Update product roadmap based on cross-partner patterns

Week 6-8 (Mid-point Check):
  - Review KPI progress with each partner
  - Assess engagement level (attendance, feedback quality)
  - Replace disengaged partners if needed

Final Week — GRADUATION DECISION MEETING
══════════════════════════════════════════════
Agenda (30 minutes per partner):
  1. Review KPI: did we hit the target? (5 min)
  2. Value assessment: "Has this been worth your time?" (5 min)
  3. Willingness to pay: "At $X/month, would you continue?" (10 min)
  4. Reference permissions: confirm logo/quote/case study (5 min)
  5. Next steps: paid conversion or friendly conclusion (5 min)

Graduation outcomes:
  - KPI met + willing to pay → Convert to paid customer
  - KPI met + not willing to pay → Capture case study, end engagement
  - KPI not met → Analyze why, end engagement, apply learnings
  - Partner requests extension → Max 4 additional weeks with new KPI

Conversion pricing:
  - Standard: 20-30% discount off list price for 12 months
  - Premium: 40-50% discount if they commit to annual contract + case study
```

**Verify**: At least 2 of your 3-5 partners complete the full program. At least 1 partner converts to paid or commits to a case study. You have a cross-partner feedback synthesis identifying the top 3 product priorities.
**If failed**: If zero partners are willing to pay, this is a strong signal that you have not achieved product-market fit. Review feedback logs for the gap between what partners need and what you built. [src8]

## Quality Benchmarks

| Quality Metric | Minimum Acceptable | Good | Excellent |
|---------------|-------------------|------|-----------|
| Partners signed | 3 | 4-5 | 5-7 with waitlist |
| Session attendance rate | > 70% | > 85% | > 95% |
| Feedback themes across 2+ partners | 3 themes | 5-7 themes | 8+ themes with clear priority |
| Partner-to-paid conversion | 0% (learning value) | 20-30% (1 of 4) | 50%+ (2+ of 4) |
| Time from outreach to first signed partner | < 4 weeks | < 2 weeks | < 1 week |

**If below minimum**: If you cannot sign 3 partners within 4 weeks, your problem hypothesis or ICP definition likely needs revision. Return to customer discovery before retrying.

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Cannot find 15 prospects matching ICP | ICP definition too narrow or niche too small | Broaden one filter (company size or industry) while keeping pain urgency high |
| Partners sign but do not attend sessions | They lack genuine urgency or champion is not the actual user | Replace with next prospect; in discovery call, verify the champion is the daily user, not just an executive [src1] |
| All feedback points in different directions | Partners are not representative of the same ICP segment | Review selection criteria; you may have recruited from different segments unintentionally |
| Partner wants custom features unrelated to vision | Single partner is an outlier, or your vision does not match market need | Apply dependency test: "Would I build this if they disappeared?" If no, log and defer [src8] |
| Partner refuses to sign IP assignment clause | Legal team pushback at larger companies | Offer mutual confidentiality + narrow IP scope to "product suggestions and feedback only"; escalate to partner's exec sponsor |
| Partner wants to pay immediately (skipping program) | Strong product-market fit signal for this segment | Accept payment but still run the feedback program — paying partners give even more honest feedback [src6] |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Recruiting prestigious logos instead of urgent users
Founders target Fortune 500 logos for credibility but these companies have slow procurement, committee decisions, and low urgency. The partnership stalls after signing because nobody internally champions daily usage. [src1] [src8]

### Correct: Recruit based on urgency and capacity first
Target mid-market or growth-stage companies where the champion is the actual daily user with decision-making authority. A passionate user at a 50-person company gives better feedback than a disengaged VP at a Fortune 500. [src7]

### Wrong: Running open-ended partnerships with no end date
Without a hard deadline, partners lose motivation, sessions become sporadic, and the founder avoids the uncomfortable graduation conversation. Six months later, you have free users, not product-market fit data. [src4]

### Correct: Set a hard 8-12 week term with a pre-scheduled decision meeting
Book the graduation meeting on the day you sign the MOU. Both sides know the timeline, which creates healthy urgency and forces a clear buy/no-buy decision. [src4] [src6]

### Wrong: Treating design partner traction as revenue traction
Founders tell investors "we have 5 design partners" as if it were customer traction. Design partners are not customers — they are getting free access in exchange for feedback. Investors know the difference. [src5]

### Correct: Separate design partners from paying customers in all reporting
Track design partners and paying customers in separate categories. The real traction metric is how many partners convert to paid after the program ends. [src5] [src8]

## When This Matters

Use this recipe when a founder has a product concept or working prototype and needs structured feedback from real target users before committing to a product direction. It produces a running design partner program — not a document about the concept of design partners. Requires an ICP definition and some form of product demo as inputs.
