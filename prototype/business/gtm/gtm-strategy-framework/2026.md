# Go-to-Market Strategy Framework

## Definition

A go-to-market (GTM) strategy is a comprehensive framework that aligns product, sales, marketing, and customer success teams around launching and scaling a product or entering a new market. It answers five core questions: who buys, why they buy, what you charge, how they find you, and how you measure success. The three primary GTM motions are product-led growth (PLG), sales-led growth, and hybrid models combining both. In 2025, 91% of SaaS companies plan to increase PLG investment, but hybrid models are the dominant approach for B2B SaaS. [src1]

## Key Properties

- **Primary GTM motions**: Product-led growth (PLG), sales-led, community-led, hybrid [src1]
- **PLG threshold**: Works when ACV < $10K and users can self-onboard [src1]
- **LTV:CAC minimum**: 3:1 ratio required for sustainable unit economics [src2]
- **B2B buyer shift**: 39% of B2B buyers willing to make purchases of $500K+ through self-service channels [src2]
- **PLG adoption**: 91% of SaaS companies plan to invest more in PLG (2025 benchmark) [src1]
- **Stage-dependent**: GTM must be rebuilt at each revenue stage ($0-$1M, $1M-$10M, $10M-$100M) [src4]

## Constraints
<!-- Agents: read this section before recommending this concept/framework.
     These are hard boundaries on when and how it applies. -->

- No universal best GTM motion — the right choice depends on ACV, product complexity, buyer persona, and sales cycle length [src5]
- PLG requires a product that delivers value within minutes without human assistance — complex enterprise products cannot be purely PLG [src1]
- LTV:CAC ratio must exceed 3:1 for any GTM motion to be sustainable — below this, the unit economics collapse regardless of motion type [src2]
- GTM strategy must be rebuilt at each revenue stage — the founder-led sales that works at $0-$1M fails at $1M-$10M [src4]
- Hybrid models require careful segmentation — trying to run PLG and enterprise sales on the same funnel creates channel conflict [src5]

## Framework Selection Decision Tree

```
START — User needs to build or optimize a GTM motion
├── What ACV range?
│   ├── < $1K → PLG only (freemium/free trial, self-serve)
│   ├── $1K-$10K → PLG + sales-assist (hybrid)
│   ├── $10K-$100K → Sales-led + PLG for acquisition ← MOST B2B SaaS
│   ├── $100K+ → Enterprise sales-led (AE + SE + CSM)
│   └── Variable (usage-based) → PLG + expansion sales
├── Product complexity?
│   ├── User can self-onboard in < 5 minutes → PLG viable
│   ├── Needs demo/setup → Sales-assist required
│   └── Multi-stakeholder decision → Enterprise sales required
├── Revenue stage?
│   ├── $0-$1M → Founder-led sales (learn the motion)
│   ├── $1M-$10M → First sales hires (repeat the motion)
│   └── $10M+ → Specialized teams (scale the motion)
└── What's the primary acquisition channel?
    ├── Inbound (content, SEO, word-of-mouth) → PLG or inbound sales
    ├── Outbound (SDR, cold outreach) → Sales-led
    └── Partner/channel → Channel-led GTM
```

## Application Checklist

### Step 1: Define ICP and buyer persona
- **Inputs needed**: Existing customer data, win/loss analysis, industry vertical focus, company size segmentation
- **Output**: Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) with firmographic, technographic, and behavioral criteria
- **Constraint**: ICP must be validated with at least 10-20 closed-won customer interviews — assumed ICPs based on founder intuition are wrong 60%+ of the time [src3]

### Step 2: Select primary GTM motion
- **Inputs needed**: ACV, product complexity, buyer persona, competitive landscape
- **Output**: Primary GTM motion selection (PLG, sales-led, or hybrid) with justification
- **Constraint**: If ACV exceeds $10K and the product requires configuration, pure PLG will fail — add a sales-assist layer [src1]

### Step 3: Build the funnel and set metrics
- **Inputs needed**: Expected traffic/lead volume, conversion rates at each stage, target ARR
- **Output**: Full funnel model (visitor → lead → MQL → SQL → opportunity → closed-won) with stage-by-stage conversion targets
- **Constraint**: LTV:CAC must be 3:1+ when modeled end-to-end; if the model cannot achieve this, reconsider pricing or ICP before investing in scaling [src2]

### Step 4: Hire and structure the team
- **Inputs needed**: Revenue stage, selected GTM motion, budget constraints
- **Output**: Hiring plan with role priorities (AE, SDR, CSM, marketing) and timing
- **Constraint**: At $0-$1M, founders must sell directly to learn the motion before hiring salespeople — skipping this step means hiring for a motion you do not understand [src4]

### Step 5: Measure, iterate, and add new motions
- **Inputs needed**: Monthly metrics (pipeline, conversion rates, CAC, LTV, payback period)
- **Output**: Performance dashboard with leading indicators and decision criteria for adding new motions
- **Constraint**: Do not add a second GTM motion until the first is repeatable — running two motions poorly is worse than one motion well [src5]

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Choosing GTM motion based on competitor strategy
Copying a competitor's PLG or sales-led approach without evaluating your own ACV, product complexity, and buyer persona leads to misaligned investment and wasted resources. [src5]

### Correct: Select GTM motion based on your product-market context
Evaluate ACV, product complexity, buyer persona, and sales cycle independently. A $50K ACV product cannot succeed with pure PLG regardless of what competitors do. [src1]

### Wrong: Hiring a VP of Sales before achieving founder-led sales success
Founders who hire senior sales leaders before personally closing 10-20 deals cannot evaluate sales talent, set realistic quotas, or understand the sales cycle. [src4]

### Correct: Founder-led sales through $1M ARR, then hire
Close 10-20 deals personally, document the playbook (objection handling, demo flow, pricing conversation), then hire a sales leader who can repeat and scale it. [src4]

### Wrong: Running PLG and enterprise sales on the same funnel
Users who self-serve through a free trial and enterprise prospects who need a demo have different needs — forcing them through one funnel creates friction for both. [src5]

### Correct: Build separate funnels for self-serve and sales-assisted
Segment acquisition channels: PLG users get in-product onboarding and usage-triggered upgrade prompts; enterprise leads get AE-led discovery calls and custom demos. [src1]

## Common Misconceptions

- **Misconception**: PLG means no sales team is needed.
  **Reality**: Most successful PLG companies (Slack, Zoom, Datadog) have large sales teams. PLG is an acquisition strategy, not a replacement for sales. It generates leads that sales teams close at higher ACVs. [src1]

- **Misconception**: GTM strategy is set once and runs forever.
  **Reality**: GTM must be rebuilt at each revenue stage. Founder-led sales ($0-$1M) gives way to first sales hires ($1M-$10M), which gives way to specialized teams ($10M+). [src4]

- **Misconception**: More marketing spend equals more pipeline.
  **Reality**: Without product-market fit and a validated ICP, increasing marketing spend just accelerates waste. Fix PMF and ICP first, then scale spend. [src3]
