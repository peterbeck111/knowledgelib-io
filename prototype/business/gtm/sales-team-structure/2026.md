# B2B Sales Team Structure

## Definition

B2B sales team structure defines how sales roles (SDR, BDR, AE, CSM) are organized, how leads flow between them, and how quotas and territories are assigned. The two dominant organizational models are the pool model (SDRs and AEs operate independently with round-robin handoffs) and the pod model (small dedicated teams of SDR + AE + CSM own a territory or segment together). In 2025-2026, AI-driven prospecting is fundamentally reshaping these structures — 36% of B2B companies cut SDR headcount in 2025, not because the function disappeared but because AI tools allow fewer people to handle equal or greater volume. [src1]

## Key Properties

- **SDR/AE ratio**: The current average is 1 SDR per 2.6 AEs — inverted from the traditional 2:1 SDR:AE ratio, reflecting AI automation of prospecting tasks [src4]
- **Pod model composition**: Typically 1 SDR + 1-2 AEs + 1 CSM working a defined territory or segment together [src2]
- **Pool model composition**: Separate SDR team and AE team with centralized lead routing (round-robin or scoring-based) [src5]
- **AI impact**: AI tools for prospecting, email sequencing, and lead qualification allow smaller SDR teams to handle the same or greater outbound volume [src1]
- **Quota attainment benchmark**: Healthy teams achieve 60-70% of reps at or above quota; below 50% signals a structural or targeting problem [src4]

## Constraints
<!-- Agents: read this section before recommending this concept/framework.
     These are hard boundaries on when and how it applies. -->

- The SDR/AE split is not cost-effective for ACV below $5K — at low deal sizes, PLG or self-serve is more capital-efficient than human-driven prospecting [src3]
- Pod models require at least 3 pods (9-12 people) to justify the management overhead and provide enough data to compare performance across pods [src2]
- AI-driven SDR automation does not eliminate the need for human SDRs entirely — complex enterprise sales with 6+ month cycles still require relationship-driven prospecting [src1]
- Quota setting disconnected from pipeline reality is the top cause of sales team attrition — quotas must be bottom-up validated against available pipeline [src4]
- Hiring two AEs simultaneously in the same territory/segment creates a natural experiment; hiring one at a time makes it impossible to distinguish individual performance from territory quality [src3]

## Framework Selection Decision Tree

```
START — User needs to structure a B2B sales team
├── What's the ACV?
│   ├── < $5K → PLG / self-serve, no dedicated sales team needed
│   ├── $5K-$25K → Inside sales (SDR + AE, pool model)
│   ├── $25K-$100K → Inside sales (pod model recommended)
│   └── > $100K → Field sales + enterprise SDR team
├── How many reps total?
│   ├── 1-4 reps → Founder-led sales, no SDR/AE split yet
│   ├── 5-12 reps → Pool model (simpler management)
│   ├── 12-30 reps → Pod model (better territory ownership)
│   └── 30+ reps → Segment-based pods + specialized teams
├── Should you split SDR and AE roles?
│   ├── ACV > $10K AND sales cycle > 30 days → YES, split
│   ├── ACV < $10K OR sales cycle < 30 days → NO, full-cycle AEs
│   └── Using AI SDR tools effectively → Fewer SDRs, higher ratio
└── Pod vs Pool?
    ├── < 12 reps → Pool (not enough people for meaningful pods)
    ├── Segment-based selling → Pods (each pod owns a segment)
    └── Geographic territories → Pods (each pod owns a region)
```

## Application Checklist

### Step 1: Define the Sales Motion
- **Inputs needed**: ACV, sales cycle length, buyer persona, product complexity, current close rates
- **Output**: Documented sales motion — PLG, inside sales, field sales, or hybrid — with the role split decision (full-cycle AE vs SDR/AE split)
- **Constraint**: If ACV is below $10K and sales cycle is under 30 days, do not split SDR and AE — full-cycle AEs are more efficient [src3]

### Step 2: Choose Organizational Model
- **Inputs needed**: Team size, territory/segment strategy, management capacity, data on lead flow patterns
- **Output**: Pool or pod model decision with documented rationale, team composition, and lead routing rules
- **Constraint**: Pod model requires minimum 3 pods (9+ people) — if you have fewer, use pool model with clear territory assignments [src5]

### Step 3: Set Quotas Bottom-Up
- **Inputs needed**: Historical pipeline data, conversion rates at each stage, average deal size, sales cycle length, SDR activity metrics
- **Output**: Individual quotas derived from: available pipeline x win rate x average deal size, with a 1.3-1.5x quota-to-target ratio (pipeline coverage)
- **Constraint**: Quotas must be achievable by 60-70% of the team — if fewer than 50% hit quota in Q1, the quota is set too high, not the team too weak [src4]

### Step 4: Design Compensation
- **Inputs needed**: Market comp data for your city/segment, quota targets, OTE benchmarks
- **Output**: Compensation plan with base/variable split (typically 50/50 for AEs, 60/40 or 70/30 for SDRs), accelerators above quota, and clear measurement criteria
- **Constraint**: SDR compensation must be tied to metrics they control (qualified meetings booked, not revenue closed) — tying SDR pay to closed deals creates frustration and attrition [src1]

### Step 5: Build the AI-Augmented Workflow
- **Inputs needed**: Current SDR workflow, tooling stack, prospecting volume targets
- **Output**: AI-augmented workflow where AI handles research, initial outreach drafting, and lead scoring, while human SDRs focus on personalization, relationship building, and complex qualification
- **Constraint**: AI augmentation reduces headcount needs by 30-50% but requires 4-6 weeks of tooling setup and workflow redesign — it is not plug-and-play [src1]

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Splitting SDR and AE roles at low ACV
Companies selling $3K-$5K ACV deals often create separate SDR and AE teams, doubling their CAC without meaningfully improving close rates. The overhead of lead handoff and coordination exceeds the benefit of specialization at low deal sizes. [src3]

### Correct: Use full-cycle AEs for ACV below $10K
Full-cycle AEs who prospect, qualify, and close their own deals eliminate handoff friction and reduce headcount. At $5-10K ACV, one strong full-cycle AE replaces an SDR + AE pair. [src1]

### Wrong: Setting quotas top-down from revenue targets
Leadership says "we need $10M in new ARR" and divides by headcount. This ignores pipeline reality, territory quality, and market dynamics, resulting in unachievable quotas and team attrition. [src4]

### Correct: Build quotas bottom-up from pipeline data
Calculate available pipeline per rep, multiply by historical win rate and average deal size, and set quota at 70-80% of that figure. Adjust territory assignments to equalize opportunity across the team. [src4]

### Wrong: Maintaining pre-AI SDR headcount in 2025-2026
Companies that built 20-person SDR teams in 2022 and have not restructured are carrying excess headcount. AI tools for prospecting and sequencing can handle 3-5x the volume of manual SDRs. [src1]

### Correct: Right-size SDR teams with AI augmentation
Audit current SDR workflows, identify tasks that AI can handle (research, initial outreach, follow-up sequencing, lead scoring), and restructure the team to focus human SDRs on high-judgment tasks — complex qualification, relationship building, and multi-threading into accounts. [src3]

## Common Misconceptions

- **Misconception**: The SDR role is dying because of AI.
  **Reality**: 36% of companies reduced SDR headcount in 2025, but the function is not disappearing — it is being restructured. Companies are doing more with fewer SDRs by leveraging AI for prospecting volume, while human SDRs focus on complex, relationship-driven outbound. [src1]

- **Misconception**: Pod models are always better than pool models.
  **Reality**: Pods provide better territory ownership and accountability but require minimum scale (3+ pods, 9+ people) and add management complexity. For teams under 12 people, pool models with clear territory assignments outperform pods. [src5]

- **Misconception**: More SDRs equals more pipeline.
  **Reality**: SDR productivity has a ceiling — adding headcount beyond the available qualified territory creates diminishing returns. A team of 5 AI-augmented SDRs targeting the right accounts generates more pipeline than 15 manual SDRs with poor targeting. [src4]
