# Best Toaster Ovens and Countertop Ovens (2026)

## Summary

The toaster oven market in 2026 has expanded well beyond simple toast-making. Today's countertop ovens serve as full-featured cooking appliances with air frying, convection baking, dehydrating, smart wireless probes, and even dual-zone cooking capabilities. Prices range from under $50 for basic models to over $400 for premium smart ovens. The best overall toaster oven is the Breville Smart Oven Pro BOV845BSS (~$270), which earns top marks from Consumer Reports for broiling, toasting consistency, and ease of use. Its Element IQ technology distributes heat intelligently across 5 quartz elements, and it offers 10 cooking functions with 1800W of power. [src1, src3, src4]

For air-fryer enthusiasts, the Cuisinart Digital AirFryer Toaster Oven TOA-65 (~$230) remains the consensus pick, combining 1800W convection power with a dedicated air-fry basket that produces crispy results rivaling standalone air fryers. The Ninja Double Oven DCT401 (~$350) stands out for dual-zone cooking, offering two independent cooking chambers with FlexDoor technology. The Typhur Sync Oven (~$230) introduced a built-in wireless meat probe with five sensors and AI-powered recipe generation, earning RTINGS' top rating for smart cooking precision. New for April 2026, the Ninja French Door Premier FO101 (~$280) has replaced the Ninja Prestige Smart XL as Tom's Guide's top overall pick — its dual swing-out doors, 20.3 QT capacity, 10-in-1 functions, and PFAS-free cooking surface deliver oven-replacement versatility in a countertop footprint. The Ninja Prestige Smart XL DT551 (~$250-$430) still brings 10x convection power and a 90-second preheat to the premium segment. [src1, src2, src3, src5]

Budget shoppers should consider the Hamilton Beach 2-in-1 Toaster Oven 31156 (~$110), the only model in Consumer Reports' ratings to earn top scores in every toasting test while also performing solidly in baking evaluations. For compact kitchens, the Panasonic FlashXpress NB-G110P (~$130) heats up instantly with double infrared technology, eliminating preheat time entirely. At the premium end, the Breville Joule Oven Air Fryer Pro BOV950BSS (~$400) adds Wi-Fi connectivity and 13 smart cooking functions with the Joule app for guided recipes. [src1, src2, src4, src6]

## Important Caveats

- Prices are approximate street prices as of March 2026. Sales, promotions, and regional pricing vary significantly. Amazon and Best Buy frequently run deals on these models.
- Wattage alone does not determine cooking performance. Element placement, convection design, and insulation all affect how evenly and efficiently a toaster oven cooks. The Panasonic FlashXpress uses only 1300W but outperforms some 1800W models on certain tasks.
- Air fry results in a toaster oven are comparable but not identical to a dedicated air fryer. Basket-style air fryers circulate air more aggressively, while toaster ovens trade slightly less crispiness for greater capacity and versatility.
- Counter space is a real consideration. Large models like the Breville BOV900BSS, Ninja DCT401, and Ninja Prestige DT551 have footprints exceeding 20" wide by 15" deep. Measure your counter before purchasing.
- Consumer Reports testing uses standardized protocols; real-world results may vary based on altitude, voltage fluctuations, and cookware used.
- Smart oven features (Typhur Sync, Breville Joule) require a 2.4 GHz Wi-Fi network and a companion app. They do not work offline or with 5 GHz-only routers.
