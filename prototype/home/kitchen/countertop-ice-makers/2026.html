Summary

The countertop ice maker market in 2026 splits into four distinct ice formats: nugget (chewable pellet), bullet (cylindrical hollow), clear square cube, and — newly mainstream — crescent (restaurant-style, slow-melting). Nugget ice makers command a price premium ($130–$600) over bullet models ($35–$130), but demand for Sonic-style chewable ice continues to drive the category. The GE Profile Opal 2.0 XL (~$450–$650, frequently $399 on Amazon promotions) is now Reviewed's "Best Overall" pick — its 1-gallon side tank triples ice production vs. the standard Opal 2.0's 0.75-gallon tank, hitting 38 lbs/day. The GoveeLife Smart Nugget Ice Maker Pro (~$500, launched January 15, 2026) pushes capacity to 60 lbs/day with first ice in 6 minutes and patented AI NoiseGuard at 40dB. For budget buyers, the Frigidaire EFIC255 (~$180–$250) delivers solid nugget ice at half the Opal's price, and the Igloo ICEB26 (~$90–$130) is the best portable bullet option. [src1, src2, src3]

The biggest shifts in 2026 are (1) the EUHOMY Luna Pro crescent ice maker (~$160–$200, launched Q1 2026), which won Reviewed's "Best for Crescent Ice" — bringing restaurant-style slow-melting ice to a sub-$200 price point; and (2) Whirlpool's announcement of the first refrigerator with a built-in nugget ice maker, signaling that the countertop nugget category may be at peak in 2026 before migrating into integrated appliances. EUHOMY and Costway nugget models ($130–$200) continue to bring chewable ice down-market, though ice quality and noise remain noticeably below the GE and GoveeLife flagships. For clear ice enthusiasts, the Frigidaire EFIC452 (~$100–$160) remains the standout — producing transparent square cubes without special water prep. [src1, src7, src8]

Best for Each Use Case

Best Overall: GE Profile Opal 2.0 XL (~$450-650) — Check price

Reviewed's 2026 "Best Overall" pick. The XL variant adds a 1-gallon detachable side tank (vs. the standard 0.75-gallon), tripling production capacity between refills. Produces authentic Sonic-style chewable nugget ice up to 38 lbs/day with first ice in under 10 minutes. SmartHQ app enables scheduling, filter status, and maintenance reminders. Automatic flush cycle with UV light keeps the system sanitized. Frequently drops to $399 during Amazon Black Friday and Prime Day events (regular price ~$600). [src1, src4, src5]

Best Smart Features: GoveeLife Smart Nugget Ice Maker Pro (~$500) — Check price

CES 2026 launch (US availability January 15, 2026 at $499.99). Industry-leading 60 lbs/day capacity with first ice in 6 minutes. Patented AI NoiseGuard actively monitors and eliminates evaporator noise, achieving 40dB operation — over two-thirds quieter than typical compressor models. Dual water tanks (2.2L + 4.9L) yield 15+ lbs per fill. App and Alexa/Google voice control. 3-year warranty with quick-clean (10 min) and deep descale (30 min) modes. Reviewed notes it runs slightly louder than the Opal in real-world testing despite its 40dB rating. [src1, src2, src7]

Best Value Nugget: Frigidaire Gallery EFIC255 (~$180-250) — Check price

Reviewed's "Best Value" pick. Produces 44 lbs of nugget ice per day with a built-in water filter for fresh-tasting ice. Available in multiple colors (navy, cream, black, stainless). Compact with a carry handle for portability. No smart features, and it runs louder than the Opal or GoveeLife, but at roughly half the flagship price, it is the best entry point for nugget ice. [src1, src6]

Best Crescent (NEW 2026): EUHOMY Luna Pro (~$160-200) — Check price

Reviewed's 2026 "Best for Crescent Ice" pick — a new category in this year's roundup. Produces 16 crescent-shaped clear ice cubes per cycle (~28 lbs/day), with first batch in 12 minutes. Crescent ice melts up to 30 minutes slower than nugget or bullet, ideal for whiskey, cocktails, and slow-sipping drinks. UV self-cleaning, app-based scheduling (1-24 hours), and adjustable ice thickness. 1.8L water tank. The first widely available sub-$200 crescent maker — previously, restaurant-style crescent ice required $400+ commercial-style units. [src1]

Best Budget Nugget: EUHOMY Nugget Ice Maker (~$130-170) — Check price

Amazon's #1 Best Seller in portable ice makers. Produces 34 lbs/day of chewable nugget ice with first batch in 6–8 minutes. Portable handle and self-cleaning cycle. 1.2-quart water tank requires more frequent refills than premium models. Nugget quality is softer and wetter than the GE Opal, but acceptable for the price. Best Clear Ice: Frigidaire EFIC452 (~$100-160) — Check price

The only widely available countertop model that produces transparent square ice cubes without requiring distilled water or special prep. Makes 24 cubes in under 15 minutes, up to 40 lbs/day. Two cube sizes. Retro design with multiple color options. Reviewed calls it “Best for Clear Ice.” Ice shutoff sensor can occasionally be blocked by wayward cubes. [src1, src3]

Best Portable: Igloo ICEB26 (~$90-130) — Check price

Reviewed's "Best for Portability" pick. Lightweight with a built-in carry handle, self-cleaning cycle, and bottom drain plug. Produces 26 lbs of bullet ice per day with 9 cubes ready in 7 minutes. Small 1.25 lb basket means you transfer ice to a freezer more frequently, but for RVs, boats, and camping, the portability trade-off is worth it. [src1, src4]

Best for Hard Water: GE Profile Opal 2.0 Ultra (~$500-700) — Check price

The Ultra variant adds a scale-inhibiting filter (proprietary GE) and an improved side-tank seal vs. the standard Opal 2.0. Same 38 lbs/day output and SmartHQ app integration. Worth the $50–100 premium over the standard Opal 2.0 only in hard-water households (>120 ppm); otherwise the regular XL is the better value. [src5]

Best for Large Households: Igloo 33 lbs (~$100-130) — Check price

Highest-capacity bullet ice maker tested, producing 33 lbs/day — 27% more than standard 26 lb models. 2.5-quart tank, automatic shutoff, flat top design. Very quiet operation. Decision Logic

If user wants nugget/pellet (chewable) ice and budget allows

→ GE Profile Opal 2.0 XL (~$450-650, often $399 on sale). Reviewed's 2026 Best Overall. The 1-gallon side tank triples capacity vs. the standard Opal 2.0. The GoveeLife Pro matches it on capacity (60 vs. 38 lbs/day) but is louder under real-world testing despite its 40dB AI NoiseGuard rating. [src1, src2, src5]

If budget < $100

→ Only bullet ice makers are available at this price. The Igloo ICEB26 (~$90) is the best option with self-cleaning and portability. No nugget or crescent ice maker exists below $100 that produces acceptable quality. [src1, src4]

If budget is $100-$200 and ice type is flexible

→ Three strong picks: EUHOMY Luna Pro (~$160-200) for crescent (new 2026, slow-melting), EUHOMY (~$130-170) or Costway (~$150-200) for budget nugget, or Frigidaire EFIC452 (~$100-160) for clear square cubes. The Frigidaire EFIC255 (~$180-250) is worth stretching to for better nugget ice quality. [src1, src3]

If primary use is home bar / cocktails / whiskey

→ EUHOMY Luna Pro for crescent (slow-melting, restaurant-style — best dilution control), Frigidaire EFIC452 for clear square cubes (looks professional), or GE Profile Opal 2.0 XL for nugget (chewable, absorbs flavor). Bullet ice is the worst choice for cocktails — it melts fast and looks cheap. [src1, src6]

If noise is a priority

→ GoveeLife Smart Nugget Pro at 40dB (rated) is the quietest nugget maker on paper, with AI NoiseGuard. GE Opal 2.0 XL is moderately quiet and Reviewed actually rates it quieter than the GoveeLife in real-world use. Bullet models are generally quieter than nugget compressors. Avoid the Frigidaire EFIC255 in noise-sensitive environments. [src1, src2, src7]

If portability matters (RV, camping, tailgating)

→ Igloo ICEB26 for its carry handle, light weight, and bottom drain. EUHOMY also has a handle. Avoid the GoveeLife Pro (40+ lbs) and GE Opal XL (heavy, fragile side tank). [src1, src4]

If user has hard water (>120 ppm) or lives in scale-prone area

→ GE Profile Opal 2.0 Ultra (~$500-700). The scale-inhibiting filter materially extends time between manual descales. Otherwise, plan to descale any unit with citric acid every 2-4 weeks regardless of self-cleaning cycles. [src5]

Default recommendation

→ GE Profile Opal 2.0 XL if budget allows ($450-650, often $399 on sale) — Reviewed's 2026 consensus best nugget ice maker. If budget is under $200, Frigidaire EFIC255 for nugget, EUHOMY Luna Pro for crescent, or Igloo ICEB26 for bullet. [src1, src4, src5]

Key Market Trends (2026)

  • Crescent ice goes mainstream: The EUHOMY Luna Pro launch (Q1 2026, ~$160-200) brought restaurant-style crescent ice — previously confined to commercial machines or $400+ specialty units — into the sub-$200 countertop tier. Reviewed added "Best for Crescent" as a new category in their 2026 roundup. [src1]
  • Smart ice makers consolidate: GoveeLife Smart Nugget Pro (CES 2026 winner, January 2026 US launch) joins the GE Opal 2.0 XL with full app + voice control. Both offer scheduling, filter monitoring, and maintenance alerts. Expect smart features to become standard in $300+ models by 2027. [src2, src7]
  • Built-in nugget ice arrives: Whirlpool announced (March 2026) the first refrigerator with an integrated nugget ice maker — a 36-inch unit with both nugget and traditional cubed ice. This signals long-term migration of nugget out of countertop and into built-in appliances over the next 2-3 years. [src8]
  • Nugget ice price floor stable: EUHOMY and Costway hold the entry-level nugget price floor at $130-170. No further price compression in 2026 — the budget tier is now mature. [src3, src6]
  • Self-cleaning is universal: Every model in this comparison includes some form of self-cleaning cycle. The differentiator is now deep descaling (GoveeLife's 30-minute mode, GE's UV-assisted flush) vs. basic rinse cycles. [src1, src2]
  • Capacity race in nugget: GoveeLife's 60 lbs/day still leads, ~58% above the GE Opal XL's 38 lbs/day. High-capacity nugget models target households that previously needed commercial machines for entertaining. [src1, src7]

Important Caveats

  • Prices are approximate US street prices as of April 2026. Sales, seasonal promotions, and retailer vary. The GE Opal 2.0 XL frequently drops to $399 during Amazon Prime Day, Black Friday, and other promotional events.
  • Daily ice production figures assume continuous operation at room temperature below 90°F with consistent water supply. Actual output drops in hot environments or with intermittent use.
  • No countertop ice maker stores ice frozen — all models recycle melted ice back through the system. Transfer ice to a freezer for storage.
  • Nugget ice quality varies significantly by model. Budget nugget makers (EUHOMY, Costway) produce softer, wetter pellets compared to the drier, crunchier nuggets from the GE Opal XL and GoveeLife Pro.
  • Self-cleaning functions sanitize but do not remove mineral scale. In hard-water areas, manual descaling with citric acid every 2–4 weeks is necessary regardless of model. The GE Opal 2.0 Ultra's scale-inhibiting filter reduces but does not eliminate this.
  • The GoveeLife Pro's 40dB rating is from the manufacturer's lab. Real-world reviewer testing (Reviewed) finds it audibly louder than the GE Opal XL during the freeze cycle.
