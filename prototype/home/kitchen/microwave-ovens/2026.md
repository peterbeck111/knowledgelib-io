# Best Microwave Ovens (2026)

## Summary

The microwave oven market in 2026 is defined by inverter technology and multi-function convergence. Wirecutter's perennial top pick, the Panasonic NN-SN686S (~$230), continues to dominate thanks to its 1,200-watt inverter motor and Genius Sensor that automatically adjusts power and time based on food humidity — it cooked more evenly and precisely than any other microwave tested, with fewer hot and cold spots. Note that stock availability fluctuates; Wirecutter now also recommends the Target-exclusive NN-SN65QSD and Costco-exclusive NN-SN68QB as equivalent alternatives. [src1, src3, src7]

For buyers seeking multi-function capability, the GE GCST10A1WSS (~$230) stands out as CNN Underscored's pick for best microwave for your money, packing 1,050-watt microwave, air fryer, convection, and broiler into a compact 1.0 cu ft countertop unit. The premium Breville Smooth Wave BMO850BSS (~$400) is RTINGS' top-rated microwave overall, earning Consumer Reports' top-level owner satisfaction and predicted reliability ratings with its Sensor iQ, Power Smoothing Inverter, and 50% quieter operation. The Panasonic HomeChef Connect NN-CV87QS (~$486) is the updated successor to the NN-CD87KS, now adding app connectivity via Panasonic Kitchen+ for automated meal prep while retaining inverter microwave, air fryer, convection, and FlashXpress broiler in one unit. The IKEA GATEBO (~$199) is a notable 2026 newcomer — a 3-in-1 microwave, air fryer, and grill with a 5-year warranty at a budget-friendly price, earning Tom's Guide's #3 pick. [src2, src3, src4, src5, src9]

Budget buyers have excellent options. The Toshiba EM131A5C-BS (~$130) remains Tom's Guide's #1 pick with even cooking performance and humidity sensor at a mid-range price point. The Sharp SMC1162HS (~$140) is RTINGS' best budget microwave pick, offering 1,000 watts in a 1.1 cu ft stainless steel cavity — it heats food very quickly and fills a gap between basic and premium models. The Commercial Chef CHM990B (~$110) is Consumer Reports' surprise mid-size value pick, earning very good scores for heating evenness, defrosting, ease of use, and noise. For ultra-budget needs, the BLACK+DECKER EM925AB9 (~$85) and Commercial Chef CHM770B (~$65) deliver capable basic microwaving. [src2, src3, src5, src6, src7]

## Key Market Trends (2026)

- **Inverter technology goes mainstream**: Panasonic, Breville, LG, and GE now offer inverter microwaves across multiple price points. Unlike conventional microwaves that pulse between full power and off, inverter models deliver continuous, adjustable power for dramatically better defrosting, reheating, and sensor cooking performance. This is the single most impactful technology upgrade in microwaves. [src1, src3]
- **Multi-function convergence reaches budget tier**: The IKEA GATEBO ($199) brings 3-in-1 microwave/air fryer/grill to under $200 with a 5-year warranty, joining the GE GCST10A1WSS ($230) and Panasonic HomeChef Connect NN-CV87QS ($486) in a crowded multi-function field. These combo units increasingly rival standalone air fryers and toaster ovens, now accessible across all price points. [src4, src5, src9]
- **Smart connectivity reaches countertop models**: The Panasonic HomeChef Connect NN-CV87QS adds Panasonic Kitchen+ app integration with automated meal prep to the countertop category. Combined with OTR smart models like the LG MVEM1825F (Scan-to-Cook, ThinQ app), connected microwaves are no longer limited to the $400+ over-the-range segment. [src2, src4]
- **Quiet operation as a premium differentiator**: The Breville Smooth Wave's 50% quieter operation and soft-close door address a common consumer complaint. Consumer Reports now includes noise as a standard test metric, and quiet performance is emerging as a key selling point at premium price points. [src2, src3]
- **Reddit reliability data maturing**: RedRecs analysis of 6,800+ Reddit comments shows Panasonic Inverter leading community recommendations (93 signal score) despite door latch durability concerns. - Prices are approximate US street prices as of April 2026. Sales and promotional pricing, especially during holiday events, can reduce prices by 20-40%.
- Wattage ratings for microwave and convection modes differ. The LG MHEC1737F's 950W microwave power and 1,650W convection power are separate specifications. Compare microwave wattage for reheating performance and convection wattage for baking/air frying performance.
- Inverter microwaves cost more upfront but deliver meaningfully better results for defrosting, sensor cooking, and reheating at reduced power levels. If you primarily use full power for quick reheating, a conventional microwave at a lower price may suffice. [src1, src3]
- Usable interior capacity is typically about half of the manufacturer-stated total capacity. A 1.2 cu ft microwave has roughly 0.6 cu ft of practical cooking space after accounting for the turntable and clearance needs. [src2]
- Over-the-range microwaves require professional installation and proper ventilation ducting. They are not interchangeable with countertop models. Both the LG MVEM1825F and LG MHEC1737F have 300 CFM exhaust, adequate for standard cooking but may not suffice for heavy-duty range ventilation. [src2]
- The IKEA GATEBO is sold exclusively through IKEA stores and ikea.com — it is not available on Amazon or other third-party retailers. [src5, src9]
- Panasonic NN-SN686S stock availability fluctuates — Wirecutter now recommends the Target-exclusive NN-SN65QSD and Costco-exclusive NN-SN68QB as identical-performance alternatives if the primary model is out of stock. [src1]
- Door latch failure is the most common microwave reliability issue across all brands, according to both Consumer Reports data and Reddit user reports (6,800+ comments analyzed). Extended warranties may be worth considering for premium models. [src2, src7]
