Best for Each Use Case

Best Overall (NEW 2026): Ninja Prestige Smart XL DT501 (~$229–400) — Check price

Tom's Guide's new #1 toaster oven for 2026. 1800W Smart Surround Convection delivers up to 10× the convection power of a traditional built-in oven with no need to flip or rotate food. 90-second preheat, cooks up to 30% faster than a conventional oven. Fits a 12 lb turkey or bakes 20 cookies on a single rack. 10 functions: air fry, air roast, whole roast, bake, broil, toast, bagel, pizza, dehydrate, reheat. MSRP $400 but routinely drops to $229–249 on Amazon. Tom's Guide called it "a super-powerful choice that can easily outperform your oven" — note it is louder than competitors. [src8, src9]

Best Premium: Breville Smart Oven Air Fryer Pro (~$400) — Check price

The consensus premium pick across Reviewed, Taste of Home, and Consumer Reports. Element iQ system uses 5 independent quartz heating elements with smart algorithms that direct heat where it is needed. Super convection mode with dual-speed fan reduces cooking time by up to 30%. Temperature stays within ±5°F of target across 200–425°F range — better precision than most full-size ovens. Fits a 13×9 pan, 12-cup muffin tray, or 9 slices of toast. Pricier than the Ninja Prestige but still the gold standard for temperature precision. [src1, src3, src6]

Best Dual-Zone Cooking: Ninja DCT401 12-in-1 Double Oven (~$249–350) — Check price

Still the only true dual-chamber countertop oven. FlexDoor design creates two independent oven chambers — the top handles broil, toast, bagel, bake, and reheat, while the bottom does air fry, convection bake, pizza, air roast, whole roast, and dehydrate. Smart Finish syncs both ovens so a full meal finishes at the same time. FlavorSeal technology prevents flavor crossover. Fits 12-inch pizza and 6 lbs of wings simultaneously, feeding up to 8 people. Cooks 65% faster than traditional ovens. Sale price has dropped to ~$249 on Amazon as the Prestige takes the flagship spotlight. [src2, src4, src5]

Best for Bakers: KitchenAid Dual Convection Countertop Oven (~$350) — Check price

Reviewed's 2026 best-for-bakers pick — "the convection setting performed better than any other model" in baking tests. Even-Heat dual convection technology cooks chicken up to 20% faster. 360-degree air fry system with no-flip basket. Built-in temperature probe alerts when food reaches target. Fast preheat hits 350°F in under 4 minutes. 12 functions including dough proofing. Quiet operation and premium build. [src1, src3, src4]

Best Smart Oven: GE Profile UltraFast Smart Oven (~$449) — Check price

WiFi-connected with SmartHQ app control, voice commands via Alexa and Google, and downloadable cooking functions. 11 preset modes up to 500°F. Zero-preheat technology targets food directly. Fits 12-inch pizza, 13×9 sheet cake, or up to 7 lbs. Garage-door style opening frees counter space. Excels at air frying and casseroles. [src1, src3]

Best Mid-Range: Breville Smart Oven Pro (~$270) — Check price

The slightly smaller, more affordable sibling of the Air Fryer Pro. Same Element iQ system with 5 quartz elements and convection up to 30% faster. 10 cooking functions. Interior fits a 13-inch pizza. Highly rated for even toasting, consistent baking, and reliable broiling. Best option if you do not need air fry or dehydrate. [src4, src6]

Best Compact: Our Place Wonder Oven (~$145–195) — Check price

At 11.6 × 10.6 × 11.5 inches (11.6 lbs), it takes up minimal counter space while delivering 6 functions: air fry, steam, bake, toast, roast, and reheat. Exclusive steam infusion technology restores moisture to reheated food. Attractive retro design in multiple colors. Cannot accommodate 12-inch pizzas — best for 1–2 person households or as a secondary oven. [src1, src7, src8]

Best Budget Smart: Cosori Smart 11-in-1 (~$145) — Check price

WiFi connectivity and app control at a budget price. 26 QT capacity fits a 12-inch pizza. 11 functions including air fry, rotisserie, and dehydrate. Dishwasher-safe accessories. Note: temperature consistency can vary, and it cooks faster than expected — monitor to avoid burning. [src1, src3]

Best Under $100: Hamilton Beach Easy Reach Convection (~$70) — Check price

Roll-top door opens upward instead of outward, freeing counter space. 6-slice capacity with convection, bake, broil, and toast. Temperature range 150–450°F. Minimal features but reliable convection performance at the lowest price in this comparison. [src4, src7]

Key Market Trends (2026)

  • Ninja overtakes Breville at the top: Tom's Guide's April 2026 ranking promoted the Ninja Prestige Smart XL DT501 to #1, citing its Smart Surround Convection (10× conventional convection power) and aggressive sale pricing (~$229 vs ~$400 for the Breville). Breville still wins on temperature precision but Ninja now wins on value-per-watt. [src8, src9]
  • Air fry is now standard: Every model above $100 includes air frying capability. Dedicated basket-style air fryers are losing ground to multi-function convection ovens that air fry equally well with more versatility. [src1, src3]
  • Dual-zone cooking arrives — and gets cheaper: The Ninja DCT401 still owns the dual-chamber niche but has dropped from a $349 launch to ~$249 on sale as the Prestige absorbs flagship marketing attention. Expect competitors to follow. [src2, src5]
  • Smart connectivity goes mainstream: WiFi, app control, and voice integration are no longer premium-only features. The Cosori offers app control at ~$145, while the GE Profile pushes the envelope with downloadable cooking functions. [src1, src3]
  • Steam infusion emerging: The Our Place Wonder Oven and Tovala use steam to improve reheating and baking. Steam-assisted cooking was previously limited to $2,000+ built-in ovens — now available under $200. [src1, src7]
  • Temperature probe integration: KitchenAid and Ninja Prestige Pro Cook System (DT551) now include built-in temperature probes — a feature borrowed from smart grills that eliminates guesswork on proteins. [src3, src9]
  • Counter space optimization: Roll-top doors (Hamilton Beach) and garage-style doors (GE Profile) reflect growing awareness that counter space is the main barrier to adoption. [src1, src4]
  • Ninja's aggressive discounting: Both Prestige DT501 and DCT401 routinely drop 30–45% below MSRP on Amazon and Best Buy — Super Bowl, Prime Day, and Black Friday windows are the best buy moments. [src8]

Important Caveats

  • Prices are approximate US street prices as of April 2026. Sales events (Prime Day, Black Friday, Super Bowl) drop Ninja and Cosori prices 30–45%. Ninja Prestige DT501 was seen at $229.99 on Amazon as recently as 2026-04-18.
  • Countertop convection ovens draw 1200–1800W — verify your kitchen circuit can handle the load alongside other appliances.
  • “Air fry” in a countertop oven is convection cooking with high airflow, not traditional deep frying. Results are healthier but texturally different.
  • Smart features require WiFi and a compatible smartphone. App experiences vary widely — GE Profile’s SmartHQ is mature, while budget brands may have less reliable apps.
  • The Café Couture Smart Oven (~$350–400), previously recommended by Reviewed, has been discontinued as of early 2026.
  • The Ninja Prestige DT501 is reportedly louder than its peers per Tom's Guide testing — note for noise-sensitive kitchens or open floor plans.
