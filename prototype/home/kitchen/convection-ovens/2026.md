- **Steam infusion emerging**: Our Place Wonder Oven and Tovala use steam to improve reheating and baking results. Steam-assisted cooking was previously limited to $2,000+ built-in ovens — now available under $200. [src1, src7]
- **Temperature probe integration**: KitchenAid and Ninja Prestige Pro Cook System (DT551) now include built-in temperature probes — a feature borrowed from smart grills that eliminates guesswork on roasts and proteins. [src3, src9]
- **Counter space optimization**: Roll-top doors (Hamilton Beach) and garage-style doors (GE Profile) reflect growing awareness that counter space is the main barrier to adoption. [src1, src4]

## Important Caveats

- Prices are approximate US street prices as of April 2026. Sales events (Prime Day, Black Friday, Super Bowl) drop Ninja and Cosori prices 30-45%. Ninja Prestige DT501 has been seen at $229.99 on Amazon as recently as 2026-04-18.
- Countertop convection ovens draw 1200-1800W — verify your kitchen circuit can handle the load alongside other appliances. Running a convection oven and microwave on the same circuit can trip breakers.
- "Air fry" in a countertop oven is convection cooking with high airflow, not traditional deep frying. Results are healthier but texturally different from deep-fried food.
- Smart features require WiFi and a compatible smartphone. App experiences vary widely — GE Profile's SmartHQ is mature, while budget brands may have less reliable apps.
- The Café Couture Smart Oven (~$350-400), previously recommended by Reviewed, has been discontinued as of early 2026. Avoid purchasing unless heavily discounted.
- The Ninja Prestige DT501 is reportedly louder than its peers per Tom's Guide testing — note for noise-sensitive kitchens or open floor plans.
