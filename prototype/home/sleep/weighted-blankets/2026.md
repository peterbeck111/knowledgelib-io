# Best Weighted Blankets (2026)

## Summary

The weighted blanket market in 2026 spans from $35 budget options to $350 luxury picks, with most highly rated models falling in the $120-$250 range. The **Bearaby Cotton Napper** (~$179-$199 at 10 lbs, $249 at 15 lbs) remains the consensus best overall pick across Sleep Foundation, Tom's Guide, and CNN Underscored for its unique open-knit 100% organic cotton construction that provides deep pressure stimulation without trapping heat. Unlike traditional bead-filled blankets, the Napper uses layered cotton yarn for weight, eliminating the risk of bead leakage and enabling full machine washability. As of April 2026, Sleepopolis has elevated the **Silk & Snow Hand-Knitted** to its best overall, citing its 100%-cotton filler-free design with zero glass beads or synthetic fillers. [src1, src2, src4, src8]

Weighted blankets work through deep pressure stimulation (DPS), which mimics the feeling of being held or hugged and may promote relaxation and reduce anxiety. Most sleep experts recommend choosing a blanket that weighs approximately 10% of the user's body weight. The key differentiators among 2026 models are fill type (glass microbeads vs. knitted cotton yarn), cover material (cotton, bamboo lyocell, minky polyester), cooling performance, weight distribution technology, and washability. Glass-bead blankets dominate the budget-to-mid segment ($35-$160), while knitted blanket-yarn designs from Bearaby and Silk & Snow command the premium tier ($179-$280). [src1, src3, src6]

For hot sleepers, the **Bearaby Tree Napper** (~$249) uses TENCEL lyocell from eucalyptus fibers for moisture-wicking coolness, while the **Luxome Removable Cover Weighted Blanket** (~$160) offers interchangeable bamboo lyocell and minky covers. The **Layla Weighted Blanket** MSRP moved to $199 in 2026 but is routinely on sale at $99 across Amazon and the Layla site, making it the single best deal in the mid-tier. Budget-conscious shoppers will find the **YnM Weighted Blanket** (~$40-$65) delivers solid glass-bead performance at the lowest price in this comparison with Tom's Guide naming it "best choice of weights" thanks to 9 weight options from 5 lbs to 30 lbs. For children ages 4+, the **Bearaby Nappling** (~$139 for 5-8 lbs) is a new 2026 addition — an open-knit kids' version of the Cotton Napper with no glass beads or choking hazards. [src2, src3, src7, src8]

## Best for Each Use Case

### Best Overall: Bearaby Cotton Napper (~$179-$199 starting) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/bearaby-cotton-napper)
The Bearaby Cotton Napper earns its top spot through a fundamentally different design philosophy: instead of filling a shell with glass beads, it uses layers of chunky hand-knit organic cotton yarn to create weight. This eliminates common complaints about bead clumping, shifting, and leakage. The open-knit construction allows excellent airflow, making it cooler than most traditional weighted blankets. Sleep Foundation named it the best overall weighted blanket for 2026, praising its temperature regulation and unique tactile comfort; CNN Underscored concludes "if you buy only one weighted blanket, make it Bearaby's Cotton Napper." Available in 10, 15, 20, and 25 lb options and multiple colors, it is fully machine washable. Starting price has dropped to $179 at 10 lbs, scaling to $249 at 15 lbs. [src1, src2, src4, src8]

### Best Cooling: Luxome Removable Cover Weighted Blanket (~$160) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/luxome-removable-cover-weighted-blanket)
The Luxome stands out for its interchangeable cover system — choose between a silky bamboo-derived lyocell cover for cooling or a plush minky cover for warmth. The inner blanket uses glass beads and polyester batting in baffle-box construction for even weight distribution. Sleep Foundation awarded it best cooling weighted blanket, noting its temperature-regulating bamboo lyocell wicks moisture effectively. Available in 8, 15, 18, and 25 lb options. The removable cover makes washing straightforward. [src1, src6, src7]

### Best Value: Layla Weighted Blanket (~$99 on sale / $199 MSRP) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/layla-weighted-blanket)
Sleep Foundation's budget pick and Sleepopolis's best machine-washable blanket, the Layla delivers dual-sided comfort with breathable 300-thread-count cotton on one side and cozy minky fleece on the other. Glass microbeads in baffle-box quilting provide even pressure distribution. Layla raised MSRP to $199 in 2026 but runs aggressive $99 sale pricing on Amazon, the Layla site, and via a 30-day sleep trial with a 5-year warranty — the single best mid-tier deal when you catch the sale. Available in 15, 20, and 25 lb weights. [src1, src2, src3]

### Best Budget / Widest Weight Range: YnM Weighted Blanket (~$40-$65) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/ynm-weighted-blanket)
The YnM is the most affordable weighted blanket in this comparison and one of Amazon's best sellers with tens of thousands of reviews. Tom's Guide names it "best choice of weights" thanks to nine weight options from 5 lbs all the way to 30 lbs across 22 color choices — unmatched selection in the category. Its 7-layer system uses small 2x2-inch compartments to keep glass beads evenly distributed. Machine washable with OEKO-TEX certified 100% cotton (bamboo cooling variant also available). The trade-off is a thinner, less luxurious feel compared to premium options. [src2, src3, src5]

### Best for Hot Sleepers: Bearaby Tree Napper (~$249) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/bearaby-tree-napper)
The Tree Napper uses Fairtrade-certified TENCEL lyocell derived from sustainably harvested eucalyptus trees, blended with organic cotton (52/48 split). TENCEL is naturally cool to the touch and wicks moisture away from the body, making it the top choice for sleepers who overheat. Like its Cotton Napper sibling, it uses a bead-free chunky knit construction with an open-loop weave that promotes airflow. Sleep Foundation specifically recommends it for hot sleepers seeking pressure therapy. [src1, src4, src7]

### Best for Couples: Gravity Weighted Blanket (~$250) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/gravity-weighted-blanket)
The original weighted blanket brand (launched via Kickstarter in 2017), Gravity offers a 35 lb king-size option specifically designed for sharing. The inner weighted piece contains fine-grade glass beads in gridded stitching for uniform distribution, enclosed in a removable micro-plush duvet cover. While most weighted blankets are designed for individual use, the Gravity king-size drapes over both sides of a queen or king bed. The 15 lb and 20 lb individual sizes also remain popular for solo use. [src3, src4, src6]

### Best Knitted / Sleepopolis Best Overall: Silk & Snow Hand-Knitted Weighted Blanket (~$245) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/silk-and-snow-knitted-weighted-blanket)
Competing directly with the Bearaby Cotton Napper, the Silk & Snow uses 100% cotton yarn in a hand-knitted open-weave design with no synthetic beads or pellets. Sleep Foundation named it the most comfortable weighted blanket, calling it "wondrously soft against your skin" with superior thermoregulation. Sleepopolis's April 2026 update elevated it to best overall, specifically citing the filler-free construction: "no glass beads or synthetic fillers, reducing noise and cold spots." It offers the widest weight range among knitted blankets (8-25 lbs), including a dedicated kids' size and travel size. A 100-night return window provides risk-free trialing. [src1, src2, src6]

### Best for Kids Age 4+: Bearaby Nappling (~$139) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/bearaby-nappling-kids)
Sleep Foundation's best-for-kids pick, the Nappling is a scaled-down open-knit version of the Cotton Napper made from OEKO-TEX certified organic cotton with no glass beads or plastic pellets — eliminating choking-hazard concerns that exclude bead-filled blankets for young children. Available in 5, 6, and 8 lb options across six color choices, sized 36"x48" (5 lb), 36"x60" (6 lb), and 40"x60" (8 lb). Machine washable. Follow the 10%-of-body-weight rule and only use for children age 4+ who can remove the blanket independently. [src1, src8]

## Important Caveats

- Prices are approximate US street prices as of April 2026. Sales, bundles, and seasonal promotions can reduce prices 20-60%, particularly during holiday periods — the Gravity Cooling was 62% off on a recent Tom's Guide roundup, and Layla is routinely $99 despite a $199 MSRP.
- The 10% body weight rule is a general guideline, not a medical prescription. Some sleepers prefer lighter or heavier blankets. Consult a physician before using a weighted blanket if you have respiratory conditions, circulatory issues, or mobility limitations.
- Weighted blankets are not recommended for children under 2 years old or for anyone who cannot remove the blanket independently. Children's weighted blankets (5-7 lbs) are designed for ages 4+.
- Knitted blankets (Bearaby, Silk & Snow) may snag on jewelry, pet claws, or rough surfaces. They also tend to stretch slightly over time.
- Machine washability varies. Most glass-bead blankets up to 15 lbs can be machine washed at home; heavier blankets (20+ lbs) may require commercial washers or spot cleaning.
- Weighted blankets do not replace medical treatment for anxiety, insomnia, or other conditions. Scientific evidence for therapeutic benefits is promising but limited in scope.
