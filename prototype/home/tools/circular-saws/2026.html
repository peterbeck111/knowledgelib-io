Summary

The circular saw market in April 2026 is dominated by cordless models that now match or exceed corded performance, thanks to brushless motors and high-voltage battery platforms. Pro Tool Reviews' April 2026 head-to-head named the Milwaukee 2732-20 M18 FUEL (~$199 bare) the best overall cordless saw — when paired with a Forge battery it topped the rip-cut test and aced the full-depth cutting test (blade buried in three sheets of 3/4" OSB with a 20-lb pull weight). The DeWalt DCS578 FlexVolt 60V MAX (~$170 bare) was named most powerful, recording the fastest full-depth time (7.7 seconds) with up to 47% more power than the older DCS575. The Skil PWRCore 20 XP CR5440 (~$169 kit) was named best value, delivering 5,300 RPM at 7.3 lbs in a kit that includes battery and charger. [src2, src6]

The Makita XSH06PT 36V LXT remains the top dual-battery option, delivering 6,000 RPM from its dual-18V system with a 2-5/8-inch cut depth at 90 degrees and a 56-degree bevel — powerful enough for professional framing and precise enough for finish work. [src1, src4] For corded duty, the Skilsaw SPT77WML-01 worm drive (~$199) is TechGearLab's top pro pick — its lightweight magnesium construction (11.6 lbs) and Dual-Field motor handle ripping and metal cuts that few sidewinders match. [src4]

For budget-conscious buyers, the SKIL 5280-01 remains unbeatable at around $65-70, offering 15 amps, 5,300 RPM, and a laser guide in a sub-9-pound corded package. [src1, src5] For tight overhead and trim work, the Milwaukee 2521-20 M12 FUEL 5-3/8" (~$159 bare) is now the most compact pick — 4.7 lbs, 3,850 RPM, and up to 190 cuts in 2x4 from an M12 HO 5.0Ah battery. [src6]

Best for Each Use Case

Best Overall Cordless: Milwaukee 2732-20 M18 FUEL (~$199 bare) — Check price

Pro Tool Reviews' April 2026 head-to-head crowned the 2732 the best overall cordless saw — when paired with a Forge battery, it topped the rip-cut test and aced the full-depth cutting test (blade buried in three sheets of 3/4" OSB plus a 20-lb pull weight). 5,800 RPM POWERSTATE brushless motor, 2-9/16" cut depth at 90°, magnesium guards/shoe, and up to 750 crosscuts per charge. Pricing has stabilized around $199 bare in 2026. [src2, src6]

Most Powerful Cordless (NEW PTR 2026): DeWalt DCS578B FlexVolt 60V MAX (~$170 bare) — Check price

PTR's 2026 testing recorded the DCS578 as the most powerful saw in the test, finishing the full-depth cut in just 7.7 seconds — fastest in the field. Up to 47% more power than the older DCS575 (2,456 unit watts out), 5,800 RPM, 2-9/16" cut depth, 57° bevel, and an 8.3-lb bare-tool weight that undercuts most cordless competitors. The runner-up cordless pick across multiple 2026 review sites and an exceptional value at $170 bare for users already on the DeWalt 60V FlexVolt platform. [src6, src7]

Best Value Cordless (NEW PTR 2026): Skil PWRCore 20 XP CR5440B (~$169 kit) — Check price

Pro Tool Reviews named the PWRCore 20 XP "best for the money" in their April 2026 cordless head-to-head — and uniquely, the $169 price is a kit that includes a 4.0Ah battery and PWRJump charger (most competitors at this performance tier sell bare-tool only). 5,300 RPM, 2-5/8" cut depth, 7.3 lbs, brushless motor, electric brake. The single best cordless saw entry point in 2026 if you are not already locked into a battery platform. [src6]

Best Dual-Battery: Makita XSH06PT 36V LXT (~$350 kit) — Check price

Two 18V LXT batteries in series deliver 36V of brushless power at 6,000 RPM (the highest cordless RPM here) with 2-5/8" cut depth and 56° bevel. Automatic Speed Change adjusts torque under load. The kit price (~$350) bundles two batteries and a dual-port charger, making it the easiest "everything included" entry into Makita's pro lineup. [src1, src4]

Best Pro Worm Drive (NEW): Skilsaw SPT77WML-01 (~$199) — Check price

TechGearLab's top corded pick for 2026 and the lightest worm drive saw on the market at 11.6 lbs. The Dual-Field magnesium-housed motor was engineered specifically for saws (not adapted from a drill platform), delivering 5,300 RPM with the torque profile and blade-left orientation that West-Coast framers prefer. 53° bevel, integrated rafter hook, oil-lubricated worm gears for long service life. The pro standard for ripping and dense material. [src4]

Most Compact (NEW): Milwaukee 2521-20 M12 FUEL 5-3/8" (~$159 bare) — Check price

Pro Tool Reviews' "most compact" pick at just 4.7 lbs and small enough for one-handed overhead, ladder, and tight-space work where a full-size 7-1/4" saw is overkill. POWERSTATE brushless motor, 3,850 RPM, 1-3/4" cut depth (still cuts dimensional 2x material in a single pass), and up to 190 cuts in 2x4 from an M12 HO 5.0Ah battery. The right tool for installers, electricians, and trim carpenters. [src6]

Best Budget: SKIL 5280-01 (~$65-70) — Check price

At roughly $65-70, the SKIL 5280-01 remains the best value in circular saws, delivering 15-amp corded power with 5,300 RPM, a single-beam laser guide, and a dust blower at 8.7 pounds. Multiple review sites continue to rank it the top budget pick for DIYers in 2026. [src1, src4, src5]

Best for DeWalt 20V Users: DeWalt DCS573B (~$189 bare) — Check price

The DCS573B leverages FLEXVOLT Advantage technology to deliver up to 77% more power when paired with a DCB606 FLEXVOLT battery. 5,500 RPM, 57° bevel (widest in this roundup), bright LED work light, electric brake. The right pick if you already own DeWalt 20V batteries and want a sidewinder rather than the FlexVolt-native DCS578. [src1, src3]

Best Lightweight Cordless: Metabo HPT C3607DA MultiVolt (~$199 bare) — Check price

At 7.5 lbs bare (9.7 lbs with battery), the Metabo HPT MultiVolt is among the lightest full-size cordless saws. It uniquely offers an optional AC adapter for corded operation when unlimited runtime is needed. Silent Mode drops RPM to 2,000 for indoor work. [src2, src6]

Best Budget Cordless: Ryobi PBLCS300B ONE+ HP (~$129 bare) — Check price

40% faster cutting than previous Ryobi models, over 325 crosscuts per charge, 7.0 lbs bare (lightest full-size in this list). 56° bevel with detents at 0, 15, 22.5, 30, 45, and 56°. Best entry into a wide battery ecosystem. [src3, src6]

Best Corded Sidewinder: Makita 5007MG (~$165) — Check price

The gold standard for corded sidewinders, with a 15A motor delivering 5,800 RPM through a magnesium body at 10.6 lbs. Dual LED lights illuminate the cut line; 56° bevel with stops at 22.5° and 45°. The included hard case and 24T carbide blade make it ready out of the box. [src2, src3]

Best DeWalt Worm Drive: DeWalt DWS535B (~$249) — Check price

4,800 RPM with maximum torque for cutting dense or wet lumber. 15A motor, magnesium shoe, electric brake, extra-wide saw hook for materials up to 2-3/4 inches. The DeWalt-platform pro alternative to the Skilsaw SPT77WML-01. [src1, src2]

Decision Logic

If budget < $100 and corded is acceptable

→ The SKIL 5280-01 (~$65-70) is the clear choice — 15-amp, 5,300 RPM, laser guide, 8.7 lbs. Below $100 there is no cordless saw that approaches the cut performance of a 15A corded sidewinder. [src1, src4, src5]

If buying first cordless saw and not yet on a battery platform

→ Skil PWRCore 20 XP CR5440B kit (~$169) is the best 2026 entry point. Pro Tool Reviews "Best for the Money" pick, and the $169 price is a kit (battery + charger included), not bare tool — ~$100 of saved entry cost vs every other premium cordless option. [src6]

If primary use is professional framing/construction

→ Prioritize power and cut depth. The DeWalt DCS578 FlexVolt (~$170 bare, fastest full-depth cut in PTR's 2026 test) or Milwaukee 2732-20 (~$199 bare, top overall) deliver corded-equivalent power. For worm-drive preference (common in western US), the Skilsaw SPT77WML-01 (~$199) is the lightest worm drive on the market at 11.6 lbs. [src2, src4, src6]

If user is already invested in a battery platform

→ Stay within the ecosystem: DeWalt 60V FlexVolt → DCS578B; DeWalt 20V MAX → DCS573B; Milwaukee M18 → 2732-20; Makita 18V LXT → XSH06PT; Ryobi ONE+ → PBLCS300B; Skil PWRCore 20 → CR5440B. Battery compatibility saves $100-$200 per tool. [src2, src6]

If primary use is overhead, ladder, or tight-space work

→ Milwaukee 2521-20 M12 FUEL 5-3/8" (~$159 bare, 4.7 lbs). PTR's "most compact" pick — small and light enough for one-handed cuts where a full 7-1/4" saw is unwieldy. Cuts dimensional 2x material in a single pass. [src6]

If weight is the top priority for full-size 7-1/4" cuts

→ The Ryobi PBLCS300B (7.0 lbs bare), Skil PWRCore 20 XP (7.3 lbs), or Metabo HPT C3607DA (7.5 lbs bare) are the lightest full-size cordless options. For corded, the SKIL 5280-01 at 8.7 lbs is the lightest 15-amp sidewinder. [src1, src3, src6]

If user needs unlimited runtime (all-day cutting)

→ A corded saw eliminates battery swaps. The Makita 5007MG (~$165) is the best corded sidewinder, the Skilsaw SPT77WML-01 (~$199) is the best worm drive, or the Metabo HPT C3607DA can operate corded via its optional AC adapter for hybrid flexibility. [src2, src4, src6]

If cuts are mostly metal/composite or thick lumber (4x material)

→ The Skilsaw SPT77WML-01 worm drive (corded) handles metal cuts that sidewinders struggle with. For thick lumber needs (4x in a single pass), the upcoming Milwaukee M18 Fuel 10-1/4" rear-handle (June 2026 release, 3-13/16" cut depth at 90°) is the cordless answer. [src4, src7]

Default recommendation

→ For most users balancing power, portability, and value in 2026, the Milwaukee 2732-20 (~$199 bare) or DeWalt DCS578B (~$170 bare) are the safest picks — both top PTR's 2026 testing in their categories. If platform-agnostic, the Skil PWRCore 20 XP CR5440B kit (~$169) is the lowest-friction entry. [src6]

  • 2026 cordless head-to-head reshuffle: Pro Tool Reviews' April 2026 testing crowned the Milwaukee 2732 best overall, the DeWalt DCS578 most powerful (fastest full-depth cut at 7.7 sec, +47% power vs the older DCS575), and the Skil PWRCore 20 XP best value.   • Silent and low-noise modes: The Metabo HPT C3607DA pioneered a 2,000 RPM Silent Mode, and other manufacturers are adding variable-speed settings for noise-restricted environments. [src2]
  • Kit pricing pressure: Skil PWRCore 20 XP at $169 with battery and charger is now competitive with most premium bare-tool prices ($170-$199), forcing competitors to bundle or discount. Expect more kit-price compression through 2026. [src6]

Important Caveats

  • Prices listed are approximate US retail as of April 2026 and vary by retailer, region, and promotions. Tool-only (bare) prices exclude batteries and chargers — except where explicitly noted as kit pricing (Skil PWRCore 20 XP, Makita XSH06PT).
  • Cordless saw performance depends heavily on battery capacity and chemistry — Pro Tool Reviews' 2026 results were measured with high-output batteries (Milwaukee Forge, DeWalt FlexVolt). Economy 2.0Ah batteries significantly reduce runtime and power.
  • Cut depth and bevel specs are manufacturer-stated maximums; real-world performance varies with blade thickness, material density, and battery charge level.
  • This comparison covers standard 7-1/4" sidewinder, 7-1/4" worm drive, and one compact 5-3/8" saw (Milwaukee M12 FUEL). 6-1/2" cordless and 10-1/4" rear-handle saws (including the upcoming Milwaukee M18 Fuel 10-1/4") are noted but not directly cross-compared here. Track saws are a separate category — see skip conditions.
  • Individual saw preference is personal — blade orientation, trigger comfort, and balance vary between brands. Hands-on testing is recommended before committing to a platform.
