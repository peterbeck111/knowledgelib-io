# Best Circular Saws (2026)

## Summary

The circular saw market in April 2026 is dominated by cordless models that now match or exceed corded performance, thanks to brushless motors and high-voltage battery platforms. Pro Tool Reviews' April 2026 head-to-head named the **Milwaukee 2732-20 M18 FUEL** (~$199 bare) the best overall cordless saw — when paired with a Forge battery it topped the rip-cut test and aced the full-depth cutting test (blade buried in three sheets of 3/4" OSB with a 20-lb pull weight). The **DeWalt DCS578 FlexVolt 60V MAX** (~$170 bare) was named most powerful, recording the fastest full-depth time (7.7 seconds) with up to 47% more power than the older DCS575. The **Skil PWRCore 20 XP CR5440** (~$169 kit) was named best value, delivering 5,300 RPM at 7.26 lbs in a kit that includes battery and charger. [src2, src6]

The Makita XSH06PT 36V LXT remains the top dual-battery option, delivering 6,000 RPM from its dual-18V system with a 2-5/8-inch cut depth at 90 degrees and a 56-degree bevel — powerful enough for professional framing and precise enough for finish work. [src1, src4] For corded duty, the **Skilsaw SPT77WML-01 worm drive** (~$199) is TechGearLab's top pro pick — its lightweight magnesium construction (11.6 lbs) and Dual-Field motor handle ripping and metal cuts that few sidewinders match. [src4]

For budget-conscious buyers, the SKIL 5280-01 remains unbeatable at around $65-70, offering 15 amps, 5,300 RPM, and a laser guide in a sub-9-pound corded package. [src1, src5] For tight overhead and trim work, the **Milwaukee 2521-20 M12 FUEL 5-3/8"** (~$159 bare) is now the most compact pick — 4.7 lbs, 3,850 RPM, and up to 190 cuts in 2x4 from an M12 HO 5.0Ah battery. [src6] Key price points range from $65 for entry-level corded saws to $350+ for premium cordless kits. Cordless tool-only prices cluster between $129 and $299, with batteries and chargers adding $100-$200. Blade-left orientation (Bosch, Metabo HPT) provides better cut-line visibility for right-handed users, while blade-right (DeWalt, Milwaukee, Makita) is the industry standard. [src1, src2, src6]

### If budget < $100 and corded is acceptable
--> The SKIL 5280-01 (~$65-70) is the clear choice — 15-amp, 5,300 RPM, laser guide, 8.7 lbs. Below $100 there is no cordless saw that approaches the cut performance of a 15A corded sidewinder. [src1, src4, src5]

### If buying first cordless saw and not yet on a battery platform
--> **Skil PWRCore 20 XP CR5440B kit (~$169)** is the best 2026 entry point. Pro Tool Reviews "Best for the Money" pick, and the $169 price is a kit (battery + charger included), not bare tool — ~$100 of saved entry cost vs every other premium cordless option. [src6]

### If primary use is professional framing/construction
--> Prioritize power and cut depth. The DeWalt DCS578 FlexVolt (~$170 bare, fastest full-depth cut in PTR's 2026 test) or Milwaukee 2732-20 (~$199 bare, top overall) deliver corded-equivalent power. For worm-drive preference (common in western US), the Skilsaw SPT77WML-01 (~$199) is the lightest worm drive on the market at 11.6 lbs. [src2, src4, src6]

### If user is already invested in a battery platform
--> Stay within the ecosystem to avoid buying redundant batteries: DeWalt 60V FlexVolt --> DCS578B; DeWalt 20V MAX --> DCS573B; Milwaukee M18 --> 2732-20; Makita 18V LXT --> XSH06PT; Ryobi ONE+ --> PBLCS300B; Skil PWRCore 20 --> CR5440B. Battery compatibility saves $100-$200 per tool. [src2, src6]

### If primary use is overhead, ladder, or tight-space work
--> **Milwaukee 2521-20 M12 FUEL 5-3/8"** (~$159 bare, 4.7 lbs). PTR's "most compact" pick — small and light enough for one-handed cuts where a full 7-1/4" saw is unwieldy. Cuts dimensional 2x material in a single pass. [src6]

### If weight is the top priority for full-size 7-1/4" cuts
--> The Ryobi PBLCS300B (7.0 lbs bare), Skil PWRCore 20 XP (7.3 lbs), or Metabo HPT C3607DA (7.5 lbs bare) are the lightest full-size cordless options. For corded, the SKIL 5280-01 at 8.7 lbs is the lightest 15-amp sidewinder. [src1, src3, src6]

### If user needs unlimited runtime (all-day cutting, station-based work)
--> A corded saw eliminates battery swaps. The Makita 5007MG (~$165) is the best corded sidewinder, the Skilsaw SPT77WML-01 (~$199) is the best worm drive, or the Metabo HPT C3607DA can operate corded via its optional AC adapter for hybrid flexibility. [src2, src4, src6]

### If cuts are mostly metal/composite or thick lumber (4x material)
--> The Skilsaw SPT77WML-01 worm drive (corded) handles metal cuts that sidewinders struggle with. For thick lumber needs (4x in a single pass), the upcoming **Milwaukee M18 Fuel 10-1/4" rear-handle** (June 2026 release, 3-13/16" cut depth at 90°) is the cordless answer. [src4, src7]

### Default recommendation
--> For most users balancing power, portability, and value in 2026, the **Milwaukee 2732-20** (~$199 bare) or **DeWalt DCS578B** (~$170 bare) are the safest picks — both top PTR's 2026 testing in their categories. If platform-agnostic, the **Skil PWRCore 20 XP CR5440B kit** (~$169) is the lowest-friction entry. [src6]
