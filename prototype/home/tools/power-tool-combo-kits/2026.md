## Decision Logic

### If budget < $250
--> Choose the Skil PWRCORE 20 Brushless 6-Tool Combo Kit (CB7443-21) at ~$220 for six brushless tools with USB-C charging. If you specifically need an impact driver (which Skil omits), look for the Ryobi ONE+ HP 6-Tool Kit (PSBCK06K) at ~$379 during sales, or the Craftsman V20 7-Tool Kit (CMCK700D2) at ~$299 with brushed motors but includes both drill and impact driver. [src1, src6]

### If budget $250-$400 and need maximum tools
--> Choose the Craftsman V20 7-Tool Combo Kit (CMCK700D2) at ~$299 for seven tools covering drilling, fastening, sawing, grinding, and lighting. For all-brushless performance, step up to the Ryobi ONE+ HP 6-Tool Kit (PSBCK06K) at ~$379 -- it offers a larger battery ecosystem (280+ tools) for long-term expansion and compact brushless motors. [src2, src6]

### If budget $400-$700 and need a balanced all-rounder
--> The Makita 18V LXT 6-Piece Combo Kit (XT612M) at ~$549 offers six all-brushless tools with the fastest charging in the category (40 minutes to full). The Flex 24V 6-Tool Kit (FXM601-2B) at ~$649 delivers 20% more power from its higher-voltage platform with a Limited Lifetime Warranty. The Ridgid 18V 6-Tool Kit (R96263) at ~$649 offers compact brushless tools with a Lifetime Service Agreement. [src2, src4]

### If primary use is professional contracting or heavy daily use
--> Prioritize the Milwaukee M18 FUEL 5-Tool (3697-25) or DeWalt 20V MAX XR 6-Tool (DCK695P2). Both deliver all-brushless performance with 5.0Ah batteries. Choose Milwaukee for maximum torque, longer warranty (5 years), and the AutoStop safety feature. Choose DeWalt for six tools including an angle grinder, excellent ergonomics, and 3-speed hammer drill transmission. [src1, src2]

### If user already owns tools on a specific battery platform
