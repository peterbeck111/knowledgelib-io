## Decision Logic

### If budget < $300
--> Go with the Roborock Q7 M5+ (~$250) for the best self-emptying vacuum with LiDAR, or the Dreame L10s Ultra Gen 2 (~$300) if you can stretch slightly for a full all-in-one dock with active mopping. The Ecovacs Deebot N20 Plus (~$280) offers the longest runtime at 300 minutes with bagless self-emptying. [src1, src2]

### If primary use is mopping
--> The Yeedi M14 Plus (~$449) with its OZMO Roller system is the clear leader for mopping under $500. The Ecovacs Deebot T80 Omni (~$499) is the runner-up with its own OZMO Roller at 3,700Pa pressure. Avoid passive mop pads on budget models (N20 Plus, Q7 M5+) for serious mopping -- they are ineffective on dried stains. [src6, src1]

### If user has pets that shed heavily
--> The MOVA P10 Pro Ultra Gen 2 (~$499) leads with 26,000Pa suction and 212 deg F hot-water dock. For a lower budget, the original MOVA P10 Pro Ultra (~$399) with 13,000Pa suction, only 4% hair tangle rate, and 4.33/5 pet performance score is excellent. Both feature RGB camera obstacle avoidance to identify and route around pet waste. [src3, src1]

### If home has mostly carpet
--> Prioritize suction power over mopping features. The MOVA P10 Pro Ultra Gen 2 (26,000Pa), Dreame L40 Ultra Gen 2 (25,000Pa), or MOVA P50 Pro Ultra (19,000Pa) deliver the best deep-cleaning. All three auto-lift mops when carpet is detected. [src1, src4]

### If home is large (2000+ sqft)
--> Battery life becomes critical. The Ecovacs Deebot N20 Plus (300 min), Yeedi M14 Plus (241 min), Dreame L10s Ultra Gen 2 (240 min), and MOVA S20 Ultra (210 min) offer the best runtime. Models with under 180 minutes may require multiple charging sessions. [src1, src5]

### If user wants stable pricing and reliable support
--> The MOVA P10 Pro Ultra (~$399) offers the most consistent pricing in the segment. Many competitors' prices swing 30-50% between sale and non-sale periods, making it hard to know the "real" price. The MOVA P10 Pro Ultra stays near $399 consistently. [src3, src1]

### Default recommendation
--> The Dreame L40 Ultra Gen 2 (~$400) is the safest pick for unknown requirements at its current price point. It balances the highest suction in its class (25,000Pa), effective mopping with extendable mop arm, reliable obstacle avoidance, and a comprehensive all-in-one dock. ## Key Market Trends (2026)

- **Former flagships continue flooding the sub-$500 range**: The Dreame L40 Ultra Gen 2 (MSRP $650) hit ~$400 in April, the Ecovacs T80 Omni (MSRP $1,000) sits at ~$499, and the MOVA P50 Pro Ultra (MSRP $999) is available at ~$494. Features that cost $1,000+ in 2024 are now standard at half the price. [src1, src4]
- **MOVA P10 Pro Ultra overtakes Yeedi M14 Plus as budget champion**: Vacuum Wars switched their budget pick from the Yeedi M14 Plus to the MOVA P10 Pro Ultra for April 2026, citing more stable pricing, strong test performance (3.53 overall, 4.39 navigation, 4.33 pet), and consistently positive user feedback. The Yeedi M14 Plus remains the mopping champion. [src3, src1]
- **Suction power race stabilizes above 25,000Pa**: The MOVA P10 Pro Ultra Gen 2 hit 26,000Pa at $499, while the Dreame L40 Ultra Gen 2 delivers 25,000Pa at ~$400. Real-world gains diminish above 10,000Pa on hard floors but remain meaningful on carpet. Independent airflow testing (CFM/kPa) provides more reliable cross-brand comparisons than Pa ratings. [src1, src3]
- **iRobot under Picea ownership remains quiet**: iRobot completed its sale to Shenzhen Picea Robotics on January 23, 2026. Three months in, no new competitive products have been announced for the sub-$500 tier. The Roomba Plus 405 at $399 (down from $799) is the most notable offering but does not match Chinese competitors on features. Most review outlets continue to advise caution. [src8, src5]
- **Self-emptying with hot-water mop washing is the new baseline**: Nearly every robot vacuum above $350 now includes a self-emptying dock with hot-water mop washing and drying. Budget models at $250-300 include self-emptying but lack active mopping docks. The meaningful performance gap is now between roller-mop robots (Yeedi OZMO, Ecovacs OZMO) and spinning-mop robots (Dreame, MOVA). [src1, src5]

## Important Caveats

- Prices are approximate US street/sale prices as of April 2026. MSRPs are often 40-60% higher, and pricing fluctuates with promotions and regional availability. International pricing varies significantly. Post-Big Spring Sale prices have stabilized somewhat but remain volatile.
- Suction power in Pascals (Pa) is a manufacturer-reported metric that lacks standardized testing methodology across brands. Vacuum Wars' independent airflow measurements (in CFM/kPa) provide more reliable cross-brand comparisons. The MOVA P50 Pro Ultra measured only 0.71 kPa despite claiming 19,000Pa.
- iRobot completed its sale to Picea Robotics on January 23, 2026. While the company claims continued US operations and data protection via iRobot Safe subsidiary, the long-term product roadmap and software support remain uncertain under new ownership.
- Robot vacuums with mopping capabilities are supplemental cleaning tools. Even the best roller-mop robots struggle with dried or ground-in stains compared to manual mopping or dedicated mop robots.
- The MOVA P10 Pro Ultra Gen 2 ($499-$599) and Ecovacs Deebot T80 Omni ($499 on sale) sit at the boundary of this price tier and may exceed $500 outside of promotional periods.
