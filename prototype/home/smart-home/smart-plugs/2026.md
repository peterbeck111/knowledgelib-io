# Best Smart Plugs (2026)

## Summary

The smart plug market in 2026 has matured significantly, with Matter protocol support now standard across most top picks, enabling cross-platform compatibility between Apple Home, Amazon Alexa, Google Home, and Samsung SmartThings. Prices range from $8 to $40 per plug, with the best options falling in the $10-$25 range. The TP-Link Kasa KP125M (~$11/plug) is the best overall smart plug — the consensus pick across Engadget, Wirecutter, Reviewed, and CNN Underscored — combining Matter certification, energy monitoring, and a compact form factor that does not block adjacent outlets. PCWorld's 2026 top pick is the Leviton Decora D215P 2nd Gen (~$36), which added Matter via firmware update and is the best choice for HomeKit-first households that also want cross-platform flexibility. [src1, src2, src3, src4, src5]

For budget buyers, the TP-Link Tapo P125M (~$10) delivers Matter compatibility in one of the smallest form factors available, though it lacks energy monitoring. The Eve Energy (~$35) stands out as the best option for Apple HomeKit users, offering Matter over Thread connectivity for faster response times and detailed energy tracking without any cloud dependency. Energy monitoring has moved from a premium feature to an expected one, with the Emporia Smart Plug (~$12) offering the most detailed per-device power analytics at the lowest price point. For outdoor use, Tom's Guide's March 2026 update confirms the TP-Link Tapo P400M (~$22-$26) remains the best overall outdoor pick, with IP65, dual-outlet independent control, and 300-foot Wi-Fi range. [src2, src3, src4, src8]

## Best for Each Use Case

### Best Overall: TP-Link Kasa KP125M (~$11/plug) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/kasa-kp125m)
The Kasa KP125M is the first "no compromise" smart plug, combining Matter certification with energy monitoring in a slim design that does not block adjacent outlets. It works instantly with Apple Home, Alexa, Google Home, and Samsung SmartThings. The Kasa app provides real-time and historical power consumption data, including estimated electricity costs based on your utility rate. Sold in 2-packs (~$22) and 4-packs (~$40), it offers excellent per-unit value. Note that energy monitoring data is only available through the Kasa app, not through Matter-connected platforms. [src1, src2, src4]

### Best Budget: TP-Link Tapo P125M (~$10) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/tapo-p125m)
One of the smallest Matter-certified smart plugs on the market, the Tapo P125M is compact enough to stack two in a single duplex outlet without blocking either socket. At ~$10 per plug (or as low as $7 in 3-packs), it is one of the most affordable ways into the Matter ecosystem. Setup is hub-free via Bluetooth and a Matter QR code. It handles up to 15A/1800W loads, suitable for lamps, coffee makers, fans, and other household appliances. It lacks energy monitoring, which is its main trade-off at this price. [src2, src3, src6]

### Best for Energy Monitoring: Emporia Smart Plug (~$12) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/emporia-smart-plug)
The Emporia Smart Plug offers the most detailed per-device energy monitoring at the lowest price point. It tracks real-time power draw, daily/weekly/monthly consumption, and estimated costs through the Emporia app. Ideal for identifying energy-hungry appliances like space heaters, dehumidifiers, and old refrigerators. It integrates with the Emporia Vue whole-home energy monitor for comprehensive tracking. The 10A continuous load limit (15A peak) is lower than most competitors, making it unsuitable for high-draw appliances above 1,200W. [src3, src4]

### Best for Apple HomeKit: Eve Energy (Matter) (~$35) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/eve-energy-matter)
The Eve Energy was the first smart plug to adopt Thread technology, providing lightning-fast response times and reliable operation without relying on Wi-Fi or cloud services. With Matter certification, it now works across all major platforms, though it remains the best choice for Apple-centric homes where Thread border routers (HomePod, Apple TV 4K) are already present. Its energy tracking provides detailed cost projections, and Eve's 100% privacy commitment means no cloud registration, no tracking, and all communication stays local. Requires a Thread border router (HomePod mini, Apple TV 4K, or equivalent). [src2, src4, src5]

### Best Outdoor: TP-Link Tapo P400M (~$22-$26) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/tapo-p400m)
Tom's Guide's March 2026 refresh confirms the Tapo P400M as the best overall outdoor smart plug. It offers Matter support, two independently controllable 15A outlets, and IP65 weather resistance rated for -4 to 122 degrees Fahrenheit. Its dual-antenna Wi-Fi delivers a 300-foot range, ensuring reliable connectivity for garden lights, holiday displays, and patio equipment placed far from the router. Comprehensive scheduling (including sunset/sunrise automations) is handled natively in the Tapo app. It undercuts most outdoor smart plugs while offering Matter compatibility that most competitors lack. [src3, src7, src8]

### Best Multi-Pack Value: TP-Link Kasa EP25 (~$13/plug in 4-packs) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/kasa-ep25)
The Kasa EP25 delivers fuss-free, reliable performance at ~$13 per plug in 4-packs (~$52). It supports Apple HomeKit, Alexa, Google Home, and IFTTT, with energy monitoring through the Kasa app. Its compact design does not block adjacent outlets. While it lacks Matter certification (unlike the newer KP125M), it remains one of the most reliable and widely recommended smart plugs, with Wirecutter and Engadget both naming it a top pick for bulk smart plug deployments. [src1, src4]

### Best for Matter/Thread: Eve Energy (Matter) (~$35) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/eve-energy-matter)
For homes already invested in Thread mesh networking, the Eve Energy operates over Matter via Thread rather than Wi-Fi, reducing network congestion and improving responsiveness. Each Eve Energy plug also extends your Thread mesh network's range. Combined with its energy monitoring, local-only processing, and TUV/UL certification, it represents the most future-proof smart plug available, though at a premium price. [src2, src4, src5]

## Key Market Trends (2026)

- **Matter protocol adoption**: Matter has become the dominant smart home interoperability standard. Most new smart plugs now ship with Matter certification, enabling seamless cross-platform control between Apple Home, Alexa, Google Home, and SmartThings without brand-specific apps. [src2, src3, src4]
- **Matter via firmware retrofits**: The Leviton Decora D215P 2nd-Gen added Matter through a firmware update, showing that established premium plugs are now gaining Matter without hardware revision — a signal that Matter is becoming a baseline feature rather than a differentiator. [src3]
- **Energy monitoring as baseline**: Energy monitoring, once a premium feature found only in $30+ plugs, is now available in sub-$15 models like the Emporia and Kasa KP125M. Rising electricity costs have made per-device energy tracking a key purchasing factor. [src3, src4]
- **Thread mesh networking**: Thread-enabled plugs like the Eve Energy are gaining traction, particularly in Apple HomeKit households. Thread offers lower latency, lower power consumption, and self-healing mesh networking compared to Wi-Fi smart plugs. [src2, src5]
- **Outdoor Matter plugs consolidating**: The Tapo P400M has become the default outdoor Matter smart plug in every major 2026 roundup (PCWorld, Tom's Guide March refresh, CNN Underscored), routinely selling for $21.99-$25.99 — significantly cheaper than Ring Outdoor Smart Plug ($30) and Lutron Caséta outdoor options. [src3, src7, src8]
- **Price compression**: Average smart plug prices have dropped 30-40% since 2023, with basic Matter-certified models now available under $10. The Tapo P125M at ~$7 in multi-packs represents a new price floor for Matter devices. [src2, src6]

## Important Caveats

- Prices are approximate US street prices as of April 2026. Multi-pack pricing often reduces per-unit costs significantly (e.g., Kasa KP125M at ~$40 for 4-pack vs ~$22 for 2-pack).
- Smart plugs are rated for resistive loads (lamps, heaters, coffee makers). Motor-driven appliances (air conditioners, large fans) may draw startup surges exceeding the plug's rated amperage. Check the appliance's power draw before connecting.
- Matter energy monitoring data is not yet standardized across platforms. Most plugs with energy monitoring expose that data only through their proprietary app, not through HomeKit, Alexa, or Google Home.
- Thread-based plugs (Eve Energy) require a Thread border router (Apple HomePod, Apple TV 4K, Google Nest Hub Max, or Samsung SmartThings Station) to function. Without one, they will not connect.
- All smart plugs listed require 2.4 GHz Wi-Fi networks (except Thread-only models). They will not connect to 5 GHz bands.
