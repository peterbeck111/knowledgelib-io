Summary

The bidet market in 2026 spans three main categories: electric bidet seats with heated water and air dryers, non-electric bidet attachments that fit under existing seats, and non-electric bidet seats that replace the entire toilet seat. The biggest development this year is TOTO completing its transition to an all-tankless S-series lineup: the new S2 (~$499) replaces the entry-level C2 with tankless instant heating, the S5 (~$650) replaces the mid-range C5, and the flagship S7A (~$1,300) remains the industry's slimmest at 4.1 inches. The C5 (~$430) is being discontinued, though remaining stock is still available. [src1, src6, src8]

The Alpha JX2 (~$389) retains BidetKing's #1 overall ranking and CNN Underscored's Best Overall Bidet for five consecutive years (2021-2025). The Alpha UX Pearl (~$450) was named Best Overall by Yahoo Tested and Best Value by Popular Science, with its photocatalyst deodorizer and 4.5-inch profile. New for 2026, the BidetKing Crown (~$429) has emerged as a trending pick with the strongest spray pressure tested (1 L/min), a multicolor in-bowl LED, and a 3-year tiered warranty. The Tushy Cloud (~$359) marks Tushy's first electric bidet seat, featuring coil-heated endless warm water, UV nozzle sterilization, and heated seat/dryer — though it's elongated only. For non-electric options, the Tushy Classic 3.0 (~$119) remains the most popular bidet attachment, praised for its comfortable stream and 8.5-minute installation, while the Luxe Bidet Neo 320 Plus (~$60) earned Reviewed's top attachment pick with dual nozzles and warm water via sink hookup. The Brondell EcoSeat S101 (~$90) splits the difference as a complete non-electric seat replacement with integrated dual nozzles and a sittable lid. [src2, src4, src7]

Best for Each Use Case

Best Overall: Alpha JX2 (~$389) — Check price

BidetKing's #1 overall pick and CNN Underscored's Best Overall Bidet for five consecutive years (2021-2025), plus Forbes Vetted's Best of 2026. The JX2 combines tankless water heating for endless warm water with bowl mist (added mid-2024), a stainless steel nozzle, LED nightlight, strong air dryer, and a sturdy sittable lid supporting 320 lbs. At ~$389 with a 3-year warranty, it delivers the core electric bidet experience without unnecessary complexity. [src3, src6]

Best TOTO Mid-Range: TOTO Washlet S5 (~$650) — Check price

The S5 replaces the C5 with three major upgrades: tankless instantaneous water heating for endless warm water (eliminating the C5's 30-60 second tank limit), a 19% slimmer profile that hugs the bowl line, and a redesigned multifunction remote storing four user presets. It retains PREMIST, EWATER+ wand cleaning, heated seat, warm air dryer, and automatic deodorizer. At ~$650, it sits between the S2 and S7A in TOTO's new all-tankless lineup. [src6, src8]

Best TOTO Entry: TOTO Washlet S2 (~$499) — Check price

New for 2026, the S2 replaces the tank-based C2 as TOTO's entry-level S-series seat. CNN Underscored's tester called it the most comfortable stream and dryer tested, with endless warm water from its tankless heater. It includes PREMIST, EWATER+ wand cleaning, heated seat, warm air dryer, and deodorizer — the same core TOTO tech as the S5 and S7A. The trade-off: no user presets and a side-panel control instead of a remote. [src3, src8]

Best Luxury: TOTO Washlet S7A (~$1,300) — Check price

TOTO's flagship stands just 4.1 inches tall at the rear — the slimmest bidet seat on the market. The auto-open and auto-close lid senses your approach and departure, while a built-in nightlight illuminates the bathroom for nighttime use. EWATER+ mists both the wand and the toilet bowl, reducing cleaning frequency. The remote stores up to four personalized wash settings. Available in contemporary and classic lid styles in Cotton White or Sedona Beige. [src3, src6]

Best Value Premium: Alpha UX Pearl (~$450) — Check price

Key Market Trends (2026)

  • TOTO completes the all-tankless S-series transition: TOTO's 2026 lineup is now fully tankless — the S2 (~$499), S5 (~$650), and S7A (~$1,300) all use instantaneous water heating. The tank-based C5 and C2 are being phased out. The S2 replaces the C2 as the entry point with CNN calling it the most comfortable stream tested. [src3, src6, src8]
  • Sub-$400 tankless segment expands: The Alpha iX Pure (~$349), Tushy Cloud (~$359), and Alpha JX2 (~$389) bring tankless heated water below the $400 mark, making endless warm water accessible to mid-budget buyers. The LE99 (~$250) remains the only hybrid/tank option under $300. [src6]
  • Tushy enters the electric bidet seat market: The Tushy Cloud (~$359) marks Tushy's first electric seat, featuring coil-heated water, UV sterilization, and heated seat/dryer. This signals Tushy's move beyond the attachment-only segment, though the elongated-only limitation narrows its audience. [src3]
  • UV sterilization becoming standard in premium seats: The Tushy Cloud, Eco Nova, and several other new entries include UV nozzle sterilization, joining TOTO's EWATER+ as standard hygiene tech. Expect UV to appear in most $350+ seats by late 2026. [src3, src6]
  • Ultra-slim designs dominate: The Brondell T44 (3.9"), BidetKing Crown (4.0"), TOTO S7A (4.1"), and Alpha iX Pure/UX Pearl (4.5") demonstrate that sub-5-inch profiles are the new standard. Bulky designs are being phased out across all price tiers. [src6]
  • Non-electric segment remains the gateway: Budget attachments from Tushy and Luxe Bidet continue to drive US bidet adoption, with Consumer Reports now lab-testing attachments alongside electric seats. The Tushy Classic 3.0 and Luxe Neo 320 Plus remain the entry points for first-time users. [src2, src7]

Important Caveats

  • Prices are approximate US street prices as of April 2026. Bidet seat prices fluctuate significantly across retailers, and refurbished or open-box units from brands like Brondell can offer 30-40% savings. The BidetKing Crown is frequently discounted from its $599 MSRP to ~$429.
  • Electric bidet seats require a GFCI electrical outlet within reach of the toilet. Many bathrooms lack this, and adding an outlet requires an electrician ($150-400). Factor installation cost into your budget for electric models.
  • Toilet shape matters: most bidet seats come in elongated and round versions at the same price, but you must measure your toilet before purchasing. The Tushy Cloud is elongated ONLY — no round version exists. [src1, src3, src5]
  • The TOTO Washlet S2 and S5 are new and have limited long-term reliability data. The S7A shares the same tankless technology with a longer track record. TOTO's 1-year warranty is shorter than Alpha (3 years) and Brondell (3 years). [src8]
  • Warranty lengths vary significantly: TOTO S2/S5 (1 year), Alpha iX Pure (1 year), BidetKing Crown (3-year tiered: 100%/75%/50%), Alpha UX Pearl/JX2 (3 years), Brondell Swash 1400/T44 (3 years), Bio Bidet BB-2000 (3 years, 6-year optional), Eco Nova (2 years), Tushy Cloud (1 year).
  • Warm-water bidet attachments like the Luxe Neo 320 Plus connect to the sink's hot water supply via a T-adapter, which limits installation to toilets near a sink and provides warm (not hot) water that depends on sink distance. [src2]
