## Important Caveats

- Prices shown are approximate U.S. street prices as of April 2026. Cordless vacuum prices fluctuate significantly; Dyson, Shark, and Samsung frequently run 20-40% off sales on Amazon, direct sites, and during holiday events. The Dyson Gen5detect has dropped from its $1,050 MSRP to ~$800 street price.
- Manufacturer-claimed battery life is measured under ideal conditions (Eco mode, non-motorized attachment, minimal debris). Real-world runtime with motorized brushrolls on Auto mode is typically 40-60% of maximum claims. The Dyson Gen5detect's 70-minute claim translates to roughly 40 minutes in Auto mode and 15 minutes on Boost. The Tineco A90S's 105-minute claim is measured with the 2-in-1 crevice tool, not the main brush.
- Suction power is measured differently across brands -- Dyson uses Air Watts (AW), Samsung uses AW, Shark quotes motor wattage, Tineco uses AW on newer models (A90S, S70) but Watts on older models. Levoit quotes Watts. Direct comparisons across brands require independent third-party testing results rather than manufacturer specs alone.
- Even the most powerful cordless stick vacuums cannot fully match the deep-cleaning performance of a quality corded upright or canister vacuum on thick carpets. For homes with predominantly thick carpet flooring, a cordless stick vacuum works best as a supplement.
- Dustbin capacities range from 0.35L (Dyson V12 Detect Slim) to 0.8L (Tineco A90S). Homes with pets or heavy debris should prioritize larger bins or self-emptying systems to avoid frequent interruptions.
