Best for Each Use Case

Best Overall: Ergotron LX (~$180) — Check price

The Ergotron LX has been the benchmark single monitor arm for years and continues to earn top recommendations across nearly every major review outlet in 2026. Its constant-force spring mechanism provides smooth, reliable height adjustment across a 13-inch range, with 24 inches of horizontal extension and full 360-degree rotation. The all-metal build quality and 10-year warranty make it a buy-it-for-life product. [src1, src4]

Best Budget: North Bayou F80 (~$30) — Check price

At roughly one-sixth the price of the Ergotron LX, the North Bayou F80 delivers surprisingly capable gas-spring articulation for monitors from 17 to 30 inches (up to 19.8 lbs). Over 17,000 Amazon reviews averaging 4.5 stars confirm that it genuinely works well for standard 24-27 inch monitors. The automotive-grade gas spring has been tested for 20,000 motion cycles. It lacks the premium feel and longevity of Ergotron, but at $30 it is hard to argue with the value. [src4, src5]

Best for Premium Workspaces: Ergotron Trace (~$330) — Check price

The Ergotron Trace is designed for users who want the absolute best in motion quality and aesthetics. It supports monitors up to 38 inches and 21.5 lbs, offers 11 inches of vertical lift, 80 degrees of tilt, and comes with a premium 15-year warranty. The low-profile design and hidden cable management make it the cleanest-looking arm on the market. [src1, src5]

Best for Heavy/Ultrawide Monitors: Ergotron HX (~$400) — Check price

The Ergotron HX is the only widely recommended arm capable of holding monitors up to 42 lbs and 49 inches. Its VESA adapter supports patterns from 100x100 up to 400x400mm, covering virtually every large-format display including the Samsung Odyssey G9 and LG UltraGear 45-inch. The HD Pivot variant adds support for 1000R-curved ultrawides. [src1, src5]

Best Value Mid-Range: Amazon Basics Lift Engine (~$110) — Check price

The Amazon Basics Lift Engine single monitor arm is widely recognized as a rebranded Ergotron LX with near-identical internal mechanisms. It supports monitors up to 25 lbs with 75x75 and 100x100 VESA mounting, 360-degree rotation, and full tilt adjustment. The trade-off versus the genuine Ergotron LX is a shorter warranty and slightly different finish options. [src1, src4]

Best for Dell Monitors: Dell MSA20 (~$130) — Check price

The Dell MSA20 is factory-calibrated for Dell monitors and includes a counterbalance indicator for easy setup. Its snap-on cable cover holds up to 10 cables, and it supports monitors from 19 to 38 inches weighing up to 22 lbs. If you have a Dell monitor, the MSA20 provides the cleanest integration and most straightforward setup experience. [src3, src5]

Best Warranty: Herman Miller Jarvis (~$225) — Check price

Wirecutter's top pick, the Herman Miller Jarvis supports monitors up to 32 inches and 19 lbs with exceptionally smooth motion. Its standout feature is the 15-year warranty from Herman Miller, the longest in the category. The arm moves smoothly into a wide range of positions, holds monitors steadily, and is easy to set up. The trade-off is a lower weight capacity compared to the Ergotron LX. [src2, src3]

Decision Logic

If budget < $50

→ The North Bayou F80 (~$30) is the clear choice. It handles standard 24-27 inch monitors well and has excellent user ratings. Skip everything else in this price range. [src4]

If monitor weighs over 25 lbs or is larger than 34 inches

→ The Ergotron HX (~$400) is the only reliable single arm that supports up to 42 lbs and 49 inches. No other widely recommended arm comes close to this weight capacity. For 1000R-curved ultrawides, get the HX with HD Pivot. [src1, src5]

If primary use is sit-stand desk or frequent repositioning

→ Prioritize constant-force spring arms (Ergotron LX, LX Pro, Trace) over gas-spring arms. Constant-force springs maintain consistent tension across the full range of motion, making one-handed height adjustment smoother and more reliable over years of daily use. [src1, src4]

If user has a Dell monitor

→ The Dell MSA20 (~$130) provides the best integration with factory calibration, counterbalance indicator, and cable management designed specifically for Dell displays. For non-Dell monitors, the Ergotron LX offers better overall value and flexibility. [src3, src5]

If user wants maximum warranty coverage

→ The Ergotron Trace (15-year) and Herman Miller Jarvis (15-year) both offer the longest warranties in the category. The Jarvis is more affordable at ~$225, while the Trace supports larger monitors up to 38 inches. [src2, src1]

Default recommendation

→ The Ergotron LX (~$180) is the safest pick for unknown requirements. It covers most monitors (up to 34 inches, 25 lbs), has a proven constant-force spring design, a 10-year warranty, and is consistently rated the best overall by PCWorld, BTOD, and multiple other reviewers. [src1, src4]

  • Ergotron LX Pro launch: Ergotron released the LX Pro as an upgraded successor to the classic LX, featuring tool-free installation, a lower minimum weight (4 lbs vs 7 lbs for the original LX), and improved cable management. The LX Pro retails for ~$210. [src1, src4]

  • Budget arms closing the gap: Arms like the North Bayou F80 and HUANUO gas-spring models now offer VESA compatibility, cable management, and smooth articulation at under $50, making a basic monitor arm accessible to virtually any budget. [src4, src5]
  • Ultrawide-specific engineering: The rise of 34-49 inch ultrawide monitors has driven dedicated heavy-duty arms like the Ergotron HX and Secretlab Magnus Monitor Arm to support up to 42 lbs with extended VESA patterns. [src1, src5]

Important Caveats

  • Prices are approximate US street prices as of March 2026 and may vary significantly by region, retailer, and promotional period.
  • Weight capacity ratings assume centered VESA mounting; monitors with off-center weight distribution (common in ultrawides) may require arms rated for higher capacity than the monitor's actual weight.
  • Desk thickness compatibility varies: most arms clamp onto desks 0.8-2.5 inches thick, but some desks with reinforced edges or cable trays may need grommet mounting instead.
  • The Amazon Basics Lift Engine arm is frequently cited as an Ergotron LX rebrand, but Amazon has not officially confirmed this, and warranty terms differ.
  • Gas-spring arms (North Bayou, HUANUO) may lose tension after 3-5 years of heavy use; constant-force spring designs (Ergotron) are mechanically more durable long-term.
