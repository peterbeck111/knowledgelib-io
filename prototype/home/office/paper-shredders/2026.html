Best for Each Use Case

Best Overall: Royal 14MC (~$60-75) -- Check price

After spending over 10 hours researching more than 40 shredders and testing 20 finalists, Wirecutter recommends the Royal 14MC as the best paper shredder for most people. It handles up to 14 sheets at once -- nearly double the capacity of competing micro-cut shredders at the same price -- and ran jam-free throughout testing even when pushing to 17 sheets including stapled stacks. The micro-cut shred size (5/32 x 15/32 inches) provides P-4 security, making documents virtually impossible to reconstruct. Operating at approximately 65 dB (comparable to a washing machine), it is one of the quieter shredders tested. The 20-minute continuous run time handles typical home-office shredding sessions, the pull-out bin is easy to empty, and a 3-year warranty on the cutter provides peace of mind. Caveat (April 2026): Wirecutter is investigating reader reports of structural and electrical defects in the Royal 14MC and may revise their recommendation; check Wirecutter for the latest status before purchasing. Best Budget: Amazon Basics 8-Sheet Micro-Cut (~$40-68) -- Check price

The Amazon Basics 8-Sheet Micro-Cut delivers P-4 high-security shredding at one of the lowest price points in our comparison, though prices have risen from ~$35 to ~$40-68 depending on the retailer. It shreds paper into tiny 5/32 x 15/32-inch particles that are virtually impossible to reassemble, and also handles credit cards and CDs one at a time. The 4.8-gallon pullout basket is adequate for light home use, and auto start/stop plus manual reverse simplify operation. The trade-off is a 5-minute run time before cool-down, making it best suited for occasional shredding rather than large batches. Best High Security (P-5): Aurora AU1060MA (~$100) -- Check price

CNN Underscored calls it the ideal balance between high performance and maximum security in document shredding. Its standout spec is a 60-minute continuous run time, the longest of any shredder we evaluated, with anti-jam auto-reverse to clear any issues automatically. The 6-gallon pullout bin with viewing window handles extended shredding sessions. Ultra-quiet operation and LED status indicators for standby, overheat, bin-full, and overload add convenience. Key Market Trends (2026)

  • Prices rising across the category: Multiple models have seen 30-70% price increases between late 2025 and April 2026. The Aurora AU120MA has risen from ~$130 to as high as ~$225, the Amazon Basics 8-Sheet from ~$35 to ~$68, and the Fellowes LX220 from ~$170 to ~$300 at some retailers. Prices vary significantly across retailers, making comparison shopping essential. [src3, src8]
  • Micro-cut is now the standard: Cross-cut shredders are increasingly being replaced by micro-cut models across all price tiers. Even budget shredders under $50, such as the Amazon Basics 8-Sheet and Bonsaii C234-A, now offer P-4 micro-cut security. This shift has been driven by growing identity theft awareness and the minimal price premium of micro-cut over cross-cut technology. [src1, src2, src4]
  • Auto-feed technology is expanding to consumer prices: Once limited to expensive commercial models like the Fellowes AutoMax 150C (~$390), auto-feed capability is now available at consumer-friendly prices. The Aurora AU120MA delivers 120-sheet auto-feed starting around $130, making "stack and walk away" shredding accessible to home offices. [src1, src5]
  • Run times have dramatically improved: The Aurora AU1060MA's 60-minute continuous run time and the Amazon Basics 24-Sheet's 40-minute run time represent a significant improvement over older models that required cool-down after 3-5 minutes. The Bonsaii 18-Sheet ($160) also offers 60-minute continuous operation with 18-sheet capacity. [src2, src3, src5, src8]
  • Safety features are becoming more sophisticated: Features like the Fellowes LX220's 100% jam-proof technology and SafeSense hand detection, plus anti-jam auto-reverse on Aurora models, have made paper shredders significantly safer and more reliable than previous generations. Child safety locks (Fellowes 62MC, TRU RED TR-NMC122A) are now standard in mid-range models. [src3, src4]

Important Caveats

  • Prices shown are approximate U.S. street prices as of April 2026. Paper shredder prices have risen significantly across the category since late 2025, with some models increasing 30-70%. Prices vary widely across retailers (Amazon, Costco, Walmart, Staples); comparison shopping is strongly recommended.
  • Manufacturer-claimed sheet capacities assume standard 20-lb bond paper. Heavier cardstock, multiple credit cards, or CDs should be fed at reduced volumes. Exceeding rated capacity is the primary cause of jams and motor damage.
  • Run times are measured under ideal conditions with standard paper loads. Continuous heavy shredding of thick stacks or mixed media (CDs, credit cards) will reduce effective run time and may trigger thermal protection earlier.
  • Security levels follow the DIN 66399 standard: P-3 (cross-cut, readable with effort), P-4 (cross-cut or micro-cut, very difficult to reconstruct), P-5 (micro-cut, virtually impossible to reconstruct). For most home-office needs, P-4 is sufficient; P-5 is recommended for sensitive financial or medical documents.
  • All models in this comparison shred paper and credit cards. CD/DVD shredding capability varies -- check individual model specifications if this is important for your use case.
  • As of April 2026, Wirecutter is investigating reader reports of structural and electrical defects in the Royal 14MC. While it remains their top pick pending investigation, buyers should check Wirecutter's latest guidance before purchasing.
  • None of these models provide HIPAA-compliant certificates of destruction. Organizations with regulatory compliance requirements should use professional shredding services.
