# Best Automatic Pet Feeders (2026)

## Summary

The automatic pet feeder market continues its rapid expansion in 2026, with the global automatic and smart pet feeder market growing from $2.09 billion in 2025 to $2.61 billion in 2026, driven by pet humanization trends and dual-income households. [src8, src4] Smart WiFi-enabled models with app scheduling, HD cameras, and battery backup now dominate the category, while RFID and AI-powered pet recognition have made multi-pet feeding far more practical than even a year ago.

After analyzing hands-on testing from Wirecutter, Cats.com, CNN Underscored, Dogster, Consumer Reports, Reviewed.com, and Chewy, the **Petlibro Granary Camera Feeder** (~$90) remains the best overall pick for 2026, combining a 5L (21-cup) capacity, 1080p camera with night vision, dual-band WiFi, and reliable dispensing at a competitive price. CNN Underscored (April 2026) names the Granary the best-tested cat feeder; Cats.com (April 2026) now ranks the SureFeed Microchip Feeder as its overall #1 for multi-pet households with different diets. [src1, src10] For pet owners who need maximum reliability and capacity, the **Whisker Feeder-Robot** (~$299) offers premium build quality with anti-jam technology and 32-cup capacity. [src1, src3] The **PETKIT YumShare Solo with Camera** (~$93) has emerged as a strong mid-range contender, offering AI-powered pet recognition and 1080p monitoring at a lower price than the dual-hopper model. [src2, src6]

Key considerations when choosing include hopper capacity (2L for single cats up to 7.5L for multi-pet or extended travel), camera and monitoring features, power redundancy (battery backup is essential for reliability), portion-control method (timer vs weight-based), and whether you need special features like microchip/RFID-activated feeding for multi-pet households or semiconductor cooling for wet food. ## Best for Each Use Case

### Best Overall: Petlibro Granary Camera Feeder (~$90) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/petlibro-granary-camera)
The Petlibro Granary strikes the best balance of features, reliability, and value in 2026. Its 1080p HD camera with night vision lets you monitor feeding times remotely, while the 5L hopper holds 2-3 weeks of food for a single cat. Wirecutter praised its easy disassembly for cleaning and the airtight seals on lid and chute that keep kibble fresh. Dual-band 5GHz WiFi support ensures stable connectivity, and the app allows scheduling up to 10 meals daily with portion sizes as small as 1/20 cup. Cats.com confirmed consistent dispensing accuracy across multiple weeks of testing. [src1, src2, src7]

### Best Budget: Cat Mate C500 (~$46) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/cat-mate-c500)
The Cat Mate C500 remains the best value for pet owners who want reliable timed feeding without smart features. Its 5-compartment rotating design accommodates wet food, dry food, or medication, and included ice packs keep perishable food fresh for workday feeding. At under $50, it costs a fraction of smart feeders while delivering dependable scheduled feeding. The trade-off is manual programming via the built-in timer and no remote monitoring. Reviewed.com and Cats.com both rank it as a top budget pick. [src2, src7]

### Best Premium: Whisker Feeder-Robot (~$299) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/whisker-feeder-robot)
Made by the same company behind the Litter-Robot, the Feeder-Robot is the most advanced gravity-based smart feeder on the market. Its anti-jam dispensing system virtually eliminates clogging — a common complaint with cheaper models — and the 32-cup capacity can feed multiple cats for weeks. WiFi connectivity through the AutoPets Connect app enables remote scheduling, feeding history tracking, and low-food notifications. Designed and assembled in the USA with a 90-day money-back trial and 1-year warranty. TechnoMEOW calls it "the most advanced smart cat feeder in 2026." [src1, src3]

### Best for Multiple Pets: PETKIT YumShare Dual-Hopper (~$170) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/petkit-yumshare-dual)
The PETKIT YumShare stands out with its AI-powered camera that can recognize individual cats, tracking which pet eats how much and when. The dual-hopper design lets you load two different foods and assign specific diets to specific pets. The 1080p HD camera with 140-degree wide-angle provides real-time monitoring with motion detection alerts, making it the most intelligent feeder for multi-cat households with different dietary needs. [src2, src6]

### Best for Travel/Portability: Petlibro Air WiFi (~$75) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/petlibro-air-wifi)
The Petlibro Air eliminates outlet dependency with a rechargeable battery providing 30 days of operation per charge. You can place it anywhere without worrying about cord access — bedroom, office, or any room. Despite its compact 2L capacity and cordless design, it retains full WiFi app control for remote scheduling and monitoring. Wirecutter picked it as their battery-powered top choice, noting the airtight food storage that keeps kibble fresh. [src1, src2]

### Best for Special Diets / Multi-Pet Separation: SureFeed Microchip Feeder (~$166) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/surefeed-microchip)
The SureFeed uses your pet's existing microchip or an included RFID collar tag to open the lid only for the authorized pet — supporting up to 32 registered microchips. This prevents food-stealing in multi-pet households where cats or dogs need different prescription diets. The sealed lid also keeps wet food fresh longer than open-bowl feeders. Endorsed by veterinarians and rated the best overall by Cats.com for its unmatched reliability in selective feeding. [src2, src3, src4]

### Best for Wet Food: Petlibro Polar Wet Food Feeder (~$130) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/petlibro-polar-wet)
Most automatic feeders only handle dry kibble, but the Petlibro Polar uses semiconductor cooling technology to chill wet food to below 50 degrees F within 4 hours, maintaining freshness for up to 72 hours. App-controlled scheduling allows up to 3 meals per day. This is the go-to choice for cats that eat exclusively wet food or for pet owners who want to supplement kibble with refrigerated wet meals. [src2, src7]

### Best Mid-Range Smart Feeder: PETKIT YumShare Solo with Camera (~$93) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/petkit-yumshare-solo-camera)
A new strong contender in 2026, the YumShare Solo offers AI-powered pet detection with 1080p HD camera, motion-triggered notifications, and 2-way audio at a price that undercuts the dual-hopper model by $77. The camera captures feeding activity and sends alerts via the PETKIT app. Ideal for single-cat households that want smart monitoring without paying premium prices. Some advanced AI features may require a subscription for extended recording. [src2, src6]

### Best Portion Accuracy: Pawsync WiFi Smart Scale Feeder (~$70) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/pawsync-smart-scale-feeder)
New in 2026 Wirecutter testing, the Pawsync uses an integrated smart scale to weigh dispensed food in grams or ounces — rather than counting timed dispenser rotations — and was the most accurate model in Wirecutter's portion tests, narrowly edging out the Petlibro Granary. The 3.6L stainless-steel bowl resists bacteria better than plastic, the PetSync app tracks intake trends over time, and dual power (AC + backup battery) ensures reliability. Ideal for pets on calorie-restricted or prescription diets where precise grams matter more than camera monitoring. [src1, src10]

## Important Caveats

- Prices cited are approximate US retail prices as of April 2026 and may vary by retailer, region, and promotional period.
- Most automatic feeders are designed for dry kibble only. Wet food requires specialized models with cooling (e.g., Petlibro Polar) or compartment-based designs (e.g., Cat Mate C500).
- WiFi-dependent feeders require stable internet; if your connection drops, scheduled meals still dispense from local memory on most models, but remote monitoring is interrupted.
- Automatic feeders are generally suitable for cats and small to medium dogs. Very large dogs may tip or damage lightweight plastic feeders.
- This comparison focuses on feeders available in the US market. Product availability and pricing differ in other regions.
- Camera-equipped feeders collect video data through manufacturer apps; review each brand's privacy policy before enabling cloud recording features. Some AI features (e.g., PETKIT pet recognition) may require paid subscriptions for full functionality.
- Portion accuracy depends on kibble size and shape — manufacturer ratings are calibrated for standard round kibble.
