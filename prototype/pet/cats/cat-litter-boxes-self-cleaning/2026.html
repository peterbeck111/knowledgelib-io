Important Caveats

  • All self-cleaning litter boxes require clumping litter (clay, tofu, cassava, or compatible mixes). Non-clumping litter will jam most rotating mechanisms.
  • Cat weight limits (3-25 lbs) are enforced by sensors. Cats below the minimum (typically 3 lbs) won't trigger the cycle; cats above the maximum may trigger safety shutoffs. The homerunPET CS106 is the only model rated for cats up to 25 lbs.
  • Ongoing costs include waste drawer liners ($5-$15/month), odor cartridges ($5-$15/month), and in some cases subscription tiers (Whisker+ ~$7.99/month for full Litter-Robot 5 Pro AI insights).
  • Power outlet must be within 5-6 feet. Manufacturer documentation does not recommend extension cords due to motor power requirements.
  • Some cats need a 1-2 week transition period from manual to automatic litter boxes. Place both side-by-side during transition.
  • CatGenie requires permanent water line hookup — it is not plug-and-play like rotating models.
  • Litter-Robot 5 and 5 Pro are not currently sold on Amazon (April 2026). They are available direct from Whisker (litter-robot.com) only. The Litter-Robot EVO is on Amazon.
  • BGR's review of the Litter-Robot 5 Pro flagged motor and sensor bugs on early units. Buyers prioritizing reliability over AI features may prefer the Litter-Robot 4 or EVO. [src8]
