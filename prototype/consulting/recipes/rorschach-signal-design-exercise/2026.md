# Rorschach Signal Design Exercise

## Purpose

This recipe executes the Module 1 hands-on exercise from the Rorschach GTM workshop: identifying domain-specific failure states in the client's target market and crafting ambiguous artifacts (Rorschach signals) that only companies experiencing specific operational distress will decode as relevant. Healthy companies see nothing actionable. The exercise produces a draft Rorschach signal library validated through team role-play. [src1, src2]

## Prerequisites
<!-- Agents: verify ALL prerequisites before executing. Missing prerequisites = failed execution. -->

- [ ] **Rorschach Protocol theory presented** -- participants understand: active inference, self-diagnosis > external diagnosis, embarrassment barrier, silence as success metric (45 min theory block)
- [ ] **Industry vertical context documented** -- client's industry, common failure states, competitive landscape
- [ ] **Target segment definitions available** -- 3-5 segments with firmographic and behavioral criteria
- [ ] **Domain expertise present** -- at least 1 participant with deep insider knowledge of target industry failure states
- [ ] **Whiteboard/Miro ready** -- collaboration surface for failure state mapping

## Constraints
<!-- Hard rules. Agents: enforce throughout execution. Violating these = broken output or legal risk. -->

- Artifacts must be genuinely ambiguous. The "healthy company test" is non-negotiable: if a company without the problem finds the artifact interesting, the ambiguity mechanism is broken. This is the single most common failure in Rorschach signal design. [src1]
- Self-diagnosis must be internal, not external. Reactance Theory shows that human beings resist being told they have a problem. [src2] Every artifact must trigger self-recognition ("this is about us") rather than external accusation ("you have a problem").
- Artifacts must require insider knowledge to decode. The depth-of-domain test validates that the signal cannot be produced by a generalist. If a first-year marketing associate could write it, it is not a Rorschach signal.
- The intellectual curiosity pathway must bypass the embarrassment barrier. Prospects in stigmatized failure states (security breaches, cultural crises, financial distress) will not engage with content that requires them to admit failure. [src3] The pathway must frame engagement as intellectual curiosity, not confession.
- Role-play validation is mandatory. Untested artifacts have a high failure rate in production.

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Identify Domain-Specific Failure States (15 min)

**Duration**: 15 minutes
**Tool**: Whiteboard/Miro -- sticky notes or columns per segment

For each of the client's 3-5 target segments, identify 5+ domain-specific failure states that the client's solution addresses. These are the operational problems that create buying urgency.

For each failure state, document:
- **Operational symptom**: What breaks internally (e.g., "deployment failures increasing from 1/month to 3/week")
- **Exhaust fume**: What leaks externally (e.g., "3 new SRE job postings in 2 weeks")
- **Decision-maker emotion**: What the responsible executive feels (e.g., "board meeting anxiety, fear of public incident")
- **Language level**: Is the problem pre-articulate ("something feels wrong") or articulated ("our deployment pipeline is failing")? [src5]

Pre-articulate problems are more valuable for Rorschach signals because the prospect cannot search for solutions they cannot name.

**Verify**: 5+ failure states per segment, each with all 4 components documented.
**If failed**: Failure states are too generic (e.g., "revenue declining"). Narrow to specific operational symptoms. Use probing questions: "What would you see in their Slack channels? What would their status page show? What would their Glassdoor reviews say?"

### Step 2: Craft Ambiguous Artifacts (20 min)

**Duration**: 20 minutes
**Tool**: Document template per artifact

For each failure state, craft 3-5 ambiguous artifacts. Each artifact must include:

1. **Surface content**: What format does this take? (benchmark report, diagnostic calculator, anonymized case study, technical deep-dive blog post, industry survey results, conference talk abstract)
2. **Ambiguity mechanism**: How does interpretation diverge? Choose from:
   - **Diagnostic ambiguity**: Data/benchmarks that only mean something if your operations match the failure pattern
   - **Vocabulary ambiguity**: Terms with different meanings for healthy vs distressed companies
   - **Scenario ambiguity**: Situations that read as hypothetical to healthy companies but autobiographical to distressed ones
   - **Metric ambiguity**: Numbers that are innocuous in healthy contexts but alarming in failing ones
3. **Distressed view**: What does a company in this failure state see? (diagnosis, urgency, "this is about us")
4. **Healthy view**: What does a healthy company see? (generic, boring, irrelevant, skip)
5. **Self-diagnosis trigger**: The specific moment the distressed reader realizes the artifact describes their reality

**Verify**: 3-5 artifacts per failure state with all 5 components. Artifacts use genuine ambiguity, not obvious pitches disguised as questions.
**If failed**: Artifacts read as marketing rather than ambiguous signals. Apply the "would a competitor's salesperson understand the pitch?" test. If yes, it is not ambiguous enough.

### Step 3: Predict Response Differentials (10 min)

**Duration**: 10 minutes
**Tool**: Spreadsheet or table on whiteboard

For each artifact, predict the response differential:

| Artifact | Distressed Engagement Rate | Healthy Engagement Rate | Self-Qualification Rate | Confidence |
|----------|---------------------------|------------------------|------------------------|------------|
| [Name] | [X%] | [Y%] | [Z%] | [High/Med/Low] |

Target differential: distressed engagement should be 5-10x higher than healthy engagement. If the ratio is less than 3x, the ambiguity mechanism needs strengthening.

**Verify**: All artifacts have predicted differentials. Ratio > 3x for viable artifacts.
**If failed**: Differential too low -- the artifact is not ambiguous enough. Return to Step 2 and increase the specificity of the failure state referenced.

### Step 4: Validate Insider Knowledge Depth (10 min)

**Duration**: 10 minutes
**Tool**: Discussion (no special tools)

For each artifact, ask: "Could someone without deep domain expertise in this failure state produce this artifact?"

The insider knowledge depth test:
- **Surface-level**: Could be written by anyone with Google access. FAIL. (e.g., "Is your deployment process reliable?")
- **Industry-level**: Requires familiarity with the industry but not the specific failure state. WEAK. (e.g., "How does your deployment frequency compare to industry benchmarks?")
- **Insider-level**: Requires knowing what the failure state actually looks and feels like from the inside. PASS. (e.g., "What happens when your rollback takes longer than the deployment?")

**Verify**: Artifacts rated as insider-level. Surface-level and industry-level artifacts flagged for redesign.
**If failed**: Lack of domain expertise in the room. Either bring in a subject matter expert or use the exhaust fume signal catalog as a research starting point.

### Step 5: Design Intellectual Curiosity Pathway (10 min)

**Duration**: 10 minutes
**Tool**: Whiteboard -- engagement pathway mapping

For each artifact, design the engagement pathway that bypasses the embarrassment barrier:

1. **Initial hook**: Intellectual curiosity ("this is interesting") rather than problem confession ("I need help")
2. **Progressive reveal**: Engagement deepens naturally -- the prospect does not realize they are self-diagnosing until they are already invested
3. **Conversion action**: Low-commitment next step that maintains the curiosity frame (download a deeper analysis, join a research cohort, attend a roundtable) rather than a high-commitment sales action (book a demo, request pricing)

The embarrassment barrier is the reason high-value prospects in stigmatized domains (security breaches, financial distress, cultural crises) ignore conventional outreach. The curiosity pathway circumvents it. [src3]

**Verify**: Each artifact has a 3-step curiosity pathway: hook -> progressive reveal -> low-commitment conversion.
**If failed**: Conversion action looks like a sales pitch. Replace with genuine value exchange (research, community, diagnostic) that maintains the curiosity frame.

### Step 6: Role-Play Validation (10 min)

**Duration**: 10 minutes
**Tool**: Team role-play (no special tools)

Test top artifacts through team role-play with two personas:

**Persona A -- Prospect Under Stress**: Team member assumes the role of a decision-maker at a company experiencing the target failure state. They encounter the artifact. What do they see? What do they feel? Do they self-diagnose? Do they engage?

**Persona B -- Healthy Prospect**: Team member assumes the role of a decision-maker at a company NOT experiencing the failure state. They encounter the same artifact. What do they see? Do they ignore it? Do they find it interesting (fail) or irrelevant (pass)?

For each tested artifact, record:
- Did Persona A self-diagnose? (Y/N)
- Did Persona A take the conversion action? (Y/N)
- Did Persona B ignore the artifact? (Y/N -- must be Yes to pass)
- What modifications would improve the artifact?

**Verify**: Artifacts pass the dual-persona test -- distressed engages, healthy ignores.
**If failed**: Artifact resonates with healthy company (ambiguity broken) -- return to Step 2 and redesign the ambiguity mechanism. Most common fix: increase the specificity of the failure state reference.

## Quality Benchmarks

| Quality Metric | Minimum Acceptable | Good | Excellent |
|---------------|-------------------|------|-----------|
| Failure states identified per segment | 3+ | 5+ | 7+ |
| Artifacts per failure state | 2 | 3-4 | 5+ |
| Healthy company test pass rate | > 50% | > 75% | > 90% |
| Insider knowledge depth (% insider-level) | > 50% | > 75% | > 90% |
| Role-play validation (% dual-persona pass) | > 40% | > 60% | > 80% |

**If below minimum**: Focus on strengthening the weakest artifacts. Most common failure is insufficient ambiguity -- artifacts read as obvious pitches.

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Cannot identify failure states | Insufficient domain expertise | Bring in domain SME or use exhaust fume signal catalog as research reference |
| All artifacts fail healthy company test | Artifacts are disguised pitches, not ambiguous signals | Return to fundamentals: the artifact must be interesting for reasons OTHER than the problem it diagnoses |
| Insider knowledge depth too shallow | Team has sold to but not worked inside the target industry | Research deeper: read Glassdoor reviews, Reddit threads, industry forums for insider language |
| Embarrassment barrier not bypassed | Curiosity pathway includes confession step | Remove all steps that require prospect to admit a problem. Replace with intellectual framing. |
| Role-play not realistic | Participants cannot separate roles | Assign specific company profiles for role-play (use real target accounts) |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Starting with the solution and working backward to the artifact
Thinking "how do I make our product look interesting?" produces marketing, not Rorschach signals. [src4]

### Correct: Start with the failure state and work forward
Think "what does this failure state look like from the inside?" and then design an artifact that only someone experiencing it would recognize.

### Wrong: Making artifacts attention-grabbing for everyone
Broad appeal destroys the filtering mechanism. An artifact that goes viral is a Rorschach failure -- it means healthy companies engaged.

### Correct: Design for silence from healthy companies
Non-engagement from healthy companies is the success metric. If 99% of recipients ignore the artifact and 1% self-diagnose, the signal is working perfectly. [src1]

### Wrong: Skipping the role-play validation
Assuming artifacts will work in production because they "feel right" to the team that designed them.

### Correct: Always role-play test with dual personas
The team that designs the artifact is biased -- they know the answer. Role-play with someone who does not reveals whether the ambiguity genuinely functions.

## When This Matters

Use as Module 1 of the Rorschach GTM workshop, or independently when a team needs to design domain-specific Rorschach signals for their target market. Requires at least one participant with deep domain expertise in the target industry's failure states. The exercise produces a draft signal library that the Rorschach Signal Designer agent can refine into production-ready artifacts.
