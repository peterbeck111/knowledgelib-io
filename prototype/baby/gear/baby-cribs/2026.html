Summary

The 2026 crib market split clearly into three tiers: ultra-versatile premium convertibles led by the new Babyletto Yuzu 8-in-1 (~$649-799, GREENGUARD Gold, includes all conversion kits + two mattresses), workhorse mid-tier 3-in-1 and 4-in-1 cribs from Babyletto, DaVinci, Storkcraft and Delta in the $189-499 range, and budget picks from Graco, Dream On Me, and IKEA at $99-190. The DaVinci Kalani 4-in-1 (~$200-250) remains Wirecutter's best-for-most-families pick, while the Babyletto Hudson 3-in-1 (~$499) holds 9.9/10 across 2,000+ reviews and is a near-universal premium pick across Babylist, Babygearlab, and What to Expect. For 2026 specifically: the Babyletto Yuzu 8-in-1 is the year's headline launch, transitioning between bassinet, midi crib, full-size crib, toddler bed, daybed, and junior bed (all kits included) — addressing the long-standing complaint that conversion kits are sold separately. The Delta Children Frankie 4-in-1 (~$189) brings GREENGUARD Gold to the budget tier with mid-century styling and includes the daybed/sofa rail in the box. The Storkcraft Sicily 3-in-1 (~$249) is a new arched-slat GREENGUARD Gold option with toddler rail included. Smart cribs (Cradlewise) remain a premium niche with auto-soothing and integrated monitoring. All picks meet current CPSC/ASTM standards. [src1, src2, src7]

Best for Each Use Case

Best Overall: DaVinci Kalani 4-in-1 (~$200-250) — Check price

Wirecutter's "best for most families" pick and one of the most popular convertible cribs on Amazon with thousands of positive reviews. GREENGUARD Gold certified with sustainably sourced New Zealand pine. Converts from crib to toddler bed (rail included), daybed, and full-size bed (rails sold separately). Four adjustable mattress heights. Available in 10+ colors. Wirecutter notes it offers "more versatility and value" at this price. [src3, src6, src8]

Best Longevity (NEW 2026): Babyletto Yuzu 8-in-1 (~$649-799) — Check price

The headline launch of 2026 and the only 8-in-1 convertible on the market. Transitions across three sizes — bassinet, midi crib, midi toddler bed, midi daybed, full-size crib, full-size toddler bed, full-size daybed, and junior bed. All conversion kits and two mattresses are included in the box (a first at this price). Removable wheels for portability. GREENGUARD Gold certified. Adapts to space constraints from newborn through age ~6. The most expensive non-smart pick on this list, but eliminates 5+ separate purchases over the child's life. [src1, src7]

Key Market Trends (2026)

  • Conversion kits now in the box: Babyletto Yuzu and Delta Frankie both ship with their conversion kits included, pressuring competitors. The DaVinci Kalani still sells full-size rails separately at $50-$100. Expect this to flip across the category by 2027. [src1, src7]
  • GREENGUARD Gold becoming table stakes: DaVinci, Babyletto, Storkcraft, and Delta all now offer GREENGUARD Gold across their lines. Cribs without it (Canton, Graco Benton, IKEA Gulliver) are increasingly positioned as budget-tier rather than mainstream. [src1, src3, src7]
  • 8-in-1 convertibility arrives: The Babyletto Yuzu pushes the convertible category further than ever — bassinet through junior bed in one product. Expect imitators by H2 2026. [src1, src7]
  • Sustainable sourcing emphasis: New Zealand pine, Brazilian mahogany, and FSC-certified woods are now standard marketing differentiators across the premium tier. Nestig's Brazilian mahogany Cloud Crib is a notable handmade example. [src3, src6, src7]
  • Smart crib niche stabilizing: Cradlewise has emerged as the dominant smart-crib brand for parents who want auto-soothing past the bassinet stage. Conventional cribs still dominate by volume. [src1, src5]
  • Wirecutter consolidates picks: Wirecutter's 2026 list narrowed to 5 cribs with the DaVinci Kalani as the clear best-for-most pick, simplifying the decision for mainstream buyers. [src8]

Important Caveats

  • Prices are approximate US prices as of April 2026. Sales and regional pricing may vary; the Yuzu in particular varies $150+ between colors and retailers.
  • Conversion kits for toddler bed and full-size bed stages are sold separately on most non-Yuzu/Frankie models ($50-$150 additional).
  • All cribs here meet CPSC and ASTM standards, but used cribs manufactured before 2011 may not comply with current regulations.
  • Standard crib mattress (27.25 x 51.625") is required for full-size cribs. Mini cribs use different dimensions (~38 x 24") — mattresses are not interchangeable.
  • Smart cribs (Cradlewise) are typically rated for 0-24 months only. Plan a separate convertible crib purchase for after that window.
  • Assembly typically requires 30-60 minutes and a Phillips screwdriver. Read instructions carefully and ensure all hardware is included before starting.
  • The Nestig Wave/Cloud cribs are sold direct via Nestig only, not Amazon — pricing and availability differ from this list.
