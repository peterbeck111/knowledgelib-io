Summary

The double stroller market in 2026 saw three meaningful shake-ups. Bugaboo launched the Donkey 6 — replacing the Donkey 5 — with a 50% larger storage basket, expanded breezy panels, and a still-narrow 29.1-inch double width. UPPAbaby released the Minu Duo (~$750), a dedicated 27.4-inch-wide side-by-side that Babylist now ranks "Best Overall" — closing the brand's gap against Bugaboo for families who want side-by-side without paying $1,700. And Thule launched the Urban Glide 3 Double, replacing the 2.0 as the leading premium jogger. [src7, src8]

For tandem buyers, the UPPAbaby Vista V3 (~$1,100) remains the gold standard for convertible single-to-double use, with a 25.7-inch width that fits standard doorways and 30+ configurations including bassinet, infant car seat, and toddler seat combos. The Mockingbird Single-to-Double 3.0 ($549) is the value champion — a 2026 refresh added a fold-out UPF 50+ LegShade and easier magnetic buckles, and the price still undercuts the Vista by roughly half. For travel, the Silver Cross Jet Double (~$650) at under 20 lbs with a suitcase-style fold remains uncontested for portability, with the lighter Zoe Twin V2 (~$529, 23 lbs) as a budget alternative that lacks car-seat support. Joggers should look at the Thule Urban Glide 3 Double (~$1,000) or the durable BOB Revolution Flex 3.0 Duallie (~$650). [src1, src2, src7, src8]

Best for Each Use Case

Best Overall Tandem: UPPAbaby Vista V3 (~$1,100) — Check price

The Vista V3 remains the convertible stroller of choice for 2026, earning top marks across BabyGearLab, Mommyhood101, and Babylist. It features improved all-wheel suspension, a magnetic buckle system for easier fastening, and a massive 30-lb storage basket. At 25.7 inches wide, it fits through standard doorways with ease. Over 30 seating configurations accommodate bassinet, infant car seat, and toddler seat combos, and the rear seat sits raised so neither child feels "stacked." [src1, src2, src7]

Best Overall Side-by-Side (NEW 2026): UPPAbaby Minu Duo (~$750) — Check price

Babylist's new "Best Overall" double stroller pick for 2026. UPPAbaby's first dedicated side-by-side double pairs Minu compactness with full newborn compatibility (with bassinets/adapters), 50 lbs per seat, and a 27.8-inch width that still fits most 30-inch doorways. Independent recline on each seat and travel-system compatibility with the Aria and Mesa infant car seats. Bridges the gap between the $529 Zoe Twin V2 (no car seat support) and the $1,699 Bugaboo Donkey 6. [src7]

Best Luxury Side-by-Side (NEW 2026): Bugaboo Donkey 6 Duo (~$1,699) — Check price

The Donkey 6 (released early 2026) is the latest convertible side-by-side. Single mode is a narrow 23.6-inch frame; converting to duo takes 3 clicks and widens to 29.1 inches — still narrower than most side-by-sides. The 2026 redesign brings a 50% larger underseat basket (up to 33 lbs / 70L), 2× larger breezy panels for airflow, organic-cotton bassinet linings, and a side bag that doubles as a changing bag. 20+ configurations with car seat or wheeled-board adapters. Each seat holds up to 50 lbs. [src7, src8]

Best Value Tandem: Mockingbird Single-to-Double 3.0 ($549) — Check price

The Mockingbird 3.0 (refreshed for 2026) delivers Vista-like versatility at roughly half the price. It offers 22+ configurations (44 with full sibling/board accessories), one-handed fold, a new fold-out UPF 50+ LegShade, redesigned magnetic buckles, and FullShade canopy coverage. Holds up to three children with the toddler seat and ride-along board. Weighs 26.5 lbs as a single, 35 lbs as a double. Reviewers consistently flag it as the clearest answer to "luxury features at half the cost of UPPAbaby." [src2, src6, src7]

Best for Travel: Silver Cross Jet Double (~$650) — Check price

At under 20 lbs with a suitcase-style self-standing fold, the Jet Double is the most travel-friendly double stroller available. Its 27-inch width is Disney-park approved and fits through most narrow passages. Independent reclines on each seat and 50 lbs capacity per seat. [src2, src5]

Best Lightweight (Travel Alternative): Zoe Twin V2 (~$529) — Check price

At just 23 lbs the Zoe Twin V2 is the lightest practical side-by-side double. UPF 50+ four-panel canopy, deep recline, independently controlled seats and canopies, and a robust underseat basket. Disney-size approved and gate-checkable. Caveat: it does not accept any infant car seats — toddler/seat-only use, so unsuitable for newborns. [src7]

Best for Jogging (NEW 2026): Thule Urban Glide 3 Double (~$999) — Check price

Thule's 2026 release of the Urban Glide 3 Double upgrades the 2.0 with a one-handed fold + self-standing design, full air-filled tires, full-suspension system, integrated twist hand brake, and adjustable independent recline with built-in leg rests. Front swivel wheel locks for higher running speeds. From-birth compatible with bassinet or compatible car seats via adapters. The current premium pick across most 2026 round-ups for parents who actually run with the stroller. [src7, src8]

Best Jogger Value: BOB Revolution Flex 3.0 Duallie (~$650) — Check price

Still the smoothest-riding budget jogging double. Mountain-bike-grade suspension, adjustable handlebar, swivel-locking front wheels, and 75% recycled-fabric construction. At 31 inches wide and 35 lbs it is heavier than most competitors but offers unmatched ride quality at $350 less than the Thule. [src2, src4]

Best Compact Side-by-Side Budget: Baby Jogger City Mini GT2 Double (~$500) — Check price

A more affordable side-by-side alternative that folds compactly to 30x31x15 inches. All-terrain tires and hand-operated parking brake provide good everyday versatility. Works well for urban families who want side-by-side convenience without the Bugaboo or UPPAbaby price tag, but at 30 inches wide it is the borderline case for standard 30-inch doorways. [src2, src3]

Decision Logic

If budget < $600

→ Mockingbird Single-to-Double 3.0 ($549) for tandem, or Zoe Twin V2 (~$529) for lightweight side-by-side without car-seat needs. Mockingbird offers 22+ configurations and is the only sub-$600 option that competes with the Vista V3 on features. [src2, src6, src7]

If primary use is air travel

→ Silver Cross Jet Double (~$650) at under 20 lbs with suitcase fold — no other double stroller comes close for portability. Zoe Twin V2 is a viable cheaper alternative if you do not need car-seat compatibility. Both fit airplane aisles and Disney park turnstiles. [src2, src5, src7]

If user wants side-by-side seating

→ UPPAbaby Minu Duo (~$750) for premium-but-not-luxury, Bugaboo Donkey 6 (~$1,699) for full luxury with the narrowest convertible width, or Baby Jogger City Mini GT2 Double (~$500) for budget. The Minu Duo is new in 2026 and now Babylist's "Best Overall" pick — the most balanced side-by-side at its price. [src7, src8]

If user plans to jog with the stroller

→ Thule Urban Glide 3 Double (~$999) for the premium pick — full air-filled tires, suspension, twist hand brake, self-standing fold. BOB Revolution Flex 3.0 Duallie (~$650) for the value alternative if you can tolerate the heavier 35-lb frame. No tandem stroller matches either on uneven surfaces. [src2, src4, src7]

If user has a 30-inch standard doorway as a hard constraint

→ Pick a tandem (Vista V3 at 25.7" or Mockingbird 3.0 at 25.5") or the UPPAbaby Minu Duo (27.8") or Silver Cross Jet Double (27"). Avoid the Baby Jogger City Mini GT2 (30"), Bugaboo Donkey 6 Duo (29.1" — usually fits but tight), BOB / Thule joggers (31"+), and Bombi Bēbee (35.75"). [src7, src8]

If user needs a single now and a double later (convertible)

→ UPPAbaby Vista V3 (tandem path) or Bugaboo Donkey 6 (side-by-side path) — both are designed for single-mode use first and convert with an add-on second seat. Avoid dedicated doubles like Mockingbird's bundle-only single-to-double or the Zoe Twin V2 (always a double). [src7, src8]

Default recommendation (unknown requirements)

→ UPPAbaby Vista V3 (~$1,100) for tandem buyers, UPPAbaby Minu Duo (~$750) for side-by-side buyers without strong luxury preference. Vista V3 remains the safest tandem pick across 4+ review sites; Minu Duo is the safest side-by-side under $1,000 in 2026. [src1, src2, src7, src8]

Key Market Trends (2026)

  • Bugaboo Donkey 6 launch (Q1 2026): Replaces Donkey 5 with a 50% larger basket (up to 33 lbs/70L), 2× larger breezy panels, organic-cotton bassinet, and a still-narrow 29.1-inch double width. Single-mode width unchanged at 23.6". [src7, src8]
  • UPPAbaby enters dedicated side-by-side category: The Minu Duo (~$750) is UPPAbaby's first true side-by-side double — Babylist named it "Best Overall." Pressures the Bugaboo Donkey 6 from below and the Zoe Twin V2 from above. [src7]
  • Thule Urban Glide 3 Double: Launched 2026, replaces the 2.0 as the premium jogger. Self-standing fold, twist hand brake, adjustable leg rests. Still at the ~$1,000 tier. [src7, src8]
  • Single-to-double convertible dominance continues: Mockingbird 3.0, UPPAbaby Vista V3, and Bugaboo Donkey 6 are all sold first as singles and convert to doubles. Direct-from-birth doubles (Zoe Twin V2, Bombi Bēbee) are now positioned as travel/budget specialists. [src1, src2, src7]
  • Magnetic buckle systems are now baseline at $500+: UPPAbaby, Mockingbird 3.0, and Bugaboo all use magnetic buckles for one-handed fastening. Traditional clip buckles are increasingly limited to sub-$300 strollers. [src2, src6]
  • Ultra-lightweight travel doubles (sub-25 lbs) maturing: Silver Cross Jet Double (under 20 lbs) and Zoe Twin V2 (23 lbs) prove that double strollers can be airline-friendly. The Zoe in particular has held its $529 price for the V2 generation. [src2, src5, src7]
  • Value disruption from D2C brands holding: Mockingbird's $549 price for a Vista-competitive feature set continues to force premium brands to differentiate on materials, suspension, and finish rather than configurations alone. [src2, src6, src7]

Important Caveats

  • Prices are US MSRP as of April 2026. Sale events, bundles, and regional pricing may differ; the Donkey 6 in particular shows wide retailer-to-retailer variation in early 2026.
  • Car seat adapters are typically sold separately ($30-$80) and are brand-specific. The UPPAbaby Minu Duo car-seat adapter is a separate $90+ purchase. Verify compatibility before purchasing.
  • Side-by-side strollers wider than 30 inches may not fit through standard doorways (30 inches) or narrow store aisles. The Bombi Bēbee (35.75 in) and the BOB/Thule joggers (31 in+) are the most likely to be excluded.
  • Several travel-friendly side-by-sides (Zoe Twin V2, some Bombi configurations) do not accept infant car seats. They are not appropriate for newborns until the child can sit up unsupported (~6 months).
  • Weight listed is stroller only — add 5-15 lbs for bassinets, second seats, and accessories. The Bugaboo Donkey 6 in full duo-bassinet configuration approaches 40 lbs.
