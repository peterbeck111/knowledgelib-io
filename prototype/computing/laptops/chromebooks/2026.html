Important Caveats

  • Prices fluctuate frequently; street prices are often 15-30% below MSRP during sales (the Acer Plus 514 dropped from $350 to $249 at some retailers). All prices listed are approximate current US retail as of April 2026.
  • Chromebooks run ChromeOS, not Windows. While Android and Linux (Crostini) app support is available, specialized Windows software will not run natively.
  • RAM and storage are typically not user-upgradeable in Chromebooks. Buy the configuration you need from the start.
  • Battery life figures combine independent test results (Engadget, Tom's Guide, RTINGS video playback tests) and manufacturer claims; real-world usage varies with screen brightness, tabs open, and workload.
  • The ASUS CM14 Flip and ASUS CX15 with 4GB RAM are limited to basic tasks (5-10 browser tabs maximum). For serious multitasking, 8GB+ RAM is strongly recommended.
  • ChromeOS automatic update expiration (AUE) varies: most 2024-2025 models receive updates through 2034-2035, but the Acer 516 GE expires June 2032. Always verify AUE before purchasing.
  • Gemini AI features in ChromeOS 144 are currently limited to Chromebook Plus devices only. The free 12-month Google One AI Premium bundle applies only to new Chromebook Plus purchases in the US at launch.
  • CES 2026-announced models are now shipping: the Lenovo Chromebook Plus i 15 ($469.99 starting) launched Q2 2026 with an optional 120Hz touchscreen variant; the HP Chromebook Plus x360 14 refresh is on sale; and the Lenovo Chromebook Plus 2-in-1 14" Intel reached retail in April 2026. Independent third-party testing for some configurations is still emerging — early Chrome Unboxed reviews note the new Lenovo 2-in-1 is best at sale prices (~$429) rather than its $579 MSRP.
  • Reviewer rankings have diverged: Tom's Guide (April 14, 2026 update) now ranks the Lenovo Chromebook Plus 14 as #1 overall, while RTINGS, PCWorld, and Engadget continue to lead with the Acer Chromebook Plus Spin 714. Both are defensible top picks — the difference is OLED display priority versus aluminum-chassis-and-Thunderbolt-4 priority.
