# Best Chromebooks (2026)

## Summary

The Chromebook landscape in April 2026 is defined by three major shifts: Google's Chromebook Plus certification has raised the floor on hardware quality, ChromeOS 144 (January 28, 2026) brought native Gemini 3 integration to every Plus-certified device, and the CES 2026 wave from HP, Lenovo, ASUS, and Acer added Wi-Fi 7 and brighter displays across the mainstream tier. The best overall Chromebook is the Acer Chromebook Plus Spin 714 (~$699) for its Intel Core Ultra 5 115U processor, aluminum build, Thunderbolt 4, and versatile 360-degree hinge with stylus. The best value pick is the Acer Chromebook Plus 514 (~$249-350) which delivers an Intel Core 3 N355 processor, 8GB RAM, a 512GB SSD, and nearly 13 hours of battery life at a remarkably low price. [src1, src2, src4, src8]

For those willing to invest in a premium experience, the Lenovo Chromebook Plus 14 (~$649-749) features a stunning 14-inch OLED display with 100% DCI-P3 color, a MediaTek Kompanio Ultra 910 with integrated NPU, and 14+ hours of real-world battery life. Engadget measured 14 hours of video playback, while Tom's Guide called it "simply the best" Chromebook available. The Samsung Galaxy Chromebook Plus (~$699) is the thinnest option at just 0.46 inches and 2.58 lbs with an AMOLED display. [src1, src4, src5]

Budget buyers have more choices than ever. The ASUS Chromebook CX15 starts at just $159 with a 15.6-inch FHD display and MIL-STD 810H durability, while the ASUS Chromebook CM14 Flip (~$299) offers a spill-resistant keyboard, antimicrobial coating, and 14-hour battery life for students and kids. For cloud gaming, the Acer Chromebook Plus 516 GE (~$549-649) pairs a 16-inch 2.5K 120Hz display with an Ethernet port for stable latency on GeForce NOW. The large-screen wave from CES 2026 is now shipping: the Lenovo Chromebook Plus i 15 (~$469.99 starting) is on sale with a 15.3-inch 2K 400-nit display, Intel Core 3 N355, 8GB LPDDR5, and an optional 120Hz touchscreen variant; the refreshed HP Chromebook Plus x360 14 (Intel Core 3 N355, 14" 2K 1920x1200 16:10) is shipping after its February 2026 release; and Lenovo's new Chromebook Plus 2-in-1 14" Intel ($579 MSRP) earned a 4-star Chrome Unboxed review on April 23, 2026 — though its real value emerges only when discounted to ~$429. Note: Tom's Guide's April 14, 2026 update now ranks the Lenovo Chromebook Plus 14 as the #1 overall pick, while RTINGS, PCWorld, and Engadget continue to favor the Acer Spin 714 — choose between them based on whether you prioritize OLED display (Lenovo) or aluminum chassis with Thunderbolt 4 (Acer). ## Important Caveats

- Prices fluctuate frequently; street prices are often 15-30% below MSRP during sales (the Acer Plus 514 dropped from $350 to $249 at some retailers). All prices listed are approximate current US retail as of April 2026.
