Best for Each Use Case

Best Overall: LG Gram Pro 17 (2026) (~$1,900) — Check price

The LG Gram Pro 17 is the rare laptop that delivers both a large 17-inch display and genuine portability at just 3.26 pounds. Its Intel Core Ultra 9 285H processor and NVIDIA RTX 5050 GPU handle demanding creative and productivity workloads, while the 90Wh battery provides up to 25.5 hours of video playback. The new Aerominum chassis material combines magnesium and aluminum alloy for scratch resistance and military-grade durability. [src3, src7]

Best Budget: HP Laptop 17 (2026) (~$550) — Check price

For users who need a large 17.3-inch display without breaking the bank, the HP Laptop 17 delivers reliable performance with AMD Ryzen 5 processors, 16GB RAM, and 1TB SSD configurations starting around $550. It handles web browsing, document editing, video streaming, and basic multitasking with ease, though the integrated graphics limit creative and gaming workloads. [src1, src2]

Best for Gaming (Value): ASUS ROG Strix G17 (~$1,400) — Check price

The ROG Strix G17 pairs AMD's 16-core Ryzen 9 7945HX with NVIDIA's RTX 4070 (140W TGP) and a QHD 240Hz display, delivering smooth high-refresh-rate gaming at 1440p. Conductonaut Extreme liquid metal on the CPU and NVIDIA Advanced Optimus with MUX switch provide 5-10% better gaming performance than laptops without direct GPU output. [src6, src1]

Best for Gaming (Next-Gen): Acer Nitro V 17 AI (~$1,430) — Check price

The Acer Nitro V 17 AI brings NVIDIA's Blackwell-architecture RTX 5070 GPU with DLSS 4 neural rendering and 798 AI TOPS to a budget-friendly 17.3-inch chassis. The AMD Ryzen 7 260 processor with 50 TOPS NPU enables Copilot+ PC features. Its QHD 165Hz display covers 100% of the DCI-P3 color gamut. [src5]

Best for Portability: LG Gram 17 (2026) (~$1,500) — Check price

At just 3.2 pounds, the LG Gram 17 remains the lightest 17-inch laptop available, weighing less than many 14-inch competitors. The Intel Core Ultra 7 355 processor with Intel Arc integrated graphics handles productivity workloads efficiently, and the 77Wh battery delivers over 12 hours of real-world use. [src2, src3]

Best for Creative Work: HP Envy 17 (~$1,000) — Check price

The HP Envy 17 combines a 17.3-inch touchscreen display with an Intel Core Ultra 7 processor and NVIDIA RTX 3050 GPU in a premium aluminum and glass chassis. The touchscreen supports stylus input for illustration and photo editing, and the overall build quality makes it pleasant for long creative sessions. [src4]

Decision Logic

If budget < $800

→ The HP Laptop 17 is the only competitive option in this range with a 17-inch display, offering AMD Ryzen 5 processors, 16GB RAM, and 1TB SSD from around $550. Accept integrated graphics and FHD resolution as trade-offs. [src1]

If primary use is gaming

→ Choose between the ASUS ROG Strix G17 (~$1,400, proven RTX 4070, QHD 240Hz) for immediate value or the Acer Nitro V 17 AI (~$1,430, next-gen RTX 5070, QHD 165Hz) for Blackwell architecture and DLSS 4 support. [src5, src6]

If portability is critical

→ The LG Gram Pro 17 (3.26 lbs with RTX 5050) or the LG Gram 17 (3.2 lbs, integrated graphics) are the only 17-inch laptops under 4 pounds. No other manufacturer comes close to this weight class at this screen size. [src3, src7]

If primary use is content creation

→ Prioritize color accuracy and touchscreen over raw GPU power. The HP Envy 17 offers a touchscreen with stylus support at ~$1,000, while the LG Gram Pro 17 provides a more powerful RTX 5050 GPU with a color-accurate 2560x1600 display at ~$1,900. [src4, src7]

Default recommendation

→ The LG Gram Pro 17 at ~$1,900 offers the best balance of performance, portability, and display quality for most users who value a 17-inch screen. If budget is a concern, the HP Envy 17 at ~$1,000 delivers solid all-around performance. [src3, src4]

Key Market Trends (2026)

  • Shrinking 17-inch category: Manufacturers are increasingly shifting to 16-inch and 18-inch form factors, making true 17-inch options scarcer than in previous years. [src1, src2]
  • Aerominum and advanced materials: LG's new Aerominum chassis material enables sub-3.3-pound 17-inch laptops with dedicated GPUs, previously impossible at this screen size. [src3]
  • RTX 50-series Blackwell arrives in 17-inch: NVIDIA's Blackwell architecture with DLSS 4 neural rendering is now available in 17-inch gaming laptops. [src5]
  • AI/NPU integration: Both Intel Core Ultra and AMD Ryzen 7000 processors now include NPUs delivering 40-50+ TOPS for on-device AI tasks. [src3, src5]
