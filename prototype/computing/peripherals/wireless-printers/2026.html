Summary

The wireless printer market in 2026 is dominated by ink tank (supertank) models that have fundamentally changed the economics of home and small-office printing. Epson's EcoTank, Canon's MegaTank, Brother's INKvestment, and HP's Smart Tank lines all deliver per-page costs under 1 cent for black-and-white text and around 2-4 cents for color, compared to 5-10+ cents per page with traditional cartridge printers [src1, src3, src6]. For most home and small-office users, a tank-based all-in-one inkjet is now the best overall value, while laser printers remain the top choice for high-volume monochrome document printing and environments where print speed and text sharpness are paramount [src4, src5].

The Epson EcoTank ET-4950 stands out as the best overall wireless printer for home offices, combining fast 18 ppm black print speeds, auto duplex, ADF, fax, and enough bundled ink for approximately 6,600 black or 5,500 color pages — roughly three years of printing for most households [src1, src3, src5]. Epson's seventh-generation EcoTank ET-3950 (~$420) is the premium mid-range pick, featuring an upgraded duty cycle twice that of its predecessor, PrecisionCore Heat-Free permanent printhead, and 18 ppm printing with a 2.4-inch color touchscreen and ADF — RTINGS now ranks it among its top home printers [src3, src5, src9]. The EcoTank ET-2980 ($300) remains the best value mid-range tank, printing 50% faster than its ET-2850 predecessor at 15 ppm [src6, src8]. New for the April 2026 update, RTINGS added the Brother MFC-J1360DW (~$130) as a budget all-in-one inkjet pick (16/9 ppm, auto duplex, 20-page ADF) and the HP Smart Tank 7602 (~$349) is now Tom's Guide's top student/home choice with 2-3 years of bundled ink [src1, src2, src4]. For users who prioritize speed, crisp text, and low total cost of ownership on high-volume mono printing, the Brother HL-L2460DW laser printer delivers 36 ppm at about 3.1 cents per page and costs under $180 [src4, src5].

Budget buyers have excellent options in the Canon PIXMA G3270 MegaTank (~$230) and the Epson EcoTank ET-2850 (~$230), both delivering thousands of pages from bundled ink bottles and solid all-in-one functionality [src2, src6, src7]. The HP DeskJet 2855e (~$80) is RTINGS's new pick for cheapest reliable wireless printer with AI-enabled setup and HP+ Instant Ink integration [src1, src7]. Color laser users should look at the Canon imageCLASS MF665Cdw (~$450), which RTINGS rates as the best overall wireless printer for its outstanding document quality, 26 ppm speed, and comprehensive 4-in-1 feature set [src1, src4]. For high-volume color laser offices, the Brother MFC-L8905CDW (~$600) delivers 33 ppm color with a 7-inch touchscreen, 70-sheet ADF, and super-high-yield toner options [src4, src5]. For mono laser users who want the same Brother reliability in a smaller all-in-one form factor, the new RTINGS pick Brother MFC-L2820DW (~$300) prints at 34 ppm with print/scan/copy/fax in a compact 2.7-inch touchscreen package [src4, src9]. For photo printing, the Epson EcoTank Photo ET-8550 offers 6-color output on paper up to A3+ size at roughly 4 cents per 4x6 print — a fraction of cartridge-based photo printer costs [src1, src6].

Best for Each Use Case

Best Overall: Epson EcoTank ET-4950 (~$350) — Check price

The ET-4950 is the most well-rounded wireless printer for home offices in 2026. It combines fast 18 ppm black printing, auto duplex, a 30-sheet ADF, fax capability, and a 2.4-inch color touchscreen in a single unit. Its PrecisionCore Heat-Free printhead is designed to last the life of the printer, and the bundled ink covers approximately 6,600 black and 5,500 color pages — Epson claims this is equivalent to about 65 traditional ink cartridges. Running costs are up to 95% lower than cartridge-based alternatives. [src1, src3, src5]

Best Premium Mid-Range Tank: Epson EcoTank ET-3950 (~$420) — Check price

The ET-3950 is Epson's seventh-generation EcoTank flagship below the ET-4950, featuring a duty cycle twice that of its predecessor and a PrecisionCore Heat-Free permanent printhead engineered for the life of the printer. It prints at 18 ppm black with a 2.4-inch color touchscreen, ADF, auto duplex, and bundled ink for approximately 6,600 black and 5,500 color pages. At roughly 0.3 cents per mono page and 2.7 cents per color page, its running costs are under 5% of a traditional cartridge inkjet. RTINGS ranks it among the best home printers of 2026. [src3, src5, src9]

Best Value Mid-Range Tank: Epson EcoTank ET-2980 (~$300) — Check price

The ET-2980 is another seventh-generation EcoTank and a significant upgrade over the ET-2850. It prints 50% faster at 15 ppm black/8 ppm color, includes a 1.44-inch color display, auto duplex, and enough ink for approximately 6,600 black and 5,500 color pages (roughly 3 years). The permanent printhead is engineered for the printer's lifetime, and uniquely keyed EcoFit bottles prevent ink from drying out. At $300, it hits the sweet spot of speed, features, and long-term value for users who do not need the ADF. [src6, src8]

Best for Students / Hybrid Home (NEW): HP Smart Tank 7602 (~$349) — Check price

Tom's Guide's top student and home pick. Refillable tanks include up to 8,000 color or 6,000 black pages of HP Original ink in the box (HP claims 2-3 years of typical use). Prints at 15 ppm black/9 ppm color with dual-band Wi-Fi for fast wireless transfers, ADF, auto duplex, and fax. Color document and photo quality are good with consistent tones. Setup runs through the HP Smart app and HP+ activation enables 3-month Instant Ink trial. Notably, this is HP's main answer to Epson EcoTank in the home/student segment. [src2, src5, src7]

Best Budget Tank Printer: Canon PIXMA G3270 MegaTank (~$230) — Check price

The G3270 is one of the best home printer values available. It prints up to 6,000 black and 7,700 color pages from the bundled ink bottles, offering an extremely low per-page cost. Print resolution reaches 4800 x 1200 dpi, and setup is straightforward via Wi-Fi. The main trade-off is the lack of auto duplex printing and ADF, which means manual page flipping for two-sided prints and feeding multi-page scans one at a time. For basic home printing with minimal ongoing cost, it is hard to beat. [src2, src6, src7]

Best Cheapest Wireless (NEW): HP DeskJet 2855e (~$80) — Check price

RTINGS's new pick for entry-level wireless printing. The DeskJet 2855e is HP's cheapest current AIO with print/scan/copy, AI-enabled setup via HP Smart, and 3-month HP+ Instant Ink trial included. Print speeds are slow at 7.5/5.5 ppm and ink cost is high without a subscription, but for users who print fewer than 30 pages per month and need a sub-$100 wireless option, it is the most reliable option below the tank-printer price floor. [src1, src7]

Best Budget Cartridge AIO (NEW): Brother MFC-J1360DW (~$130) — Check price

RTINGS added this as its top budget all-in-one inkjet pick. The MFC-J1360DW is a compact wireless AIO with auto duplex printing, a 20-page ADF, 1.8-inch color display, and Brother's Refresh ink subscription option. Prints at 16 ppm black and 9 ppm color, with ink costs of about 10.7 cents per page on standard cartridges. Best fit for users who print 50-100 pages per month and want auto-duplex + ADF features without paying tank-printer prices. [src1, src5]

Best Budget Mono Laser: Brother HL-L2460DW (~$170) — Check price

With dual paper trays (250 + 50 sheets), Ethernet, and NFC tap-to-print, it is built for workgroup environments handling 500+ pages per month. [src4, src5]

Best Mono Laser All-in-One (Speed): HP LaserJet Pro MFP 4101fdw (~$430) — Check price

The 4101fdw is the fastest printer on this list at 42 ppm, with a dual-scan ADF that copies or scans both sides of pages automatically. It handles print, scan, copy, and fax, and supports up to 350 sheets across two input trays. Text printing costs approximately 3.7 cents per page. This is a strong choice for home offices or small teams that print high volumes of mono documents and need fax capability. [src4, src5, src7]

Best for Photos: Epson EcoTank Photo ET-8550 (~$600) — Check price

The ET-8550 uses a 6-color Claria ink system (including photo black and gray) in a refillable tank format, printing on paper up to A3+ (13 x 19 inches) and even 2-meter banners. Photo quality approaches professional-grade output with vibrant colors and smooth tonal transitions at up to 5760 x 1440 dpi resolution. At roughly 4 cents per 4x6 photo compared to 40 cents with cartridge printers, it is dramatically cheaper to operate. The bundled ink is enough for approximately 2,300 4x6 prints. [src1, src6]

Decision Logic

If budget < $100 and print volume is low (<30 pages/month)

→ The HP DeskJet 2855e (~$80) is the only sub-$100 wireless AIO worth recommending. Slow (7.5/5.5 ppm) and high cost-per-page on cartridges, but fine for occasional use with HP+ Instant Ink subscription. Avoid if you print regularly — ink costs add up fast. [src1, src7]

If budget is $100-$200 and color is needed

→ The Brother MFC-J1360DW (~$130) is the new RTINGS budget AIO pick: auto duplex, 20-page ADF, 16/9 ppm. For mono-only at this price tier, the Brother HL-L2460DW (~$170) at 36 ppm with 3.1 cents/page running costs is the better value. [src1, src4, src5]

If primary use is home office documents with occasional color

→ The Epson EcoTank ET-4950 (~$350) is the default recommendation: fast, full-featured (ADF, duplex, fax), and <1 cent/page mono running costs. It handles both mono documents and color graphics well. [src1, src3]

If user is a student or hybrid worker who prints regularly

→ The HP Smart Tank 7602 (~$349) is Tom's Guide's top student pick with 2-3 years of bundled ink, dual-band Wi-Fi, and HP Smart app integration. Or step up to the Epson EcoTank ET-2980 (~$300) for slightly faster mono printing with no HP+ account requirement. [src2, src6]

If budget is $300-$450 and user wants the best tank AIO

→ The Epson EcoTank ET-3950 (~$420) is the premium pick with 18 ppm, ADF, 2.4-inch touchscreen, and 0.3 cents/page mono running costs. The Epson EcoTank ET-2980 (~$300) saves $120 with 15 ppm and no ADF. Both include 3 years of bundled ink. [src3, src6]

If print volume > 500 pages/month

→ Prioritize laser over inkjet for duty cycle and speed. The HP LaserJet Pro MFP 4101fdw (42 ppm, dual-scan ADF) handles high-volume mono; the Brother MFC-L2820DW (~$300, 34 ppm) is a smaller, cheaper compact mono laser AIO; the Brother MFC-L8905CDW (33 ppm, super-high-yield toner, 70-sheet ADF) is best for high-volume color. [src4, src5, src9]

If user needs photo printing

→ The Epson EcoTank Photo ET-8550 (~$600) is the clear winner with 6-color ink, A3+ format, and ~4 cents per 4x6 print. No other printer in this comparison matches its photo output quality. [src1, src6]

If user wants minimal ink/toner hassle

→ Tank printers (EcoTank, MegaTank, Smart Tank) include 2-3 years of ink and use open refill bottles — no cartridge DRM, no subscriptions. The Epson EcoTank ET-2980 (~$300) offers the best speed-to-value ratio in this category. [src5, src6, src8]

Default recommendation

→ For unknown requirements, the Epson EcoTank ET-4950 (~$350) is the safest pick: it covers printing, scanning, copying, and faxing with ultra-low running costs and enough bundled ink for years of typical home use. [src1, src3]

Key Market Trends (Q2 2026)

  • Tank printers are now mainstream: Refillable ink tank systems from Epson (EcoTank), Canon (MegaTank), Brother (INKvestment), and HP (Smart Tank) have moved from niche to dominant for home and small-office use. Running costs are 80-95% lower than traditional cartridge printers, with bundled ink often lasting 2-3 years. Epson's seventh-generation EcoTank lineup (ET-4950, ET-3950, ET-2980) features PrecisionCore Heat-Free permanent printheads engineered for the life of the printer, underscoring how mature this technology has become. [src1, src3, src6, src8]
  • HP Smart Tank reaches mainstream traction: Tom's Guide now lists the HP Smart Tank 7602 as its top home/student pick, marking the first time HP's tank line has reached parity with Epson EcoTank in mainstream review rankings. HP's 2-3 years of bundled ink and dual-band Wi-Fi finally close the gap with Epson on home segment recommendations. [src2]
  • RTINGS expanded its top picks for Q2 2026: New additions to RTINGS rankings include the Brother MFC-J1360DW (budget AIO inkjet, $130), Brother MFC-L2820DW (compact mono laser AIO, $300), and HP DeskJet 2855e (cheapest wireless AIO, $80). The expansion reflects more granular price tiers in the wireless printer market. [src1, src4, src9]
  • Laser remains king for mono volume: Despite tank printer advances, monochrome laser printers still offer the fastest print speeds (34-42 ppm), sharpest text quality, and most reliable high-duty-cycle performance for offices prioritizing black-and-white documents. [src4, src5]
  • Wi-Fi 6 and mobile printing standardized: Newer models like the Brother MFC-L8395CDW include Wi-Fi 6 and dual-band connectivity. Mobile printing via AirPrint, Mopria, and manufacturer apps is universal across all price tiers. [src1, src3]
  • AI-enabled setup spreads downmarket: HP's DeskJet 2855e and Smart Tank 7602 both market "AI-enabled" setup and workflow assistance via HP Smart app — features that previously appeared only in business-class equipment. Brother and Epson have countered with simplified app-based setup but no comparable AI branding. [src1, src2]
  • Subscription and DRM concerns persist: HP continues to push its Instant Ink subscription model and uses HP+ firmware to block third-party cartridges on select models (the DeskJet 2855e ships HP+ on by default). Tank printers from Epson, Canon, and Brother avoid these restrictions, using open refill bottles. Buyers increasingly favor cartridge-free systems to avoid these lock-in practices. [src5, src7]
  • Color laser prices falling: Models like the Brother MFC-L3780CDW (~$400) and Canon imageCLASS MF665Cdw (~$450) bring color laser all-in-one functionality below $500, making color laser accessible to home offices for the first time. [src1, src4, src8]

Important Caveats

  • Prices shown are approximate US retail prices as of April 2026 and may vary by retailer, region, and current promotions.
  • Print speeds are manufacturer-rated ISO ppm figures; real-world speeds depend on document complexity, connection type, and settings.
  • Tank/supertank printers have higher upfront costs than entry-level cartridge printers but dramatically lower per-page costs — break-even typically occurs within 6-12 months of regular printing.
  • Photo quality comparisons are based on 4x6 and 8x10 prints on manufacturer-recommended photo paper; results may vary with third-party media.
  • Laser printers generally produce sharper text but inkjet tank printers offer better color reproduction and photo output at lower per-page cost.
  • Ink/toner cost-per-page figures are based on manufacturer yield ratings using ISO standards; actual costs may vary with document coverage and settings.
  • HP+ activation on DeskJet and Smart Tank models requires an HP account and may permanently lock the printer to HP Original ink — review terms before activation if you intend to use third-party cartridges.
