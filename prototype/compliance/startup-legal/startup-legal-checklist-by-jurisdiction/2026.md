# Startup Legal Checklist by Jurisdiction

## Purpose

This recipe produces a jurisdiction-specific legal formation checklist for a new startup, covering entity type selection, formation filing, tax registrations, licenses, permits, and ongoing compliance obligations. The output is a step-by-step checklist with filing links, costs, deadlines, and required documents tailored to the chosen jurisdiction.

## Prerequisites

- [ ] **Business description** — what the startup does, target market, and planned operations
- [ ] **Founder information** — names, addresses, identification (SSN/ITIN for US, NI number for UK)
- [ ] **Operating jurisdiction(s)** — where the business will be headquartered and where it will have customers
- [ ] **Fundraising intent** — determines entity type recommendation (VC = Delaware C-Corp; bootstrap = LLC)
- [ ] **$100-$1,500 budget** — for filing fees, registered agent, and legal documents

## Constraints

- Entity formation requirements differ substantially by jurisdiction. Always verify current requirements with the relevant Secretary of State or Companies House — rules change frequently. [src1]
- Most US states require foreign qualification if you incorporate in one state but operate in another. A Delaware C-Corp operating in California must register in both states. [src5]
- US Beneficial Ownership Information (BOI) reporting under the Corporate Transparency Act requires most entities to report beneficial owners to FinCEN within 90 days of formation (2024+ formations). [src6]
- Tax registration deadlines: most US states require state tax board registration within 30-90 days of formation. Missing deadlines triggers penalties. [src4]
- Entity type directly impacts fundraising — most US VCs require Delaware C-Corp. LLCs require conversion before institutional fundraising. [src3]

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Select Entity Type and Jurisdiction

**Duration**: 30 minutes (decision)
**Tool**: Decision framework

Apply this decision logic:
- **Raising VC?** → Delaware C-Corp (required by most institutional investors)
- **Bootstrapping, US?** → LLC in home state or Wyoming (no state income tax, lowest fees)
- **Bootstrapping, UK?** → UK Ltd via Companies House
- **Non-US founder needing US entity?** → Stripe Atlas (Delaware C-Corp, handles EIN + bank account)

Key jurisdiction comparison:
- **Delaware**: $89 filing + $300/yr franchise tax minimum. Gold standard for VC. Court of Chancery specializes in corporate law.
- **Wyoming**: $100 filing + $60/yr annual report. No state income tax. Best for bootstrapped companies.
- **California**: $70 filing + $800/yr minimum franchise tax. Required if physically operating in CA regardless of incorporation state.
- **UK**: GBP 12 online filing. 24-hour turnaround. Corporation tax 25% (19% for profits under GBP 50K).

**Verify**: Entity type aligns with fundraising plan. Jurisdiction selected matches operational needs.
**If failed**: Consult with startup attorney if edge case (dual-entity structures, holding companies).

### Step 2: File Formation Documents

**Duration**: 30-60 minutes (filing) + 1-5 days (processing)
**Tool**: State filing portal, Stripe Atlas, or Companies House

**US C-Corp (Delaware):**
1. File Certificate of Incorporation with Delaware Division of Corporations ($89 standard, $189 24-hour, $589 same-day)
2. Appoint registered agent in Delaware (required — $39-$299/yr)
3. Adopt bylaws (template from Clerky or your attorney)
4. Issue founder stock (83(b) election must be filed within 30 days)
5. Hold organizational board meeting (can be by written consent)

**US LLC (Wyoming):**
1. File Articles of Organization with Wyoming Secretary of State ($100)
2. Appoint registered agent in Wyoming
3. Draft Operating Agreement (required by most banks, not filed with state)
4. Obtain EIN from IRS

**UK Ltd:**
1. File via Companies House online portal (GBP 12)
2. Provide: company name, registered office, director(s), shareholder(s), SIC code
3. Receive Certificate of Incorporation (usually within 24 hours)
4. Register for Corporation Tax with HMRC within 3 months

**Verify**: Certificate of Formation/Incorporation received. Entity appears in state/national registry.
**If failed**: Check for name conflicts. Resolve any filing rejections (usually name unavailability or missing information).

### Step 3: Obtain Tax Identification Numbers

**Duration**: 15 minutes (US online) to 4-6 weeks (mail)
**Tool**: IRS.gov, state tax portals

**US — Federal:**
- Apply for EIN at irs.gov/ein — free, instant online (requires SSN/ITIN of responsible party)
- If no SSN: apply by mail/fax using Form SS-4 (4-6 weeks)

**US — State:**
- Register with state tax authority (Department of Revenue or Franchise Tax Board)
- Sales tax permit if selling taxable goods/services (required in 45 US states)
- Payroll tax registration if hiring employees

**UK:**
- HMRC Corporation Tax registration (within 3 months of incorporation)
- VAT registration if turnover exceeds GBP 90,000 (or voluntary registration)
- PAYE registration if hiring employees

**Verify**: EIN confirmation letter received. State tax registration numbers assigned. Sales tax permit active if needed.
**If failed**: For EIN issues, call IRS Business line (800-829-4933). For state issues, contact state revenue department directly.

### Step 4: Obtain Business Licenses and Permits

**Duration**: 1-3 hours research + 1-4 weeks processing
**Tool**: SBA Permit Me tool, local government portals

Required licenses vary by location and industry:
- **Federal**: Most startups need no federal license unless in regulated industry (alcohol, firearms, agriculture, aviation)
- **State**: Professional licenses (accounting, legal, medical), contractor licenses, seller's permits
- **Local/County**: Business license or tax certificate, zoning permit, home occupation permit (if home-based), sign permit

Use SBA's Permit Me tool (sba.gov) to identify specific requirements by state and industry.

Common deadlines:
- Business license: before conducting business (many cities require within 30 days)
- Seller's permit: before first taxable sale
- Professional license: before practicing (varies by profession)

**Verify**: All required licenses identified and applications submitted. No outstanding permit requirements.
**If failed**: Contact local Small Business Development Center (SBDC) for free guidance. Check with city/county clerk's office.

### Step 5: Complete Post-Formation Requirements

**Duration**: 1-2 hours
**Tool**: Various government portals

Post-formation compliance checklist:
- [ ] Open business bank account (requires EIN + formation documents)
- [ ] File BOI report with FinCEN (within 90 days for new 2024+ formations)
- [ ] Set up accounting/bookkeeping system
- [ ] Obtain business insurance (general liability at minimum)
- [ ] File 83(b) election with IRS within 30 days (C-Corp founders receiving restricted stock)
- [ ] Register for state unemployment insurance if hiring
- [ ] Verify compliance calendar for annual filings (annual report, franchise tax, etc.)

**Verify**: Bank account open. BOI filed. Insurance active. Calendar set for ongoing filings.
**If failed**: Banks often require in-person visit for business accounts. BOI late filing triggers $500/day penalty.

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Entity name rejected | Name already in use or restricted word | Search state business name database, choose alternative, consider DBA |
| EIN application rejected | SSN/ITIN mismatch or duplicate filing | Verify responsible party info, call IRS at 800-829-4933 |
| Foreign qualification missed | Operating in state other than formation state | File foreign qualification immediately — penalties accrue daily in some states |
| 83(b) election missed | Not filed within 30 days of stock issuance | Cannot be corrected — significant tax consequences. Consult tax attorney |
| BOI report missed | Not filed within 90 days | File immediately — $500/day penalty up to $10,000 under CTA |
| Sales tax permit missing | Selling taxable goods without registration | Register immediately, may need to pay back taxes + interest |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Incorporating in Delaware for a bootstrapped local business
Delaware C-Corp adds unnecessary complexity and cost ($300+/yr franchise tax, dual-state filing) for businesses not seeking VC funding. [src3]

### Correct: Match entity type to business model
Bootstrapped businesses should use home-state LLC. Delaware C-Corp only when VC fundraising is planned. Wyoming LLC for maximum cost savings and privacy.

### Wrong: Skipping foreign qualification when operating across states
Operating in California without registering means no access to state courts, potential back taxes, and penalties. California's $800/yr minimum franchise tax applies regardless. [src5]

### Correct: Register in every state where you have nexus
Physical presence, employees, significant sales, or property in a state typically creates nexus requiring foreign qualification.

## When This Matters

Use this recipe as the first legal step for any new startup. Entity formation is a prerequisite for opening bank accounts, signing contracts, hiring employees, and raising capital. Every subsequent legal and financial activity depends on proper formation.
