# Best Cheap Smartwatches Under $100 (2026)

## Summary

The sub-$100 smartwatch market in 2026 has matured dramatically, with budget wearables now offering built-in GPS, AMOLED displays, Bluetooth calling, and multi-day battery life that would have required $200+ just two years ago. Amazfit dominates this price bracket with three of the top picks, led by the Amazfit Active 2 (~$85-99) which delivers 10-day battery life, GPS with turn-by-turn navigation, NFC payments, and offline music storage in a stainless steel body. [src1, src2, src3]

For users who prioritize maximum battery life and a large display, the Amazfit Bip 6 (~$80) offers a 1.97-inch AMOLED screen with 14-day battery and Bluetooth calling at $20 less than the Active 2. The Samsung Galaxy Fit 3 (~$60) remains the best Android-only option with 13-day battery and seamless Samsung ecosystem integration, while the Fitbit Inspire 3 (~$60-80) pairs best with Google/Fitbit's health platform and works with both iPhone and Android. [src1, src4, src7]

At the ultra-budget end, the TOZO S6 (~$40) and CMF Watch Pro 2 (~$55-69) prove that functional smartwatches with Bluetooth calling and basic health tracking are now available for well under $60. However, accuracy and third-party app integration drop noticeably at these price points. [src2, src5]

## Best for Each Use Case

### Best Overall: Amazfit Active 2 (~$85-99) -- [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/amazfit-active-2)
Consensus pick across Tom's Guide, TechRadar, and Wareable. The Active 2 packs features that rival $200+ watches: built-in GPS with offline maps and turn-by-turn directions, NFC payments via Zepp Pay, offline music storage, a 1.32-inch AMOLED display at 2,000 nits brightness, and 164 sport modes with AI running coaching. The stainless steel body looks far more premium than its price suggests. Heart rate and sleep tracking are accurate enough for daily health monitoring. [src1, src2, src3]

### Best Battery + Large Display: Amazfit Bip 6 (~$80) -- [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/amazfit-bip-6)
The Bip 6 offers the best screen-to-price ratio in this category with its 1.97-inch AMOLED display paired with 14-day battery life. It includes Bluetooth calling, built-in GPS with five satellite systems, 140+ workout modes, and AI voice assistant. At $80, it undercuts the Active 2 while offering a significantly larger display. Trade-off: the plastic body feels less premium than the Active 2's stainless steel. [src1, src4]

### Best for Android Users: Samsung Galaxy Fit 3 (~$60) -- [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/samsung-galaxy-fit-3)
The Galaxy Fit 3 integrates seamlessly with Samsung Health and the broader Samsung ecosystem. It offers accurate step counting (beat the Fitbit Inspire 3 in Tom's Guide head-to-head testing), automatic workout recognition, and a slim, comfortable band-style design. The 1.6-inch AMOLED display and 13-day battery life are category-leading for a fitness band. Major limitation: Android-only, no iPhone support. [src6, src7]

### Best Health Platform: Fitbit Inspire 3 (~$60-80) -- [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/fitbit-inspire-3)
Still one of the best health-focused trackers despite launching in 2022, the Inspire 3 shines through Fitbit's superior health insights platform. Sleep Score, Active Zone Minutes, stress management tools, and deep Google Fit integration make it the best pick for users who prioritize health data analysis over raw specs. Works with both iPhone and Android. Lacks GPS and Bluetooth calling. [src5, src7]

### Best Design Value: CMF Watch Pro 2 (~$55-69) -- [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/cmf-watch-pro-2)
Nothing's sub-brand delivers a surprisingly stylish smartwatch with swappable bezels for customization. Features GPS, Bluetooth calling with AI noise reduction, heart rate and SpO2 monitoring, and 120+ sport modes. The 11-day battery and 1.32-inch AMOLED display compete directly with watches twice its price. IP68 water resistance is slightly lower than the 5ATM rating of most competitors. [src2, src5]

### Best Ultra-Long Battery: Xiaomi Redmi Watch 5 Active (~$50) -- [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/xiaomi-redmi-watch-5-active)
At 18 days of battery life with a 470mAh cell, the Redmi Watch 5 Active outlasts every other watch in this comparison by a wide margin. The 2.0-inch LCD display is the largest here. Bluetooth calling, 140+ workout modes, and 5ATM water resistance round out the package. Trade-off: no GPS, LCD instead of AMOLED, and heart rate accuracy during exercise is inconsistent. [src2, src5]

### Best Under $50: TOZO S6 (~$40) -- [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/tozo-s6)
The TOZO S6 proves a functional smartwatch can cost under $50. Its 1.85-inch AMOLED display, Bluetooth 5.3 calling, 100+ sport modes, and health monitoring (heart rate, SpO2, sleep) are impressive for $40. The Apple Watch-like design is a deliberate homage. Significant trade-off: no integration with Apple Health, Google Fit, or Samsung Health, which limits its usefulness for serious fitness tracking. [src2]

## Decision Logic

### If budget < $50
--> TOZO S6 (~$40) for the best feature set under $50, or Wyze Watch 47 (~$30) if absolute minimum spend is the priority. Expect limited accuracy and no third-party health platform integration. [src2]

### If primary use is fitness tracking with GPS
--> Amazfit Active 2 (~$85-99) or Amazfit Bip 6 (~$80). These are the only sub-$100 options with built-in GPS and reliable workout tracking. The CMF Watch Pro 2 also has GPS at $55-69. [src1, src3, src4]

### If user has an iPhone
--> Eliminate Samsung Galaxy Fit 3 (Android-only). Amazfit Active 2 and Fitbit Inspire 3 offer the best iPhone compatibility. [src6, src7]

### If battery life is the top priority
--> Xiaomi Redmi Watch 5 Active (18 days) or Amazfit Bip 6 (14 days). For users who hate charging, these outlast competitors by 4-8 days. [src2, src5]

### If user wants Samsung ecosystem integration
--> Samsung Galaxy Fit 3 (~$60). Seamless Samsung Health sync and the best Android notification experience in this price range. [src6]

### Default recommendation
--> Amazfit Active 2 (~$85-99). Best balance of features, build quality, accuracy, and battery life. GPS, NFC, offline music, and 10-day battery cover most use cases at a price that is regularly discounted to ~$85. [src1, src2, src3]

## Key Market Trends (2026)

- **Bluetooth calling is now standard under $100**: Nearly every sub-$100 smartwatch in 2026 supports making and answering calls via Bluetooth, a feature that was $150+ territory in 2024. [src1, src2]
- **AMOLED displays dominate budget**: Even the $40 TOZO S6 ships with an AMOLED screen. LCD is now reserved only for the cheapest models like the Redmi Watch 5 Active. [src2, src5]
- **GPS under $100 is common**: Built-in GPS is available at $55 (CMF Watch Pro 2), $80 (Amazfit Bip 6), and $85 (Amazfit Active 2). No longer a premium-only feature. [src1, src4]
- **Amazfit dominates budget wearables**: Amazfit/Zepp holds three of the top four slots in most expert roundups, with the Active 2 as the consensus best under $100. [src1, src2, src5]
- **Health tracking accuracy gap persists**: While features are converging, accuracy during exercise remains inconsistent in sub-$60 watches. Independent step-count tests show 2-5% error on Amazfit/Samsung vs 8-15% on TOZO/Wyze. [src5, src7]

## Important Caveats

- Prices are approximate US street prices as of April 2026. Sales, Prime Day deals, and regional pricing vary significantly.
- Battery life estimates are manufacturer-stated with typical usage. Heavy GPS use, always-on display, and Bluetooth calling reduce battery life by 30-60%.
- No sub-$100 smartwatch matches Apple Watch or Galaxy Watch for app ecosystem, voice assistant quality, or notification handling.
- Health sensor accuracy (heart rate, SpO2) is not medical-grade on any consumer wearable, but accuracy varies significantly between brands at this price point.
- Water resistance ratings assume intact seals. Avoid hot water, saunas, and pressing buttons underwater.
