# Retail Dossier Scoring Thresholds, Delivery Channels, and Feedback Loops

## Rule

Generate and deliver a retail signal dossier when the composite confidence score reaches 7.0 or higher with at least 2 independent signals from different source categories. Scores between 4.0 and 6.9 go to the watchlist for continued monitoring. Scores below 4.0 are discarded. All dossiers scoring 7.0-8.5 require human review before sending; auto-send is permitted only above 8.5 after the initial 100-dossier calibration period demonstrates a >5% meeting rate. Reduce all signal weights by 30% during Q4 (October-December) and increase by 20% during January-February post-holiday distress peak. [src1, src2]

## Evidence

Signal-based outreach consistently achieves 5-8% meeting rates compared to 1-2% for cold outreach across B2B verticals, representing a 3-5x improvement — but only when confidence thresholds are properly calibrated. [src5] A/B testing across 12,000 campaigns showed that requiring a minimum of 2 independent signal sources before outreach reduced false-positive dossiers by 62% while only sacrificing 11% of true positives. [src5] Q4 retail seasonal noise inflates distress signals by 30-60% due to normal holiday hiring surges, inventory build-up, and promotional markdowns — teams that fail to apply seasonal dampening see meeting rates drop to 1.8% during Q4 vs 6.2% in non-seasonal periods. [src3] Tuesday-Thursday delivery windows between 9-11am recipient local time produce 34% higher open rates and 28% higher reply rates compared to Monday/Friday sends. [src6]

## Key Properties

- **Generate threshold**: Score >= 7.0 with minimum 2 independent signals from different source categories
- **Watchlist range**: Score 4.0-6.9 — continue monitoring, do not generate dossier
- **Discard threshold**: Score < 4.0 — insufficient evidence for action
- **Human review band**: Score 7.0-8.5 — mandatory human review before delivery
- **Auto-send threshold**: Score > 8.5, permitted only after first 100 dossiers achieve >5% meeting rate
- **Q4 dampening**: Reduce all signal weights by 30% (October-December) to compensate for holiday noise
- **Q1 amplification**: Increase all signal weights by 20% (January-February) during post-holiday distress peak
- **Per-retailer cap**: Maximum 3 dossiers per retailer per quarter
- **Calibration cadence**: Recalculate precision (meetings/dossiers sent) after every 50 dossiers
- **Target precision**: >5% meeting rate (vs. 1-2% cold outreach baseline)

## Conditions

- **Applies when**: Operating a retail signal detection pipeline that generates outreach dossiers for B2B sales teams targeting retailers showing distress or transformation signals
- **Does NOT apply when**: Selling to non-retail verticals (signal weights and seasonal factors are retail-specific); conducting inbound-only sales motions; targeting retailers below $10M or above $5B revenue (signal detectability constraints from detection-rules unit apply)
- **Confidence degrades when**: Pipeline has fewer than 50 dossiers of historical data (insufficient calibration); market conditions shift rapidly (e.g., pandemic-level disruption); scoring formula thresholds are older than 12 months without recalibration

## Constraints
<!-- Agents: read this section before recommending this rule.
     These are hard boundaries on when and how it applies. -->

- First 100 dossiers require mandatory human review regardless of score — no exceptions, no overrides [src1]
- Maximum 3 dossiers per retailer per quarter — exceeding this causes unsubscribe spikes of 4-6x [src2]
- Auto-send requires BOTH conditions: score >8.5 AND >5% meeting rate across first 100 dossiers [src1]
- Q4 dampening (30% weight reduction) is mandatory October 1 through December 31 — applying standard weights during Q4 systematically generates false positives [src3]
- Feedback loop calibration must run after every 50 dossiers — skipping cycles causes 15-20% scoring drift within 90 days [src4]
- Follow-up cadence (Day 3, Day 7, Day 14) must suppress after 3 total attempts — additional attempts degrade domain reputation [src2]

## Rationale

The scoring thresholds exist to balance precision against recall in a sales context where false positives waste expensive human outreach capacity while false negatives simply mean delayed engagement with an eventual buyer. The 7.0 threshold with 2-source minimum was derived empirically: single-source signals produce 60%+ false positives, while the dual-source requirement drops this to under 25%. [src5] Seasonal calibration is critical because retail Q4 patterns (holiday hiring, inventory build-up, promotional markdowns) mimic genuine distress signals — without dampening, Q4 produces 3x the dossier volume at 1/3 the conversion rate, exhausting sales team capacity on false signals precisely when real opportunities are emerging in Q1. [src3]

## Application Checklist

### Step 1: Determine pipeline stage and apply correct review policy
- **Inputs needed**: Total dossiers sent to date, historical meeting rate
- **Output**: Pipeline classification (calibration vs production) and review policy (all-human vs threshold-based)
- **Constraint**: If fewer than 100 dossiers sent, ALL dossiers require human review regardless of score — no shortcuts [src1]

### Step 2: Apply seasonal calibration to incoming signals
- **Inputs needed**: Current date, raw signal weights from detection-rules
- **Output**: Seasonally adjusted signal weights
- **Constraint**: Q4 dampening (30% reduction) is mandatory October-December; Q1 amplification (20% increase) applies January-February; failing to apply seasonal adjustment invalidates all scores generated during those periods [src3]

### Step 3: Score and route the dossier
- **Inputs needed**: Seasonally adjusted signal scores, number of independent source categories contributing signals
- **Output**: Composite score + routing decision (generate/watchlist/discard)
- **Constraint**: Minimum 2 independent signal sources required for the 7.0 threshold — a single source scoring 9.0 still goes to watchlist, not generate [src5]

### Step 4: Deliver via appropriate channel and cadence
- **Inputs needed**: Score, pipeline stage, retailer's quarterly dossier count, recipient timezone
- **Output**: Dossier delivered (or queued for human review)
- **Constraint**: Check per-retailer quarterly cap (max 3) before sending; deliver Tuesday-Thursday 9-11am recipient local time; for compound signals >9.0 also fire Slack alert [src2, src6]

### Step 5: Record feedback and run calibration
- **Inputs needed**: Delivery outcome (opened, clicked, replied, meeting booked, deal closed, unsubscribed, marked spam)
- **Output**: Updated feedback scores; precision calculation if 50-dossier threshold reached
- **Constraint**: Calibration must run after every 50 dossiers; if precision drops below 5%, pause auto-send and revert to all-human-review until root cause is identified [src4]

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Optimizing for dossier volume over precision
Generating dossiers at a 5.0 threshold to "fill the pipeline" floods the sales team with low-confidence leads. Teams that lower thresholds below 7.0 see meeting rates drop to 1.5% while consuming 3x the outreach capacity, resulting in net negative ROI compared to cold outreach. [src5]

### Correct: Hold the 7.0 threshold and invest in signal coverage
Maintain the 7.0 minimum and instead focus on increasing the number of signal sources monitored. Adding one new signal source category typically increases true-positive detection by 15-20% without degrading precision. [src1]

### Wrong: Ignoring negative feedback signals
Treating unsubscribes and spam reports as neutral events rather than negative calibration inputs. Pipelines that ignore negative feedback see domain reputation scores degrade within 60 days, reducing email deliverability by 20-40% across all outreach. [src2]

### Correct: Weight negative signals heavily in calibration
Assign unsubscribed (-0.5) and marked_spam (-1.0) weights in the feedback loop. After calibration, if negative signal rates exceed 2% of total dossiers, suspend auto-send and review targeting criteria. [src4]

### Wrong: Sending dossiers during Q4 without seasonal dampening
Running the pipeline at standard weights during October-December. Holiday hiring surges, inventory build-ups, and promotional markdowns mimic distress patterns, generating 3x false-positive volume. [src3]

### Correct: Apply mandatory 30% weight reduction October-December
Reduce all signal weights by 30% during Q4. Accept that dossier volume will drop 40-50% during this period. The dossiers that do clear the adjusted threshold have 2x higher meeting rates than undampened Q4 output. [src3]

### Wrong: Auto-sending before calibration is complete
Enabling auto-send for >8.5 scores before completing the 100-dossier human-review calibration period. Early-stage scoring models have insufficient training data; auto-sending premature dossiers produces 3-4x higher unsubscribe rates and poisons the domain. [src1]

### Correct: Complete the full 100-dossier calibration with human review
Every dossier in the first 100 must be human-reviewed. Only after achieving >5% meeting rate across those 100 should auto-send be enabled for scores >8.5. If the 5% threshold is not met, extend calibration and adjust detection-rules thresholds. [src1]

## Counter-Arguments

- Signal-based pipelines with high thresholds miss time-sensitive opportunities — a retailer entering distress rapidly may not accumulate enough signals to reach 7.0 before a competitor identifies them through less rigorous methods. Speed-to-contact matters in competitive markets. [src5]
- The 3-dossier-per-retailer quarterly cap may be too restrictive for accounts showing genuine multi-phase transformation (e.g., new CTO + digital replatforming + store portfolio restructuring happening simultaneously). Some practitioners argue for per-signal-cluster caps instead.
- Q1 weight amplification (20% increase) risks overcorrecting for post-holiday distress — some retailers show temporary January distress that self-corrects by March without any intervention, leading to outreach that arrives after the buying window closes. [src3]

## Common Misconceptions

- **Misconception**: Higher dossier volume equals more meetings and more pipeline.
  **Reality**: Meeting rates are inversely correlated with volume below the 7.0 threshold. Teams sending 200 dossiers/month at 5.0 threshold book fewer meetings than teams sending 60 dossiers/month at 7.0 threshold, because low-confidence outreach trains recipients to ignore future emails. [src5]

- **Misconception**: Auto-send should be enabled as soon as the scoring model is built.
  **Reality**: Scoring models require calibration data from at least 100 human-reviewed dossiers to achieve stable precision. Auto-sending before calibration produces 3-4x higher negative feedback rates and can permanently damage domain reputation. [src1]

- **Misconception**: Seasonal adjustment is optional fine-tuning.
  **Reality**: Q4 seasonal noise is the single largest source of false positives in retail signal detection. Without mandatory dampening, Q4 produces more false positives than all other quarters combined, exhausting outreach capacity precisely when Q1 opportunities are emerging. [src3]

- **Misconception**: The feedback loop is a reporting dashboard.
  **Reality**: The feedback loop is a calibration mechanism that directly adjusts scoring thresholds. Every 50 dossiers, precision is recalculated and, if below target, triggers threshold adjustments or auto-send suspension. Treating it as passive reporting allows scoring drift of 15-20% per quarter. [src4]
