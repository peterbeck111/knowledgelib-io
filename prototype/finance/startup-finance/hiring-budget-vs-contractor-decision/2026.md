# FTE vs Contractor vs Agency — Hiring Cost Decision Framework

## Purpose

This recipe produces a total cost comparison worksheet that quantifies the true all-in cost of hiring a full-time employee versus engaging an independent contractor versus using a staffing agency — including hidden costs like benefits overhead (1.25-1.4x salary), recruiting fees ($5K-$25K), onboarding productivity loss (2-6 months ramp), equipment ($2K-5K), and misclassification risk penalties. The output is a side-by-side cost model over 12- and 24-month horizons with a clear recommendation for which path to take based on role duration, budget, and risk tolerance.

## Prerequisites

- [ ] **Role description** — clear scope of work, required skills, expected duration, and performance expectations
- [ ] **Market salary data** — base salary range from [Levels.fyi](https://levels.fyi), [Glassdoor](https://glassdoor.com), or [Robert Half Salary Guide](https://www.roberthalf.com/us/en/insights/salary-guide)
- [ ] **Contractor rate quotes** — hourly/daily rates from platforms (Toptal, Upwork) or direct referrals
- [ ] **Agency rate quotes** — if considering staffing agencies, request bill rate breakdowns
- [ ] **Spreadsheet tool** — Google Sheets, Excel, or Notion for cost modeling
- [ ] **State/jurisdiction** — employment law varies significantly by state (CA, NY, MA have stricter classification rules)

## Constraints

- Total cost of employment is 1.25-1.4x base salary — never compare raw salary to contractor hourly rate directly. Benefits alone average 33% of total compensation. [src2]
- The DOL Final Rule uses a six-factor economic realities test for worker classification. Contract language does not override actual working conditions. [src7]
- Misclassification penalties include back employment taxes, benefits, overtime, and fines — cumulative liability can exceed $100K per worker over several years. [src4]
- Staffing agency markups typically run 25-50% over the worker's pay rate for temporary/contract staff, and 18-25% of first-year salary for direct-hire placements. [src3]
- Break-even analysis must account for opportunity cost of management time spent on recruiting, onboarding, and contractor oversight.

## Execution Flow

### Step 1: Calculate True FTE Cost (Loaded Rate)

**Duration**: 20-30 minutes
**Tool**: Spreadsheet

Build the fully-loaded cost of hiring a full-time employee. Most founders only look at salary — the actual cost is 1.25-1.4x higher. [src2]

```
FTE TOTAL COST WORKSHEET
═══════════════════════════════════════════════
BASE COMPENSATION
  Annual salary (market rate):          $________

EMPLOYER TAX BURDEN (~7.65-10%)
  Social Security (6.2% up to cap):     $________
  Medicare (1.45%):                     $________
  Federal unemployment (FUTA):          $________
  State unemployment (SUTA):            $________
  Workers' compensation:                $________
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  Subtotal taxes:                       $________

BENEFITS (~15-25% of salary)
  Health insurance:                     $________ (avg $7,500-$16,000/yr employer share)
  Dental + vision:                      $________ (avg $600-$1,200/yr)
  401(k) match (3-4%):                  $________
  PTO cost (15-20 days):                $________ (salary ÷ 260 × PTO days)
  Life/disability insurance:            $________
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  Subtotal benefits:                    $________

ONE-TIME COSTS (amortize over expected tenure)
  Recruiting (job boards, screening):   $________ ($5,000-$25,000)
  Recruiter fee (if used, 15-25%):      $________
  Onboarding + training:               $________ ($1,000-$5,000)
  Equipment (laptop, monitors, etc.):   $________ ($2,000-$5,000)
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  Subtotal one-time:                    $________

ONGOING OVERHEAD
  Software licenses/tools per seat:     $________ ($2,000-$8,000/yr)
  Office space per person (if any):     $________ ($0-$12,000/yr)
  Management time (10-15% of mgr):      $________
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  Subtotal overhead:                    $________

═══════════════════════════════════════════════
TOTAL ANNUAL FTE COST:                  $________
EFFECTIVE HOURLY RATE (÷ 2,080 hrs):    $________/hr
LOADED MULTIPLIER (total ÷ salary):     ____x
```

**Verify**: Loaded multiplier should be between 1.25x and 1.50x. If lower, you are likely missing cost categories. If higher than 1.50x, you may be double-counting or including discretionary perks. [src1]
**If failed**: Cross-check with BLS Employer Costs for Employee Compensation data for your industry and region.

### Step 2: Calculate True Contractor Cost

**Duration**: 15-20 minutes
**Tool**: Spreadsheet

Contractors eliminate benefits and tax burden but command a rate premium. Calculate the true cost including platform fees and management overhead.

```
CONTRACTOR TOTAL COST WORKSHEET
═══════════════════════════════════════════════
DIRECT COSTS
  Hourly rate:                          $________/hr
  Expected hours/week:                  ________ hrs
  Expected duration:                    ________ weeks
  Annual equivalent (rate × hrs × 50):  $________

PLATFORM FEES (if using marketplace)
  Toptal: $79/mo subscription +
    hourly rates $60-200+/hr:           $________
  Upwork: 5% client marketplace fee:    $________
  Direct hire: $0 platform fee:         $________

MANAGEMENT OVERHEAD
  Time spent on contractor mgmt
    (5-10% of manager's time):          $________
  Contract/legal review:                $________ ($500-$2,000 per contract)
  IP assignment agreement:              $________ ($500-$1,500)

ONBOARDING (lighter than FTE)
  Context transfer (20-40 hrs):         $________ (at contractor rate)
  Documentation for handoff:            $________ (internal team time)

RISK PREMIUM
  Key-person risk (if sole contractor
    on critical path):                  Qualitative — HIGH/MED/LOW
  Knowledge loss at contract end:       Qualitative — HIGH/MED/LOW

═══════════════════════════════════════════════
TOTAL ANNUAL CONTRACTOR COST:           $________
EFFECTIVE HOURLY (all-in):              $________/hr
```

**Verify**: Contractor effective hourly should be 1.3-2.0x an equivalent FTE's base hourly rate ($salary / 2,080). If the ratio is below 1.3x, you may be getting below-market talent. If above 2.0x, FTE likely becomes cheaper within 12 months. [src1]
**If failed**: Get 3 competitive quotes from different sourcing channels to validate market rate.

### Step 3: Calculate Agency / Staffing Firm Cost

**Duration**: 15-20 minutes
**Tool**: Spreadsheet

Agency costs include significant markups but transfer compliance, payroll, and sourcing burden to the vendor. [src3]

```
AGENCY TOTAL COST WORKSHEET
═══════════════════════════════════════════════
BILL RATE STRUCTURE
  Agency bill rate:                     $________/hr
  Estimated worker pay rate:            $________/hr
  Implied markup:                       ________% (typically 25-50%)

ANNUAL COST
  Bill rate × hours/week × 50 weeks:    $________

ADDITIONAL FEES
  Contract initiation/placement fee:    $________ ($0-$5,000)
  Temp-to-perm conversion fee
    (if converting to FTE later):       $________ (typically 10-20% of salary)
  Minimum engagement terms:             ________ weeks
  Early termination penalty:            $________

WHAT THE AGENCY HANDLES (cost included in markup)
  Payroll processing:                   Included
  Employer taxes (FICA, FUTA, SUTA):    Included
  Workers' compensation insurance:      Included
  Background checks:                    Included
  W-2 administration:                   Included

WHAT YOU STILL HANDLE
  Day-to-day management:                $________ (mgr time)
  Equipment/access provisioning:        $________
  Onboarding to internal systems:       $________

═══════════════════════════════════════════════
TOTAL ANNUAL AGENCY COST:              $________
EFFECTIVE HOURLY (all-in):             $________/hr
PREMIUM OVER DIRECT CONTRACTOR:        ________%
```

**Verify**: Agency markup should be 25-50% for temporary staff. If quoted markup exceeds 50%, negotiate or get competing quotes. Markups below 20% may indicate the agency is underpaying the worker. [src3]
**If failed**: Request bill rate breakdown from agency showing worker pay, burden, and margin separately.

### Step 4: Run Break-Even Analysis

**Duration**: 15 minutes
**Tool**: Spreadsheet

Determine at what point the FTE path becomes cheaper than contractor or agency. [src1]

```
BREAK-EVEN ANALYSIS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════
                        FTE         Contractor    Agency
Monthly cost:           $________   $________     $________
Cumulative 3 months:    $________   $________     $________
Cumulative 6 months:    $________   $________     $________
Cumulative 12 months:   $________   $________     $________
Cumulative 18 months:   $________   $________     $________
Cumulative 24 months:   $________   $________     $________

FTE one-time costs
  (recruiting + onboarding + equipment):  $________

Break-even month (FTE vs Contractor):     Month ________
Break-even month (FTE vs Agency):         Month ________

RULE OF THUMB:
  < 6 months  → Almost always contractor or agency
  6-12 months → Contractor usually wins (no one-time FTE costs)
  12-18 months → FTE starts winning (amortized one-time costs)
  18+ months  → FTE almost always wins on pure cost
```

**Verify**: Break-even should typically fall between 12-18 months. If break-even is under 6 months, your contractor rate is unusually low or FTE costs are unusually high — double-check inputs.
**If failed**: Revisit one-time FTE costs — high recruiting fees push break-even later, while low-cost channels pull it earlier.

### Step 5: Assess Non-Financial Factors

**Duration**: 15 minutes
**Tool**: Decision matrix

Cost alone does not determine the right path. Score these critical non-financial factors.

```
NON-FINANCIAL DECISION MATRIX
═══════════════════════════════════════════════
Factor                    FTE    Contractor  Agency   Weight
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IP ownership clarity      5/5    3/5         4/5      0.20
Cultural integration      5/5    2/5         2/5      0.15
Availability/speed        2/5    4/5         5/5      0.15
Flexibility to scale      2/5    5/5         4/5      0.15
Knowledge retention       5/5    2/5         2/5      0.15
Compliance risk           5/5    2/5         4/5      0.10
Management burden         3/5    3/5         4/5      0.10
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WEIGHTED SCORE:           __     __          __

Scoring guide:
  5 = Strong advantage for this path
  3 = Neutral
  1 = Significant disadvantage

COMBINED RECOMMENDATION:
  Financial winner (from Step 4):       ____________
  Non-financial winner (from above):    ____________
  Recommended path:                     ____________
  Confidence in recommendation:         HIGH/MED/LOW
```

**Verify**: If financial and non-financial winners disagree, the non-financial factors should typically win for roles longer than 12 months — culture and IP protection compound over time.
**If failed**: If scores are within 0.5 points, the decision is genuinely close — default to the path with lower compliance risk.

### Step 6: Generate Risk-Adjusted Recommendation

**Duration**: 10 minutes

Compile the final recommendation with risk factors and compliance considerations.

```
HIRING PATH RECOMMENDATION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════
Role:                       ________________________
Duration:                   ________________________
Recommended path:           [ ] FTE  [ ] Contractor  [ ] Agency
Financial advantage (24mo): $________ savings vs next-best option
Break-even point:           Month ________

KEY RISKS:
  If choosing contractor:
  - [ ] Misclassification risk assessed (IRS 20-factor test)
  - [ ] Independent contractor agreement reviewed by counsel
  - [ ] Worker controls own schedule, tools, and methods
  - [ ] IP assignment clause in contract

  If choosing FTE:
  - [ ] 12+ month commitment justified
  - [ ] Recruiting pipeline identified
  - [ ] Benefits package defined and costed
  - [ ] Onboarding plan ready (reduce ramp from 8-12mo to 4-6mo)

  If choosing agency:
  - [ ] Temp-to-perm conversion terms negotiated upfront
  - [ ] SLA for replacement if worker underperforms
  - [ ] Bill rate competitively benchmarked

NEXT STEPS:
1. ________________________________________
2. ________________________________________
3. ________________________________________
```

**Verify**: Recommendation includes specific risk mitigations for the chosen path.
**If failed**: If unable to clearly recommend one path, default to contractor for the first 3-6 months with a structured evaluation point for conversion to FTE.

## Quality Benchmarks

| Quality Metric | Minimum Acceptable | Good | Excellent |
|---------------|-------------------|------|-----------|
| Cost categories included | Salary + taxes + benefits | + recruiting + onboarding + equipment | + management overhead + opportunity cost + risk premium |
| Salary data sources | 1 source (Glassdoor) | 2 sources cross-referenced | 3+ sources including Robert Half/Levels.fyi |
| Break-even accuracy | Rough estimate (nearest 6mo) | Within 3 months | Monthly precision with sensitivity analysis |
| Risk assessment completeness | Financial risks only | + compliance/classification | + IP, cultural, retention, key-person risk |
| Contractor rate validation | Single quote | 2-3 competing quotes | Market survey + platform data + referral rates |

**If below minimum**: Add missing cost categories and get at least 2 contractor quotes before making a decision. Decisions based on incomplete cost data systematically favor contractors (hidden FTE costs are overlooked) or FTEs (contractor premiums are underestimated).

## Error Handling

| Error | Likely Cause | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| FTE loaded cost below 1.20x salary | Missing tax or benefit categories | Add employer FICA (7.65%), state unemployment, workers' comp, and health insurance at minimum |
| Contractor rate seems too low | Below-market talent or geographic arbitrage | Verify skills match — below-market rates often indicate junior talent that requires more management |
| Agency refuses to disclose markup | Opaque pricing model | Request bill rate and estimated worker pay separately — or get quotes from 2-3 agencies for comparison |
| Break-even analysis shows FTE never wins | Very high recruiting costs or very low contractor rate | Check if in-house recruiting (vs. recruiter fees) changes the math — 15-25% recruiter fees dramatically shift break-even |
| Worker classification unclear | Role has both employee and contractor characteristics | Apply IRS behavioral, financial, and relationship tests — when in doubt, classify as employee to avoid penalties [src4] |

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Comparing raw salary to contractor hourly rate
Founders see a $120K salary vs. a $75/hr contractor ($156K annualized) and conclude the contractor costs 30% more. They forget the FTE actually costs $150K-$168K fully loaded — making the gap much smaller or nonexistent over 12 months. [src1]

### Correct: Always compare loaded FTE cost to all-in contractor cost
Run Step 1 to get the true loaded FTE rate before comparing. The loaded rate includes taxes, benefits, equipment, recruiting, and onboarding amortized over expected tenure.

### Wrong: Defaulting to contractors to "stay lean" without break-even analysis
Many early-stage founders use contractors for everything to avoid headcount commitment. For roles lasting 18+ months, this can cost 30-50% more than an FTE while also creating IP ownership ambiguity and cultural fragmentation. [src2]

### Correct: Use contractors for defined scope with clear end dates
Contractors are cost-optimal for projects under 12 months, surge capacity, and specialized skills not needed full-time. For ongoing core roles, run the break-even analysis — the FTE path usually wins by month 12-18.

### Wrong: Ignoring misclassification risk for long-term contractors
Engaging a contractor for 2+ years, requiring set hours, providing equipment, and integrating them into daily standups creates substantial reclassification liability — regardless of what the contract says. [src4]

### Correct: Apply the IRS classification test honestly
If the role looks like employment (set schedule, your tools, integrated into team, ongoing with no project end date), hire as FTE or use an agency/EOR structure to shift compliance risk. The agency markup is cheaper than misclassification penalties.
