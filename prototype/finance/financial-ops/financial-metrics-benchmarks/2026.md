# Financial Metrics Benchmarks 2026

## Summary

These benchmarks cover core financial performance metrics — gross margin, EBITDA margin, operating margin, current ratio, quick ratio, and cash conversion cycle — across seven major industry sectors. Data is drawn from publicly traded U.S. companies with supplemental global data, representing Q3-Q4 2025 actuals. The single most important shift from 2024: working capital performance improved 4% overall as payables management rebounded, but receivables and inventory efficiency continued to lag, leaving $1.7 trillion trapped in excess working capital across the S&P 1500. [src3]

**Data vintage**: Based on Q3-Q4 2025 data from 7,000+ publicly traded companies (Damodaran dataset) and 1,000 largest U.S. nonfinancials (Hackett Group).
**Key shift**: Gross margins held steady across most sectors, but EBITDA margins compressed 2-4 points in manufacturing due to input cost inflation and supply chain normalization. SaaS gross margins remained above 75% with top performers exceeding 82%. [src1]

## Constraints
<!-- Agents: read before citing any benchmark number. -->

- These benchmarks represent publicly traded companies. Private companies typically run 3-8% lower margins due to scale disadvantages and limited pricing power.
- Figures are medians unless labeled otherwise. Mean values are skewed by outliers; use median for planning, 75th percentile for ambitious targets.
- Primarily U.S. data. European margins run 2-5% lower in most sectors; APAC varies by country. Adjust accordingly.
- Data collected Q3-Q4 2025. If citing after Q2 2026, search for updated figures before making decisions.
- Only compare companies within the same segment row. Cross-segment comparison (e.g., SaaS vs. manufacturing) is meaningless and produces false conclusions.

## Metric Category 1: Profitability Metrics

### Gross Margin

**Definition**: Revenue minus cost of goods sold (COGS), divided by revenue. COGS includes direct costs of delivering the product or service — raw materials, direct labor, hosting costs (for SaaS). Excludes SG&A, R&D, depreciation.

| Segment | Median | 25th Percentile | 75th Percentile | Top Decile |
|---------|--------|-----------------|-----------------|------------|
| SaaS/Software | 75.2% | 68.0% | 82.1% | 87.5% |
| Professional Services | 34.8% | 28.5% | 42.0% | 51.2% |
| Healthcare | 52.3% | 38.0% | 62.5% | 71.0% |
| Manufacturing | 32.5% | 24.0% | 41.0% | 48.5% |
| Retail/E-commerce | 28.4% | 21.0% | 36.5% | 44.0% |
| Financial Services | 61.5% | 48.0% | 72.0% | 80.0% |
| Construction | 22.0% | 16.0% | 28.5% | 35.0% |

**Trend**: SaaS gross margins stable YoY (+0.3pp). Manufacturing down 1.8pp due to persistent input cost inflation. Retail flat as pricing power offset supply chain costs. [src1]
**Red flag threshold**: Gross margin below 25th percentile for the segment signals structural pricing or cost issues that require immediate investigation.
**Action trigger**: If gross margin is declining quarter-over-quarter for 3+ periods, investigate unit economics and pricing strategy.

[src1, src4]

### EBITDA Margin

**Definition**: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, divided by revenue. Measures operating profitability before capital structure and accounting decisions. Include all operating expenses; exclude interest, taxes, D&A.

| Segment | Median | 25th Percentile | 75th Percentile | Top Decile |
|---------|--------|-----------------|-----------------|------------|
| SaaS/Software | 15.0% | 5.0% | 24.0% | 32.0% |
| Professional Services | 14.5% | 8.0% | 21.0% | 28.0% |
| Healthcare | 12.8% | 6.0% | 19.5% | 26.0% |
| Manufacturing | 11.2% | 5.5% | 17.0% | 23.5% |
| Retail/E-commerce | 7.5% | 3.0% | 12.0% | 17.0% |
| Financial Services | 32.0% | 22.0% | 42.0% | 52.0% |
| Construction | 8.5% | 4.0% | 13.0% | 18.0% |

**Trend**: EBITDA margins compressed across most sectors. SaaS down 1.5pp as companies shifted from growth-at-all-costs to profitability focus but still invested heavily in AI. Manufacturing down 2.4pp. Financial services stable. [src1, src5]
**Red flag threshold**: Negative EBITDA for 4+ consecutive quarters in a growth-stage company, or EBITDA margin below 25th percentile in a mature company.
**Action trigger**: If EBITDA margin is below median and declining, perform cost structure analysis and benchmark SG&A as percentage of revenue.

[src1, src2]

### Operating Margin

**Definition**: Operating income divided by revenue. Differs from EBITDA by including depreciation and amortization. More conservative profitability measure, reflects capital intensity.

| Segment | Median | 25th Percentile | 75th Percentile | Top Decile |
|---------|--------|-----------------|-----------------|------------|
| SaaS/Software | 10.5% | -2.0% | 20.0% | 28.0% |
| Professional Services | 11.0% | 5.0% | 17.5% | 24.0% |
| Healthcare | 8.5% | 2.0% | 15.0% | 21.0% |
| Manufacturing | 7.8% | 2.5% | 13.5% | 19.0% |
| Retail/E-commerce | 4.5% | 1.0% | 8.5% | 13.0% |
| Financial Services | 28.0% | 18.0% | 38.0% | 48.0% |
| Construction | 5.5% | 2.0% | 10.0% | 15.0% |

**Trend**: Directionally consistent with EBITDA trends. Capital-intensive industries (manufacturing, construction) show wider gap between EBITDA and operating margin due to higher D&A. [src1]
**Red flag threshold**: Negative operating margin in a company that claims profitability — often signals reliance on non-operating income or aggressive D&A schedules.

[src1, src4]

## Metric Category 2: Liquidity Metrics

### Current Ratio

**Definition**: Current assets divided by current liabilities. Measures ability to pay short-term obligations. Current assets include cash, receivables, inventory, and prepaid expenses maturing within 12 months.

| Segment | Median | 25th Percentile | 75th Percentile | Alarm Threshold |
|---------|--------|-----------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| SaaS/Software | 2.1 | 1.5 | 3.2 | < 1.0 |
| Professional Services | 1.6 | 1.2 | 2.3 | < 0.9 |
| Healthcare | 1.8 | 1.2 | 2.7 | < 1.0 |
| Manufacturing | 1.9 | 1.3 | 2.6 | < 1.0 |
| Retail/E-commerce | 1.3 | 0.9 | 1.8 | < 0.8 |
| Financial Services | 1.1 | 0.8 | 1.5 | < 0.6 |
| Construction | 1.4 | 1.0 | 2.0 | < 0.9 |

**Trend**: Broadly stable YoY. SaaS companies accumulating cash reserves, pushing median up. Retail ratios tightened slightly due to inventory management challenges. [src2]
**Red flag threshold**: Current ratio below 1.0 means the company cannot cover short-term obligations with current assets. Exception: financial services firms routinely operate below 1.0 due to liability-driven balance sheets.
**Action trigger**: If current ratio drops below alarm threshold, run cash flow management assessment immediately.

[src2, src6]

### Quick Ratio (Acid Test)

**Definition**: (Current assets minus inventory) divided by current liabilities. More conservative than current ratio — strips out inventory which may not be quickly convertible to cash.

| Segment | Median | Healthy Range | Alarm Threshold |
|---------|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| SaaS/Software | 1.9 | 1.5 - 3.0 | < 1.0 |
| Professional Services | 1.4 | 1.0 - 2.0 | < 0.8 |
| Healthcare | 1.3 | 0.9 - 2.0 | < 0.7 |
| Manufacturing | 1.0 | 0.7 - 1.5 | < 0.5 |
| Retail/E-commerce | 0.5 | 0.3 - 0.8 | < 0.2 |
| Financial Services | 0.9 | 0.6 - 1.3 | < 0.4 |
| Construction | 1.1 | 0.7 - 1.6 | < 0.6 |

**Red flag threshold**: Quick ratio well below 1.0 (except retail, where inventory-heavy models naturally compress this ratio). A declining quick ratio over 3+ quarters suggests liquidity deterioration.

[src2]

## Metric Category 3: Working Capital Efficiency

### Cash Conversion Cycle (CCC)

**Definition**: DSO + DIO - DPO. Measures the number of days between paying for inputs and collecting cash from customers. Lower is better; negative means the company collects before it pays (common in SaaS and retail).

| Segment | Median | 25th Percentile | 75th Percentile | Top Decile |
|---------|--------|-----------------|-----------------|------------|
| SaaS/Software | -15 days | -45 days | 10 days | -60 days |
| Professional Services | 42 days | 25 days | 60 days | 15 days |
| Healthcare | 55 days | 35 days | 75 days | 20 days |
| Manufacturing | 68 days | 45 days | 95 days | 30 days |
| Retail/E-commerce | 12 days | -5 days | 30 days | -15 days |
| Financial Services | N/A | N/A | N/A | N/A |
| Construction | 78 days | 50 days | 110 days | 35 days |

**Trend**: Overall CCC across S&P 1500 improved to 37 days (down 4% YoY) driven by a 3% leap in DPO to 59 days. DSO and DIO worsened slightly. [src3]
**Red flag threshold**: CCC above 75th percentile for the segment signals working capital inefficiency. Investigate which component (DSO, DIO, or DPO) is the driver.
**Action trigger**: If CCC is worsening quarter-over-quarter, run AP/AR diagnostic and cash flow management assessment.

[src3, src6]

### Days Sales Outstanding (DSO)

**Definition**: Average accounts receivable divided by revenue per day. Measures how quickly the company collects payment from customers. Lower is better.

| Segment | Median | Healthy Range | Alarm Threshold |
|---------|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| SaaS/Software | 55 days | 35 - 65 days | > 80 days |
| Professional Services | 48 days | 30 - 60 days | > 75 days |
| Healthcare | 52 days | 35 - 65 days | > 80 days |
| Manufacturing | 45 days | 30 - 55 days | > 70 days |
| Retail/E-commerce | 8 days | 3 - 15 days | > 25 days |
| Construction | 65 days | 45 - 80 days | > 100 days |

**Trend**: DSO worsened slightly across most industries (+1-2 days YoY) as companies extended payment terms to retain customers in a competitive market. [src3]

[src2, src3]

### Days Payable Outstanding (DPO)

**Definition**: Average accounts payable divided by COGS per day. Measures how long the company takes to pay suppliers. Higher DPO improves working capital but may strain supplier relationships.

| Segment | Median | Healthy Range | Alarm Threshold |
|---------|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| SaaS/Software | 45 days | 30 - 60 days | > 90 days |
| Professional Services | 30 days | 20 - 45 days | > 60 days |
| Healthcare | 38 days | 25 - 50 days | > 70 days |
| Manufacturing | 42 days | 28 - 55 days | > 75 days |
| Retail/E-commerce | 35 days | 20 - 50 days | > 65 days |
| Construction | 48 days | 30 - 65 days | > 85 days |

**Trend**: DPO rebounded to 59 days across S&P 1500 (up 3% YoY), as companies intentionally extended payment terms to preserve cash. [src3]

## Composite Metrics & Rules of Thumb

| Rule | Formula / Threshold | Interpretation |
|------|--------------------|----------------|
| Rule of 40 (SaaS) | Revenue growth rate + EBITDA margin >= 40% | Healthy balance of growth and profitability for SaaS; below 30% signals trouble |
| Current Ratio > 1.5 | Current assets / current liabilities > 1.5 | Adequate short-term liquidity buffer for most non-financial industries |
| Quick Ratio > 1.0 | (Current assets - inventory) / current liabilities > 1.0 | Company can meet obligations without selling inventory |
| Working Capital Ratio | (Current assets - current liabilities) / revenue | Below 10% signals tight liquidity; above 25% may indicate inefficient capital deployment |
| CCC Improvement Target | Reduce CCC by 5-10 days annually | Each day of CCC improvement frees approximately 0.3% of annual revenue in working capital |
| DSO / Terms Ratio | DSO / standard payment terms < 1.2 | Collecting within 20% of stated terms indicates healthy collection process |

**Constraint**: Rule of 40 is not meaningful for companies below $5M ARR. Working capital ratio norms shift significantly between asset-light (SaaS) and asset-heavy (manufacturing) businesses. CCC is not applicable to financial services firms. [src3, src1]

| Segment | Definition | Typical Characteristics |
|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| SaaS/Software | Recurring subscription revenue, ACV-based pricing | Asset-light, high gross margins, negative CCC possible, high R&D spend |
| Professional Services | Revenue from labor/expertise (consulting, legal, accounting) | People-intensive, moderate margins, low inventory, high DSO |
| Healthcare | Hospitals, pharma, medtech, health services | Complex revenue cycles, regulatory overhead, moderate margins |
| Manufacturing | Physical product production (discrete and process) | Capital-intensive, inventory-heavy, long CCC, cyclical margins |

| Financial Services | Banking, insurance, asset management | Liability-driven balance sheets, high margins, unique liquidity metrics |

## Common Misinterpretations

- **Comparing gross margins across industries**: A 30% gross margin is excellent in construction but poor in SaaS. Always use industry-specific benchmarks. The most common agent error is applying SaaS-like margin expectations to physical-product businesses. [src4]
- **Treating median as target**: Median means half of companies are below this number. Use 75th percentile for ambitious targets, median for sanity checks, and 25th percentile as an alarm threshold.
- **Ignoring CCC components in isolation**: A company can have excellent DSO but terrible DIO, resulting in a poor CCC. Always decompose CCC into its three components (DSO, DIO, DPO) before diagnosing working capital issues. [src3]
- **Applying public company benchmarks to private companies**: Private companies typically run 3-8% lower margins due to scale disadvantages. Adjust benchmarks downward when advising private companies.
