Treasury & Cash Management Assessment

How well-managed is treasury — banking relationships, cash pooling, FX exposure, investment policy?

Type: Assessment Confidence: 0.85 Sources: 6 Verified: 2026-03-10

Purpose

This assessment evaluates treasury and cash management maturity across six dimensions: cash visibility and forecasting, banking structure, liquidity management, FX risk management, investment and debt management, and treasury technology. The output identifies where cash is trapped, risk is unmanaged, or inefficiency erodes returns. [src1]

Constraints

  • Requires bank account structures, cash forecasts, investment policies, FX data, and treasury tech stack
  • Not meaningful for single bank account with no international operations
  • Should involve Treasurer/VP Finance, CFO, and banking relationship managers
  • Diagnostic only — does not create policies or hedging strategies
  • Re-run semi-annually; treasury priorities shift with rates, FX volatility, and growth

Common Pitfalls in Assessment

  • Cash visibility overconfidence: Checking bank portals daily is not cash visibility. True visibility means automated, consolidated, multi-currency, real-time.
  • Ignoring idle cash opportunity cost: Cash earning zero in operating accounts costs 4-5% in foregone yield in the 2025-2026 rate environment.
  • FX risk denial: Companies with 15%+ non-functional currency revenue often underestimate exposure.
  • Technology leap-frogging: Sophisticated TMS without fixing data quality creates expensive shelf-ware.

When This Matters
