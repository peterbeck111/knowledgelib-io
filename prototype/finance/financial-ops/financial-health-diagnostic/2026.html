Financial Health Diagnostic

How healthy is the business financially — liquidity, profitability, efficiency, leverage with benchmarks?

Type: Assessment Confidence: 0.86 Sources: 6 Verified: 2026-03-10

Purpose

This assessment evaluates a business's financial health across six critical dimensions: liquidity and cash position, profitability and margins, operating efficiency, leverage and debt management, growth trajectory, and financial controls and reporting quality. The output identifies whether the business is financially resilient or approaching distress, and routes to specific improvement strategies. [src1]

Constraints

  • Requires financial statements (income statement, balance sheet, cash flow) for at least 2-3 fiscal years
  • Industry benchmarks vary dramatically — always compare within sector
  • Financial health diagnostic, not investment advice
  • Private company financials may be limited — public benchmarks may not directly apply
  • Assumes GAAP or IFRS reporting; non-standard accounting distorts ratios

Common Pitfalls in Assessment

  • Profitability without cash flow: Profitable on paper while running out of cash. Always assess both income statement and cash flow together. [src1]
  • Industry benchmark misapplication: 20% gross margin is great in grocery but catastrophic in SaaS. Benchmark within industry. [src3]
  • Growth-at-all-costs blind spot: Rapid growth can mask deteriorating unit economics and unsustainable burn. [src5]
  • Quarterly snapshot vs trends: Single quarter ratios can mislead. Assess 8-12 quarter trends. [src2]
