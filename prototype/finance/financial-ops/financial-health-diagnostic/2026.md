# Financial Health Diagnostic

## Purpose

This assessment evaluates a business's financial health across six critical dimensions: liquidity and cash position, profitability and margins, operating efficiency, leverage and debt management, growth trajectory, and financial controls and reporting quality. The output is a composite health score (1-5) that identifies whether the business is financially resilient or approaching distress, and routes to specific financial improvement strategies. Use this when diagnosing why a profitable company has cash flow problems, preparing for fundraising due diligence, or evaluating whether the business can sustain its current growth rate without additional capital. [src1]

## Constraints
<!-- Agents: read before running this assessment with a user. -->

- Requires access to financial statements (income statement, balance sheet, cash flow) for at least 2-3 fiscal years
- Industry benchmarks vary dramatically — compare within sector, not across all industries
- This is a financial health diagnostic, not investment advice — do not use for investment decisions
- Private company financials may be limited and may not compare directly to public company benchmarks
- Score thresholds assume GAAP or IFRS reporting; non-standard accounting distorts ratios

## Assessment Dimensions

<!-- Each dimension is scored independently. The structured format lets agents
     walk through this conversationally with a user, one dimension at a time. -->

### Dimension 1: Liquidity & Cash Position

**What this measures**: The organization's ability to meet short-term obligations, maintain adequate cash reserves, and manage working capital effectively.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | Cash crises frequent; current ratio below 1.0; no cash flow forecasting; payroll or vendor payments delayed | Operating on overdraft; cannot cover 1 month of expenses from cash; AR collections not managed; cash position unknown beyond bank balance |
| 2 | Emerging | Current ratio 1.0-1.2; cash covers 1-2 months of expenses; basic cash flow tracking but no forecasting; occasional payment delays | Cash monitored weekly; current ratio adequate but thin; 30-60 day cash runway; AR aging monitored but not managed; seasonal cash gaps occur |
| 3 | Defined | Current ratio 1.3-1.8; quick ratio above 1.0; 3-6 months cash runway; 13-week rolling cash flow forecast; working capital actively managed | Cash flow forecast updated weekly; AR aging managed with collections process; AP optimized for cash flow timing; line of credit available but rarely used; DSO tracked and benchmarked |
| 4 | Managed | Current ratio 1.5-2.5; 6-12 months cash runway; scenario-based cash flow modeling; working capital optimization program; cash conversion cycle benchmarked | Multiple scenario cash models; cash conversion cycle optimized; treasury management active; working capital KPIs in financial dashboard; excess cash invested per policy |
| 5 | Optimized | Optimal liquidity with minimal idle cash; real-time cash visibility across all entities; predictive cash flow with AI; zero unplanned cash shortfalls | AI-powered cash forecasting with 95%+ accuracy; real-time multi-entity cash visibility; dynamic working capital optimization; treasury function operates at best-in-class |

**Red flags**: Current ratio below 1.0; no cash flow forecast exists; payroll has been delayed; vendor payments consistently past 60 days; cash position checked monthly or less; line of credit fully drawn. [src2]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "What is your current ratio and quick ratio, and how many months of operating expenses could you cover from cash today?"

### Dimension 2: Profitability & Margins

**What this measures**: Whether the business generates adequate profit at each level — gross margin, operating margin, and net margin — relative to industry benchmarks.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | Negative net income with no path to profitability; gross margins below industry floor; cost structure unsustainable | Burning cash with no profitability timeline; gross margin below industry 25th percentile; COGS or operating expenses growing faster than revenue; unit economics negative |
| 2 | Emerging | Gross margins at industry average but operating and net margins negative or near zero; profitability path exists but unproven | Gross margins acceptable; operating losses due to investment but not yet showing leverage; net margin negative but narrowing; break-even timeline established |
| 3 | Defined | Gross margin above industry median; operating margin positive; net margin positive; margin trends improving year-over-year | Gross margin above 50th percentile; operating margin positive for 4+ quarters; net margin 5-10% for asset-light businesses; margin improvement trend visible |
| 4 | Managed | Margins consistently above industry median at all levels; margin expansion through operating leverage; unit economics strong and improving | All margins above industry median; operating leverage visible (revenue growing faster than costs); CAC payback within industry targets; contribution margin by segment tracked |
| 5 | Optimized | Best-in-class margins for industry; pricing power demonstrated; sustainable competitive advantage reflected in margin premium | Margins in industry top quartile; pricing power demonstrated through margin stability during downturns; sustainable margin advantage documented; margin optimization is a continuous program |

**Red flags**: Gross margin declining year-over-year; net margin negative with no break-even date; revenue growing but losses widening; pricing pressure from competitors eroding margins; no contribution margin analysis by product or segment. [src1]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "What are your gross, operating, and net margins, and how do they compare to your industry benchmarks?"

### Dimension 3: Operating Efficiency

**What this measures**: How efficiently the organization converts resources (capital, labor, assets) into revenue and profit — measured through efficiency ratios and operational metrics.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No efficiency metrics tracked; revenue per employee unknown; asset turnover declining; no budget vs actual analysis | No operational KPIs; resource allocation based on requests not data; budget overruns common and unanalyzed; overhead allocation unknown |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic efficiency metrics tracked (revenue per employee); budget vs actual reviewed quarterly; some cost center visibility | Revenue per employee known but not benchmarked; quarterly budget review; cost centers exist but allocation inconsistent; SG&A as % of revenue monitored |
| 3 | Defined | Efficiency KPIs benchmarked against industry; monthly budget vs actual with variance analysis; cost allocation methodology documented; resource utilization tracked | Revenue per employee benchmarked; monthly P&L by department; variance analysis with explanations; utilization tracked for service businesses; SG&A ratio improving |
| 4 | Managed | Efficiency optimization programs active; zero-based budgeting or similar discipline; real-time resource utilization; operational excellence metrics in executive dashboard | ZBB or driver-based budgeting; real-time utilization dashboards; efficiency KPIs in board reporting; continuous improvement programs; procurement optimization |
| 5 | Optimized | AI-optimized resource allocation; predictive efficiency modeling; best-in-class operating leverage; efficiency is a competitive advantage | AI recommends resource reallocation; predictive models forecast efficiency gains; top-quartile industry efficiency; operating leverage documented and sustained |

**Red flags**: Revenue per employee declining; SG&A growing faster than revenue; no budget vs actual analysis; headcount growing faster than revenue; no visibility into cost per unit or cost per transaction. [src4]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "What is your revenue per employee, and how does your SG&A as a percentage of revenue trend over the past 3 years?"

### Dimension 4: Leverage & Debt Management

**What this measures**: Whether the organization's debt levels are sustainable, debt servicing capacity is adequate, and capital structure is appropriate for its stage and industry.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | Debt-to-equity ratio above 3.0; interest coverage ratio below 1.5; debt covenants at risk; no debt management strategy | Overleveraged; struggling to make interest payments; covenant violations possible; no refinancing plan; relying on credit facilities for operations |
| 2 | Emerging | Debt-to-equity ratio 2.0-3.0; interest coverage 1.5-2.5; debt manageable but constraining; capital structure not optimized | Debt serviced on time but constrains investment; limited headroom for additional borrowing; interest coverage adequate but thin; no active debt management |
| 3 | Defined | Debt-to-equity ratio 1.0-2.0; interest coverage above 3.0; debt covenants comfortably met; capital structure reviewed annually | Debt at industry-appropriate levels; comfortable covenant headroom; annual capital structure review; weighted average cost of capital calculated |
| 4 | Managed | Debt-to-equity ratio optimized for industry; interest coverage above 5.0; active debt portfolio management; capital structure optimized for cost of capital | Active management of debt maturity profile; interest rate hedging where appropriate; WACC optimization; regular debt market access; credit rating maintained |
| 5 | Optimized | Best-in-class capital structure; investment-grade credit rating; optimal leverage for shareholder returns; stress-tested for multiple scenarios | Credit rating monitored; capital structure stress-tested; optimal leverage modeled; debt capacity used strategically for growth; zero unplanned covenant breaches |

**Red flags**: Debt-to-equity above 3.0 without industry justification; interest coverage below 2.0; covenant violations in past 12 months; relying on revolving credit for ongoing operations; no awareness of debt maturity schedule. [src3]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "What is your debt-to-equity ratio and interest coverage ratio, and are all debt covenants comfortably met?"

### Dimension 5: Growth Trajectory

**What this measures**: Whether revenue growth is sustainable, diversified, and generating appropriate returns on invested capital.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | Revenue declining or flat; no growth strategy; customer concentration above 40% in top 3 customers; no new revenue streams | Revenue declining year-over-year; growth plan nonexistent; dangerously concentrated customer base; no pipeline of new products or markets |
| 2 | Emerging | Revenue growing below industry average; growth from existing customers only; limited new customer acquisition; seasonal patterns not managed | Below-median industry growth; net revenue retention positive but new logo acquisition weak; customer concentration improving but still high; growth inconsistent quarter-to-quarter |
| 3 | Defined | Revenue growing at or above industry average; balanced growth from expansion and new customers; customer base diversified; growth levers identified | At or above industry median growth; net revenue retention and new logo both contributing; top 3 customers below 20%; growth levers documented with investment plans |
| 4 | Managed | Revenue growing significantly above industry; multiple growth engines active; market expansion underway; growth capital deployed with measured returns | Top-quartile industry growth; multiple products/markets contributing; international or new market expansion active; ROIC measured against cost of capital |
| 5 | Optimized | Sustained best-in-class growth with profitability; growth compounding through network effects or platform dynamics; addressable market expanding | Industry-leading growth sustained 3+ years; growth creating competitive moats; capital efficiency of growth improving; addressable market expanding through platform effects |

**Red flags**: Revenue declining year-over-year; customer concentration above 30% in one customer; growth rate below industry median for 2+ years; all growth from pricing not volume; no new product or market pipeline. [src5]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "What is your revenue growth rate, and what percentage comes from existing customer expansion versus new customers?"

### Dimension 6: Financial Controls & Reporting Quality

**What this measures**: The reliability and timeliness of financial reporting, the strength of internal controls, and the quality of financial planning processes.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | Financial close takes 30+ days; no internal controls; financial statements unreliable; no budgeting process | Books not closed monthly; reconciliations not performed; no segregation of duties; financial data in spreadsheets; no audit |
| 2 | Emerging | Monthly close within 15-20 days; basic controls exist; financial statements reviewed but not audited; annual budget created | Monthly close process exists but slow; basic reconciliations; annual budget but limited variance analysis; no external audit; some segregation of duties |
| 3 | Defined | Monthly close within 10 days; SOX-like controls (even if not required); financial statements audited annually; rolling forecast supplements budget | Close within 10 business days; documented control procedures; annual external audit; 12-month rolling forecast; variance analysis monthly; controller function established |
| 4 | Managed | Monthly close within 5 days; robust internal controls with testing; board-quality financial reporting; FP&A function providing strategic insight | Close within 5 days; controls tested and documented; board reporting package standardized; FP&A partners with business units; scenario planning for major decisions |
| 5 | Optimized | Continuous close capability; AI-assisted anomaly detection; real-time financial visibility; world-class FP&A with predictive capabilities | Near-real-time close; AI flags anomalies in transactions; predictive financial models; FP&A is a strategic differentiator; zero material control weaknesses |

**Red flags**: Monthly close exceeds 15 days; no reconciliation process; financial data in spreadsheets without controls; no external audit; segregation of duties not enforced; budget created annually and never revised. [src6]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "How many days does your monthly close take, and when was your last external audit?"

## Scoring & Interpretation

### Overall Score Calculation

All dimensions are weighted equally for a general assessment. Weight liquidity and leverage more heavily (1.5x) for companies with significant debt, and growth and profitability more heavily for growth-stage companies.

```
Overall Score = (Liquidity + Profitability + Efficiency + Leverage + Growth + Controls) / 6
```

### Score Interpretation

| Overall Score | Maturity Level | Interpretation | Recommended Next Step |
|---------------|---------------|----------------|----------------------|
| 1.0 - 1.9 | Critical | Financial distress signals present; immediate intervention required; business sustainability at risk | Emergency cash management; engage turnaround advisor; restructure debt; stabilize revenue |
| 2.0 - 2.9 | Developing | Financial foundation exists but vulnerabilities present; growth constrained by financial weaknesses | Address liquidity gaps; implement budgeting and variance analysis; optimize working capital; seek growth capital if needed |
| 3.0 - 3.9 | Competent | Solid financial health with room for optimization; fundable and scalable with targeted improvements | Optimize capital structure; implement rolling forecasts; build FP&A capability; benchmark and close margin gaps |
| 4.0 - 4.5 | Advanced | Strong financial health; ready for significant growth or market opportunities; investor-ready | Fine-tune efficiency; explore strategic debt; build predictive financial models; optimize ROIC |
| 4.6 - 5.0 | Best-in-class | Exceptional financial health; competitive advantage through financial discipline; acquisition-ready | Maintain excellence; deploy capital strategically; pursue M&A from strength; innovate financial operations |

## Common Pitfalls in Assessment

- **Profitability without cash flow**: Companies can be profitable on paper while running out of cash. Accrual accounting can mask cash flow problems — always assess both the income statement and the cash flow statement together. A profitable company with negative operating cash flow has a collections, inventory, or working capital problem. [src1]
- **Industry benchmark misapplication**: A 20% gross margin is excellent in grocery retail but catastrophic in SaaS. Always benchmark within your industry and business model. Using cross-industry benchmarks produces misleading assessments. [src3]
- **Growth-at-all-costs blind spot**: Rapid revenue growth can mask deteriorating unit economics and unsustainable burn rates. Assess whether growth is capital-efficient by examining incremental margins and ROIC, not just top-line growth. [src5]
- **Quarterly snapshot vs trend analysis**: A single quarter's financial ratios can be misleading due to seasonality, one-time items, or timing effects. Always assess trends over 8-12 quarters to identify genuine trajectories versus noise. [src2]
