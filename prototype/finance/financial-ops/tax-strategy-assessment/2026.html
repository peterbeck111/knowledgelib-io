Tax Strategy Assessment

How optimized is the tax strategy — entity structure, transfer pricing, R&D credits by jurisdiction?

Type: Assessment Confidence: 0.82 Sources: 6 Verified: 2026-03-10

Purpose

This assessment evaluates the maturity of an organization's tax strategy across five dimensions: entity structure and tax planning, transfer pricing, tax credits and incentives, compliance and reporting, and Pillar Two / international tax readiness. The output identifies where tax value is left on the table and compliance risks exist. [src2]

Constraints

  • Requires tax returns (3 years), entity docs, TP documentation, and provision workpapers
  • Not meaningful for single-jurisdiction companies under $5M revenue
  • Should involve VP Tax, CFO, and external advisors for complex structures
  • Diagnostic only — does not constitute tax advice; validate with qualified professionals
  • Tax law changes frequently — benchmarks valid as of March 2026; verify current law

Common Pitfalls in Assessment

  • Confusing compliance with strategy: Filing on time is compliance, not strategy. Mature tax functions proactively optimize.
  • R&D credit underclaiming: Many companies miss credits or lack project-level documentation. OBBBA changes create new opportunities and requirements.
  • Pillar Two procrastination: Organizations assume it does not apply or rely on safe harbours without validation.
  • TP as afterthought: Documentation created retroactively creates audit risk and misses optimization.

When This Matters

Fetch when evaluating tax strategy, optimizing entity structure, assessing R&D credit programs, preparing for Pillar Two, or benchmarking tax function maturity. Critical during international expansion, M&A, and annual tax planning.
