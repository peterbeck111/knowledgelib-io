Insurance Risk Management Assessment

How adequate is insurance coverage — D&O, E&O, cyber liability, key-person benchmarks by size?

Type: Assessment Confidence: 0.84 Sources: 6 Verified: 2026-03-10

Purpose

This assessment evaluates the adequacy of an organization's insurance program across five critical coverage dimensions: D&O liability, E&O/professional liability, cyber liability, key-person insurance, and general commercial coverage. The output is a composite coverage adequacy score (1-5) with dimension-specific gap identification calibrated to company stage and revenue. Use this when evaluating whether insurance coverage matches actual risk exposure. [src1]

Constraints

  • Requires current insurance policies, declarations pages, and broker correspondence for reliable scoring
  • Not meaningful for pre-revenue solo founders — basic general liability is sufficient
  • Should involve CFO, legal counsel, and insurance broker for cross-functional accuracy
  • Diagnostic only — identifies coverage gaps but does not recommend carriers or policies
  • Re-run at every annual renewal; also after fundraising, M&A, major contracts, or regulatory changes

Common Pitfalls in Assessment

  • Policy existence vs. coverage adequacy: Having a policy does not mean coverage is adequate. Many organizations never revisit founding-stage limits, leaving gaps discovered only during claims.
  • Cyber-E&O gap: Both policies may exist but coverage gaps or overlaps between them are common. A data breach during services delivery may trigger both or neither.
  • Key-person valuation drift: Coverage amounts set at founding become inadequate as economic value grows. A CTO worth $200K at seed may represent $5M+ at Series C.
  • Self-assessment bias on D&O exposure: Executives underestimate D&O exposure, believing lawsuits only affect large public companies. Private company claims are common and growing.
