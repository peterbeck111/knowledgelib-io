# Procurement Maturity Assessment

## Purpose

This assessment evaluates the maturity of an organization's procurement function across six critical dimensions: strategic sourcing, supplier relationship management, spend visibility and analytics, contract lifecycle management, process automation, and savings tracking. The output is a composite maturity score (1-5) that identifies capability gaps and routes to specific improvement actions. Use this when evaluating procurement's contribution to the bottom line, preparing for a CFO-level operational review, or benchmarking against industry peers. [src1]

## Constraints
<!-- Agents: read before running this assessment with a user. -->

- Requires access to spend data (12+ months), supplier master data, and contract repository for reliable scoring
- Not meaningful for organizations with fewer than $2M in addressable spend or fewer than 20 active suppliers
- Assessment should involve CPO/VP Procurement and finance leadership for accurate cross-functional scoring
- Diagnostic only — identifies current state; pair with sourcing strategy playbooks for recommendations
- Re-run semi-annually; procurement maturity shifts with market conditions, technology adoption, and organizational changes

## Assessment Dimensions

<!-- Each dimension is scored independently. The structured format lets agents
     walk through this conversationally with a user, one dimension at a time. -->

### Dimension 1: Strategic Sourcing

**What this measures**: How effectively the organization identifies, evaluates, and selects suppliers using structured category strategies rather than reactive purchasing.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | Purchasing is reactive — buyers fulfill requests without category strategies or market analysis | No sourcing events; single-source relationships dominate; no competitive bidding |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic competitive bidding on large purchases; some supplier research before major commitments | RFP/RFQ process exists for purchases above a threshold; 2-3 bids per event |
| 3 | Defined | Category strategies exist for top 5-10 spend categories; regular sourcing events with structured evaluation criteria | Documented category plans; weighted scoring for supplier selection; annual sourcing calendar |
| 4 | Managed | Category strategies cover 70%+ of addressable spend; total cost of ownership (TCO) analysis standard; market intelligence informs sourcing decisions | TCO models used in evaluations; regular market benchmarking; should-cost models for key categories |
| 5 | Optimized | AI-assisted category intelligence; predictive sourcing; innovation-focused supplier partnerships; value creation beyond cost savings | Autonomous spend classification; predictive market analytics; supplier innovation programs yielding measurable value |

**Red flags**: No sourcing events in the past 12 months; 80%+ single-source relationships; buyers cannot articulate category strategies for top spend areas. [src2]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "For your top 5 spend categories, do you have documented sourcing strategies with defined evaluation criteria and regular competitive events?"

### Dimension 2: Supplier Relationship Management

**What this measures**: How effectively the organization manages, develops, and segments its supplier base beyond transactional purchasing.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | Supplier relationships are transactional — no formal performance tracking or segmentation | No supplier scorecards; relationships managed by individual buyers without coordination |
| 2 | Emerging | Key suppliers identified informally; some performance discussions during contract renewals | Informal preferred supplier lists; annual reviews with top 5-10 suppliers |
| 3 | Defined | Formal supplier segmentation (strategic, preferred, transactional); quarterly business reviews with strategic suppliers | Documented segmentation model; supplier scorecards with defined KPIs; regular review cadence |
| 4 | Managed | Supplier development programs; risk monitoring across tiers; collaborative innovation initiatives | Tier 2/3 visibility; supplier development investments; joint innovation roadmaps; formal risk assessments |
| 5 | Optimized | Real-time supplier risk monitoring; AI-powered supplier intelligence; ecosystem orchestration with strategic partners | Continuous risk scoring; predictive supplier health indicators; measurable innovation pipeline from supplier collaboration |

**Red flags**: Cannot name top 10 suppliers by spend; no supplier performance data beyond on-time delivery; critical suppliers have no backup sources. [src3]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "Do you formally segment your suppliers into tiers with different management approaches, and how often do you conduct structured business reviews with strategic suppliers?"

### Dimension 3: Spend Visibility and Analytics

**What this measures**: The organization's ability to capture, classify, and analyze procurement spend data to drive informed decisions.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | Spend data is scattered across systems; no consolidated spend view; analysis is manual and ad hoc | No spend cube; data pulled from AP invoices manually; unknown maverick spend levels |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic spend reports from ERP/AP; top-level category visibility but limited granularity | Monthly spend reports by vendor or department; manual data cleansing; 50-60% spend classified |
| 3 | Defined | Spend analytics tool deployed; 80%+ spend classified; regular spend analysis drives sourcing decisions | Automated spend classification; dashboards for category managers; tail spend identified and quantified |
| 4 | Managed | Real-time spend visibility; predictive analytics; benchmarking against external data; compliance monitoring | Live dashboards; contract compliance tracking; price variance analysis; demand forecasting from spend patterns |
| 5 | Optimized | AI-driven spend intelligence; autonomous anomaly detection; prescriptive analytics recommending sourcing actions | ML-powered classification (95%+ accuracy); automated savings opportunity identification; predictive compliance alerts |

**Red flags**: Finance and procurement report different spend numbers; more than 30% of spend is unclassified; no one can answer "what did we spend with Supplier X last year?" within an hour. [src4]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "What percentage of your total spend can you classify by category and supplier within 24 hours, and do you have a single source of truth for spend data?"

### Dimension 4: Contract Lifecycle Management

**What this measures**: How effectively the organization creates, negotiates, executes, monitors, and renews contracts to maximize value and minimize risk.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | Contracts stored in email or shared drives; no standard templates; renewal dates frequently missed | Contracts lost or unfindable; no clause libraries; auto-renewals occur without review |
| 2 | Emerging | Central contract repository exists; standard templates for common agreements; basic expiration tracking | Shared drive or basic CLM tool; template library started; manual renewal reminders |
| 3 | Defined | CLM system deployed; standardized workflows for approval; obligation tracking; 90%+ contracts in repository | Automated approval routing; milestone and obligation alerts; compliance clause tracking |
| 4 | Managed | Contract analytics driving negotiations; obligation compliance monitored; value leakage identified and recovered | Contract performance dashboards; automated compliance monitoring; value recovery programs; risk scoring |
| 5 | Optimized | AI-assisted contract review and drafting; predictive analytics on contract performance; self-service contracting for low-risk agreements | AI redlining and clause extraction; contract intelligence feeding sourcing strategy; fully automated low-value contracting |

**Red flags**: Cannot locate 20%+ of active contracts; no one tracks contract obligation compliance; renewal dates discovered only when suppliers send invoices at new rates. [src5]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "Where are your contracts stored, and can you tell me how many active contracts are expiring in the next 90 days?"

### Dimension 5: Process Automation and Technology

**What this measures**: The degree to which procurement processes are digitized, automated, and integrated with enterprise systems.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | Manual, paper-based processes; email-driven approvals; no procurement technology beyond basic ERP | Purchase requests via email; manual PO creation; no e-sourcing or P2P tools |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic P2P system or e-procurement module; some electronic approvals; limited adoption by users | ERP procurement module deployed but low adoption (<50%); some electronic catalogs |
| 3 | Defined | Integrated P2P platform with 70%+ adoption; e-sourcing for competitive events; automated three-way matching | Defined P2P workflows; electronic approvals standard; touchless PO processing for catalog items |
| 4 | Managed | End-to-end procurement suite (S2P); integrated with ERP, finance, and supplier portals; analytics embedded | S2P platform with high adoption (85%+); supplier self-service portal; embedded spend analytics |
| 5 | Optimized | AI-powered procurement copilots; autonomous purchasing for routine items; predictive demand sensing; RPA for repetitive tasks | Autonomous PO creation for standard items; AI-driven supplier recommendations; intelligent workflow routing |

**Red flags**: Purchase orders created manually in spreadsheets; approval cycles exceed 5 business days; procurement team spends 60%+ time on tactical/transactional work. [src2]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "What percentage of your purchase orders are processed without manual intervention, and what is your average requisition-to-PO cycle time?"

### Dimension 6: Savings Measurement and Value Tracking

**What this measures**: How rigorously the organization measures, validates, and reports procurement savings and value creation beyond cost reduction.

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Ad hoc | No formal savings tracking; procurement cannot quantify its financial impact | No savings methodology; anecdotal claims; finance does not recognize procurement savings |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic savings tracked (negotiated vs. previous price); reported annually but not validated by finance | Spreadsheet-based savings log; savings defined differently by procurement and finance |
| 3 | Defined | Agreed savings methodology with finance; cost avoidance tracked separately; quarterly reporting with validation | Documented savings taxonomy; finance sign-off on savings claims; cost avoidance and hard savings separated |
| 4 | Managed | Savings tracked to P&L impact; value beyond savings measured (risk reduction, quality improvement, speed); benchmarked against peers | Savings-to-P&L bridge; total value of procurement (TVP) framework; Hackett or CAPS benchmarking |
| 5 | Optimized | Real-time savings pipeline; predictive savings forecasting; value creation metrics linked to business outcomes and ESG goals | Live savings dashboard; forward-looking savings pipeline; ESG value metrics; procurement contribution to revenue |

**Red flags**: Procurement claims $10M in savings but finance sees no P&L impact; savings methodology not documented or agreed with finance; same savings counted multiple times. [src6]
**Quick diagnostic question**: "Does finance validate and sign off on procurement's reported savings, and can you show the P&L impact of last year's sourcing initiatives?"

## Scoring & Interpretation

### Overall Score Calculation

All six dimensions are weighted equally for organizations running this assessment for the first time. Organizations with established procurement functions may weight Strategic Sourcing and Spend Visibility higher.

```
Overall Score = (Strategic Sourcing + Supplier Mgmt + Spend Visibility + Contract Mgmt + Process Automation + Savings Tracking) / 6
```

### Score Interpretation

| Overall Score | Maturity Level | Interpretation | Recommended Next Step |
|---------------|---------------|----------------|----------------------|
| 1.0 - 1.9 | Critical | Procurement is purely transactional — reactive purchasing with no strategic contribution. Significant value leakage likely. | Build foundational procurement processes: establish spend visibility, create basic category strategies, implement P2P workflows |
| 2.0 - 2.9 | Developing | Basic procurement capabilities exist but are inconsistent and under-resourced. Organization is leaving 10-15% savings on the table. | Prioritize spend visibility and strategic sourcing for top categories; formalize supplier segmentation |
| 3.0 - 3.9 | Competent | Procurement is a recognized function with defined processes. Focus shifts from building capabilities to optimizing and scaling. | Deploy analytics, expand category management coverage, integrate procurement technology |
| 4.0 - 4.5 | Advanced | Procurement is a strategic partner to the business — contributing to value beyond cost reduction. | Focus on innovation, ESG integration, predictive analytics, and supplier ecosystem orchestration |
| 4.6 - 5.0 | Best-in-class | World-class procurement driving competitive advantage. Benchmark for peers. | Maintain edge through AI adoption, continuous innovation partnerships, and thought leadership |

## Common Pitfalls in Assessment

- **Self-assessment inflation**: Procurement teams consistently over-score by 0.8-1.2 points. Calibrate by requiring specific evidence (reports, dashboards, documented processes) — not descriptions of intent or planned initiatives. [src4]
- **Confusing activity with maturity**: Running e-auctions does not equal strategic sourcing maturity. Scoring should reflect outcomes (savings realized, supplier performance improvements) not just tool deployment.
- **Ignoring tail spend**: Organizations focus assessment on top categories while 20-30% of spend (tail) remains unmanaged. A mature function addresses the full spend spectrum, not just strategic categories.
- **Technology as a proxy for maturity**: Deploying a best-in-class S2P platform does not automatically elevate maturity if adoption is low, data quality is poor, or processes are not redesigned.
