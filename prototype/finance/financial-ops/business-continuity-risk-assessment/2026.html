Business Continuity Risk Assessment

How prepared is the business for disruption — DR readiness, insurance, key-person dependency?

Type: Assessment Confidence: 0.86 Sources: 6 Verified: 2026-03-10

Purpose

This assessment evaluates an organization's preparedness for disruptive events across six dimensions: business impact analysis, disaster recovery readiness, key-person dependency, insurance and financial resilience, crisis communication, and testing practices. Aligned with ISO 22301 principles, the output identifies the most critical gaps in organizational resilience. [src1]

Constraints

  • Requires BCP documentation, insurance policies, DR test results, and org charts for reliable scoring
  • Not meaningful for solo founders or companies with fewer than 5 employees
  • Should involve CEO/COO, CFO, IT leadership, and HR for cross-functional accuracy
  • Diagnostic only — identifies gaps but does not create BCP plans or DR runbooks
  • Re-run annually; also after major organizational changes, M&A events, or near-miss incidents

Common Pitfalls in Assessment

  • Plan existence vs. effectiveness: Having a BCP document does not equal being prepared. Score based on tested capabilities, not document completeness.
  • IT-centric bias: BC encompasses people, processes, suppliers, and facilities — not just technology.
  • Key-person blind spot: Leadership underestimates this risk because the key person is themselves.
  • Insurance complacency: Coverage gaps are typically discovered during claims, not during assessments.
