# Investor Due Diligence Metrics

## Definition

Investor due diligence metrics are the 25 core financial, operational, and customer health KPIs that venture capital investors and acquirers systematically analyze when evaluating a SaaS company for investment or acquisition. Each metric has specific red flag thresholds that trigger deeper investigation, valuation discounts, or deal rejection. The diligence process verifies management claims against raw data — 85% of deals see purchase price adjustments during this phase. These metrics fall into five categories: revenue quality, unit economics, retention and engagement, capital efficiency, and growth sustainability. [src1]

## Key Properties

- **Revenue quality metrics (5)**: ARR/MRR accuracy, revenue concentration (red flag: any customer >20% of revenue), recurring vs non-recurring mix (target: >85% recurring), gross margin (red flag: <70%), revenue recognition compliance (ASC 606) [src1]
- **Unit economics metrics (5)**: CAC by channel, LTV:CAC ratio (red flag: <3:1 at growth stage), CAC payback period (red flag: >24 months), gross margin per customer, blended vs segmented unit economics [src2]
- **Retention metrics (5)**: NRR (red flag: <100%), GRR (red flag: <85%), logo churn (red flag: >5% annual at scale), cohort retention curves, expansion revenue rate [src3]
- **Capital efficiency metrics (5)**: Burn multiple (red flag: >2x past Series A), Rule of 40 score, runway remaining, cash conversion score, operating leverage trend [src4]
- **Growth sustainability metrics (5)**: YoY revenue growth rate, pipeline coverage ratio (red flag: <3x), sales efficiency (Magic Number), growth rate of growth rate (acceleration vs deceleration), market share trajectory [src5]

## Constraints

     These are hard boundaries on when and how it applies. -->

- Red flag thresholds are stage-dependent — 15% annual logo churn is acceptable at seed but disqualifying at Series C [src2]
- Custom metric definitions (e.g., counting non-recurring services in ARR) trigger immediate skepticism and recalculation [src3]
- Investors verify metrics against raw billing data, bank statements, and CRM exports — management-reported metrics are treated as claims, not facts [src1]
- Technical diligence (code quality, security, infrastructure) is equally important but separate from financial metrics [src4]
- AI-native SaaS faces additional diligence on model defensibility, data moats, and revenue model risk (per-seat vs usage pricing) [src2]

## Application Checklist

### Step 1: Revenue quality audit
- **Inputs needed**: 24 months of billing data, contract details, revenue breakdown by type (subscription, services, usage), top 20 customers by revenue
- **Output**: Verified ARR, recurring revenue percentage, customer concentration analysis, gross margin calculation
- **Constraint**: If any single customer represents >20% of revenue, flag as concentration risk. If non-recurring revenue exceeds 15% of total, adjust ARR calculation downward [src1]

### Step 2: Unit economics verification
- **Inputs needed**: Fully-loaded CAC by channel, cohort-based LTV calculations, payback period data, gross margin per customer segment
- **Output**: Verified LTV:CAC ratio, CAC payback period, channel-level unit economics
- **Constraint**: Verify CAC includes all costs (salaries, tools, overhead) — most companies undercount by 20-40%. If LTV:CAC is below 3:1 at growth stage or payback exceeds 24 months, flag as red flag [src2]

### Step 3: Retention deep dive
- **Inputs needed**: Monthly cohort data for 12+ months, NRR and GRR calculations, logo churn by segment, expansion revenue breakdown
- **Output**: Cohort retention heatmap, NRR/GRR trend analysis, churn root cause assessment
- **Constraint**: NRR below 100% is a fire alarm at any stage past seed — it means the existing customer base is shrinking. Verify calculation methodology matches industry standard (not custom definitions that hide churn) [src3]

### Step 4: Capital efficiency and growth sustainability check
- **Inputs needed**: Burn multiple (trailing 4 quarters), Rule of 40 score, pipeline coverage, YoY growth rates for last 8 quarters
- **Output**: Efficiency assessment, growth acceleration/deceleration trend, runway analysis
- **Constraint**: Burn multiple above 2x past Series A signals inefficient growth. Decelerating growth rate (growth rate of growth rate negative) requires explanation — is it market saturation, execution problems, or planned shift to profitability [src4]

### Step 5: Red flag synthesis and valuation impact
- **Inputs needed**: All findings from steps 1-4
- **Output**: Red flag summary with estimated valuation impact per issue
- **Constraint**: Each red flag typically causes a 5-15% valuation discount. Multiple red flags compound — three concurrent issues can reduce valuation by 30-50% from initial offer [src1]

## Anti-Patterns

### Wrong: Presenting custom-defined metrics without disclosure
A company includes one-time services revenue and multi-year contract prepayments in "ARR" to inflate the headline number. Investors recalculate from billing data and discover actual recurring ARR is 25% lower, destroying trust and killing the deal. [src1]

### Correct: Use standard metric definitions and disclose methodology
Present ARR using standard definitions (annualized monthly recurring subscription revenue only). Include a methodology appendix explaining exactly how each metric is calculated. Proactive transparency builds trust; discovered discrepancies destroy it. [src3]

### Wrong: Hiding deteriorating metrics behind aggregate numbers
A company reports healthy 115% NRR overall, but enterprise segment NRR is 140% while SMB segment NRR is 75%. Investors who segment the data discover the SMB business is collapsing, which aggregate reporting obscured. [src2]

### Correct: Present metrics by segment proactively
Break out key metrics by customer segment (SMB, mid-market, enterprise), acquisition channel, and geography. Address weak segments directly with root cause analysis and improvement plans. Investors respect founders who identify and address problems transparently. [src3]

### Wrong: Addressing metric red flags only when asked
A founder hopes investors won't notice that logo churn spiked from 8% to 14% in the last two quarters. The investor discovers it on page 3 of the data room and immediately questions what else is being hidden. [src2]

### Correct: Proactively flag and explain red flags
In the management presentation, call out the churn spike, explain the root cause (e.g., pricing change, product migration, market event), and present the remediation plan with leading indicators showing improvement. [src4]

## Common Misconceptions

- **Misconception**: Investors primarily evaluate the product and market during due diligence.
  **Reality**: Financial and operational metrics consume 60-70% of diligence effort. Product and market assessment happen earlier in the funnel. By diligence stage, investors are verifying whether the numbers support the thesis, not whether the thesis is correct. [src1]

- **Misconception**: Strong topline growth compensates for poor unit economics.
  **Reality**: Post-2022, investors weight capital efficiency equally with growth. A company growing 80% with a 4x burn multiple and <3:1 LTV:CAC will receive significantly lower multiples than one growing 40% with 1.5x burn and 4:1 LTV:CAC. [src4]

- **Misconception**: Due diligence metrics are the same for VC and M&A.
  **Reality**: M&A diligence adds customer concentration risk (integration dependency), revenue transferability (key-person risk), technology debt assessment, and contractual risks (change-of-control clauses). VC diligence focuses more on growth sustainability and market opportunity. [src5]

- **Misconception**: A clean data room is sufficient for diligence.
  **Reality**: Investors independently verify metrics against raw data sources (bank statements, Stripe/billing exports, CRM pulls). A clean data room accelerates the process but does not substitute for verification. 85% of deals see adjustments. [src1]
