# Best Cordless Leaf Blowers (2026)

## Summary

Cordless leaf blowers have evolved dramatically, and the best battery-powered models in 2026 now match or exceed the performance of gas-powered equivalents while running quieter and producing zero emissions. Pro Tool Reviews' April 2026 head-to-head named the new Ryobi RY40HPLB01 Whisper Series (~$229 bare / ~$329 kit) the overall winner with 800 CFM, 180 MPH, 24.8 Newtons of blow force, and 85 minutes of runtime from its 40V HP 8.0Ah battery — the best power-per-dollar ratio tested. The EGO Power+ LB7654 (~$280) remains the strongest 56V pick at 26N of blow force, while the Stihl BGA 250 (~$329 bare) launches as a quieter professional alternative with 615 CFM, 190 MPH, 26N, and only 60 dB(A). [src1, src2, src7, src9]

The cordless leaf blower market now spans from sub-$100 handhelds (Kobalt KHB 4224A-03) to $700 commercial backpack units (EGO LBPX8004-2), with the CES 2026-announced Tone T1 (~$600, <52 dB, 25N, 150 MPH) redefining the premium ultra-quiet niche. Key differentiators are airflow volume (CFM), air speed (MPH), blow force (Newtons), battery runtime, and weight. For typical suburban yards under half an acre, a mid-range platform handheld like the EGO Power+ LB6504 (~$250), Greenworks Pro 80V (~$200), or Ryobi 40V HP 800 CFM (~$229 bare) provides ample power. For maximum raw airflow the Toro 60V MAX 900 CFM (~$350) still pushes the highest CFM ceiling; for the quietest high-power operation the DeWalt DCBL777 (75-79 dB) and Stihl BGA 250 (60 dB) lead. The Ryobi RY404150 (~$300) uniquely combines blower, vacuum, and mulcher functions in one tool. [src1, src3, src4, src6, src9, src10]

## Best for Each Use Case

### Best Overall: Ryobi RY40HPLB01 Whisper Series (~$229 bare / ~$329 kit) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/ryobi-ry40hplb01-whisper)
Pro Tool Reviews' April 2026 head-to-head scored the Ryobi RY40HPLB01 at 95/100 — the highest of any cordless handheld tested — with 800 CFM, 180 MPH, and a measured 24.8 Newtons of blow force. The 40V HP brushless motor with 8.0Ah battery delivers up to 85 minutes of runtime total (11 min 37 sec on full-blast high), and the "Whisper Series" tuning keeps it notably quieter than the Toro 900 CFM and Husqvarna 350iB. At 10.6 lbs with the 8.0Ah pack and a 5-year warranty, it matches the performance of 56V flagships at ~40-50% lower cost, and it plugs into the Ryobi 40V ecosystem of 300+ tools — making it the new power-per-dollar benchmark. [src4, src9]

### Best 56V Platform Pick: EGO Power+ LB7654 (~$280) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/ego-power-plus-lb7654)
The EGO Power+ LB7654 still leads the 56V ARC Lithium ecosystem with 765 CFM at 200 MPH and a maximum blow force of 26 Newtons — the highest measured among handheld cordless blowers. Its variable-speed trigger with cruise control allows fine-tuned control from gentle sweeping to turbo-mode leaf clearing, and the 5.0Ah battery provides up to 150 minutes on low or 15 minutes on turbo (~90 minutes in mixed use). Bob Vila's testing confirmed it can handle yards up to an acre. If you already own EGO tools (75+ in the 56V lineup), this remains the best-in-ecosystem choice over switching to the Ryobi 40V HP platform. [src1, src3]

### Best Budget: Kobalt KHB 4224A-03 (~$99) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/kobalt-khb-4224a-03)
At under $100 including battery and charger, the Kobalt KHB 4224A-03 costs less than 80% of other battery-powered blowers while providing above-average performance and superior comfort. It delivers 500 CFM at 120 MPH and weighs just 5.4 lbs — the lightest model in this comparison. With up to 50 minutes of runtime on the 24V 4.0Ah battery, it handles small to mid-size yards efficiently. The trade-off is lower blow force compared to 56V and 80V models. Available exclusively at Lowe's. [src2, src3]

### Best for Large Yards: Husqvarna Leaf Blaster 350iB (~$350) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/husqvarna-leaf-blaster-350ib)
The Husqvarna Leaf Blaster 350iB generates 800 CFM at 200 MPH with 23 Newtons of blow force, making it the most powerful handheld option for heavy-duty fall cleanups on properties over half an acre. Its perfectly balanced, 10.4-lb design with cruise control and boost mode (20% more power) reduces arm and shoulder fatigue during extended sessions, and the 40V 7.5Ah battery delivers up to 60 minutes of runtime with a 90-minute recharge. Multiple reviewers noted its phenomenal measured blowing power and highly ergonomic balance. [src1, src3, src4]

### Highest CFM: Toro 60V MAX 900 CFM (~$350) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/toro-60v-900-cfm-51827)
New for 2026, the Toro 60V MAX 900 CFM (model 51827) pushes the highest airflow of any handheld cordless blower at 900 CFM and 165 MPH. The 3-phase brushless motor with RunSmart technology optimizes performance and runtime. However, at 11.9 lbs with the 4.0Ah battery, it is the heaviest handheld in this comparison — a significant factor for extended use. Turbo runtime is limited to 10-16 minutes. Backed by a 3-year warranty on tool and battery. [src4, src7]

### Best Lightweight: Stihl BGA 60 (~$230) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/stihl-bga-60)
At just under 8 pounds with battery, the Stihl BGA 60 offers 459 CFM at 154 MPH in a highly controllable package ideal for smaller properties, patios, and driveways. Bob Vila's testers praised its easy-to-control airflow in confined spaces — a significant advantage when clearing debris around flower beds, garden furniture, and landscaping features. The 36V 5.2Ah battery provides 16 minutes at high power, which is sufficient for routine cleanups on quarter-acre lots. [src1, src3]

### Best Blower/Vacuum Combo: Ryobi RY404150 Jet Fan (~$300) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/ryobi-ry404150-jet-fan)
The Ryobi RY404150 is the only model in this comparison that combines blowing, vacuuming, and mulching in a single cordless tool. It delivers 600 CFM at 100 MPH in blower mode and features a 20:1 mulch ratio with a metal impeller that condenses 20 bags of leaves into one bag of mulch. The single-tube inline design allows transitioning from blower to vacuum mode with just the turn of a dial. At 15.2 lbs with two included 40V 4.0Ah batteries, it is the heaviest handheld option but eliminates the need to buy a separate vacuum. [src3, src4]

### Best for Quiet High-Power Operation: DeWalt DCBL777 60V MAX (~$270) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/dewalt-dcbl777-60v)
The DeWalt DCBL777 replaces the older DCBL772X1 with major improvements: 760 CFM at 160 MPH (up from 600 CFM / 125 MPH), yet produces only 75 dB on Eco mode, 77 dB on Normal, and 79 dB on Turbo — 78% quieter than gas equivalents. Pro Tool Reviews praised the significant noise reduction with improved pitch characteristics. Runtime is 18 minutes 40 seconds on Normal, 14 minutes 8 seconds on Turbo. The 60V MAX FlexVolt battery is compatible with DeWalt's full lineup of 250+ tools. [src5, src6]

### Best Quiet Pro-Grade: Stihl BGA 250 (~$329 bare / ~$769 kit) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/stihl-bga-250)
New for 2026, the Stihl BGA 250 is the most powerful battery handheld in the Stihl AP lineup: 615 CFM at 190 MPH, 26 Newtons of blow force (tied with EGO LB7654 for the highest measured), and just 60 dB(A) — quieter than any pro-grade blower in this list except the Makita CBU01Z. At 6 lbs bare (10.1 lbs with AP 300S battery), it is well-balanced for extended sessions and includes cruise control plus Smart Connector 2 A compatibility for fleet management. Runtime with the AP 300S is 27-40 minutes depending on speed. Ideal for commercial landscapers working in noise-restricted municipalities where the 70 dB electric-blower cap applies. [src9]

### Best Ultra-Quiet Premium (Pre-Order): Tone T1 (~$599.99) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/tone-t1-quiet-blower)
Announced at CES 2026 and developed by a former NASA engineer, the Tone T1 targets suburban homeowners under noise ordinances with <52 dB operation — quieter than a refrigerator and 8 dB below the Makita CBU01Z. It delivers 25 Newtons of blow force and 150 MPH from a 5.0Ah battery (~50 minutes in Eco), with five operational modes, an integrated nightlight, and one-handed operation. Available for pre-order at $100 deposit with final payment due August 2026. A new premium ultra-quiet category entrant that validates the "noise as a competitive axis" trend but unproven in real-world durability tests. [src10]

### Best Runtime: Makita CBU01Z ConnectX (~$300) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/makita-cbu01z)
The Makita CBU01Z offers up to 300 minutes of runtime on a single charge at low speed — by far the longest in this comparison — making it the top choice for commercial landscapers who need all-day operation without battery swaps. At just 5.7 lbs, it is also the lightest high-performance option. It produces only 59 dB, making it the quietest handheld on the list. The ConnectX 36V platform is expanding but has fewer compatible tools than EGO or DeWalt ecosystems. [src3, src4]

### Best Commercial-Grade: EGO Power+ LBPX8004-2 Backpack (~$700) — [Check price](https://knowledgelib.io/go/ego-lbpx8004-2-backpack)
For professional landscapers or homeowners with large properties exceeding one acre, the EGO Power+ LBPX8004-2 delivers 800 CFM at 190 MPH with 26 Newtons of blow force from a 1,900-watt motor. The backpack design distributes the 29-lb weight across the shoulders and back, and the two included 56V 6.0Ah batteries provide up to 120 minutes on low or 30 minutes on high. It matches gas backpack blowers in raw output while running significantly quieter. [src1, src3]

## Important Caveats

- Prices are approximate US street prices as of April 2026. Sales, bundles, and regional pricing vary significantly. Some models (Kobalt) are exclusive to specific retailers (Lowe's). Toro is widely available at Lowe's and Home Depot.
- CFM and MPH are manufacturer-rated specifications measured at the nozzle tip under ideal conditions. Real-world performance varies with nozzle attachment, battery charge level, and speed setting.
- Runtime figures represent manufacturer claims under optimized conditions. Expect 20-40% less runtime at higher speed settings or with partially depleted batteries. The Toro 900 CFM drops to 10-16 minutes on turbo despite a 120-minute low-speed claim.
- Battery and charger inclusion varies by SKU. Tool-only versions cost significantly less but require compatible batteries. Always verify what is included before purchasing.
- Weight listed is with battery installed. Tool-only weight is typically 2-5 lbs less.
- Noise levels vary by measurement method and distance. The Makita CBU01Z at 59 dB and DeWalt DCBL777 at 75 dB are manufacturer-measured figures; independent testing may vary.
